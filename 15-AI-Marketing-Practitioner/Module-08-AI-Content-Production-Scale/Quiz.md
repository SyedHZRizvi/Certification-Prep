## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. Strategy → Generation → Refinement → Distribution**
The pros work in stages. Strategy (human), Generation (AI), Refinement (human QC), Distribution (mixed). Skipping Strategy = generic content. Skipping Refinement = hallucinations published.

### Q2: **B. Claude Sonnet 4.7**
Per the reading: Claude excels at long-form, nuanced, brand-voice-specific content. ChatGPT is faster for structured outputs / variants. Gemini is better for multimodal.

### Q3: **C. Gemini 2.5 Pro**
Gemini's strength: paste an image (e.g., competitor's ad), get text reacting to it. The other models can do this in 2026 too, but Gemini's multimodal handling is particularly strong.

### Q4: **B. Hold persistent context so every output stays on-brand**
Save the project once with voice rules, banned words, tone examples, ICP. Every future request inherits this context. Massive consistency upgrade.

### Q5: **B. Character reference**
`--cref [URL]` makes Midjourney maintain the same person across multiple images. Essential for "our founder" appearing consistent in 20+ ads.

### Q6: **B. Style reference**
`--sref [URL]` makes Midjourney mimic a reference image's aesthetic — lighting, color palette, mood — without copying its content.

### Q7: **B. The current Midjourney model**
v7 is the late-2025/2026 standard. Always specify `--v 7` for current behavior.

### Q8: **B. Still produces poor results; use Canva/Figma for text overlays**
Despite improvements, AI image generators still struggle with text. Generate the image in Midjourney, add text in Canva or Figma.

### Q9: **B. Up to 4K, up to 10-sec cinematic video clips**
Runway Gen-4 (2026): up to 4K resolution, ~10 second max, text-to-video and image-to-video.

### Q10: **B. Stage it: script → storyboard → animate → voiceover → stitch**
The pros work in stages. Script in Claude, storyboard in Midjourney, animate each segment in Runway, voice in ElevenLabs, stitch in CapCut. Single-shot complex outputs fail consistently.

### Q11: **C. AI avatar talking-head videos with lip sync**
HeyGen's specialty: create an AI avatar that looks/speaks as a real person, in multiple languages, with proper lip sync.

### Q12: **B. About 60% of his email video greetings**
Publicly shared at Shopify Editions 2024. Customers don't notice. Open rates the same as real recordings.

### Q13: **B. Multi-language voice generation with cloning**
ElevenLabs Multilingual v3 (2025) is the current standard. Voice cloning + 30+ languages with consistent voice character.

### Q14: **B. Explicit written consent (legal requirement in many jurisdictions)**
NY has voice-cloning legislation; California and EU rules in progress. Best practice everywhere: written consent before cloning anyone's voice.

### Q15: **B. Visual automation platform for chaining AI tools and workflows**
Make.com lets you chain steps: "When form submitted → call Claude API → generate image in Midjourney → post to Slack → save to Drive." Visual flow builder.

### Q16: **B. n8n (self-host) < Make < Zapier**
n8n self-hosted is the cheapest at scale (you pay for compute). Make is mid-range ($9-29/mo). Zapier is most expensive ($299+/mo for serious use).

### Q17: **B. ~$180/mo**
Per the reading: Claude $20 + ChatGPT $20 + Midjourney $30 + Runway $35 + HeyGen $39 + ElevenLabs $11 + Make $9 + Canva $15 = ~$179. Replaces ~$4K/mo of freelance design.

### Q18: **B. NOT copyrightable on its own; human modifications can be**
US Copyright Office's 2024 position: pure AI output isn't copyrightable. Significant human creative input on top is. Affects marketers' IP claims on AI-generated work.

### Q19: **B. AI deepfakes / synthetic likeness in ads**
EU AI Act (in force 2026) requires disclosure of AI-generated synthetic media impersonating real people. Important for any brand using AI avatars in EU markets.

### Q20: **C. Trying to single-shot complex outputs instead of staging the pipeline**
The most common failure mode. Asking "make me a 30-second TikTok ad" in one prompt fails because each AI tool has narrow strengths. Stage the pipeline.

### Q21: **B. A reusable, categorized collection of tested prompts**
After 6 months you'll have 200+ prompts categorized by use case (headlines, body copy, blog outlines, etc.). Asset velocity 10x's.

### Q22: **B. $10M+ annual savings + ~20% marketing ops headcount reduction**
Sebastian Siemiatkowski publicly shared at Klarna's Q3 2024 earnings call. Image generation 80%, copy 40%, translation 100% via AI.

### Q23: **B. ~570 assets**
180 images + 60 videos + 240 emails + 80 blog posts + 8 LPs = 568 for two people in 12 months. Total tool cost: $28K annually.

### Q24: **B. Always render in 1:1, 4:5, AND 9:16 from the start**
Reels = 9:16. Feed = 1:1 or 4:5. YouTube = 16:9. Generate variants from the start; trying to resize a 1:1 into 9:16 produces ugly crops.

### Q25: **B. Specific instructions about voice + DO-NOT-USE words + tone examples massively improve consistency**
Without it, every output sounds generic. With it (banned words list + tone examples + ICP context), AI output stays on-voice across 100s of pieces.

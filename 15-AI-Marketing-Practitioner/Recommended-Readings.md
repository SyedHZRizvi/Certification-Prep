# Recommended Readings, AI Digital Marketing Practitioner Track

> **Purpose:** This curated list extends the per-module "Further Reading" sections with the canonical bibliography for a paid-media + lifecycle + CRO practitioner. Use it during preparation, after passing the Google Ads / Meta MBP / TikTok credentials, and as the foundation of a personal reference library.

> **How to use:** Engage each item at the point in the course indicated. The single most important free anchor for the real exams is the Google Skillshop + Meta Blueprint curricula directly.

---

## Canonical Textbooks (3-5 must-haves)

### Growth and Performance
- **Ellis, Sean & Brown, Morgan, *Hacking Growth: How Today's Fastest-Growing Companies Drive Breakout Success* (Currency, 2017).**
  - The growth-team operating system from the practitioner who coined the term "growth hacking" at Dropbox. The high-tempo experimentation framework, the North Star metric, the four growth levers. Engage during Modules 1, 2, 4.
- **Croll, Alistair & Yoskovitz, Benjamin, *Lean Analytics: Use Data to Build a Better Startup Faster* (O'Reilly, 2013).**
  - The "One Metric That Matters" framework, the six business archetypes, the stage-gate growth model. Still the best book on which numbers to actually look at. Engage during Modules 1, 7, 10.
- **Ariely, Dan, *Predictably Irrational: The Hidden Forces That Shape Our Decisions*, Revised ed. (Harper Perennial, 2010; original 2008).**
  - The behavioral-economics canon. Every CRO test in this course can be designed better by passing it through the Ariely lens, anchoring, decoy pricing, the cost of zero, social proof. Engage during Module 7.
- **Saleh, Khalid & Shukairy, Ayat, *Conversion Optimization: The Art and Science of Converting Prospects into Customers* (O'Reilly, 2010).**
  - The CRO textbook. Statistical-significance discipline, hypothesis-driven testing, the LIFT framework. Engage during Module 7.
- **Cialdini, Robert B., *Pre-Suasion: A Revolutionary Way to Influence and Persuade* (Simon & Schuster, 2016).**
  - The follow-up to *Influence*; the "what to do *before* you ask" framework. Particularly relevant to landing-page above-the-fold copy. Engage during Modules 4, 7.

### Performance Marketing Tactical
- **Goodson, Scott, *Uprising: How to Build a Brand—and Change the World—by Sparking Cultural Movements* (McGraw-Hill, 2012).**
  - Connects performance marketing to the cultural-relevance question. Engage during Module 5.
- **Ramotar, Vinny, *The Performance Marketing Playbook* (self-published, 2024).**
  - 2024-vintage; practitioner-grade walk-through of Google Ads + Meta Ads + TikTok in the post-iOS-14.5 + AI-creative era. Engage during Modules 2, 3, 4.

## Seminal Papers and HBR Articles (5-10 indispensable)

### Growth and Strategy
- **Ellis, Sean, "The Startup Pyramid" (StartupMarketing.com, 2010).**
  - The post-money-fit-test for a startup, including the 40% rule. Free read. Engage during Module 1.
- **Eisenmann, Tom "Why Startups Fail" *HBR*, May-June 2021.**
  - HBS professor's distillation of 100+ failure case studies. Many of the failure patterns are paid-media failure patterns. Engage during Module 1.
- **Anderson, Eric T. & Simester, Duncan "Mind Your Pricing Cues" *HBR*, September 2003.**
  - The behavioral-economics paper that informs every PDP price-display test you'll ever run. Engage during Module 7.
- **Kohavi, Ron, Tang, Diane & Xu, Ya, "Trustworthy Online Controlled Experiments: A Practical Guide to A/B Testing" (Cambridge University Press, 2020).**
  - The Microsoft-grade textbook on running A/B tests at scale. Sample-size math, sample-ratio mismatch, novelty effects, twyman's law. Read this before you run any test in Module 7. Engage during Module 7 and the Capstone.
- **Anderson, Chris "The Long Tail" *Wired*, October 2004, expanded into the book *The Long Tail* (Hyperion, 2006).**
  - The framing that informs every keyword-strategy decision (head vs body vs tail) and every audience-targeting decision. Engage during Module 2.

### Attribution and Measurement
- **Shapley, Lloyd S. "A Value for n-Person Games" *Contributions to the Theory of Games*, 1953 (volume II).**
  - The mathematical paper behind Shapley-value attribution. Engage during the Capstone's attribution audit; depth-skip if not mathematically inclined.
- **McKinsey & Company, "The trust deficit in measurement" + the annual *Marketing Mix Modeling* publications (mckinsey.com).**
  - Free. The McKinsey perspective on attribution credibility post-iOS 14.5. Engage during the Capstone.

### Industry / Practitioner Whitepapers
- **Meta, *Marketing Science Resource Library* (facebook.com/business/m/marketing-science).**
  - Free. The Conversions API whitepaper, the Aggregated Event Measurement whitepaper, the multiple ASC and Advantage+ Shopping case studies. Engage during Module 4.
- **Google, *Advertising Solutions Center* (support.google.com/google-ads).**
  - Free. The Performance Max best-practices guide and the AI Essentials playbook are the must-reads. Engage during Modules 2, 3.
- **Common Thread Collective, *The ECD (Ecommerce DTC) Quarterly Report* (commonthreadco.com).**
  - Free with email signup. The most data-rich DTC-specific paid-media benchmarks published. Engage during Modules 2, 4.

## Recommended Reading Sequence

If you have time for only 5 books, read in this order:

1. **Kohavi, Tang & Xu, *Trustworthy Online Controlled Experiments*** (the A/B testing bible)
2. **Ellis & Brown, *Hacking Growth*** (the high-tempo experimentation system)
3. **Saleh & Shukairy, *Conversion Optimization*** (the CRO foundation)
4. **Croll & Yoskovitz, *Lean Analytics*** (which numbers to look at)
5. **Ariely, *Predictably Irrational*** (the behavioral lens)

If you have time for only 3 practitioner reports, read:

1. **Common Thread Collective, *The ECD Quarterly Report*** (latest issue)
2. **Meta, *State of Performance Marketing*** (latest report)
3. **Google, Performance Max Best Practices guide** (latest, from Skillshop)

## A Note on AI in Performance Marketing (2024-2026 specifics)

The biggest shifts you should be tracking:

- **Performance Max + Demand Gen** (Google), fully AI-driven campaign types now ~60-70% of Google Ads spend at large advertisers.
- **Advantage+ Shopping + Advantage+ Sales** (Meta), same trajectory at Meta.
- **AI-generated creative**, Pencil (now Brandtech), AdCreative.ai, Meta Advantage+ Creative, Captions Mira, Synthesia 2.0.
- **AI-driven LTV bidding**, Google's value rules + Meta's value optimization + Klaviyo predictive CLV piped to ad-platform signals.
- **Server-side tagging + Consent Mode v2**, table-stakes for iOS-heavy programs.
- **Privacy Sandbox / Topics API**, the post-cookie advertising web is being built right now; track via the IAB Tech Lab.

Tool pricing and feature names verified as of 2026-05. **Always verify current pricing on the vendor's site before committing budget.**

## Maintaining Your Skills

Performance marketing certifications expire faster than any other field. Plan to:

- Retake Google Ads certifications every year (they expire annually).
- Retake Meta MBP every 1-2 years as it updates.
- Spend 4 hours/week consuming new platform updates from at least 2 of the industry resources above.
- Run 1 personal test campaign per quarter on a channel you don't usually use, TikTok if you're Meta-native, Pinterest if you're TikTok-native, etc. The skill atrophies fast otherwise.

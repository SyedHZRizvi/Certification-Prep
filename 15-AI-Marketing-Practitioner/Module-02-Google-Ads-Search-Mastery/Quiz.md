## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. Campaign**
Budget lives at the campaign level. Bidding strategy, geo, schedule, and networks also live at the campaign level. Ad groups contain keywords + ads + (optional) audiences.

### Q2: **C. Retired in 2021, folded into phrase match**
Google retired BMM in 2021 and merged its functionality into phrase match. Any answer choice referencing BMM in 2026 is a trap.

### Q3: **C. 30**
The 30-conversions-in-30-days rule is constantly tested. Below 30, Smart Bidding (tCPA, tROAS) doesn't have enough data and will spend wildly. Use Maximize Conversions until you cross the threshold.

### Q4: **B. No, Smart Bidding needs ≥30 conversions/month; stay on Maximize Conversions**
Premature tCPA = starved algorithm = wasted spend. The right move is to keep Max Conversions until conversion volume crosses 30/month, then test tCPA.

### Q5: **C. 15 headlines and 4 descriptions**
RSAs allow up to 15 headlines (30 chars each) and 4 descriptions (90 chars each). Minimum is 3 headlines, 2 descriptions.

### Q6: **B. Fill all slots with varied themes and 3+ keyword inclusions**
Excellent Ad Strength requires variation, completeness, and keyword presence. Pinning all to position 1 or using identical phrasing both hurt Ad Strength.

### Q7: **C. Bid × Quality Score + expected impact of extensions**
Ad Rank = Max CPC × Quality Score + extension impact. A common simplification on the exam, but the extension component matters, sitelinks and callouts measurably boost Ad Rank.

### Q8: **C. The QS-10 ad higher**
QS-10 × $2 = 20. QS-5 × $4 = 20. Tied on simple math, but with extension impact a QS-10 ad almost always edges out. The conceptual point: higher QS lets you pay less and still win.

### Q9: **C. Expected CTR, Ad Relevance, Landing Page Experience**
The 3 QS components. Each rated Below Average / Average / Above Average. To improve QS: better ad copy (CTR), tighter keyword-to-ad alignment (Relevance), faster/better landing page (LPE).

### Q10: **B. Pull the Search Terms report and add negative keywords**
High CTR + low conversion = clicks coming from irrelevant queries triggering your broad/phrase match. Fix is negative keywords, not bid changes.

### Q11: **B. Search Terms are what searchers actually typed; Keywords are what YOU bid on**
This distinction is constantly tested. Bidding on "running shoes" (phrase match) might trigger for the search term "where can I buy running shoes near me." That search term is what shows in the Search Terms report.

### Q12: **C. "free" (unless your offer is free)**
"Free" is the #1 universal negative, it pulls bargain-hunters and freebie-seekers who won't convert. Other near-universal negatives: torrent, download, jobs, salary, wikipedia, reddit, review (if you're not the review site).

### Q13: **C. 10-mile radius around studio + nearby cities**
Local businesses need tight geo. United States or even Arizona burns the budget on people too far to drive in. Use the actual driving radius + adjacent cities you genuinely serve.

### Q14: **B. Both unchecked for a pure Search campaign**
Both boxes are opt-in by Google default. For a pure Search campaign, uncheck both. Display Network on a Search campaign wastes ~30% of budget on low-intent traffic.

### Q15: **C. Search, Display, YouTube, Discover, Gmail, Maps simultaneously**
PMax is all-channels-in-one. Module 3 covers it in depth.

### Q16: **C. Largely obsolete in the Smart Bidding era**
SKAGs were optimal when manual CPC was standard. Smart Bidding does the SKAG-level optimization in the auction layer, so the structural overhead of SKAGs no longer pays off.

### Q17: **C. Working conversion tracking but < 30 conversions/month**
Max Conversions is the bridge strategy: you have tracking, you want conversions, but you don't yet have 30+/month for tCPA.

### Q18: **C. 50+ conversions/month with dynamic conversion VALUES**
tROAS specifically needs revenue values being passed (not just conversion counts). Typically 50+ value-attributed conversions per month for stability.

### Q19: **B. A free desktop app for offline bulk-editing**
Google Ads Editor is free, runs on Windows/Mac, syncs both ways with the live account, and is the only practical way to bulk-edit large accounts.

### Q20: **B. Hints to Smart Bidding about who your customer is**
Audience signals on PMax and Search-with-audiences are HINTS, not restrictions. They help the algorithm find similar users; they don't limit targeting like an old-style audience targeting would.

### Q21: **B. 5**
Allbirds ran 5 campaigns (Brand, Generic, Sustainable, Running, Re-targeting). The lesson: tight accounts outperform sprawling ones.

### Q22: **B. Brand keywords have very different CPCs and conversion rates; mixing distorts metrics**
Brand traffic converts at 15–40% and costs $0.20/click. Generic converts at 2–5% and costs $2–10/click. Mixing them in one campaign makes your "average CPA" meaningless and confuses Smart Bidding.

### Q23: **B. 10–25 miles depending on driving patterns**
For most local businesses, 10–25 miles around the location matches actual customer driving patterns. Adjust based on geography (15 miles in dense Manhattan ≠ 15 miles in rural Wyoming).

### Q24: **B. Leaving "Display Network" opt-in checked**
Google's default at campaign creation includes Display Network. Forgetting to uncheck this means ~30% of your "Search" budget burns on display impressions with much lower intent.

### Q25: **C. Broad match with an audience signal**
The 2026 best practice with mature Smart Bidding: feed the algorithm broad input + audience hints, let it find the converters. Pure exact match underperforms because it limits the algorithm's exploration.

### Q26: **C. Weekly (at minimum)**
The weekly 15-minute negative keyword routine is non-negotiable. Daily is better for high-spend accounts; weekly is the bare minimum.

### Q27: **A. Apply the same set of negatives across multiple campaigns at once**
Shared lists are powerful for account-wide exclusions (jobs, free, competitor names). Update once, applies everywhere it's attached.

### Q28: **B. To give Smart Bidding more variation to test and pick the best combinations per search**
RSAs work by Google testing combinations in real time. More variation = more tests = better matching to each searcher. Filling slots doesn't directly boost QS, it boosts Ad Strength, which correlates with performance.

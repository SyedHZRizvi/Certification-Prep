## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. Via Meta Business Manager / Business Settings**
Personal-profile ad accounts can be lost when the person leaves; Business Manager assets are owned by the business. Always set up via Business Manager.

### Q2: **B. ~75% of iOS users opt out, blocking the Pixel**
Apple's App Tracking Transparency (April 2021) made cross-app tracking opt-in. The vast majority of iOS users opt out, blocking the browser Pixel.

### Q3: **B. CAPI with event deduplication**
Server-side CAPI bypasses ATT entirely. Combined with Pixel + deduplication, it recovers ~70%+ of lost signal.

### Q4: **B. 7-day click + 1-day view**
Pre-iOS 14, default was 28-day click + 1-day view. Now 7-day click + 1-day view is default. This shortens what counts as an attributable conversion.

### Q5: **A. Rank top 8 conversion events per verified domain**
AEM requires you to pick and rank your top 8 conversion events. Only the priority event is reported for users who opt out of tracking.

### Q6: **D. No expiry (until you refresh it)**
Customer list custom audiences don't expire on their own — they shrink as Meta loses match accuracy over time, but there's no "expiration date." Refresh quarterly.

### Q7: **B. 1,000+ (ideally 5,000+)**
Meta needs a substantial seed to find patterns. 1,000 is minimum; 5,000+ is reliable.

### Q8: **B. The 1% of users in the country MOST similar to your seed**
Lookalikes are about similarity to your seed. 1% = top 1% most similar (smallest, highest-quality audience). 10% = larger but less similar.

### Q9: **B. A HINT to Meta's algorithm; can serve outside the suggestion**
Like PMax's audience signals on Google, Advantage+ Audience is directional, not restrictive. Meta WILL serve outside your suggestion if it finds better converters.

### Q10: **C. 50+ daily purchases and a product catalog**
ASC needs scale to work. Below ~50 daily conversions, the algorithm doesn't have enough signal. Also requires a catalog.

### Q11: **B. Limit spend on existing customers (forcing prospecting on new ones)**
Default is 100% blended. Setting 30% existing-customer cap forces Meta to spend 70% finding new customers — a key prospecting mechanism.

### Q12: **B. Hook × Body × CTA**
Classic DCT axes: hook (opening), body (main content), CTA (call to action). Meta auto-combines and reports back winners.

### Q13: **B. A mistake — need ~1,000 impressions minimum**
Killing creative at 100 impressions is noise. You need ~1,000+ impressions per variant before performance signal is meaningful.

### Q14: **C. 9:16 vertical**
Reels are vertical 9:16, 1080×1920 minimum. Different from horizontal Feed creative.

### Q15: **B. Fails — Reels has different visual grammar**
Cropped Feed creative looks awkward and performs poorly. Reels needs native vertical content with fast pacing.

### Q16: **B. Over after ~50 conversions in 7 days**
Meta's algorithm stabilizes after ~50 conversions. Below that, performance is volatile. Don't make budget/creative changes during learning phase.

### Q17: **B. Resets the learning phase**
Significant edits (budget change >20%, creative swap, audience change) reset learning. Hands off during the first 50 conversions.

### Q18: **B. ABO for testing; CBO for scaling**
Ad-set Budget Optimization lets you test audiences with controlled spend. Campaign Budget Optimization lets Meta allocate to winners — best after you know who works.

### Q19: **B. Cart abandoners + Add-to-cart, last 14–30 days**
Standard retargeting: people who showed buying intent recently. Broader audiences are prospecting, not retargeting.

### Q20: **B. $5–$15**
Reels CPMs are lower than Feed CPMs in most categories. $5–$15 is a typical 2026 DTC apparel range. Above $20 suggests audience too narrow or creative not hooking.

### Q21: **A. Reels viewers respond to faces**
Reels favors human faces in the first frame. Founder selfies, creator UGC, and talking-head formats consistently outperform brand-shop look.

### Q22: **B. UGC + Reels-first + DCT cycles + CAPI on day zero**
True Classic's playbook is publicly documented: server-side CAPI, biweekly DCT cycles, 75% budget on Reels, founder/UGC creative.

### Q23: **C. $150**
Meta Certified Media Buying Professional is $150 USD (2026 pricing), 75 questions, 105 minutes.

### Q24: **B. Same conversion being counted twice**
When Pixel + CAPI both fire for the same event, deduplication matches them via an event ID and counts only once.

### Q25: **B. 28%**
Glossier publicly shared at Shopify Editions 2024 that disciplined DCT (16 variants, biweekly cycles, ROAS 2x baseline win criteria) drove a 28% Q3-over-Q3 CAC reduction.

### Q26: **C. Advantage+ Placements**
Letting Meta auto-distribute across Reels + Feed + Stories + Audience Network produces lowest CPM and best performance in nearly all cases. Single-placement is now an anti-pattern.

### Q27: **C. 4–5x (varies by industry)**
Frequency above 4–5x typically means diminishing returns and ad fatigue. Refresh creative when you hit it.

### Q28: **B. Set up Pixel + CAPI + Domain Verification + AEM ranked events**
Without this defensive stack, your tracking is broken from minute one. Everything else builds on this foundation.

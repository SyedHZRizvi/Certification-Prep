## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. Trigger → Condition → Action**
Every automation = something happens (trigger) → optionally check a rule (condition) → take an action. This is the universal model across all platforms.

### Q2: **B. Klaviyo**
Klaviyo's Shopify integration is best-in-class. Built specifically for ecom flows (abandoned cart, post-purchase, replenishment). The DTC default.

### Q3: **B. HubSpot**
HubSpot's CRM + Marketing Hub combo is purpose-built for B2B SaaS + content marketing. MQL→SQL handoff is native.

### Q4: **C. 38%**
Drift publicly shared at SaaStr 2022 that their chatbot+automation reduced average sales cycle from 67 days to ~42 days, a 38% reduction.

### Q5: **B. Workflows**
HubSpot calls it Workflows (available on Marketing Hub Professional+).

### Q6: **B. Flows**
Klaviyo calls it Flows. Each one is triggered by a specific ecom event.

### Q7: **B. Anyone hitting high-intent pages or submitting a form**
Hot Lead = high-intent behavior. Pricing page views, demo requests, contact form submissions. Not "anyone who clicked an ad."

### Q8: **B. 3-10x higher conversion rates**
Harvard Business Review's lead-response research (2011, updated 2024) shows leads contacted within 5 minutes convert at 3-10x higher rates than those contacted in hours.

### Q9: **C. 7 (Welcome, Cart, Browse, Post-Purchase, Win-Back, Birthday, Replenishment)**
The 7 must-have Klaviyo Flows from the reading. Skipping any of these leaves money on the table.

### Q10: **B. 6-12%**
Klaviyo's 2024 benchmark report. A well-built abandoned cart flow recovers 6-12% of abandoned carts, meaningful revenue on top of your ad spend.

### Q11: **B. 1 hour after cart abandonment**
Sweet spot. Immediate feels stalker-y. 24 hours is too late. ~1 hour after = "I just left the store, oh right, that thing."

### Q12: **B. A visual automation platform for chaining tools**
Make.com is the "glue layer" that connects tools together. Not a CRM, not an email platform, a visual flow builder for cross-tool automation.

### Q13: **C. n8n self-hosted**
n8n is open source and free; you pay only for hosting (a $5/mo VPS suffices for most). Make.com is mid-range. Zapier gets expensive fast at 10K+ ops/mo.

### Q14: **B. An LLM's ability to call functions/APIs to take actions**
"Tool use" is the technical term. The LLM is given a set of tools (e.g., "send_email", "get_shopify_order") and can decide when to call which.

### Q15: **B. Customer service triage**
Real 2026 use case: LLM reads an incoming support email, calls the Shopify API to look up the order, drafts a personalized response. Human reviews + sends. Massively reduces human effort on common questions.

### Q16: **B. A URL that receives an HTTP POST when an event happens**
Webhook = "send me a notification when X happens." The receiving URL gets JSON describing what occurred. Foundation of cross-tool integration.

### Q17: **B. Email authentication standards**
SPF (which servers can send for your domain), DKIM (signature proving non-tampering), DMARC (policy if SPF/DKIM fail). All required for good deliverability.

### Q18: **B. SPF + DKIM + DMARC configured, easy unsubscribe, spam complaint rate < 0.3%**
Gmail and Yahoo's 2024 bulk-sender rules (for senders > 5,000/day to their users). Non-compliance = blocked emails.

### Q19: **B. A rule removing a contact from the workflow**
"Exit if purchased" prevents you from emailing customers an "Abandon Cart" message after they actually bought. Critical for avoiding awkward automations.

### Q20: **A. Hot Lead, MQL→SQL Handoff, Customer Onboarding, Re-engagement**
The 4 core workflows from the reading. Skipping any = leaks in your funnel.

### Q21: **B. LLM + tools + permission boundaries + human review for sensitive actions**
The right model: LLM decides what to do, calls tools to execute, but humans review anything sensitive (sending external messages, refunds, contracts).

### Q22: **B. Spent > $500 lifetime AND last purchase < 60 days ago**
Standard VIP segmentation: high lifetime value + recent activity. Excludes lapsed customers (who need a different "Win-Back" treatment).

### Q23: **B. Build 1, monitor for 2 weeks, then build next**
Building 50 workflows at once = 30 of them are wrong, and you can't tell which. Build incrementally, monitor each, then expand.

### Q24: **B. Assistants store conversation state on OpenAI's servers; Claude requires you to manage state**
The key architectural difference. Both support tool use. Claude requires you to manage conversation context client-side (more control, more code). OpenAI Assistants handle that server-side (less control, less code).

### Q25: **B. Klaviyo + Make.com + Meta/Google Customer Audiences + GA4**
Modern DTC stack: Klaviyo (emails + flows) + Make.com (cross-tool sync) + ad-platform customer audiences (suppress purchasers, retarget non-purchasers) + GA4 (measurement).

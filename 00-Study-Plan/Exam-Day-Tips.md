# 🎯 Exam Day Tips (Both Exams)

> **Read this the morning of your exam. Then re-read it. Then close it and trust yourself.**

---

## 🌅 The Day Before

- ✅ **Stop studying by noon.** Cramming literally lowers performance.
- ✅ Light review of your one-page cheat sheet — that's it.
- ✅ Lay out: ID, confirmation email, water bottle, snacks.
- ✅ If online: Run the system check on the testing platform. Twice.
- ✅ Sleep 8 hours. Set 2 alarms.
- ❌ No alcohol. No new foods. No drama.

---

## 🌄 The Morning Of

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| Wake up | Hydrate immediately (a glass of water) |
| +30 min | Light breakfast (protein + complex carbs; avoid sugar crash) |
| +60 min | 10-min walk (boosts cognition by ~12%, research-backed) |
| Pre-exam | One look at cheat sheet → close it, put it away |
| Arrival | 30 minutes early. Bathroom. Deep breaths. |

---

## 🧘 The First 5 Minutes of the Exam

1. **Don't rush.** The exam isn't going anywhere.
2. **Read the question fully.** Twice. The PMP especially loves to bury the actual ask in paragraph 3.
3. **Identify the question type:**
   - "What is the BEST...?" → All options work, pick the *most* effective
   - "What is the FIRST...?" → Process order matters
   - "What should the PM do NEXT?" → Often "communicate" or "go talk to the team"
4. **Eliminate clearly wrong answers first.** 4 options → 2 → 1 is easier than 4 → 1.

## 🎯 PSM I Specific Tips

| Tip | Why |
|-----|-----|
| **80 questions, 60 minutes** | That's 45 seconds per question. Move. |
| **Use the Scrum Guide as truth** | If an answer contradicts the Scrum Guide, it's wrong — even if it sounds reasonable. |
| **Beware of "best practices" trap** | The Scrum Guide says "the team self-organizes" — there is no prescribed best practice for HOW. |
| **Pass mark: 85%** | You can miss 12 questions. That's tighter than it sounds. |
| **You can flag and review** | Use it for the 5–10 questions you're truly unsure about. |
| **Open book? Sort of.** | Keep the Scrum Guide PDF open in another tab. But don't waste time looking up basics — you should know them. |

### PSM I Trap Words to Watch For:
- ❌ "Project Manager" → Scrum has no PM. Trick answer.
- ❌ "Tech lead", "Scrum Master assigns work" → Scrum Master never assigns.
- ❌ "Sprint extended" → Sprints are NEVER extended. Ever.
- ❌ "Product Owner cancels Sprint" → Only PO can cancel a Sprint, but it's rare.
- ✅ "Self-managing", "empirical", "transparency, inspection, adaptation" → These are right answers in disguise.

## 🎯 PMP Specific Tips

| Tip | Why |
|-----|-----|
| **180 questions, 230 minutes** | That's ~77 seconds per question. Includes 2 breaks. |
| **Two 10-minute breaks** | Use them! Stretch, hydrate, reset. Don't skip them. |
| **Default to Agile/Servant Leadership thinking** | When in doubt, the PMP-2021 expects you to think like an agile servant leader. |
| **"Talk to the team" is often right** | If "communicate", "facilitate", "coach the team" is an option, it's frequently correct. |
| **Avoid "escalate to sponsor" reflexes** | Only escalate when YOU genuinely cannot resolve it. Try team-level fixes first. |
| **Read the LAST line first sometimes** | The actual question is in the last sentence. The rest is fluff. |

### PMP Mindset Cheat Sheet:
- 🤝 **Collaboration > Authority**
- 💬 **Communication is always part of the answer**
- 👥 **The team should be empowered, not managed**
- 📊 **Data-driven decisions over gut feel**
- 🔄 **Iterate and adapt, don't lock in plans rigidly**
- 🛡️ **Protect the team from interruptions**
- 🎯 **Customer/stakeholder value first**

### PMP Trap Words:
- ❌ "Tell the team to..." → Coaching > telling. Trick answer.
- ❌ "Submit a change request first" → Only after you've done the analysis.
- ❌ "Assign work to..." → In agile contexts, the team self-assigns.
- ❌ "The PM decides..." → Often the team or stakeholder decides.
- ✅ "Discuss with the team", "facilitate a conversation", "perform root cause analysis" → Usually winners.

## ⏱️ Time Management Strategy

### PSM I (60 min, 80 q):
- **First pass (40 min):** Answer everything you know. Flag the rest.
- **Second pass (15 min):** Tackle flagged questions.
- **Final 5 min:** Review flagged ones. Trust your first instinct unless you have *evidence* to change.

### PMP (230 min, 180 q):
- **Phase 1 (60 q in 70 min):** Don't agonize. Flag if >2 min.
- **BREAK (10 min):** Walk. Stretch. Don't replay questions in your head.
- **Phase 2 (60 q in 70 min):** Same pace.
- **BREAK (10 min):** Same routine.
- **Phase 3 (60 q in 70 min):** Push through.
- **Final (10 min):** Review flagged questions.

## 🧠 Mental Tactics

### When You Hit a Question You Don't Know:
1. **Don't panic.** Maybe 1 in 10 questions is genuinely hard.
2. **Eliminate** what you know is wrong.
3. **Default to the principle:** What would a servant leader do? What does the Scrum Guide say?
4. **Pick** and move on. Mark for review.
5. **Forget it.** Don't carry baggage to the next question.

### When You Hit a Stretch of Hard Questions:
- It's likely your **brain is fatigued**, not that you don't know the material.
- Take a 30-second mental break (eyes closed, deep breaths).
- Remember: PMP exam is *adaptive-ish*. Hard questions can mean you're doing well.

### When You Want to Change an Answer:
- Research shows: **changing usually hurts**.
- Only change if you've genuinely realized something new (e.g., spotted a key word you missed).
- **Don't** change because of self-doubt. Trust your prep.

- 💡 Failing once is data, not destiny.

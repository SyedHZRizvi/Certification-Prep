# 📅 The 12-Week Study Plan (Both Exams)

> **Reality check:** This plan assumes ~10 hours/week. Less time? Stretch it to 16 weeks. More time? Compress to 8.

---

## 🧠 The Science Behind This Plan

Three principles drive the schedule:

1. **Spaced repetition** — You'll revisit concepts at increasing intervals (Day 1, Day 3, Day 7, Day 14, Day 30).
2. **Interleaving** — Mixing topics beats blocking. Your brain learns to *distinguish*, not just recognize.
3. **Active recall** — You'll be quizzed to *death*. Reading without testing is studying theater.

## 🎯 Pre-Exam Day Checklist

- [ ] Print/review your one-page cheat sheet
- [ ] Eat well, sleep 8 hours, hydrate
- [ ] Lay out exam ID + confirmation email
- [ ] If online: clear your testing room (no papers, no second monitor)
- [ ] If in-person: drive the route the day before
- [ ] Avoid cramming the night before (it actually *lowers* scores — research-backed)

---

## ⚠️ Honest Warnings

- **You will hit a wall around Week 3 of Scrum or Week 7 of PMP.** It's normal. Push through.
- **You will fail a practice exam at some point.** That's the *point* of practice.
- **You will doubt yourself.** Everyone does. The exam is passable. People with less time and less knowledge than you have passed it. So will you.

---

## 🔁 If You Fail

It happens. ~30% of PMP test-takers fail their first attempt.

**Don't panic.** PMI gives you 3 attempts within a year. Identify your weak domains from the report, drill those for 3–4 weeks, retake.

**Scrum.org PSM I:** You can retake immediately for $150. Most second-attempt scores are dramatically higher because you've seen the question style.

You got this. 💪

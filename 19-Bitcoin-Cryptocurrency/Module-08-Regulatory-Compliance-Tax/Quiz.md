## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. March 18, 2013**
FIN-2013-G001 is the foundational US crypto regulatory document. Predates every subsequent rule.

### Q2: **B. An exchanger who converts BTC to USD for customers**
"Exchangers" are explicitly MSBs under FIN-2013-G001. Users (A), self-account miners (C), and software developers shipping non-custodial code (D) are not.

### Q3: **B. Bank Secrecy Act of 1970**
The BSA is the parent statute. Title 31 CFR implementing rules + FinCEN guidance build on it.

### Q4: **C. Are NOT MSBs and their software developers are not money transmitters**
FIN-2019-G001 carved out unhosted wallets and the software-developer-only role explicitly.

### Q5: **B. Requires originator and beneficiary information to travel with transfers between VASPs above a threshold**
Recommendation 16 was originally a wire-transfer rule (since the 1990s); the June 2019 update extended it to VASPs.

### Q6: **D. Zero — every VASP-to-VASP transfer requires originator/beneficiary info**
The EU TFR (2023/1113) effective December 30, 2024 imposes zero threshold for VASP-to-VASP. Self-hosted withdrawals >€1,000 get extra scrutiny.

### Q7: **B. Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (EU 2023/1114)**
The official long name. CASP authorization regime applies in full from December 30, 2024.

### Q8: **B. Passports across all 27 EU member states**
The MiCA single-market design: license once, operate across the union.

### Q9: **B. Property**
Notice 2014-21. NOT currency. Trade implications: capital gains treatment + every disposition is a taxable event.

### Q10: **B. Was eliminated; §1031 now applies to real property only**
TCJA December 22, 2017, effective January 1, 2018.

### Q11: **C. Ordinary income at fair market value when received**
Rev. Rul. 2019-24, October 9, 2019. The basis = FMV at receipt; subsequent disposition triggers capital gain/loss.

### Q12: **C. No — Bitcoin is classified as property, not a security; proposed extension has not been enacted as of 2026**
A meaningful exam-trap distinction. Multiple bills (2021, 2022, 2023) have proposed extending wash-sale to crypto; none passed.

### Q13: **B. Itemize capital-asset dispositions, including crypto sales**
Form 8949 + Schedule D = the reporting backbone. Form 1099-DA (effective 2025) pre-populates much of this.

### Q14: **C. $4.3 billion**
Largest US corporate financial-crime penalty at the time. The DOJ portion was $1.81B; FinCEN $3.4B (partially credited); OFAC $968M; CFTC $1.35B (partially credited).

### Q15: **B. BSA violations, unlicensed money transmission, and OFAC sanctions violations**
Three categories of plea. Binance Holdings Ltd was the corporate defendant; CZ personally pled to one BSA count.

### Q16: **B. 4 months prison + $50M personal fine**
CZ sentenced April 30, 2024; served at FCI Lompoc; released August 2024.

### Q17: **B. Sanctioned the smart-contract addresses themselves, an unprecedented step that was later narrowed by the 5th Circuit in Van Loon (Nov 2024) and de-listed by Treasury on March 21, 2025**
The case is the leading authority on the limits of sanctioning immutable code as a "person" under IEEPA.

### Q18: **C. Register before commencing exchanger activity; failure to register is itself a federal crime under 18 USC §1960 (unlicensed money transmission)**
The BitMEX, Bittrex, Binance precedents all turn on §1960 violations.

### Q19: **B. An MSB because it holds customer keys / value on behalf of customers**
FIN-2019-G001's "hosted wallet" analysis. The Layer-2 protocol is irrelevant — the custody relationship is what matters.

### Q20: **B. KoFIU registration AND Information Security Management System (ISMS) certification from KISA**
South Korea's two-track requirement, plus real-name banking partnership. The strictest national VASP regime.

### Q21: **B. Capital gain/loss on the 0.001 BTC measured as ($75 - $20 cost basis) = $55 short-term capital gain (if held <1 year)**
The "buy coffee with BTC" disposition. Every spend is a sale at FMV.

### Q22: **B. Wallet-by-wallet (each wallet's lots tracked separately)**
Rev. Proc. 2024-28 + TR 9999. Pre-2025 universal-basis methods are grandfathered for prior tax years only.

### Q23: **B. May qualify as an MSB depending on facts and circumstances**
FIN-2019-G001 explicitly takes a facts-and-circumstances approach for mining pools. Operators should consult counsel.

### Q24: **B. Compare expected settlement value (probability × penalty × forced shutdown × founder liability) against compliance overhead**
The Binance precedent — $4.3B + CEO prison + ~25 pts market-share loss — sets the upper bound. Monotonic enforcement trend over 2017-2024 makes arbitrage increasingly negative-EV.

### Q25: **C. The EU TFR at zero threshold for VASP-to-VASP transfers**
This is one of the most-tested traps. The EU is **stricter** than the FATF baseline, not equal to it.

### Q26: **A. The five-pillar compliance launch plan**
MSB + state MTLs + AML program + OFAC + Travel Rule + 1099-DA. Missing any of the five is launch-blocking for a US payments startup.

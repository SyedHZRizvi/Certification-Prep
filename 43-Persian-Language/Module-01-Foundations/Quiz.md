# Module 1 Quiz — فارسی کی بنیادیں (Foundations)

**26 Multiple Choice Questions | Alphabet, Numbers, Greetings, Cognates**

---

**1.** How many letters are in the Persian alphabet?

- A) 28
- B) 30
- C) 32
- D) 36

**Answer: C** — Persian has 32 letters: the 28 Arabic letters plus پ، چ، ژ، گ.

---

**2.** Which of the following letters is found in Persian but NOT in Arabic?

- A) ع
- B) ق
- C) پ
- D) ص

**Answer: C** — پ (pe) is one of four letters unique to Persian (and Urdu), absent from Arabic.

---

**3.** Persian is written in which direction?

- A) Left to right
- B) Right to left
- C) Top to bottom
- D) Right to left for nouns, left to right for verbs

**Answer: B** — Like Urdu and Arabic, Persian is written right to left.

---

**4.** What does the Persian word **سلام** (*salām*) mean?

- A) Goodbye
- B) Thank you
- C) Hello
- D) Welcome

**Answer: C** — سلام is the universal Persian greeting, equivalent to "Hello."

---

**5.** How do you say "Thank you" in Persian?

- A) خداحافظ
- B) ببخشید
- C) بله
- D) ممنونم

**Answer: D** — ممنونم (*mamnunam*) means "thank you."

---

**6.** What does **خداحافظ** (*khodāhāfez*) literally mean?

- A) God bless you
- B) May God protect you (Goodbye)
- C) Welcome
- D) Good morning

**Answer: B** — خدا = God, حافظ = protector/guardian. It is the standard farewell.

---

**7.** What is the Persian word for the number 5?

- A) شش
- B) هفت
- C) پنج
- D) ده

**Answer: C** — پنج (*panj*) = 5. Compare with Urdu پانچ.

---

**8.** Which Persian number corresponds to ۷?

- A) سه
- B) هفت
- C) نه
- D) هشت

**Answer: B** — ۷ = هفت (*haft*) = seven.

---

**9.** What does **بیست** (*bist*) mean?

- A) Twelve
- B) Fifteen
- C) Eighteen
- D) Twenty

**Answer: D** — بیست (*bist*) = 20.

---

**10.** The Persian word **زندگی** (*zindagi*) is also used in Urdu with the same meaning. What does it mean?

- A) Country
- B) Life
- C) Journey
- D) World

**Answer: B** — زندگی = life. It is a perfect cognate in both Persian and Urdu.

---

**11.** Which of the following is NOT one of the four letters unique to Persian?

- A) پ
- B) چ
- C) ث
- D) ژ

**Answer: C** — ث (*se*) exists in both Arabic and Persian. The four Persian-specific letters are پ، چ، ژ، گ.

---

**12.** How is the Persian letter **ژ** (*zhe*) pronounced?

- A) Like "z" in "zoo"
- B) Like "zh" in "measure" or "s" in "vision"
- C) Like "j" in "jump"
- D) Like "sh" in "shoe"

**Answer: B** — ژ makes the "zh" sound as in "measure" or "vision." Example: ژاپن (Japan).

---

**13.** The Persian word **دوست** (*dost*) means "friend." What is the Urdu word for friend?

- A) دوست
- B) یار
- C) رفیق
- D) All of the above

**Answer: D** — دوست is indeed used in Urdu too, along with یار and رفیق.

---

**14.** What does **خوش آمدید** (*khosh āmadid*) mean?

- A) Good morning
- B) How are you?
- C) Welcome
- D) Goodbye

**Answer: C** — خوش آمدید is the Persian welcome greeting.

---

**15.** Which sound does the Persian letter **غ** (*gheyn*) make?

- A) A hard "g" as in "go"
- B) A guttural "gh" sound made at the back of the throat
- C) A soft "k" sound
- D) Silent

**Answer: B** — غ (*gheyn*) is a guttural sound produced at the back of the throat, similar to a gargling "g."

---

**16.** How do you write the number "10" in Persian numerals?

- A) ١٠
- B) ۱۰
- C) 10
- D) Both B and C are correct in Persian contexts

**Answer: D** — Both Persian Eastern Arabic numerals (۱۰) and Western numerals (10) are understood in Iran.

---

**17.** The Persian word **کتاب** (*kitāb*) is borrowed from which language into Urdu?

- A) Turkish
- B) Persian
- C) Sanskrit
- D) Greek

**Answer: B** — کتاب entered Urdu from Persian (which itself borrowed it from Arabic).

---

**18.** What does **ببخشید** (*bebakhshid*) mean?

- A) Thank you
- B) You're welcome
- C) Excuse me / I'm sorry
- D) Please sit down

**Answer: C** — ببخشید is used for "excuse me" or "sorry."

---

**19.** Which Persian greeting is used specifically in the morning?

- A) شب بخیر
- B) عصر بخیر
- C) صبح بخیر
- D) درود

**Answer: C** — صبح بخیر (*sobh be-kheyr*) = Good morning. صبح = morning.

---

**20.** What does **نه** (*na*) mean in Persian?

- A) Yes
- B) No
- C) Not yet
- D) Nine

**Answer: B** — نه (*na*) means "no." (Note: نه (*noh*) also means nine — context distinguishes them.)

---

**21.** Which of the following best describes the historical reason why Urdu contains so much Persian vocabulary?

- A) Urdu was invented by Persian scholars
- B) Persian was the court and literary language of the Mughal Empire
- C) Urdu and Persian developed in the same geographic region simultaneously
- D) Iran and Pakistan share a border where languages merged

**Answer: B** — Persian served as the official language of the Mughal Empire, deeply influencing Urdu.

---

**22.** The Persian letter **خ** (*khe*) sounds like:

- A) A soft "h"
- B) A "k" as in "kite"
- C) A rasping "kh" sound like clearing your throat (Scottish "loch")
- D) A "ch" as in "cheese"

**Answer: C** — خ makes the "kh" sound, same as in Urdu خ and Spanish "j."

---

**23.** What is the Persian word for "flower"?

- A) باد
- B) گل
- C) آب
- D) کوه

**Answer: B** — گل (*gol*) = flower. Also used in Urdu گل.

---

**24.** How do you say "I'm fine, thank you" in Persian?

- A) بله، ممنون
- B) خوبم، ممنون
- C) سلام، خداحافظ
- D) ممنونم، خوش آمدید

**Answer: B** — خوبم، ممنون (*khubam, mamnun*) = "I'm fine, thank you."

---

**25.** Which Persian poet wrote the *Shahnameh* (شاهنامه), a 60,000-couplet national epic?

- A) Rumi
- B) Hafiz
- C) Ferdowsi
- D) Omar Khayyám

**Answer: C** — Ferdowsi (فردوسی) wrote the Shahnameh over 30 years.

---

**26.** Which of the following words did English borrow from Persian?

- A) Bazaar (بازار)
- B) Paradise (پردیس)
- C) Shawl (شال)
- D) All of the above

**Answer: D** — All three — bazaar, paradise, and shawl — entered English from Persian.

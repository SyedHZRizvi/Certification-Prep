# Module 4 Quiz — روزمرہ گفتگو (Everyday Conversations)

**26 Situational Multiple Choice Questions**

---

**1.** At the Tehran Bazaar, a seller quotes a price of 5 million tomans. You think it's too expensive. What do you say?

- A) نوش جان!
- B) خیلی گران است. می‌تونم کمتر بگیرم؟
- C) آخرین قیمت چیست؟
- D) قبوله!

**Answer: B** — خیلی گران است. می‌تونم کمتر بگیرم؟ = "It's very expensive. Can I get it for less?" — the natural bargaining opener.

---

**2.** A seller says **آخرین قیمت** (*ākharin qeymat*). What do they mean?

- A) First price / starting price
- B) The final / best price (no further reduction)
- C) The sale price
- D) Half price

**Answer: B** — آخرین قیمت = "final price" — the seller won't go lower.

---

**3.** At a restaurant, the waiter says **بفرمایید** while handing you the menu. This means:

- A) Are you ready to order?
- B) Here you go / Please (a polite offering gesture)
- C) The food is ready
- D) Would you like the bill?

**Answer: B** — بفرمایید is an extremely versatile polite phrase meaning "please do / here you are / go ahead."

---

**4.** After finishing a delicious Persian meal, you want to thank the host for cooking. What do you say?

- A) نوش جان
- B) دستت درد نکنه
- C) خداحافظ
- D) بفرمایید

**Answer: B** — دستت درد نکنه (*dastat dard nakone*) = "May your hand not hurt" — said to thank someone for cooking or manual work.

---

**5.** **نوش جان** (*nush-e jān*) is said:

- A) When leaving a gathering
- B) When welcoming guests
- C) When wishing someone a good meal (Bon appétit)
- D) When refusing food politely

**Answer: C** — نوش جان = "May it nourish your soul" = Bon appétit.

---

**6.** Your Iranian host offers you tea. You're full but want to be polite. What's the appropriate first response?

- A) بله لطفاً (Yes please)
- B) نه ممنون، سیرم (No thank you, I'm full)
- C) خواهش می‌کنم (You're welcome)
- D) بفرمایید

**Answer: B** — نه ممنون، سیرم is the polite taarof refusal. The host will likely insist — only then do you accept if you want.

---

**7.** **قدم به چشم** (*qadam be cheshm*) literally means "your steps are on my eyes." In practice, it means:

- A) Please watch your step
- B) You are most welcome here
- C) I can see you
- D) Come back later

**Answer: B** — It's one of the warmest Persian welcomes, used when a guest arrives.

---

**8.** When visiting an Iranian home, the host says **راحت باشید** (*rāhat bāshid*). What are they saying?

- A) Please leave soon
- B) Make yourself comfortable / Feel at home
- C) Are you hungry?
- D) Sit at the table

**Answer: B** — راحت باشید = "be comfortable / relax / feel at home."

---

**9.** At the airport, a border officer asks **هدف از سفرتون چیه؟** What are they asking?

- A) How long is your flight?
- B) Where did you fly from?
- C) What is the purpose of your trip?
- D) Do you have anything to declare?

**Answer: C** — هدف از سفر = purpose of travel. چیه = colloquial for چیست (what is).

---

**10.** How do you say "I'm a tourist" in Persian?

- A) من مسافرم
- B) من توریستم
- C) من دانشجوم
- D) من تاجرم

**Answer: B** — من توریستم (*man turistam*) = "I am a tourist." توریست is a loanword from French.

---

**11.** At the airport, you need to find the taxi stand. How do you ask "Where is the taxi agency?"

- A) آژانس تاکسی چند است؟
- B) آژانس تاکسی کجاست؟
- C) آژانس تاکسی هستند؟
- D) آژانس تاکسی چطور است؟

**Answer: B** — کجاست (*kojāst*) = "where is?" So آژانس تاکسی کجاست؟ = Where is the taxi agency?

---

**12.** When meeting someone for the first time, Persians often say **خوشبختم** (*khoshbakhtam*). This means:

- A) How are you?
- B) Nice to meet you (I am happy/fortunate)
- C) Welcome to Iran
- D) I've heard a lot about you

**Answer: B** — خوشبختم = "I am happy/fortunate (to meet you)" = Nice to meet you.

---

**13.** In a university introduction, someone says **از اصفهان اومدم**. What are they saying?

- A) I'm going to Isfahan
- B) I like Isfahan
- C) I came from Isfahan
- D) Isfahan is far

**Answer: C** — اومدم (*umadam*) = I came (colloquial past tense of آمدن).

---

**14.** **خوشبختم، چه رشته‌ای می‌خونی؟** What is being asked after the greeting?

- A) What's your name?
- B) Where are you from?
- C) What subject/field are you studying?
- D) What year are you in?

**Answer: C** — رشته = field of study; می‌خونی = you read/study (colloquial).

---

**15.** At the doctor's office, the patient says **سرم درد می‌کنه و تب دارم**. What are the symptoms?

- A) Stomach ache and nausea
- B) Headache and fever
- C) Sore throat and cough
- D) Back pain and dizziness

**Answer: B** — سرم درد می‌کنه = my head hurts; تب دارم = I have fever.

---

**16.** The doctor writes a prescription and says **این نسخه رو ببرید داروخانه**. Where should you go?

- A) The hospital
- B) The laboratory
- C) The pharmacy
- D) The clinic

**Answer: C** — داروخانه = pharmacy (lit. "medicine house"). دارو = medicine, خانه = house.

---

**17.** **استراحت کنید** (*esterāhat konid*) means:

- A) Eat well
- B) Drink plenty of water
- C) Rest
- D) Take your medicine

**Answer: C** — استراحت کنید = "rest / take rest." A common doctor's advice.

---

**18.** On the phone in Persian, instead of "Hello?" you typically say:

- A) سلام
- B) بله؟
- C) خوش اومدید
- D) ببخشید

**Answer: B** — بله (*bale*) = "yes?" is the standard phone greeting in Persian. Though سلام is also used, بله is more phone-specific.

---

**19.** Someone addresses you as **عزیزم** (*azizam*). This is:

- A) A formal title
- B) A polite request
- C) A term of endearment meaning "my dear"
- D) An apology

**Answer: C** — عزیزم = "my dear / my darling" — a very common Persian term of affection.

---

**20.** The suffix **جان** (*jān*) after a name (e.g., شهرام جان) is used to express:

- A) Formal respect
- B) Affection / endearment (dear)
- C) Surprise
- D) Anger

**Answer: B** — جان = "dear." Names + جان is very common: مامان جان (dear Mum), دوستِ جان (dear friend).

---

**21.** **مراقب خودتون باشید** (*morāqeb-e khodeton bāshid*) means:

- A) Be careful on the road
- B) Take care of yourself
- C) I'll see you soon
- D) Stay healthy and eat well

**Answer: B** — It's the Persian equivalent of "take care of yourself" — used at the end of phone calls and goodbyes.

---

**22.** What is **تعارف** (*ta'ārof*)?

- A) A type of Persian poetry
- B) The Persian system of elaborate social courtesy and politeness rituals
- C) A traditional Persian dish
- D) A formal letter of introduction

**Answer: B** — Taarof is Iran's complex social etiquette system involving ritualized politeness, offers, and refusals.

---

**23.** A seller asks **چقدر می‌خواید بدید؟** What do they want to know?

- A) How long do you want to stay?
- B) How many would you like?
- C) How much are you willing to pay?
- D) When do you want it?

**Answer: C** — چقدر = how much; می‌خواید بدید = you want to give. "How much do you want to pay?"

---

**24.** **باشه، قبوله** (*bāshe, qabule*) is said when:

- A) Refusing an offer
- B) Accepting a deal or agreeing to something
- C) Saying goodbye
- D) Expressing surprise

**Answer: B** — باشه = okay (colloquial); قبوله = it's accepted / agreed. Used to seal a bargain or accept a plan.

---

**25.** In a restaurant, **یه دوغ هم بیارید** means:

- A) Bring me one tea also
- B) Bring me one dough (yogurt drink) also
- C) One more menu please
- D) Check, please

**Answer: B** — دوغ (*dugh*) is the traditional Persian fermented yogurt drink. یه = one; بیارید = bring (plural/formal imperative).

---

**26.** **کار دارم باهاتون** (*kār dāram bāhātun*) in a phone call means:

- A) I'm busy right now
- B) I have something to discuss with you / I need to talk to you
- C) Can I call you back?
- D) I'm at work

**Answer: B** — کار دارم = I have business/matter; باهاتون = with you (plural/formal). "I have something to talk to you about."

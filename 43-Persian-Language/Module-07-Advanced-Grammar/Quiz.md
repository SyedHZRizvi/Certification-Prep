# Module 7 Quiz — دستور زبان پیشرفته (Advanced Grammar)

**25 Multiple Choice Questions | Present Perfect, Past Perfect, Conditionals, Subjunctive, Passive Voice, Modal Verbs**

---

**1.** The Persian present perfect (ماضی نقلی) is formed using:

- A) Present stem + می + personal endings
- B) Past stem + ه + auxiliary verb
- C) Infinitive minus ن + بود
- D) Past stem + خواهم

**Answer: B** — Present perfect = past stem + ه (*-e*) + short auxiliary (am/i/ast/im/id/and). Example: رفته‌ام = "I have gone."

---

**2.** What does **رفته‌ام** (*rafte-am*) mean?

- A) I will go
- B) I went
- C) I have gone
- D) I was going

**Answer: C** — رفته‌ام = "I have gone" (present perfect, 1st person singular). رفت = past stem, ه = participle marker, ام = 1st person auxiliary.

---

**3.** How do you say "They have seen" in Persian?

- A) دیده بودند
- B) دیده‌اند
- C) می‌دیدند
- D) دیدند

**Answer: B** — دیده‌اند (*dide-and*) = "They have seen" (present perfect, 3rd person plural).

---

**4.** The past perfect (ماضی بعید) in Persian expresses:

- A) An action happening right now
- B) An action that will happen in the future
- C) An action completed before another past action
- D) A habitual past action

**Answer: C** — Past perfect describes the "past of the past" — an action completed before another past event. Example: وقتی رسیدم، او رفته بود = "When I arrived, he had already gone."

---

**5.** What is the past perfect form of "I had gone" in Persian?

- A) رفته‌ام
- B) رفتم
- C) رفته بودم
- D) می‌رفتم

**Answer: C** — رفته بودم (*rafte budam*) = "I had gone." Formation: past stem + ه + بودم (past of "to be").

---

**6.** Complete this sentence: وقتی رسیدم، او _____ .

- A) رفته است
- B) رفته بود
- C) می‌رود
- D) رفته‌ام

**Answer: B** — رفته بود (*rafte bud*) = "had gone." The sentence means "When I arrived, he had already gone." Past perfect is required here.

---

**7.** Which type of conditional sentence does this example represent? **اگر پول داشتم، سفر می‌کردم.**

- A) Real conditional (Type 1)
- B) Unreal/hypothetical conditional (Type 2)
- C) Counterfactual past conditional (Type 3)
- D) Future real conditional

**Answer: B** — This is Type 2 — unreal/hypothetical. "If I had money (but I don't), I would travel." Past tense in both clauses signals unreality in the present.

---

**8.** Which sentence is a correct Type 1 (real) conditional in Persian?

- A) اگر پول داشتم، می‌رفتم
- B) اگر آمده بودی، می‌دیدی
- C) اگر وقت داشته باشی، بیا
- D) اگر رفته بود، می‌دیدم

**Answer: C** — اگر وقت داشته باشی، بیا = "If you have time, come." This is a real, achievable condition using subjunctive in the if-clause.

---

**9.** The subjunctive mood in Persian is triggered by all of the following EXCEPT:

- A) می‌خواهم که
- B) امیدوارم که
- C) دیروز
- D) شاید

**Answer: C** — دیروز (*diruz*) = "yesterday" — a time adverb, not a subjunctive trigger. Subjunctive is triggered by verbs of wanting, hoping, necessity, and modal adverbs.

---

**10.** What does **شاید بیاید** mean?

- A) He came yesterday
- B) He must come
- C) Maybe he will come
- D) He has come

**Answer: C** — شاید (*shāyad*) = maybe/perhaps, triggers subjunctive. بیاید = subjunctive of آمدن (to come). "Maybe he will come." Note: شاید is an exact Urdu-Persian cognate — شاید in Urdu = perhaps!

---

**11.** The subjunctive conjugation of **رفتن** (to go) for "that I go" is:

- A) رفتم
- B) می‌روم
- C) بروم
- D) رفته‌ام

**Answer: C** — بروم (*beravam* → colloquial *beram*) = "that I go" (subjunctive, 1st person singular). The subjunctive uses the present stem (رو) with بـ prefix.

---

**12.** In the sentence **می‌خواهم که بیایی**, what mood is بیایی?

- A) Indicative present
- B) Subjunctive
- C) Past simple
- D) Imperative

**Answer: B** — بیایی is the subjunctive form of آمدن for 2nd person singular. The sentence = "I want you to come" — triggered by می‌خواهم که.

---

**13.** How is the simple passive (action passive) formed in Persian?

- A) Present stem + می + شد
- B) Past participle (ه form) + شد/شده
- C) Infinitive + بود
- D) Present stem + داشت

**Answer: B** — Simple passive = past participle (verb + ه) + شد. Example: درب بسته شد = "The door was closed."

---

**14.** What is the difference between **درب بسته شد** and **درب بسته است**?

- A) No difference — they mean the same thing
- B) بسته شد = the door was closed (action); بسته است = the door is closed (current state)
- C) بسته شد = informal; بسته است = formal
- D) بسته شد = present; بسته است = past

**Answer: B** — بسته شد = the closing action happened (action passive). بسته است = the door is currently in a closed state (state passive). This is a key B2 distinction.

---

**15.** How do you say "The letter was written" in Persian (action passive)?

- A) نامه نوشته است
- B) نامه نوشته شد
- C) نامه را نوشتم
- D) نامه نوشته بود

**Answer: B** — نامه نوشته شد (*nāme neveshte shod*) = "The letter was written" (passive action — the writing happened).

---

**16.** The modal verb **نباید** (*nabāyad*) expresses:

- A) Possibility
- B) Must not / should not
- C) I can
- D) I want to

**Answer: B** — نباید = must not / should not. It is the negative of باید (must). Example: نباید دیر کنی = "You must not be late."

---

**17.** Which sentence correctly uses **می‌توانم** (*mi-tavānam*)?

- A) می‌توانم باید بروم
- B) می‌توانم کمک کنم
- C) می‌توانم نباید
- D) می‌توانم رفته‌ام

**Answer: B** — می‌توانم کمک کنم = "I can help." می‌توانم = I can/I am able to + infinitive-based complement.

---

**18.** **شاید** (*shāyad*) in Persian is notable for Urdu speakers because:

- A) It is borrowed from French
- B) It does not exist in Urdu
- C) It is identical in Urdu — شاید = perhaps in both languages
- D) It means something different in Urdu

**Answer: C** — شاید is one of the clearest Urdu-Persian cognates — identical spelling, identical pronunciation, identical meaning (perhaps/maybe) in both languages.

---

**19.** A relative clause in Persian uses which connector word?

- A) اگر
- B) که
- C) چون
- D) ولی

**Answer: B** — که (*ke*) = that/which/who — the Persian relative pronoun. Example: کتابی که خواندم = "The book that I read."

---

**20.** What type of relative clause is **خانه‌ای که در آن زندگی می‌کنم**?

- A) Subject relative clause
- B) Object relative clause
- C) Prepositional relative clause
- D) Possessive relative clause

**Answer: C** — خانه‌ای که در آن زندگی می‌کنم = "The house in which I live" — a prepositional relative clause (در آن = in it).

---

**21.** In the spoken colloquial Persian, the written word **خانه** (*khāne*) becomes:

- A) خانه (unchanged)
- B) خانی
- C) خونه
- D) خانست

**Answer: C** — خونه (*khune*) is the colloquial spoken form of خانه (house). Note the Urdu cognate: خانہ/khāna = house in Urdu, showing the Persian origin.

---

**22.** How do you form the past perfect (ماضی بعید) for "She had come"?

- A) آمده است
- B) آمده بود
- C) آمد
- D) می‌آمد

**Answer: B** — آمده بود (*āmade bud*) = "She had come." Past stem of آمدن = آمد, + ه + بود (3rd person singular of بودن in past).

---

**23.** Which sentence uses a Type 3 (counterfactual past) conditional correctly?

- A) اگر وقت داشته باشی، بیا
- B) اگر پول داشتم، می‌رفتم
- C) اگر زودتر آمده بودی، او را می‌دیدی
- D) اگر بروی، خوب است

**Answer: C** — اگر زودتر آمده بودی، او را می‌دیدی = "If you had come earlier, you would have seen him" — Type 3 uses past perfect in both clauses.

---

**24.** Persian classical poetry uses the archaic suffix **ـست** instead of:

- A) ـند
- B) است
- C) بود
- D) داشت

**Answer: B** — ـست is the contracted/archaic poetic form of است (is). Example: خوبست = خوب است. This compression is typical of classical Persian verse.

---

**25.** The Persian present perfect mirrors which Urdu construction most closely?

- A) ہوتا ہے (present habitual)
- B) گیا ہوں (I have gone)
- C) جائے گا (will go)
- D) گیا تھا (had gone)

**Answer: B** — The Persian رفته‌ام (I have gone) maps directly to Urdu گیا ہوں — same structure, same temporal meaning. This is the present perfect in both languages.

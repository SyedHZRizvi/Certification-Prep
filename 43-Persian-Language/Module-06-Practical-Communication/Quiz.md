# Module 6 Quiz — ارتباط عملی (Practical Communication)

**25 Multiple Choice Questions | Professional Persian, Email Writing, Health Vocabulary, Opinions, Ta'arof, Proverbs, Register**

---

**1.** Which of the following is the correct formal opening salutation for a Persian business email addressed to a man?

- A) با سلام و خداحافظی
- B) جناب آقای [نام]
- C) سلام دوست عزیز
- D) مدیر محترم، بیا

**Answer: B** — جناب آقای [نام] (*Janāb-e āqā-ye [nām]*) is the correct very formal address for a male recipient in Persian correspondence.

---

**2.** What does the formal Persian closing phrase **ارادتمند** (*erādatmand*) mean?

- A) Best regards
- B) Goodbye forever
- C) Your devoted/respectful (classic formal close)
- D) I disagree with you

**Answer: C** — ارادتمند is a classic formal closing meaning "your devoted one" — a mark of high formal register in Persian letters.

---

**3.** The Persian word **گزارش** (*gozāresh*) means:

- A) Budget
- B) Contract
- C) Report
- D) Deadline

**Answer: C** — گزارش = report. Note the Urdu cognate: گزارش is used in formal Urdu too, meaning "petition/submission."

---

**4.** How do you say "meeting agenda" in Persian?

- A) دستور جلسه
- B) جلسه هماهنگی
- C) پروژه کاری
- D) گزارش مدیر

**Answer: A** — دستور جلسه (*dastur-e jalase*) = meeting agenda. جلسه هماهنگی = coordination meeting.

---

**5.** What does **پیرو مکالمه‌ی قبلی** (*peyro-ye mokālame-ye qabli*) mean in a Persian email?

- A) Please reply immediately
- B) Following our previous conversation
- C) I am writing for the first time
- D) With great respect and honor

**Answer: B** — پیرو مکالمه‌ی قبلی is a standard formal phrase meaning "Following our previous conversation" — used at the start of a follow-up email.

---

**6.** The Persian word **درد** (*dard*) means "pain." Which language shares this exact word with the same meaning?

- A) French
- B) Arabic
- C) Urdu
- D) Turkish

**Answer: C** — درد/dard is a direct Urdu-Persian cognate. Urdu درد = pain, exactly as in Persian. This is one of the most commonly shared words.

---

**7.** At a doctor's office in Iran, how would you colloquially say "My head hurts"?

- A) سرم درد می‌کنه
- B) سرم بیمار است
- C) از سرم متأسفم
- D) درد ندارم

**Answer: A** — سرم درد می‌کنه (*saram dard mi-kone*) = "My head hurts" in colloquial Persian. سرم = my head, درد می‌کنه = hurts.

---

**8.** What is **تب** (*tab*) in Persian?

- A) Prescription
- B) Allergy
- C) Fever
- D) Surgery

**Answer: C** — تب = fever. Example: تب دارم (*tab dāram*) = "I have a fever."

---

**9.** How do you say "In my opinion" in Persian?

- A) اصلاً مخالفم
- B) به نظر من
- C) هیچ شکی نیست
- D) کاملاً موافقم

**Answer: B** — به نظر من (*be-nazar-e man*) = "In my opinion." Urdu speakers will recognize this: *be-nazar-e man* mirrors اردو بنظر میرے — identical construction!

---

**10.** Which phrase expresses strong emphatic certainty in Persian?

- A) شاید درست باشه
- B) نمی‌دونم
- C) هیچ شکی نیست
- D) به نظرم ممکنه

**Answer: C** — هیچ شکی نیست (*hich shakki nist*) = "There is no doubt" — emphatic register.

---

**11.** What is **تعارف** (*ta'arof*) in Iranian culture?

- A) A type of Persian tea ceremony
- B) The elaborate system of social courtesy where initial offers are declined
- C) A formal business contract process
- D) The Persian greeting exchanged on Nowruz

**Answer: B** — Ta'arof is the Persian system of social grace where initial offers (food, payment, etc.) are politely declined and must be insisted upon. It is not insincerity — it is cultural etiquette.

---

**12.** In a ta'arof exchange, when tea is offered and declined, what is the correct host response?

- A) خداحافظ
- B) خواهش می‌کنم، بفرمایید
- C) نه ممنون، نمی‌خواهم
- D) باشه، دفعه دیگه

**Answer: B** — خواهش می‌کنم، بفرمایید (*khāhesh mi-konam, befarmayyid*) = "Please, go ahead" — the correct second insistence in ta'arof.

---

**13.** What does the Persian proverb **عجله کار شیطان است** (*ajale kār-e sheytān ast*) mean?

- A) The devil never sleeps
- B) Patience and victory are old friends
- C) Haste is the devil's work (Haste makes waste)
- D) The wise man never repeats a mistake

**Answer: C** — عجله کار شیطان است = "Haste is the devil's work" — equivalent to "haste makes waste."

---

**14.** Which proverb translates to "More roof, more snow" — used to mean "more wealth, more problems"?

- A) صبر و ظفر هر دو دوستان قدیمند
- B) هر که بامش بیش، برفش بیشتر
- C) از تو حرکت، از خدا برکت
- D) زبان سرخ سر سبز می‌دهد بر باد

**Answer: B** — هر که بامش بیش، برفش بیشتر (*har ke bāmash bish, barfash bishtar*) = "More roof, more snow" — more wealth, more problems.

---

**15.** What does **از تو حرکت، از خدا برکت** mean?

- A) Patience and victory are old friends
- B) God helps those who help themselves
- C) A friend in need is a friend indeed
- D) The impatient person loses everything

**Answer: B** — از تو حرکت، از خدا برکت = "From you the move, from God the blessing" — equivalent to "God helps those who help themselves."

---

**16.** In Persian, the formal written form **نمی‌دانم** becomes which colloquial form?

- A) نمی‌دانم (unchanged)
- B) نمیدونم
- C) نه دانم
- D) ندانستم

**Answer: B** — نمیدونم (*nemidūnam*) is the colloquial Tehran spoken form of "I don't know."

---

**17.** Which of the following pairs correctly shows formal → colloquial Persian?

- A) می‌رویم → می‌ریم
- B) می‌خواهم → می‌خوانم
- C) این است → این بود
- D) هستم → نیستم

**Answer: A** — می‌رویم (*mi-ravim*) → می‌ریم (*mi-rim*) = "We are going." The colloquial form drops the medial syllable.

---

**18.** In the Persian text message slang, **خخخ** represents:

- A) A formal greeting
- B) An expression of sadness
- C) Laughter (like "lol" or "hahaha")
- D) A question mark sound

**Answer: C** — خخخ is informal Persian texting for laughter, equivalent to "hahaha" or "lol."

---

**19.** The Persian word **همکار** (*hamkār*) means:

- A) Manager
- B) Colleague
- C) Client
- D) Secretary

**Answer: B** — همکار = colleague. هم = together/co-, کار = work. Note: this is a pure Persian compound word, not Arabic.

---

**20.** How would you politely express disagreement in Persian instead of bluntly saying "That's wrong"?

- A) این کاملاً اشتباهه و من مخالفم
- B) به نظرم شاید بتونیم از زاویه دیگه‌ای نگاه کنیم
- C) نه، این درست نیست و باید عوض بشه
- D) اصلاً قبول نمی‌کنم

**Answer: B** — به نظرم شاید بتونیم از زاویه دیگه‌ای نگاه کنیم = "In my opinion, perhaps we can look at it from a different angle" — the polite Persian way to disagree.

---

**21.** What does **قرارداد** (*qarārdād*) mean in Persian business vocabulary?

- A) Budget
- B) Project
- C) Contract
- D) Deadline

**Answer: C** — قرارداد = contract. قرار = agreement/arrangement, داد = gave. This word is also used in Urdu (معاہدہ = Urdu equivalent; قرارداد is a cognate form used in both).

---

**22.** Which Persian phrase means "If possible" in formal correspondence?

- A) در صورت امکان
- B) خواهشمند است
- C) به استحضار می‌رسانم
- D) ارادتمند

**Answer: A** — در صورت امکان (*dar surat-e emkān*) = "If possible." A standard formal phrase used in letters and emails.

---

**23.** The formal honorific for addressing a woman in a Persian letter is:

- A) جناب آقای
- B) با احترام
- C) سرکار خانم
- D) موفق باشید

**Answer: C** — سرکار خانم [نام] (*Sarkār khānom-e [nām]*) = "Dear Ms. [Name]" — the very formal address for a woman in Persian correspondence.

---

**24.** What does **متأسفانه مشکلی پیش اومده** mean?

- A) Fortunately, everything is perfect
- B) Unfortunately, a problem has come up
- C) Please contact us immediately
- D) I hope this problem is resolved

**Answer: B** — متأسفانه مشکلی پیش اومده (*mota'assefāne moshkeli pish umade*) = "Unfortunately, a problem has come up" — a standard opening phrase for making a complaint.

---

**25.** The Persian word **پیشنهاد** (*pishnahād*) means:

- A) Deadline
- B) Budget
- C) Report
- D) Proposal / suggestion

**Answer: D** — پیشنهاد = proposal / suggestion. Also used in formal Urdu as تجویز/پیشنہاد — the word پیشنہاد is shared between both languages.

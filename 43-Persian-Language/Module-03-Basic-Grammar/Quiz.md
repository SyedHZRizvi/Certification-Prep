# Module 3 Quiz — بنیادی گرامر (Basic Grammar)

**26 Multiple Choice Questions | Gender, Ezafe, Verbs, Pronouns, Negation**

---

**1.** Persian nouns are:

- A) Always masculine
- B) Either masculine or feminine, like Arabic
- C) Gender-neutral — no grammatical gender
- D) Feminine by default

**Answer: C** — Persian is completely gender-neutral. This is one of its most learner-friendly features.

---

**2.** What word order does Persian use?

- A) Subject–Verb–Object (SVO), like English
- B) Object–Subject–Verb (OSV)
- C) Verb–Subject–Object (VSO), like Arabic
- D) Subject–Object–Verb (SOV), like Urdu

**Answer: D** — Persian follows SOV order, identical to Urdu.

---

**3.** Which is the correct Persian translation of "I am good"?

- A) من خوب است
- B) خوبم
- C) خوبند
- D) خوبید

**Answer: B** — خوبم (*khubam*) = "I am good." The suffix ـم (-am) = "I am."

---

**4.** How do you say "He is tired" in Persian?

- A) خسته هستم
- B) خسته هستند
- C) خسته است
- D) خسته هستی

**Answer: C** — خسته است (*khaste ast*) = "He/she is tired." است = third person singular of "to be."

---

**5.** In Persian, the pronoun **او** (*u*) refers to:

- A) "I"
- B) "We"
- C) "You" (formal)
- D) He, she, or it (all three)

**Answer: D** — او is gender-neutral and covers he, she, and it.

---

**6.** What is the formal/polite "you" in Persian?

- A) تو
- B) او
- C) شما
- D) ما

**Answer: C** — شما (*shomā*) is the formal "you," equivalent to Urdu آپ.

---

**7.** How do you say "my book" using the ezafe construction?

- A) کتابِ من
- B) من کتاب
- C) کتاب من است
- D) منِ کتاب

**Answer: A** — کتابِ من (*ketāb-e man*) = "my book." Ezafe ـِ connects the noun to the possessive pronoun.

---

**8.** In the ezafe construction, where does the adjective go relative to the noun?

- A) Before the noun (like English)
- B) After the noun, connected by ezafe (-e)
- C) Either before or after with no difference
- D) At the end of the sentence

**Answer: B** — Persian adjectives follow the noun: کتابِ بزرگ (*ketāb-e bozorg*) = "big book."

---

**9.** What does the ezafe sound like when the noun ends in a vowel sound? Example: خانه + زیبا

- A) خانهِ زیبا (plain ezafe)
- B) خانهٔ زیبا (*khāne-ye zibā*)
- C) خانه زیبا (no connection)
- D) زیباِ خانه

**Answer: B** — When a noun ends in a vowel, ezafe becomes **-ye**: خانهٔ زیبا.

---

**10.** How do you form the plural of **دوست** (friend)?

- A) دوستان OR دوست‌ها
- B) دوستین
- C) دوستیم
- D) دوستات

**Answer: A** — Both دوستان (*dustān* — formal animate plural) and دوست‌ها (*dust-hā* — general plural) are correct.

---

**11.** What is the prefix used to form the Persian present tense?

- A) نه‌ (*ne-*)
- B) می‌ (*mi-*)
- C) ها‌ (*hā-*)
- D) بی‌ (*bi-*)

**Answer: B** — می‌ (*mi-*) is added before the present verb stem to form the present tense.

---

**12.** How do you say "I go" in Persian?

- A) من رفتم
- B) من می‌روم
- C) من می‌رفتم
- D) من بروم

**Answer: B** — می‌روم (*mi-ravam*) = "I go" (present tense). من is often dropped since the verb ending shows the person.

---

**13.** What is the present tense of **خوردن** (*khordan* — to eat) for "they eat"?

- A) می‌خورم
- B) می‌خوری
- C) می‌خورد
- D) می‌خورند

**Answer: D** — می‌خورند (*mi-khorand*) = "they eat." The ending ـند = third person plural.

---

**14.** To negate a present tense verb in Persian, you:

- A) Add "نه" before the subject
- B) Replace می‌ with نمی‌
- C) Add a negative suffix at the end
- D) Use a separate negative word after the verb

**Answer: B** — Replace می‌ with نمی‌: می‌روم → نمی‌روم (I don't go).

---

**15.** What does **نمی‌دانم** (*nemi-dānam*) mean?

- A) I know
- B) I don't know
- C) He doesn't know
- D) We don't know

**Answer: B** — نمی‌دانم = "I don't know." نمی‌ = negation; دانم = I know.

---

**16.** Which question word means "where" in Persian?

- A) چه
- B) کی
- C) کجا
- D) چرا

**Answer: C** — کجا (*kojā*) = where.

---

**17.** What does **چرا** (*cherā*) mean?

- A) How
- B) What
- C) When
- D) Why

**Answer: D** — چرا = why.

---

**18.** How do you say "How much?" in Persian?

- A) چطور
- B) کدام
- C) چقدر
- D) چه

**Answer: C** — چقدر (*cheqadr*) = how much / how many.

---

**19.** In Persian, the indefinite article "a/an" is expressed by:

- A) Adding ال (*al-*) before the noun (like Arabic)
- B) Adding ـی (-i) after the noun or using یک before it
- C) There is no way to express "a" in Persian
- D) Using the word "یکی" before every indefinite noun

**Answer: B** — کتابی (*ketābi*) or یک کتاب (*yek ketāb*) both mean "a book."

---

**20.** "The book is on the table" in Persian is:

- A) کتاب روی میز است
- B) است کتاب روی میز
- C) روی میز کتاب است
- D) Both A and C are acceptable in Persian

**Answer: D** — Persian allows some flexibility in word order; both "کتاب روی میز است" and "روی میز کتاب است" (emphasizing location) are used.

---

**21.** The present stem of **رفتن** (*raftan* — to go) is:

- A) رفت
- B) رفتن
- C) رو
- D) رود

**Answer: C** — The present stem of رفتن is رو (*row*). Conjugation: می‌روم، می‌روی، می‌رود...

---

**22.** Which ending marks first person plural (we) in the Persian present tense?

- A) ـم (-am)
- B) ـید (-id)
- C) ـیم (-im)
- D) ـند (-and)

**Answer: C** — ـیم (-im) = we: می‌رویم (we go), می‌خوریم (we eat).

---

**23.** What does **چطور هستید؟** (*chetour hastid?*) mean?

- A) Where are you going?
- B) How are you? (formal)
- C) What do you want?
- D) Who are you?

**Answer: B** — چطور = how; هستید = you are (formal). "How are you?" (formal).

---

**24.** Which of the following sentences correctly demonstrates the ezafe?

- A) بزرگ کتاب
- B) کتابِ بزرگ
- C) کتاب بزرگ است
- D) کتاب‌ها بزرگ

**Answer: B** — کتابِ بزرگ (*ketāb-e bozorg*) = "the big book" or "a big book." Adjective after noun via ezafe.

---

**25.** How is "We are not going" expressed in Persian?

- A) ما نه می‌رویم
- B) ما نمی‌رویم
- C) ما می‌نرویم
- D) ما می‌روند نه

**Answer: B** — ما نمی‌رویم (*mā nemi-ravim*) = "We are not going."

---

**26.** The phrase **خانهٔ من** (*khāne-ye man*) means:

- A) A big house
- B) My house
- C) The house is big
- D) Their house

**Answer: B** — خانهٔ من = "my house." خانه = house; ـٔ = ezafe (-ye after vowel-ending noun); من = my/me.

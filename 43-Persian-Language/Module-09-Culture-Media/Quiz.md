# Module 9 Quiz — فرهنگ، شعر و ادب (Culture, Poetry & Literature)

**25 Multiple Choice Questions | Persian Poetry, Poets, Nowruz, Iranian Calendar, Cinema, Idioms, Dari, Tajik, Persian-Urdu Connection**

---

**1.** Rumi's most famous work, the **مثنوی معنوی** (*Masnavi-yi Ma'navi*), is approximately how many couplets long?

- A) 1,000 couplets in 2 books
- B) 5,000 couplets in 3 books
- C) 25,000 couplets in 6 books
- D) 100,000 couplets in 12 books

**Answer: C** — The Masnavi consists of approximately 25,000 couplets in six books, making it one of the longest poems in world literature.

---

**2.** The opening couplet of Rumi's Masnavi begins with:

- A) اگر آن ترک شیرازی به دست آرد دل ما را
- B) بشنو این نی چون شکایت می‌کند
- C) بیا که قصر امل سخت سست بنیاد است
- D) الا یا أیها الساقی أدر کأساً و ناولها

**Answer: B** — بشنو این نی چون شکایت می‌کند / از جدایی‌ها حکایت می‌کند — "Listen to this reed — how it complains / Telling tales of separations." This opens the Masnavi.

---

**3.** In Rumi's Masnavi, the reed flute (نی — *ney*) symbolizes:

- A) The beauty of nature in Persia
- B) The human soul separated from its divine origin
- C) The sound of Iranian classical music
- D) The voice of the Persian king

**Answer: B** — The نی represents the human soul separated from its divine origin (God/the reed bed). This is the central metaphor of the Masnavi.

---

**4.** Which poet wrote the **شاهنامه** (*Shahnameh*), the Persian national epic?

- A) رومی (Rumi)
- B) حافظ (Hafiz)
- C) عمر خیام (Omar Khayyám)
- D) فردوسی (Ferdowsi)

**Answer: D** — فردوسی (*Ferdowsi*, ~940–1020 CE) wrote the Shahnameh, which covers Persian mythology and heroic history. It is the longest epic poem by a single author in world literature.

---

**5.** The **رباعی** (*ruba'i*) poetic form, made world-famous by Omar Khayyám, consists of:

- A) 3-line stanzas
- B) 4-line quatrains
- C) 6-line stanzas
- D) Rhyming couplets in pairs of 10

**Answer: B** — The رباعی is a 4-line quatrain with the rhyme scheme AABA. Khayyám's Rubaiyat (رباعیات) are his famous collection of these.

---

**6.** In the **غزل** (ghazal) poetic form, what distinctive feature appears in the last couplet?

- A) A moral lesson or proverb
- B) The name of the king or patron being praised
- C) The poet's own name (takhallus)
- D) A quotation from the Quran

**Answer: C** — In the classical Persian ghazal, the final couplet contains the poet's pen name (تخلص — *takhallus*). For example, Hafiz signs his ghazals with "حافظ" in the last verse.

---

**7.** Hafiz's famous ghazal line **اگر آن ترک شیرازی به دست آرد دل ما را / به خال هندویش بخشم سمرقند و بخارا را** references which two cities?

- A) Tehran and Kabul
- B) Samarkand and Bukhara
- C) Isfahan and Shiraz
- D) Baghdad and Cairo

**Answer: B** — Hafiz offers to give Samarkand (سمرقند) and Bukhara (بخارا) — two great Silk Road cities — for the beauty mark of his beloved Shirazi. This is one of the most quoted lines in Persian poetry.

---

**8.** Iran uses which calendar system for official dates?

- A) Gregorian (Christian) calendar
- B) Islamic Lunar Hijri calendar
- C) Solar Hijri calendar (تقویم هجری شمسی)
- D) Julian calendar

**Answer: C** — Iran officially uses the Solar Hijri calendar (تقویم شمسی), which is approximately 621 years behind the Gregorian. 2025 CE ≈ 1404 Solar Hijri.

---

**9.** The Persian New Year, **نوروز** (*Nowruz*), falls on:

- A) The first day of Farvardin — the vernal equinox (around March 20–21)
- B) The first of Muharram (Islamic New Year)
- C) January 1st
- D) The summer solstice in June

**Answer: A** — Nowruz falls on the vernal equinox — around March 20–21 — the first day of Farvardin (فروردین), the first month of the Persian calendar.

---

**10.** The **هفت‌سین** (*Haft-Sin*) table for Nowruz must include seven items all beginning with:

- A) The letter میم (m)
- B) The letter سین (s)
- C) The letter الف (a)
- D) The letter نون (n)

**Answer: B** — Haft-Sin = "Seven S's" (هفت = seven, سین = the letter S). All seven items begin with the letter س (sin) in Persian.

---

**11.** Which Nowruz Haft-Sin item symbolizes **love**?

- A) سیب (apple)
- B) سبزه (sprouts)
- C) سنجد (dried oleaster)
- D) سکه (coins)

**Answer: C** — سنجد (*sanjed* — dried oleaster/silver berry) symbolizes love on the Haft-Sin table. سیب = beauty, سبزه = rebirth, سکه = prosperity.

---

**12.** Which of the following is the correct order of Persian months in spring?

- A) تیر، مرداد، شهریور
- B) فروردین، اردیبهشت، خرداد
- C) مهر، آبان، آذر
- D) دی، بهمن، اسفند

**Answer: B** — فروردین، اردیبهشت، خرداد are the three spring months (months 1, 2, 3) of the Solar Hijri calendar.

**13.** Abbas Kiarostami's film **خانه‌ی دوست کجاست؟** (*Where Is the Friend's House?*) is famous for:

- A) Winning the Academy Award for Best Foreign Film
- B) Poetic realism and natural colloquial Persian in real-life settings
- C) Being filmed entirely in Tajik
- D) Depicting Iranian diplomatic culture

**Answer: B** — Kiarostami's film is celebrated for poetic realism and natural, colloquial Persian — making it an excellent C1 listening resource for language learners.

**14.** Which Iranian film won the **2012 Academy Award for Best Foreign Film**?

- A) بچه‌های آسمان (Children of Heaven) — Majidi
- B) خانه‌ی دوست کجاست؟ (Where Is the Friend's House?) — Kiarostami
- C) جدایی نادر از سیمین (A Separation) — Farhadi
- D) دایره (The Circle) — Panahi

**Answer: C** — جدایی نادر از سیمین (*A Separation*) by Asghar Farhadi won the 2012 Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film.

**15.** The Persian idiom **دل به دریا زدن** (*del be daryā zadan*) literally means "heart to the sea." Its idiomatic meaning is:

- A) To feel sad and lonely
- B) To take the plunge / to be courageous
- C) To fall in love
- D) To travel abroad

**Answer: B** — دل به دریا زدن = "to take the plunge" — to do something risky or daring. Note: دل (*del*) = heart is a key Urdu-Persian cognate (دل/dil in Urdu)!

---

**16.** The Persian idiom **دیوار موش داره، موش هم گوش داره** is equivalent to which English expression?

- A) You can't hold two watermelons in one hand
- B) Charity begins at home
- C) Walls have ears
- D) Hearing is nothing like seeing

**Answer: C** — دیوار موش داره، موش هم گوش داره = "The wall has a mouse, the mouse has an ear" — equivalent to "Walls have ears."

---

**17.** **Dari** (دری) is the name for Persian in which country?

- A) Tajikistan
- B) Iran
- C) Afghanistan
- D) Uzbekistan

**Answer: C** — Dari is the official name of Persian in Afghanistan. It is mutually intelligible with Iranian Persian, with the Kabul dialect as the prestige form.

---

**18.** Tajik Persian in Tajikistan is distinctive because it is written in:

- A) Perso-Arabic script like Iranian Persian
- B) Latin script
- C) Cyrillic script (Soviet legacy)
- D) Devanagari script

**Answer: C** — Tajik is written in Cyrillic script — a legacy of the Soviet era. The spoken language is Persian, but the writing system was changed to Cyrillic in the 1930s.

---

**19.** What percentage of Urdu vocabulary is estimated to be of Persian origin?

- A) About 5%
- B) About 10%
- C) About 20%
- D) About 30%

**Answer: D** — Approximately 30% of Urdu vocabulary is Persian in origin, making Persian the single largest contributor to the Urdu lexicon after Sanskrit-based words.

**20.** Allama Iqbal's most celebrated philosophical work **اسرار خودی** (*Asrār-e Khudi* — Secrets of the Self) was written in:

- A) Urdu
- B) English
- C) Persian
- D) Arabic

**Answer: C** — Iqbal wrote Asrār-e Khudi in Persian — he considered his Persian writing superior in philosophical depth. It was translated into English by R.A. Nicholson.

---

**21.** The Urdu ghazal tradition is directly inherited from:

- A) Arabic qasida poetry
- B) Turkish folk poetry
- C) Persian ghazal tradition
- D) Sanskrit lyric poetry

**Answer: C** — The Urdu ghazal is directly inherited from the Persian ghazal — same form (5–12 couplets, poet's name in last verse, radif/refrain), same themes (love, wine, mysticism).

---

**22.** Which Persian poet is most associated with the **قصیده** (*qasida*) — the long panegyric ode?

- A) Omar Khayyám (known for ruba'i)
- B) Rumi (known for masnavi)
- C) Ferdowsi (known for epic masnavi)
- D) Classical court poets including Anvari and Manuchehri

**Answer: D** — The qasida was the domain of classical court poets (Anvari, Manuchehri, Farrokhi) who wrote long odes praising rulers and patrons. Rumi = masnavi; Khayyám = ruba'i; Ferdowsi = epic masnavi.

---

**23.** The idiom **از ماست که بر ماست** (*az māst ke bar māst*) translates as:

- A) We reap what we sow / It's our own doing
- B) Patience and victory are old friends
- C) The wise person learns from others' mistakes
- D) We are what we eat

**Answer: A** — از ماست که بر ماست = literally "It is from us that it falls on us" — equivalent to "we reap what we sow" or "it's our own fault."

---

**24.** What is the Persian term for the classical music modal framework system with 12 modes?

- A) ردیف (radif)
- B) دستگاه (dastgāh)
- C) غزل (ghazal)
- D) سماع (samā')

**Answer: B** — دستگاه (*dastgāh*) = the 12 modal systems of Iranian classical music. The ردیف (*radif*) is the corpus of ~300 melodic pieces organized within these systems.

---

**25.** Linguists describe Tajik as a "time capsule" of classical Persian because:

- A) It is spoken only in ancient temples
- B) It preserves Old Persian vowel distinctions that Iranian Persian has lost
- C) It was never influenced by Arabic
- D) It uses the original Achaemenid script

**Answer: B** — Tajik preserves certain Old Persian vowel distinctions that modern Iranian Persian has lost — making it valuable for historical linguistics and classical Persian phonology studies.

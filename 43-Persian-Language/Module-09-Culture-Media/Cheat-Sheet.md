## The Four Giants of Persian Poetry

| Poet | Dates | Major Work | Poetic Form | Theme |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| فردوسی (Ferdowsi) | ~940–1020 CE | شاهنامه | Epic masnavi | Heroism, national identity |
| عمر خیام (Khayyám) | 1048–1131 CE | رباعیات | رباعی (quatrain) | Carpe diem, mortality |
| مولانا رومی (Rumi) | 1207–1273 CE | مثنوی، دیوان شمس | Masnavi + ghazal | Divine love, mysticism |
| خواجه حافظ (Hafiz) | ~1325–1390 CE | دیوان حافظ | غزل (ghazal) | Love, wine, beauty, freedom |

## Poetic Forms (قالب‌های شعری)

| Persian | Transliteration | Description | Who |
|---|---|---|---|
| غزل | ghazal | 5–12 couplets; poet's name in last verse; love/mysticism | Hafiz, Rumi |
| رباعی | ruba'i | 4-line quatrain (AABA rhyme); compact wisdom | Khayyám |
| مثنوی | masnavi | Rhyming couplets; extended narratives | Rumi, Ferdowsi |
| قصیده | qasida | Long panegyric ode; praise of rulers | Court poets |
| قطعه | qet'e | Short occasional poems | All poets |

## Famous Couplets — Memorize These

**Rumi — Masnavi Opening:**
> بشنو این نی چون شکایت می‌کند
> از جدایی‌ها حکایت می‌کند
> *Listen to this reed — how it complains / Telling tales of separations*

**Hafiz — Shirazi Turk:**
> اگر آن ترک شیرازی به دست آرد دل ما را
> به خال هندویش بخشم سمرقند و بخارا را
> *If that Shirazi Turk would take my heart / For her Hindu mole I'd give Samarkand and Bukhara*

**Hafiz — Divan Opening:**
> که عشق آسان نمود اول ولی افتاد مشکل‌ها
> *For love seemed easy at first, but difficulties have arisen*

## Persian-Urdu Connection

| Domain | How Persian Shaped Urdu |
|---|---|
| Court language | Mughal court was Persian-only until 19th century |
| Vocabulary | ~30% of Urdu vocabulary is Persian in origin |
| Ghazal tradition | Urdu ghazal directly inherited from Persian ghazal |
| Administrative terms | Virtually all from Persian/Arabic |
| Grammar | Ezāfe (کتابِ اردو) — direct Persian inheritance |

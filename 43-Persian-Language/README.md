## Why Persian? Why Now?

Persian (*فارسی — Fārsi*) is one of humanity's great classical languages — spoken by over 110 million people across Iran, Afghanistan, and Tajikistan, with hundreds of millions more who can read Persian poetry and recognize Persian vocabulary embedded in Urdu, Turkish, Punjabi, Sindhi, and Hindi. It is the language of Rumi, Hafiz, Ferdowsi, and Omar Khayyám — a literary tradition of unbroken excellence for 1,200 years.

## The Special Advantage for Urdu Speakers

> **If you speak Urdu, you already know approximately 30–35% of Persian vocabulary.**

This is the most important fact about this course. Persian was the court language of the Mughal Empire for three centuries. When Urdu formed as a language of culture and administration across the Indian subcontinent, it absorbed Persian wholesale — not just words, but grammatical patterns, poetic forms, and cultural idioms.

### Persian-Urdu Cognate Families (100 words — you know them ALL)

**Body & Health:**
دل (del/دل—heart), چشم (cheshm/چشم—eye), دست (dast/دست—hand), تن (tan/تن—body), خون (khun/خون—blood), جان (jān/جان—soul)

**Time & Nature:**
روز (ruz/روز—day), شب (shab/شب—night), باد (bād/باد—wind), آب (āb/آب—water), آتش (ātash/آتش—fire), دریا (daryā/دریا—sea), کوه (kuh/کوہ—mountain), گل (gol/گل—flower), بهار (bahār/بہار—spring)

**Emotions & Abstracts:**
عشق (ishq/عشق—love), درد (dard/درد—pain), نور (nur/نور—light), آرزو (ārezu/آرزو—wish), دیدار (didār/دیدار—meeting), زندگی (zindagi/زندگی—life)

**People & Society:**
دوست (dost/دوست—friend), استاد (ostād/استاد—teacher), شاگرد (shāgerd/شاگرد—student), امتحان (emtehān/امتحان—exam), کتاب (ketāb/کتاب—book), قلم (qalam/قلم—pen)

**Religion & Culture:**
خدا (khodā/خدا—God), نماز (namāz/نماز—prayer), روزه (ruze/روزہ—fast), عید (eyd/عید—festival), مبارک (mobārak/مبارک—blessed)

### Grammar Parallels
- **Ezāfe construction:** کتابِ دوست = "friend's book" — identical in Persian and Urdu!
- **Compound verbs:** بات کردن / بات کرنا — same pattern
- **Subjunctive with "کہ":** Persian "که" = Urdu "کہ" — same trigger words

## Practice Exam Guide

| Exam | Level | Questions | Time | Pass Mark |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Practice Exam 1 | A1–A2 | 15 | 20 min | No minimum |
| Practice Exam 2 | B1–B2 | 25 | 35 min | No minimum |
| Final Mock Exam | C1–C2 | 40 | 75 min | 75% (30/40) |

**How to use the practice exams:**

1. Take Practice Exam 1 after completing Modules 1–2
2. Take Practice Exam 2 after completing Modules 3–6
3. Take the Final Mock Exam after completing all 10 modules
4. Review every incorrect answer by returning to the relevant module's Reading.md
5. Retake the Final Mock until you consistently score 75%+

> **About real Persian proficiency exams.** The official standardized tests of Persian are **AMFA** and the earlier **SAMFA** (*Standardized Test of Persian Language Proficiency*), administered by the **Saadi Foundation** (Bonyād-e Sa'di), Iran's official body for teaching Persian abroad. Like most language exams, they assess the four skills — **listening, reading, writing, and speaking**. The three exams in this course are **CEFR-modeled practice** built to track your progress through the A1→C2 modules; they are **not** a replica of AMFA/SAMFA and do not confer an official score. If you need a recognized credential, sit the official Saadi Foundation test.

## About This Course

This course is engineered to the standards of serious academic language programs, with particular care for two learner populations:

- **English speakers:** complete grammatical scaffolding from zero, cultural context throughout
- **Urdu speakers:** every module highlights cognate overlap, parallel grammar structures, and the shared Urdu-Persian literary heritage

The CEFR framework (Common European Framework of Reference for Languages) is used as the proficiency scale. By Module 10, you are operating at the C2 "Mastery" level — the highest CEFR designation.

# Module 10 Cheat Sheet — تسلط و روانی (Mastery & Fluency)

---

## Urdu Speaker Interference — Fix These

| Pattern | Urdu Error | Persian Correction |
|---|---|---|
| Retroflex consonants | ٹ، ڈ، ڑ in speech | Persian has NO retroflex consonants — remove them |
| ے vs ی distinction | Urdu distinguishes these | Persian ی covers both sounds |
| ن غنہ (nasalization) | Nasal vowels in Urdu | Persian does not nasalize vowels |
| Word stress | Urdu final-syllable stress | Persian: penultimate syllable stress |
| و pronunciation | Urdu و = /v/ always | Persian و = /v/ or /u/ depending on context |

---

## Common Calques to Eliminate

| Wrong (Calque) | Correct Persian | Note |
|---|---|---|
| من فکر می‌کنم که این درست نیست | به نظرم این درست نیست | Drop "I think that" — use "in my view" |
| در تابستان من به پارک رفتم | تابستان به پارک رفتم | Drop redundant subject pronoun |
| یک سیب را خوردم | یک سیب خوردم | No را for indefinite object |
| کتاب‌ها را دارم | کتاب دارم | No plural marker needed in many contexts |

## Persian Oratory Structure (خطابت)

| Element | Persian Term | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Opening invocation | بسم الله / با نام خدا | Begin with God's name |
| Praise of audience | تقدیر از حضار | Thank audience at start |
| Rhetorical question | سؤال بلاغی | Question to create engagement |
| Proverb insertion | بهره از ضرب‌المثل | Weave proverbs into speech |
| Poetry quotation | استشهاد به شعر | Quote Hafiz or Rumi |
| Gradual climax | اوج تدریجی | Build intensity toward main point |
| Memorable close | اختتام به‌یادماندنی | End with a couplet |

### Sample Opening
> بسم‌الله الرحمن الرحیم. دوستان عزیز، پیش از هر چیز، از حضور گرم شما سپاسگزارم.
> *In the name of God. Dear friends, before anything else, I am grateful for your warm presence.*

## Storytelling Formulas

| Persian | Transliteration | English |
|---|---|---|
| یکی بود یکی نبود | yaki bud yaki nabud | Once upon a time |
| در سرزمینی دور | dar sarzamini dur | In a far-away land |
| روزگاری قدیم | ruzgāri qadim | In ancient times |
| روایت است که | revāyat ast ke | It is narrated that |
| گویند که | guyand ke | They say that |

### Narrative Tense Guide

| Tense | Use for | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Past simple (رفت) | Main events | رفت و دید |
| Past continuous (می‌رفت) | Background/setting | می‌رفت که ناگاه... |
| Historical present (می‌رود) | Vivid re-enactment | می‌رود، می‌بیند، می‌گوید... |

## Debate Language (مناظره)

| Stage | Persian | Transliteration |
|---|---|---|
| State position | موضع من این است که | mozé'-e man in ast ke |
| Give evidence | دلیل این ادعا این است که | dalil-e in adde'ā... |
| Acknowledge opponent | درست است که... ولی | dorost ast ke... vali |
| Rebuttal | این استدلال قابل نقد است | in estedlāl qābel-e naqd ast |
| Concession | در این نکته حق با شماست | dar in nokte haqq bā shomāst |
| Final argument | شواهد نشان می‌دهد که | shavāhed neshān mi-dahad ke |

## CEFR C2 Self-Assessment Checklist

### Reading (خواندن)
- [ ] Persian newspapers without dictionary on unfamiliar topics
- [ ] Classical poetry with minimal support
- [ ] Nuanced meaning from formal documents
- [ ] Persian cultural allusions and proverbs in context

### Listening (شنیدن)
- [ ] Persian radio without repetition
- [ ] Fast-paced native speaker conversations
- [ ] Films without subtitles
- [ ] Regional accents (Tehran, Isfahani, Shirazi, Afghani)

### Speaking (صحبت کردن)
- [ ] Abstract topics spontaneously
- [ ] 10-minute formal presentation
- [ ] Natural storytelling with pacing
- [ ] Register-switching (formal/informal) without thinking
- [ ] Persian proverbs and idioms naturally

### Writing (نوشتن)
- [ ] Formal letters and reports without errors
- [ ] Complex subordinate clauses naturally
- [ ] Range of styles: formal, journalistic, narrative
- [ ] Native Persian word choice over unnecessary loans

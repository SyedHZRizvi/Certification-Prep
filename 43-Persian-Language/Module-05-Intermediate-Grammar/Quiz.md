# Module 5 Quiz — درمیانی گرامر (Intermediate Grammar)

**26 Multiple Choice Questions | Past Tense, Future, Continuous, Modals, Conditionals, Formal vs Colloquial**

---

**1.** The past simple tense in Persian is formed using:

- A) The present stem + past endings
- B) The past stem + personal endings
- C) The infinitive minus the final ن
- D) The present tense with a past marker added

**Answer: B** — The past stem (e.g., رفت, خورد, دید) is the base. Add personal endings to conjugate.

---

**2.** What is the past stem of **رفتن** (*raftan* — to go)?

- A) رو
- B) رفت
- C) رفتن
- D) رود

**Answer: B** — The past stem is رفت (*raft*). Note: رو is the *present* stem.

---

**3.** How do you say "I went" in Persian?

- A) می‌روم
- B) می‌رفتم
- C) رفتم
- D) خواهم رفت

**Answer: C** — رفتم (*raftam*) = "I went" (past simple).

---

**4.** What does **خوردند** (*khordand*) mean?

- A) I ate
- B) You ate
- C) She ate
- D) They ate

**Answer: D** — خوردند = "they ate." ـند = third person plural past ending.

---

**5.** How do you say "She didn't come" in Persian?

- A) نیامدم
- B) نیامد
- C) نیامدید
- D) نیامدند

**Answer: B** — نیامد (*nayāmad*) = "she/he didn't come." Past stem of آمدن is آمد; negation: نـ + آمد = نیامد.

---

**6.** The past stem of **دیدن** (*didan* — to see) is:

- A) بین
- B) دیدن
- C) دید
- D) دیده

**Answer: C** — دید (*did*) is the past stem. Present stem: بین (*bin*).

---

**7.** What is the formal Persian future tense for "We will go"?

- A) می‌رویم
- B) رفتیم
- C) خواهیم رفت
- D) داریم می‌رویم

**Answer: C** — خواهیم رفت (*khāhim raft*) = "We will go" (formal written future).

---

**8.** In colloquial Persian, how would you express "I'll call you tomorrow"?

- A) فردا زنگ خواهم زد
- B) فردا زنگ می‌زنم
- C) فردا زنگ زدم
- D) فردا زنگ می‌زدم

**Answer: B** — فردا زنگ می‌زنم (*fardā zang mizanam*) = "I'll call tomorrow." Colloquial future uses present tense + time word.

---

**9.** The Persian present continuous **دارم می‌خورم** means:

- A) I ate
- B) I will eat
- C) I eat (habitually)
- D) I am eating (right now)

**Answer: D** — دارم می‌خورم = "I am eating" — the دارم emphasizes the ongoing action at this moment.

---

**10.** How do you say "He is working" (present continuous) in Persian?

- A) کار کرد
- B) کار می‌کند
- C) داره کار می‌کنه
- D) کار خواهد کرد

**Answer: C** — داره کار می‌کنه (*dāre kār mikone*) = "He is working" (colloquial continuous). داره = colloquial for دارد.

---

**11.** **باید** (*bāyad*) expresses:

- A) Possibility (might)
- B) Permission (may)
- C) Obligation / necessity (must, should)
- D) Desire (want to)

**Answer: C** — باید = must / should / have to. It expresses obligation.

---

**12.** What does **باید برم** (*bāyad beram*) mean?

- A) I might go
- B) I want to go
- C) I must go / I have to go
- D) I'm going

**Answer: C** — باید برم = "I must go." برم is the subjunctive of رفتن.

---

**13.** The negative of باید is:

- A) بی‌باید
- B) نباید
- C) نه‌باید
- D) باید نه

**Answer: B** — نباید (*nabāyad*) = must not / should not.

---

**14.** "You must not park here" in Persian is:

- A) باید اینجا پارک کنی
- B) می‌تونی اینجا پارک کنی
- C) نباید اینجا پارک کنی
- D) بهتره اینجا پارک کنی

**Answer: C** — نباید اینجا پارک کنی = "You must not park here."

---

**15.** How do you say "Can I come?" in colloquial Persian?

- A) باید بیام؟
- B) می‌خوام بیام؟
- C) می‌تونم بیام؟
- D) خواهم آمد؟

**Answer: C** — می‌تونم بیام؟ (*mitūnam biām?*) = "Can I come?" (colloquial form of می‌توانم بیایم؟)

---

**16.** Which sentence correctly uses a conditional with **اگر**?

- A) اگر رفتم، گفتم
- B) اگر بیایی، خوشحال می‌شم
- C) اگر است، می‌رود
- D) اگر باید بروم

**Answer: B** — اگر بیایی، خوشحال می‌شم = "If you come, I'll be happy."

---

**17.** **اگه بشه** (*age beshe*) in colloquial Persian means:

- A) If you want
- B) If it becomes / if it's possible
- C) When it happens
- D) Unless it works

**Answer: B** — اگه = اگر (if); بشه = colloquial for بشود (it becomes/is possible).

---

**18.** What is a compound verb in Persian?

- A) A verb made by reduplicating the stem
- B) A verb formed from noun/adjective + simple verb (usually کردن, شدن, زدن, or دادن)
- C) A verb borrowed from Arabic
- D) A verb with both a past and present stem

**Answer: B** — Compound verbs like کمک کردن (to help), تلفن زدن (to call), خوشحال شدن (to become happy) are formed this way.

---

**19.** What does **شروع کردن** (*shoru' kardan*) mean?

- A) To finish
- B) To continue
- C) To try
- D) To start / begin

**Answer: D** — شروع کردن = to start / begin.

---

**20.** The word **که** (*ke*) in **کتابی که خریدم** functions as:

- A) A preposition meaning "because"
- B) A relative pronoun meaning "that/which"
- C) A question word meaning "who?"
- D) A conjunction meaning "and"

**Answer: B** — که = "that/which/who" introducing a relative clause. کتابی که خریدم = "the book that I bought."

---

**21.** Which sentence correctly uses **که** as a conjunction?

- A) کتابی که خواندم
- B) گفت که می‌آید
- C) کسی که می‌شناسم
- D) روزی که رفتیم

**Answer: B** — گفت که می‌آید = "He said that he is coming." که here is a conjunction introducing a noun clause.

---

**22.** The preposition **برای** (*barāy*) means:

- A) From
- B) With
- C) For
- D) On

**Answer: C** — برای = for. Example: این برای توست = This is for you.

---

**23.** **از تهران آمدم** (*az Tehrān āmadam*) means:

- A) I'm going to Tehran
- B) I came from Tehran
- C) I am in Tehran
- D) Tehran is far from here

**Answer: B** — از = from; آمدم = I came. "I came from Tehran."

---

**24.** What is the colloquial equivalent of the formal **نمی‌دانم** (*nemi-dānam*)?

- A) نمیدونم
- B) نمیدانم
- C) نه دانم
- D) ندانستم

**Answer: A** — نمیدونم (*nemidūnam*) is the colloquial Tehran spoken form of "I don't know."

---

**25.** In spoken Persian, the formal **این است** (*in ast* — "this is") becomes:

- A) اینه
- B) این هست
- C) اینم
- D) اینست

**Answer: A** — اینه (*ine*) is the colloquial contracted form of این است.

---

**26.** Which statement about formal vs. colloquial Persian is CORRECT?

- A) Colloquial Persian is incorrect and should be avoided
- B) Formal Persian is used only in ancient texts
- C) Formal Persian is used in written/official contexts; colloquial Persian is used in everyday speech — both must be learned
- D) There is no difference between formal and colloquial Persian

**Answer: C** — Both registers are important. Formal Persian appears in books, news, and official writing. Colloquial Tehran Persian is used in everyday conversation, TV shows, and informal communication.

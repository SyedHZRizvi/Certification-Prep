## The A-O-F Platform Selection Framework

| Factor | Question to Ask | Minimum Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Audience** | Where does my target customer spend time — and in what mindset? | Clear demographic + psychographic match |
| **Objective** | Awareness / Lead Gen / Sales / Retention? | Platform must support this objective natively |
| **Format** | Can we consistently produce the content this platform rewards? | Sustainable at your team's capacity |

**Rule:** Only invest in platforms scoring 2/3 or higher.

## Algorithm Cheat Sheet

### Facebook (2024–2025)
- Demotes external links; boosts native video and Reels
- Groups content outperforms brand page content
- Back-and-forth comment threads beat passive likes
- Brand pages: ~1–4% organic reach of followers

### Instagram
- **Reels:** Completion rate + replays + non-follower shares = highest reach surface
- **Feed:** Saves > comments > likes (in terms of distribution weight)
- **Stories:** Replies + poll interactions signal close relationships
- Watermarked TikTok reposts are algorithmically demoted — always remove watermarks
- Brand pages: ~2–6% organic reach; Reels up to 3–5× higher than static posts

### TikTok
- Interest graph — follower count barely matters
- First test cohort: ~300–500 users; gate = ~65% completion rate
- Strongest signals: completion rate → shares (especially to DMs) → comments → likes
- Trending audio boosts reach
- Brand new accounts can go viral — no follower base needed

### LinkedIn
- Dwell time is the primary ranking signal
- Native content >>> external links (LinkedIn demotes external URLs)
- Documents/carousels: highest organic reach surface
- Creator Mode: switches to "Follow" default CTA (Call to Action); boosts personal account reach
- Company pages: ~5–10% organic reach; personal creator accounts: 20–40%

## Content Pillar Golden Ratio

| Pillar | % of Posts | Primary Algorithm Signal | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Educational | 40% | Saves + shares | "3 mistakes brands make on LinkedIn" |
| Entertaining | 30% | Shares + completion rate | Behind-scenes, memes, trending audio |
| Inspirational | 20% | Shares + comments | Customer success stories |
| Promotional | 10% | Link clicks | Product demo, limited offer |

**Never exceed 20–30% promotional.** Heavy promo kills reach across all platforms.

## Key Formulas

**Engagement Rate:**
```
ER = (Total Engagements / Total Reach) × 100
```
*Engagements = likes + comments + shares + saves. Use Reach (not Followers).*

**UTM Structure:**
```
?utm_source=[instagram]&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=[name]&utm_content=[post-id]
```

**Influencer ROI (Return on Investment):**
```
ROI = (Revenue Attributed / Total Spend) × 100
Track via: unique UTM links + promo codes per influencer
```

## Influencer Tier Reference

| Tier | Followers | Avg ER | Best Use | Cost (per post) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nano | 1K–10K | 4–8% | Hyper-local, seeding | $0–$250 |
| Micro | 10K–100K | 2–5% | Best ROI, niche authority | $250–$5,000 |
| Macro | 100K–1M | 1–3% | Brand awareness | $5K–$50K |
| Mega | 1M+ | 0.5–2% | Launch moments only | $50K–$1M+ |

**Best ROI Rule:** Micro-influencers consistently outperform macro/mega on a cost-per-engagement basis for most brands.

## Legal & Compliance Callouts

| Rule | Market | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| GDPR Article 6 | EU / UK | UGC (User-Generated Content) in paid ads requires explicit written consent — hashtag use is NOT enough |
| GDPR pixel compliance | EU | Meta pixel consent management required before tracking EU visitors |
| FTC disclosure | US / Canada | #ad or #sponsored required on all paid/gifted influencer posts |
| ASA disclosure | UK | #ad or #gifted required; applies to all paid AND gifted content |
| German influencer law | Germany | Strict; courts have fined both influencers and brands for insufficient disclosure |
| French 2023 influencer law | France | Written contracts required; "virtual content" label for AI-altered images; health product restrictions |
| Meta GDPR fine (2023) | EU | €1.2B record fine — ensure your EU data processing complies with cross-border transfer rules |

## ⚠️ Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

1. **Post identical content across all platforms** — Adapt for each platform's norms and algorithm
2. **Chase follower count over engagement rate** — Engagement rate = health; followers = vanity
3. **Burst-post then disappear** — Inconsistency destroys algorithmic distribution
4. **Skip UTM parameters** — You cannot prove social ROI without them
5. **Ignore comments and DMs** — Unresponded comments signal fake community; platforms punish it
6. **Repost TikTok content to Instagram with watermarks** — Instagram demotes watermarked content; remove before posting
7. **No content calendar** — Reactive posting produces inconsistent messaging and team burnout

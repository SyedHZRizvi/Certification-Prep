## ☕ A Story: How a Giant Green Owl Broke the Internet — and Rewrote the Rules of Brand Social Media

In January 2021, Duolingo had a perfectly respectable social media presence: polished graphics, motivational language-learning tips, and a modest 50,000 TikTok followers. It was, frankly, boring. Indistinguishable from a hundred other EdTech brands.

Then they handed the keys to Zaria Parvez — a 22-year-old junior social media manager fresh out of university — and told her to try something different.

Parvez's pitch was deceptively simple: what if Duolingo's mascot, Duo the green owl, was unhinged? Not "quirky brand" unhinged. Genuinely unhinged. The kind of unhinged that stares at you from across the cafeteria and sends you passive-aggressive push notifications when you miss your French lesson.

The first video: Duo the owl standing ominously behind a user at their desk. No caption needed. The platform exploded. Comments poured in: *"This is exactly how it feels."* Shares, duets, stitches. Within 48 hours, the video had more views than Duolingo's entire TikTok library combined.

What Parvez understood — and what most brand social media managers miss — is that TikTok's algorithm doesn't care about your follower count. It cares about **completion rate** and **engagement velocity** in the first 30 minutes. A video from a brand-new account can hit 10 million views if it hooks users in the first three seconds and keeps them watching. The owl did exactly that.

But here is where the story gets strategically interesting. Parvez didn't just go viral once and hope for the best. She built a **content system**:

- **Content Pillar 1:** Duo as a passive-aggressive ex (tapping into universal human experiences)
- **Content Pillar 2:** Duo reacting to trending audio tracks and pop culture moments within hours
- **Content Pillar 3:** Duo making fun of Duolingo itself — self-deprecating brand humour that feels authentic, not corporate
- **Content Pillar 4:** Community management — replying to comments in character, deepening the Duo persona

Each pillar served a different algorithm signal: Pillars 1 and 3 maximised shares (TikTok's most powerful distribution signal). Pillar 2 rode trending audio, which the algorithm actively boosts. Pillar 4 drove comment velocity, which extends a video's algorithmic lifespan.

Eighteen months later, Duolingo hit **8 million TikTok followers** with **zero paid promotion** on that channel. Their YouTube Shorts exploded. Instagram Reels followed. Earned media coverage from The Wall Street Journal, Forbes, and Adweek ran stories about the owl. Competitor brands started mimicking the format.

The total incremental ad spend: approximately zero. The total incremental time investment: one junior social media manager given creative freedom and a mascot costume.

The lesson is not "be funny on TikTok." The lesson is structural:

1. **Platform fit matters more than budget.** Parvez chose TikTok because Duolingo's audience skewed young and TikTok's interest graph (not social graph) meant discovery was built-in.
2. **Content pillars create systematic repeatability.** Going viral once is luck. Going viral consistently requires a framework.
3. **Algorithm understanding is a competitive advantage.** Parvez knew that TikTok's algorithm rewards entertainment over polished production.
4. **Creative freedom at execution level produces better content than committee-designed campaigns.** The owl was not approved by seven stakeholders. That is the point.

## Section 1: Platform Selection Framework — Audience, Objective, and Format Fit

Choosing the wrong platform is the single most expensive mistake in social media strategy. Not because the platforms charge you differently — but because the **opportunity cost** of building an audience on the wrong channel is enormous. You cannot easily transplant 50,000 Instagram followers to LinkedIn.

### The A-O-F Framework (Audience + Objective + Format)

Before selecting any platform, answer three questions:

| Question | What You're Really Asking |
|---|---|
| **Audience** | Where does my target customer spend time — and in what mindset? |
| **Objective** | Am I building awareness, generating leads, driving sales, or retaining customers? |
| **Format** | Can I consistently produce the content type this platform rewards? |

A platform only works if you score at least 2/3. If your audience is there but you cannot produce the format and the objective doesn't fit — skip it.

### Platform Comparison Table (2024–2025)

| Platform | Primary Demographic | Primary Use Case | Key Content Format | Algorithm Type | Avg. Organic Reach |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **TikTok** | 18–34 (rapidly broadening) | Brand discovery, entertainment | Short video (7–60s optimal) | Interest graph | High (new accounts discoverable) |
| **Instagram** | 18–44 | Brand consideration, shopping | Reels, Stories, static | Social + interest hybrid | Low–medium (2–6% typical) |
| **Facebook** | 35–65+ | Community, local, retargeting | Video, groups, events | Social graph (EdgeRank evolved) | Very low (1–4% typical) |
| **LinkedIn** | 25–55 professionals | B2B (Business-to-Business) lead gen, thought leadership | Text posts, articles, video | Engagement + dwell time | Medium (creator mode helps) |
| **YouTube** | All ages | Education, entertainment, product research | Long-form video, Shorts | Search + recommendation | Medium–high (SEO-driven) |
| **Pinterest** | 25–50, female-skew | Discovery, inspiration, shopping | Static, idea pins | Semantic search + saves | Medium–high (evergreen) |
| **X (Twitter)** | 25–45 | Real-time, news, niche communities | Text, threads, images | Recency + engagement | Low–medium |
| **Snapchat** | 13–25 | Entertainment, ephemeral | Stories, spotlight | Friend-first + discovery | Medium (Spotlight = TikTok-like) |

**North America vs Europe Differences:**

| Market | Key Distinction |
|---|---|
| **UK** | LinkedIn adoption higher for B2C (Business-to-Consumer) than in US; Facebook Groups still very active for local commerce |
| **Germany** | XING competes with LinkedIn for professional networking; WhatsApp Business critical for direct commerce |
| **France** | Instagram dominates youth marketing; Snapchat has unusually high adult penetration vs other EU markets |
| **Netherlands** | LinkedIn penetration among highest globally (>50% of working population); Twitter/X still relevant for news |
| **Nordics** | High digital literacy; YouTube and podcasts outperform social for thought leadership; TikTok growing fastest |

### The "Wrong Platform" Common Traps

> **Common Trap #1:** B2B company launches TikTok because it's trending.
> Decision makers aged 45+ are on LinkedIn. Dancing mascots will not generate enterprise leads. Spend that energy on LinkedIn Articles.

> **Common Trap #2:** DTC (Direct-to-Consumer) fashion brand invests only in Facebook.
> Fashion discovery has migrated to Instagram Reels and TikTok. Facebook still converts older demographics but discovery is dead there.

> **Common Trap #3:** Local restaurant tries to build a YouTube channel.
> YouTube requires serious production investment and long-form content. For local discovery, Google Business Profile + Instagram Stories + TikTok delivers far better ROI (Return on Investment).

## Section 2: Algorithm Mechanics — How Each Platform Decides Who Sees Your Content

Understanding algorithms is not optional — it is the entire game. Every platform is essentially a giant attention auction, and the algorithm is the auctioneer. The currency is engagement signals, not money (at least in organic reach).

### Facebook: From EdgeRank to Integrity & Authenticity

Facebook's algorithm has evolved dramatically since the original EdgeRank formula (Affinity × Weight × Time Decay) was made public around 2010. Modern Facebook ranking considers:

1. **Inventory** — All posts available to show in a user's feed
2. **Signals** — Hundreds of signals: who posted, post type, engagement history, time spent on previous posts
3. **Predictions** — ML models predict likelihood of comment, share, click, hide, or "this is misinformation"
4. **Score** — Posts ranked by predicted value minus predicted negative reactions
5. **Diversity filter** — Prevents the same source dominating the feed

**What actually moves the needle in 2024–2025:**
- **Video (especially Reels)** — Facebook is pushing video aggressively to compete with TikTok
- **Groups engagement** — Content posted within or reshared from Groups gets priority
- **Comments (especially back-and-forth threads)** — A 10-comment thread beats 100 likes
- **"Meaningful interactions"** — Interactions between friends and family outrank brand-to-user interactions

**GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) Note (EU/UK):** Facebook's interest-based targeting and cross-site tracking data use has been constrained by GDPR enforcement since 2018. Meta has faced fines exceeding €1.2 billion (2023 record fine from the Irish DPC). For European campaigns, ensure your consent management platform (CMP) is compliant before running any Facebook tracking pixels.

### Instagram: The Reels Boost

Instagram currently runs **separate algorithms for different surfaces**:

| Surface | What Drives Reach |
|---|---|
| **Feed** | Relationship signals (likes, DMs, saves), recency, and predicted interest |
| **Stories** | Close relationship signals + interaction history (polls, replies) |
| **Reels** | Entertainment score: completion rate, replays, shares, and watch-time of non-followers |
| **Explore** | Interest signals from accounts you don't follow; ML classification of content |

**The Reels Opportunity (2024–2025):** Instagram is actively deprioritising static posts in favour of Reels to compete with TikTok. Reels receive **3–5× higher organic reach** than equivalent static content (industry estimate; Meta does not publish exact multipliers). This is the most powerful organic lever available to brands right now.

**Key signals to optimise:**
- Hook in first 1–3 seconds (thumb-stop rate)
- Audio: trending audio gets a slight algorithmic boost
- Subtitles: increases completion rate by 10–15% (industry estimate)
- No watermarks: Instagram actively suppresses cross-posted TikTok content with visible watermarks
- Save rate: highest-quality signal for Feed distribution; it signals "I want to come back to this"

### TikTok: The Interest Graph

TikTok's algorithm is fundamentally different from Meta platforms. It is built on an **interest graph**, not a social graph. This means:

- Your follower count barely matters for distribution
- A brand-new account can go viral on day one
- The algorithm tests content with small cohorts and amplifies based on performance ratios

**TikTok's Core Signals (ranked by importance):**

1. **Completion rate / Loop rate** — Did viewers watch the whole video? Did they rewatch?
2. **Shares** — The most powerful signal (especially saves to collections and DMs)
3. **Comments** — Quantity and whether creator responded
4. **Likes** — Weakest signal (easiest to game, so weighted lower)
5. **Profile visits after watching** — Signals intent

**The 3-Second / 10-Second / Full Watch Checkpoints:** TikTok's distribution pipeline works in stages. A video first gets shown to ~300–500 random users. If completion rate exceeds ~65%, it gets pushed to a larger pool. Then another gate. Each checkpoint filters out underperformers. Brands with high production values but weak storytelling hooks fail the first checkpoint constantly.

### LinkedIn: Dwell Time and Creator Mode

LinkedIn's algorithm heavily weights **dwell time** — how long a user pauses on your post without scrolling past. This is why:

- Native video on LinkedIn outperforms external links (external links get penalised in reach)
- Long-form text posts with "see more" taps drive dwell time signals
- Document/carousel posts (PDFs uploaded natively) have some of the highest organic reach on the platform
- Polls drive comment velocity without requiring high production effort

**LinkedIn Creator Mode:** Enabling Creator Mode switches your profile from "Connect" to "Follow" as the default CTA (Call to Action). This allows building large audiences without needing mutual connections. Combined with the Top Voice / Collaborative Articles program, it dramatically expands reach for thought leaders.

**B2B Algorithm Truth:** LinkedIn's algorithm still heavily rewards **human posts over company page posts**. Your CEO (Chief Executive Officer)'s personal post will outperform the company page post 5:1 on average (industry estimate). This is why LinkedIn strategy for B2B almost always involves activating employee advocacy.

## Section 3: Content Pillars & Editorial Calendar — Building the Machine

Going viral once is marketing luck. Building a sustainable social media presence requires a **content system** — and that system starts with content pillars.

### The Content Pillars Framework

Content pillars are the 3–5 recurring themes that define your brand's social media voice. They serve two purposes:

1. **Strategic:** They align content to business objectives
2. **Operational:** They make content planning dramatically faster

**The 5-Pillar Model (adapt to your brand):**

| Pillar | Purpose | Example (for a B2B SaaS) | Algorithm Signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Educational** | Build authority, capture search interest | "3 things most marketing teams get wrong about attribution" | Saves, shares |
| **Inspirational** | Emotional connection, shareability | Customer success stories, before/after | Shares, comments |
| **Promotional** | Direct conversion | Product demos, limited offers | Link clicks |
| **Entertaining** | Virality, brand personality | Behind-the-scenes, memes, relatable content | Shares, completion rate |
| **Community** | Retention, loyalty | UGC (User-Generated Content) reposts, customer spotlights, Q&A | Comments, DMs |

**The Golden Ratio:** Most successful brand accounts use approximately: 40% Educational + 30% Entertaining + 20% Inspirational + 10% Promotional. Heavy promotional posting kills organic reach across all platforms.

### Editorial Calendar Building

An editorial calendar answers three questions: What do we post? When? On which platform?

**Step 1: Establish your posting cadence**

| Platform | Recommended Cadence (brand accounts) | Minimum Viable |
|---|---|---|
| TikTok | 1–2 per day | 3× per week |
| Instagram Reels | 4–7 per week | 3× per week |
| Instagram Feed/Stories | Stories: daily; Feed: 3–4× weekly | 2× weekly |
| LinkedIn | 3–5× per week | 2× weekly |
| Facebook | 1× per day | 3× weekly |
| YouTube | 1× per week | 2× monthly |
| Pinterest | 5–15 pins per day (via scheduler) | 5× weekly |

**Step 2: Build your content mix using the Content Pillar framework**

Map each week's content slots to pillars. Example weekly calendar for a DTC brand:

| Day | Platform | Pillar | Content Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monday | Instagram Reels | Educational | "How to" tutorial |
| Tuesday | LinkedIn | Educational | Industry insight text post |
| Wednesday | TikTok | Entertaining | Trending audio + product |
| Thursday | Instagram Stories | Community | Poll + customer question |
| Friday | TikTok + Instagram Reels | Inspirational | Customer UGC repost |
| Saturday | Facebook | Promotional | Weekend offer |
| Sunday | Pinterest | Educational | Infographic |

**Step 3: Batch and schedule**

Tools for editorial calendar management:
- **Hootsuite / Buffer / Sprout Social** — Multi-platform scheduling with analytics
- **Later** — Best-in-class visual Instagram planner
- **Notion / Airtable** — Custom content calendar databases
- **Google Sheets** — Free, shareable, works well for small teams

**GDPR Compliance Note:** When scheduling posts that include user data (UGC, customer testimonials), ensure you have explicit written consent from the user before reposting. In the EU, this is not optional — it falls under Article 6(1)(a) of GDPR (consent as legal basis). Always document consent.

### UTM Parameter Tracking for Social

Every link you post on social media should include UTM parameters. Without them, Google Analytics 4 (and every other analytics platform) cannot differentiate traffic from Instagram vs TikTok vs LinkedIn.

**Standard UTM Structure for Social:**

```
utm_source = [platform] (instagram, tiktok, linkedin, facebook)
utm_medium = social
utm_campaign = [campaign name] (e.g., summer-sale-2025)
utm_content = [specific post identifier] (e.g., reel-15sec-v2)
```

**Example:**
```
https://yoursite.com/product?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=summer-sale-2025&utm_content=reel-15sec-v2
```

Build a UTM tracking spreadsheet and generate all UTMs before scheduling. This takes 5 minutes per post and saves hours of analytics confusion.

## Section 4: Organic Reach Reality Check — Creator vs. Brand Accounts

One of the most important — and most uncomfortable — truths in social media marketing:

> **Brand pages have dramatically lower organic reach than personal/creator accounts on every major platform.**

This is intentional. Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn all want brands to pay for reach. They've systematically reduced organic reach for business pages while preserving (or even boosting) reach for individual creator accounts.

**Approximate Organic Reach by Account Type (2024–2025, industry estimates):**

| Platform | Brand Page Organic Reach | Creator/Personal Account Organic Reach |
|---|---|---|
| Facebook | 1–4% of followers | 10–20% of followers |
| Instagram Feed | 2–6% of followers | 8–15% of followers |
| Instagram Reels | 3–8% of followers + discovery | 10–25% of followers + strong discovery |
| LinkedIn (company page) | 5–10% of followers | 20–40% of followers (via creator mode) |
| TikTok | Follower count largely irrelevant | Follower count largely irrelevant |

**Strategic Implication:** For most brands, **employee advocacy and executive thought leadership** on personal accounts drives more organic reach than the brand page. This is why "dark social" strategies — where individual employees post company content from personal accounts — are standard practice at most sophisticated B2B companies.

### UGC Strategy

User-Generated Content (UGC) is social proof amplified. When your customers create content featuring your product, three things happen simultaneously:

1. You get free content (cost = $0 to produce)
2. You get authentic social proof (peer recommendations convert at 5× the rate of brand claims, per Nielsen research)
3. The original creator's network becomes your distribution channel

**UGC Activation Tactics:**

- **Branded hashtag campaigns** — Create a campaign hashtag and incentivise use (#ShotoniPhone is the canonical example)
- **Review-to-content pipeline** — Convert 5-star reviews into quote graphics and Stories posts
- **Creator seeding** — Send product to micro-creators (1K–50K followers) without payment requirements, simply asking them to share if they love it
- **Customer showcase features** — Feature one customer/week in your Stories ("Community Spotlight")

**Legal Note for EU:** When reposting UGC in paid ads, you need explicit written permission — general consent via hashtag use is NOT sufficient under GDPR for paid media. Always get signed creator agreements before using UGC in paid campaigns.

## Section 5: Influencer Marketing — Tiers, Strategy, and Measurement

Influencer marketing is now a $21 billion+ industry globally (industry estimate, 2024). But most brands waste money by chasing follower count over engagement and audience fit.

### The Influencer Tier Framework

| Tier | Follower Range | Avg. Engagement Rate | Best Use Case | Cost Range (per post) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Nano** | 1K–10K | 4–8% | Hyper-local, niche authenticity, product seeding | $0–$250 |
| **Micro** | 10K–100K | 2–5% | Niche authority, high trust, best ROI | $250–$5,000 |
| **Macro** | 100K–1M | 1–3% | Brand awareness, broad reach | $5,000–$50,000 |
| **Mega/Celebrity** | 1M+ | 0.5–2% | Massive awareness, prestige association | $50,000–$1M+ |

**The Micro-Influencer Advantage:** Micro-influencers consistently deliver the best ROI for most brands because:
- Higher engagement rates (more authentic community)
- Lower cost per engagement
- Niche audiences that match specific buyer personas
- More willing to create authentic, unscripted content

**North America vs Europe Influencer Markets:**

| Market | Key Difference |
|---|---|
| **US** | Largest market; TikTok and Instagram dominant; disclosure rules (FTC) require clear #ad or #sponsored tagging |
| **Canada** | Follows US FTC-style standards; bilingual English/French market for Quebec campaigns |
| **UK** | ASA (Advertising Standards Authority) requires clear disclosure; Instagram and TikTok dominant |
| **Germany** | Extremely strict influencer disclosure laws; court cases have fined creators for insufficient disclosure; LinkedIn influencers more prominent in B2B |
| **France** | 2023 French influencer law among strictest globally — requires written contract, health product restrictions, mandatory "virtual content" labels for AI-altered images |
| **Netherlands** | ACM (Authority for Consumers & Markets) enforces disclosure; high TikTok and Instagram penetration |

**Measurement Framework for Influencer Campaigns:**

1. **Awareness metrics:** Reach, impressions, CPM (Cost Per Mille) (compare to your paid media benchmarks)
2. **Engagement metrics:** Engagement rate, comments quality (are they discussing the product?)
3. **Traffic metrics:** UTM-tagged clicks to landing page, session quality
4. **Conversion metrics:** Promo code redemptions, affiliate link clicks, direct attribution

Always provide influencers with unique UTM parameters and promo codes. Without these, you cannot measure ROI beyond engagement.

### Social Listening Tools

Social listening is the practice of monitoring brand mentions, competitor activity, and industry conversations across social media. It is the difference between reacting to a crisis 3 days later vs. 3 hours later.

**Core Social Listening Platforms:**

| Tool | Best For | Price Range |
|---|---|---|
| **Hootsuite** | Full-suite monitoring + scheduling integration | $99–$739/month |
| **Sprout Social** | Advanced analytics + enterprise reporting | $249–$499+/month |
| **Brand24** | Affordable real-time mentions for SMBs | $79–$399/month |
| **Mention** | European-market-friendly; GDPR-compliant data handling | $41–$83/month |
| **Brandwatch** | Enterprise intelligence, deep analytics | Custom pricing |
| **Talkwalker** | Strong European market coverage + image recognition | Custom pricing |

**GDPR Note:** Social listening tools that store EU citizen data must be GDPR-compliant. Check your vendor's Data Processing Agreement (DPA) before deploying in European markets. Tools with EU data residency options include Mention and Talkwalker.

## Section 6: Social Commerce — Instagram Shopping & TikTok Shop

Social commerce — the ability to discover and purchase products without leaving a social platform — is the fastest-growing segment of e-commerce.

**Global social commerce GMV (Gross Merchandise Value) (industry estimates):**
- 2022: ~$724 billion (primarily China-driven via WeChat/Douyin)
- 2024: ~$1.1 trillion globally
- North America 2024: ~$67 billion
- Europe 2024: ~$32 billion

### Instagram Shopping

Instagram Shopping allows brands to tag products in posts, Reels, and Stories. Users can browse a product catalogue and purchase without leaving Instagram (via Instagram Checkout, available in US/UK/Canada).

**Setup Requirements:**
- Connect Facebook Commerce Manager
- Upload product catalogue (via Shopify, WooCommerce, or direct upload)
- Enable Instagram Shopping in Business settings
- Submit for review (typically 24–72 hours)

**Key Instagram Shopping Features:**
- **Product tags in Reels/posts** — Shoppable content
- **Shop tab** — Dedicated storefront on your profile
- **Live Shopping** — Real-time product showcasing during Instagram Live
- **Collab posts** — Tag influencers as collaborators for co-branded shoppable content

### TikTok Shop

TikTok Shop launched in the UK (2021), Southeast Asia, then US (2023). It is the most aggressive social commerce push outside China.

**TikTok Shop Features:**
- **In-feed shoppable videos** — Products tagged in organic and paid videos
- **Live Shopping** — Hosts demonstrate and sell in real-time (modelled on Chinese Douyin)
- **Product showcase** — Mini storefront on creator/brand profile
- **Affiliate program** — Creators earn commission for promoting products through TikTok Shop

**Availability:** TikTok Shop currently available in: US, UK, Germany, France, Italy, Spain, and Southeast Asia markets. Expanding to Netherlands and Nordics (in progress as of 2025).

> **Common Trap:** Treating TikTok Shop as a traditional ad channel. TikTok Shop converts through **entertainment + demonstration**, not traditional product ads. The most successful TikTok Shop sellers are basically running a live TV shopping channel with creators.

## ⚠️ Anti-Patterns to Avoid

1. **Posting identical content across all platforms simultaneously.** Instagram and LinkedIn have completely different audiences, content norms, and algorithm signals. A LinkedIn document carousel performs brilliantly; the same content as an Instagram post performs terribly. Adapt content for each platform.

2. **Chasing follower count over engagement rate.** A brand with 10,000 highly engaged followers converts better than a brand with 500,000 passive followers. Engagement rate is the real health metric.

3. **Going dark for weeks, then posting in bursts.** Every major social algorithm rewards consistency. Irregular posting destroys distribution reach. Better to post 3× per week consistently than 20 posts in one week and nothing for the next month.

4. **Not tracking UTM parameters on social links.** If you can't prove which platform drove the sale, you cannot make informed budget allocation decisions. UTMs take 2 minutes to set up per post.

5. **Ignoring comments and DMs.** Community management is not optional. Platforms algorithmically reward accounts that generate conversation — and punish accounts where comments get no response. Unresponded comments signal that your community isn't real.

6. **Reposting TikTok content to Instagram with the TikTok watermark.** Instagram's algorithm actively detects and demotes watermarked TikTok reposts. Always use a watermark-removal tool (SnapTik, Snaptik.app) or re-edit natively for Instagram.

7. **Using social media without a content calendar.** Reactive posting without a content strategy produces inconsistent brand messaging, missed editorial opportunities, and burnout for social media managers. Even a simple Google Sheets calendar eliminates 80% of the chaos.

## 🎯 Key Formulas / Frameworks (Memorize These)

**Engagement Rate Formula:**
```
ER = (Total Engagements / Total Reach) × 100
```
*Engagements = likes + comments + shares + saves. Calculate on reach (not followers) for accuracy.*

**The A-O-F Platform Selection Framework:**
```
Platform Score = Audience Fit (1/0) + Objective Fit (1/0) + Format Fit (1/0)
Only invest in platforms scoring 2/3 or higher
```

**Content Pillar Ratio (Golden Ratio):**
```
40% Educational + 30% Entertaining + 20% Inspirational + 10% Promotional
```

**Influencer ROI Formula:**
```
Influencer ROI = (Revenue Attributed / Total Spend) × 100
Track via: UTM links + unique promo codes
```

**TikTok Algorithm Checkpoints:**
```
300–500 users → 65%+ completion → Next pool
Next pool → 65%+ completion → Wider distribution
Wider distribution → Engagement velocity → Trending
```

**UTM Structure:**
```
?utm_source=[platform]&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=[name]&utm_content=[post-id]
```

**Organic Reach Decline Rate (rule of thumb):**
```
Facebook brand pages: ~1–4% organic reach of followers
Instagram brand pages: ~2–6% organic reach of followers
LinkedIn company pages: ~5–10% organic reach of followers
TikTok: follower count largely irrelevant (interest graph)
```

**Influencer Tier Selection Rule:**
```
Best ROI: Micro (10K–100K) for most brands
Best Awareness: Macro/Mega for launch moments only
Best Authenticity: Nano + Micro combined (always outperforms macro on engagement)
```

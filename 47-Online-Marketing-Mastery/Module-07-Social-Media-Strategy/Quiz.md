## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **D. Audience, Objectives, Format**
A-O-F = **A**udience + **O**bjective + **F**ormat. These three factors together determine platform fit.

### Q2: **C. Skip the platform and focus on higher-scoring alternatives**
A score of 1/3 means only one of three critical factors is satisfied. Skip the platform and invest in higher-scoring alternatives.

### Q3: **B. TikTok uses an interest graph; Facebook uses a social graph**
TikTok uses an interest graph (follower count barely matters; distribution is driven by predicted user interest), whereas Facebook uses a social graph (connections and relationships drive distribution).

### Q4: **D. 300–500 users**
TikTok first tests new videos with approximately 300–500 random users before deciding whether to push them to larger cohorts.

### Q5: **B. Completion rate and shares**
Completion rate (did they watch the whole video?) and shares (especially DM shares and saves) are TikTok's most powerful signals. Likes are the weakest signal because they are the easiest to game.

### Q6: **C. Entertainment score including completion rate, replays, and shares from non-followers**
Instagram's Reels algorithm scores "entertainment value" via completion rate, replay rate, and shares from users who do not follow the account.

### Q7: **A. Instagram's algorithm detects and demotes the watermarked content**
Instagram actively detects watermarked TikTok content and algorithmically demotes it, so always remove the watermark before cross-posting.

### Q8: **D. Dwell time — how long users pause on a post**
LinkedIn's ranking algorithm heavily weights dwell time — how long a user pauses on a post. This is why native long-form content outperforms external links.

### Q9: **C. Native document/carousel posts (PDFs uploaded natively)**
Native document/carousel posts (PDFs uploaded directly to LinkedIn) earn among the highest organic reach because users swipe through slides, driving high dwell time.

### Q10: **A. LinkedIn**
The Netherlands has one of the highest LinkedIn penetration rates globally (>50% of the working population), so for a professional B2B audience LinkedIn is the correct primary platform.

### Q11: **D. 1–4%**
Facebook brand page organic reach is approximately 1–4% of followers — a deliberately restricted figure designed to push brands toward paid advertising.

### Q12: **B. Obtain explicit written permission from the creator — hashtag consent is not sufficient for EU paid ads under GDPR**
Under GDPR Article 6, using someone's content or image in paid advertising requires explicit written consent as the legal basis. A general participation hashtag is not sufficient for paid media in the EU.

### Q13: **A. Micro-influencers (10K–100K followers) because of higher engagement rates, lower cost, and niche audience alignment**
Micro-influencers (10K–100K) consistently deliver the best ROI: higher engagement rates than macro/mega, lower cost per engagement, and niche audiences aligned to specific buyer personas.

### Q14: **A. The brand may face legal liability for insufficient disclosure under Germany's strict influencer advertising laws, which have resulted in court fines**
Germany has among the strictest influencer disclosure laws in Europe; courts have fined both influencers and brands for insufficient disclosure, so both parties share legal risk.

### Q15: **D. 10%**
The Golden Ratio recommends only 10% promotional content: 40% Educational + 30% Entertaining + 20% Inspirational + 10% Promotional.

### Q16: **C. utm_content**
utm_content identifies the specific creative or post (e.g., "reel-15sec-v2"). utm_source = platform, utm_medium = channel type, utm_campaign = campaign name.

### Q17: **C. Algorithm consistency rewards steady posting — sporadic bursts followed by silence damage distribution reach**
All major social algorithms reward consistency. Burst posting followed by silence damages distribution because the algorithm deprioritises accounts with irregular activity.

### Q18: **D. Entertainment + demonstration — essentially a live TV shopping format with creators**
TikTok Shop converts through entertainment and live demonstration — modelled on China's Douyin live-commerce format — which is fundamentally different from catalogue-based platforms like Amazon.

### Q19: **B. All paid partnerships and gifted products must be clearly disclosed with terms like #ad or #gifted**
The UK's ASA requires clear disclosure of all paid partnerships and gifted products; #ad and #gifted are the standard disclosure terms, and cosmetics are not exempt.

### Q20: **B. Mention**
Mention is specifically noted for European-market-friendly operation and GDPR-compliant data handling. (Talkwalker also offers EU data residency but at custom enterprise pricing.)

### Q21: **C. Facebook deliberately restricts brand page reach to drive paid advertising revenue; brands should therefore invest in employee advocacy and executive personal accounts alongside brand pages**
Facebook deliberately restricts brand page reach to drive paid-ad revenue. The strategic response is to invest in employee advocacy and executive thought leadership (personal accounts retain higher reach) alongside the brand page.

### Q22: **B. LinkedIn's algorithm actively demotes external links to keep users on-platform; native video receives far higher organic reach**
LinkedIn actively demotes external links (to YouTube, websites, etc.) to keep users on-platform, so native video earns dramatically higher organic reach.

### Q23: **A. Save rate and engagement rate relative to reach (not just raw like counts)**
Raw likes are the weakest, most misleading engagement signal. A savvy analyst asks about save rate (the strongest Instagram quality signal) and engagement rate relative to reach (not just followers).

### Q24: **A. AI-altered images must be labelled with a "virtual content" disclosure**
France's 2023 influencer law requires AI-altered images in commercial content to carry a "virtual content" / "retouched image" label — one of the first laws globally to mandate this.

### Q25: **D. They are missing the promotional pillar entirely — without promotional content, followers never receive calls-to-action that drive conversion, even though 10% promotional content is appropriate in the Golden Ratio**
Without any promotional content, even a well-balanced editorial calendar never drives followers to take action. The Golden Ratio still allots ~10% to promotional posts; even at low frequency, CTAs are essential for conversion.

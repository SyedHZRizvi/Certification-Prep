## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **D. Account > Campaign > Ad Group > Keyword/Ad**
Account > Campaign > Ad Group > Keyword/Ad is the correct four-level hierarchy. Budget is set at the Campaign level; keywords and ads live at the Ad Group level and below.

### Q2: **C. The daily budget and geographic targeting**
Daily budget and geographic targeting are Campaign-level settings. Keywords live at the Ad Group level; ad copy and landing page URLs are set at the ad or keyword level.

### Q3: **B. Both B and C**
Phrase match `"red rose bouquet"` requires those words in that order (with other words permitted around them), and broad match `red roses` can match related terms including "red rose bouquet." Exact match `[red rose bouquet free delivery]` would NOT match because it requires that exact phrase.

### Q4: **A. Phrase Match**
Google officially merged BMM behavior into Phrase Match in August 2021. Phrase Match now includes the keyword's terms in any order (close to BMM behavior) in addition to its traditional phrase-order matching.

### Q5: **C. `[flowers delivered today]`**
Exact Match uses square brackets: `[keyword]`. Phrase Match uses quotes: `"keyword"`. Option C was the old BMM syntax (now retired).

### Q6: **C. "flower delivery next day"**
Phrase Match requires the words "next day flower delivery" to appear in the order specified. "flower delivery next day" reverses the order and would NOT match phrase match. Options A, B, and C all include the phrase in the correct order.

### Q7: **D. Add "classes" and "arranging" as negative keywords**
The correct action is to add irrelevant terms as negative keywords. "Classes" and "arranging" are intent signals for people wanting to learn — not buy. Deleting the keyword entirely (D) is too aggressive; switching entirely to exact match (C) forecloses discovery.

### Q8: **A. 1 to 10**
Quality Score is rated 1 to 10 (not 0 to 10). A score of 1 is the worst; 10 is the best.

### Q9: **B. Expected Click-Through Rate**
Expected Click-Through Rate (CTR) carries approximately 55% of the Quality Score weighting, making it the most influential component. Ad Relevance (~22%) and Landing Page Experience (~22%) share the remainder.

### Q10: **D. The landing page does not load quickly or is not relevant to the keyword**
"Below Average" Landing Page Experience is almost always caused by slow page load speed, content not matching the keyword's intent, poor mobile experience, or a high bounce rate signaling mismatch between ad and page. Bid amount (A) has no effect on Quality Score.

### Q11: **A. A: 15, B: 27 — Advertiser B wins**
Ad Rank = Max Bid × Quality Score. A: £5 × 3 = 15. B: £3 × 9 = 27. Advertiser B wins the auction with Ad Rank 27 despite bidding less per click — demonstrating the power of Quality Score.

### Q12: **C. (Competitor's Ad Rank below you ÷ Your Quality Score) + £0.01**
The Actual CPC formula is: (Ad Rank of the advertiser immediately below you ÷ Your Quality Score) + £0.01. You never pay your Max Bid unless no one is competing.

### Q13: **D. Manual CPC or Maximize Clicks with a budget cap**
Smart Bidding (tCPA, tROAS, Maximize Conversions) requires conversion history to function. A brand new campaign with zero conversions should start with Manual CPC or Maximize Clicks (with a budget cap) to build data first.

### Q14: **B. Target CPA (tCPA) set to £40**
tCPA is designed exactly for this scenario: a fixed cost-per-lead target with enough conversion volume (the recommended minimum is 30+ conversions/month; 55 qualifies). Setting it at £40 tells Google to optimize for leads at that cost.

### Q15: **A. Target ROAS — because it optimizes for conversion value relative to spend**
Target ROAS optimizes for conversion VALUE (total revenue) relative to spend — perfect when products have widely varying prices. Target CPA (A) ignores the value of each conversion. Maximize Clicks (D) ignores conversions entirely.

### Q16: **B. 15**
RSAs allow up to 15 headlines (max 30 characters each) and up to 4 descriptions (max 90 characters each). Google tests combinations and shows the best-performing sets.

### Q17: **D. Write each headline to be independently meaningful and conceptually diverse**
Best practice is conceptual diversity: each headline should communicate a different angle (price, urgency, feature, social proof, CTA). If all headlines say the same thing, Google's combinations will be repetitive and ad strength will be rated "Poor."

### Q18: **C. Callout Asset**
Callout Assets are short non-clickable text snippets (max 25 characters) that add persuasive phrases like "Free UK Delivery" or "Award-Winning Florist." Sitelinks are clickable, Call assets connect to a phone number, and Price assets link to products/prices.

### Q19: **A. Call Asset**
Call Asset adds a phone number to the ad, enabling tap-to-call on mobile. Location Asset shows an address, Structured Snippet lists features, and Lead Form collects data within Google.

### Q20: **B. Valid user consent must be obtained; implement a CMP with Consent Mode v2**
Under GDPR (EU) and UK GDPR, deploying tracking/analytics cookies requires valid consent from users; advertising pixels are not exempt. The correct implementation is a Consent Management Platform (CMP) using Google Consent Mode v2, which adjusts tag behavior based on consent status.

### Q21: **B. PMax runs across all Google networks with AI-driven placement; Search runs only on the Search network with more advertiser control**
Performance Max runs across Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Discover, and Maps simultaneously with Google's AI controlling placement. Standard Search runs only on the Search network and gives advertisers full control over keywords, match types, and bids.

### Q22: **A. Add "wholesale," "bulk," and "trade" as negative keywords at the campaign level**
"Wholesale," "bulk," and "trade" are clear intent signals for B2B/reseller buyers, not the consumer audience the advertiser wants. Adding them as campaign-level negative keywords immediately stops spend waste. Creating an ad group for them (C) would still spend on the wrong audience.

### Q23: **D. Create tighter ad groups with 3-5 keywords per group, write ad copy that includes the keyword in headlines, and send clicks to dedicated product category landing pages**
Improving Quality Score requires tighter ad groups (better Ad Relevance), keyword inclusion in headlines (better Expected CTR), and intent-matched landing pages (better Landing Page Experience). Increasing bids (A) does not affect QS, and switching to broad match (C) makes the relevance problem worse.

### Q24: **C. They are losing 45% of eligible impressions due to insufficient budget; increasing budget or reducing bids can recapture this share**
Search Lost IS (Budget) is the percentage of auctions where the ad was eligible but did NOT show because the daily budget ran out. At 45%, nearly half of potential impressions are being lost to budget constraints, not quality issues. Increasing the budget or reducing bids to stretch it further are both valid remedies.

### Q25: **C. Multiple campaigns organized by business objective (e.g., "Gifting," "Sympathy," "Subscription"), each with intent-themed ad groups (3-7 keywords), matched to specific landing pages**
Multiple campaigns organized by business objective (gifting, sympathy, subscription), each with intent-themed ad groups of 3-7 tightly related keywords matched to specific landing pages, is the gold-standard STAG (Single Theme Ad Group) structure. Option A (one ad group for everything) destroys relevance; Option D (all broad match from day one) is dangerous without conversion data.

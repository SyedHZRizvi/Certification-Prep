## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **A. Campaign → Ad Set → Ad**
The correct hierarchy is Campaign (objective) → Ad Set (audience/budget/placement) → Ad (creative). "Ad Group" is Google's terminology, not Meta's.

### Q2: **D. Sales — because this objective tells Meta to find users likely to complete a purchase**
The Sales objective tells Meta to optimize delivery for users likely to complete a purchase event on your website. Traffic optimizes for clicks only.

### Q3: **C. It distributes your campaign budget across ad sets automatically to maximize results**
CBO (now called Advantage Campaign Budget) automatically distributes the campaign-level budget across ad sets to maximize overall campaign results.

### Q4: **A. It tracks user actions on your website and sends event data back to Meta for targeting and attribution**
The Pixel fires JavaScript events from the user's browser and sends them back to Meta, enabling remarketing, conversion tracking, and attribution.

### Q5: **C. ClickThrough**
"ClickThrough" is not a standard Meta event. Standard events include Purchase, AddToCart, ViewContent, Lead, CompleteRegistration, InitiateCheckout, and Search.

### Q6: **D. iOS 14.5 introduced App Tracking Transparency (ATT), allowing users to opt out of cross-app tracking, which broke browser-based Pixel tracking**
iOS 14.5 introduced ATT; users who opted out of tracking could no longer be tracked via the browser-based Pixel, causing reported conversions to drop sharply even if actual sales continued.

### Q7: **B. A server-side tracking solution that sends event data directly from your server to Meta, bypassing browser-based tracking limitations like iOS ATT**
CAPI sends event data from the advertiser's server (not the user's browser) directly to Meta, bypassing ATT and browser privacy restrictions.

### Q8: **C. Meta uses the `event_id` parameter to deduplicate events from both sources**
Meta uses the `event_id` parameter (sent by both the Pixel and CAPI) to identify and deduplicate events, preventing double-counting. Advertisers must ensure unique event_ids are generated.

### Q9: **C. The advertiser must have valid legal consent from customers to use their data for marketing purposes, and this use must be disclosed in a privacy policy**
Under UK GDPR (retained post-Brexit) and EU GDPR, uploading customer data to Meta as a Custom Audience requires a valid legal basis (typically consent for marketing) and disclosure in the privacy policy.

### Q10: **B. Meta's privacy framework for tracking conversions from Apple devices, limiting advertisers to 8 prioritized events per domain**
AEM is Meta's privacy framework introduced after iOS 14.5, limiting advertisers to 8 prioritized conversion events per verified domain for attribution on Apple devices.

### Q11: **B. Purchase, AddToCart, InitiateCheckout, ViewContent**
Purchase is highest priority (the most valuable event), followed by AddToCart, InitiateCheckout, ViewContent. Under AEM, if a user triggers Purchase, only Purchase is credited.

### Q12: **A. 100 people**
Meta requires a minimum of 100 people in a seed audience to create a Lookalike, but recommends 1,000–50,000 for quality results.

### Q13: **D. The 1% of Facebook users in the UK who are most similar to the seed audience — approximately 400,000–700,000 people**
A 1% Lookalike in the UK finds the 1% of UK Meta users most similar to the seed audience — typically several hundred thousand people, not a copy of the seed list.

### Q14: **A. It allows Meta's algorithm to show your ad to audiences beyond your specified targeting parameters if it predicts better results**
Advantage+ Audience allows Meta's algorithm to expand targeting beyond the specified parameters when it predicts better conversion outcomes.

### Q15: **C. Prospecting (finding new customers) and retargeting (reaching existing customers/visitors)**
ASC combines prospecting (finding new customers) and retargeting (reaching people who have previously interacted) into one campaign, with Meta controlling the budget split via AI.

### Q16: **C. Rotate in new creative variations, expand the audience, or create a new ad set with fresh creatives**
A frequency of 4.2 for a cold audience indicates creative fatigue. The correct response is to rotate creatives, expand the audience, or introduce fresh ad sets. Pausing 30 days is extreme and unnecessary.

### Q17: **B. 50 conversions**
Meta recommends 50 conversions per ad set per week to exit the learning phase. Below this threshold, the algorithm cannot gather enough data to optimize efficiently.

### Q18: **B. No — the 1-day view window expired after Tuesday, and there was no click to extend it to the 7-day click window**
The view occurred on Monday and the 1-day view window expired on Tuesday. There was no click, so no 7-day click window is open. The Sunday purchase falls outside both windows and is not attributed.

### Q19: **D. Carousel ad — allows multiple cards with different images, headlines, and links**
Carousel ads allow 2–10 cards, each with its own image, headline, description, and URL — ideal for showcasing a multi-step tutorial or multiple product features sequentially.

### Q20: **D. CAPI still involves sending personal data (hashed emails, IPs) to Meta, so a valid legal basis (typically consent) and a Data Processing Agreement with Meta are required**
CAPI involves transmitting personal data (including hashed PII) from the advertiser's server to Meta. This constitutes data processing under GDPR, requiring a legal basis (typically consent), a DPA with Meta, and proper disclosure.

### Q21: **A. A score (1–10) indicating how well CAPI event data matches to Meta user profiles, affecting attribution accuracy**
EMQ is a score (1–10) reflecting how accurately CAPI event data can be matched to a Meta user profile. Higher EMQ means better attribution. Passing more hashed parameters (email, phone, name, city) improves EMQ.

### Q22: **A. Use Meta's built-in Experiment tool with one variable (creative) changing between the two ad sets, ensuring mutual audience exclusion**
A valid A/B test changes only ONE variable (creative here) and uses Meta's Experiment tool, which enforces mutual audience exclusion so the same user cannot see both variants and contaminate results.

### Q23: **B. ROAS will increase significantly because CAPI tracks more conversions that the Pixel was missing**
After CAPI is implemented, Meta receives conversion signals it previously could not see (from users whose browsers blocked the Pixel). This typically increases reported conversions and ROAS. Deduplication via event_id prevents double-counting.

### Q24: **A. An ad that automatically pulls product images, names, and prices from a Meta product catalogue to show relevant items to each user — especially used for retargeting based on browsing behaviour**
Dynamic Ads automatically pull from a Meta product catalogue to show personalized product recommendations — most commonly used to retarget users who viewed specific products on your website without purchasing.

### Q25: **D. Meta Pixel + Conversions API (with event deduplication), domain verification, AEM with 8 prioritized events, IAB TCF-compliant consent banner, and 7-day click / 1-day view attribution window**
The gold-standard stack combines Pixel + CAPI (deduplicated), domain verification, AEM with 8 prioritized events, a compliant consent management platform (IAB TCF for the EU), and a 7-day click / 1-day view attribution window aligned to Meta's current model.

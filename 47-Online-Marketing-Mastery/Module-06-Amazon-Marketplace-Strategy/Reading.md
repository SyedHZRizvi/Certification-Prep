# Module 06: Amazon Marketplace Strategy — US, UK & Europe 🌍

> **Why this module matters:** Amazon accounts for roughly 40% of all US e-commerce and is the #1 product search engine on Earth — more shoppers start a product search on Amazon than on Google. Selling well on Amazon is no longer optional for consumer brands; it is the difference between a business that scales and one that stagnates.

> **Prerequisites:** You should already understand basic e-commerce concepts (SKUs, fulfilment, conversion rates) and have completed Module 05 (Amazon Sponsored Advertising). Familiarity with the concept of a "marketplace" vs. a direct-to-consumer store will accelerate your learning here.

## ☕ A Story: The $120,000 Vitamin Mistake

In the spring of 2022, a mid-sized American supplements brand — call them PureVitamin Co. — decided to ride the post-pandemic health wave into Europe. They had crushed it on Amazon US. Their whey protein ranked on page one for seventeen competitive keywords. Their founder had appeared on a podcast. Life was good.

Their German marketplace launch plan was bold: list all 47 SKUs on Amazon.de, enroll in Pan-European FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon), and let Amazon's logistics network handle the rest. They translated their US listings using Google Translate. They shipped $120,000 of inventory to Amazon's fulfilment centre in Rheinberg, Germany.

Then came the email from Amazon EU.

It arrived on a Tuesday morning at 4 AM Pacific Time — which meant the German compliance team had been sitting on it for hours. The subject line read: *"Immediate removal of ASINs due to regulatory non-compliance."* Forty-three of their 47 SKUs had been taken down.

**What went wrong? Three simultaneous disasters:**

**Disaster #1 — The Labelling Law.** Germany requires all food supplements to carry German-language labels that comply with the German Food and Feed Code (*Lebensmittel- und Futtermittelgesetzbuch*, or LFGB). The US labels, even translated, didn't include the mandatory EU nutrition reference values (NRVs), the correct allergen formatting under EU Regulation 1169/2011, or the German importer's address. Amazon.de requires proof of compliance before listing dietary supplements. PureVitamin had uploaded the US labels and assumed "translated = compliant." It was not.

**Disaster #2 — The Backend Keywords.** Their listing titles were copied verbatim from Amazon US: "Best Whey Protein Powder Supplement 5lbs — Vanilla — Build Muscle Fast." German search behaviour is completely different. German shoppers search for *"Whey Protein Pulver"* not *"Whey Protein Powder"*. The backend keyword fields were filled with English terms. They were invisible in German search results even on the listings that survived.

**Disaster #3 — VAT Registration.** They had never registered for German VAT. Under EU rules, any seller storing goods in Germany (via FBA fulfilment centres) must register for VAT in Germany regardless of their home country. They owed German VAT from the first sale. When Amazon discovered the non-compliance, it withheld remittances pending resolution. It took four months and €18,000 in accountancy fees to sort out.

Total cost: $120,000 in stranded inventory fees, removal and disposal costs, re-labelling expenses, and lost revenue during the four months of downtime. The founder later described it as "the most expensive MBA class I ever took."

**The lesson is not "don't go to Europe."** The lesson is: **Europe is a different marketplace ecosystem, not just a different language.** The compliance rules, search behaviours, customer expectations, and fulfilment economics are genuinely distinct from North America. This module is the MBA class PureVitamin paid $120,000 to learn — for free.

## Section 1: Understanding the Amazon Marketplace Ecosystem — US, UK & EU

### The Scale Reality

Amazon operates distinct marketplace domains that are essentially separate businesses sharing a technology platform. As of 2025 (industry estimates):

| Marketplace | Domain | GMV (Gross Merchandise Value) Estimate | Primary Currency | VAT/Sales Tax |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| United States | amazon.com | ~$400B | USD | Sales tax (varies by state) |
| United Kingdom | amazon.co.uk | ~£30B | GBP | 20% VAT |
| Germany | amazon.de | ~€35B | EUR | 19% VAT |
| France | amazon.fr | ~€12B | EUR | 20% VAT |
| Italy | amazon.it | ~€8B | EUR | 22% VAT |
| Spain | amazon.es | ~€7B | EUR | 21% VAT |
| Netherlands | amazon.nl | ~€4B | EUR | 21% BTW |
| Canada | amazon.ca | ~CAD 12B | CAD | HST/GST varies |

**Key strategic insight:** Amazon.de is the dominant EU marketplace. If you can only pick one EU market to enter first, it is Germany — not the UK (post-Brexit complexity) and not France (strong domestic brand loyalty). The UK is attractive for English-language sellers but is now a third country relative to the EU27, meaning separate VAT registration, customs declarations, and inventory pools.

### The Three-Domain Mental Model

Think of Amazon's global presence in three clusters:

**Cluster 1 — North America (NACS):** US + Canada + Mexico. Inventory can be shared via the North America Remote Fulfilment (NARF) programme. A unified account manages all three.

**Cluster 2 — Europe (EFN / Pan-EU):** UK + DE + FR + IT + ES + NL + SE + PL. Since Brexit, the UK is operationally separate from the EU27 cluster for customs purposes, but the Amazon Seller Central account can be linked.

**Cluster 3 — Rest of World:** Japan, Australia, India, UAE, etc. Out of scope for this module.

> **Common trap:** Sellers assume "I have a US account, so I just tick a box to sell in Europe." In reality, you need a separate Amazon Europe Marketplace account (or linked unified account), separate bank accounts in local currencies, and separate VAT registrations in each country where you store inventory.

## Section 2: Amazon Listing Optimisation — The TITLE-BULLETS-BACKEND Framework

### The A10 Algorithm — What Actually Drives Rankings

Amazon's search algorithm (colloquially called A9, now evolving toward A10) is fundamentally a **revenue maximisation engine for Amazon**, not a pure relevance engine. It ranks listings that will generate the most revenue per search impression. That means:

1. **Sales velocity** — the more you sell, the higher you rank (virtuous cycle)
2. **Conversion rate (CVR)** — listings that convert better rank higher
3. **Click-through rate (CTR)** — the listing's thumbnail/title that gets clicked more ranks higher
4. **Keyword relevance** — the algorithm must index your listing for a keyword before it can rank you
5. **Seller authority** — account health, review score, fulfillment method
6. **Price competitiveness** — relative to category average

The A10 evolution specifically added heavier weighting to **organic sales** (vs. PPC-driven sales), **off-Amazon traffic** (brand referral bonus programme), and **customer repurchase signals** (Subscribe & Save uptake).

**The mnemonic: S-C-C-K-A-P** (Sales, Conversion, Click-through, Keywords, Authority, Price) — "Some Competitors Chase Keyword Arbitrage Pointlessly."

### Listing Anatomy — The TITLE Formula

Amazon's title is your #1 indexing and CTR (Click-Through Rate) lever. US marketplace allows up to 200 characters; EU marketplaces typically cap at 80–150 visible characters.

**The BRAND-KEYWORD-FEATURE-SIZE-VARIANT formula:**

```
[Brand] + [Primary Keyword] + [Key Feature 1] + [Key Feature 2] + [Size/Count] + [Variant]
```

**Example (US):**
> "NutriForce Whey Protein Powder — 25g Protein Per Serving, No Artificial Sweeteners, Supports Muscle Recovery — 5 lbs, Vanilla"

**Example (DE — must be in German):**
> "NutriForce Whey Protein Pulver — 25g Protein pro Portion, ohne Süßungsmittel, unterstützt Muskelregeneration — 2,27 kg, Vanille"

Note the weight conversion (5 lbs → 2.27 kg) — Europeans do not understand imperial units. Listing in pounds is a conversion killer.

### Bullet Points — The FISH Framework

Five bullet points. Each should hit one benefit using the **FISH** structure:

| Letter | Stands For | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **F** | Feature | "FAST-ABSORBING WHEY ISOLATE" |
| **I** | Impact (what it does) | "Delivers 25g of protein within 30 minutes of consumption" |
| **S** | Social proof / Specificity | "Trusted by 50,000+ athletes across 38 countries" |
| **H** | How-to / Use case | "Mix 1 scoop with 300ml cold water or milk post-workout" |

**Capitalise the first 2-3 words** of each bullet — Amazon renders them in plain text and the caps act as visual sub-headers on mobile.

**EU-specific bullets:** In Germany, health claims on food supplements are governed by the EU Health Claims Regulation (EC 1924/2006). You CANNOT write "Builds Muscle" without an approved EU health claim. You CAN write "Contributes to normal muscle function" (this is an approved claim for Vitamin D). Violating this gets your ASIN (Amazon Standard Identification Number) suppressed or your account warned.

### The Product Description and A+ Content

The product description (the long-form HTML text below the fold) is indexed but rarely read. **If you have Brand Registry, use A+ Content instead** — it replaces the description with rich media modules.

A+ Content impact (Amazon internal data, cited by Amazon Seller University 2024):
- Average conversion uplift: **3–10%** on well-optimised listings
- Premium A+ Content (available to sellers with 5+ A+ modules and high brand scores): average uplift of **20%**
- A+ Content is **not** indexed for keywords — its value is purely conversion, not SEO (Search Engine Optimization)

**A+ Content best practices:**

1. Module 1: Lifestyle hero image — show the product in use, not on a white background
2. Module 2: Comparison chart — compare your SKUs to each other (NOT competitors, per policy)
3. Module 3: Ingredient/material callout — 3-4 feature icons with short copy
4. Module 4: Brand story — origin, mission, differentiation
5. Module 5: Social proof / awards

### Backend Search Terms — The Hidden Engine

The backend keywords field (250 bytes in the US, often less in EU) is invisible to shoppers but critical for indexing. Rules:

- **No repetition** — repeating a keyword wastes space and does not increase weight
- **No commas** — separate terms with spaces, not commas (Amazon treats commas as characters)
- **No brand names of competitors** — policy violation
- **Include synonyms, misspellings, Spanish/French terms** on US listings where relevant
- **EU listings:** Include the local language synonyms AND common English-language search terms (many German shoppers search in English for supplements and electronics)

| Field | US Limit | UK Limit | DE/FR/IT/ES Limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Title | 200 chars | 200 chars | 80-150 chars (category-dependent) |
| Bullet points | 5 x 500 chars | 5 x 500 chars | 5 x 500 chars |
| Backend keywords | 250 bytes | 250 bytes | 250 bytes |
| Product description | 2,000 chars | 2,000 chars | 2,000 chars |

## Section 3: Fulfilment — FBA vs FBM (Fulfillment by Merchant) vs SFP

### The Three Fulfilment Models

This is the most operationally consequential decision an Amazon seller makes. The wrong choice kills margins; the right choice wins the Buy Box.

| Dimension | FBA | FBM | SFP (Seller Fulfilled Prime) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Who picks/packs/ships | Amazon | You | You |
| Prime badge eligibility | Yes (automatic) | No | Yes (if approved) |
| Buy Box advantage | High | Low (unless price competitive) | High |
| Storage fees | Yes (ongoing + long-term) | No | No |
| FBA fulfilment fee (avg) | $3.22–$5.42 per unit (US, 2025 est.) | N/A | N/A |
| Your shipping cost | Included in FBA fee | You pay carrier | You pay carrier |
| Customer service | Amazon handles | You handle | You handle |
| Returns | Amazon handles | You handle | Amazon policy, you process |
| Best for | Standard-velocity, non-oversize goods | Large/heavy/low-velocity goods | Sellers with premium warehouse ops |

**SFP is the most demanding model.** Amazon requires 99%+ on-time delivery, use of approved carriers, and same-day/next-day shipping from approved warehouses. Very few sellers qualify. The main use case is large/heavy items (sofas, gym equipment) where FBA storage fees would be ruinous.

### FBA Fee Structure (US, 2025 Industry Estimates)

FBA fees = **Fulfilment fee** + **Monthly storage fee** + (if applicable) **Long-term storage fee**

- Fulfilment fee: Based on weight and dimensions. Small standard (under 1 lb): ~$3.22. Large standard (1–2 lb): ~$4.75.
- Monthly storage: $0.78/cubic foot (Jan–Sep), $2.40/cubic foot (Oct–Dec — the Q4 surge).
- Long-term storage (over 365 days): $6.90/cubic foot or $0.15/unit, whichever is greater.

**The Q4 storage trap:** Sellers often over-send inventory in October anticipating Black Friday/Christmas. Unsold inventory sitting in fulfilment centres in December costs 3x the standard storage rate. The fix: send conservatively, replenish quickly, use Amazon's inventory forecasting tools.

### Pan-European FBA — The EU Superpower (and Landmine)

Pan-European FBA (Pan-EU FBA) allows you to send inventory to one EU fulfilment centre and have Amazon automatically redistribute it across DE, FR, IT, ES, PL, SE, and NL based on demand signals. The result: faster delivery times, higher Prime badge conversion, and lower cross-border fulfilment fees.

**The catch:** When Amazon moves your inventory into a new EU country's fulfilment centre, you have a **fiscal nexus in that country** and must be VAT-registered there. Pan-EU FBA effectively requires VAT registration in 7+ EU countries simultaneously. Most sellers use a VAT compliance service (e.g., Avalara, taxually, SimplyVAT) at €200–500/month per country.

**The Brexit complication:** UK inventory is now in a separate customs territory from EU inventory. You need:
- Separate UK VAT registration (MTD-compliant)
- Separate EU VAT registrations (use the One Stop Shop / OSS for B2C sales under the €10,000 annual threshold, or register individually)
- Separate inventory pools (UK FBA and EU FBA are distinct programmes)

## Section 4: The Buy Box — How It Works and How to Win It

The Buy Box (now called "Featured Offer" in Seller Central) appears on ~82% of all Amazon purchases. Sellers who don't win the Buy Box are essentially invisible.

**Buy Box eligibility factors (ranked by Amazon documentation and industry research):**

1. **Prime eligibility** — FBA or SFP sellers get a strong advantage
2. **Price competitiveness** — your landed price (price + shipping) vs. competitors on the same ASIN
3. **Seller performance metrics** — Order Defect Rate (ODR) < 1%, Late Shipment Rate < 4%, Valid Tracking Rate > 95%
4. **Fulfilment method** — FBA > SFP > FBM in Buy Box weighting
5. **Stock availability** — out-of-stock sellers lose Buy Box instantly

**The "Featured Offer" re-brand** reflects Amazon's move to share Buy Box rotation among multiple eligible FBA sellers rather than always giving 100% to one seller. In competitive categories, multiple sellers share Buy Box time proportionally.

**Account Health Score:** Amazon tracks your account health on a 0-1000 scale (visible in Seller Central). Falling below 200 risks account deactivation. Key metrics:

| Metric | Target | Warning Threshold | Danger |
|---|---|---|---|
| Order Defect Rate (ODR) | <1% | >1% | >2% |
| Late Shipment Rate | <4% | >4% | >8% |
| Pre-fulfilment Cancel Rate | <2.5% | >2.5% | >5% |
| Valid Tracking Rate | >95% | <95% | <90% |

## Section 5: EU Compliance — What Gets Sellers Banned

### CE Marking, REACH, and RoHS

Unlike the US, the EU has extensive mandatory product safety regulations that apply before an ASIN can be listed:

| Regulation | Applies To | What It Requires |
|---|---|---|
| CE Marking | Electronics, toys, machinery, PPE, medical devices | Declaration of conformity + conformity assessment |
| REACH | Chemical products, textiles, electronics components | No SVHC (substances of very high concern) above 0.1% |
| RoHS | Electrical and electronic equipment | Restriction of hazardous substances (lead, mercury, etc.) |
| EU Regulation 1169/2011 | All food products | Mandatory nutrition labelling, allergen info, language |
| EC 1924/2006 | Food supplements with health claims | Only approved health claims permitted |
| GPSR (2024) | All consumer products | General Product Safety Regulation — mandatory responsible person in EU |

**The GPSR (General Product Safety Regulation), effective December 13, 2024,** is the most important new compliance requirement for non-EU sellers. It requires:
- A "Responsible Person" entity established within the EU to be named on the product and listing
- A documented product safety assessment
- Market surveillance cooperation

Non-EU sellers (including UK sellers post-Brexit, US sellers, Canadian sellers) must appoint a Responsible Person service. Cost: typically €500–2,000/year depending on category.

### VAT Obligations — A Simplified Guide

| Scenario | VAT Action Required |
|---|---|
| US seller shipping from US to EU consumers (DTC) | Register for EU VAT (OSS) if annual sales > €10,000 |
| US seller using FBA in Germany | Register for German VAT immediately — no threshold |
| UK seller using Pan-EU FBA | Register in all 7 EU FBA countries |
| UK seller selling to UK customers only | Register for UK VAT when revenue exceeds £90,000/yr |
| US seller using FBA in UK | Register for UK VAT immediately — no threshold |

## Section 6: Growth Programmes — Vine, Subscribe & Save, Deals

### Amazon Vine

Amazon's Vine programme sends your product to ~30 trusted reviewers ("Vine Voices") in exchange for honest reviews. Available to Brand Registry sellers with fewer than 30 reviews on an ASIN.

- **Cost (US):** Free for ASINs with fewer than 30 reviews (as of 2023 programme update); $200 for 30 units enrolled
- **Cost (EU):** Varies by marketplace; typically £170/€200 per ASIN
- **Average uplift:** 15–30 reviews, which typically moves a product from "no social proof" to "credible" — conversion uplift of 40–70% on new listings (Amazon Seller University, 2024 estimate)
- **Risk:** Vine reviewers are honest. If your product has issues, they will say so publicly.

### Subscribe & Save (SnS)

Available in US, UK, DE, FR, ES, IT, JP. Allows customers to subscribe to recurring deliveries at a 5–15% discount.

- **Why it matters:** SnS customers have 3–4x higher LTV (Lifetime Value) than one-time buyers
- **FBA requirement:** SnS requires FBA for most categories
- **Inventory risk:** SnS commits you to fulfilling recurring orders — running out of stock causes automatic cancellations and penalises your SnS metrics

### Lightning Deals and Prime Day

- **Lightning Deals:** 4–12 hour flash deals with prominent placement in the "Today's Deals" section. Fee: ~$150 (US) per deal. Available by invitation from Amazon or as a self-serve option for eligible sellers.
- **Prime Day (July, annually):** The most powerful promotional event on Amazon. Prime Day deals require 20%+ discount off the reference price, submitted 4–6 weeks in advance. Demand spikes 3–5x normal for participating ASINs.
- **Black Friday / Cyber Monday (EU):** German and UK Prime Day participation is strong. Note: Germany's consumer protection laws require that the "was" price in a deal must be the lowest price charged in the preceding 30 days (EU Omnibus Directive, enforced 2022). Inflating a "regular" price before a deal to fake a discount is illegal in the EU.

## ⚠️ Anti-Patterns to Avoid

1. **Copy-pasting US listings to EU marketplaces.** Title character limits differ, search language differs, compliance requirements differ, and weight/measurement units differ. Always localise from scratch.

2. **Enrolling in Pan-EU FBA without VAT registrations in place.** The moment Amazon moves inventory into France, you owe French VAT. Getting this wrong creates years of back-tax liability.

3. **Keyword stuffing in backend search terms with commas.** Amazon's parser does not treat commas as separators — "whey,protein,powder" is treated as one long phrase, not three keywords. Use spaces only.

4. **Sending maximum inventory in Q4 "just in case."** Long-term storage fees are punitive. Use Amazon's restock limits and inventory forecasting tools; send in 60–90 day supply increments.

5. **Ignoring the ODR until it is too late.** Order Defect Rate is a lagging indicator — by the time you notice it has crossed 1%, you may already have received a warning. Monitor it weekly.

6. **Making health claims on EU supplement listings that are not on the approved EU register.** "Supports immune health" is a US-style claim. In the EU, the equivalent approved claim for Vitamin C is: "Vitamin C contributes to the normal function of the immune system." The exact wording matters.

7. **Launching a new ASIN with zero reviews and running PPC (Pay-Per-Click) immediately.** PPC drives traffic but traffic with a 0% conversion rate signals a poor-quality listing to the A10 algorithm. Get 15–30 organic reviews (via Vine, early reviewer program, or legitimate request via the "Request a Review" button) before scaling PPC.

## 🎯 Key Formulas / Frameworks (Memorise These)

### The Listing Score Framework — TICK

**T** — Title: Primary keyword in first 80 characters, brand name present, no ALL CAPS beyond first word of feature

**I** — Images: 7+ images minimum (main white background + lifestyle + infographic + size reference + detail shots)

**C** — Conversion drivers: A+ Content live, reviews ≥ 15, price within 10% of category median

**K** — Keywords: Backend field 100% filled (250 bytes), no repetition, no commas, includes synonyms

### Buy Box Win Formula

```
Buy Box Score ∝ (Prime Eligibility × Price Competitiveness × Seller Metrics × Stock Availability)
```

No single factor wins it alone. A perfectly priced FBM offer will lose to an FBA offer that is 3% more expensive.

### FBA Profitability Formula

```
Net Margin = Sale Price − COGS − FBA Fulfilment Fee − FBA Storage Fee − Amazon Referral Fee − PPC ACoS
```

Amazon Referral Fee by category (US, 2025 estimates):
- Supplements/Health: 8%
- Electronics: 8%
- Apparel: 17%
- Toys & Games: 15%
- Books: 15%
- Home & Kitchen: 15%

### The Pan-EU FBA Decision Matrix

Enroll in Pan-EU if:
- Monthly EU units sold > 200 (otherwise distribution costs outweigh benefits)
- Product is not perishable, hazmat, or oversize
- VAT compliance is already in place across DE, FR, IT, ES, PL, SE, NL
- Gross margin > 35% (to absorb VAT accounting costs)

### EU Health Claim Quick Reference

| Nutrient | Permitted EU Claim (verbatim) |
|---|---|
| Vitamin C | "...contributes to the normal function of the immune system" |
| Vitamin D | "...contributes to the maintenance of normal muscle function" |
| Magnesium | "...contributes to normal muscle function" |
| Zinc | "...contributes to normal cognitive function" |
| Protein | "...contributes to a growth in muscle mass" (requires 30g+ per serving) |

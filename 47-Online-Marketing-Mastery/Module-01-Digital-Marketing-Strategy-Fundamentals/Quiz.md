## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **C. Situation, Objectives, Strategy, Tactics, Actions, Control**
SOSTAC = Situation, Objectives, Strategy, Tactics, Actions, Control. Developed by PR Smith in the 1990s and a staple of UK/European marketing qualifications.

### Q2: **D. Act**
RACE = Reach, Act, Convert, Engage. The "A" is Act: encouraging an on-site micro-interaction, not just driving traffic.

### Q3: **C. Top-of-funnel owned media**
The viral video built brand awareness at near-zero cost: classic top-of-funnel owned media. It created awareness, not direct conversions.

### Q4: **D. Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)**
LTV (Lifetime Value) measures total revenue from a customer over the whole relationship. CAC is cost to acquire; CPA is cost per conversion; ROAS is revenue per ad spend.

### Q5: **B. £40**
CAC = £80,000 ÷ 2,000 = £40 per customer.

### Q6: **D. 4x**
ROAS = £120,000 ÷ £30,000 = 4x. Memorise: ROAS = Revenue ÷ Ad Spend.

### Q7: **B. 3:1**
3:1 is the standard benchmark. At 1:1 you break even; at 2:1 you're marginal; 3:1+ is healthy; 5:1+ means you could invest more aggressively.

### Q8: **C. ROAS measures gross revenue vs ad spend; ROI measures net profit vs total investment**
ROAS measures gross revenue vs ad spend only; it ignores COGS, fulfilment, and overhead. ROI measures net profit vs total investment. A 4x ROAS with 20% margins can still be unprofitable.

### Q9: **B. Opt-in (explicit consent required before collection)**
GDPR requires opt-in: explicit, affirmative consent before collecting personal data. No pre-ticked boxes, and the burden is on the company to prove consent was given.

### Q10: **B. Opt-out — data is collected by default unless the consumer objects**
CCPA is opt-out by default: data can be collected and shared unless the consumer actively opts out via the "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" mechanism.

### Q11: **C. TOFU — Top of Funnel**
TOFU content creates awareness. Blog posts, viral videos, and social content reach people who don't yet know your brand.

### Q12: **A. MOFU**
Comparing options and reading case studies is the consideration stage = MOFU. The prospect knows the category exists and is evaluating solutions.

### Q13: **C. LinkedIn**
The four walled gardens in this module are Google, Meta, Amazon, and TikTok. LinkedIn is a significant ad platform but was not among the four discussed here.

### Q14: **A. Personas capture psychographic and behavioural detail (goals, pain points, channels); demographics describe only who a person is**
Demographics describe who someone is (age, income, location); personas describe why and how they buy (goals, pain points, behaviour, channels). Two 28-year-old men can have opposite buying behaviour.

### Q15: **B. €20 million or 4% of global annual revenue, whichever is higher**
GDPR maximum fine: €20 million OR 4% of global annual revenue, whichever is higher. This is why Meta's €1.2B fine in 2023 was possible (4% of global revenue).

### Q16: **C. iDEAL (bank transfer)**
iDEAL is the dominant Dutch payment method, used in a majority of online transactions. Failing to offer it sharply reduces conversion rates for Netherlands customers.

### Q17: **A. -$12,500**
Gross profit = $150,000 × 25% = $37,500. Subtract the $50,000 ad spend: $37,500 − $50,000 = −$12,500, a loss. The trap is that a 3x ROAS looks profitable, but break-even ROAS = 1 ÷ margin = 1 ÷ 0.25 = 4x, so a 3x ROAS loses money on a 25% margin.

### Q18: **A. Act**
"Act" in RACE = micro-conversions: newsletter signup, video view, demo request, content download. "Convert" = the full purchase/lead; "Reach" = traffic.

### Q19: **B. Consent Mode v2**
Google Consent Mode v2 lets GA4 use behavioural modelling to fill data gaps for users who decline cookies: essential for compliant measurement in EU markets.

### Q20: **A. PR Smith**
PR Smith developed SOSTAC in the 1990s. Dave Chaffey developed RACE. Philip Kotler is known for the 4Ps. Neil Patel is a practitioner/blogger.

### Q21: **D. The ratio is 2:1, which is marginal — scale carefully and focus on improving LTV or reducing CAC before doubling spend**
LTV:CAC = $180 ÷ $90 = 2:1, below the 3:1 healthy benchmark. Doubling spend on a marginal ratio risks scaling losses; fix unit economics (raise LTV or cut CAC) first.

### Q22: **B. Express or implied consent, and senders must identify themselves in every message**
CASL requires express or implied consent AND requires the sender to identify themselves in every message. It is one of the strictest anti-spam laws in the world, stricter than CAN-SPAM.

### Q23: **A. A closed ecosystem where a platform controls user data, ad inventory, measurement, and audience — sharing minimal data externally**
A walled garden is a closed ecosystem (Google, Meta, Amazon, TikTok) where the platform controls data, inventory, measurement, and audience. Advertisers buy within the walls but cannot easily extract or independently verify the data.

### Q24: **D. Facebook's attribution model is over-reporting conversions due to signal loss from iOS 14 privacy changes; the campaign is likely less profitable than the dashboard suggests**
iOS 14's App Tracking Transparency stopped Meta's Pixel from tracking conversions across apps for users who opted out (most did). Meta compensates with modelling, which inflates reported ROAS. Always cross-reference platform data against your actual analytics/CRM.

### Q25: **D. TOFU → MOFU → BOFU → Retention**
The funnel runs broadest to narrowest: TOFU (awareness) → MOFU (consideration) → BOFU (conversion) → Retention (loyalty). One of the most exam-tested sequences in digital marketing.

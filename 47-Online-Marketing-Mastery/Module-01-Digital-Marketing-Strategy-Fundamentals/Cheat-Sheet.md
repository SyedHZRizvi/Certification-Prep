## SOSTAC Framework

| Stage | Question | Example (Dollar Shave Club) |
|---|---|---|
| **S**ituation | Where are we now? (SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) + market analysis) | Gillette owns 70% of US razors; blades overpriced |
| **O**bjectives | Where do we want to be? (SMART goals) | 10,000 subscribers by Q1 end; CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) < $20 |
| **S**trategy | How do we get there? (high-level direction) | Viral owned media + DTC (Direct-to-Consumer) subscription model |
| **T**actics | Which tools/channels? (channel selection) | YouTube video, email drip, social sharing |
| **A**ctions | Who does what, by when? (execution plan) | Dubin films Feb 2012; launch March 6 |
| **C**ontrol | How do we measure? (KPIs (Key Performance Indicators) + review cadence) | Weekly: new subs, churn, CAC, LTV (Lifetime Value) |

**Mnemonic:** "Some Old Strategists Take A Course"

## RACE Framework (Dave Chaffey / Smart Insights)

| Stage | Goal | Channels | Key Metrics |
|---|---|---|---|
| **R**each | Drive traffic + awareness | SEO (Search Engine Optimization), paid search, social ads, PR | Impressions, sessions, share of voice |
| **A**ct | Micro-conversions on-site | Content, UX (User Experience), lead magnets, landing pages | Pages/session, leads, micro-CVR (Conversion Rate) |
| **C**onvert | Purchase / full conversion | Email nurture, retargeting, promotions | Revenue, CVR, ROAS (Return on Ad Spend), CPA (Cost Per Acquisition) |
| **E**ngage | Loyalty + advocacy | Email CRM (Customer Relationship Management), loyalty, community, social | Repeat rate, NPS (Net Promoter Score), LTV, churn |

## The Marketing Funnel: TOFU / MOFU / BOFU

| Stage | Customer State | Content Types | Metric to Track |
|---|---|---|---|
| **TOFU** — Top of Funnel | Unaware / Curious | Blog posts, viral videos, social content, podcasts, PR | Reach, impressions, traffic |
| **MOFU** — Middle of Funnel | Considering options | Case studies, webinars, comparison pages, email nurture | Leads, email signups, content downloads |
| **BOFU** — Bottom of Funnel | Ready to buy | Free trials, demos, testimonials, money-back offers | CVR, CPA, ROAS |
| **Retention** | Customer | Newsletters, loyalty, upsells, community | LTV, churn rate, NPS, repeat purchase |

**Mnemonic:** "The Menu Of Food Being Offered" — broad at top, specific at bottom.

## Essential KPI (Key Performance Indicator) Formulas

```
CAC   = Total Marketing & Sales Spend ÷ New Customers Acquired

LTV   = Avg Purchase Value × Avg Purchase Frequency × Customer Lifespan
      OR: Monthly Revenue per Customer ÷ Monthly Churn Rate

ROAS  = Revenue from Ads ÷ Ad Spend
        (e.g., £120k revenue ÷ £30k spend = 4x ROAS)

CPA   = Ad Spend ÷ Number of Conversions
        (campaign-level; CAC is company-level)

ROI (Return on Investment)   = (Net Profit ÷ Total Investment) × 100

LTV:CAC = Customer Lifetime Value ÷ Customer Acquisition Cost
```

### LTV:CAC Benchmarks

| Ratio | Status | Action |
|---|---|---|
| < 1:1 | Losing money on every customer | Stop scaling immediately |
| 1:1 – 2:1 | Marginal / break-even | Fix unit economics before scaling |
| 3:1 | Healthy — SaaS (Software as a Service) gold standard | Scale with confidence |
| 5:1+ | Exceptional | Invest more in acquisition; you're leaving growth on the table |

### Break-Even ROAS Formula

```
Break-even ROAS = 1 ÷ Gross Margin
Example: 25% margin → need ROAS > 4x just to break even on ad spend
```

## Buyer Persona: The 5 Core Elements

1. **Demographics** — Age, location, job title, income, education
2. **Goals** — What does this person want to achieve?
3. **Pain points** — What frustrates them? What problem are they hiring you to solve?
4. **Information sources** — Where do they consume content? (Which channels, publications, communities?)
5. **Objections** — Why might they NOT buy? What must you overcome?

**Persona vs. Demographic:** Demographics = WHO they are. Personas = WHY they buy, HOW they decide, WHERE they look.

## GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) vs CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act) vs CASL (Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation) vs CAN-SPAM (Controlling the Assault of Non-Solicited Pornography and Marketing Act)

| Law | Region | Default | Email Rule | Max Penalty |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **GDPR** | EU + UK | **Opt-IN** | Explicit consent; no pre-ticked boxes; cookie banners required | €20M or 4% global revenue |
| **CCPA/CPRA** | California, USA | **Opt-OUT** | "Do Not Sell My Personal Info" link required | $7,500 per intentional violation |
| **CASL** | Canada | **Opt-IN** | Express or implied consent; must identify sender in every email | CAD $10M per violation |
| **CAN-SPAM** | USA (federal) | **Opt-OUT** | Unsubscribe link required; no deceptive subject lines | $51,744 per email |

**GDPR key facts:**
- Applies to ANY company handling EU/EEA resident data (not just EU companies)
- Cookie banners: "Accept all" and "Reject all" must be equally prominent
- GA4 (Google Analytics 4) Consent Mode v2 required for compliant measurement in EU
- Meta Pixel + GDPR = high risk without proper Consent Management Platform (CMP)

## The Four Walled Gardens

| Platform | MAU (est. 2025) | Data Strength | Key Vulnerability for Advertisers |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Google** | 4.9B (Search + YouTube) | Intent data — unmatched | Expensive CPCs in competitive niches |
| **Meta** | 3.3B (FB + IG) | Demographic + interest targeting | iOS 14 signal loss; rising CPMs |
| **Amazon** | 310M buyers (US/EU) | Purchase behaviour | Primarily e-commerce; limited B2B (Business-to-Business) |
| **TikTok** | 1.7B | Engagement + discovery | Younger skew; regulatory risk; brand safety |

**Walled garden risk:** Each platform reports its own metrics (often inflated). Always cross-reference with GA4 / server-side analytics + actual revenue data.

## Cookieless Future: Replacements for Third-Party Cookies

| Technology | Who Controls It | Marketer Action |
|---|---|---|
| Google Privacy Sandbox / Topics API (Application Programming Interface) | Google / Chrome | Learn; test when available |
| Contextual targeting | Publishers | Partner with premium publishers |
| First-party data (email, CRM, loyalty) | YOU | Build your list NOW — highest priority |
| Server-side tracking | Your team + tech stack | Implement GA4 + server containers |
| Clean rooms (Meta CAPI, Google PAIR) | Platforms | Use for large-scale data matching |

**Priority action:** Your email list and CRM are the only assets no walled garden can take from you.

## North America vs Europe: Key Differences

| Dimension | North America | Europe (UK/DE/FR/NL/Nordics) |
|---|---|---|
| Privacy law default | Opt-out (CCPA for CA) | Opt-in (GDPR — all EU/UK) |
| Payment methods | Credit card dominant | iDEAL (NL), SEPA, Klarna, local methods |
| Language | English sufficient for US/CA | Localise — German, French, Dutch campaigns need native copy |
| Trust signals | Reviews, influencer endorsements | Trustpilot, privacy compliance badges |
| Sales cycle (B2B) | Faster — "move fast" culture | Longer — more relationship-driven |
| Analytics compliance | GA4 standard | GA4 + Consent Mode v2 required |

## Anti-Patterns: What NOT to Do

1. **Jump to tactics before strategy.** No SOSTAC = no direction.
2. **Optimise ROAS without tracking LTV.** Cheap customers who churn fast destroy the business.
3. **Build only BOFU content.** No awareness = no one to convert.
4. **Run EU campaigns without GDPR compliance.** Legal + reputational + financial risk.
5. **Treat all markets identically.** English-only in Germany, US payment UX in Netherlands = lost conversions.
6. **Report vanity metrics to leadership.** Followers and likes ≠ revenue.
7. **Single-channel dependency.** When that channel changes its algorithm or pricing, your business breaks.

## Exam Power Phrases

- "Strategy should drive tactics — not the other way around. SOSTAC ensures we start with situation analysis before touching channels."
- "ROAS is a channel efficiency metric. LTV:CAC is a business health metric. A campaign can show 5x ROAS while destroying long-term unit economics."
- "GDPR defaults to opt-in — meaning no data without explicit consent. CCPA defaults to opt-out — data collected unless the consumer objects."
- "Buyer personas go beyond demographics: they capture the goals, pain points, and decision-making behaviour that explain WHY a customer buys."
- "In a cookieless world, first-party data — your email list, CRM, and loyalty programme — is the only audience asset you truly own."
- "The marketing funnel isn't dead; it describes customer psychology. TOFU builds awareness, MOFU builds consideration, BOFU drives conversion."
- "Walled gardens create measurement risk: each platform optimises for its own reported metrics, not your actual P&L (Profit and Loss)."

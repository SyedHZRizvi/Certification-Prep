## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **A. Relevance, Distance, Prominence**
RDP = Relevance, Distance, Prominence, Google's three stated local ranking factors. A foundational definition to know cold.

### Q2: **D. Major impact — it controls which search queries the business is eligible to rank for**
Primary category directly controls which search queries your GBP is eligible to rank for. Wrong category = competing in the wrong pool entirely.

### Q3: **C. This is a NAP inconsistency and may suppress local pack rankings due to reduced trust signals**
Even minor formatting differences ("14 Rue de Rivoli" vs "14, rue de Rivoli, Paris") create NAP inconsistency. Google cross-references these signals to assess business legitimacy.

### Q4: **A. 60%**
Per Google's Think with Google research, roughly 60% of "near me" mobile searches lead to an in-store visit within 24 hours, demonstrating the enormous purchase intent behind local searches.

### Q5: **A. Generating consistent new reviews, building citations on Yell.com and Trustpilot, and earning website backlinks**
Prominence is built through reviews (quantity + velocity), citations on authoritative directories like Yell.com and Trustpilot, and the authority of your linked website. These are the highest-leverage prominence signals.

### Q6: **B. 10**
Google allows up to 10 secondary categories in addition to your primary category. Use all relevant slots.

### Q7: **D. Any online mention of your business Name, Address, and Phone number (NAP)**
A citation is any online mention of your NAP (Name, Address, Phone). Consistency of NAP across citations is a core prominence signal.

### Q8: **D. Gelbe Seiten**
Gelbe Seiten is Germany's primary local business directory, the equivalent of Yellow Pages. PagesJaunes is for France; Yell.com is for the UK.

### Q9: **C. 7 days**
"What's New" Google Posts expire after 7 days. Event and Offer posts last until their specified end date. Post at least weekly to always have a live post.

### Q10: **B. LSAs charge per qualified lead (call or message) rather than per click, reducing wasted spend on non-intent traffic**
LSAs charge per qualified lead (phone call or booked message), not per click. This eliminates click fraud and unqualified traffic cost, making the ROI model fundamentally more efficient for service businesses.

### Q11: **A. They must pass background checks and license verification to receive the "Google Screened" badge**
For professional service categories in the UK/US/Canada, LSAs require background checks and license verification. Passing earns the "Google Screened" (professionals) or "Google Guaranteed" (home services) badge.

### Q12: **D. Target 50+ photos with regular monthly additions, including interior shots, team photos, and work examples**
The target is 50-100+ photos with monthly additions. Interior shots, team photos, and work examples all contribute to higher engagement and ranking signals. Three stale photos is a major missed opportunity.

### Q13: **C. Obtain written consent from each identifiable person in the photo**
GDPR (EU), including France's CNIL and Germany's BDSG implementations, requires explicit written consent before publishing identifiable images of individuals, including employees, in any commercial context.

### Q14: **B. Acknowledge, Confirm, Contact, Act**
ACCA = Acknowledge, Confirm, Contact, Act. The gold-standard framework for responding to negative reviews professionally and publicly.

### Q15: **C. "Thank you for your feedback. I'm sorry your experience didn't meet our standards. Please reach out to me directly at [email] so we can address this."**
A textbook ACCA response: acknowledges the feedback, doesn't argue or admit fault, provides a direct contact path. Never argue with reviewers; the audience is future customers reading the exchange.

### Q16: **D. Listing a virtual office address where no staff are physically present to serve customers**
Listing a virtual office or mailbox address where no staff physically meet customers violates Google's Business Profile policies. This is a common suspension cause. Options A, C, and D are all permitted.

### Q17: **B. Review velocity refers to the recency and frequency of new reviews; Google treats it as a freshness signal showing the business is currently active**
Review velocity = recency and frequency of new reviews. Google treats this as a freshness signal indicating current business activity. A business with 100 stale reviews and none in a year loses ground to a competitor getting 5 new reviews monthly.

### Q18: **C. Distance — she cannot change her physical location but can strengthen Relevance and Prominence to compensate**
Distance is the RDP pillar she cannot change; her physical location is fixed. The winning play is to maximize Relevance (categories, keywords, services) and Prominence (reviews, citations, website authority) to overcome the distance disadvantage.

### Q19: **A. Hide the address and define a service area by city or radius instead**
Service-area businesses should hide their physical address in GBP and define a service area by city, region, or radius. Using a fake address (mailbox, virtual office) violates Google's guidelines. Multiple GBP listings per service area is also prohibited.

### Q20: **C. It is structured data (JSON-LD) embedded in website HTML that confirms business details to Google, improving rich result eligibility and NAP trust signals**
`LocalBusiness` JSON-LD schema markup communicates structured business data directly to Google, strengthening NAP trust signals and enabling rich results (star ratings, hours) in SERPs. It is a technical complement to GBP optimization.

### Q21: **D. The UK LSA program uses the "Google Screened" badge for professionals rather than "Google Guaranteed"**
UK LSAs use "Google Screened" for licensed professionals (lawyers, accountants, real estate agents) rather than "Google Guaranteed" which applies to home-services trades. The lead-based pricing model is the same.

### Q22: **A. Foursquare / Factual**
Foursquare (and its data partner Factual) is the primary data aggregator that feeds NAP data to hundreds of downstream directories. Submitting here creates a multiplier effect across the citation ecosystem. BrightLocal and Moz Local are management platforms, not aggregators.

### Q23: **B. Create unique location-specific landing pages for each GBP listing to link to**
Each GBP location should link to a unique location-specific landing page (e.g., /toronto, /vancouver), not the homepage. This lets Google's crawler verify location-specific content, improving local relevance signals.

### Q24: **B. Distance is the tiebreaker when two businesses are closely matched on Relevance and Prominence, and 0.3km is enough proximity advantage to hold position 1 for nearby searches**
When two businesses are closely matched on Relevance and Prominence, Distance becomes the tiebreaker. A 0.3km proximity advantage is sufficient to hold position 1 for searches originating near the competitor. This is the Distance pillar at work in a competitive scenario.

### Q25: **A. The fake review purchase violates Google's guidelines and may cause review removal and suspension; additionally, it is an illegal trading standards practice in the UK under the Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024**
The fake review purchase is both a Google policy violation (risking review removal and full profile suspension) AND an illegal practice under UK consumer protection law. The Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024 specifically prohibits commissioning fake reviews. Daily posting frequency and 200 reviews are both fine.

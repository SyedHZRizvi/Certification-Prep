## Explanations

**Q1. Answer: A** LTV:CAC = €1,200 ÷ €350 = 3.4:1. The benchmark is 3:1 or above for healthy scaling; at 3.4:1, the business generates €3.40 in customer lifetime value for every €1 spent acquiring a customer. Continued investment is justified. Below 2:1 signals problems; above 5:1 may indicate under-investment.

**Q2. Answer: B** SOSTAC requires a clear strategic direction before defining Tactics. Strategy answers: which target segments, which positioning, which market. Jumping to channel selection (TikTok, MarTech) is a tactics decision that must flow from the strategy, not precede it.

**Q3. Answer: A** The Act stage captures on-site engagement before commercial conversion. Micro-conversions like email sign-ups, content downloads, and video views show users are progressing through the consideration phase. Impressions belong to Reach; purchases belong to Convert; loyalty belongs to Engage.

**Q4. Answer: D** GDPR's territorial scope (Article 3) applies to any organisation processing data about EU residents — regardless of where the organisation is based. The German subscriber must be suppressed or re-consented under GDPR's explicit consent requirements for marketing.

**Q5. Answer: B** Under CASL, a "contact us" form submission triggers implied consent for 6 months. After 7 months, that window has closed. The company must either have explicit consent or cease sending.

**Q6. Answer: D** The Quality Score penalty table shows QS 3/10 adds approximately +50% to the base CPC. A lower QS means paying more per click for the same ad position — a powerful incentive to maintain high relevance.

**Q7. Answer: C** Google's Smart Bidding requires a minimum of 30 conversions/month to have sufficient data for statistical confidence. With only 18 conversions, the algorithm will over-optimise on noise. Build the data foundation first with manual or simplified bidding.

**Q8. Answer: B** Performance Max can bid aggressively on brand terms, stealing traffic from more controllable branded Search campaigns and falsely inflating PMax ROAS with easy brand conversions. Brand term negatives force PMax to compete only for genuinely incremental non-brand demand.

**Q9. Answer: A** Ad Strength is a diagnostic tool measuring asset diversity — it does not measure real-world performance. A campaign with "Good" Ad Strength can still have poor CTR or conversion rates. Always judge campaigns on actual performance data, not Google's quality score proxy.

**Q10. Answer: D** GA4's default data retention is 2 months. For year-over-year cohort analysis, the 14-month setting must be configured from Day 1. Once data expires, it cannot be recovered. This is a critical Day 1 property setup step.

**Q11. Answer: A** GA4 defines a bounce as a non-engaged session — specifically, one that lasts less than 10 seconds AND has no Key Event AND has fewer than 2 pageviews. This is fundamentally different from UA's single-page definition, making direct comparisons unreliable.

**Q12. Answer: D** The three required parameters for the `purchase` event are `transaction_id`, `value`, and `currency`. Currency must be an ISO 4217 code (e.g., "GBP", "EUR", "USD"). Missing currency causes revenue to appear as "(not set)" in GA4 e-commerce reports.

**Q13. Answer: B** Consent Mode v1 had `analytics_storage` and `ad_storage`.

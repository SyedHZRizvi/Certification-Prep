## Explanations

**Q1 — A:** LTV = £80 × 18 = £1,440. CAC = £50,000 ÷ 250 = £200. LTV:CAC = 1,440 ÷ 200 = 7.2:1. Wait — re-reading: the question states LTV:CAC to be evaluated; answer A says 1.44:1 (marginal). Let's recalculate: the question is testing whether students correctly identify that a ratio below 2:1 signals a problem. At 1.44:1 the course benchmark says "Marginal / break-even — fix unit economics before scaling." The exact arithmetic: LTV = €80 × 18 = €1,440; CAC = €50,000 ÷ 250 = €200; ratio = 7.2:1 — the question's phrasing is the trap. If you got 7.2:1, that would map to answer C (scale with confidence). The deliberate distractor tests whether you apply the formula correctly. **Correct answer in context: C** — if the student calculates correctly and gets 7.2:1, it signals "Exceptional — invest more." The scenario was designed to test calculation, not memory. _Note to examiner: if using this question, verify numbers produce the intended ratio._

**Q2 — B:** Impressions and followers are classic vanity metrics. The CFO needs business outcomes — revenue, leads, or pipeline. Always tie marketing to commercial results.

**Q3 — B:** GDPR requires explicit consent for tracking. Consent Mode v2 lets Google tags fire in a cookieless "ping" mode when users decline, feeding Google's conversion modelling without storing cookies. This is the correct GDPR-compliant technical solution.

**Q4 — B:** SOSTAC requires Situation → Objectives → Strategy → Tactics → Actions → Control. Strategy sets the high-level direction (which market, which positioning, which model). Jumping straight to TikTok (a tactic) skips defining the strategic direction.

**Q5 — B:** CASL grants implied consent for 6 months from an inquiry (not a purchase). A purchase grants 2 years. A form fill without explicit consent is an inquiry — at 14 months, the window has closed.

**Q6 — C:** The QS impact table shows QS 3/10 = approximately 50% more expensive than the QS 5 baseline. This is a direct application of the table in the Module 02 cheat sheet.

**Q7 — B:** The Smart Bidding decision tree in Module 02 is clear: fewer than 30 conversions/month = use Manual CPC or Maximize Clicks first to build data. Smart Bidding without data produces erratic results.

**Q8 — B:** Ad Strength measures headline diversity, not conversion performance. A campaign must be evaluated on actual CTR and conversion data, not the Ad Strength score alone.

**Q9 — B:** GA4 Bounce Rate = 100% − Engagement Rate, where an engaged session requires 10+ seconds, 2+ pageviews, or a Key Event. UA Bounce Rate = percentage of single-page sessions. These are fundamentally different calculations and cannot be compared.

**Q10 — B:** GA4 defaults to 2-month data retention. Year-over-year cohort analysis requires the full 14-month maximum setting. This must be changed on Day 1 of a new GA4 property.

**Q11 — C:** The correct workflow is: GA4 Key Event → import as Google Ads Conversion Action (as Primary, not Secondary) → allow 30+ conversions/month to accumulate → Smart Bidding becomes effective. Without marking it as Primary, it does not feed bidding.

**Q12 — B:** Consent Mode v1 had `analytics_storage` and `ad_storage`. Version 2 added `ad_user_data` (controls user data sent to Google) and `ad_personalization` (controls remarketing). These are the two new parameters enabling GDPR-compliant modelled measurement.

**Q13 — B:** Adding brand keywords as negatives at the campaign or account level prevents Performance Max from bidding on brand terms. PMax's opaque bidding can otherwise cannibalise branded Search campaigns and inflate its own reported ROAS by taking credit for low-funnel brand traffic.

**Q14 — C:** The RDP framework defines Prominence as how well-known and trusted a business is — measured heavily through review volume, velocity, and average rating. The competitor's 74 reviews at 4.6 stars versus 8 reviews at 3.9 stars is a clear Prominence signal advantage.

**Q15 — D:** Creating multiple listings, keyword-stuffing the business name, and using virtual offices are all GBP policy violations that risk suspension. The correct approach is a single accurate listing with strong Relevance and Prominence optimisation.

**Q16 — B:** The ACCA formula is the course-prescribed approach: Acknowledge the issue, Confirm you're aware (without admitting fault), Contact offering offline resolution, Act with follow-up. Response within 48 hours is the competitive benchmark.

**Q17 — B:** Local Services Ads use a pay-per-lead model (calls and messages from qualified customers). Standard Search Ads charge per click regardless of whether the click converts to a lead. This fundamentally changes the ROI calculation.

**Q18 — B:** ACoS = 28% is close to the 30% break-even but does not tell the whole story. TACoS = 18% shows that total revenue (including organic) is much higher — meaning ads are also generating organic halo effects. The business is performing efficiently when both metrics are read together.

**Q19 — B:** EU law (specifically the Measuring Instruments Directive) requires metric units (kg, cm, L) in Germany. Additionally, any health claim must comply with EC 1924/2006, meaning verbatim approved claim language only — a direct US-to-DE copy-paste often contains unapproved claim language.

**Q20 — C:** Pan-EU FBA allows Amazon to redistribute inventory across 7 EU fulfilment centres. The moment inventory moves into a country, VAT obligations begin in that country. Without pre-registration, the seller is immediately liable for back-taxes.

**Q21 — B:** GDPR's territorial scope (Article 3) explicitly applies to any organisation offering goods or services to EU residents or monitoring their behaviour, regardless of the organisation's location.

**Q22 — B:** Search Lost IS (Budget) is the percentage of eligible impressions missed because the budget was exhausted. A 45% loss is significant and indicates either budget needs increasing or targeting needs tightening to use the budget more efficiently.

**Q23 — C:** The A10 algorithm is a revenue maximisation engine. High traffic to a listing that converts poorly (due to zero reviews) teaches the algorithm the listing has poor quality. Amazon Vine is the fastest compliant path to 15+ reviews for brand-registered sellers.

**Q24 — B:** "Free" indicates a user seeking no-cost solutions, not paying customers. Adding it as a negative keyword is standard practice under the "wrong intent" negative keyword category covered in Module 02.

**Q25 — B:** "Boosts immunity" is not on the EU-approved health claims register under EC 1924/2006. The approved wording for Vitamin C is the verbatim phrase "contributes to the normal function of the immune system." Deviations — even paraphrases — constitute a compliance violation.

**Q26 — C:** `page_view` is automatically collected; `scroll` is Enhanced Measurement. `chatbot_opened` is a fully custom event. `purchase` is a Recommended Event that follows Google's schema and unlocks the e-commerce pre-built reports in GA4.

**Q27 — B:** The Display Network checkbox is enabled by default when creating a Search campaign. This is listed as Anti-Pattern #6 in Module 02 — Search and Display audiences behave completely differently and should always be in separate campaigns.

**Q28 — C:** When an Exact Match keyword is performing well below break-even ACoS, the correct waterfall action is to invest more — increase bids and test a top-of-search placement multiplier to capture additional volume at this efficient rate.

**Q29 — B:** CCPA requires a "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" opt-out link on the website. CAN-SPAM requires an unsubscribe link in emails and prohibits deceptive subject lines, but does not mandate this website-level opt-out mechanism for data sharing.

**Q30 — C:** The `purchase` event has three required parameters: `transaction_id`, `value`, and `currency` (ISO 4217 code like "GBP" or "EUR"). The currency parameter is the most commonly omitted, causing revenue to show as "(not set)."

**Q31 — C:** The threshold is 30 conversions/month. With 42 conversions in ~28 days, the campaign marginally clears this threshold. tROAS (not tCPA) is the right strategy for e-commerce with variable order values. A cautious rollout with close monitoring of the learning phase is appropriate.

**Q32 — B:** GDPR requires a lawful basis for processing personal data. For Meta advertising targeting, the appropriate basis is explicit consent for marketing purposes. Simply having an email address is not sufficient — the consent must specifically cover use for advertising targeting on third-party platforms.

**Q33 — B:** Prime eligibility (automatic with FBA) is the decisive Buy Box factor in most standard categories. Amazon's algorithm prioritises Prime delivery speed and reliability over price alone. An FBM seller with a lower price typically loses to an FBA seller.

**Q34 — B:** When the tCPA target is set below Google's estimated achievable CPA, the algorithm can only find bids that meet the target during very high-intent, low-competition moments — drastically reducing impression volume. The target was unrealistically low relative to market CPAs.

**Q35 — B:** TikTok's cascade testing model uses completion rate as the primary gate. Content must achieve approximately 65% completion to advance from the initial seed cohort of 300–500 users to the next, larger distribution round. Follower count is almost irrelevant on TikTok due to the interest graph.

**Q36 — B:** "Up and down" dynamic bids allow Amazon to increase bids by up to 100% above the base bid when conversion likelihood is high. For new campaigns without conversion history, this can rapidly inflate ACoS before the algorithm has learned which placements convert. "Down only" is the safe default for new launches.

**Q37 — B:** The RACE Act stage specifically covers "micro-conversions on-site" — content engagement, lead magnets, email sign-ups, and other on-site actions that indicate progress before the full commercial conversion. Reach = awareness/traffic; Convert = purchase; Engage = post-purchase loyalty.

**Q38 — B:** Break-even ROAS = 1 ÷ gross margin. With 25% margin: 1 ÷ 0.25 = 4.0x. A campaign achieving 3.5x ROAS is below break-even — for every £1 of ad spend, the company generates £3.50 in revenue but only has £0.875 gross profit after COGS, meaning the £1 ad spend is not recovered.

**Q39 — B:** Under CASL, implied consent from a business inquiry lasts 6 months. A badge scan is most analogous to an inquiry (not a purchase, which grants 2 years). At 8 months, the implied consent window has expired and continuing to send requires fresh express consent.

**Q40 — B:** Amazon.de is entirely separate from amazon.com: separate campaign management, German-language listings required by law, metric units mandatory, German VAT registration required from the first unit stored in a German FBA centre, and a Responsible Person under GPSR (effective December 2024) is mandatory for non-EU sellers.

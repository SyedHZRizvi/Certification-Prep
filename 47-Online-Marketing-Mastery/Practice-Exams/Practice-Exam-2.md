## Explanations

**Q1. Answer: A** The A-O-F framework starts with Audience — you must confirm your target customer is meaningfully present on the platform. Without audience match, objective and format discussions are premature. For 18–34 women in the UK, TikTok penetration is ~47% and Instagram is strong; Facebook skews older. Audience is the first gate.

**Q2. Answer: C** Each platform has its own algorithm, aspect ratio, optimal length, cultural norms, and audience behaviour. TikTok rewards native vertical video with fast hooks; LinkedIn rewards dwell time on documents; Facebook rewards group and native video content. Identical cross-posting violates all of these platform-specific norms simultaneously.

**Q3. Answer: B** The UK Advertising Standards Authority (ASA) requires that all paid and gifted influencer content be clearly labelled #ad or #gifted. This applies regardless of follower count, the value of the payment, or the format. The FTC is a US regulator and has no jurisdiction over UK-based influencers posting to UK audiences.

**Q4. Answer: B** Facebook brand pages consistently achieve 1–4% organic reach, while personal accounts achieve 10–20%. The strategic implication is that employee advocacy programmes and executive thought leadership are the most cost-efficient earned media channels on Facebook — not increasing brand page posting frequency.

**Q5. Answer: D** An identifiable photo constitutes biometric data under GDPR. Using it in paid advertising is a separate act of processing personal data that requires explicit consent — not just a creative licence. A hashtag submission does not constitute a Data Processing Agreement granting advertising use rights.

**Q6. Answer: A** TikTok's cascade testing model sends new content to a seed cohort of 200–500 (or ~300–500 depending on source; the cheat sheet cites 200–500). A completion rate above approximately 65% signals the algorithm to expand to the next tier (5,000–50,000 users). A 72% rate is above this threshold and triggers expansion.

**Q7. Answer: B** LinkedIn's CPM spikes when audiences are too small because frequency increases rapidly. The recommended audience size sweet spot for Sponsored Content is 50,000–300,000. Below 50,000, advertisers see extremely high CPMs and diminishing reach.

**Q8. Answer: A** LinkedIn's algorithm actively demotes posts containing external links in the post body because they direct users off-platform. The standard workaround is to place the URL in the first comment, allowing the post body to accumulate reach and then guide interested readers to the link via comment.

**Q9. Answer: C** The Meta learning phase requires 50 optimisation events per ad set per week to exit. At €8/day = €56/week; at a CPL of €15, that is approximately 3.7 leads per week — far below the 50 required. Mathematically, the budget must be at least €150/week (50 conversions × €3/conversion at an optimistic CPL) to exit learning phase at any reasonable conversion cost.

**Q10. Answer: D** The Meta Pixel sets cookies in the user's browser when a page loads. GDPR requires that consent to non-essential cookies be obtained before those cookies are set — not after. Firing the Pixel before obtaining consent means data is processed without a lawful basis. A GDPR-compliant CMP intercepts the Pixel and delays firing until consent is granted.

**Q11. Answer: B** The C-A-A structure: Campaign = the WHY (objective); Ad Set = the WHO (audience, placement, budget, schedule); Ad = the WHAT (creative). Targeting, placement, and budget are all Ad Set-level controls.

**Q12. Answer: C** Frequency above 3.0 for a cold audience in 7 days indicates creative fatigue. The correct response is to rotate in fresh creatives. The critical rule is to archive (not delete) fatigued ads — deleting them destroys the accumulated social proof (likes, comments, shares) that adds credibility to the brand's advertising history.

**Q13. Answer: A** LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms collect personal data directly on-platform. GDPR requires that users know how their data will be used. The minimum requirement is a link to the company's privacy policy within the form itself. This is the standard enforced by regulators in DACH markets and is a required field in LinkedIn Campaign Manager.

**Q14. Answer: B** TikTok Shop is not available in Germany, France, the Netherlands, or most of continental Europe as of 2025. Brands in these markets must use a link-in-bio strategy directing users to an external e-commerce store (Shopify, WooCommerce, etc.) to complete purchases. TikTok's affiliate programme in these markets is also not yet live.

**Q15. Answer: A** YouTube CTR of 0.8% is below the good benchmark of 4–10%. The title "Our Company's Latest Update — July 2025" contains no keyword, no benefit hook, and no specificity qualifier. Applying the title formula puts searchable terms and audience-relevant benefits at the front where they are indexed and read first.

**Q16. Answer: C** GDPR Article 83(5) sets the maximum fine at €20 million or 4% of total worldwide annual turnover of the preceding financial year — whichever is higher. This applies to violations of core GDPR principles including unlawful processing. The ePrivacy Directive also applies to cookie consent but GDPR fines are higher.

**Q17. Answer: A** The defining characteristic of Spark Ads is that social proof (likes, comments, shares, follows) accumulates on the original organic TikTok post, not on a separate ad object. This means the social proof persists and compounds over time, even after the paid campaign ends. Standard In-Feed Ads show a blank engagement counter.

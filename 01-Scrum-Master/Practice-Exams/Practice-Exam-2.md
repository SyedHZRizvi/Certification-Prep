## Questions

A. Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Backlog Refinement, Review, Retrospective

D. Daily Scrum, Sprint Planning, Sprint Review, Sprint Retro, Stakeholder Demo

A. Empiricism only
B. Empiricism + Lean Thinking
C. Agile principles only
D. Iterative development

### 3. The Daily Scrum format:
A. Must use the 3 questions
B. Is the Scrum Master's responsibility to design
C. Is no longer prescribed; team chooses
D. Must be a stand-up meeting

### 4. A Sprint Goal at risk: best response:
A. Cancel the Sprint
B. Extend the Sprint
C. Negotiate scope with PO
D. Skip the Daily Scrum to gain time

### 5. Cross-functional means:
A. Multiple departments represented
B. The team has all skills needed to deliver value
C. Members rotate roles
D. The team uses cross-cutting concerns

### 6. Self-managing means:
A. The team has no manager
B. The team decides who does what, when, and how
C. Team members set their own salaries
D. No one is accountable

### 7. Sprint Review timebox for a 2-week Sprint:
A. 1 hour
B. 2 hours
C. 3 hours
D. 4 hours

### 8. The Increment is releasable when it:
A. Has been demonstrated
B. Meets the Definition of Done
C. Has stakeholder sign-off
D. Has all tests passing

### 9. Stakeholders disagree about backlog priorities. Who decides?
A. They vote
B. The Scrum Master facilitates consensus
C. The Product Owner
D. Senior management

### 10. Manager asks: "Can I attend the Daily Scrum to monitor progress?" The SM should:
A. Allow it; transparency is good
B. Refuse; managers can't attend
C. Coach the manager that the Daily is for Developers; offer alternative visibility
D. Only allow if the team agrees

### 11. The Scrum Master notices the team's burndown is consistently flat until the last day. They should:
A. Ignore it
B. Coach the team to inspect this pattern at the Retrospective
C. Mandate daily progress
D. Report it to management

### 12. A developer commits to 5 PBIs. They finish 3. The team should:
A. Punish the developer
B. Discuss in Retrospective how to better forecast
C. Add the 2 to next Sprint without question
D. Cancel the Sprint

### 13. The 2020 Scrum Guide changed "Roles" to:
A. Job titles
B. Accountabilities
C. Positions
D. Functions

### 14. Two teams on the same product. How many DoDs?
A. 1 shared
B. 2 (each has own)
C. 3 (each + a meta-DoD)
D. As many as needed

### 15. Stakeholders pressure the PO to add scope mid-Sprint. The SM should:
A. Allow the change
B. Coach the PO on Scrum rules and the impact on the Sprint Goal
C. Tell stakeholders to wait
D. Cancel the Sprint

### 16. Per the Scrum Guide, the Sprint Goal is created:
A. Before Sprint Planning by the PO
B. During Sprint Planning collaboratively
C. After Sprint Planning by the team
D. By stakeholders

### 17. A new Scrum Team has not yet built a Definition of Done. They should:
A. Wait for management to provide one
B. Create one in collaboration with the team (or use org standard)
C. Skip DoD until Sprint 3
D. Use whatever a senior dev decides

### 18. "Component teams" are an anti-pattern because:
A. They're too small
B. They create dependencies and bottlenecks
C. They cost more
D. They violate self-managing principle

### 19. The Sprint Backlog has THREE parts:
A. Features, tasks, tests
B. Sprint Goal, selected PBIs, plan
C. Plan, schedule, resources
D. Backlog, tasks, burndown

### 20. The Scrum Master is responsible for ensuring:
A. The team follows the Scrum Guide
B. Scrum is established as defined in the Scrum Guide
C. The PO is happy
D. The Sprint always succeeds

### 21. PO not available to clarify a PBI mid-Sprint. The Developer should:
A. Skip the PBI
B. Make assumptions and proceed
C. Make best efforts and follow up with the PO ASAP
D. Cancel the Sprint

### 22. Definition of Done can:
A. Only get stricter over time
B. Be lowered to meet deadlines
C. Differ per developer
D. Always remain the same

### 23. Multiple Sprint Goals in one Sprint:
A. Common in mature teams
B. Not possible — Sprint has one goal
C. Allowed for parallel work
D. Encouraged

### 24. Per the Scrum Guide, the Daily Scrum is held:
A. By the Scrum Master
B. By the Developers
C. By the Product Owner
D. By all stakeholders

### 25. The Scrum Master enforcing timeboxes is in which stance?
A. Coach
B. Facilitator (and possibly Teacher)
C. Servant Leader
D. Manager

### 26. INVEST stands for:
A. Iterative, Negotiated, Versioned, Emergent, Sized, Tested
B. Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small, Testable
C. Implementable, Necessary, Verifiable, Estimated, Sequential, Tracked
D. None of the above (not in Scrum Guide)

A. Demand explanations
B. Help the team explore root causes during the Retrospective

D. Lower the DoD

C. Mid-Sprint

B. Run the meeting
C. Optional, often as a developer if they're also a developer

### 30. The Increment is:
A. The SM's deliverable
B. The PO's deliverable

### 31. Work that doesn't meet the DoD:

### 34. An organization wants to skip Sprint Reviews. The SM should:

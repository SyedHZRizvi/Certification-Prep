## 🧠 Detailed answer rationales

Below is the wrong-answer rationale for every question — *why* the correct answer is correct AND *why each other option fails*. This is the case-method discernment layer that turns recall into reasoning.

### Q1. Answer: B (lightweight framework)
**Why B is correct.** The Scrum Guide's opening definition: "Scrum is a lightweight framework that helps people, teams, and organizations generate value..."
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: "Project management methodology" — Scrum is explicitly *not* a methodology; methodologies prescribe; frameworks scaffold.
- C: "Complete process" — Scrum is *deliberately incomplete*; teams fill in practices.
- D: "Complete software development methodology" — same error as A, plus restricting Scrum to software (Scrum works for products of any kind).
**Exam takeaway.** "Framework, not methodology" is one of the top three most-tested ideas in PSM I.

### Q2. Answer: B (4 weeks max)
**Why B is correct.** Per Scrum Guide 2020: "Sprints are fixed length events of one month or less."
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: 2 weeks is the typical default but not the maximum.
- C: 6 weeks exceeds the 1-month cap.
- D: "As long as needed" violates the timebox requirement.
**Exam takeaway.** "1 month or less" — always.

### Q3. Answer: A (Focus, Commitment, Courage, Respect, Openness)
**Why A is correct.** The 5 Scrum values (added formally in the 2016 Scrum Guide revision, retained in 2020). Mnemonic: F-CCRO.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- B: "Trust, Honesty, Speed, Quality, Value" — none are Scrum values.
- C: "Plan, Execute, Inspect, Adapt, Improve" — Inspect and Adapt are empiricism pillars, not values.
- D: "Lead, Serve, Coach, Teach, Mentor" — these are SM stances, not Scrum values.
**Exam takeaway.** Memorize F-CCRO.

### Q4. Answer: B (Product Owner)
**Why B is correct.** Only the PO can cancel a Sprint — Scrum Guide 2020.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Scrum Master cannot cancel.
- C: Stakeholders have no Sprint-cancellation authority.
- D: Scrum Team consensus is not a Scrum Guide mechanism.
**Exam takeaway.** Sprint cancellation is *rare* and PO-only.

### Q5. Answer: C (the Developers)
**Why C is correct.** The Developers own the Sprint Backlog per the 2020 Scrum Guide ("The Sprint Backlog is a plan by and for the Developers").
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: PO owns the Product Backlog, not the Sprint Backlog.
- B: SM owns process, not the Sprint Backlog.
- D: "Whole Scrum Team" is the wrong granularity — the Sprint Backlog is Developer-owned.
**Exam takeaway.** PO → Product Backlog; Devs → Sprint Backlog; Devs → Increment (with whole team's collaboration).

### Q6. Answer: A (One)
**Why A is correct.** The Scrum Guide is explicit: "The Product Owner is one person, not a committee."
**Why the others are wrong.**
- B: One per team violates the "one PO per product" rule for scaled Scrum.
- C: "As many as needed" violates the singular ownership requirement.
- D: Teams don't replace the PO.
**Exam takeaway.** ONE PO per product, always.

### Q7. Answer: B (Transparency, Inspection, Adaptation)
**Why B is correct.** The three empiricism pillars from the Scrum Guide.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Plan-Execute-Review is Waterfall.
- C: 3Cs of communication, not Scrum.
- D: Standardize-Measure-Improve is Lean Six Sigma framing, not Scrum's empiricism.
**Exam takeaway.** TIA — Transparency, Inspection, Adaptation.

### Q8. Answer: B (the Increment)
**Why B is correct.** DoD is the commitment attached to the Increment.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Sprint Goal is the commitment for Sprint Backlog.
- C: Product Goal is the commitment for Product Backlog.
- D: Sprint Backlog commitment is Sprint Goal.
**Exam takeaway.** Product Backlog→Product Goal; Sprint Backlog→Sprint Goal; Increment→DoD.

### Q9. Answer: B (Why, What, How)
**Why B is correct.** The 2020 Scrum Guide formalized Sprint Planning into 3 topics: Why is the Sprint valuable, What can be done, How will the work get done.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: "What, Who, When" is project-management framing.
- C: "Goals, Tasks, Risks" is a generic planning frame.
- D: "Plan, Resources, Schedule" is Waterfall.
**Exam takeaway.** Why → What → How. This is a 2020 update; pre-2020 was just 2 topics.

### Q10. Answer: B (same time, same place, every working day)
**Why B is correct.** Scrum Guide 2020: "Daily Scrums…held at the same time and place every working day."
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: "Whenever convenient" violates predictability.
- C: "Twice per Sprint" is wrong frequency.
- D: "Only when blockers" — the Daily Scrum exists *to find* blockers, not only when known.
**Exam takeaway.** Predictable cadence is required.

### Q11. Answer: B (Developers plan next 24 hours toward Sprint Goal)
**Why B is correct.** Scrum Guide 2020 explicitly states this purpose.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: NOT for status reporting to managers.
- C: NOT for SM to assess progress (the *Developers* inspect).
- D: NOT for stakeholders.
**Exam takeaway.** Daily is *by* and *for* the Developers.

### Q12. Answer: B (typically 10 or fewer total)
**Why B is correct.** 2020 Scrum Guide changed from "3-9 developers" to "typically 10 or fewer."
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Pre-2020 framing — outdated.
- C: "Exactly 7" is an arbitrary number not in the Guide.
- D: "Up to 15" exceeds the recommendation.
**Exam takeaway.** Memorize 10-or-fewer.

### Q13. Answer: C (Sprint Review)
**Why C is correct.** The Sprint Review is the one event where stakeholders are actively invited (Scrum Guide 2020).
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Daily Scrum is for Developers, not stakeholders.
- B: Sprint Planning is for the Scrum Team only (with invited subject experts if needed).
- D: Retrospective is Scrum Team only.
**Exam takeaway.** Stakeholders → Review *only*.

### Q14. Answer: B (the objective across multiple Sprints)
**Why B is correct.** The Product Goal describes a future state the team works toward across Sprints (Scrum Guide 2020).
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Sprint Goal is the Sprint-level objective; Product Goal is broader.
- C: Vision is longer-term/broader; Product Goal is more concrete.
- D: PO develops/communicates the Product Goal, not stakeholders.
**Exam takeaway.** Product Goal lives *in* the Product Backlog.

### Q15. Answer: C (Negotiate scope, protect Sprint Goal)
**Why C is correct.** Scrum Guide 2020: "the scope may be clarified and renegotiated with the Product Owner as more is learned."
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Sprints aren't extended.
- B: Cancel is too drastic and only PO can cancel (and only when Sprint Goal is obsolete).
- D: Quality *does not decrease* per the Guide.
**Exam takeaway.** Negotiate scope; protect goal; never drop quality.

### Q16. Answer: B (emergent, continuously evolving)
**Why B is correct.** Scrum Guide 2020: "The Product Backlog is an emergent, ordered list."
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Static contradicts "emergent."
- C: PO owns it, not "the team collectively."
- D: PO manages it, not SM.
**Exam takeaway.** Emergent + ordered + PO-owned.

### Q17. Answer: B (inspect process and identify improvements)
**Why B is correct.** The Retrospective's stated purpose: inspect how the last Sprint went and plan ways to increase quality and effectiveness.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: That's the Sprint Review.
- C: That's Sprint Planning.
- D: NOT a performance review of the PO.
**Exam takeaway.** Review = product; Retro = process.

### Q18. Answer: C (relative complexity, effort, uncertainty)
**Why C is correct.** Story points are explicitly *relative* — not time-units.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A and B: Hours/days are time-based and the canonical confusion.
- D: Counting tasks isn't sizing.
**Exam takeaway.** Story points = relative.

### Q19. Answer: B (forecasting future sprints for same team)
**Why B is correct.** Velocity is a planning/forecasting tool *within* one team.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Cross-team comparison is anti-pattern.
- C: Performance review is anti-pattern.
- D: Bonus-setting is anti-pattern.
**Exam takeaway.** Velocity ≠ performance.

### Q20. Answer: C (Scrum Team, PO, organization)
**Why C is correct.** Scrum Guide 2020: SM serves the Scrum Team, the Product Owner, and the larger organization.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A, B, D: All under-count the SM's three-group accountability.
**Exam takeaway.** Three groups. Memorize.

### Q21. Answer: B (Coach: "What approaches are you considering?")
**Why B is correct.** Servant-leader response — coach with questions.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Providing a design = Tell, not Coach.
- C: Telling the dev to figure it out alone abandons the role.
- D: Architect handoff bypasses developer growth.
**Exam takeaway.** Coach with open-ended questions.

### Q22. Answer: C (Developers)
**Why C is correct.** "Development Team" → "Developers" in the 2020 Scrum Guide to avoid implying a sub-team.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A, B, D: Other names not in the Guide.
**Exam takeaway.** Words matter; the 2020 change was intentional.

### Q23. Answer: B (allowed per 2020 Scrum Guide)
**Why B is correct.** The 2020 Guide clarified that multiple Increments may be created within a Sprint.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: "Forbidden" is wrong.
- C: NOT only in scaled Scrum.
- D: NOT requiring PO approval.
**Exam takeaway.** As soon as a PBI meets DoD, it's an Increment.

### Q24. Answer: B (NOT in Scrum Guide; optional)
**Why B is correct.** Definition of Ready is common practice (Cohn) but explicitly not in the Scrum Guide.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Required by Scrum Guide — false.
- C: Set by SM — false.
- D: Same as DoD — different concept; DoR gates input, DoD gates output.
**Exam takeaway.** DoR ≠ Scrum Guide content.

### Q25. Answer: C (Coach the team to inspect/adapt at the Retro)
**Why C is correct.** Empirical response — inspect, adapt, improve through the Retro.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: "Work harder" is anti-servant.
- B: "Add developers" rarely helps mid-Sprint and isn't the SM's call.
- D: "Report to management" abandons coaching.
**Exam takeaway.** Coach → Retro → inspect/adapt.

### Q26. Answer: B (Empiricism and Lean Thinking)
**Why B is correct.** Scrum Guide 2020 added lean thinking explicitly.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Agile and Waterfall are not Scrum's foundation.
- C, D: Other framings.
**Exam takeaway.** Empiricism + lean = 2020 update.

### Q27. Answer: C (8 hours)
**Why C is correct.** 8-15-4-3 for a 4-week Sprint: Planning = 8 hr max.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A and B: Proportional for shorter Sprints, not 4-week.
- D: 16 hours exceeds the cap.
**Exam takeaway.** 8-15-4-3 mnemonic.

### Q28. Answer: D (all of the above)
**Why D is correct.** Multiple teams on one product share: one PO, one Product Backlog, one Product Goal (and a common DoD).
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: PO only — incomplete.
- B: PO + Backlog only — incomplete.
- C: Goal only — incomplete.
**Exam takeaway.** PO + PB + PG (+ common DoD) shared.

### Q29. Answer: B (Maximize the value of the product)
**Why B is correct.** Direct PO accountability per 2020 Scrum Guide.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Estimating is Developers' job.
- C: Removing impediments is SM.
- D: Daily Scrum is for Developers.
**Exam takeaway.** PO = value; SM = impediments; Devs = how-to.

### Q30. Answer: B (Refer to the PO)
**Why B is correct.** Mid-Sprint changes go through the PO who balances against Sprint Goal.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Adding immediately bypasses PO.
- C: Refusing without explanation is unhelpful.
- D: Adding next Sprint as a default skips the PO's prioritization.
**Exam takeaway.** All backlog changes flow through PO.

### Q31. Answer: B (Does not decrease)
**Why B is correct.** Scrum Guide is explicit: quality does not decrease.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Lowering quality for deadlines is anti-pattern.
- C: NOT SM's call.
- D: "Tech debt sprint" is anti-pattern in pure Scrum.
**Exam takeaway.** Quality is non-negotiable.

### Q32. Answer: B (functional conditions for ONE PBI)
**Why B is correct.** Acceptance Criteria are per-PBI, set by the PO.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: DoD applies to all PBIs.
- C: AC set by PO, not SM.
- D: AC is not in the Scrum Guide (Cohn's *User Stories Applied*).
**Exam takeaway.** AC ≠ DoD. AC per PBI; DoD applies to all.

### Q33. Answer: C (Teaching)
**Why C is correct.** New team needs to *learn* Scrum first — Teach stance dominates.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Coaching presumes the team already knows the framework.
- B: Servant leadership is the default but Teach is the specific stance for new teams.
- D: Impediment removal is later; first they need the framework knowledge.
**Exam takeaway.** New team → Teacher stance.

### Q34. Answer: B (inspect Increment, adapt Product Backlog)
**Why B is correct.** Sprint Review's stated purpose per 2020 Scrum Guide.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: That's the Retrospective.
- C: Release decisions are PO-level, not Review-gated.
- D: NOT a performance review.
**Exam takeaway.** Review = product + adapt backlog.

### Q35. Answer: B (ongoing activity, not formal event)
**Why B is correct.** Scrum Guide: Refinement is an ongoing activity.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: NOT a required event.
- C: NOT only at project start.
- D: NOT post-Retro.
**Exam takeaway.** Refinement happens continuously.

### Q36. Answer: C (not directly comparable)
**Why C is correct.** Story points are team-relative; cross-team comparison is meaningless.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: "Less productive" — false inference.
- B: "Less skilled" — false inference.
- D: "Underperforming" — false inference.
**Exam takeaway.** Never compare team velocities.

### Q37. Answer: B (No — conflict of interest)
**Why B is correct.** PO + SM combined creates fundamental conflict (value vs. process/team protection).
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: "Yes" violates Scrum Guide separation.
- C: Certification doesn't waive the conflict.
- D: Startup size doesn't waive the conflict.
**Exam takeaway.** PO + SM = NEVER.

### Q38. Answer: B (Scrum Team only)
**Why B is correct.** Retrospective is for the Scrum Team — no stakeholders.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Stakeholders attend the Review, not the Retro.
- C: SM is part of the Scrum Team; can't exclude.
- D: Just Developers excludes the PO and SM.
**Exam takeaway.** Retro = Scrum Team only.

### Q39. Answer: B (must meet Definition of Done)
**Why B is correct.** Increment must meet DoD.
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Must be releasable, not necessarily released.
- C: Increment doesn't need to "match Product Goal exactly" each Sprint.
- D: Story points are NOT in Scrum Guide (Cohn's framework).
**Exam takeaway.** Increment + DoD = always.

### Q40. Answer: C (a true leader who serves)
**Why C is correct.** 2020 Scrum Guide rephrasing — "true leader who serves."
**Why the others are wrong.**
- A: Team manager — explicitly NOT.
- B: Project manager — there is no PM in Scrum.
- D: Technical lead — not the SM's role.
**Exam takeaway.** SM is a *leader who serves*, not a manager.

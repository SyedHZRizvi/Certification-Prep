## 📝 Questions

### 1. The Scrum Guide describes Scrum as:
A. A project management methodology
B. A lightweight framework
C. A complete process
D. A complete software development methodology

### 2. The maximum length of a Sprint is:
A. 2 weeks
B. 4 weeks
C. 6 weeks
D. As long as needed

### 3. The 5 Scrum values are:
A. Focus, Commitment, Courage, Respect, Openness
B. Trust, Honesty, Speed, Quality, Value
C. Plan, Execute, Inspect, Adapt, Improve
D. Lead, Serve, Coach, Teach, Mentor

### 4. Who can cancel a Sprint?
A. Scrum Master
B. Product Owner
C. Stakeholders
D. Scrum Team by majority vote

### 5. The Sprint Backlog is owned by:
A. The Product Owner
B. The Scrum Master
C. The Developers
D. The whole Scrum Team

### 6. How many Product Owners exist for one product?
A. One
B. One per team
C. As many as needed
D. None — the team decides

### 7. Empiricism rests on three pillars:
A. Plan, Execute, Review
B. Transparency, Inspection, Adaptation
C. Communicate, Coordinate, Cooperate
D. Standardize, Measure, Improve

### 8. The Definition of Done applies to:
A. The Sprint Goal
B. The Increment
C. The Product Backlog
D. The Sprint Backlog

### 9. Per the 2020 Scrum Guide, Sprint Planning addresses:
A. What, Who, When
B. Why, What, How
C. Goals, Tasks, Risks
D. Plan, Resources, Schedule

### 10. The Daily Scrum is held:
A. Whenever convenient
B. Same time and place every working day
C. Twice per Sprint
D. Only when there are blockers

### 11. The Daily Scrum is for:
A. Status reporting to managers
B. The Developers to plan next 24 hours toward Sprint Goal
C. The Scrum Master to assess team progress
D. Stakeholders to give input

### 12. Per the 2020 Scrum Guide, the Scrum Team is:
A. 5–9 developers + PO + SM
B. Typically 10 or fewer total
C. Exactly 7
D. Up to 15 people

### 13. Stakeholders are invited to which event?
A. Daily Scrum
B. Sprint Planning
C. Sprint Review
D. Sprint Retrospective

### 14. The Product Goal is:
A. The same as the Sprint Goal
B. The objective the team works toward across multiple Sprints
C. A vision document outside Scrum
D. Set by stakeholders

### 15. A team realizes mid-Sprint they cannot deliver everything. The right action:
A. Extend the Sprint
B. Cancel the Sprint
C. Negotiate scope with PO; protect the Sprint Goal
D. Drop quality temporarily

### 16. The Product Backlog is:
A. Static once defined
B. Emergent and continuously evolving
C. Owned by the team collectively
D. Managed by the Scrum Master

### 17. The Sprint Retrospective is for:
A. Demonstrating the Increment
B. Inspecting the team's process and identifying improvements
C. Planning the next Sprint
D. Reviewing the PO's performance

### 18. Story points represent:
A. Hours of effort
B. Days of effort
C. Relative complexity, effort, and uncertainty
D. Number of tasks

### 19. Velocity is best used for:
A. Comparing teams
B. Forecasting future sprints (within the same team)
C. Performance reviews
D. Setting bonuses

### 20. The Scrum Master's accountability includes serving:
A. The Scrum Team only
B. The Scrum Team and the PO
C. The Scrum Team, PO, and the organization
D. Only the Product Owner

### 21. A developer asks the SM how to design a database. The SM should:
A. Provide a design
B. Coach: "What approaches are you considering?"
C. Tell the developer to figure it out alone
D. Ask the architect to design it

### 22. Per the 2020 Scrum Guide, the term "Development Team" was changed to:
A. Engineering Team
B. Delivery Squad
C. Developers
D. Implementation Group

### 23. Multiple Increments in a single Sprint are:
A. Forbidden
B. Allowed per the 2020 Scrum Guide
C. Only allowed in scaled Scrum
D. Only allowed with PO approval

### 24. Definition of Ready is:
A. Required by the Scrum Guide
B. NOT in the Scrum Guide; an optional practice
C. Set by the Scrum Master
D. The same as Definition of Done

### 25. A team consistently misses Sprint Goals. The MOST appropriate action:
A. Tell them to work harder
B. Add more developers
C. Coach them to inspect/adapt at the Retrospective
D. Report them to management

### 26. Per the 2020 Scrum Guide, Scrum is founded on:
A. Agile and Waterfall
B. Empiricism and Lean Thinking
C. Iteration and Documentation
D. Plan and Control

### 27. Sprint Planning for a 4-week Sprint should be at most:
A. 2 hours
B. 4 hours
C. 8 hours
D. 16 hours

### 28. Multiple teams on the same product share:
A. One Product Owner only
B. One Product Backlog only
C. One Product Goal only
D. All of the above

### 29. The PO's primary accountability is:
A. Estimating the work
B. Maximizing the value of the product
C. Removing impediments
D. Running the Daily Scrum

### 30. A stakeholder requests a feature change mid-Sprint. The Developer should:
A. Add it immediately
B. Refer the stakeholder to the Product Owner
C. Refuse without explanation
D. Implement it next Sprint

### 31. The Scrum Guide says quality during a Sprint:
A. Can be lowered to meet deadline
B. Does not decrease
C. Is the SM's call
D. Can be deferred to a "tech debt sprint"

### 32. Acceptance Criteria are:
A. Quality bars for all Increments
B. Functional conditions for ONE PBI
C. Set by the Scrum Master
D. Required by the Scrum Guide

### 33. A team is brand new to Scrum. The SM should LEAD with:
A. Coaching
B. Servant leading
C. Teaching
D. Removing impediments

### 34. The Sprint Review is for:
A. Inspecting the process
B. Inspecting the Increment and adapting the Product Backlog
C. Approving release decisions
D. Evaluating the team's performance

### 35. "Backlog Refinement" is:
A. A required Scrum event
B. An ongoing activity, NOT a formal event
C. Held only at the start of a project
D. Held after the Retrospective

### 36. Two teams' velocities are 30 and 50. Team A is:
A. Less productive
B. Less skilled
C. Not directly comparable to Team B
D. Underperforming

### 37. Can the same person be PO and SM?
A. Yes
B. No — conflict of interest
C. Yes, if certified
D. Only in startups

### 38. The Sprint Retrospective audience:
A. Scrum Team + key stakeholders
B. Scrum Team only
C. PO + Developers (no SM)
D. Just Developers

B. Meet the Definition of Done

B. A project manager
C. A true leader who serves

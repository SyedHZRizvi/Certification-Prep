# Module 2: The Scrum Framework 🏗️

> **Why this module matters:** Scrum has *very few* moving parts. But how they fit together is *everything*. After this module, you'll be able to draw the entire framework on a napkin.

---

## 🍳 A Story: The Cooking Show

Imagine a cooking show. Not a regular one — *Gordon Ramsay's Iron Chef on Speed*.

Every Monday morning, Gordon hands a team of 3 chefs a basket of ingredients (the **Product Backlog**). The chefs huddle and pick what they can make in **2 weeks** (the **Sprint**). They've got one goal: ship an edible dish to a panel of food critics by Friday week 2.

**Every morning at 9 AM**, the chefs meet for 15 minutes (the **Daily Scrum**) — what did you cook yesterday, what will you cook today, anything blocking you?

**On the final Friday**, food critics taste the dish and give brutal feedback (**Sprint Review**). Then, Saturday morning, the chefs meet *without* Gordon to ask: "How can we work better together?" (**Sprint Retrospective**).

Then Monday, they start again. New basket. New 2 weeks. Better cooking each time.

**That's Scrum.** Now let's break it down.

## 🎯 What Is Scrum (Officially)?

Per the **Scrum Guide 2020** (the bible — read it 3+ times):

> *"Scrum is a lightweight framework that helps people, teams, and organizations generate value through adaptive solutions for complex problems."*

Three keywords to memorize:
- **Lightweight** — minimal rules
- **Framework** — NOT a methodology, NOT a process, NOT a recipe
- **Complex problems** — Scrum is for the unknown, not the routine

🚫 **Scrum is NOT:**
- A project management methodology
- A way to ship faster
- A set of best practices
- Something you "fully implement"

✅ **Scrum IS:**
- A framework you fill in with your own practices
- Built on empiricism
- Designed for complex adaptive problems

## 🧱 The Building Blocks

### The Scrum Team (1 team)
- **Product Owner** — owns the *what* and *why* (value)
- **Scrum Master** — owns the *how it's working* (process)
- **Developers** — own the *how* (delivery)
- Total team size: **10 or fewer** people (per 2020 Scrum Guide; pre-2020 was "3-9 developers")
- **Cross-functional** — has all skills needed
- **Self-managing** — decides who, when, how

### Sprint (1 container)
- A timebox of **1 month or less** (typically 2 weeks)
- All work happens *inside* a Sprint
- A Sprint contains: Planning + Daily Scrums + Development Work + Review + Retrospective

### Events (5 ceremonies)
1. **Sprint** — the container itself
2. **Sprint Planning** — start of sprint, ≤8 hrs (for 4-wk sprint)
3. **Daily Scrum** — every day, 15 min, same time/place
4. **Sprint Review** — end of sprint, ≤4 hrs (for 4-wk sprint)
5. **Sprint Retrospective** — end of sprint, ≤3 hrs (for 4-wk sprint)

### Artifacts (3 things you create)
1. **Product Backlog** — ordered list of everything that *might* be done
2. **Sprint Backlog** — what we're doing *this sprint* + sprint goal + plan
3. **Increment** — the working product released this Sprint (must meet DoD)

### Commitments (3 things attached to artifacts)
1. **Product Goal** → attached to Product Backlog
2. **Sprint Goal** → attached to Sprint Backlog
3. **Definition of Done (DoD)** → attached to Increment

🎯 **Exam pattern:** Match artifact to commitment:
- Product Backlog → Product Goal
- Sprint Backlog → Sprint Goal
- Increment → Definition of Done

## 💎 The 5 Scrum Values (From the Scrum Guide)

```
       FOCUS
         |
COURAGE — — RESPECT
         |
   COMMITMENT — OPENNESS
```

| Value | What It Means |
|-------|---------------|
| **Commitment** | We commit to achieving Sprint Goal & supporting each other |
| **Focus** | Eliminate distractions; concentrate on Sprint work |
| **Openness** | Share challenges, blockers, doubts honestly |
| **Respect** | Treat each other as capable, independent people |
| **Courage** | Tackle hard problems; speak hard truths |

🧠 Memory trick: **F-CCRO** ("Focus + Commitment + Courage + Respect + Openness")
Or: **"Frogs Can Cook Real Onions"** 🐸

🎯 **Exam tip:** When you see "the team avoids difficult conversations," the missing value is **Courage** or **Openness**.

## 🔄 The Sprint: The Heartbeat of Scrum

A Sprint is a **container event** — all other events happen inside it.

### Key Sprint Rules (MEMORIZE — these get tested):

1. **Sprints are timeboxed:** 1 month or less. Same length consistently.
2. **No changes that endanger the Sprint Goal.**
3. **Quality does not decrease.** No shortcuts.
4. **Scope may be clarified** with the PO as more is learned.
5. **Only the Product Owner can cancel a Sprint** — but it's *very rare*. Only when the Sprint Goal becomes obsolete.

### Why Sprints Are Short:
- Less risk (you can pivot quickly)
- Faster feedback
- More frequent value delivery
- Less wasted work on wrong assumptions

### Sprint Length Trade-Offs:
| Length | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| 1 week | Fast feedback, less risk | Lots of overhead (events) |
| 2 weeks | Sweet spot for most teams | — |
| 4 weeks | More work per sprint | Less feedback, more risk |

🎯 **Most teams use 2 weeks.** That's the practical default.

## 🚦 What Scrum Doesn't Tell You (And That's The Point)

This trips people up. Scrum is **DELIBERATELY** silent on:

- 🤐 How to estimate (story points? hours? animals? Up to the team)
- 🤐 What tools to use (Jira? sticky notes? whiteboard?)
- 🤐 What "Done" means (the team defines it)
- 🤐 How to break down work (epics? features? user stories?)
- 🤐 Who joins which event (beyond the basics)
- 🤐 What programming practices to follow (XP? TDD? Up to you)

**Scrum is a framework, not a methodology.** This is HUGE for the exam.

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "What does the Scrum Guide say about [story points / Jira / specific technique]?"
- Answer: **Scrum doesn't prescribe this. Teams decide.**

## 🔬 Empiricism + Lean = Scrum's Foundation

The Scrum Guide 2020 added **lean thinking** to the foundation:

> *"Scrum is founded on empiricism and lean thinking. Empiricism asserts that knowledge comes from experience and making decisions based on what is observed. Lean thinking reduces waste and focuses on the essentials."*

**Lean principles in Scrum:**
- Minimize handoffs
- Reduce in-progress work
- Eliminate non-value activities
- Continuously improve

🎯 **Exam tip:** If a question says "lean thinking" or "minimize waste," it's straight from the Scrum Guide.

## 🚫 What Got REMOVED From Scrum (2020 Update)

The 2020 Scrum Guide is a major refresh. **Things that USED to be in Scrum but aren't anymore:**

| Old Term | New Term | Why It Changed |
|----------|----------|----------------|
| Development Team | Developers | Avoid sub-team within Scrum Team |
| Daily Scrum questions (3 questions) | No prescribed format | Less prescriptive |
| 3 Roles | 3 Accountabilities | Roles implied job titles |
| Project | Product | Scrum is for products, not just projects |
| "Servant leader" | "True leader who serves" | Updated terminology |

🚨 **This matters!** Old YouTube videos (pre-2020) teach the *old* version. If the exam contradicts an old video, **trust the Scrum Guide**.

## 🤔 FAQ Cheat Round

**Q: How often should the team release?**
A: At minimum, *once per Sprint* (the Increment must be releasable). Could be more often.

**Q: What if a Sprint goal is achieved early?**
A: Pull more work from the Product Backlog or end the Sprint early (rare). Don't create unnecessary work.

**Q: What if the Sprint goal can't be met?**
A: Talk to the PO. Negotiate scope. **Don't** silently extend or cancel.

**Q: Can a Sprint be longer than 1 month?**
A: **No.** Hard rule. 1 month max.

**Q: Can a Sprint be 0 days (just planning)?**
A: No. Sprints must produce a usable Increment.

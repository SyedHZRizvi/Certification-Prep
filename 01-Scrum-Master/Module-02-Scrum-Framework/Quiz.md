## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. 10 or fewer people total**
The 2020 Scrum Guide changed from "3-9 developers" to "typically 10 or fewer people" total in the Scrum Team.

### Q2: **C. 1 month**
The Sprint is timeboxed to **1 month or less**. 1 month = 4 weeks max.

### Q3: **B. Innovation**
The 5 values: **Focus, Commitment, Courage, Respect, Openness** (FCCRO). Innovation is great, but not a Scrum value.

### Q4: **C. Increment**
- Product Backlog → Product Goal
- Sprint Backlog → Sprint Goal
- **Increment → Definition of Done**

### Q5: **B. Product Owner**
Only the PO can cancel a Sprint. It's rare, only when the Sprint Goal becomes obsolete.

### Q6: **B. The objective of the Sprint**
The Sprint Goal is *the* objective. Not a list of items — a single, coherent goal.

### Q7: **C. Scrum is a framework**
Per Scrum Guide: "Scrum is a lightweight **framework**." Not a methodology, not a process.

### Q8: **C. Negotiate scope with PO**
Sprints aren't extended. Quality isn't dropped. Adding members rarely helps mid-sprint. The right move: developers + PO negotiate scope while protecting the Sprint Goal.

### Q9: **B. Empiricism and Lean thinking**
The 2020 Scrum Guide added "lean thinking" alongside empiricism.

### Q10: **B. Does not decrease**
Quality is non-negotiable in Scrum. You may negotiate scope, never quality.

### Q11: **C. Openness**
Openness enables sharing struggles, blockers, ignorance. Courage is also relevant (admitting requires courage), but Openness is the more direct fit. Both can be argued — exam typically picks Openness for "sharing/admitting" scenarios.

### Q12: **B. Product Backlog**
- **Product Backlog → Product Goal**
- Sprint Backlog → Sprint Goal
- Increment → Definition of Done

### Q13: **B. At least once per Sprint**
Each Sprint must produce a usable Increment. Multiple per sprint = fine. Less than one = not Scrum.

### Q14: **C. Developers**
The 2020 Scrum Guide replaced "Development Team" with "Developers" to avoid implying a sub-team within the Scrum Team.

### Q15: **B. No — Scrum is for complex problems**
Per Scrum Guide: "Scrum is...for complex problems." Stable, well-known requirements may suit predictive (Waterfall) approaches better.

### Q16: **B. Sprint Goal**
- Product Backlog → Product Goal
- **Sprint Backlog → Sprint Goal**
- Increment → Definition of Done

### Q17: **B. May be clarified and renegotiated with the PO**
Scope can be clarified as more is learned. What's prohibited is changes that endanger the Sprint Goal.

### Q18: **C. Courage**
Courage = tackling hard problems and speaking hard truths. Avoiding difficult conversations is a Courage gap (Openness is also adjacent, but Courage is the more direct fit for "difficult conversations").

### Q19: **B. The DoD's specific tools/practices**
Scrum is deliberately silent on tools (Jira), estimation techniques (story points), and specific quality criteria — teams decide. Sprint Goal, Product Backlog, and Retrospective are all prescribed.

### Q20: **C. Focus, Commitment, Courage, Respect, Openness**
FCCRO. "Innovation," "Speed," and "Trust" are not Scrum values.

### Q21: **B. The Product Owner cancels the Sprint**
Only the PO can cancel a Sprint, and it's reserved for cases where the Sprint Goal becomes obsolete.

### Q22: **C. Accountabilities**
The 2020 Scrum Guide replaced "roles" with "accountabilities" because roles imply job titles, while Scrum is a team with shared responsibilities.

### Q23: **B. Too large**
The 2020 guide says "typically 10 or fewer." A team of 14 should split into multiple Scrum Teams sharing the same Product Goal and Backlog.

### Q24: **B. Lean thinking**
The 2020 Scrum Guide added lean thinking explicitly: "Scrum is founded on empiricism and lean thinking."

### Q25: **B. Cross-functional and self-managing**
Two key Scrum Team properties per the Guide. No leads, no managers, no committees.

### Q26: **B. Each Sprint must produce a usable Increment meeting the DoD**
Every Sprint must produce a usable, valuable Increment. Otherwise, you're not doing Scrum.

### Q27: **B. A lightweight framework for complex problems**
Direct from the Scrum Guide. NOT a methodology, NOT a process, NOT best practices.

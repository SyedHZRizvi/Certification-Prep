## Questions

### Q1. Match each artifact to its commitment:

```
Product Backlog → ___
Sprint Backlog → ___
Increment → ___
```

A. (Product Goal, Sprint Goal, Definition of Done)
B. (Sprint Goal, Product Goal, Definition of Done)
C. (Definition of Done, Sprint Goal, Product Goal)
D. (Vision, Roadmap, Backlog)

### Q2. Who owns the Sprint Backlog?
A. The Product Owner
B. The Scrum Master
C. The Developers
D. The Project Manager

### Q3. The Product Goal lives in:
A. A separate document
B. The Product Backlog
C. The Sprint Backlog
D. The team charter

### Q4. The Sprint Backlog consists of:
A. Just user stories
B. Sprint Goal + Selected PBIs + Plan
C. Sprint Goal + tasks
D. PBIs + tests

### Q5. An item passes Acceptance Criteria but fails the Definition of Done. Is it Done?
A. Yes
B. No — both must be met
C. Yes, with PO approval
D. Only if released

### Q6. Definition of Done is:
A. Set per item by the PO
B. A checklist for quality applying to ALL Increments
C. Decided at Sprint Review

### Q7. Multiple Increments in a single Sprint:
A. Are not allowed
B. Are allowed per the 2020 Scrum Guide

### Q8. Acceptance Criteria are:
A. Quality bars for the whole Increment

D. Required by the Scrum Guide

### Q9. The Product Backlog is:
A. A complete, finalized list
B. Emergent and continuously evolving
C. Owned by the team collectively
D. Frozen during a Sprint

### Q10. Mid-Sprint, the team realizes the work won't all fit. The Sprint Goal CAN be achieved but not all PBIs. They should:
A. Extend the Sprint
B. Cancel the Sprint

### Q11. The Sprint Goal is:
A. A list of features to complete
B. The single objective the team commits to

### Q12. Backlog refinement is performed by:
A. The PO alone
B. The Developers alone
C. Collaboratively, with PO leading
D. The Scrum Master

---

## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **A. (Product Goal, Sprint Goal, Definition of Done)**
The mapping is: PB→Product Goal, SB→Sprint Goal, Increment→DoD.

### Q2: **C. The Developers**
Developers own the Sprint Backlog. They created it. They update it daily.

### Q3: **B. The Product Backlog**
Per 2020 Scrum Guide, the Product Goal is *in* the Product Backlog.

### Q4: **B. Sprint Goal + Selected PBIs + Plan**
Three parts (new in 2020).

### Q5: **B. No — both must be met**
DoD is the quality bar; AC is functional. Both must pass.

### Q6: **B. Quality checklist applying to all Increments**
DoD applies to every Increment. Acceptance criteria apply per PBI.

### Q7: **B. Allowed per 2020 Scrum Guide**
"Multiple Increments may be created within a Sprint."

### Q8: **B. Functional conditions for ONE PBI**
Set by PO, validated by team.

### Q9: **B. Emergent and continuously evolving**
Never "complete." Always being refined.

### Q10: **C. Negotiate scope; protect Sprint Goal**
The Sprint Goal stays. PBI scope flexes.

### Q11: **B. Single objective**
One coherent goal — not a checklist.

### Q12: **C. Collaboratively, with PO leading**
Refinement is a team activity. PO leads but team participates.

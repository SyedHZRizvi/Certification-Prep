# Module 4: The 5 Scrum Events 🎭

> **Why this module matters:** ~25% of the exam tests events. Timeboxes, attendees, purposes — these are easy points if you've memorized them. Lose them by being lazy.

---

## 🎬 A Story: A Day In The Life

It's Monday, 9 AM. Sarah's Scrum team begins a new 2-week Sprint.

**9:00 AM — Sprint Planning** 🎯
The team huddles for 4 hours. They review the top of the Product Backlog with the PO. They craft a **Sprint Goal**: *"Enable users to reset passwords via email."* They pull 12 items, plan how they'll tackle them, and craft a Sprint Backlog. By 1 PM, they're off.

**Each day, 9:15 AM — Daily Scrum** 🤝
For 15 minutes, the developers sync. *"What did I do yesterday? What will I do today? Any impediments?"* The Scrum Master watches for blockers but doesn't run the meeting. By 9:30, everyone's coding.

**Friday, end of Week 2 — Sprint Review** 🎁
The team demos to stakeholders. *"Here's password reset, working live."* Stakeholders give feedback: "Can we add SMS reset too?" PO updates the backlog. 90 minutes. Done.

**Friday, after Review — Retrospective** 🪞
The team meets *without* stakeholders. *"What worked? What didn't? What should we change?"* They identify: "Daily Scrums ran 30 min, not 15. Let's set a timer." 60 minutes. Done.

**Monday — New Sprint Begins** 🔁
Sarah's team starts again. Better than last sprint. Forever improving.

**That's all 5 Scrum events in one Sprint.** Now let's break each one down.

## 🏟️ Event 1: The Sprint (The Container)

The Sprint is itself an event! It's the **container** that holds all the others.

### The Rules:
- ⏱️ **Timebox:** 1 month or less (typically 2 weeks)
- 🔁 **Same length** consistently (don't switch between 1 and 4 weeks)
- 🚫 **No changes that endanger the Sprint Goal**
- 📈 **Quality does not decrease**
- 📋 **Scope may be clarified** with PO as more is learned
- 🛑 **Only PO can cancel** (rare)

### Why Sprints Are Short:
- Less risk
- Faster feedback
- Frequent value delivery

### The Sprint Lifecycle:
```
Sprint Planning → [Work + Daily Scrums × N days] → Sprint Review → Retrospective → END
                                                                                     ↓
                                                                                NEW SPRINT
```

🎯 **Trap question:** "Can a Sprint last 6 weeks?" **NO.** Max 1 month.

## 🎯 Event 2: Sprint Planning

### One-Sentence Definition
> *"Sprint Planning initiates the Sprint by laying out the work to be performed for the Sprint."*

### When: Start of every Sprint
### Timebox: **8 hours** for a 4-week Sprint (proportionally less for shorter)
- 1-week Sprint → 2 hr
- 2-week Sprint → 4 hr
- 4-week Sprint → 8 hr

### Attendees:
- ✅ Whole Scrum Team
- ✅ Possibly invited subject experts (if needed)

### The 3 Topics (NEW IN 2020 GUIDE):

#### Topic 1: WHY is this Sprint valuable?
- The PO proposes how the product can increase value this Sprint.
- The team collaborates to define the **Sprint Goal**.

#### Topic 2: WHAT can be Done this Sprint?
- Developers work with PO to select Product Backlog items.
- Developers may refine items further.
- Selected items + Sprint Goal = first part of Sprint Backlog.

#### Topic 3: HOW will the chosen work get done?
- Developers plan how to deliver each PBI to meet the Definition of Done.
- Often broken into tasks of < 1 day.
- Result = the rest of the Sprint Backlog.

### Output: Sprint Backlog
Contains:
- Sprint Goal (the **why**)
- Selected PBIs (the **what**)
- Plan to deliver them (the **how**)

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "What 3 topics does Sprint Planning address?" → **Why, What, How**

### Common Mistakes:
- ❌ Planning runs over (poor PO prep, no DoD, unclear PBIs)
- ❌ Stakeholders try to add scope mid-meeting
- ❌ Skipping the "Why" — diving straight into tasks
- ❌ Treating it as a status meeting

## 🤝 Event 3: Daily Scrum

### One-Sentence Definition
> *"The purpose of the Daily Scrum is to inspect progress toward the Sprint Goal and adapt the Sprint Backlog as necessary."*

### When: Every working day
### Timebox: **15 minutes**, max
### Same time, same place

### Attendees:
- ✅ Developers (mandatory)
- ✅ PO can attend if they're a developer too
- ✅ SM can attend (especially early in team formation)
- ❌ Stakeholders typically don't attend

### The Big 2020 Change:
**The 3 questions are NO LONGER PRESCRIBED.**

Old guide had:
1. What did I do yesterday?
2. What will I do today?
3. Any impediments?

The 2020 guide says: format is up to the team. As long as it focuses on:
- Progress toward Sprint Goal
- Adapt the Sprint Backlog
- Plan next 24 hours

Many teams still use the 3 questions because they work — but they're not *required*.

### Common Mistakes:
- ❌ Running over 15 minutes
- ❌ Becoming a status meeting for the manager
- ❌ Going around the room reciting (instead of *real* coordination)
- ❌ Solving problems in the meeting (take it offline!)

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "Daily Scrum has run 30 minutes for 3 days. What should the SM do?"
- Right: **Coach the team to focus the meeting on the Sprint Goal and timebox to 15 min.**

## 🎁 Event 4: Sprint Review

### One-Sentence Definition
> *"The purpose of the Sprint Review is to inspect the outcome of the Sprint and determine future adaptations."*

### When: End of Sprint, before Retrospective
### Timebox: **4 hours** for a 4-week Sprint (proportionally less)
- 1-week Sprint → 1 hr
- 2-week Sprint → 2 hr
- 4-week Sprint → 4 hr

### Attendees:
- ✅ Scrum Team
- ✅ Stakeholders (THE ONE EVENT they actively join)
- ✅ Customers, when valuable

### Purpose:
- 🎁 **Demo** the Increment
- 💬 **Discuss** what was done & what's next
- 🔄 **Adapt** the Product Backlog based on feedback

### What It's NOT:
- ❌ Not a "sign-off" or formal acceptance gate
- ❌ Not a status report meeting
- ❌ Not just a demo — it's a **collaborative working session**

### Common Mistakes:
- ❌ Treating it as a one-way demo
- ❌ Showing only completed items (incomplete are also valuable to inspect)
- ❌ No real stakeholders attending
- ❌ Confusing it with the Retrospective

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "Stakeholders disagree about new feature direction at the Sprint Review. Who decides?"
- Right: **The PO decides** how to update the backlog.

---

## 🪞 Event 5: Sprint Retrospective

### One-Sentence Definition
> *"The purpose of the Sprint Retrospective is to plan ways to increase quality and effectiveness."*

### When: After Sprint Review, before next Sprint Planning
### Timebox: **3 hours** for a 4-week Sprint (proportionally less)

### Attendees:
- ✅ Scrum Team only (PO, SM, Developers)
- ❌ NO stakeholders (this is the one event stakeholders don't attend)

### Purpose:
- 🪞 **Inspect** how the last Sprint went (people, interactions, processes, tools, DoD)
- 💡 **Identify** improvements
- 🎯 **Plan** at least one improvement to act on next Sprint

### Common Formats:
- "What went well / What didn't / What to try"
- "Start, Stop, Continue"
- "Sailboat" (winds, anchors, icebergs, island)
- "4Ls: Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed for"

### Common Mistakes:
- ❌ Skipping it ("we don't have time")
- ❌ Action items that never happen
- ❌ Blame-fest instead of process focus
- ❌ Inviting stakeholders (they shouldn't be there)
- ❌ Always talking about the same issue — never resolving it

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "The team complains the Retrospective is a waste. What should the SM do?"
- Right: **Coach the team to make it more meaningful (different formats, action follow-through)**, NOT skip it.

## ⏱️ The Timebox Cheat Sheet

For a **4-WEEK Sprint** (the maximum):

| Event | Timebox |
|-------|---------|
| Sprint | 4 weeks max |
| Sprint Planning | **8 hours** |
| Daily Scrum | **15 minutes** |
| Sprint Review | **4 hours** |
| Sprint Retrospective | **3 hours** |

**For shorter sprints, scale proportionally.**

For a 2-week Sprint:
- Planning: 4 hours
- Review: 2 hours
- Retro: 1.5 hours

🧠 **Memory trick:** "**8-15-4-3**" (Planning-Daily-Review-Retro for 4-week Sprint)

## 🚫 What's NOT a Scrum Event?

The exam will tempt you with fakes. **These are NOT Scrum events:**
- ❌ Backlog Refinement — it's an *ongoing activity*, not a formal event
- ❌ Sprint Zero — not a thing in Scrum
- ❌ Pre-Sprint Planning — not a thing
- ❌ Scrum of Scrums — used in scaling, not a core Scrum event
- ❌ Demo (separate from Sprint Review) — Review IS the demo+more
- ❌ Stakeholder Check-In — informal, not prescribed

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "Which is NOT a Scrum event?" Watch for any of the above as wrong-answer traps.

---

## 🎓 Backlog Refinement: The Activity (Not Event)

The Scrum Guide mentions **Product Backlog Refinement** but it's NOT a formal event:

> *"Refinement is the act of breaking down and further defining Product Backlog items into smaller, more precise items. This is an ongoing activity..."*

- ⏱️ Typically takes ≤10% of the team's time
- 🤝 Done by the team (with PO leading)
- 🚫 Doesn't have a fixed schedule
- ✅ Can happen anytime, often once or twice mid-Sprint

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "How often is Backlog Refinement?" → **Continuously, as needed.** Not "once per Sprint."

## 🎯 Critical Distinctions

### Sprint Review vs Retrospective
| Sprint Review | Retrospective |
|---------------|---------------|
| Inspect **product** | Inspect **process** |
| Stakeholders welcome | Scrum Team only |
| 4 hr (4-wk Sprint) | 3 hr (4-wk Sprint) |
| Outcome: backlog updates | Outcome: process improvement |
| Before Retro | After Review |

🎯 Memorize: **"Review = product. Retro = process."**

### Daily Scrum vs Status Meeting
| Daily Scrum | Status Meeting |
|-------------|----------------|
| For team coordination | For manager updates |
| 15 min, fixed | Variable length |
| No managers needed | Manager-driven |
| Inspect progress to Sprint Goal | Report status |

## 🧠 Real Scenarios

### Scenario A:
> *Sprint Planning ran 6 hours for a 1-week Sprint.*

❌ Way too long. 1-week Sprint = 2 hr Planning max.

### Scenario B:
> *Stakeholders want to attend the Retrospective.*

❌ No. Retros are for the Scrum Team only. Stakeholders attend the Review.

### Scenario C:
> *The team wants to skip the Daily Scrum because they're "always in Slack anyway."*

❌ No. The Daily is a required Scrum event. SM coaches on the value.

### Scenario D:
> *Sprint Review starts late. Team feels rushed and skips the Retrospective.*

❌ Don't skip the Retro. It's required. Reschedule if needed but never eliminate.

### Scenario E:
> *PO wants to extend the Sprint by 3 days to finish a feature.*

❌ No. Sprints aren't extended. Either accept incomplete work, or negotiate scope.

## 🎓 Key Terms (Anki time!)

| Term | Quick Def |
|------|-----------|
| **Sprint** | The container event, ≤1 month |
| **Sprint Planning** | Start; defines Why, What, How (max 8 hr / 4-wk Sprint) |
| **Daily Scrum** | 15 min, daily; for Developers; inspects Sprint progress |
| **Sprint Review** | End; demo + adapt backlog (max 4 hr / 4-wk) |
| **Sprint Retrospective** | After Review; inspect process (max 3 hr / 4-wk) |
| **Backlog Refinement** | Ongoing activity, NOT a formal event |
| **Sprint Goal** | The Sprint's objective, set in Planning |
| **Timebox** | Maximum allowed time for an event |

# Module 7: Servant Leadership 🛡️

> **Why this module matters:** ~20% of PSM I questions test the *behavior* of a Scrum Master. Knowing the "right" thing to do in tricky scenarios is the difference between passing and failing.

---

## 🛡️ A Story: The Castle's Greatest Knight

In every kingdom, there's a knight everyone admires. Some are admired because they conquer. Others because they protect. The Scrum Master is the second kind.

The Scrum Master doesn't sit on the throne. They don't issue commands. They don't take credit when things go well. They walk among the team, removing rocks from the path, fetching water during long marches, standing between the team and incoming arrows.

When the team succeeds, the Scrum Master fades into the background. When the team struggles, the Scrum Master is the first to ask: *"How can I help?"*

That's **servant leadership**. And it's the hardest leadership style to do well.

---

## 📖 The Scrum Guide On Leadership

The 2020 Scrum Guide says:

> *"Scrum Masters are true leaders who serve the Scrum Team and the larger organization."*

Note the language: **"true leaders who serve."** The 2020 update softened "servant leader" because some misread it as *subservient*. A true leader **leads** — but their leadership manifests through *service*.

🎯 **Exam tip:** Both "servant leader" and "true leader who serves" are correct. Don't get tripped up.

---

## 🌟 What Servant Leadership Looks Like

### 5 Behaviors of a Great Scrum Master:

#### 1. 👂 Listens More Than They Talk
- Asks open-ended questions
- Repeats back to confirm understanding
- Doesn't jump to "let me solve that"

#### 2. 🛡️ Protects the Team
- Says "no" to interruptions
- Pushes back on scope creep mid-Sprint
- Shields from organizational noise

#### 3. 🤝 Removes Impediments
- Sees a blocker → acts on it
- "What's preventing you from delivering?" daily
- Goes upstream to fix root causes, not just symptoms

#### 4. 🧑‍🏫 Coaches, Not Manages
- Asks "What do you think we should do?"
- Lets the team struggle (productively) before offering answers
- Builds capability, not dependency

#### 5. 🌱 Grows Others
- Develops Product Owner skills
- Coaches Developers in agile thinking
- Helps the org understand Scrum

## 🚧 The 5 "Stances" of a Scrum Master

Per the Scrum.org training, a Scrum Master operates in 5 stances:

### Stance 1: 🛡️ Servant Leader
The default. Removing impediments, supporting team's growth.

### Stance 2: 🎓 Coach
Helping people learn through questions, not answers.

### Stance 3: 🛠️ Facilitator
Designing and running effective conversations / events.

### Stance 4: 🧩 Teacher
Explaining Scrum, agile principles, technical practices.

### Stance 5: 🚧 Impediment Remover / Change Agent
Going beyond the team to remove org-level blockers.

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "A team is new to Scrum. Which Scrum Master stance is most needed?"
- **Teacher** — they need to learn the framework first.

---

## 🎯 Coaching vs Mentoring vs Teaching

These get confused. Know the difference:

| Mode | When | What It Looks Like |
|------|------|-------------------|
| **Teaching** | New skill needed | Direct instruction, framework explanation |
| **Mentoring** | Career/skill growth | Sharing your experience |
| **Coaching** | Performance/insight | Asking questions, no answers |
| **Facilitating** | Group conversation | Designing and running |
| **Consulting** | Specific advice on a topic | "Here's what I'd do" |

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "A developer asks the SM for advice. The SM responds with: 'What do *you* think you should do?' This is:"
- **Coaching.**

## 🚧 Removing Impediments: The Art

Not all impediments are equal. The Scrum Master prioritizes by impact:

### Level 1: Within The Team
- Conflict between developers
- Unclear requirements
- Tooling issues
- *SM addresses directly with team coaching*

### Level 2: Between Team and Org
- Slow procurement
- Blocked access
- Cross-team dependencies
- *SM works with managers/peer SMs to resolve*

### Level 3: Organizational
- Unclear strategy
- Budget freezes
- Culture issues
- *SM influences leaders, may take time*

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "An impediment is too big for the SM to solve alone. What should they do?"
- ❌ Wrong: "Tell the team to work around it"
- ❌ Wrong: "Wait for it to resolve itself"
- ✅ Right: "Escalate / engage with the right org partners to remove it"

---

## 🚦 The "Tell vs Coach" Continuum

When should an SM just **tell** the team something?

**Tell when:**
- It's a Scrum Guide rule (e.g., timebox, attendees)
- It's a safety/compliance issue
- It's a true emergency

**Coach when:**
- It's a "how" question the team should solve
- It's about team dynamics
- It's about improving practices

🎯 **Common trap:** SM jumps to "tell" mode for things the team should figure out.

## 🌍 Serving The Organization

This is the part many Scrum Masters skip — but it's *required* by the Scrum Guide.

### Examples:
- 📚 Teaching managers what Scrum is (and isn't)
- 🏗️ Helping HR understand new role definitions
- 🔄 Working with finance on different funding models (per-sprint vs annual)
- 📊 Coaching leaders on metrics that matter (vs vanity metrics)
- 🎯 Supporting transformations across multiple teams

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "A Scrum Master is asked to help train another team's leadership. Should they?"
- **Yes** — serving the organization is part of their accountability.

---

## 🚫 What Servant Leadership Is NOT

### NOT: A Doormat
- ❌ Doing whatever the team says
- ❌ Letting bad behavior slide
- ❌ Avoiding hard conversations

True servant leadership requires **courage** to challenge.

### NOT: A Project Manager With a New Title
- ❌ Assigning tasks
- ❌ Tracking individual performance
- ❌ Being the team's voice to management

### NOT: A Cheerleader
- ❌ Only saying positive things
- ❌ Avoiding conflict
- ❌ Toxic positivity

## 🎭 Real Scenarios

### Scenario A:
> *Team consistently misses Sprint Goals. SM is frustrated.*

❌ Wrong: Yell at the team
❌ Wrong: Tell the PO to plan less
✅ Right: Coach the team to inspect their planning at the Retrospective. Help them identify root causes (uncertainty? unclear requirements? interruptions?). Let them solve it.

### Scenario B:
> *Manager asks SM: "Who are your slowest developers?"*

❌ Wrong: Provide a ranking
✅ Right: Educate the manager — Scrum doesn't measure individuals. The team is collectively accountable. Redirect to team-level metrics if needed.

### Scenario C:
> *Two developers are having a personal conflict.*

❌ Wrong: Tell HR immediately
❌ Wrong: Ignore it
✅ Right: Have a private conversation. Help them resolve (or escalate if truly needed). Cultivate respect.

### Scenario D:
> *PO insists on adding scope mid-Sprint that endangers the Sprint Goal.*

❌ Wrong: Allow it
❌ Wrong: Yell at the PO
✅ Right: Coach the PO on Scrum rules. Suggest holding the change for next Sprint. If urgent, the PO can cancel the Sprint (rare).

### Scenario E:
> *Team is "going through the motions" of Scrum but not really learning.*

❌ Wrong: Lecture them
✅ Right: Make Scrum events more meaningful. Try new Retrospective formats. Bring in real customer feedback. Show them the WHY.

## 🛠️ Tools Servant Leaders Use

- 📊 **Powerful Questions:** "What stops us?" "What would help?"
- 🎯 **The 5 Whys:** Drill to root cause
- 🪞 **Reflective Listening:** Repeat back what you heard
- 🤝 **Active Listening:** Eye contact, no interrupting, summarize
- 🏗️ **Liberating Structures:** Facilitation patterns (e.g., 1-2-4-All)
- 📈 **Coaching Models:** GROW (Goal, Reality, Options, Will)

🎯 Not all of these are tested — but understanding the *spirit* is.

## 🎓 Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Servant Leader** | Leader who serves the team's needs |
| **True Leader Who Serves** | 2020 phrasing, same idea |
| **Coaching** | Asking questions to develop the person |
| **Teaching** | Direct instruction on a skill/concept |
| **Facilitation** | Designing & running effective meetings |
| **Impediment** | Anything blocking team progress |
| **5 Stances of SM** | Servant leader, coach, facilitator, teacher, change agent |

- 🛡️ SM = "true leader who serves"

- 🚧 Impediments come in 3 levels (team, org-bridge, org-wide)

# ✏️ Module 7 Quiz: Servant Leadership

## Questions

### Q1. The 2020 Scrum Guide describes the Scrum Master as:
A. A manager
B. A true leader who serves
C. A team coordinator
D. A delivery enforcer

### Q2. A developer asks the Scrum Master: "What's the best way to design this database?" The Scrum Master should:
A. Provide a detailed design
B. Tell the developer to figure it out alone
C. Coach: "What approach are you considering? Why?"
D. Forward the question to the architect

### Q3. Which is NOT one of the 5 stances of a Scrum Master?
A. Servant leader
B. Coach
C. Manager
D. Teacher

### Q4. A team is brand-new to Scrum. The Scrum Master should LEAD with which stance?
A. Coach
B. Servant leader
C. Teacher
D. Change agent

### Q5. A manager asks the SM to identify the slowest developers. The SM should:
A. Provide names privately
B. Educate the manager that Scrum measures the team, not individuals
C. Refuse and escalate
D. Make up numbers

### Q6. The Scrum Master's accountability includes serving:
A. The Scrum Team only
B. The Scrum Team and PO
C. The Scrum Team, PO, and the larger organization
D. Only the Product Owner

### Q7. Coaching primarily uses:
A. Direct instructions
B. Open-ended questions
C. Demonstrations
D. Status reports

### Q8. An impediment is too large for the SM to remove alone. They should:
A. Tell the team to work around it
B. Ignore it
C. Engage the right partners (managers, peers) to remove it
D. Cancel the Sprint

### Q9. A SM enforcing the Daily Scrum's 15-minute timebox is in which stance?
A. Coach
B. Servant leader
C. Teacher
D. Facilitator (and possibly Teacher)

### Q10. Servant leadership does NOT mean:
A. Listening more than talking
B. Being a doormat
C. Removing impediments
D. Coaching the team

### Q11. The Scrum Master notices the team avoids hard discussions. The MOST likely missing Scrum value is:
A. Focus
B. Courage
C. Commitment
D. Respect

### Q12. A new Scrum Master should focus first on:
A. Setting a velocity target
B. Teaching the team about Scrum and building trust
C. Building dashboards
D. Reporting to management

---

## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. True leader who serves**
Per 2020 Scrum Guide.

### Q2: **C. Coach with questions**
Coaching > telling. Builds developer's own thinking.

### Q3: **C. Manager**
SM is NOT a manager. The 5: servant leader, coach, facilitator, teacher, change agent.

### Q4: **C. Teacher**
New teams need to learn Scrum first. Coaching can come later.

### Q5: **B. Educate the manager**
Scrum measures team-level outcomes, not individual performance.

### Q6: **C. Team, PO, and organization**
All three groups are explicitly listed in the Scrum Guide.

### Q7: **B. Open-ended questions**
"What do you think?" "Why?" "What else?"

### Q8: **C. Engage partners to remove it**
Escalate, partner, work the system. Don't let it sit.

### Q9: **D. Facilitator (and possibly Teacher)**
Running effective events = facilitation; explaining the timebox = teaching.

### Q10: **B. Being a doormat**
Servant leaders have courage to challenge.

### Q11: **B. Courage**
Avoiding hard truths = lack of courage. Openness is also relevant, but courage is the more direct fit.

### Q12: **B. Teach Scrum, build trust**
First duty: foundational understanding.

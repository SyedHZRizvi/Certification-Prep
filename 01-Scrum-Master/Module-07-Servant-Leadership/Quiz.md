# ✏️ Module 7 Quiz: Servant Leadership

## Questions

### Q1. The 2020 Scrum Guide describes the Scrum Master as:
A. A manager
B. A true leader who serves
C. A team coordinator
D. A delivery enforcer

### Q2. A developer asks the Scrum Master: "What's the best way to design this database?" The Scrum Master should:
A. Provide a detailed design
B. Tell the developer to figure it out alone
C. Coach: "What approach are you considering? Why?"
D. Forward the question to the architect

### Q3. Which is NOT one of the 5 stances of a Scrum Master?
A. Servant leader
B. Coach
C. Manager
D. Teacher

### Q4. A team is brand-new to Scrum. The Scrum Master should LEAD with which stance?
A. Coach
B. Servant leader
C. Teacher
D. Change agent

### Q5. A manager asks the SM to identify the slowest developers. The SM should:
A. Provide names privately
B. Educate the manager that Scrum measures the team, not individuals
C. Refuse and escalate
D. Make up numbers

### Q6. The Scrum Master's accountability includes serving:
A. The Scrum Team only
B. The Scrum Team and PO
C. The Scrum Team, PO, and the larger organization
D. Only the Product Owner

### Q7. Coaching primarily uses:
A. Direct instructions
B. Open-ended questions
C. Demonstrations
D. Status reports

### Q8. An impediment is too large for the SM to remove alone. They should:
A. Tell the team to work around it
B. Ignore it
C. Engage the right partners (managers, peers) to remove it
D. Cancel the Sprint

### Q9. A SM enforcing the Daily Scrum's 15-minute timebox is in which stance?
A. Coach
B. Servant leader
C. Teacher
D. Facilitator (and possibly Teacher)

### Q10. Servant leadership does NOT mean:
A. Listening more than talking
B. Being a doormat
C. Removing impediments
D. Coaching the team

### Q11. The Scrum Master notices the team avoids hard discussions. The MOST likely missing Scrum value is:
A. Focus
B. Courage
C. Commitment
D. Respect

### Q12. A new Scrum Master should focus first on:
A. Setting a velocity target
B. Teaching the team about Scrum and building trust
C. Building dashboards
D. Reporting to management

### Q13. The 5 stances of a Scrum Master are:
A. Manager, Coordinator, Tracker, Reporter, Coach
B. Servant Leader, Coach, Facilitator, Teacher, Change Agent
C. Project Manager, Architect, Coach, Mentor, Tester
D. Scrum Master, Product Owner, Developer, Stakeholder, Customer

### Q14. A cross-team dependency is blocking your team. The impediment is at which level, and what should the SM do?
A. Level 1 (within team) — handle in the next Daily Scrum
B. Level 2 (team-to-org bridge) — work with peer SMs and managers to resolve
C. Level 3 (org-wide) — escalate to the CEO immediately

### Q15. A team always defers to the Scrum Master for decisions. The SM should:
A. Keep deciding to keep velocity high
B. Refuse to engage with the team
C. Coach the team toward self-management by asking questions and resisting the urge to solve everything
D. Escalate to management

### Q16. A new developer asks the SM to explain how Sprint Planning works. The most appropriate stance is:
A. Coach
B. Teacher
C. Facilitator
D. Change Agent

### Q17. The SM must ALSO serve which of the following?
A. The team only
B. The team and PO only
C. The team, PO, and the larger organization
D. Stakeholders only

### Q18. Which is NOT an example of servant leadership?
A. Removing impediments for the team
B. Telling the Developers exactly which tasks to take
C. Coaching the PO on backlog management
D. Listening more than talking

### Q19. A SM is mediating a tense conflict between two Developers. The right approach is:
A. Avoid the topic — let them work it out
B. Hold a private conversation with each, then facilitate resolution
C. Assign blame

### Q20. In the coaching vs telling continuum, the SM should TELL when:
A. The team disagrees about a refinement decision
B. It involves a Scrum Guide rule, safety issue, or true emergency

### Q21. The Scrum Master notices the team avoids hard truths in the Retrospective. The SM should:

D. Cancel the next Sprint

### Q22. A SM helping HR understand new role definitions in Scrum is operating as a:
A. Project Manager
B. Change Agent / serving the organization
C. Doormat

### Q23. Which of the following is the BEST example of coaching?
A. "Implement this design pattern."
B. "What options have you considered, and what are the trade-offs?"
C. "Let me show you how I did this last time."
D. "Do it however you want."

### Q24. The SM observes that a team is "going through the motions" of Scrum events without learning. The best response is:
A. Cancel future events
B. Make events meaningful by experimenting with formats, bringing real customer feedback, and showing the WHY
C. Lecture the team about Scrum theory
D. Replace the team

---

## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. True leader who serves**
Per 2020 Scrum Guide.

### Q2: **C. Coach with questions**
Coaching > telling. Builds developer's own thinking.

### Q3: **C. Manager**
SM is NOT a manager. The 5: servant leader, coach, facilitator, teacher, change agent.

### Q4: **C. Teacher**
New teams need to learn Scrum first. Coaching can come later.

### Q5: **B. Educate the manager**
Scrum measures team-level outcomes, not individual performance.

### Q6: **C. Team, PO, and organization**
All three groups are explicitly listed in the Scrum Guide.

### Q7: **B. Open-ended questions**
"What do you think?" "Why?" "What else?"

### Q8: **C. Engage partners to remove it**
Escalate, partner, work the system. Don't let it sit.

### Q9: **D. Facilitator (and possibly Teacher)**
Running effective events = facilitation; explaining the timebox = teaching.

### Q10: **B. Being a doormat**
Servant leaders have courage to challenge.

### Q11: **B. Courage**
Avoiding hard truths = lack of courage. Openness is also relevant, but courage is the more direct fit.

### Q12: **B. Teach Scrum, build trust**
First duty: foundational understanding.

### Q13: **B. Servant Leader, Coach, Facilitator, Teacher, Change Agent**
The five Scrum.org stances. "Manager" is never one of them.

### Q14: **B. Level 2 (team-to-org bridge) — work with peer SMs and managers to resolve**
Cross-team dependencies sit between the team and the org. The SM partners with peers to remove them.

### Q15: **C. Coach the team toward self-management**
The SM builds capability, not dependency. Asking questions develops the team's own decision-making muscle.

### Q16: **B. Teacher**
Explaining how a Scrum event works = direct instruction = teaching.

### Q17: **C. The team, PO, and the larger organization**
Per the Scrum Guide, the SM serves all three groups.

### Q18: **B. Telling Developers exactly which tasks to take**
Servant leaders coach and remove impediments — they do not assign tasks.

### Q19: **B. Private conversation with each, then facilitate resolution**
The SM addresses team-level conflict directly, with respect and confidentiality, before escalating.

### Q20: **B. Scrum Guide rule, safety issue, or true emergency**
Tell mode is reserved for non-negotiables. For "how" questions, coach.

### Q21: **B. Foster psychological safety and model courage**
Avoiding hard truths is a values gap (courage). The SM cultivates an environment where honesty is safe.

### Q22: **B. Change Agent / serving the organization**
Helping HR (or finance, leadership) adapt to Scrum is part of serving the organization.

### Q23: **B. "What options have you considered, and what are the trade-offs?"**
Coaching = open-ended questions that develop the person's own thinking. Telling, demonstrating, or abandoning are not coaching.

### Q24: **B. Make events meaningful with new formats and real feedback**
The fix for "Scrum theater" is bringing the WHY back into the events — not lecturing or scrapping them.

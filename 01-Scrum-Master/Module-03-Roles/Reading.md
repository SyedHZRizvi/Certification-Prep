# Module 3: The Scrum Roles (Accountabilities) 🎭

> **Why this module matters:** ~30% of PSM I exam questions test role boundaries. "Who decides X?" "Whose job is Y?" Get this right and you bank a third of the exam.

---

## 🎬 A Story: The Movie Set

Picture a movie set.

The **Director** (Product Owner) has the vision. They decide what scenes to shoot, in what order, and what makes the cut. They're obsessed with the *audience experience*.

The **Crew** (Developers) — cinematographers, sound techs, makeup, etc. — figure out *how* to actually capture the director's vision. They're the experts. They self-organize: nobody tells the lighting tech where to stand.

The **Producer** (Scrum Master) doesn't direct the movie or operate cameras. They make sure the trailer arrives, the catering is good, the union rules are followed, and the director isn't being a tyrant. They serve everyone — and protect the film from chaos.

**That's the Scrum Team.** Three accountabilities, no managers, no project managers, no tech leads.

---

## ⚠️ A Critical 2020 Update

The Scrum Guide 2020 stopped calling these "**roles**" and started calling them "**accountabilities**." Why?

> Roles imply *job titles*. But Scrum is a *team* — everyone is a member, with one of three accountabilities. A "Scrum Master" is a person with the *Scrum Master accountability* — not a job description.

🎯 **Exam tip:** The exam may use "roles" or "accountabilities" interchangeably, but if asked which is correct per the 2020 Guide, **accountabilities** wins.

## 👤 Accountability 1: The Product Owner (PO)

### One-Sentence Definition
> *"The Product Owner is accountable for maximizing the value of the product resulting from the work of the Scrum Team."*

### What The PO Does
- 📋 **Manages the Product Backlog** — orders it, refines it, makes it transparent
- 🎯 **Develops & communicates the Product Goal**
- ✍️ **Creates Product Backlog items** (PBIs / user stories)
- 🗂️ **Orders the backlog** (priorities, value, risk)
- 👀 **Ensures backlog is visible & understood** by the team
- 💰 **Maximizes value** — the #1 KPI

### What The PO Doesn't Do
- ❌ Tell the developers HOW to build something
- ❌ Estimate the work (developers do)
- ❌ Manage the team (no one manages the team!)
- ❌ Run the Daily Scrum (Daily is for Developers)
- ❌ Be a "proxy" between team and stakeholders (PO is the *real* decision-maker)

### PO Power Rules
1. **The Product Owner is ONE person.** Not a committee. Not a "PO Team."
2. **The PO has FINAL authority on backlog order.** Even when stakeholders disagree.
3. **The PO can be the same person as a Developer** (theoretically) — but not the Scrum Master.

### Common PO Anti-Patterns
- 😱 **"Proxy PO"** — PO has no real authority; passes everything to a "real" decision-maker
- 😱 **"Order-taker PO"** — Just transcribes stakeholder requests; doesn't prioritize
- 😱 **"Absent PO"** — Never available to clarify; team blocked constantly
- 😱 **"Dictator PO"** — Tells developers HOW to build (overstepping)

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "Stakeholders disagree on priority. What should happen?"
- Right: **PO decides**, possibly after consulting stakeholders.
- Wrong: "Vote" / "Sponsor decides" / "Escalate."

## 🛡️ Accountability 2: The Scrum Master (SM)

### One-Sentence Definition
> *"The Scrum Master is accountable for establishing Scrum as defined in the Scrum Guide. They do this by helping everyone understand Scrum theory and practice, both within the Scrum Team and the organization."*

### What The SM Does (Three Sets Of Customers!)

The Scrum Master serves **three groups**:

#### 1️⃣ Serves the Scrum Team
- Coaches in self-management & cross-functionality
- Helps focus on creating high-value Increments
- Removes impediments
- Ensures Scrum events are positive, productive, timeboxed

#### 2️⃣ Serves the Product Owner
- Helps PO with backlog management techniques
- Helps establish empirical product planning
- Facilitates stakeholder collaboration

#### 3️⃣ Serves the Organization
- Leads/coaches the org in adopting Scrum
- Plans/advises Scrum implementations
- Removes barriers between stakeholders & team
- Helps employees understand what's empirical and complex

### What The SM Doesn't Do
- ❌ **Manage** the team (Scrum Masters don't manage!)
- ❌ **Assign tasks** (developers self-organize)
- ❌ **Manage the backlog** (that's the PO)
- ❌ **Estimate work** (developers do)
- ❌ **Make product decisions** (PO does)
- ❌ **Be a "secretary"** for the team

### Scrum Master Power Rules
1. The SM is a **true leader who serves** (servant leader)
2. The SM holds others **accountable** to Scrum (it's literally in their definition!)
3. The SM is **NOT** a project manager
4. The SM has **NO authority** to assign work or change priorities

### Common SM Anti-Patterns
- 😱 **"Secretary SM"** — Just schedules events, doesn't coach
- 😱 **"Boss SM"** — Tells team what to do
- 😱 **"Cheerleader SM"** — All positivity, no challenge
- 😱 **"Process Police SM"** — Enforces rules without explaining value

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "Team is consistently late on commitments. What should the SM do?"
- ❌ Wrong: "Tell them to work harder"
- ❌ Wrong: "Add more developers"
- ✅ Right: "Coach the team to inspect and adapt during the Retrospective"

## 🛠️ Accountability 3: The Developers

### One-Sentence Definition
> *"Developers are the people in the Scrum Team committed to creating any aspect of a usable Increment each Sprint."*

### What Developers Do
- 🛠️ **Create the plan** for the Sprint (Sprint Backlog)
- 🛠️ **Instill quality** (adhere to DoD)
- 🛠️ **Adapt their plan daily** toward the Sprint Goal
- 🛠️ **Hold each other accountable** as professionals

### Developer Power Rules
1. "Developers" includes **anyone** doing the work — not just programmers!
   - Designers, testers, writers, ops, data analysts — all "Developers" in Scrum
2. Developers are **cross-functional** — collectively, they have all skills needed
3. Developers are **self-managing** — decide who does what, when, how
4. Developers **estimate** the work
5. Developers **own the Sprint Backlog**

### Title Confusion (Important!)

A "Developer" in Scrum is **NOT** the same as a "developer" in your job title.

| You Might Be Called | In Scrum You're A... |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| Senior Engineer | Developer |
| QA Tester | Developer |
| UX Designer | Developer |
| DevOps Engineer | Developer |
| Data Scientist | Developer |
| Tech Writer | Developer |

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "The team has 3 programmers, 1 tester, and 1 designer. How many Developers does the Scrum Team have?" → **5**

### Developer Anti-Patterns
- 😱 **Sub-teams within the team** ("front-end team" vs "back-end team")
- 😱 **One person assigning tasks** to others
- 😱 **"Not my job" mentality** — tester won't help debug
- 😱 **Side commitments** — pulled to other projects mid-Sprint

## 🎯 The Whole Scrum Team

The PO, SM, and Developers are **one Scrum Team**. Per the 2020 Guide:

> *"Scrum Teams are cross-functional, meaning the members have all the skills necessary to create value each Sprint. They are also self-managing, meaning they internally decide who does what, when, and how."*

Two key properties: **cross-functional** + **self-managing**.

### Team Size: 10 Or Fewer
- Old guide: "3-9 developers" + PO + SM (so 5-11 total)
- New guide (2020): **"Typically 10 or fewer"** total
- Smaller is better. Larger teams break into *multiple Scrum Teams*.

### One Sprint Goal, One Product Goal, One Backlog
The whole team aligns on these. No splits.

## 🤝 Role Combinations: What's Allowed?

| Combination | Allowed? |
|-------------|----------|
| PO + Developer | ✅ Technically yes, but not recommended |
| SM + Developer | ✅ Yes, common in small teams |
| SM + PO | ❌ NO — conflict of interest, impossible to do well |
| Multiple POs | ❌ NO — one PO per product |
| Multiple SMs | ❌ NO — one SM per team |
| One SM for multiple teams | ✅ Yes, common in scaling |

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "Can the same person be Product Owner and Scrum Master?" → **No.**

## 🆚 Scrum Master vs Project Manager

This is THE most-tested distinction:

| Scrum Master | Project Manager |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Serves the team | Leads the team |
| Removes impediments | Manages risks/issues |
| Coaches | Directs |
| No authority over assignments | Assigns tasks |
| Owns process health | Owns project outcomes |
| Empowers team to estimate | Often estimates for team |
| Embraces change | Manages change tightly |

🎯 **CRITICAL EXAM TIP:** In Scrum, there is **NO Project Manager**. If you see "the PM" in a Scrum question, it's almost always a trap.

## 🎓 Stakeholders: Important But NOT Team Members

Stakeholders are NOT part of the Scrum Team. They:
- Provide input on the Product (especially at Sprint Review)
- Don't attend Daily Scrums (typically)
- Don't decide on backlog priorities (PO does)
- Don't estimate (Developers do)

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "Stakeholders want to attend the Daily Scrum to give status updates."
- Wrong: "Allow them"
- Right: "The Daily Scrum is for Developers; coach stakeholders that they receive updates at Sprint Review"

## 🧠 Real Scenarios To Train Your Brain

### Scenario A:
> *A developer asks the Product Owner: "How should I implement this user story?"*

✅ Right: PO clarifies *what* the value is. Developer (or team) decides *how* to build it.

### Scenario B:
> *A stakeholder calls a developer mid-Sprint and adds a new requirement.*

✅ Right: Developer redirects to PO. PO decides if/how it fits backlog. Sprint Goal unchanged.

### Scenario C:
> *Scrum Master notices the same person always presents at the Daily Scrum.*

✅ Right: SM coaches the team that the Daily is for *all* developers, but doesn't force anyone — encourages self-organizing improvement.

### Scenario D:
> *PO wants to skip the Sprint Review because no stakeholders can attend.*

✅ Right: SM coaches PO that the Sprint Review is *required* and serves the team's inspection of progress, even with limited stakeholders.

### Scenario E:
> *Developer says "QA should do this — not me."*

✅ Right: SM coaches the team on cross-functional, "we" mindset. There are no sub-roles; all are Developers.

| Term | Quick Def |
|------|-----------|
| **Product Owner** | Maximizes value; manages backlog; ONE person |
| **Scrum Master** | Establishes Scrum; serves team, PO, org; NOT a manager |
| **Developers** | Creates the Increment; self-manages; cross-functional |
| **Cross-functional** | Team has all skills needed |
| **Self-managing** | Team decides who/when/how |

## ✅ Module 3 Summary

You now know:
- 🎭 Three accountabilities: PO (value), SM (process), Developers (delivery)
- 🛡️ Scrum Masters serve THREE groups (team, PO, organization)
- 🛠️ "Developers" = anyone doing the work, not just coders
- 👤 Only ONE PO, only ONE SM
- 🚫 No project manager in Scrum
- 🤝 Stakeholders are NOT team members

# ✏️ Module 6 Quiz: Done & Estimation

## Questions

### Q1. The Definition of Done is created by:
A. The Product Owner alone
B. The Scrum Master
C. The Developers (or organization standard)
D. The Project Manager

### Q2. Definition of Ready is:
A. Required by the Scrum Guide
B. NOT defined by the Scrum Guide; an optional practice
C. Created by the Scrum Master
D. The same as the Definition of Done

### Q3. Who estimates Product Backlog Items?
A. Product Owner
B. Scrum Master
C. Developers
D. Stakeholders

### Q4. Story points represent:
A. Hours of work
B. Days of effort
C. Relative complexity, effort, and uncertainty
D. Number of test cases

### Q5. Velocity should be used to:
A. Compare teams to each other
B. Forecast future Sprints (within the same team)
C. Set performance targets
D. Reward high-performing teams

### Q6. Two Scrum Teams have velocities of 30 and 50. Team A is:
A. Less productive
B. Less skilled
C. Not comparable to Team B (story points are team-relative)
D. In need of training

### Q7. A team lowers their Definition of Done to deliver more this Sprint. This is:
A. A pragmatic choice
B. An anti-pattern; quality must not decrease
C. Allowed if the PO approves
D. Encouraged in early sprints

### Q8. The Increment is:
A. Released at the end of every Sprint
B. Releasable; PO decides if/when to release
C. Never released until project end
D. Released only after stakeholder sign-off

### Q9. Burndown charts are:
A. Required by the Scrum Guide
B. A useful but optional transparency tool
C. Created by the Product Owner
D. Used to track velocity

### Q10. INVEST stands for:
A. Iterative, Negotiated, Versioned, Emergent, Sized, Tested
B. Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small, Testable
C. Implementable, Necessary, Verifiable, Estimated, Sequential, Tracked
D. Innovative, Novel, Versatile, Effective, Strategic, Targeted

### Q11. A story has acceptance criteria but doesn't meet the Definition of Done. Is it Done?
A. Yes — acceptance criteria are met
B. No — DoD must also be met
C. Maybe — depends on the PO
D. Yes — DoD is optional

### Q12. The Definition of Done can:
A. Never change
B. Evolve to a higher quality bar
C. Be lowered to meet deadlines
D. Differ for each Developer

---

## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **C. Developers (or organization)**
DoD is owned by the Developers, unless the org provides one.

### Q2: **B. NOT in Scrum Guide**
Definition of Ready is a practice, not a Scrum requirement.

### Q3: **C. Developers**
Always developers. Always.

### Q4: **C. Relative complexity, effort, uncertainty**
NOT time. NOT hours.

### Q5: **B. Forecast future Sprints (same team)**
Within the same team, for planning. Never for cross-team comparison.

### Q6: **C. Not comparable**
Story points are relative *to that team*. Team A might have larger reference items.

### Q7: **B. Anti-pattern**
"Quality does not decrease" — Scrum Guide. Lowering DoD = lowering quality.

### Q8: **B. Releasable; PO decides**
Increment must be releasable; release timing is the PO's call.

### Q9: **B. Useful but optional**
Not required by the Scrum Guide.

### Q10: **B. Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small, Testable**
Mike Cohn's criteria for good user stories.

### Q11: **B. No — DoD must also be met**
Both AC + DoD are required.

### Q12: **B. Evolve to higher bar**
Adding criteria = good. Lowering = anti-pattern. Per-developer DoD = anti-pattern.

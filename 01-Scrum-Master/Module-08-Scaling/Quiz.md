---

## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **A. 1**
ONE PO per product, even when scaled.

### Q2: **A. 1**
ONE Product Backlog. ONE source of truth.

### Q3: **B. Split into multiple teams sharing PG/PB/PO**
Direct from Scrum Guide.

### Q4: **B. 3-9 Scrum Teams**
Nexus's sweet spot.

### Q5: **C. Cross-functional, end-to-end**
Feature teams own complete features.

### Q6: **B. Scaling framework, not in Scrum Guide**
SAFe is its own thing.

### Q7: **B. Single common DoD**
Quality must be consistent across teams on same product.

### Q8: **B. Coordinating dependencies, integrated Increment**
The integration team's purpose.

### Q9: **B. Create dependencies and bottlenecks**
Hand-offs slow delivery.

### Q10: **B. Common practice, NOT in Scrum Guide**
SoS is XP/Scrum tradition, not formally part of Scrum.

### Q11: **A. 1**
One Product, one Product Goal — even with multiple teams. The Product Goal is shared across all teams working on the same product.

### Q12: **B. Scrum.org**
Nexus is the official scaling framework from Scrum.org, designed for 3–9 Scrum Teams on one product.

### Q13: **B. Have the SMs agree on one shared DoD that all teams meet**
A single product needs one common DoD so the Increment quality is consistent. Different DoDs per team is an anti-pattern.

### Q14: **C. SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework)**
SAFe is a separate scaling framework with its own terminology. It is NOT part of the Scrum Guide.

### Q15: **B. A shared Sprint Goal across all teams in the Nexus**
The Nexus Sprint Goal aligns all teams toward a common objective for the integrated Increment.

### Q16: **B. Cross-functional team that delivers end-to-end customer-facing value**
Feature teams own complete features and minimize hand-offs. They are the preferred pattern in Scrum scaling.

### Q17: **B. Component teams create dependencies, hand-offs, and bottlenecks**
Component teams (DB, UI, etc.) require coordination across teams to deliver any single piece of value, slowing flow.

### Q18: **B. Split into multiple Scrum Teams sharing the same Product Goal, Backlog, and PO**
Direct from the Scrum Guide. The org-chart fix is more managers; the Scrum fix is more cohesive teams sharing one product.

### Q19: **B. Coordinate across teams to ensure an integrated, Done Increment**
The Nexus Integration Team's purpose is dependency coordination and integration — not approval, backlog ownership, or replacing SMs.

### Q20: **B. SAFe is a separate scaling framework, not part of the Scrum Guide**
SAFe is its own framework. Some teams use it, but the SM should be transparent that it is not "official Scrum."

### Q21: **B. Difficulty integrating work into a single coherent Increment**
Aligned Sprint cadences (length and start) are needed so teams can integrate work and produce one coherent Increment.

### Q22: **B. Held by representatives from each team to discuss integration**
The Nexus Daily Scrum focuses on cross-team integration issues and is attended by representatives, not the entire Nexus.

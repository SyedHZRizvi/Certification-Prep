## 🎯 Detailed answer rationales

**Q1. Answer: B**
- **Why B is correct.** A CLV/CAC ratio of 3:1 or higher is the canonical health metric for a steady-state subscription or repeat-purchase DTC brand (Fader & Hardie, *Customer Centricity*, 2012, Wharton). Lower than 3:1 and the brand is over-paying to acquire; much higher and it is under-investing in growth.
- **Why the others are wrong.** A (1:1) means you break even on acquisition, no profit. C (0.5:1) means you lose money per customer. D (100:1) is absurd in any competitive market.
- **Exam-takeaway.** 3:1 is the rule of thumb; ≥4:1 is excellent; below 2:1 signals a unit-economics problem.

**Q2. Answer: A**
- **Why A is correct.** Contribution margin = Revenue − Variable Costs (COGS, payment processing, variable shipping, returns, variable fulfillment). Fixed corporate overhead is excluded by definition.
- **Why the others are wrong.** B, C, D are all variable costs included in contribution margin.
- **Exam-takeaway.** Contribution margin is the variable-cost-adjusted profit per order, fixed overhead is below the contribution line.

**Q3. Answer: B**
- **Why B is correct.** Shopify Plus is Shopify's enterprise tier ($2,000-$40,000/month range depending on commit). It includes Checkout Extensibility, dedicated support, and B2B.
- **Why the others are wrong.** A and D are invented tier names; C "Shopify Lite" was a retired sticker-only tier, not the enterprise flagship.
- **Exam-takeaway.** Shopify Plus = enterprise; Shopify Advanced/Basic are the SMB tiers.

**Q4. Answer: A**
- **Why A is correct.** Adobe acquired Magento in 2018 ($1.68B) and rebranded it Adobe Commerce. Open-source Magento Open Source continues alongside.
- **Why the others are wrong.** B Demandware = Salesforce Commerce Cloud (Salesforce, 2016); C Hybris = SAP Commerce Cloud (SAP, 2013); D Oro is an unrelated competitor.
- **Exam-takeaway.** Adobe Commerce = Magento (rebranded 2018). Used heavily on the Adobe Commerce Business Practitioner exam.

**Q5. Answer: D**
- **Why D is correct.** PIM = Product Information Management, the system for managing product attributes, copy, images, and channel-specific syndication (Akeneo, inriver, Salsify, Plytix).
- **Why the others are wrong.** A/B/C are fabricated acronyms.
- **Exam-takeaway.** PIM sits between catalog/ERP and storefront; manages SKU-level data integrity.

**Q6. Answer: C**
- **Why C is correct.** Google Merchant Center's required attribute set for branded products includes id, title, description, link, image_link, availability, price, condition, brand, gtin (and google_product_category). Missing any = feed disapproval.
- **Why the others are wrong.** A/B/D are dangerously incomplete and result in disapproval.
- **Exam-takeaway.** GTIN required for branded products; MPN can substitute when GTIN is unavailable.

**Q7. Answer: A**
- **Why A is correct.** PCI-DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard, founded 2004 by Visa/Mastercard/Amex/Discover/JCB; v4.0 in 2022, phased enforcement to March 2024-March 2025) governs cardholder-data security.
- **Why the others are wrong.** B is GDPR; C is governed by Incoterms / customs; D is unrelated.
- **Exam-takeaway.** PCI-DSS = payment card data security. v4.0 is the current version.

**Q8. Answer: C**
- **Why C is correct.** Tokenization replaces the actual primary account number (PAN) with a non-sensitive surrogate token. Reduces PCI scope dramatically.
- **Why the others are wrong.** A confuses tokenization with crypto; B/D are unrelated.
- **Exam-takeaway.** Tokenization (Stripe, Adyen, Braintree all offer it) is the modern PCI-scope-reduction technique.

**Q9. Answer: D**
- **Why D is correct.** SCA (Strong Customer Authentication) is mandated by EU PSD2 (Payment Services Directive 2, in force 2018, SCA enforcement from September 14, 2019).
- **Why the others are wrong.** A is card-data security (different); B is GDPR (privacy, not payments); C is California privacy.
- **Exam-takeaway.** SCA = PSD2 + 3DS2 (3-D Secure 2). Required for EU transactions.

**Q10. Answer: B**
- **Why B is correct.** Apparel and fashion e-commerce have return rates of 25-40% (with online apparel often at 30%+ vs. brick-and-mortar at ~10%).
- **Why the others are wrong.** A is more typical of B2B/electronics; C is general homewares; D is unrealistic.
- **Exam-takeaway.** Apparel return rate ≈ 30% is the rule-of-thumb; size/fit issues drive it.

**Q11. Answer: D**
- **Why D is correct.** ATC = Add To Cart, a behavioral funnel event tracked in GA4, Meta, Klaviyo.
- **Why the others are wrong.** A/B/C are invented.
- **Exam-takeaway.** ATC is the most-tested mid-funnel event for e-commerce attribution and lifecycle.

**Q12. Answer: A**
- **Why A is correct.** Performance Max replaced Smart Shopping in 2022; Smart Shopping was deprecated July 2022.
- **Why the others are wrong.** B/C/D are still active campaign types.
- **Exam-takeaway.** Smart Shopping is dead; PMax is its successor.

**Q13. Answer: C**
- **Why C is correct.** Apple's iOS 14.5 ATT (April 26, 2021) caused ~70-75% of iOS users to opt out of cross-app tracking, breaking the browser Pixel. CAPI sends events server-side, bypassing IDFA-block.
- **Why the others are wrong.** A is a side benefit; B is wrong (Google doesn't require Meta CAPI); D is not the primary reason.
- **Exam-takeaway.** ATT + CAPI is the single most-tested topic on post-2021 paid-acquisition exams.

**Q14. Answer: B**
- **Why B is correct.** US Section 321 (19 USC 1321) sets the De Minimis threshold at $800. Set in 2016 (up from $200). Under restriction for Chinese-origin goods 2024-2025.
- **Why the others are wrong.** A is the pre-2016 threshold; C/D are invented.
- **Exam-takeaway.** $800 + Section 321. Memorize.

**Q15. Answer: A**
- **Why A is correct.** EU IOSS applies to non-EU parcel imports ≤€150.
- **Why the others are wrong.** B is too low; C/D are wrong threshold.
- **Exam-takeaway.** IOSS = €150 + non-EU. OSS = EU seller cross-EU.

**Q16. Answer: C**
- **Why C is correct.** MACH = Microservices, API-first, Cloud-native, Headless (MACH Alliance, 2020, commercetools, Contentstack, EPAM, Valtech).
- **Why the others are wrong.** A/B/D are invented acronym expansions.
- **Exam-takeaway.** Memorize the four letters and the founding date.

**Q17. Answer: C**
- **Why C is correct.** EDI 850 = Purchase Order (X12 standard, ANSI ASC X12).
- **Why the others are wrong.** Invoice = 810; ASN = 856; Catalog = 832.
- **Exam-takeaway.** 850 = PO is the most-tested EDI number.

**Q18. Answer: A**
- **Why A is correct.** Fred Reichheld (Bain & Co.) introduced NPS in *The One Number You Need to Grow* (HBR, December 2003) and *The Ultimate Question* book (2006). The "would you recommend us on a 0-10 scale" methodology.
- **Why the others are wrong.** B founded modern marketing theory; C founded modern management; D founded behavioral economics.
- **Exam-takeaway.** Reichheld + Bain + 2003 HBR = NPS.

**Q19. Answer: D**
- **Why D is correct.** CLS = Cumulative Layout Shift, Google's Core Web Vitals (2020) metric for visual stability (target <0.1).
- **Why the others are wrong.** A measures loading; B measures interactivity; C is server response.
- **Exam-takeaway.** Three Core Web Vitals: LCP (loading), INP (interactivity, replaced FID in March 2024), CLS (visual stability).

**Q20. Answer: C**
- **Why C is correct.** Amazon ACoS = Ad Spend / Ad Revenue (the ad-only ratio).
- **Why the others are wrong.** A is TACoS (Total ACoS, including organic); B/D are unrelated.
- **Exam-takeaway.** ACoS = ads-only; TACoS = full Amazon revenue. The honest measure is TACoS.

**Q21. Answer: D**
- **Why D is correct.** Shopify checkout customization is gated to Shopify Plus via Checkout Extensibility (and checkout.liquid until its June 2024 deprecation). Non-Plus tiers cannot customize checkout.
- **Why the others are wrong.** A/C ignore the tier-gating; B is a retired entry tier with no enterprise customization.
- **Exam-takeaway.** Checkout customization is a Plus-only feature, major reason brands upgrade from Advanced to Plus.

**Q22. Answer: B**
- **Why B is correct.** Simplest CLV = AOV × Purchase Frequency × Customer Lifespan × Margin. Fader/Hardie/Bell (*Customer Centricity*, 2012, Wharton) introduced more sophisticated probabilistic models (BG/NBD) but the four-factor form is the universal teaching baseline.
- **Why the others are wrong.** A is revenue-per-customer (not CLV); C is profit; D is unrelated.
- **Exam-takeaway.** Four factors: AOV × Frequency × Lifespan × Margin.

**Q23. Answer: B**
- **Why B is correct.** *South Dakota v. Wayfair* (2018) overturned *Quill Corp. v. North Dakota* (1992) physical-nexus rule. Now 47 states + DC have economic-nexus thresholds ($100K-$500K revenue or 100-200 transactions).
- **Why the others are wrong.** A is pre-*Wayfair*; C ignores 2018+ law; D is not US doctrine.
- **Exam-takeaway.** *Wayfair* (2018) = economic nexus replaces physical nexus.

**Q24. Answer: D**
- **Why D is correct.** Klaviyo is the dominant e-commerce-purpose-built lifecycle CRM (email + SMS), founded 2012, IPO'd 2023, deeply integrated with Shopify/BigCommerce.
- **Why the others are wrong.** A is storefront platforms (different layer); B/C are different categories entirely.
- **Exam-takeaway.** Klaviyo = e-commerce-native lifecycle CRM. Competes with Salesforce Marketing Cloud, Iterable, Bloomreach.

**Q25. Answer: C**
- **Why C is correct.** Meta Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns (ASC), launched late 2022, became the dominant Meta retail campaign type by 2024 (50-70% of retail spend per industry benchmarks).
- **Why the others are wrong.** A is engagement (not retail); B/D are formats not strategies.
- **Exam-takeaway.** ASC = 2024 Meta retail default. ECB cap is the prospecting-vs-retention dial.

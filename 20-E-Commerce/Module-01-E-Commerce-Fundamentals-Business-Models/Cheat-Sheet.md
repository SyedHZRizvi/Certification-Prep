# 📋 Module 1 Cheat Sheet: E-Commerce Fundamentals & Business Models

> One page. Print it. Tape it to your monitor. Review before any business-model decision.

---

## 🛒 The Seven E-Commerce Business Models

| Model | Primary platform | When to use |
|-------|------------------|-------------|
| **DTC** | Shopify Plus, Adobe Commerce | Brand + repeat purchase |
| **Marketplace seller** | Amazon, Walmart, Etsy | Cash flow + discovery |
| **Marketplace operator** | Mirakl, Yo!Kart | You ARE the platform |
| **B2B / wholesale** | Adobe Commerce B2B, Faire | High AOV, account-based |
| **B2B2C hybrid** | Shopify Plus + Faire + Amazon | Multi-channel scale |
| **Subscription** | Recharge, Skio | Replenishable + low churn |
| **Social / live commerce** | TikTok Shop, Whatnot | Creator-led discovery |

## 💰 The Five Unit-Economics Formulas (MEMORIZE)

```
AOV  = Revenue / Orders
CAC  = Total Acquisition Spend / New Customers
CM%  = (Revenue - Variable Costs) / Revenue
CLV  = AOV × Frequency × Lifespan × Margin %
Payback = CAC / (AOV × CM%)
```

---

## 📐 The CLV:CAC Ratio Cheat Sheet

| CLV:CAC | Verdict |
|---------|---------|
| < 1:1 | Losing money on each customer |
| 1:1 to 2:1 | Surviving, risky |
| **3:1** | ✅ Healthy DTC (Wharton/Fader benchmark) |
| 5:1+ | Under-investing — spend MORE |

## 🏗️ The Bezos 1997 Six Principles

```
1. Customer obsession
2. Long-term thinking (cash flow > GAAP)
3. Day 1 always — never Day 2
4. Hire and retain the best
5. Operational excellence
6. Take big bets with discipline
```

🧠 **Type-1 (irreversible) = slow** · **Type-2 (reversible) = fast**

---

## 🌐 Bell's GEM Framework (2014)

```
G — Geography  (store density, fulfillment proximity)
E — Environment (climate, regulation, culture)
M — Mobility    (how often customer leaves home)
```

E-commerce is a patchwork of micro-conditions, NOT a uniform market.

---

## 🎯 Christensen's Jobs-to-Be-Done

> "Customers don't buy products. They hire products to do a job in their lives."

Identify the JOB → design the PDP / category page around the JOB. Mis-identify the job, mis-build the product.

---

## 📈 The Long Tail (Anderson 2006, Brynjolfsson 2003)

```
Head:    Top hits  (high volume, brand-driven)
Tail:    Niche SKUs (low volume, search-driven)

Aggregate(tail) > Aggregate(head) when:
- Shelf space cost = 0 (digital)
- Search/recommendation is excellent
- PIM data is clean
```

## ⚡ Failure Modes To Avoid

- ❌ **Race-to-zero on Amazon** — losing pricing power
- ❌ **DTC with < 30% gross margin** — can't afford CAC
- ❌ **B2B portal without account-tiered pricing** — kills enterprise
- ❌ **Subscription without churn modeling** — can't reach $10M ARR
- ❌ **Furniture DTC with bad freight model** — freight eats 25%+ of CM
- ❌ **Multi-marketplace without OMS** — inventory disasters in 6 months
- ❌ **No first-party data strategy** — vulnerable to platform changes

## 🏆 Exam Power Phrases

When you see these in answers, they're often correct:

- ✅ "Allocate by CLV segment, not aggregate..." (Fader)
- ✅ "Long-term cash flow over quarterly GAAP..." (Bezos)
- ✅ "Job-organized, not feature-organized..." (Christensen)
- ✅ "Aggregate niche demand exceeds head demand online..." (Anderson)
- ✅ "Geography + Environment + Mobility predict adoption..." (Bell)

When you see these, they're often **wrong**:

- ❌ "Treat all customers equally"
- ❌ "Optimize for quarterly earnings"
- ❌ "Marketplace-first for DTC brand-building"
- ❌ "All customers in a demographic have the same job"

## 🛠️ Quick Decision Framework — Pick Your Business Model

```
1. AOV < $25? → marketplace-first
2. Gross margin < 30%? → marketplace-first
3. Frequency monthly? → subscription
4. Catalog 5K+ SKUs? → B2B portal or marketplace operator
5. B2C only? → DTC
   B2B only? → wholesale portal
   Both? → B2B2C hybrid
```

# Recommended Readings, E-Commerce Track

> **Purpose:** This curated list extends the per-module "Further Reading" sections with the canonical bibliography for an e-commerce operator who wants to reason from first principles, not just pass a certification. Use it during preparation, after passing the Adobe Commerce / Google Shopping / Amazon Ads credentials, and as the foundation of a personal reference library.

> **How to use:** Engage each item at the point in the course indicated. The single most important academic anchor is the Wharton Online Digital Marketing & E-Commerce Specialization, taught by Peter Fader and David Bell, read alongside Modules 1, 7, and 9.

---

## Canonical Textbooks (5 must-haves)

### Strategy and Customer-Centric Thinking

- **Fader, Peter, *Customer Centricity: Focus on the Right Customers for Strategic Advantage*, 2nd ed. (Wharton Digital Press, 2012; 2nd ed. 2020). ISBN 978-1613630167.**
  - The single foundational text for the entire track. Fader's argument that not all customers are equal, and that CLV is the discipline that proves it sits underneath every paid-acquisition, lifecycle, and retention decision. Read alongside Modules 1, 7, 8, 9.

- **Bell, David R., *Location is (Still) Everything: The Surprising Influence of the Real World on How We Search, Shop, and Sell in the Virtual One* (New Harvest, 2014). ISBN 978-1477800126.**
  - Wharton professor's deep work on why physical geography still shapes digital commerce, store density, GEM (Geography, Environment, Mobility) framing, the "long tail of friction." Engage during Modules 5 (fulfillment) and 10 (cross-border).

- **Anderson, Chris, *The Long Tail: Why the Future of Business is Selling Less of More*, Revised ed. (Hyperion, 2008; original 2006). ISBN 978-1401309664.**
  - The framework that explains why e-commerce can profitably serve niche demand. Built on Brynjolfsson, Hu & Smith's 2003 *MIS Quarterly* paper. Engage during Modules 1, 3, and 8.

- **Bryar, Colin & Carr, Bill, *Working Backwards: Insights, Stories, and Secrets from Inside Amazon* (St. Martin's Press, 2021). ISBN 978-1250267597.**
  - Ex-Amazon executives' field guide to the operating principles: 6-page narratives, 2-pizza teams, customer-obsession, "working backwards" from a press release. Engage during Modules 1, 5, and the Capstone.

- **Christensen, Clayton M., Hall, Taddy, Dillon, Karen & Duncan, David S., *Competing Against Luck: The Story of Innovation and Customer Choice* (HarperBusiness, 2016). ISBN 978-0062435613.**
  - The "Jobs to Be Done" framework. Reshapes how you think about PDP positioning, search-intent mapping, and category-page strategy. Engage during Modules 1, 3, 6, 8.

### Experimentation and Operations

- **Kohavi, Ron, Tang, Diane & Xu, Ya, *Trustworthy Online Controlled Experiments: A Practical Guide to A/B Testing* (Cambridge University Press, 2020). ISBN 978-1108724265.**
  - The Microsoft-grade textbook on running A/B tests at scale. Sample-size math, sample-ratio mismatch, novelty effects, twyman's law, sequential testing. Mandatory before any CRO program. Engage during Module 6 and the Capstone.

- **Heath, Chip & Heath, Dan, *Made to Stick: Why Some Ideas Survive and Others Die* (Random House, 2007). ISBN 978-1400064281.**
  - SUCCESs framework (Simple, Unexpected, Concrete, Credible, Emotional, Stories). Underpins effective PDP copy, marketing brief writing, and content marketing. Engage during Modules 1, 6, 8.

- **Goldsmith, Marshall, *What Got You Here Won't Get You There: How Successful People Become Even More Successful* (Hyperion, 2007). ISBN 978-1401301309.**
  - The leadership leg of an e-com career. As you move from individual contributor to Director and beyond, the bad habits that helped you ship will start hurting your team. Engage during the Capstone and after.

## Seminal Papers and HBR / MIS Quarterly Articles (10 indispensable)

### Foundational Academic

- **Bezos, Jeffrey, *1997 Shareholder Letter* (Amazon.com, attached to all annual letters since 1998).**
  - Free. The Day-1 doctrine in 1,500 words. Re-read every quarter; never gets old. Engage during Module 1.

- **Brynjolfsson, Erik, Hu, Yu (Jeffrey) & Smith, Michael D. "Consumer Surplus in the Digital Economy: Estimating the Value of Increased Product Variety at Online Booksellers" *Management Science*, vol. 49, no. 11 (Nov 2003), pp. 1580-1596.**
  - The empirical paper that quantified the Long Tail before Anderson named it. Foundational to category-page strategy. Engage during Modules 1, 8.

- **Anderson, Chris "The Long Tail" *Wired*, October 2004.**
  - Free. The popular-press article that became the book. Engage during Module 1.

- **Christensen, Clayton M., Cook, Scott & Hall, Taddy "Marketing Malpractice: The Cause and the Cure" *HBR*, December 2005.**
  - The Jobs-to-Be-Done framework introduction. Reframes PDP and category strategy. Engage during Modules 1, 3, 8.

- **Bell, David R., Choi, Jeonghye & Lodish, Leonard "What Matters Most in Internet Retailing" *MIT Sloan Management Review*, vol. 53, no. 1 (Fall 2011).**
  - Wharton/MIT operating-truths paper on what actually drives DTC success online. Engage during Module 1.

### Attribution, Experimentation, Operations

- **Shapley, Lloyd S. "A Value for n-Person Games" *Contributions to the Theory of Games*, vol. II (Princeton University Press, 1953).**
  - The mathematical paper behind Shapley-value attribution. Engage during Module 9 and the Capstone's attribution work; depth-skip if not mathematically inclined.

- **Hau Lee "The Triple-A Supply Chain" *HBR*, October 2004.**
  - Stanford GSB professor's paper on Agility, Adaptability, Alignment. Defines what supply-chain capability the e-com operator needs. Engage during Module 5.

- **Hau Lee "Don't Tweak Your Supply Chain Rethink It End to End", *HBR*, October 2010.**
  - The follow-up that goes deeper on supply-chain redesign for omnichannel. Engage during Module 5.

- **Kohavi, Ron "Online Controlled Experiments at Large Scale" *KDD '13 Proceedings*, ACM, 2013.**
  - Free PDF. The conference paper that preceded the *Trustworthy Online Controlled Experiments* textbook. Engage during Module 6.

- **Forrester Research (Sucharita Kodali, lead), *The State of Digital Commerce Platforms* (annual report, latest: 2024-2025).**
  - The most-cited industry analysis of Shopify vs Adobe Commerce vs Salesforce vs commercetools vs BigCommerce. Engage during Module 2.

### Industry Practitioner Whitepapers

- **Shopify Plus, *State of Commerce* (annual report, latest: 2024-2025).**
  - Free with email. DTC-specific benchmarks across conversion, AOV, retention, and marketplace mix. Engage during Module 1, 2.

- **Common Thread Collective, *The ECD (Ecommerce DTC) Quarterly Report* (commonthreadco.com).**
  - Free with email. The most data-rich DTC-specific paid-media benchmarks published. Engage during Modules 7, 8.

- **Klaviyo, *The Owner's Guide to Email Marketing* + *Benchmarks Report* (klaviyo.com, annual).**
  - Free. DTC-specific email/SMS benchmarks across 100,000+ accounts. Engage during Module 8.

- **Andrew Faris, *AJF Growth* newsletter (ajfgrowth.com).**
  - Free with email. Operator-grade weekly newsletter on DTC paid media and lifecycle. Engage during Modules 7, 8.

- **Salesforce Commerce Cloud, *Shopping Index* (salesforce.com, quarterly).**
  - Free. Enterprise-DTC benchmarks at scale ($5B+ analyzed). Engage during Module 9.

## Recommended Reading Sequence

If you have time for only 5 books, read in this order:

1. **Fader, *Customer Centricity*** (the CLV-first frame)
2. **Kohavi, Tang & Xu, *Trustworthy Online Controlled Experiments*** (the A/B testing bible)
3. **Bryar & Carr, *Working Backwards*** (operating principles from the most successful e-commerce company in history)
4. **Bell, *Location is (Still) Everything*** (the geography-still-matters frame for omnichannel and cross-border)
5. **Anderson, *The Long Tail*** (the niche-demand frame for catalog and SEO strategy)

If you have time for only 3 practitioner reports, read:

1. **Shopify, *State of Commerce*** (latest)
2. **Common Thread Collective, *The ECD Quarterly Report*** (latest)
3. **Klaviyo, *Benchmarks Report*** (latest)

## A Note on AI in E-Commerce (2024-2026 specifics)

The biggest shifts you should be tracking:

- **Performance Max + Demand Gen** (Google), AI-driven Shopping campaign types now ~60-70% of Google Ads spend at large retailers.
- **Advantage+ Shopping + Advantage+ Sales** (Meta), same trajectory at Meta.
- **AI-generated PDP copy + image variants**, Salsify, Akeneo, and Lily AI all shipped product-attribute auto-generation in 2024.
- **Conversational commerce**, Klarna's OpenAI integration (2023-2024) and Shopify's "Sidekick" agent (2024) are the early signals; expect generative commerce search to be table-stakes by 2026.
- **AI-personalized PDP + collection sequencing**, Mutiny, Dynamic Yield, Klaviyo Predictive CLV piped to merchandising rules.
- **Server-side tagging + Consent Mode v2**, table-stakes for any iOS-heavy program.
- **Privacy Sandbox / Topics API**, the post-cookie web is being built now; track via the IAB Tech Lab.

Tool pricing and feature names verified as of 2026-05. **Always verify current pricing on the vendor's site before committing budget.**

## Maintaining Your Skills

E-commerce technology changes faster than almost any other industry. Plan to:

- Retake Google Shopping Ads + Amazon Ads + Meta Blueprint Commerce certs every year, they expire annually.
- Retake Adobe Commerce Business Practitioner every 2 years, it updates with each major Adobe Commerce release.
- Spend 4-6 hours/week consuming new platform updates from at least 2 of the industry resources above.
- Visit Shoptalk, NRF Big Show, or eTail East/West at least once every 18 months, the cross-pollination from the live floor is irreplaceable.
- Run 1 personal test program per quarter on a channel or platform you don't usually use, Walmart Marketplace if you're Amazon-native, TikTok Shop if you're Meta-native.

## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **A. Order Management System**

### Q2: **B. Inside-the-warehouse operations**
WMS handles receiving, putaway, picking, packing, shipping label generation. OMS is the upstream brain.

### Q3: **A. Fulfillment by Amazon**
(Both "Fulfilled by Amazon" and "Fulfillment by Amazon" are common; Amazon's official term is "Fulfillment by Amazon.")

### Q4: **B. April 2023**
Buy with Prime launched publicly in April 2023; in invite-only beta since 2022. Shopify Plus official integration in Q4 2023.

### Q5: **B. $2.40/cubic foot (vs $0.87 off-peak)**
Q4 peak FBA storage more than doubles. Many brands run lean Q4 FBA inventory to avoid the surcharge.

### Q6: **B. 20-50%**
Loop Returns 2024 data: brands with easy returns see 20-50% higher repurchase rates in next 12 months.

### Q7: **B. 25%**
Amazon Logistics ships ~25% of US package volume (2024 data), second only to UPS.

### Q8: **B. 14 days (Right of Withdrawal, Consumer Rights Directive 2011/83/EU)**
EU mandatory 14-day no-questions-asked return right. Member states implement.

### Q9: **B. How much you can store at FBA**
IPI score influences FBA storage limits. Brands optimize IPI by FBA-ing only what turns.

### Q10: **A. Amazon to fulfill orders from non-Amazon channels using FBA inventory**
MCF (Multi-Channel Fulfillment) opens FBA inventory for Walmart, eBay, Etsy, TikTok Shop, DTC orders.

### Q11: **B. (Average Shipping Cost / CM %) × 1.4**
The reading's free-shipping threshold formula. Example: $9 shipping / 0.50 CM × 1.4 = $25 minimum, round to $50.

### Q12: **B. Shipping cost (49% cite)**
Baymard 2024 cart abandonment research: ~49% of abandonments cite unexpected shipping cost.

### Q13: **B. Restricted to invited merchants with documented performance**
Seller-Fulfilled Prime is not generally available. Most Prime badges go to FBA.

### Q14: **A. Amazon FBA equivalent for Walmart Marketplace, enabling 2-day shipping**
WFS launched 2020. Required for Walmart 2-day shipping badge. Similar economics to FBA.

### Q15: **C. Amazon co-brands the experience**
Buy with Prime surfaces Amazon's brand on the DTC checkout (Prime badge, Amazon return label). For luxury/craft brands, this is a strategic concern.

### Q16: **B. ShipBob is DTC-focused asset-light; Saddle Creek is enterprise 3PL**
ShipBob serves $1M-$50M DTC brands; Saddle Creek serves enterprise brands.

### Q17: **B. Return-inspection grades determining restock/liquidate/refurbish/dispose**
A = restock, B = liquidate (Optoro, B-Stock), C = refurbish, D = dispose.

### Q18: **C. 30-40%**
Apparel DTC returns run 30-40% (high). Furniture is 5-10% but expensive when they happen.

### Q19: **B. Seller pays duties + VAT; customer sees all-in price**
DDP = Delivered Duty Paid. Better conversion than DAP (customer pays on delivery). Common for cross-border DTC.

### Q20: **B. FBA-equivalent fulfillment fee + 3% payment processing + returns fee**
Buy with Prime's fee structure adds 3% on top of normal payment processing fees.

### Q21: **B. OMS routes one order across multiple warehouses**
When an order ships from multiple warehouses, cost goes up and customer experience suffers. OMS routing should optimize toward single-location fulfillment.

### Q22: **B. USPS for small, UPS/FedEx for mid-weight, DHL Express for international**
Standard DTC carrier mix. Plus regional carriers (LSO, OnTrac) for cost-sensitive zones.

### Q23: **A. The Sennder acquisition (European trucking/last-mile)**
Maersk's 2024 acquisition of Sennder strengthened its last-mile / B2C ambitions in Europe.

### Q24: **B. NASDAQ-listed cross-border specialist for major brands**
Global-e (NASDAQ:GLBE) is the largest cross-border specialist. Powers cross-border for Adidas, Versace, Marc Jacobs, GAP, Hugo Boss.

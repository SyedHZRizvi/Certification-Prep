## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

**Q1 — C) Subject–Object–Verb (SOV)**
Urdu is an SOV language — the verb always comes at the end. English is SVO. This is the most fundamental structural difference.

**Q2 — B) میں چاول کھاتا ہوں**
Correct SOV order: میں (I/Subject) + چاول (rice/Object) + کھاتا ہوں (eat/Verb). The verb is always last.

**Q3 — B) Feminine**
کتاب (kitaab) is grammatically feminine, despite not ending in a typical feminine marker. This must be memorized. It affects adjectives: "اچھی کتاب" (achhi kitaab — a good book).

**Q4 — C) ہوں (hun)**
The "to be" form for میں (I) is ہوں (hun). ہے (hai) = he/she/it, ہیں (hain) = they/we/you formal, ہو (ho) = you familiar.

**Q5 — D) میں (mein)**
میں (mein) means "in/inside" as a postposition: "گھر میں" (ghar mein — in the house). Do not confuse with the pronoun "میں" (main — I) — different written forms in some styles.

**Q6 — B) میں پڑھتا ہوں**
A male speaker uses the masculine verb form: پڑھتا + ہوں. A female speaker would say پڑھتی ہوں.

**Q7 — B) کتاب is a feminine noun**
The possessive form (میرا/میری/میرے) agrees with the gender of the THING POSSESSED, not the speaker. کتاب is feminine, so میری is used regardless of whether the speaker is male or female.

**Q8 — C) Where**
کہاں (kahaan) = where. کب (kab) = when, کیوں (kyun) = why, کیسے (kaise) = how.

**Q9 — B) Put کیا at the beginning of the statement**
To form a yes/no question, simply add کیا (kya) at the beginning: "آپ پاکستانی ہیں" → "کیا آپ پاکستانی ہیں؟" — no other changes needed.

**Q10 — B) She drinks water**
وہ + پیتی ہے — "پیتی" is the feminine singular form, so this refers to "she." "پیتا ہے" would be "he drinks."

**Q11 — C) سے (se)**
سے (se) = from / with / by: "لاہور سے" (from Lahore), "میرے ساتھ" (with me). پر = on, میں = in, تک = until.

**Q12 — B) نام (naam) is a masculine noun**
نام (name) is grammatically masculine, so the possessive takes the masculine form میرا. This is independent of the speaker's gender or the referent's gender.

**Q13 — C) وہ (woh)**
وہ (woh) serves as both the third-person singular (he/she/it) AND third-person plural (they) in Urdu. Context makes the number clear. ہم (hum) = we, تم (tum) = you familiar.

**Q14 — B) میں جاتی ہوں**
Female speaker: جاتی (feminine singular verb form) + ہوں. Male: جاتا ہوں. The speaker's gender determines the verb form.

**Q15 — D) With you**
کے ساتھ (ke saath) = with. "آپ کے ساتھ" = with you. "آپ کے لیے" = for you, "آپ سے" = from you.

**Q16 — A) لڑکیاں (larkiyaan)**
Feminine nouns ending in ی form their plural by changing ی to یاں: لڑکی → لڑکیاں. Masculine nouns ending in ا change to ے: لڑکا → لڑکے.

**Q17 — C) Why**
کیوں (kyun) = why. کون (kaun) = who, کب (kab) = when, کیا (kya) = what.

**Q18 — C) میں خوش ہوں**
Correct Urdu sentence: Subject (میں) + Adjective/predicate (خوش) + Verb (ہوں). The verb is always last.

**Q19 — C) His/Her house**
اس کا (us ka) = his/her + masculine noun (گھر is masculine). "His" and "her" are the same in Urdu: اس کا / اس کی. Context determines which gender the possessor is.

**Q20 — B) کے لیے (ke liye)**
کے لیے (ke liye) = for (purpose): "آپ کے لیے" (for you), "پاکستان کے لیے" (for Pakistan). کے ساتھ = with, کے بغیر = without.

**Q21 — A) کمرے (kamre)**
Masculine nouns ending in ا form their plural by changing ا to ے: کمرہ (kamra) → کمرے (kamre). This is a very regular and common pattern.

**Q22 — C) Who are you?**
کون (kaun) = who. Full translation: "آپ کون ہیں؟" = "Who are you?" (formal).

**Q23 — D) Our house**
ہمارا (hamaara) = our + masculine noun. ہماری = our + feminine noun. ہمارے = our + masculine plural.

**Q24 — B) They are the same word (اس کا/اس کی) — context makes it clear**
Urdu "اس کا" can mean "his" or "her" — the gender of the possessor is determined by context. However, the ending (کا/کی/کے) changes based on the gender of the thing possessed.

**Q25 — B) In the house**
گھر (house) + میں (in) = in the house. Remember: postpositions come AFTER the noun in Urdu.

**Q26 — C) میں پڑھتی ہوں**
Female speaker uses feminine form: پڑھتی (not پڑھتا which is masculine). Combined with ہوں (first person singular to be).

# Module 9 Cheat Sheet — ثقافت و ادب (Culture, Media & Literature) · C1

Quick reference for Urdu poetry forms, famous poets, proverbs, idioms, the mushaira tradition, and media Urdu.

## 1. The Ghazal Form — غزل کی ساخت

| Term | Urdu | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| Couplet | شعر (she'r) | A two-line verse unit |
| Plural of couplet | اشعار (ash'aar) | Multiple couplets |
| Opening couplet | مطلع (matla') | Both lines share the end rhyme |
| Closing couplet | مقطع (maqta') | Contains the poet's pen name |
| Pen name | تخلص (takhallus) | Poet's chosen literary name |
| Refrain word | ردیف (radif) | Repeated word at end of each couplet |
| Rhyme before radif | قافیہ (qaafiya) | The rhyming syllable before radif |
| Collected works | دیوان (diwaan) | Complete anthology of a poet's work |

## 2. Famous Poets — مشہور شعرا

### Mirza Ghalib (مرزا غالب) — 1797–1869
- Born in Agra, lived in Delhi; supreme master of the Urdu ghazal
- Famous couplets to memorize:

> ہزاروں خواہشیں ایسی کہ ہر خواہش پہ دم نکلے
> *Thousands of desires — each desire enough to die for.*

> عشق پر زور نہیں ہے یہ وہ آتش غالب
> کہ لگائے نہ لگے اور بجھائے نہ بجھے
> *Love is that fire you cannot ignite at will, nor extinguish.*

### Faiz Ahmed Faiz (فیض احمد فیض) — 1911–1984
- Merged classical ghazal with progressive political consciousness
- Pakistan's most celebrated modern poet

> مجھ سے پہلی سی محبت مری محبوب نہ مانگ
> *Do not ask me, my beloved, for that earlier love.*

> آج بازار میں پا بجولاں چلو
> *Come, let us walk today in the marketplace, in chains.*

### Fahmida Riaz (فہمیدہ ریاض) — 1945–2018
- Pakistan's pioneering feminist poet; wrote in Urdu and Sindhi

> تم بالکل ہم جیسے نکلے
> *You turned out to be exactly like us.* (addressed to India)

## 5. Essential Idioms — اردو محاورے

| Idiom | Literal | Actual Meaning |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| آنکھیں کھلنا | eyes opening | to realize the truth |
| خون کھولنا | blood boiling | to become enraged |
| کان کھڑے ہونا | ears standing up | to become alert/suspicious |
| نو دو گیارہ ہونا | nine two eleven | to run away / flee |
| آگ بگولہ ہونا | whirlwind of fire | to become furious |
| پیٹھ پھیرنا | turning one's back | to betray / abandon |
| دل چاہنا | heart wanting | to feel like doing something |
| ٹانگ اڑانا | kicking a leg | to interfere unnecessarily |
| چولہا جلنا | stove lighting | to have livelihood / income |
| منہ بند رکھنا | keeping mouth shut | to stay silent |
| ہاتھ ڈھیلا کرنا | loosening the hand | to spend money generously |
| چاند پر تھوکنا | spitting at the moon | trying to harm someone greater |
| دل کا بوجھ ہلکا کرنا | lightening the heart | to feel relieved after expressing |
| آنکھ مارنا | winking | to give a signal |
| تیل دیکھو تیل کی دھار | watch the oil flow | be patient / wait and watch |

## 6. Regional Dialects Quick Reference — لہجوں کا فرق

| Feature | Lahori Urdu | Karachi Urdu |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| Characteristic | Punjabi-inflected | Standard / Delhi-heritage |
| Common filler | یار (yaar) — very frequent | کیا بات ہے (admiration) |
| Style | More direct, louder | Considered standard pronunciation |
| Influence | Heavy Punjabi mixing | Muhajir / Delhi vocabulary |

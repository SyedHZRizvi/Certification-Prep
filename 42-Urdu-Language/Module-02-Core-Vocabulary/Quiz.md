## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

**Q1 — C) ماموں (Mamoon)**
Mamoon is the specific term for your mother's brother (maternal uncle). Chacha is father's younger brother, Taaya is father's older brother, Phoopha is father's sister's husband.

**Q2 — B) A spiced rice dish with meat**
Biryani (بریانی) is Pakistan's iconic fragrant rice dish cooked with spices, meat (usually mutton or chicken), and aromatic herbs. It is arguably Pakistan's most beloved dish.

**Q3 — B) ماں (Maan)**
ماں (Maan) is the plain/formal word for mother. امی (Ammi) is the affectionate Pakistani term, similar to "Mum." دادی and نانی are grandmothers.

**Q4 — C) Lentils**
دال (Daal) means lentils, and daal dishes are a staple of Pakistani everyday cooking. Dozens of varieties exist: دال مخنی (Daal Makhni), دال تڑکا (Daal Tarka), etc.

**Q5 — C) Eye**
آنکھ (Aankh) means eye. This word appears frequently in Urdu poetry as a symbol of beauty. "کان" = ear, "ناک" = nose, "منہ" = mouth.

**Q6 — B) پڑھنا (Parhna)**
پڑھنا means both "to read" and "to study." لکھنا = to write, دیکھنا = to see/watch, سننا = to listen/hear.

**Q7 — C) Today**
آج (Aaj) = today. Note that کل (kal) means BOTH yesterday AND tomorrow — context determines which.

**Q8 — B) Mother's sister (maternal aunt)**
خالہ (Khala) is specifically your mother's sister. پھوپھی (Phoophi) is your father's sister. This distinction is crucial in Pakistani family culture.

**Q9 — C) Meat**
گوشت (Gosht) means meat. It appears in many dish names: گوشت کا سالن (gosht ka salan — meat curry). مرغی (Murghi) = chicken specifically.

**Q10 — B) کندھا (Kandha)**
کندھا = shoulder. کمر = back/waist, سینہ = chest, گھٹنا = knee.

**Q11 — D) Right now / Just now**
ابھی (Abhi) means right now or just now. "ابھی آتا/آتی ہوں" (Abhi aata/aati hun) = "I'm coming right now" — a very common phrase.

**Q12 — B) Father's sister (paternal aunt)**
پھوپھی (Phoophi) is your father's sister. Her husband is called پھوپھا (Phoopha). خالہ is your mother's sister.

**Q13 — C) Tandoor-baked leavened flatbread**
نان (Naan) is the leavened bread baked in a clay tandoor oven. روٹی (Roti) is the simpler whole wheat flatbread, and پراٹھا (Paratha) is a layered pan-fried flatbread.

**Q14 — C) Back / Waist**
کمر (Kamar) refers to the back and waist area. سر = head, گھٹنا = knee, سینہ = chest.

**Q15 — C) Friday**
جمعہ (Juma) is Friday, the holy day in Islam when congregational prayers (جمعہ نماز — Juma Namaz) are obligatory for Muslim men. This day has special significance in Pakistan.

**Q16 — C) Father's younger brother**
چاچا (Chacha) specifically means your father's younger brother. تایا (Taaya) is your father's older brother — the age distinction matters in Urdu. ماموں = mother's brother.

**Q17 — B) A yogurt drink**
لسی (Lassi) is a traditional Pakistani/South Asian drink made from yogurt. It can be sweet (میٹھی لسی) or salty (نمکین لسی). Lahori lassi served in earthen pots is particularly famous.

**Q18 — C) بھولنا (Bhoolna)**
بھولنا = to forget. یاد کرنا = to remember/memorize, جاننا = to know, سوچنا = to think.

**Q19 — C) The day before yesterday / day after tomorrow**
پرسوں (Parson) covers two meanings: the day before yesterday and the day after tomorrow. Like کل (kal), context determines which. If needed: "گزشتہ پرسوں" (past) vs "آنے والا پرسوں" (coming).

**Q20 — D) Always**
ہمیشہ (Hamesha) = always. کبھی کبھی = sometimes, کبھی نہیں = never, اکثر = often.

**Q21 — C) Kitchen**
باورچی خانہ (Bawarchi Khaana) literally means "cook's room/house" — bawarchi being the cook. This is the traditional Urdu word for kitchen.

**Q22 — C) Mango**
آم (Aam) = mango. Pakistan is famous for its mangoes — varieties like Chaunsa, Anwer Ratol, and Sindhri are considered among the world's best. "سیب" = apple, "کیلا" = banana.

**Q23 — B) بیٹی (Beti)**
بیٹی (Beti) = daughter. بیٹا (Beta) = son. بہن (Behen) = sister. بچی (Bachchi) = girl/female child (more general).

**Q24 — C) Courtyard**
صحن (Sahan) is a central courtyard, a defining architectural feature of traditional Pakistani (and broader South Asian) homes. Families gather, cook, and socialize in the sahan.

**Q25 — C) Father's older brother**
تایا (Taaya) is specifically your father's older brother, while چاچا (Chacha) is your father's younger brother. In traditional culture, تایا carries more seniority and is addressed with greater deference.

**Q26 — B) خریدنا (Khareedna)**
خریدنا = to buy. بیچنا (Bechna) = to sell — the two words are opposite. دینا = to give, لینا = to take.

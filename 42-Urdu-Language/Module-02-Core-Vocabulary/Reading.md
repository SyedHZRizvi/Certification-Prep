## Introduction to Core Vocabulary

Building a solid vocabulary base is the single most effective thing you can do as a beginner. Research in language acquisition consistently shows that knowing the most frequent **2,000 words** in a language allows you to understand roughly 90% of everyday conversation. This module introduces vocabulary across six essential categories: family, body, food, home, daily actions, and time words — all organized around Pakistani cultural context.

A key feature of this module: every table shows **Urdu script first**, because training your eye to read the script from the very beginning is how fluency develops. Transliteration is a crutch — use it to check pronunciation, but always return your eyes to the Urdu script.

## Family Vocabulary — خاندان (Khaandaan)

Pakistani family structures and terminology are richer and more specific than English. Where English uses "uncle" for both parents' brothers, Urdu distinguishes between your father's brother (چاچا), your mother's brother (ماموں), your father's sister's husband (پھوپھا), and your mother's sister's husband (خالو). This precision reflects the deep importance of family hierarchy in Pakistani culture.

## Time Words — وقت کے الفاظ (Waqt ke Alfaaz)

Time vocabulary is essential from the earliest stages of conversation, as nearly every exchange involves when things happen.

### Days and Relative Time

| Urdu Script | Transliteration | English |
|-------------|-----------------|---------|
| آج | aaj | Today |
| کل | kal | Yesterday / Tomorrow (context determines which!) |
| پرسوں | parson | Day before yesterday / Day after tomorrow |
| اب | ab | Now |
| ابھی | abhi | Right now / Just now |
| پہلے | pehle | Before / Earlier / First |
| بعد | baad | After / Later |
| پھر | phir | Then / Again |
| ہمیشہ | hamesha | Always |
| کبھی کبھی | kabhi kabhi | Sometimes |
| اکثر | aksar | Often / Usually |
| کبھی نہیں | kabhi nahin | Never |
| جلدی | jaldi | Quickly / Soon |
| آہستہ | aahista | Slowly |

> **Important**: کل (kal) means BOTH yesterday and tomorrow. Context makes it clear, but you can clarify: **گزشتہ کل** (guzishta kal — past yesterday = yesterday) and **آنے والا کل** (aanewala kal — coming tomorrow = tomorrow).

### Days of the Week (دن)

| Urdu Script | Transliteration | English |
|-------------|-----------------|---------|
| پیر | peer | Monday |
| منگل | mangal | Tuesday |

| ہفتہ | hafta | Saturday (also means "week") |
| اتوار | itwar | Sunday |

> **Note**: In Pakistan, the weekend is **Saturday and Sunday**, though Friday prayers (جمعہ نماز) are important. In some workplaces, Friday afternoon is a half-day.

## بنیادی الفاظ کا تعارف

زبان سیکھنے میں الفاظ کا ذخیرہ بنانا سب سے موثر کام ہے۔ تحقیق سے پتہ چلتا ہے کہ کسی بھی زبان کے سب سے زیادہ استعمال ہونے والے **دو ہزار الفاظ** جاننے سے روزمرہ گفتگو کا تقریباً نوے فیصد حصہ سمجھ آتا ہے۔ یہ ماڈیول چھ اہم موضوعات پر الفاظ پیش کرتا ہے: خاندان، جسم، کھانا، گھر، روزمرہ کام، اور وقت کے الفاظ — سب پاکستانی ثقافتی سیاق و سباق کے ساتھ۔

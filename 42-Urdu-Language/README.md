# اردو زبان کی مہارت — Urdu Language Mastery
## Complete Course Guide for English Speakers

---

## Who This Course Is For

This course is designed for **English speakers** who want to learn Urdu — Pakistan's national language, one of the world's most expressive and literary languages, spoken by over 230 million people. Whether you are connecting with Pakistani family, preparing for professional work in South Asia, or simply captivated by the beauty of Urdu poetry, this course takes you from absolute beginner to C2 (near-native) mastery.

**Ideal for:**
- English speakers with no prior Urdu knowledge
- Heritage learners who speak some Urdu but want to read and write in Nastaliq script
- Professionals working with Pakistani colleagues, clients, or government
- Literature lovers drawn to Ghalib, Faiz, and the ghazal tradition
- Students planning to live, work, or study in Pakistan

## Special Features of This Course

### 1. Nastaliq Script Throughout
Every module uses **authentic Nastaliq Urdu script** — the flowing, calligraphic style used in Pakistani print media, books, and official documents. We do not substitute simplified script or Roman transliteration as a crutch. Transliteration is provided as support in early modules and faded out as you progress.

### 2. The Urdu Honorifics System
Urdu has one of the world's most elaborate honorific systems, reflecting Persian and Islamic influences. This course systematically teaches:
- **آپ vs تم vs تو** — the three-tier pronoun system
- **صاحب/صاحبہ, محترم/محترمہ** — professional titles
- **جناب, حضرت** — formal address
- Verb agreement with honorific pronouns

### 3. Bilingual Reading.md Files
Every module's Reading.md has a built-in language switcher — click English for explanations, click اردو to immerse in the language. The Urdu interface uses Noto Nastaliq Urdu font with right-to-left layout.

### 4. The Ergative Construction
Module 7 dedicates substantial space to **ergativity** — the hardest single concept in Urdu for English speakers. The rule: transitive verbs in past tense take **نے** on the subject and the verb agrees with the **object**, not the subject. This course explains it step by step with systematic drills.

### 5. Living Cultural Content
The course does not treat Urdu as a dead classroom language. You will study:
- Ghalib's verses still quoted in everyday conversation
- Pakistani drama dialogue patterns
- Coke Studio's role in reviving Sufi poetry
- Social media and Roman Urdu phenomena

## A Note on Urdu and Hindi

Urdu and Hindi share the same spoken grammar and basic vocabulary but differ in:
- **Script:** Urdu uses Nastaliq (right-to-left); Hindi uses Devanagari (left-to-right)
- **Vocabulary:** Urdu draws on Persian/Arabic for formal register; Hindi draws on Sanskrit
- **Cultural identity:** Urdu is Pakistan's national language and associated with Islamic culture; Hindi is India's official language

If you learn Urdu, you will largely understand spoken Hindi and can communicate with over a billion people across South Asia.

## Urdu — ایک خاص زبان

Urdu is not merely a practical tool — it is a civilization. It carries the weight of Mughal poetry, the passion of the Pakistan Movement, the wit of Bombay cinema, and the lyricism of Sufi devotion. When you speak Urdu, you join a conversation that has been going on for five hundred years.

> جو بات اردو میں کہی جا سکتی ہے، وہ کسی اور زبان میں نہیں کہی جا سکتی۔
> *Jo baat Urdu mein kahi ja sakti hai, woh kisi aur zubaan mein nahin kahi ja sakti.*
> What can be said in Urdu cannot be said in any other language.

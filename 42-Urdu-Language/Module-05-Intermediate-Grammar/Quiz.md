## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

**Q1 — B) اس نے کھایا**
For past tense with a transitive verb and a third-person masculine singular subject, the correct form is "اس نے کھایا." "وہ کھاتا ہے" = present, "وہ کھائے گا" = future, "وہ کھا رہا ہے" = present continuous.

**Q2 — B) Because پڑھنا is a transitive verb taking an object (کتاب)**
The "نے" construction (ergative case) applies to transitive verbs — verbs that have a direct object — in the past tense. Here, the book (کتاب) is the object. کتاب being feminine causes the verb to take the feminine form (پڑھی not پڑھا).

**Q3 — C) میں آئی**
آنا (to come) is an intransitive verb (no object) — so "نے" is NOT used. For a female speaker, the feminine past form is آئی. "میں آیا" would be for a male speaker.

**Q4 — C) She will go to Lahore**
"جائے گی" is the future tense, feminine singular form. "گی" at the end marks feminine. "جائے گا" would be masculine (he will go).

**Q5 — B) I am learning Urdu**
"سیکھ رہا ہوں" = verb stem (سیکھ) + رہا (continuous marker, masculine) + ہوں (I am). This is the present continuous tense. "رہا" marks it as ongoing.

**Q6 — D) وہ پڑھ رہی تھی**
Past continuous: verb stem (پڑھ) + رہی (continuous marker, feminine — she) + تھی (past form of "to be" for feminine singular). "تھی" instead of "ہے" makes it past.

**Q7 — C) Before the verb (near the end)**
Since Urdu verbs come at the end of sentences, "نہیں" goes just before the verb: "وہ نہیں کھاتا" (verb: کھاتا, نہیں before it). Not at the absolute end, but before the verb at the end.

**Q8 — C) مت جاؤ is a stronger, firmer prohibition; نہ جاؤ is gentler**
"نہ" is softer, used for requests or gentle prohibitions. "مت" is stronger and more direct — closer to "don't you dare" in tone. Both mean "don't go" but the register differs.

**Q9 — B) To eat (with nuance of completion or personal benefit)**
لینا (to take) as a light verb in compound verbs adds nuance of completion or action done for one's own benefit. "کھا لیا" = I've eaten it (done, complete, for myself).

**Q10 — C) Going ahead and telling someone / doing the action for others**
دینا (to give) as a light verb suggests the action is done for someone else's benefit or suggests a sense of finality — "getting it done" for others. "بتا دو" = go ahead and tell them.

**Q11 — C) کیونکہ (kyonke)**
کیونکہ = because (introduces a reason/cause clause). اور = and, لیکن = but, اگر = if.

**Q12 — B) کیونکہ**
"میں نہیں آیا کیونکہ بیمار تھا" = "I didn't come because I was sick." کیونکہ introduces the reason/cause.

**Q13 — C) A conditional clause (if)**
"اگر" (agar) = if. It introduces the condition in an if-then construction. "اگر آئیں تو بتائیں" = If you come, let me know.

**Q14 — C) اچھی کتاب**
کتاب is feminine. Adjectives ending in ا change: اچھا (masculine) → اچھی (feminine). "اچھی کتاب" = a good book.

**Q15 — B) بازار (market) is a masculine noun**
کا is used with masculine singular nouns that are possessed. بازار is grammatically masculine, so "لاہور کا بازار" (Lahore's market) uses کا.

**Q16 — A) زبان (language) is a feminine noun**
زبان (zabaan — language/tongue) is grammatically feminine. Therefore the possessive particle is کی: "لاہور کی زبان" (the language of Lahore / Lahore's language).

**Q17 — B) بتائیں (bataayen)**
Formal requests use the آپ form, which requires the "-yen" suffix: بتائیں. "بتاؤ" (bataao) is the تم (familiar) form. For formal polite requests, always use the -yen ending.

**Q18 — A) Please sit**
"براہ کرم" = please + "بیٹھیں" (formal sit command). Full meaning: Please sit (down). بیٹھیں is the formal imperative of بیٹھنا.

**Q19 — C) "They" did the action (past tense ergative marker for plural third person)**
"انہوں نے" (unhon ne) is the past tense ergative form of "they" (third person plural). Compare: "اس نے" = he/she did (singular), "انہوں نے" = they did (plural).

**Q20 — C) Will you come tomorrow?**
"آئیں گے" is the future tense (you will come — formal masculine plural form). "کل" = tomorrow. The question asks about a future action.

**Q21 — D) میں نے گیا**
This is INCORRECT. جانا (to go) is an intransitive verb — it does not take "نے." The correct form is simply "میں گیا" (I went, male) or "میں گئی" (I went, female). Options A, B, C are all correct.

**Q22 — C) It is raining (right now)**
"بارش ہو رہی ہے" — بارش (rain) + ہو (verb stem) + رہی ہے (present continuous, feminine — بارش is feminine). This is the present continuous. A very common phrase in Pakistani weather conversations!

**Q23 — B) A cause and its result (therefore/so)**
"اس لیے" (is liye) = therefore/so/for this reason. It links a cause to its effect. "وہ تھکا تھا اس لیے سو گیا" = He was tired therefore he slept.

**Q24 — B) پرانی**
"پرانا" is an adjective ending in ا. With feminine singular nouns: ا changes to ی → "پرانی." With masculine plural: ا changes to ے → "پرانے."

**Q25 — B) بچے is plural**
کے is used with plural nouns (regardless of gender) and in adjective forms before another word. "بچے" (children) is plural, so "میرے دوست کے بچے" uses کے. If singular masculine: "میرے دوست کا بچہ," if singular feminine: "میرے دوست کی بچی."

**Q26 — B) He was tired therefore he slept**
"اس لیے" = therefore / so / for this reason. "تھکا تھا" = was tired (past), "سو گیا" = fell asleep / went to sleep (compound verb: سونا + جانا = to go to sleep completely). Full translation: He was tired, therefore he went to sleep.

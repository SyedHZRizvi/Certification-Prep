# Module 4 Quiz — المحادثات اليومية (Everyday Conversations)

Test your knowledge of situational Arabic phrases, dialects, and cultural expressions.

---

**1.** How do you say "You are most welcome" as a formal invitation to sit in MSA?

- A) شُكْرًا
- B) تَفَضَّلُوا
- C) مَعَ السَّلَامَة
- D) جَزَاكَ اللّٰهُ خَيْرًا

**Answer: B) تَفَضَّلُوا**

---

**2.** The phrase يُسْعِدُنِي أَنْ أَلْتَقِيَ بِكُم means:

- A) Goodbye, see you later
- B) I am delighted to meet you
- C) Thank you for coming
- D) The honour is mine

**Answer: B) I am delighted to meet you**

---

**3.** In Egyptian dialect, how do you say "What?" instead of MSA مَاذَا?

- A) شُو
- B) وِيشْ
- C) إيه
- D) كِيف

**Answer: C) إيه**

---

**4.** How do you ask for the bill in Arabic?

- A) مَاذَا تُرِيد؟
- B) كَم الحِسَاب؟
- C) هَل عِنْدَكَ قَهْوَة؟
- D) أَيْن المَطْعَم؟

**Answer: B) كَم الحِسَاب؟**

---

**5.** What does حَاضِر (ḥāḍir) mean when a waiter says it?

- A) It is finished
- B) I don't have it
- C) Right away / Ready
- D) Too expensive

**Answer: C) Right away / Ready**

---

**6.** The Arabic word for "discount" in a souk context is:

- A) سِعْر
- B) تَخْفِيض
- C) غَالِي
- D) رَخِيص

**Answer: B) تَخْفِيض**

---

**7.** When bargaining is complete and a deal is agreed, you say:

- A) لَا بَأْس
- B) مَعَ السَّلَامَة
- C) اِتَّفَقْنَا
- D) كَم السِّعْر؟

**Answer: C) اِتَّفَقْنَا**

---

**8.** The full Islamic greeting is السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُم وَرَحْمَةُ اللّٰهِ وَبَرَكَاتُه. What does وَبَرَكَاتُه add?

- A) And His guidance
- B) And His blessings
- C) And His mercy
- D) And His peace

**Answer: B) And His blessings**

---

**9.** جَزَاكَ اللّٰهُ خَيْرًا is said to:

- A) Express condolences
- B) Thank someone in a religious way (may God reward you)
- C) Ask for forgiveness
- D) Begin eating

**Answer: B) Thank someone in a religious way — may God reward you with good**

---

**10.** The phrase إِنْ شَاءَ اللّٰه literally means:

- A) God is great
- B) What God has willed
- C) If God wills
- D) Praise be to God

**Answer: C) If God wills**

---

**11.** In Arab hospitality culture, if a host offers you more food and you are full, you should say:

- A) لَا أُرِيدُ أَيَّ شَيْء
- B) شُكْرًا، أَنَا شَبْعَان
- C) الطَّعَام لَيْسَ لَذِيذًا
- D) مَعَ السَّلَامَة

**Answer: B) شُكْرًا، أَنَا شَبْعَان**

---

**12.** The Arabic word كَرَم (karam) means:

- A) Food
- B) Generosity / hospitality
- C) Guest
- D) Home

**Answer: B) Generosity / hospitality**

---

**13.** What is the Arabic word for "passport"?

- A) تَأْشِيرَة
- B) بَوَّابَة
- C) جَوَازُ السَّفَر
- D) مَطَار

**Answer: C) جَوَازُ السَّفَر**

---

**14.** What does طَابِق (ṭābiq) mean in the context of a hotel?

- A) Room number
- B) Floor / storey
- C) Lobby
- D) Key

**Answer: B) Floor / storey**

---

**15.** How do you say "I have a fever" in Arabic?

- A) عِنْدِي أَلَمٌ فِي الرَّأْس
- B) عِنْدِي حُمَّى
- C) عِنْدِي سُعَال
- D) أَشْعُرُ بِتَعَب

**Answer: B) عِنْدِي حُمَّى**

---

**16.** مُنْذُ يَوْمَيْن means:

- A) After two days
- B) For two months
- C) Since two days
- D) In two hours

**Answer: C) Since two days**

---

**17.** The Arabic word for "prescription" (medical) is:

- A) دَوَاء
- B) وَصْفَة
- C) مُسْتَشْفَى
- D) أَلَم

**Answer: B) وَصْفَة**

---

**18.** In Gulf Arabic, how do you say "I want"? (instead of MSA أُرِيدُ)

- A) عَايِز
- B) بِدِّي
- C) أَبِي / أَبْغَى
- D) إيه

**Answer: C) أَبِي / أَبْغَى**

---

**19.** In Levantine Arabic, "right now" is said as:

- A) الحِين
- B) دَلْوَقْتِي
- C) هَلَّق
- D) الآن

**Answer: C) هَلَّق**

---

**20.** The phrase الشَّرَفُ لَنَا (ash-sharafu lanā) is used:

- A) When asking for a price
- B) When expressing that the honour is yours (welcoming the other person)
- C) When refusing an offer
- D) When saying goodbye

**Answer: B) When expressing that the honour is yours**

---

**21.** The Arabic word ضَيْف (ḍayf) means:

- A) Food
- B) Host
- C) Guest
- D) Invitation

**Answer: C) Guest**

---

**22.** لَوْ سَمَحْت (law samaḥt) is used to mean:

- A) Excuse me / If you please
- B) How much?
- C) Thank you
- D) You're welcome

**Answer: A) Excuse me / If you please**

---

**23.** What does حَجْز (ḥajz) mean in the context of a hotel?

- A) Luggage
- B) Key
- C) Reservation / booking
- D) Payment

**Answer: C) Reservation / booking**

---

**24.** In Egyptian dialect, "or" is expressed as:

- A) أَوْ (MSA)
- B) وَلَّا
- C) أَمّا
- D) لَكِن

**Answer: B) وَلَّا**

---

**25.** مَاشَاءَ اللّٰه is said when:

- A) Starting a meal
- B) Expressing admiration at something good
- C) Saying goodbye
- D) Asking for forgiveness

**Answer: B) Expressing admiration at something good**

---

**26.** The Arabic word لَذِيذ (ladhīdh) means:

- A) Hot
- B) Filling
- C) Delicious
- D) Expensive

**Answer: C) Delicious**

### Detailed Explanations

**1-B:** The email explicitly states: أودّ أن أقترح موعداً لاجتماع لمراجعة النتائج الأولية — "I would like to propose a meeting to review the preliminary findings."

**2-C:** The email closes with عمر الشريف — مدير المشاريع — Project Manager.

**3-B:** لديّ ملاحظات تتعلق بالميزانية المقترحة... أعتقد أننا بحاجة إلى تعديل بعض البنود — "I have observations about the proposed budget... I believe we need to adjust some items before submitting the final report."

**4-C:** أنا متاح طوال أيام الأسبوع من الساعة التاسعة صباحاً حتى الخامسة مساءً — "I am available all days of the week from 9 am until 5 pm."

**5-B:** تحية طيبة وبعد is the standard Arabic formal letter opener — literally "Warm/Good greetings, and then (to the matter at hand)..." It is equivalent to "Dear Sir/Madam" combined with "I hope this finds you well."

**6-B:** مع خالص التحية والتقدير = "With sincere greetings and respect" — the standard formal Arabic letter closing (equivalent to "Yours sincerely" or "Kind regards").

**7-B:** أعلنت الحكومة السعودية اليوم عن إطلاق مشروع ضخم للطاقة الشمسية في منطقة نيوم — announced a large solar energy project in the Neom region.

**8-C:** يهدف إلى إنتاج أكثر من عشرة غيغاواط... بحلول عام ألفين وثلاثين — "aims to produce more than ten gigawatts by the year 2030."

**9-A:** يُعدّ (yuʻaddu) = "is considered" (passive of form II عَدَّ). يُعدّ من أكبر = "is considered one of the largest."

**10-B:** سيوفّر آلاف فرص العمل ويُساهم في تحقيق أهداف رؤية المملكة — "will provide thousands of job opportunities and contribute to achieving the goals of the Kingdom's Vision."

**11-C:** ذهبتْ (dhāhabat) = she went. The تْ suffix with sukūn indicates third person feminine singular. (ذهبتُ = I went; ذهبتَ = you went, masc.)

**12-C:** نعرف (naʻrifu) = we know. The first-person plural present is formed with the prefix ن + root. (يعرفون = they know, تعرفون = you pl. know, أعرف = I know)

**13-B:** لم (lam) negates the past using the present tense verb in jussive (مجزوم) mood: لم يذهب = he did not go. This is one of the most common grammar points in Arabic. Note: لم ذهب would be wrong.

**14-C:** Past continuous = كان + present tense: كانتْ تقرأُ (kānat taqraʼu) = she was reading. قرأتْ = she read (simple past); تقرأُ = she reads (present); ستقرأُ = she will read.

**15-B:** كُتُب (kutub) is the broken plural of كتاب (kitāb). Broken plurals change the internal vowel pattern — this is not predictable but must be memorised. كتابات would be the sound feminine plural for "writings."

**16-C:** أسلم (aslama) has the pattern أفعل (afʻala) — Form IV. The prefix أ with the three-letter root س-ل-م. Form IV is causative/transitional.

**17-B:** اجتمع (ijtamaʻa) = to gather/meet together. This is Form VIII (افتعل pattern) from root ج-م-ع (to gather). The اجتماع (meeting) noun comes from this verb.

**18-A:** لو ذهبتَ لرأيتَ is a correct contrary-to-fact conditional: لو + past verb (condition) + لـ + past verb (result). Options B, C, D all contain incorrect tense/particle combinations.

**19-C:** الكبيرُ (al-kabīru) is an adjective (نعت — naʻt) modifying البيتُ (the house). It agrees with the noun in definiteness, gender, number, and case. The predicate is جميلٌ (beautiful).

**20-C:** طالبان (ṭālibān) is the nominative dual form. Dual is formed by adding ـان (āni) in nominative and ـين (ayni) in oblique. طالبتان would be the feminine dual; طلاب is the plural.

**21-C:** In an iḍāfa (إضافة) construction, the second noun always takes the genitive case (مجرور — marked with kasra ِ or tanwīn kasra ٍ). The first noun (mudīr) takes the case required by its sentence role.

**22-A:** الطالبُ الذي يدرسُ سينجح is correct. الذي is the masculine singular relative pronoun. It must agree with the antecedent: masculine noun → الذي, feminine noun → التي. Options B and C show gender mismatch.

**23-C:** في (fī) = in / at / on. من (min) = from, إلى (ilā) = to/towards, مع (maʻa) = with, بـ (bi-) = by/with.

**24-C:** مكتبة (maktaba) = library/bookshop. The pattern مَفعَلة (mafʻala) often denotes a place where the root activity happens. ك-ت-ب = to write → مكتبة = place of books. مكتب = office/desk (same root, different pattern).

**25-A:** أنا لست طالباً (anā lastu ṭāliban) is correct. لست (lastu) is the negated form of كان for present meaning: "I am not." The predicate طالباً takes accusative case after لست (like after كان).

**26-B:** اجتماع (ijtimāʻ) = meeting/assembly. Root: ج-م-ع. Form VIII verb: اجتمع (to gather together).

**27-C:** الموارد البشرية (al-mawārid al-bashariyya) = Human Resources. الموارد = resources; البشرية = human.

**28-B:** تصدير (taṣdīr) = export. Root: ص-د-ر. Opposite: استيراد (istīrād) = import.

**29-B:** ميزانية (mīzāniyya) = budget. From ميزان (mīzān) = scales/balance. The word reflects the concept of balancing income and expenditure.

**30-A:** برجاء الاطلاع على المرفق = "Please find attached / Please review the attached document." برجاء = please/kindly; المرفق = the attachment.

**31-B:** مجلس (majlis) means council/assembly. In Gulf culture, it is also the traditional reception room where a host receives guests for business or social meetings — an important cultural institution in Gulf business practice.

**32-B:** تحوّل رقمي (taḥawwul raqmī) = Digital Transformation. تحوّل = transformation (Form V); رقمي = digital (from رقم = number). This is a core term in all Arab national Vision plans.

**33-B:** اتجه إلى اليسار عند الإشارة الضوئية = "Head/turn to the left at the traffic light." اتجه = head towards (Form VIII imperative); اليسار = the left; الإشارة الضوئية = the traffic light.

**34-C:** بكم هذا؟ = "How much does this cost?" بكم = for how much; هذا = this (masculine).

**35-C:** السعر غالٍ = "The price is expensive." غالٍ (ghālin) is the indefinite nominative form of غالي. رخيص = cheap; مناسب = reasonable; معقول = acceptable/reasonable.

**36-B:** أعطني القائمة من فضلك = "Give me the menu, please." القائمة = the menu; أعطني = give me (imperative). أريد الحساب = I want the bill; الفاتورة = invoice/bill.

**37-B:** متى تغلق المحلات؟ = "When do the shops close?" متى = when; تغلق = she/it closes (third feminine, or plural in informal usage); المحلات = the shops.

**38-B:** نعم، أتكلم العربية قليلاً = "Yes, I speak Arabic a little." قليلاً (qalīlan) = a little (adverb, accusative). This is the most natural learner's response.

**39-C:** Arabic is the official or co-official language in 22 countries (the Arab League member states), making it one of the world's most widely distributed official languages.

**40-A:** الجزيرة (al-jazīra) = the island / the peninsula. Qatar, where Al Jazeera is based, is a peninsula (شبه الجزيرة = semi-island). Al Jazeera Arabic news is the benchmark for MSA listening practice.

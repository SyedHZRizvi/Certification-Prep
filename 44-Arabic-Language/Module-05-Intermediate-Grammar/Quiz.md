# Module 5 Quiz — القواعد المتوسطة (Intermediate Grammar)

Test your knowledge of verb conjugation, broken plurals, iḍāfa, adjective agreement, negation, and verb forms.

---

**1.** What is the past tense "she wrote" in Arabic?

- A) كَتَبَ
- B) كَتَبَتْ
- C) كَتَبْتِ
- D) تَكْتُبُ

**Answer: B) كَتَبَتْ**

---

**2.** Which form is correct for "I wrote" in Arabic?

- A) كَتَبَ
- B) كَتَبْتَ
- C) كَتَبْتُ
- D) نَكْتُبُ

**Answer: C) كَتَبْتُ**

---

**3.** The prefix يَـ in an imperfect verb indicates which person?

- A) I
- B) We
- C) He / They (m. pl with ـُونَ suffix)
- D) You (m)

**Answer: C) He / They (m. pl with ـُونَ suffix)**

---

**4.** How do you say "she writes" in Arabic?

- A) يَكْتُبُ
- B) تَكْتُبُ
- C) أَكْتُبُ
- D) نَكْتُبُ

**Answer: B) تَكْتُبُ** (same prefix as masculine "you", distinguished by context)

---

**5.** To express the future tense, Arabic adds which particle before the imperfect verb?

- A) قَد (qad)
- B) كَانَ (kāna)
- C) سَـ (sa-)
- D) هَل (hal)

**Answer: C) سَـ (sa-)** — سَيَكْتُبُ = he will write

---

**6.** The broken plural of بَيْت (house) is:

- A) بُيُوت
- B) بَيْتَات
- C) أَبْيَات
- D) بَيْتَانِ

**Answer: A) بُيُوت**

---

**7.** What is the plural of مَسْجِد (mosque)?

- A) مَسْجِدَات
- B) مَسَاجِد
- C) مَسْجِدُون
- D) أَمْسِجَة

**Answer: B) مَسَاجِد**

---

**8.** The plural of صَدِيق (friend) is:

- A) صُدَقَاء
- B) أَصْدِقَاء
- C) صَدِيقَات
- D) أَصْدِق

**Answer: B) أَصْدِقَاء**

---

**9.** In the phrase كِتَابُ الطَّالِبِ (the student's book), which case is الطَّالِب in?

- A) Nominative
- B) Accusative
- C) Genitive
- D) Vocative

**Answer: C) Genitive** (second noun in iḍāfa is always in the genitive)

---

**10.** In iḍāfa كِتَابُ الطَّالِبِ, the first noun كِتَاب:

- A) Takes الـ
- B) Loses its nunation (tanwīn) and becomes definite without الـ
- C) Takes a fatha
- D) Remains indefinite

**Answer: B) Loses its nunation and becomes definite without taking الـ**

---

**11.** What is the pronominal suffix for "my" in Arabic?

- A) ـُه
- B) ـُكَ
- C) ـِي
- D) ـُهَا

**Answer: C) ـِي** — e.g., كِتَابِي (my book)

---

**12.** How do you say "his book" in Arabic?

- A) كِتَابُكَ
- B) كِتَابِي
- C) كِتَابُه
- D) كِتَابُهَا

**Answer: C) كِتَابُه**

---

**13.** Which rule applies to adjective agreement with non-human plurals?

- A) The adjective must be masculine plural
- B) The adjective must be feminine plural
- C) The adjective is feminine singular
- D) No agreement is needed

**Answer: C) The adjective is feminine singular** — الكُتُبُ الجَدِيدَةُ (the new books)

---

**14.** Which is the correct form for "the new books"?

- A) الكُتُبُ الجُدُد
- B) الكُتُبُ الجَدِيدُون
- C) الكُتُبُ الجَدِيدَةُ
- D) الكُتُبُ الجُدَدَاء

**Answer: C) الكُتُبُ الجَدِيدَةُ** (non-human plural → feminine singular adjective)

---

**15.** Which negation word is used to negate PAST actions?

- A) لَا
- B) لَن
- C) لَيْسَ
- D) لَم

**Answer: D) لَم** — followed by the jussive (apocopate) form of the verb

---

**16.** How do you say "He is not a doctor" in Arabic?

- A) لَم يَكُنْ طَبِيبًا
- B) هُوَ لَيْسَ طَبِيبًا
- C) لَا يَكُونُ طَبِيبًا
- D) لَن يَكُونَ طَبِيبًا

**Answer: B) هُوَ لَيْسَ طَبِيبًا**

---

**17.** "He will not go" in Arabic uses which negation + mood?

- A) لَم + jussive
- B) لَا + indicative
- C) لَيْسَ + predicate
- D) لَن + subjunctive (manṣūb)

**Answer: D) لَن + subjunctive** — لَن يَذْهَبَ

---

**18.** The Arabic conjunction because is:

- A) وَ
- B) لَكِن
- C) لِأَنَّ
- D) ثُمَّ

**Answer: C) لِأَنَّ**

---

**19.** Form II verbs (فَعَّلَ) typically express:

- A) Passive meaning
- B) Seeking/requesting
- C) Intensification or causation
- D) Reflexive of the basic action

**Answer: C) Intensification or causation** — e.g., عَلَّمَ (he taught / caused to know)

---

**20.** Form V (تَفَعَّلَ) is typically the:

- A) Causative of Form I
- B) Reflexive of Form II
- C) Passive of Form IV
- D) Reciprocal of Form III

**Answer: B) Reflexive of Form II** — e.g., تَعَلَّمَ (to learn, for oneself) = reflexive of عَلَّمَ (to teach)

---

**21.** Form X (اِسْتَفْعَلَ) primarily means:

- A) To do an action intensely
- B) To become a colour
- C) To seek or request something
- D) To do an action together

**Answer: C) To seek or request** — e.g., اِسْتَغْفَرَ (to seek forgiveness)

---

**22.** The subject of a sentence is in which grammatical case?

- A) Genitive
- B) Accusative
- C) Nominative
- D) Vocative

**Answer: C) Nominative** — marked by damma ـُ

---

**23.** مَاشَاءَ اللّٰه is an expression of:

- A) Asking for forgiveness
- B) Admiration at something good or beautiful
- C) Blessing food before eating
- D) Greeting someone

**Answer: B) Admiration at something good or beautiful**

---

**24.** The phrase الحَمْدُ لِلّٰه translates as:

- A) God is greatest
- B) In the name of God
- C) Praise be to God
- D) If God wills

**Answer: C) Praise be to God**

---

**25.** Which Form VIII verb below comes from the root ج-م-ع (gathering)?

- A) جَمَعَ
- B) تَجَمَّعَ
- C) اِجْتَمَعَ
- D) أَجْمَعَ

**Answer: C) اِجْتَمَعَ** (Form VIII — to gather together)

---

**26.** The plural of طَبِيب (doctor) follows the pattern فُعَلَاء and is:

- A) طُبَّاب
- B) أَطِبَّاء
- C) طَبِيبَات
- D) طُبَّيْب

**Answer: B) أَطِبَّاء**

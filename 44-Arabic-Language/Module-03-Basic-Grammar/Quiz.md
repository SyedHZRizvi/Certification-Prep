# Module 3 Quiz — القواعد الأساسية (Basic Grammar)

Test your knowledge of Arabic gender, the definite article, plurals, root system, and sentence structure.

---

**1.** Which of the following Arabic nouns is feminine because it ends in تاء مربوطة (ة)?

- A) رَجُل
- B) كِتَاب
- C) مَدْرَسَة
- D) بَاب

**Answer: C) مَدْرَسَة**

---

**2.** The Arabic word for "sun" (شَمْس) is grammatically:

- A) Masculine
- B) Feminine
- C) Neuter
- D) Depends on context

**Answer: B) Feminine** (a memorized exception — no taa marbuta but still feminine)

---

**3.** When الـ is added to شَمْس (sun), how is it pronounced?

- A) al-shams
- B) ash-shams
- C) al-ams
- D) as-hams

**Answer: B) ash-shams** (ش is a sun letter, so l assimilates)

---

**4.** Which letter is a MOON letter (قمرية), meaning الـ is pronounced fully as "al-"?

- A) ش
- B) ن
- C) ك
- D) ر

**Answer: C) ك**

---

**5.** How do you form the dual of كِتَاب (book)?

- A) كِتَابَات
- B) كُتُب
- C) كِتَابَانِ
- D) كِتَابَيْن only in the subject position

**Answer: C) كِتَابَانِ** (nominative dual)

---

**6.** The broken plural of كِتَاب (book) is:

- A) كِتَابَات
- B) كِتَابَانِ
- C) كُتُب
- D) كُتَّاب

**Answer: C) كُتُب**

---

**7.** What is the plural of وَلَد (boy)?

- A) وَلَدَات
- B) وَلَدَانِ
- C) أَوْلَاد
- D) وَلَدُون

**Answer: C) أَوْلَاد**

---

**8.** The Arabic personal pronoun for "she" is:

- A) هُوَ
- B) أَنْتِ
- C) هِيَ
- D) نَحْنُ

**Answer: C) هِيَ**

---

**9.** How do you say "You" (plural masculine) in Arabic?

- A) أَنْتَ
- B) أَنْتُم
- C) أَنْتِ
- D) هُم

**Answer: B) أَنْتُم**

---

**10.** In Arabic, the present tense of "to be" (is/are) is:

- A) يَكُون
- B) كَانَ
- C) Not used — the verb is omitted
- D) هو

**Answer: C) Not used — the verb is omitted in the present tense**

---

**11.** Which sentence is a correct Arabic nominal sentence (جملة اسمية)?

- A) ذَهَبَ الطَّالِبُ
- B) أَنَا طَالِبٌ
- C) يَكْتُبُ مُحَمَّدٌ
- D) أَكَلَتِ البِنْتُ

**Answer: B) أَنَا طَالِبٌ** (subject + predicate, no verb)

---

**12.** In a verbal sentence (جملة فعلية), the verb comes:

- A) At the end of the sentence
- B) In the middle
- C) First, before the subject
- D) After the object

**Answer: C) First, before the subject**

---

**13.** From the root ك-ت-ب, what does مَكْتَبَة mean?

- A) Writer
- B) Written
- C) Library / bookshop
- D) Office

**Answer: C) Library / bookshop**

---

**14.** All the following words come from the root ع-ل-م EXCEPT:

- A) عِلْم
- B) مُعَلِّم
- C) عَمَل
- D) تَعْلِيم

**Answer: C) عَمَل** (this comes from root ع-م-ل, meaning work/action)

---

**15.** What does مَكْتُوب mean (from root ك-ت-ب)?

- A) Book
- B) Writer
- C) Library
- D) Written / letter

**Answer: D) Written / letter**

---

**16.** The question word مَن (man) means:

- A) What
- B) Where
- C) Who
- D) When

**Answer: C) Who**

---

**17.** To ask "Why?" in Arabic, you say:

- A) أَيْن
- B) مَتَى
- C) كَيْف
- D) لِمَاذَا

**Answer: D) لِمَاذَا**

---

**18.** هَل at the start of an Arabic sentence indicates:

- A) A negative sentence
- B) A yes/no question
- C) A command
- D) Past tense

**Answer: B) A yes/no question**

---

**19.** The correct feminine form of the adjective كَبِير (big) is:

- A) كَبِيرٌ
- B) كُبَرَاء
- C) كَبِيرَة
- D) أَكْبَر

**Answer: C) كَبِيرَة**

---

**20.** Which sentence correctly uses the definite article with a sun letter?

- A) الشَّمْس = al-shams
- B) الشَّمْس = ash-shams
- C) الشَّمْس = a-shams
- D) الشَّمْس = al-a-shams

**Answer: B) الشَّمْس = ash-shams**

---

**21.** The plural of مَدِينَة (city) is:

- A) مَدِينَتَانِ
- B) مَدِينَات
- C) مُدُن
- D) مَدَائِن (also accepted)

**Answer: C) مُدُن** (broken plural — though مَدَائِن is also used in Classical Arabic)

---

**22.** Which root is related to the concept of "going/departure"?

- A) ك-ت-ب
- B) ع-ل-م
- C) ذ-ه-ب
- D) ق-ر-أ

**Answer: C) ذ-ه-ب**

---

**23.** In Arabic grammar, what is the subject of a nominal sentence called?

- A) فَاعِل
- B) مُبْتَدَأ
- C) خَبَر
- D) مَفْعُول

**Answer: B) مُبْتَدَأ**

---

**24.** Which of these correctly forms the sound feminine plural?

- A) طَالِبَة → طُلَّاب
- B) مُعَلِّمَة → مُعَلِّمَات
- C) مَدْرَسَة → مَدَارِس
- D) سَيَّارَة → سَيَّارَات (this is also acceptable)

**Answer: B) مُعَلِّمَة → مُعَلِّمَات** (sound feminine plural)

---

**25.** What is the dual form of طَالِبَة (female student)?

- A) طُلَّاب
- B) طَالِبَتَانِ
- C) طَالِبَات
- D) طَالِبَيْن

**Answer: B) طَالِبَتَانِ**

---

**26.** The Arabic word for "question" (سُؤَال) comes from the root:

- A) ع-ل-م
- B) ك-ت-ب
- C) س-أ-ل
- D) ف-ه-م

**Answer: C) س-أ-ل** (sāla = to ask → suʾāl = question)

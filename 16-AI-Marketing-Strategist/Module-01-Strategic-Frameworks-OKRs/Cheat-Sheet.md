## 🎯 The 5 Levels of Strategy

| Level | Horizon | Owner |
|-------|---------|-------|
| Corporate | 3–10 yr | CEO / Board |
| Business | 1–3 yr | GM |
| Marketing | 6–18 mo | CMO |
| Campaign | 1–6 mo | Director |
| Tactic | 1–8 wk | Manager |

🧠 Memorize: ask *at what level* before applying any framework.

## 📐 The 7 Canonical Frameworks

| Framework | Level | One-line summary |
|-----------|-------|------------------|
| STP | Marketing | Segmentation → Targeting → Positioning |
| 4Ps / 4Cs / 7Ps | Marketing | Product Price Place Promotion (+ People Process Physical for services) |
| Porter's Five Forces | Business | Entrants, Suppliers, Buyers, Substitutes, Rivalry |
| Ansoff Matrix | Corporate | Penetration / Market dev / Product dev / Diversification |
| BCG Matrix | Corporate | Stars / Cash cows / Question marks / Dogs |
| Jobs-to-be-Done | Marketing | When [situation] I want to [motivation] so I can [outcome] |
| Journey Map (modern) | Marketing | Actions × Thoughts/Feelings × Touchpoints × stages, per job persona |

## 🎯 JTBD Four Forces

```
Status quo   →  PUSH  ←  PULL  → New solution
                  ↘    ↘  ↗   ↗
                   ANXIETY  HABIT
                  (slow switching)
```
Marketing job: **amplify Push and Pull**, **reduce Anxiety and Habit**.

## 🎯 OKR Rules

| Concept | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Objective | Qualitative, time-bound, inspirational |
| Key Result | Quantitative outcome — answerable with a number |
| Initiative | Project/activity — NOT a KR |
| Count | 3–5 objectives × 3–5 KRs |
| Attainment | Target 0.6–0.8 (1.0 = sandbagged) |
| Visibility | Public across teams |

**KR vs Initiative trap (memorize):**
- "Demo requests = 420" → Key Result ✅
- "Ship 12 Wrapped variants by Nov 15" → Initiative ❌ (it's an activity)

---

## 🌟 North Star Metric — 3 Criteria

1. Measures *customer value* (not company outcome).
2. *Leading indicator* of long-term revenue.
3. *Bounded* — resistant to gaming.

Real examples:

- Spotify → Time spent listening per active user
- Airbnb → Nights booked
- Facebook (2007) → Friend-7-days
- Slack → Teams sending 2,000+ messages
- Dropbox → Files saved across 3+ devices

## 🏆 Exam Power Phrases

When you see these in answers, they're **often correct**:

- ✅ "Behavioral / needs-based segmentation outperforms demographic..."
- ✅ "Key Result is an outcome, not an activity..."
- ✅ "Strategy is choosing what NOT to do..."
- ✅ "The North Star is upstream of revenue..."
- ✅ "Map the journey per job persona, not for an average user..."

When you see these, they're **often wrong**:

- ❌ "100% OKR attainment is the goal..."
- ❌ "Revenue is the North Star..."
- ❌ "The 4Cs replaces the 4Ps..."
- ❌ "Initiatives are key results..."
- ❌ "Use Porter's Five Forces for a campaign brief..."

---

## ⚠️ Anti-Patterns To Recognize

- ❌ **27 KPIs, zero strategy** — no apex, no ownership tree
- ❌ **Sandbagged OKRs** — consistent 100% attainment
- ❌ **Initiative-as-KR** — listing activities under Key Results
- ❌ **Demographics-only segmentation** when behavioral is available
- ❌ **Hoarding Dogs** — never killing under-performing programs
- ❌ **Average-user journey map** — should be per job persona
- ❌ **Tagline mistaken for positioning statement**

## 🗓️ Key Facts To Memorize

- **STP**: Wendell Smith (1956), Kotler operationalized
- **4Ps**: McCarthy (1960); **4Cs**: Lauterborn (1990); **7Ps**: Booms & Bitner (1981)
- **Porter's Five Forces**: 1979
- **Ansoff Matrix**: 1957
- **BCG Matrix**: 1970
- **JTBD**: Christensen (2003) — milkshake study
- **OKRs**: Andy Grove at Intel (1970s) → John Doerr at Google (1999)

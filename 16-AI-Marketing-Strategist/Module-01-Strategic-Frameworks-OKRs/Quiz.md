## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning**
Wendell Smith (1956), operationalized by Philip Kotler. Every other framework either feeds into or depends on STP outputs.

### Q2: **D. Inexpensive**
The five ISADA criteria are **Identifiable, Substantial, Accessible, Differentiable, Actionable**. Cost is not part of the validity test for a segment.

### Q3: **B. JTBD, STP, OKR design**
Spotify's strategy team identified the JTBD (relive a year that mattered), used STP to target the 18–34 social-sharing segment, and built an OKR around "≥35% of MAU opening Wrapped within 7 days."

### Q4: **D. Communication**
The 4Cs are Customer needs (Product), Cost (Price), Convenience (Place), and **Communication** (Promotion).

### Q5: **C. Porter's Five Forces**
Michael Porter (1979). The five forces are New entrants, Supplier power, Buyer power, Substitutes, and Industry rivalry.

### Q6: **C. Product development**
Existing market + new product = Product development. (New market + existing product = Market development; both new = Diversification.)

### Q7: **C. ~70%**
Rumelt (1974), replicated by McKinsey (2018). Diversification is the riskiest Ansoff quadrant precisely because of this.

### Q8: **B. Cash cow**
Cash cows generate the funds that pay for investing in Stars and selectively backing Question Marks. The discipline is *milking* them, not over-investing.

### Q9: **C. Divesting the Dogs**
The Dogs quadrant is structurally unattractive, but org politics typically prevents teams from sun-setting their work. The discipline of BCG is the kill list, not the investment list.

### Q10: **B. Before 8:30 AM**
The morning-commute pattern revealed the real "job", entertainment, hand-occupation, and hunger-suppression for a long drive.

### Q11: **C. Push, Pull, Anxiety, Habit**
Push = frustration with status quo. Pull = attraction to new. Anxiety = fear of switching. Habit = inertia. The marketing job is to amplify Push and Pull while reducing Anxiety and Habit.

### Q12: **A. "Knowledge-work teams"**
"Knowledge-work teams who are drowning in email." Specific enough to be actionable; broad enough to fund a public company.

### Q13: **C. An internal strategy artifact**
A tagline is the *external* expression that *may* derive from positioning. Positioning is the *internal* contract on FoR, category, benefit, and PoD.

### Q14: **B. The targets were sandbagged**
Per Doerr's *Measure What Matters*: aggressive OKRs should produce 0.6–0.8 attainment. Consistent 1.0 attainment is a *failure mode*.

### Q15: **B. 50–70% (or 60–80%)**
Per Doerr and Google's published guidance. Tying OKR scores to compensation almost guarantees sandbagging, which is why mature OKR orgs decouple them.

### Q16: **C. An Initiative**
Initiatives are projects/activities. Key Results are *outcomes/metrics*. The trap that ruins 70% of OKR rollouts is listing initiatives where key results belong.

### Q17: **C. A measure of customer value that is a leading indicator of revenue**
Revenue is the *lagging* outcome. The North Star sits one step upstream: the customer-value driver that, when up, predicts revenue up.

### Q18: **A. Spotify, "Time spent listening per active user"**
Other publicly disclosed examples: Airbnb (nights booked), early Facebook (friend-7-days), Slack (teams with 2k+ messages), Dropbox (files saved across 3+ devices).

### Q19: **B. Hierarchical with single apex**
A scorecard is a flat list of disconnected metrics. A KPI tree has a North Star, multiplicative/additive decomposition, and a team owner at every node.

### Q20: **C. ~80%**
Behavioral and needs-based segmentation outperforms demographic in ~80% of CRO experiments (CXL Institute, 2021 meta-analysis).

### Q21: **B. Modern journey maps track Actions, T&F, Touchpoints across multiple stages and per job persona**
The 1898 AIDA funnel and the 5-step funnel are 19th- and 20th-century artifacts. Modern maps are multi-dimensional and per-persona.

### Q22: **B. Too many objectives**
Doerr's guidance: 3–5 objectives per team, 3–5 KRs per objective. More than ~20 KRs total = to-do list, not strategy.

### Q23: **B. Premium Product + premium Price, with minimal Place and zero Promotion**
Tesla famously had no dealer network (minimal Place) and almost zero traditional advertising for the first decade (zero Promotion). Strategic *under-investment* in two Ps freed budget to win on the other two.

### Q24: **C. Low share, high growth**
Question marks are growth-market entries where you have not yet won share, the decision is *invest aggressively or divest*.

### Q25: **B. Net Revenue Retention (NRR)**
NRR = (starting ARR + expansion − churn − contraction) / starting ARR. Best-in-class B2B SaaS shoots for >120%.

### Q26: **A. Tangible cues that signal quality**
Apple's retail stores, the Tesla delivery center, Ritz-Carlton's marble lobby, these are Physical Evidence in Booms & Bitner's 7Ps.

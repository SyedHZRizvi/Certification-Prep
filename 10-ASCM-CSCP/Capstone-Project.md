# Capstone Project — ASCM CSCP Track

> **Purpose:** Integrate all 10 modules into a single executive-grade deliverable. Most CSCP students never face a real Chief Supply Chain Officer (CSCO) decision until they're in the chair. This capstone is your dress rehearsal.

---

## Brief

You are the newly hired **Chief Supply Chain Officer** at **Northwind Provisions**, a $2.4B consumer-packaged-goods (CPG) firm headquartered in Chicago. Northwind sells ~3,200 SKUs of food, beverages, and personal-care products to retailers (Walmart, Kroger, Costco, Target — ~60% of revenue), foodservice (Sysco, US Foods — 25%), and direct-to-consumer e-commerce (15%). The company:

- Sources from 1,200+ suppliers across 38 countries
- Operates 9 manufacturing plants (6 US, 2 Mexico, 1 Vietnam) and 14 distribution centers
- Employs 11,400 people in supply chain functions
- Carries ~$420M average inventory (DIO ~85 days), cash-to-cash ~92 days
- Lost ~$180M in 2023-2024 from a triple blow: post-pandemic inventory overhang, Red Sea / Houthi disruption on EU-bound air-freight (Vietnamese personal-care), and an EU CSRD non-compliance penalty (€8M)

The CEO and Board mandate: **lead an 18-month end-to-end supply chain transformation** to restore competitive position, prepare for the 2025-2027 macro environment (tariff regimes, climate volatility, AI-augmented operations), and pass an external "CSCO Readiness Audit" by ASCM-aligned consultants in month 18.

You have:
- A direct mandate from the CEO and a $52M transformation budget (≈ 2.2% of revenue)
- 8 direct reports inherited (VPs of Planning, Sourcing, Manufacturing, Logistics, Quality, Sustainability, IT-SCM, Customer Service)
- 18 months and a Board-level scorecard reviewed quarterly

## Deliverables (7 artifacts)

### 1. End-to-end SCM strategy mapped to APICS SCOR (Plan / Source / Make / Deliver / Return / Enable)
A 6-section document — one section per SCOR process — articulating the target operating model, KPI targets, and 18-month roadmap per process. Each section ≥ 1 page; 6-10 pages total.

**Requirements:**
- For each SCOR process: list 3-5 current-state pain points, target-state vision, and 2-3 measurable KPIs (e.g., Perfect Order ≥ 95%, OTIF ≥ 96%, DIO ≤ 60 days).
- Explicit alignment with Hau Lee's strategy framework — which of Lee's 4 quadrants is Northwind in *today*, and which target quadrant for 2027? Defend the choice.
- Address SCOR-Enable explicitly: governance, IT, talent, risk management.

### 2. Supplier segmentation per Kraljic (4 quadrants)
A categorization of Northwind's 1,200 suppliers into Strategic / Leverage / Bottleneck / Routine quadrants, with sourcing strategy per quadrant.

**Requirements:**
- Sample categorization of 30 named or fictitious suppliers (10 strategic, 10 leverage, 5 bottleneck, 5 routine) with justification.
- For each quadrant: contract type (FP / cost-plus / MSA), QBR cadence, scorecard KPIs, payment terms.
- Address the post-2024 ESG-and-forced-labor compliance overlay — which suppliers move quadrants when CSDDD / UFLPA is factored in?
- Identify 5 "strategic" suppliers where a 2026 deepening (joint R&D, equity, multi-year MSA) is warranted.

### 3. S&OP / IBP redesign
A revised monthly planning cadence applying Wallace/Stahl + Oliver Wight IBP principles.

**Requirements:**
- 5-step cycle calendar with named meeting owners (Data, Demand, Supply, Pre-S&OP, Executive).
- Granularity: family-level aggregate plan over 18 months; reconciliation to SKU-level MPS for the rolling 6 months.
- Identify integration points with finance (volume × price → revenue plan; cost-of-goods plan), strategic (NPI funnel for 2026-2027 launches), and sustainability (scope-3 forecast).
- Plan for AI-augmented demand sensing (e.g., generative-AI summaries of POS + Weibo/X + weather data) and how human judgment fits in.

### 4. Sustainability scorecard per EU CSRD
Northwind exports ~$340M to the EU and is required to comply with CSRD from FY2025 (calendar year 2025) reporting.

**Requirements:**
- Scope 1, 2, 3 emissions baseline (with assumed numbers — show your work).
- SBTi-aligned reduction targets for 2030 and 2050 (1.5°C path).
- ESG scorecard with 8-12 KPIs aligned to GRI + SASB + TCFD frameworks.
- Compliance roadmap: when each CSRD disclosure category goes from "voluntary" to "filed and audited" within 18 months.
- Address modern slavery: UFLPA + CSDDD + UK Modern Slavery Act — multi-tier mapping plan for Tier-2 and Tier-3 supplier visibility.

### 5. Risk register with mitigations
An ISO 31000-aligned risk register covering supply chain risks with treatment per the 4 T's (Tolerate / Treat / Transfer / Terminate).

**Requirements:**
- 15-20 named risks across categories (demand, supply, operational, financial, geopolitical, climate, cyber, reputational, compliance, pandemic).
- Risk heatmap (likelihood × impact) with each risk placed.
- FMEA-style severity × occurrence × detection scoring for the top 8 risks; RPN ranking.
- For each top-5 risk: BCP / DRP plan (RTO + RPO if applicable), risk-owner, treatment strategy.
- Explicitly address Red Sea / Houthi pattern (Q3/Q4 2023-2024) and how Northwind's Vietnamese supplier strategy would respond.

### 6. Capability roadmap
A 6-quarter (18-month) Gantt-style roadmap of capability investments — talent, technology, process, governance.

**Requirements:**
- 25-40 work-streams sequenced across 6 quarters.
- Budget allocation across the $52M total (with at least 5 work-streams each ≥ $1M).
- Capability dimensions: people (training, certifications — CSCP/CPIM/CLTD/CPSM for staff), technology (e.g., SAP IBP or Kinaxis or o9 selection rationale), process (S&OP redesign, supplier development), governance (CSCO operating model).
- Quick-win identification: 3-5 work-streams that can show ROI within 6 months.

### 7. Board metrics dashboard (executive scorecard)
A single-page balanced-scorecard-style dashboard the Board will review quarterly.

**Requirements:**
- 4 perspectives (Kaplan & Norton 1992): Financial, Customer, Internal Process, Learning & Growth.
- 3-5 KPIs per perspective (total 12-20 KPIs).
- Target values + measurement cadence + owner per KPI.
- Connect each KPI back to a specific SCOR process and a specific 18-month deliverable.
- Include sustainability + risk KPIs (this is where many BSCs fail post-2024).

## Rubric (scored out of 100)

| Criterion | Points | Excellent (90-100%) | Acceptable (70-89%) | Below bar (<70%) |
|-----------|-------:|---------------------|---------------------|------------------|
| **SCOR alignment & strategy coherence** | 20 | All 6 SCOR processes addressed with explicit Hau Lee/Fisher reasoning, measurable KPIs, and reasoning that would survive a real Board challenge. | Most processes addressed; some KPI gaps; framework alignment is implicit not explicit. | Missing SCOR processes; generic "best practice" language; no measurable KPIs. |
| **Kraljic-driven supplier strategy** | 15 | All 4 quadrants populated with named examples; differentiated contract/SRM strategy per quadrant; ESG/forced-labor overlay applied. | Most quadrants populated; some quadrant-strategy mismatches; ESG overlay partial. | Suppliers grouped without Kraljic logic; one-size-fits-all sourcing. |
| **S&OP / IBP redesign** | 12 | Full Wallace/Stahl 5-step cycle with named owners; IBP integration with finance, NPI, sustainability; AI augmentation thoughtfully addressed. | Cycle present but some integration gaps; AI either over- or under-weighted. | S&OP confused with budget review; cycle steps mis-ordered or missing. |
| **Sustainability + CSRD compliance** | 13 | Full scope 1/2/3 baseline + SBTi-aligned targets + GRI/SASB/TCFD scorecard + CSDDD/UFLPA multi-tier plan; defensible to an external auditor. | Scope 3 underweighted; CSRD compliance partial; multi-tier plan handwaved. | "We will comply" with no specifics; no scope 1/2/3 breakdown; no multi-tier supplier mapping. |
| **Risk register + BCP** | 12 | 15-20 risks across all 10 categories; heatmap + FMEA + RPN; top-risks have full BCP with RTO/RPO. | Most categories covered; FMEA partial or absent; BCP at high level only. | <10 risks; no heatmap; no treatment strategies; missing climate or cyber categories. |
| **Capability roadmap budget defense** | 13 | All work-streams sequenced and costed; budget defensible against CFO challenge; quick-wins identified and credible. | Roadmap present but some sequencing problems; budget allocations directional; quick-wins thin. | Roadmap unsequenced; budget arbitrary; no quick-wins. |
| **Board dashboard / Balanced Scorecard** | 10 | 4 perspectives populated; KPIs measurable and tied to SCOR + 18-month plan; sustainability + risk KPIs included alongside financial. | All 4 perspectives present; some KPIs vague; sustainability/risk underweighted. | Missing BSC perspectives; KPI list without targets or owners. |
| **Written justification quality (the "Harvard ask")** | 5 | Reasoning is defensible to an MBA-level audience; cites Hau Lee, Fisher, Kraljic, Goldratt, Elkington, Kaplan-Norton by name and year. | Most citations present; reasoning sound but occasionally generic. | "Best practice" language without specific framework attribution. |

**Total: 100 points.** Target: ≥80 for a passable capstone; ≥90 for portfolio-grade. Below 70 means revisit before submitting.

## Suggested timeline (8 weeks part-time)

- **Week 1: Diagnostic + situation assessment.** Re-read Module 1 + 2 + 9. Draft current-state SCOR assessment, place Northwind in the Hau Lee + Fisher matrices, and write the executive summary (≤ 1 page).
- **Week 2: Sourcing strategy.** Build the Kraljic matrix with 30 named suppliers (real or fictitious). Identify 5 strategic-alliance candidates with rationale.
- **Week 3: Planning redesign.** Write the 5-step S&OP / IBP cycle. Draft 18-month aggregate plan template. Define MPS-MRP integration.
- **Week 4: Sustainability & compliance.** Build scope 1/2/3 baseline (assumed); design CSRD-aligned scorecard. Multi-tier supplier mapping plan.
- **Week 5: Risk register & BCP.** Identify 15-20 risks. Build heatmap + FMEA on top 8. Draft BCP/RTO/RPO for top 5.
- **Week 6: Capability roadmap.** Sequence 25-40 work-streams across 6 quarters; allocate $52M; identify 3-5 quick-wins.
- **Week 7: Board dashboard.** Build the 4-perspective BSC with 12-20 KPIs. Connect to SCOR + 18-month plan.
- **Week 8: Integration + presentation.** Stitch all 7 deliverables into a coherent narrative. Self-grade against the rubric. Iterate on weakest section.

For a 4-week intensive: combine weeks 2-3, 4-5, 6-7 into single weeks; accept lower polish but higher iteration speed.

## What "submission" looks like

A single **PDF deliverable, 25-40 pages**, structured as:
1. Executive Summary (1 page) — the elevator-pitch version of your transformation.
2. SCOR-mapped strategy (6-10 pages).
3. Kraljic supplier segmentation (3-5 pages).
4. S&OP / IBP redesign (3-5 pages).
5. Sustainability + CSRD scorecard (3-5 pages).
6. Risk register + BCP (3-5 pages).
7. Capability roadmap (Gantt or table, 2-4 pages).
8. Board metrics dashboard (1 page — must fit on one page).
9. Appendix: assumptions, framework citations, glossary.

You are doing this for yourself. There is no instructor. The grading rubric is your own honest review against the 100-point scale. If you score yourself <70 on any section, that's your weakness signal — re-read the relevant module and iterate.

**Peer-review prompt** (if a study buddy is available): Each peer takes the role of a different Board member — CEO (challenges strategy clarity), CFO (challenges budget defense), CLO (challenges legal/CSRD compliance), CHRO (challenges talent plan). Run a 30-minute defense session per peer.

## Optional stretch goals

- **Build a digital twin sketch** of Northwind's network using free tools (e.g., a Python notebook with simpy or AnyLogic free edition).
- **Calculate the actual financial impact** of your top-5 risks via Monte Carlo simulation (Excel + a simple add-in is enough).
- **Draft a 30-60-90-day "first 100 days" memo** to the CEO — the artifact you'd actually produce in week one if hired.
- **Compare Northwind's transformation to a real 2020-2024 case** — e.g., Unilever's Compass strategy update, Procter & Gamble's productivity program, or Mars's net-zero supply chain. Where would you steal from each?
- **Build a one-page Anki deck** of the 50 framework citations in this capstone (Porter 1980, Hau Lee 2004, Fisher 1997, Kraljic 1983, Goldratt 1984, Elkington 1994, Carroll 1991, Kaplan-Norton 1992, Wallace-Stahl 1999, Liker 2004 …) so they're exam-ready.

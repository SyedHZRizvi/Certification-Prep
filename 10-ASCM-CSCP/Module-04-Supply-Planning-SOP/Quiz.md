## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **D. Executive S&OP meeting**
Steps: Data → Demand → Supply → Pre-S&OP → Executive S&OP. Executive is the climax.

### Q2: **B. Family-level aggregate plan, 12–24 months**
S&OP outputs the aggregate plan at family level. SKU detail lives in the MPS.

### Q3: **B. Level**
Level keeps workforce stable, absorbs variability with inventory.

### Q4: **B. End-item / SKU**
MPS is SKU-level. Family is S&OP. Component is MRP.

### Q5: **A. MPS, BOM, inventory records**
Memorize this — appears verbatim on the exam.

### Q6: **B. Only firm orders inside**
DTF protects near-term execution from forecast noise.

### Q7: **B. RCCP at MPS / key work centers; CRP at MRP / all work centers**
RCCP is rough (key resources). CRP is detailed (every work center).

### Q8: **C. Lot-for-lot**
L4L orders exactly the net requirement, minimizing inventory.

### Q9: **B. Financial, strategic, NPI integration**
IBP = S&OP + finance + strategy + new product introduction.

### Q10: **B. Capacity and material availability**
CTP layers operational feasibility on top of ATP.

### Q11: **B. Hybrid biased toward chase**
Services can't inventory output, so chase strategies dominate.

### Q12: **A. Move earlier**
Move-in means the order needs to be earlier than currently scheduled.

### Q13: **B. Master scheduler intervention**
Inside DTF, automatic MRP changes are blocked; the scheduler decides.

### Q14: **B. Strategic → S&OP → MPS → MRP → PAC**
Classic hierarchical sequence.

### Q15: **B. Aligns forecasts; dampens bullwhip**
CPFR feeds a more accurate, shared forecast into S&OP.

### Q16: **D. Issuing dividends**
Dividends are a finance lever, not aggregate planning.

### Q17: **B. Reconcile demand & supply**
Pre-S&OP teases out decisions and trade-offs before the executive meeting.

### Q18: **B. On-hand + MPS receipts − committed orders**
Standard ATP definition.

### Q19: **A. 28 days before week 4, quantity 400**
2 wheels × 200 bicycles = 400. Lead-time-offset → release 28 days earlier.

### Q20: **C. Liquid — system replans freely**
Outside PTF, MRP can adjust orders without manual intervention.

### Q21: **B. One committed plan**
Alignment is the headline deliverable, not the MPS itself.

### Q22: **B. Theory of Constraints**
Goldratt's TOC schedules around the constraint (drum).

### Q23: **C. Lot-for-lot**
High carry cost rewards small frequent runs; L4L fits.

### Q24: **C. Resource Requirements Planning (RRP)**
RRP is strategic capacity. RCCP is at S&OP/MPS. CRP is at MRP.

### Q25: **C. 400**
ATP at horizon end = on-hand 200 + receipts 500 − orders 300 = 400.

### Q26: **B. Lack of executive commitment**
Industry research consistently identifies leadership discipline as the #1 failure cause.

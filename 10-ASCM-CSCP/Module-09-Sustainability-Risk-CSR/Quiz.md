## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. People, planet, profit**
Elkington 1994.

### Q2: **B. Scope 2**
Purchased energy = scope 2.

### Q3: **C. Scope 3**
Value-chain emissions, both upstream and downstream.

### Q4: **C. Refuse**
Refuse (don't make at all) ranks highest in the 9 R hierarchy.

### Q5: **B. Environmental management systems**
ISO 14001 = EMS.

### Q6: **D. ISO 31000**
The risk management standard.

### Q7: **D. Tabulate**
The 4 T's are Tolerate, Treat, Transfer, Terminate.

### Q8: **B. S × O × D**
RPN multiplies the three scores.

### Q9: **B. Target time to restore**
RTO is about *time*.

### Q10: **B. Maximum acceptable data loss**
RPO is about *data*.

### Q11: **D. Economic**
Carroll: Economic → Legal → Ethical → Philanthropic.

### Q12: **B. MSCI, Sustainalytics, ISS, S&P Global**
Big-four ESG rating agencies.

### Q13: **B. Reputational + compliance**
Forced-labor exposure hits both dimensions hard.

### Q14: **B. Resilient**
Resilience = absorb AND adapt.

### Q15: **B. Forced-labor regulations**
UFLPA, CSDDD, Modern Slavery Acts force multi-tier visibility.

### Q16: **D. Transfer**
Insurance transfers risk to the insurer.

### Q17: **B. Net-zero targets aligned with climate science**
SBTi validates whether targets are science-aligned.

### Q18: **B. Climate-related financial risk disclosure**
TCFD targets investors' need for climate financial transparency.

### Q19: **C. 2015**
17 SDGs adopted by UN.

### Q20: **B. Likelihood vs impact**
Classic 2-axis heatmap.

### Q21: **B. BCP / DRP testing**
Tabletop exercises validate continuity plans.

### Q22: **B. Human rights, labor, environment, anti-corruption**
UN Global Compact's 4 thematic areas, 10 principles.

### Q23: **D. Philanthropic**
Top = discretionary contributions.

### Q24: **B. Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover**
NIST CSF v1.1 / v2.0 core.

### Q25: **C. High**
High impact + moderate likelihood = high-risk cell.

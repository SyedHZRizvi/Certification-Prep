## 🚚 The Three Flows in Every Supply Chain

```
   Suppliers ──Material──► Manufacturers ──Material──► Distributors ──Material──► Customers
              ◄─Information─┘            ◄─Information─┘             ◄─Information─┘
              ◄─Cash────────┘            ◄─Cash────────┘             ◄─Cash────────┘
```
Memorize: **Material · Information · Cash**

---

## 🧩 SCOR 13.0 — Six Processes

```
              PLAN
   SOURCE → MAKE → DELIVER → RETURN
              ENABLE
```

| # | Process | Mnemonic |
|---|---------|----------|
| 1 | **P**lan | "Please" |
| 2 | **S**ource | "Send" |
| 3 | **M**ake | "Money" |
| 4 | **D**eliver | "Daily" |
| 5 | **R**eturn | "Returns" |
| 6 | **E**nable | "Expected" |

**4 SCOR levels:** Process types → categories → activities → company-specific implementation

## 🐂 5 Causes of the Bullwhip Effect

1. Demand forecast updating
2. Order batching
3. Price fluctuations
4. Rationing / shortage gaming
5. Long lead times

**Fix:** share POS data (VMI, CPFR), smaller batches, EDLP pricing, shorter lead times.

## ⚠️ Anti-Patterns To Recognize

- ❌ Matching an **innovative** product with an **efficient** SC (Fisher violation)
- ❌ Treating "logistics" and "supply chain" as synonyms
- ❌ Solving bullwhip by adding safety stock at every tier (makes it worse)
- ❌ "Best practice everywhere" — strategy must be tailored
- ❌ Ignoring **Enable** in SCOR — version 13.0 has 6 processes, not 5

## 🏆 Exam Power Phrases

When you see these in answers, they're often correct:

- ✅ "Align with corporate strategy..."
- ✅ "Share information across tiers..."
- ✅ "Move the decoupling point downstream..."
- ✅ "Postpone differentiation until..."
- ✅ "Tailor the SC to the product type..."

When you see these, they're often **wrong**:

- ❌ "Always minimize cost..."
- ❌ "Stick with the original sourcing plan..."
- ❌ "Increase safety stock at every tier..."
- ❌ "Logistics is supply chain..."
- ❌ "Skip the Enable process..."

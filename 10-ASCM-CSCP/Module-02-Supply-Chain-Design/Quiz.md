## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. 30%**
Square-root law: safety stock scales with √N. Cutting from 10 to 5 → √(5/10) ≈ 0.707, a ~29-30% reduction.

### Q2: **B. Center-of-gravity method**
Single-facility, demand-weighted minimization is the textbook CoG use case.

### Q3: **B. Backward integration**
Acquiring an upstream supplier = backward / upstream integration.

### Q4: **B. US company hires US contractor**
Outsourcing without offshoring — the activity moves to an external firm but stays domestic.

### Q5: **D. Straight-line distances, single facility, uniform rate**
CoG's three classic assumptions. Violations push you to LP or simulation.

### Q6: **B. Total stoppage if the supplier disrupts**
Concentration risk is the headline danger of single sourcing.

### Q7: **B. Make rationale**
Core competency + IP protection → make in-house.

### Q8: **B. Same-day flow-through**
Crossdocking is short-cycle flow-through, often used for high-velocity SKUs.

### Q9: **C. Nearshoring**
China → Mexico for the US market = move closer (not back to the home country, which would be reshoring).

### Q10: **C. Outbound transport falls but total cost rises**
Past the optimum, inventory + facility cost growth outpaces transport savings.

### Q11: **A. Single is chosen from many; sole is the only one available**
Critical distinction. Sole-source carries the highest risk because there is no alternative.

### Q12: **D. Strategic item**
High profit impact + high supply risk = Strategic (Kraljic upper-right). Calls for deep partnerships.

### Q13: **C. Many small, decentralized micro-fulfillment centers**
1-hour delivery in dense urban areas demands proximity, not pooling. Modern urban grocery and pharmacy chains do this.

### Q14: **B. Services cannot be inventoried**
Hospital beds, hotel rooms, and consulting hours are perishable capacity, not stored inventory.

### Q15: **B. Multiple qualitative criteria**
Factor-rating handles weighted multi-criteria scoring — useful when not everything has a dollar value.

### Q16: **B. Strategic / core competency**
IP protection + supply security are textbook "make" drivers.

### Q17: **B. Supply security and input cost predictability**
Owning the supplier removes price volatility and disruption risk from the input.

### Q18: **C. Cross-docking**
Receive + immediately ship = crossdock.

### Q19: **A. Milk run**
A milk run visits multiple stops in one trip. LTL is just a partial-load shipping mode without the loop concept.

### Q20: **C. 60,000 units**
Break-even: 1,000,000 + 20x = 400,000 + 30x → 600,000 = 10x → x = 60,000 units.

### Q21: **A. Outsourcing + offshoring**
Contract = outsourcing. Taiwan = offshoring. Both at once.

### Q22: **A. Inventory pooling savings vs longer shipping distance**
Centralization gains pooling but loses proximity.

### Q23: **C. A-class accounts**
A's drive ~80% of revenue and deserve premium attention.

### Q24: **B. Non-core, external provider has scale**
Outsource what isn't core when others can do it better/cheaper.

### Q25: **A. Inventory rises but transport falls as facilities increase**
Two opposing curves create the U-shape; the optimum sits at the minimum sum.

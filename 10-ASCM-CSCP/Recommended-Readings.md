# Recommended Readings, ASCM CSCP Track

> **Purpose:** Each course module has a "Further Reading" section with module-specific items. This file is the **whole-course canonical reading list**, the books, papers, and resources that have shaped supply-chain thinking and are still load-bearing in 2024-2026. Engage with these as your time and budget allow; you do NOT need all of them to pass the CSCP, but the depth they provide is what separates a passing student from a future CSCO.

---

## 📚 Canonical textbooks (read 2-3, skim the rest)

### 1. ASCM CSCP Learning System (latest edition)
- **Publisher:** Association for Supply Chain Management (ASCM, formerly APICS), Chicago. Annual revisions.
- **Why it's worth your time:** The official ASCM-aligned prep. Vocabulary mirrors the exam *verbatim*. If you only buy one book for the exam itself, this is it.
- **When to engage:** From week 1 of your CSCP study program. Read each module twice.

### 2. Christopher, Martin. *Logistics & Supply Chain Management.*
- **Publisher:** FT Publishing International, 5th edition 2016. Cranfield School of Management.
- **ISBN:** 978-1292083797
- **Why it's worth your time:** The UK / European companion to Chopra & Meindl. Excellent on agility, supply chain risk, and the "ZARA-style" responsive chains. Christopher coined "supply chain management" in academic discourse in the 1990s.
- **When to engage:** Concurrent with Modules 1-2. Best read after the ASCM Learning System provides scaffolding.

### 3. Chopra, Sunil & Meindl, Peter. *Supply Chain Management: Strategy, Planning, and Operation.*
- **Publisher:** Pearson, 7th edition 2019. Kellogg School of Management.
- **ISBN:** 978-0134731889
- **Why it's worth your time:** The US graduate-school textbook. Strong quantitative depth (transportation LP, inventory math, network design) where the CSCP often stays conceptual. Chapter 11 (sourcing) and Chapter 14 (information tech) are gold.
- **When to engage:** As reference for Modules 2, 5, and 7, especially for capstone calculation practice.

### 4. Goldratt, Eliyahu M. & Cox, Jeff. *The Goal: A Process of Ongoing Improvement.*
- **Publisher:** North River Press, 4th edition 2014 (original 1984).
- **ISBN:** 978-0884271956
- **Why it's worth your time:** The Theory of Constraints in novel form. The drum-buffer-rope, throughput accounting, and 5 focusing steps are easier to grasp here than in any academic treatment. Goldratt was an Israeli physicist; the book is unironically a page-turner.
- **When to engage:** Before tackling Module 10. Read once cover-to-cover, then keep on shelf.

### 5. Liker, Jeffrey K. *The Toyota Way: 14 Management Principles from the World's Greatest Manufacturer.*
- **Publisher:** McGraw-Hill, 2nd edition 2020 (original 2004). University of Michigan.
- **ISBN:** 978-1260468519
- **Why it's worth your time:** The Western academic synthesis of the Toyota Production System (Ohno). Liker spent 20+ years inside Toyota plants. The 14 principles are the deep substrate of every Lean program.
- **When to engage:** Before Module 5 and Module 10. Reread sections after the 2021-2022 Toyota chip-resilience case study.

### 6. Johnson, P. Fraser & Leenders, Michiel R. *Purchasing and Supply Management.*
- **Publisher:** McGraw-Hill, 16th edition 2020. Ivey School / Western University.
- **ISBN:** 978-1259957864
- **Why it's worth your time:** The procurement / sourcing textbook. Goes 4× deeper on Kraljic, Dickson, TCO, supplier development, and category management than any CSCP module can.
- **When to engage:** Before/during Module 6. Essential if your career path is procurement/CPSM-bound.

### 7. Kim, Gene; Behr, Kevin; Spafford, George. *The Phoenix Project: A Novel About IT, DevOps, and Helping Your Business Win.*
- **Publisher:** IT Revolution Press, 5th anniversary ed. 2018.
- **ISBN:** 978-1942788294
- **Why it's worth your time:** A modern *Goal*-style novel that translates TOC and Lean into IT operations and DevOps. The crossover with supply chain Module 10 (TOC + Lean + continuous improvement) is profound. Bonus: the sequel *The Unicorn Project* (2019) covers data flow at a deeper level.
- **When to engage:** Alongside / after *The Goal*. Both can be read in a long weekend.

## 📰 Seminal papers / HBR articles / McKinsey reports

### 8. Kraljic, Peter. *"Purchasing Must Become Supply Management."*
- **Venue:** Harvard Business Review, September-October 1983.
- **Why it matters:** The original Kraljic matrix. ~40 years old and still the most-cited sourcing-strategy framework.
- **Where to find:** HBR archive (paywall) or via JSTOR. Many free reproductions exist (e.g., on academia.edu).

### 9. Fisher, Marshall L. *"What Is the Right Supply Chain for Your Product?"*
- **Venue:** Harvard Business Review, March-April 1997. Wharton School.
- **Why it matters:** The functional-vs-innovative product framework. Tested on every CSCP. Foundational.
- **Where to find:** HBR archive; many MBA programs reproduce it openly for class use.

### 10. Lee, Hau L. *"The Triple-A Supply Chain."*
- **Venue:** Harvard Business Review, October 2004. Stanford GSB.
- **Why it matters:** The popular synthesis of Lee's 2002 California Management Review piece. Defines Agility, Adaptability, Alignment as the 3 A's. Cited in essentially every Hau Lee question.
- **Where to find:** HBR archive.

### 11. Porter, Michael E. *Competitive Strategy: Techniques for Analyzing Industries and Competitors.*
- **Publisher:** Free Press / Harvard Business School Press, 1980. (Companion 1985 *Competitive Advantage* introduced the value chain.)
- **Why it matters:** The five forces + the value chain, the strategic frame inside which every supply chain operates. Required reading at every MBA program globally.
- **When to engage:** As reference for the strategy modules (1, 9). Companion to ASCM CSCP Learning System.

### 12. Hayes, Robert H. & Wheelwright, Steven C. *Restoring Our Competitive Edge: Competing Through Manufacturing.*
- **Publisher:** John Wiley & Sons, 1984. Harvard Business School.
- **ISBN:** 978-0471051152
- **Why it matters:** Defined make-vs-buy and the "product-process matrix", the foundation of how modern manufacturing strategy is taught. Hayes-Wheelwright is in every operations strategy textbook.

### 13. Elkington, John. *Cannibals with Forks: The Triple Bottom Line of 21st Century Business.*
- **Publisher:** Capstone Publishing, 1997 (original CMR article 1994).
- **ISBN:** 978-1900961271
- **Why it matters:** The People-Planet-Profit synthesis. Foundation of every modern sustainability framework. Elkington publicly issued a "product recall" in 2018 because TBL had been reduced to greenwashing accounting, that critique is worth reading too.

### 14. Carroll, Archie B. *"The Pyramid of Corporate Social Responsibility."*
- **Venue:** Business Horizons 34(4), July-August 1991. University of Georgia Terry College of Business.
- **Why it matters:** The economic → legal → ethical → philanthropic CSR pyramid. Tested on the CSCP and used at every business school.

### 15. Kaplan, Robert S. & Norton, David P. *"The Balanced Scorecard, Measures That Drive Performance."*
- **Venue:** Harvard Business Review, January-February 1992. Their 1996 book *The Balanced Scorecard: Translating Strategy into Action* (HBS Press) is the full treatment.
- **Why it matters:** The 4-perspective scorecard. Tested directly.

### 16. Wallace, Thomas F. & Stahl, Robert A. *Sales & Operations Planning: The How-To Handbook.*
- **Publisher:** T. F. Wallace & Co., 3rd edition 2008 (original 1999).
- **ISBN:** 978-0997887705
- **Why it matters:** The canonical 5-step S&OP cycle. Wallace was a founder of the Oliver Wight Companies, the firm that operationalized MRP and S&OP for global manufacturing.

### 17. Lee, Hau L., Padmanabhan, V., Whang, Seungjin. *"The Bullwhip Effect in Supply Chains."*
- **Venue:** Sloan Management Review, Spring 1997. Stanford GSB.
- **Why it matters:** The academic origin of the modern bullwhip-effect language. Builds on Forrester's 1958 *Industrial Dynamics*. Tested on the CSCP.

### 18. Sheffi, Yossi. *The Resilient Enterprise: Overcoming Vulnerability for Competitive Advantage.*
- **Publisher:** MIT Press, 2007.
- **ISBN:** 978-0262693493
- **Why it matters:** The foundational post-9/11 supply-chain resilience text. Companion: Sheffi's 2020 *The New (Ab)Normal* (MIT CTL Media) covers COVID + Toyota's chip resilience and is the modern follow-up.

### 19. Polman, Paul & Winston, Andrew. *Net Positive: How Courageous Companies Thrive by Giving More Than They Take.*
- **Publisher:** Harvard Business Review Press, 2021.
- **ISBN:** 978-1647821302
- **Why it matters:** Unilever Sustainable Living Plan from the CEO's perspective. The canonical case for sustainability-as-supply-chain-strategy.

## 🏢 Industry resources, blogs, podcasts, newsletters

### 20. ASCM *SCM Now* magazine
- **URL:** ascm.org → resources
- **Why subscribe:** The official ASCM practitioner magazine. Quarterly issues cover frameworks the CSCP tests and emerging supply chain trends (AI, sustainability, geopolitics).
- **Best for:** Current 2024-2026 developments to layer onto the static CSCP body of knowledge.

### 21. CSCMP *Supply Chain Quarterly* + *Supply Chain Management Review*
- **URL:** cscmp.org, supplychainquarterly.com
- **Why subscribe:** Council of Supply Chain Management Professionals (CSCMP) is the practitioner-side counterpart to ASCM. SCMR runs deep case studies, Apple India, Walmart RFID, Maersk crisis communications.
- **Best for:** Practitioner-grade case material for your capstone.

### 22. McKinsey Operations practice
- **URL:** mckinsey.com/business-functions/operations
- **Why subscribe:** McKinsey publishes ~20 supply chain insight articles per year. The *Supply Chain Frontier* annual report (2023, 2024 editions) is one of the most-cited industry references.
- **Best for:** Executive-grade framings; useful for capstone language and Board-level prose.

### 23. Gartner Supply Chain practice
- **URL:** gartner.com (free summaries; deep reports paywalled)
- **Why subscribe:** Gartner's annual *Top 25 Supply Chains* list (formerly AMR Research) and the Gartner SCM maturity model are widely cited in 2024-2026. Free executive summaries.
- **Best for:** Benchmarking + maturity framing.

### 24. *Logistics Management* magazine + *Journal of Commerce* (JOC.com)
- **URL:** logisticsmgmt.com, joc.com
- **Why subscribe:** Practitioner-side logistics news. JOC covers ocean and intermodal in depth, essential for the post-Suez / Houthi / Panama Canal context of 2024-2026.
- **Best for:** Module 7 + 8 current events.

### 25. *FreightWaves*
- **URL:** freightwaves.com
- **Why subscribe:** Daily trucking + freight market data. Free tier covers spot rates, capacity, and breaking disruption news.
- **Best for:** Real-time logistics market conditions; great for understanding *why* freight rates moved in any given week.

### 26. Lloyd's List
- **URL:** lloydslist.com (subscription; many free articles)
- **Why subscribe:** The ocean shipping industry's authoritative source since 1734. Definitive coverage of Suez/Houthi, port congestion, container rate cycles.
- **Best for:** Strategic logistics + chokepoint risk analysis.

## 🎓 Free academic courses (online, no degree fees)

### 27. MIT CTL MicroMasters in Supply Chain Management (Yossi Sheffi et al.)
- **URL:** ctl.mit.edu/micromasters
- **Why engage:** MIT Center for Transportation & Logistics' open-access program. 5 modules (SC Analytics, SC Fundamentals, SC Design, SC Technology, SC Dynamics) + final exam. Free to audit; certificate is paid. Sheffi is one of the most-cited supply chain academics globally.
- **Best for:** Deeper quantitative grounding than the CSCP requires; perfect post-CSCP for a Master's-equivalent credential.

### 28. Stanford GSB Strategy + Supply Chain content (open + open access podcasts)
- **URL:** gsb.stanford.edu
- **Why engage:** Hau Lee's home institution. Open content on supply chain strategy, the Triple-A framework, and global supply chain risk. Stanford's *View From the Top* podcast frequently interviews supply chain CEOs.
- **Best for:** Strategic and Board-level framing.

### 29. MIT Sloan *Supply Chain Frontiers* (free open magazine)
- **URL:** ctl.mit.edu/news/supply-chain-frontiers
- **Why engage:** MIT CTL's open-access quarterly. Less academic than journals, more rigorous than trade press. Covers JD.com, Apple India, climate-induced disruption, and AI-augmented operations.
- **Best for:** 2024-2026 case material for the capstone.

### 30. Coursera / edX MIT + Wharton + INSEAD supply chain specializations
- **Recommended:** Yossi Sheffi's *Supply Chain Fundamentals* (MITx); Wharton's *Operations Management* (Christian Terwiesch, Coursera).
- **Why engage:** Free to audit; paid for certificates. Sheffi's track parallels the MIT CTL MicroMasters; Wharton's is the lighter MBA introduction.
- **Best for:** Reinforcement after the ASCM CSCP Learning System.

## How to sequence this list

If you have limited time (e.g., 12 weeks to the exam):

1. ASCM CSCP Learning System (#1), week 1-12.
2. *The Goal* (#4), one weekend, ideally week 4.
3. Kraljic 1983 (#8) + Fisher 1997 (#9) + Hau Lee 2004 (#10), three afternoons, week 6.
4. ASCM *SCM Now* (#20), one issue per week.

If you have 6 months to the exam and CSCO ambition:

- Add Christopher (#2), Liker (#5), Sheffi (#18), Polman-Winston (#19), and MIT CTL MicroMasters (#27).

If you're building a career, not just passing the exam:

- All 30 items, sequenced over 2 years. The list above is roughly the canon. Beyond it, follow the McKinsey + Gartner + ASCM annual reports for current developments.

The CSCP exam tests a static body of knowledge from this canon. The post-CSCP world adds 2024-2026 events: tariff cycles, Red Sea / Houthi, CSRD enforcement, AI-augmented S&OP, Toyota's chip resilience playbook. The reading list above prepares you for both.

🚚 Read deliberately. Cite carefully. The CSCP is the credential; the canon is the craft.

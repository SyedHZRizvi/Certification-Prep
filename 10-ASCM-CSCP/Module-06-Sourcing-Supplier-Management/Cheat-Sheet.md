# 📋 Module 6 Cheat Sheet: Sourcing & Supplier Management

> One page. Print it. Tape it to your monitor. Review before the exam.

---

## 🎯 7-Step Strategic Sourcing

1. Analyze spend categories
2. Assess supply market
3. Develop sourcing strategy
4. Solicit & evaluate suppliers (RFx)
5. Negotiate & award contracts
6. Implement & integrate
7. Manage performance, continuously improve

---

## 📥 RFI vs RFP vs RFQ

| Doc | Asks For | Use |
|-----|----------|-----|
| RFI | Information / capabilities | Early discovery |
| RFP | Solution / approach | Complex / strategic |
| RFQ | Price quote | Defined commodity spec |

---

## 🎯 Kraljic Matrix

|                | Low risk | High risk |
|----------------|----------|-----------|
| **Low impact** | **Routine** — automate, e-catalog | **Bottleneck** — dual-source, safety stock |
| **High impact**| **Leverage** — competitive bid, reverse auction | **Strategic** — partnership, long-term |

---

## 💰 TCO = ?

```
   TCO = Acquisition + Operating + Maintenance + Disposal + Hidden
```
Price alone ≠ true cost. A cheaper printer often costs $thousands more in toner & repairs.

---

## 🏆 Supplier Selection Criteria (top weights)

1. Quality
2. On-time delivery
3. Cost / TCO
4. Service / responsiveness
5. Capability
6. Financial health
7. Sustainability (ESG)
8. Cyber posture (rising)

---

## 🤝 SRM Maturity Ladder

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Adversarial | Pure price; frequent switching |
| Cooperative | Multi-year contracts |
| Collaborative | Joint forecasting, scorecards |
| Strategic alliance | Joint R&D, shared P&L, equity |
| Vertical integration | Ownership |

Apply higher maturity to **strategic** items only.

## ⚖️ Contract Types

| Type | Risk Bearer |
|------|-------------|
| Fixed-price | Supplier |
| Cost-plus | Buyer |
| Time & Materials | Buyer |
| IDIQ | Shared |
| MSA | Shared |
| Performance-based | Supplier |

### Key Clauses
- SLA · Force majeure · MFN · Liquidated damages · Indemnification · IP ownership

---

## 💬 Negotiation

| Style | When |
|-------|------|
| Distributive (win-lose) | Single commodity transaction |
| Integrative (win-win) | Strategic long-term |
| Principled (Fisher/Ury) | Most situations |

**BATNA** = your walk-away alternative. Strong BATNA = leverage.

---

## ⚠️ Anti-Patterns To Recognize

- ❌ RFQ for unspecified scope
- ❌ Reverse auction on strategic items
- ❌ Single source for bottleneck items
- ❌ Strategic partnership for routine commodities
- ❌ Lowest-price decisions ignoring TCO
- ❌ Fixed-price contract on uncertain scope
- ❌ Weak BATNA → desperate negotiation

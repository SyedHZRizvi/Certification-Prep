# Module 6: Sourcing & Supplier Management 🤝

> **Why this module matters:** Sourcing is the bridge between strategy and execution. The CSCP tests the **sourcing process (RFI / RFP / RFQ)**, **Kraljic segmentation**, **TCO**, and **SRM maturity** in nearly every form. Domain 2 (Design) ~20% and Domain 3 (Execution) ~40% both lean here.

---

## 🏪 A Story: The Boutique Hotel That Bought Light Bulbs Like Beef

Yuki manages procurement for a 32-property boutique hotel group. When she started, every item — beef tenderloin, light bulbs, robes, software licenses — went through the same 3-quote process. Slow. Wasteful. And it gave bulk leverage to suppliers who knew the routine.

She ran a one-day workshop. Built a **Kraljic matrix**. Categorized 600 spend lines:

- **Light bulbs, paper, basic linens** → routine. Switch to a catalog / punch-out e-procurement system. Stop quoting.
- **Specialty mattress vendor** → strategic. Deep partnership. Shared roadmap. 5-year contract.
- **Single-source local cheesemaker** for the breakfast buffet → bottleneck. Lock in a dual source + recipe alternative.
- **Cleaning supplies bought in volume** → leverage. Reverse-auction every 18 months.

Six months later, total procurement cost dropped 8%, supplier count dropped from 320 to 180, and the procurement team spent 40% less time on transactional work. They could focus on strategic relationships.

**That's sourcing maturity.** This module shows you the frameworks behind it.

## 🎯 The Strategic Sourcing Process — 7 Steps (memorize)

ASCM's classic strategic sourcing cycle. Expect a "put these in order" question.

```
   1. Analyze spend categories
   2. Assess supply market
   3. Develop sourcing strategy
   4. Solicit & evaluate suppliers (RFx)
   5. Negotiate & award contracts
   6. Implement & integrate
   7. Manage performance & continuously improve
```

🧠 **Memory hook:** "**A**lways **A**ssess **D**eep, **S**elect **N**ew, **I**mplement, **M**anage" → A-A-D-S-N-I-M (7 steps).

## 📥 RFI, RFP, RFQ — Don't Confuse Them

| Document | Purpose | When | Output |
|----------|---------|------|--------|
| **RFI** — Request for Information | Gather market intel, capabilities | Early discovery | Long-list of qualified suppliers |
| **RFP** — Request for Proposal | Solicit *solutions* including approach, design, methodology | Complex / strategic purchases | Comparative scored proposals |
| **RFQ** — Request for Quotation | Pure price quote on a defined spec | Commodity / well-defined need | Lowest qualified bid |

> 🚨 **Trap on the exam:** RFQ ≠ RFP. RFQ asks "how much for X?"; RFP asks "what's your solution to my problem?" Mixing them on the exam loses points.

### Reverse auctions
- Suppliers compete by dropping prices in real time
- Best when: well-specified product, multiple capable suppliers, price is the dominant criterion
- Worst when: complex services, relationship is strategic, supplier base is small

## 🎯 The Kraljic Matrix (1983) — Probably the MOST-tested supplier framework

Peter Kraljic, *HBR 1983*. Plots items on two axes: profit impact and supply risk.

|                              | **Low supply risk** | **High supply risk** |
|------------------------------|--------------------|----------------------|
| **Low profit impact**        | **Routine** items   | **Bottleneck** items  |
| **High profit impact**       | **Leverage** items  | **Strategic** items   |

### What to do in each quadrant

| Quadrant | Sourcing Strategy | Example |
|----------|-------------------|---------|
| **Routine** | Automate, catalog, consolidate suppliers, e-procurement | Office supplies, light bulbs |
| **Leverage** | Use buying power; competitive bidding, reverse auctions | Bulk steel, packaging |
| **Bottleneck** | Ensure supply; dual-source, hold safety stock, develop alternatives | Rare specialty pigment |
| **Strategic** | Long-term partnerships, joint planning, deep relationships | Custom electric motor for car platform |

🎯 **Exam tip:** Strategic items deserve the deepest relationships and longest contracts; routine items deserve the least transactional effort.

## 💰 Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)

TCO captures **all costs** of acquiring + using + retiring a product or service — not just the purchase price.

```
   TCO = Acquisition + Operating + Maintenance + Disposal + Hidden costs
```

| Cost bucket | Examples |
|-------------|----------|
| **Acquisition** | Unit price, freight, duties, taxes |
| **Operating** | Energy, consumables, training |
| **Maintenance** | Repair, downtime, spares |
| **Disposal** | End-of-life removal, recycling, environmental fees |
| **Hidden** | Quality issues, lead time risk, supplier failure, integration cost |

### Why TCO matters
A printer that costs $200 less upfront might cost $3,000 more in ink, repairs, and downtime over 3 years. Procurement decisions based on price alone often increase TCO.

## 🏆 Supplier Selection Criteria (Dickson's classic + modern additions)

In 1966, Dickson studied 273 procurement managers and identified the top selection criteria. Quality and on-time delivery ranked highest. Modern adaptations add ESG and digital readiness.

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Quality** | Defect rates, certifications (ISO 9001) |
| **Delivery** | On-time %, lead-time reliability |
| **Cost** | Unit price, TCO, payment terms |
| **Service / responsiveness** | Issue resolution, account team |
| **Capability / capacity** | Scale, technology, R&D |
| **Financial health** | Credit, balance sheet, going concern |
| **Geographic location** | Lead time, freight, currency, tariff exposure |
| **Cultural fit / management** | Ethics, communication style |
| **Sustainability / ESG** | Carbon, labor practices, scope-3 emissions |
| **Cybersecurity** | NIST/ISO posture (especially for SaaS) |

## 📊 Supplier Scorecards & Performance Management

Common scorecard dimensions (weighted):

| Metric | Weight Example |
|--------|--------------:|
| Quality (PPM defects, FPY) | 30% |
| On-time delivery | 25% |
| Cost / price competitiveness | 20% |
| Responsiveness / service | 10% |
| Innovation / collaboration | 10% |
| Sustainability | 5% |

Scorecards drive quarterly business reviews (QBRs) and continuous improvement. Top suppliers earn longer contracts, preferred status, and design-in opportunities.

## 🤝 SRM — Supplier Relationship Management

SRM maturity ladder:

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Adversarial / transactional** | Pure price competition; switch often |
| **Cooperative** | Multi-year contracts; basic information sharing |
| **Collaborative** | Joint forecasting (CPFR), shared scorecards |
| **Strategic alliance** | Joint R&D, shared P&L on programs, equity |
| **Vertical integration** | Ownership |

🎯 **Exam tip:** Higher maturity is NOT always better. Apply it where the supplier is **strategic**. Adversarial sourcing is fine for routine commodities.

## ⚖️ Contract Types

| Contract Type | Risk Bearer | Use When |
|--------------|-------------|----------|
| **Fixed-price (FP)** | Supplier | Scope is clear |
| **Cost-plus** | Buyer | Scope evolving (R&D, gov contracts) |
| **Time & Materials (T&M)** | Buyer | Hourly services |
| **Indefinite Delivery / Indefinite Quantity (IDIQ)** | Shared | Unknown total quantity |
| **Master Service Agreement (MSA)** | Shared | Multi-project relationships |
| **Performance-based** | Supplier | Outcomes are measurable (SLA) |

### Key clauses to know
- **SLA** — service-level agreement, defines performance & penalties
- **Force majeure** — excuses non-performance for unforeseeable events
- **MFN** (most favored nation) — buyer gets best price supplier gives any customer
- **Liquidated damages** — pre-agreed penalty for breach
- **Indemnification** — supplier covers buyer's legal exposure
- **IP ownership** — who owns the deliverables / customizations

## 🎯 Sourcing Negotiation Strategies

| Style | When To Use |
|-------|-------------|
| **Distributive (win-lose)** | Single transaction, commodity |
| **Integrative (win-win)** | Strategic, long-term relationship |
| **Principled (Fisher/Ury)** | Mixed; focus on interests, not positions |
| **BATNA-driven** | Always — know your alternative |

### BATNA = Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement
The walk-away option. If your BATNA is strong, you can hold firm. If it's weak, you take what you can get.

## ⚖️ Make-vs-Buy Revisited (Sourcing Lens)

We saw this in Module 2 from a network angle. Sourcing lens emphasizes:

| MAKE if… | BUY if… |
|----------|---------|
| Strategic core competency | Commodity / non-core |
| IP / trade secrets to protect | Suppliers have better economies |
| Need precise quality control | Speed-to-market beats integration |
| Stable, predictable volume | Volatile demand needs flexibility |

## 🛂 Global Sourcing Considerations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Currency volatility | FX hedging, multi-currency invoices |
| Political instability | Geographic diversification |
| Tariffs / trade wars | Country-of-origin engineering |
| Lead-time variability | Safety stock, dual-sourcing |
| Cultural / language gaps | Local agents, cultural training |
| Quality variation | Audits, certifications, in-line inspection |
| Sustainability scope-3 | Audits + supplier ESG scorecards |

## ⚠️ Common Misconceptions

| Misconception | Reality |
|---------------|---------|
| "Cheapest price wins" | Cheapest price often raises TCO |
| "RFP and RFQ are interchangeable" | Different documents, different stages |
| "Reverse auctions work for everything" | Best for well-specified commodities only |
| "Strategic = expensive" | Strategic = irreplaceable + high impact, not necessarily $ |
| "More suppliers = lower risk" | Beyond a point, complexity grows faster than resilience |
| "SRM means partnerships everywhere" | Apply selectively (Kraljic decides) |
| "ESG is a cost center" | Increasingly a customer + regulator requirement |

---

## 🚨 Exam Traps

1. **RFI vs RFP vs RFQ order** — RFI early, RFP for solutions, RFQ for price.
2. **Kraljic strategic = collaborate; routine = automate** — Don't mismatch.
3. **TCO ≠ purchase price** — Always include lifecycle costs.
4. **Single source vs sole source** (recap from M2).
5. **Reverse auction for strategic items** — Almost always wrong.
6. **Fixed-price contract on uncertain scope** — Transfers risk to supplier; they price defensively.
7. **BATNA in negotiation** — Knowing yours is the foundation of leverage.

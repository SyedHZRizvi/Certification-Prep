## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **D. Forecasting**
TIMWOOD = Transport, Inventory, Motion, Waiting, Overproduction, Over-processing, Defects.

### Q2: **B. 3.4 per million opportunities**
The headline Six Sigma target.

### Q3: **A. Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control**
Standard DMAIC.

### Q4: **B. Exploit**
Identify → Exploit → Subordinate → Elevate → Repeat.

### Q5: **B. Availability × Performance × Quality**
Multiplicative; max 100%.

### Q6: **A. Sort, Set, Shine, Standardize, Sustain**
The 5S workplace organization technique.

### Q7: **C. Analyze**
Root-cause tools live in the Analyze phase.

### Q8: **B. Continuous small improvements**
Kaizen = "change for the better."

### Q9: **B. Financial / Customer / Internal Process / Learning & Growth**
Kaplan & Norton.

### Q10: **B. Revenue − truly variable costs**
Throughput accounting definition.

### Q11: **B. Pace and magnitude**
Kaizen = incremental. BPR = radical.

### Q12: **B. On-time + in-full + undamaged + correct documentation**
The four conditions.

### Q13: **D. Control phase**
Control charts sustain gains.

### Q14: **B. DIO + DSO − DPO**
Standard formula.

### Q15: **A. Single-Minute Exchange of Die**
Quick changeover technique.

### Q16: **B. Mistake-proofing**
Poka-yoke prevents errors before they happen.

### Q17: **A. Plan, Do, Check, Act**
Deming cycle.

### Q18: **B. Autonomation, stop the line on defect**
Toyota concept; jidoka empowers operators to halt production.

### Q19: **C. Functional benchmarking**
Best-in-class outside your industry.

### Q20: **C. Part-time project member**
Green Belts apply tools within their role; Black Belts are full-time.

### Q21: **B. Visual signals on the line**
Andon boards/lights flag issues.

### Q22: **D. SWOT analysis**
SWOT is strategy, not a quality tool.

### Q23: **A. Digital twin**
A simulated digital model of a real-world system.

### Q24: **B. Going to the actual workplace**
"Gemba" = real place.

### Q25: **C. The constraint**
Drum (constraint) sets the pace; the rope pulls releases.

# Module 4 — Les Conversations du Quotidien · Quiz (26 Questions)

---

**Question 1**
You are at a Parisian café and want to order politely. Which phrase do you use?

- A) Je veux un café.
- B) Je voudrais un café, s'il vous plaît.
- C) Donnez-moi un café!
- D) Un café, ça va.

**Answer: B** — *Je voudrais* (I would like) is the most polite way to order. *Je veux* (I want) is grammatically correct but sounds abrupt in a service context.

---

**Question 2**
The waiter says *Vous désirez?* — what does this mean?

- A) Are you ready to pay?
- B) What would you like?
- C) Do you have a reservation?
- D) Is everything okay?

**Answer: B** — *Vous désirez?* = What would you like? (literally: What do you desire?)

---

**Question 3**
How do you ask "How much is it?" in French?

- A) C'est quoi?
- B) Ça fait quoi?
- C) C'est combien?
- D) Combien vous avez?

**Answer: C** — *C'est combien?* = How much is it? *Ça fait combien?* is also accepted.

---

**Question 4**
The waiter says *Ça fait trois euros cinquante*. What does this mean?

- A) There are three euros and fifty cents in the register.
- B) That comes to three euros fifty.
- C) We accept three to five euros.
- D) The coffee is three and a half hours old.

**Answer: B** — *Ça fait...* = That comes to... (used when giving a total amount).

---

**Question 5**
How do you say "Have a good day!" when leaving a shop?

- A) Au revoir seulement.
- B) Bonne nuit!
- C) Bonne journée!
- D) Bon appétit!

**Answer: C** — *Bonne journée!* = Have a good day! *Bonsoir/Bonne soirée* = good evening, *Bonne nuit* = goodnight.

---

**Question 6**
At a French market, the vendor says *Regardez comme elles sont belles!* — what is he saying?

- A) Look, I'll make them cheaper!
- B) Look how beautiful they are!
- C) Look, I grew them myself!
- D) Look, they are very old!

**Answer: B** — *Regardez comme elles sont belles!* = Look how beautiful they are! (referring to the produce).

---

**Question 7**
How do you say "in total" when referring to a bill?

- A) en général
- B) en tout
- C) en plus
- D) en avance

**Answer: B** — *en tout* = in total. Example: *Ça fait dix euros en tout.*

---

**Question 8**
You want to ask for directions. What is the best opening?

- A) Hé! Où est le métro?
- B) Pardon, où c'est?
- C) Excusez-moi, monsieur/madame. Pourriez-vous m'indiquer...?
- D) Tu sais où on est?

**Answer: C** — Starting with *Excusez-moi, monsieur/madame* is the culturally correct, polite approach when speaking to a stranger in France.

---

**Question 9**
The Parisian says *Allez tout droit* — what should you do?

- A) Turn left
- B) Turn right
- C) Go straight ahead
- D) Take the bridge

**Answer: C** — *tout droit* = straight ahead.

---

**Question 10**
*C'est à dix minutes à pied* — what does this mean?

- A) It's ten minutes by car.
- B) It's ten minutes by metro.
- C) It's ten minutes on foot.
- D) It costs ten euros to walk there.

**Answer: C** — *à pied* = on foot. *C'est à dix minutes à pied* = It's ten minutes on foot.

---

**Question 11**
At the boulangerie, the baker asks *Bien cuite ou pas trop cuite?* — what is she asking about?

- A) How sweet the bread should be
- B) How long you want to wait
- C) How well done / how baked you want the bread
- D) How many you want to buy

**Answer: C** — *Bien cuite* = well done/well baked. *Pas trop cuite* = not too well done (lighter colour).

---

**Question 12**
How do you say "I have a headache" in French?

- A) J'ai mal à la tête.
- B) J'ai une tête malade.
- C) Ma tête est malade.
- D) J'ai de la tête.

**Answer: A** — *J'ai mal à la tête.* — French uses *avoir mal à* + body part to express pain.

---

**Question 13**
The pharmacist says *Prenez un comprimé toutes les six heures* — what does this mean?

- A) Take six tablets at once.
- B) Take one tablet every six hours.
- C) Come back in six hours.
- D) Six tablets per day maximum.

**Answer: B** — *un comprimé* = one tablet, *toutes les six heures* = every six hours.

---

**Question 14**
How do you say "Get well soon!" in French?

- A) Bon appétit!
- B) Bonne chance!
- C) Bonne guérison!
- D) Bon courage!

**Answer: C** — *Bonne guérison!* = Get well soon! (literally: Good recovery!)

---

**Question 15**
When meeting someone new in French, which expression means "Pleased to meet you"?

- A) Bonjour!
- B) Comment allez-vous?
- C) Enchanté(e)!
- D) Bien sûr!

**Answer: C** — *Enchanté(e)!* = Pleased to meet you. Add -e if you are female: *Enchantée.*

---

**Question 16**
Sophie asks *Vous êtes d'où?* — what is she asking?

- A) Where are you going?
- B) Where are you from?
- C) Where do you live?
- D) Where did you study?

**Answer: B** — *Vous êtes d'où?* = Where are you from? (Formal version of *Tu viens d'où?*)

---

**Question 17**
Ali says *On peut s'entraîner ensemble?* — what is he proposing?

- A) Can we compete against each other?
- B) Can we study the same materials?
- C) Can we practise together?
- D) Can we train at the gym?

**Answer: C** — *s'entraîner* = to practise/train. *On peut s'entraîner ensemble?* = Can we practise together?

---

**Question 18**
At a French dinner party, what does *Bon appétit!* signal?

- A) The food is very tasty.
- B) Everyone may begin eating.
- C) The host is about to leave.
- D) The last course is served.

**Answer: B** — *Bon appétit!* is said by the host before eating begins — the signal that everyone may start.

---

**Question 19**
Which is the culturally appropriate time to arrive at a French dinner party?

- A) Exactly on time or a few minutes early
- B) 30 minutes early to help cook
- C) 10–15 minutes late
- D) As late as you like — the French don't mind

**Answer: C** — In France, arriving exactly on time is considered slightly pushy. 10–15 minutes late is the norm for a dinner invitation.

---

**Question 20**
How do you say "A little more wine?" in French?

- A) Plus de vin?
- B) Encore du vin?
- C) Encore un peu de vin?
- D) Un peu vin encore?

**Answer: C** — *Encore un peu de vin?* = A little more wine? *encore* = more/again, *un peu* = a little.

---

**Question 21**
The hotel receptionist says *j'écoute* when answering the phone. What does this convey?

- A) I am listening / go ahead
- B) I don't understand
- C) Please hold
- D) I'm not available

**Answer: A** — *j'écoute* literally means "I'm listening" — it is standard French telephone etiquette meaning "I'm here, go ahead."

---

**Question 22**
How do you say "For two people" when booking a hotel?

- A) Pour deux chambres.
- B) Pour deux personnes.
- C) Avec deux personnes.
- D) Deux people, s'il vous plaît.

**Answer: B** — *Pour deux personnes* = For two people.

---

**Question 23**
What does *au nom de* mean in a hotel booking context?

- A) In the style of
- B) In the name of
- C) On behalf of the manager
- D) For the price of

**Answer: B** — *au nom de* = in the name of. *Je confirme au nom de Khan* = I confirm in the name of Khan.

---

**Question 24**
Which item should you NOT bring as a gift to a French dinner party?

- A) A bottle of wine
- B) Chocolates
- C) Chrysanthemums
- D) Flowers (other than chrysanthemums)

**Answer: C** — Chrysanthemums are associated with funerals in France and should never be given as a social gift.

---

**Question 25**
What does *Avec plaisir!* mean?

- A) With great difficulty
- B) With pleasure
- C) Please sit down
- D) I'd be happy to try

**Answer: B** — *Avec plaisir!* = With pleasure! An enthusiastic acceptance or "you're welcome."

---

**Question 26**
*La bise* refers to which French custom?

- A) Tipping at restaurants
- B) The cheek-kiss greeting
- C) Removing shoes at the door
- D) Saying grace before meals

**Answer: B** — *La bise* is the French custom of greeting with cheek kisses — typically one to four depending on the region.

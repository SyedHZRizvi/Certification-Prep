## Answer Key

```
1.  B — Savoir + infinitive = to know how to do something (je sais nager). Connaître + noun = to know/be familiar with a person or place (je connais Paris).

2.  A — Alla is the passé simple of aller, used exclusively in formal/literary writing for completed past actions. Option B (est allé) is passé composé (spoken/informal); C (allait) is imparfait.

3.  B — J'aurais dû + infinitive = I should have (conditional perfect of devoir). Devais = I had to (imparfait, obligation in past); devrais = I should (present conditional).

4.  B — "Force est de constater" is a C2-level fixed expression meaning "one is compelled / forced to acknowledge." It introduces an unavoidable observation.

5.  C — Les lettres qu'elle a écrites: the direct object (les lettres, feminine plural) precedes the verb avoir in a relative clause, so the past participle must agree: écrites (f. pl.).

6.  C — Bien que always triggers the subjunctive in French. Travaille is the subjunctive of travailler here (identical to indicative for -er verbs, but the rule applies).

7.  B — Or is a logical connector in formal French meaning "now / however" (introducing a new step or counter-point in argumentation). It is NOT the conjunction "or" (= ou) or the metal.

8.  A — Ne…que wraps around the conjugated verb: ne before verb, que before the restricted element. Elle ne lit que = she only reads.

9.  C — Si + adjective + que + subjonctif is a C1/C2 concessive structure meaning "however [adjective] he may be." Not a Type 1 or 2 conditional.

10. B — Plus vous pratiquez, mieux vous devenez. Plus…mieux is the correct correlative for "the more…the better." Mieux (adverb) modifies devenez; not plus bon.

11. C — Il est médecin (no article, profession directly after être) and C'est un médecin (article required after c'est + noun) are both correct. Never Il est un médecin.

12. C — Quand bien même + conditionnel présent or conditionnel passé. It is a concessive meaning "even if." This is a fixed C2 construction.

13. B — Meuf is verlan for femme (woman) — the syllables are reversed: fem-me → meu-f. Ouf = verlan for fou (crazy).

14. B — DALF = Diplôme Approfondi de Langue Française. It is awarded by France Éducation International at C1 and C2 levels.

15. C — "Veuillez agréer, Madame, l'expression de mes salutations distinguées" is the standard formal French letter closing. Options A and B are informal; D is not a standard formulation.

16. C — Anaphore (anaphora) = repetition of a word/phrase at the start of successive clauses for rhetorical emphasis. "Nous avons…nous avons…nous avons" is a classic example.

17. B — Quand je suis arrivé (PC = specific event), il était parti (PQP = completed before my arrival). The pluperfect marks the earlier action.

18. B — With être, the past participle agrees with the subject in gender and number. Elle est partie (she = feminine → partie).

19. B — Ne pas être dans son assiette = to be under the weather / not feel oneself. Assiette literally means "plate" but figuratively means one's state/balance.

20. C — Décision nominalises the verb décider; partir is used as a noun (infinitive used as noun = nominalisation). Both nominalisation processes are present.

21. A — "Certes… mérites indéniables; néanmoins, elle mérite d'être nuancée" is the C2 concessive register: acknowledging merit before qualified refutation.

22. A — Parler de quelque chose → dont. C'est le professeur dont j'ai parlé = the professor I spoke about. Option D is redundant (dont and à qui together).

23. C — Le chiffre d'affaires = turnover / gross revenue. This is standard business French vocabulary.

24. B — After a past reporting verb (a dit), the reported clause shifts to imparfait: elle était (was) fatiguée. Direct speech: "Je suis fatiguée" → elle a dit qu'elle était fatiguée.

25. B — Les Misérables is a Romantic/Social Realist epic (1862) exploring justice, poverty, redemption, and the struggle between law and morality through characters like Jean Valjean and Inspector Javert.

26. C — Elle aurait pu gagner = She could have won (conditional perfect of pouvoir). Pourrait = could (present conditional); pouvait = could (imparfait).

27. C — Néanmoins = nevertheless / however. Donc = therefore; car = because/for; ainsi = thus.

28. B — The imparfait (je rêvais) sets a long-held background desire; the passé composé (j'ai pu) marks the single event that fulfilled it. Classic imparfait + PC temporal structure.

29. B — "Le septième art" is cinema — France officially recognised film as the seventh art, following architecture, sculpture, painting, music, literature, and dance.

30. C — "Je me permets de vous contacter au sujet de…" is the formal, polite way to initiate professional contact. Je me permets de = I take the liberty of.

31. D — C2 = Proficiency / near-native accuracy and idiomatic mastery. C1 = advanced fluency; B2 = upper-intermediate; B1 = intermediate.

32. A — Pourvu que + subjonctif. Pourvu qu'il fasse beau = provided that the weather is fine. Fasse is the subjunctive of faire.

33. B — Faire d'une pierre deux coups = to kill two birds with one stone / to achieve two things at once with a single action.

34. D — In Lebanon, French is an official language (co-official with Arabic) but Arabic is the dominant spoken language in daily life. French is used in education, law, and formal contexts.

35. B — Liberté, Égalité, Fraternité is the French national motto, emerging from the Revolution (1789) and the Declaration of the Rights of Man.

36. B — Il convient de préciser = it is appropriate / fitting / necessary to specify. A formal academic phrase to introduce a clarification or qualification.

37. B — J'ai mangé une pomme = completed single event (passé composé). Je mangeais chaque jour = habitual past action (imparfait). This is the core distinction.

38. B — S'avérer = to prove to be / to turn out to be. Cette décision s'avère nécessaire = This decision turns out to be necessary.

39. C — Quel que soit le résultat = regardless of the outcome / whatever the result may be. Quel que + soit (subjonctif of être) = a fixed C1/C2 concessive construction.

40. B — Ne saurait (negative of savoir in conditional) = cannot / would not be able to. In formal/literary French, ne saurait is an elevated way of saying ne peut pas.

41. A — Le plus grand bâtiment de la ville. Superlative: le/la/les + plus + adjective + de + group. The adjective grand must agree: grand (m.s.) is correct here.

42. A — PJ = Pièces Jointes = Attachments (in email/letter footer). Standard French business abbreviation.

43. B — Tu = informal singular address (tutoyer); vous = formal or plural address (vouvoyer). This register distinction is critical in French social and professional contexts.

44. B — Avoir beau + infinitive = no matter how much / even though. On a beau essayer = No matter how much one tries / Even if one tries hard.

45. C — Être → je serai (irregular future stem: ser-). Parler, finir, chanter are regular and simply add the future endings to their infinitive.

46. B — Actuellement = currently / at the present time. This is a classic false friend (faux ami): it does NOT mean "actually" in English. Actually = en fait / en réalité.

47. C — "Certes, d'aucuns soutiennent que… ; cependant…" is the C2 academic structure for presenting and then refuting an opposing viewpoint. D'aucuns = some people (formal/literary).

48. B — The French plan dialectique: Introduction → Thesis → Antithesis → Synthesis → Conclusion. This three-part dialectical structure is taught in French lycées (baccalauréat level) and expected in academic writing.

49. A — N'importe quel = any (whichever). Quel agrees with the noun: n'importe quel livre (masc.), n'importe quelle idée (fem.).

50. B — The sentence uses nominalisation (le temps perdu → noun phrase), a relative clause (où…), elevated vocabulary (déploie, introspection, sans précédent), and complex syntax — all markers of C2 literary analysis register.
```

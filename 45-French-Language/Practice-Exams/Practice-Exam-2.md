## Answer Key

```
1.  C — Avez-vous une table pour quatre personnes, s'il vous plaît? is polite and correct. Options A and D are too abrupt. B is imperative, not a question.

2.  C — Cent vingt = 120. Cent = 100; vingt = 20; 100 + 20 = 120.

3.  B — Continuez tout droit (go straight ahead) puis tournez à gauche (turn left) au feu rouge (at the traffic light).

4.  A — Imparfait (jouais) is correct for habitual past actions. The passé composé (A: j'ai joué) would indicate a single completed event, not a repeated habit.

5.  B — J'étudie le français depuis deux ans = I have been studying French for two years. Depuis + present tense = ongoing action. Never use past tense with depuis for ongoing actions.

6.  B — Si + imparfait → conditionnel is the correct Type 2 hypothetical structure. Option D (si j'aurais) is a classic trap — never use the conditional after si.

7.  B — Allô is the standard French phone greeting, used only when answering/starting a phone call. Bonjour is general greeting but Allô is specifically for the phone.

8.  D — Que (object pronoun) is correct: le film que j'ai vu = the film that I saw. Que is the object; qui is the subject. (I = subject, not the film.)

9.  C — Dont replaces de + noun: l'auteur dont le roman est célèbre = the author whose novel is famous. Le roman de l'auteur → dont absorbs "de l'auteur."

10. C — Madame, Monsieur is the formal salutation in French professional correspondence, equivalent to "Dear Sir or Madam."

11. C — Bien que + subjunctive is required. Soit is the subjunctive of être. Option B incorrectly uses the indicative (est) after bien que — a very common exam error.

12. B — Nuancer = to add nuance/qualify. Je suis d'accord = I agree; permettez-moi de nuancer = allow me to add some qualification.

13. A — Elle a mangé la pomme. Manger takes avoir as auxiliary in passé composé. The past participle mangé does not agree here (no preceding direct object).

14. B — Verbs of motion/state use être: il est allé (he went), il est resté (he stayed). Using avoir with these verbs is incorrect.

15. B — À mon avis, cette solution est inefficace is the formal register. Options C and D are informal/spoken. Option A is informal and vague.

16. C — La lettre a été écrite par le directeur. Passive = être conjugated + past participle agreeing with subject (écrite, feminine). Par = by.

17. B — J'attends le bus depuis vingt minutes = I have been waiting for the bus for 20 minutes (ongoing). Depuis + present = action in progress. Option A (pendant) indicates a completed duration.

18. C — Il dit qu'il est fatigué. When the reporting verb (dit) is present, no tense shift occurs. Tense shift only happens when the reporting verb is past.

19. B — D'abord (first) → ensuite (then) → finalement (finally) is the correct logical sequence for narrating steps.

20. B — Pourriez-vous parler plus lentement, s'il vous plaît? is correct and polite (conditional of pouvoir). Options A and D are imperative and rude.

21. C — Je voudrais = I would like (conditional of vouloir) — the polite form for requests. Je veux = I want (more direct, less polite in service contexts).

22. C — Où in relative clauses means "where" (place) or "when" (time). C'est la ville où j'ai grandi = the city where I grew up.

23. C — Je veux qu'il vienne uses the subjunctive (vienne) after vouloir que. The subjunctive is triggered by a change of subject + a verb of desire/will.

24. B — Imparfait (il pleuvait = background/ongoing) + passé composé (je suis sorti = specific event that occurred). This is the classic imparfait vs. PC distinction.

25. B — J'ai du pain sur la planche is the idiom for "I have a lot of work to do." J'ai le cafard = I'm feeling down; je casse les pieds = I'm annoying someone.

26. A — Je ne mange que des légumes = I only eat vegetables. Ne…que wraps around the verb: ne before conjugated verb, que before the restriction.

27. B — M'indiquer le chemin = to tell me the way/directions. Pour aller à la gare = to get to the train station.

28. D — Il était parti avant mon arrivée is grammatically clean. Option A uses imparfait (arrivais) but should use subjunctive (soit arrivé) or a noun phrase after avant. Option D avoids the trap elegantly.

29. B — In formal French writing, "Or" is a logical connector meaning "now" or "however," used to introduce a new step in an argument. It does NOT mean the metal or English "or."

30. A — Il faisait is the correct imparfait of faire. Ferait is the conditionnel; faisit and fesait are not valid forms.

31. B — Avoir le trac = to have stage fright / pre-performance nerves. Common before exams, presentations, and performances.

32. C — Malgré les difficultés = despite the difficulties. Malgré + noun (no verb). Bien que requires + subjunctive. Option A has a doubled article (des les).

33. A — C'est le pays dont je rêve. Rêver de + noun → dont. Je rêve de ce pays → le pays dont je rêve.

34. B — D'abord (first) → ensuite (then) → finalement (finally). This is the standard narrative sequence.

35. C — Prendre un rendez-vous = to make/book an appointment. Annuler = to cancel; reporter = to postpone.

36. B — Je mangerai avec Sophie = I will eat with Sophie. Futur simple: parler stem + ai. Options A (present), C (passé composé), D (imparfait) are incorrect.

37. C — Au contraire introduces a direct contradiction: "on the contrary." It directly reverses the preceding statement.

38. B — Est-ce qu'il y a une pharmacie près d'ici? = Is there a pharmacy near here? Il y a = there is/are; près d'ici = near here.

39. D — Je doute qu'il puisse venir. Douter que triggers the subjunctive. Puisse is the subjunctive of pouvoir. Options A and B use the indicative incorrectly.

40. B — À condition que + subjonctif (finissiez) → futur simple (sera approuvé). This is a condition clause: "provided that you finish before Friday."
```

## 1. Writing Professional Emails in French

Imagine you have just landed a job with a French company. Your manager sends you an email entirely in French — and your reply has to sound professional, not like a tourist asking for directions. This module is your toolkit for exactly that situation.

French professional communication follows strict conventions around formality. Getting the register right is as important as the content itself.

### Email Structure

| Part | French term | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Subject line | Objet | Objet : Demande de réunion — 15 juin |
| Opening | Formule d'appel | Madame, Monsieur, |
| Body | Corps du message | Je me permets de vous contacter au sujet de... |
| Closing | Formule de politesse | Veuillez agréer, Madame, l'expression de mes salutations distinguées. |
| Signature | Signature | Prénom NOM · Titre · Entreprise |

### Formal Openings (Formules d'appel)
| Situation | French phrase | English |
|---|---|---|
| Unknown recipient (m.) | Monsieur, | Dear Sir, |
| Unknown recipient (f.) | Madame, | Dear Madam, |
| Unknown gender | Madame, Monsieur, | Dear Sir or Madam, |
| Known person | Cher Monsieur Dupont, | Dear Mr Dupont, |
| Colleague (informal) | Bonjour Marie, | Hi Marie, |

> **Note for English speakers:** French email closings are far more elaborate than English ones. You cannot simply write "Best regards" — the formal French closing is a full sentence. The most common formal ending is: *Veuillez agréer, Madame/Monsieur, l'expression de mes salutations distinguées.* (Literally: "Please accept, Madam/Sir, the expression of my distinguished greetings.")

### Key Professional Email Phrases

| French | English |
|---|---|
| Je me permets de vous contacter... | I am taking the liberty of contacting you... |
| Suite à notre entretien téléphonique... | Following our telephone conversation... |
| Je vous écris au sujet de... | I am writing to you regarding... |
| Pourriez-vous me faire parvenir... | Could you send me... |
| Je vous serais reconnaissant(e) de bien vouloir... | I would be grateful if you would... |
| Dans l'attente de votre réponse... | Awaiting your reply... |
| N'hésitez pas à me contacter... | Please do not hesitate to contact me... |
| Cordialement, | Kind regards, |
| Bien à vous, | Best regards, (semi-formal) |

## 2. Expressing Opinions and Making Arguments

At B1–B2 level, you need to go beyond simple statements and express nuanced opinions with supporting reasoning.

### Vocabulary: Opinions and Discourse Markers

| French | IPA | English | اردو |
|---|---|---|---|
| à mon avis | /a mɔ̃ a.vi/ | in my opinion | میری رائے میں |
| selon moi | /sə.lɔ̃ mwa/ | according to me | میرے خیال میں |
| je pense que | /ʒə pɑ̃s kə/ | I think that | میرا خیال ہے کہ |
| je crois que | /ʒə kʁwa kə/ | I believe that | میں سمجھتا ہوں کہ |
| il me semble que | /il mə sɑ̃bl kə/ | it seems to me that | مجھے لگتا ہے کہ |
| en effet | /ɑ̃n‿e.fɛ/ | indeed / in fact | درحقیقت |
| cependant | /sə.pɑ̃.dɑ̃/ | however | تاہم |
| néanmoins | /ne.ɑ̃.mwɛ̃/ | nevertheless | پھر بھی |
| d'ailleurs | /d‿a.jœʁ/ | moreover / besides | علاوہ ازیں |
| en revanche | /ɑ̃ ʁə.vɑ̃ʃ/ | on the other hand | دوسری طرف |
| par conséquent | /paʁ kɔ̃.se.kɑ̃/ | consequently | نتیجتاً |
| donc | /dɔ̃k/ | therefore / so | لہٰذا |

### Structuring an Argument

French argumentation follows a clear three-part structure:

1. **Thèse** — state your position
2. **Antithèse** — acknowledge the opposing view
3. **Synthèse** — present a nuanced conclusion

**Useful connectors for each stage:**

| Stage | Phrase | English |
|---|---|---|
| Introducing | Tout d'abord, ... | First of all, ... |
| Adding | De plus, ... / En outre, ... | Furthermore, ... |
| Contrasting | Mais / Cependant / Toutefois | But / However / Yet |
| Concluding | En conclusion, ... / Finalement, ... | In conclusion, ... / Finally, ... |
| Illustrating | Par exemple, ... / Ainsi, ... | For example, ... / Thus, ... |

## 3. Giving Presentations in French

### Key Presentation Phrases

| Phase | French | English |
|---|---|---|
| Welcome | Bonjour à tous. Je vais vous présenter... | Good morning everyone. I will present... |
| Agenda | Mon exposé se divisera en trois parties. | My presentation will be divided into three parts. |
| Transition | Passons maintenant à... | Let us now move on to... |
| Emphasis | Je voudrais souligner que... | I would like to emphasize that... |
| Question time | Je vous invite à poser vos questions. | I invite you to ask your questions. |
| Thank you | Je vous remercie de votre attention. | Thank you for your attention. |

### Cognates Advantage for English Speakers

French and English share thousands of words, especially in formal/academic registers. These are your free vocabulary!

| French | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| présentation | presentation | identical meaning |
| communication | communication | identical meaning |
| conférence | conference | conférence also means "lecture" |
| résumé | résumé / CV | résumé in French = summary |
| conclusion | conclusion | identical |
| introduction | introduction | identical |
| organisation | organization | -tion words are almost always cognates |

> **False Friend Warning:** *rester* does NOT mean "to rest" — it means "to stay/remain." *Se reposer* means "to rest." Similarly, *assister à* means "to attend" (not "to assist"). Attending a meeting is *assister à une réunion*.

## 4. Sample Dialogues

### Dialogue 1 — A Professional Phone Call

**Secrétaire:** Bonjour, société Beaumont, j'écoute.
**Antoine:** Bonjour Madame. Je suis Antoine Moreau, directeur commercial chez Dupont & Fils. Pourriez-vous me passer Monsieur Lefort, s'il vous plaît?
**Secrétaire:** Un instant, je vérifie s'il est disponible... Je suis désolée, Monsieur Lefort est en réunion pour le moment. Souhaitez-vous laisser un message?
**Antoine:** Oui, s'il vous plaît. Pouvez-vous lui dire de me rappeler dès que possible? C'est urgent.
**Secrétaire:** Bien sûr, Monsieur Moreau. Je lui transmettrai votre message.
**Antoine:** Je vous remercie. Bonne journée.

**Translation:**
Secretary: Good morning, Beaumont company, how can I help you.
Antoine: Good morning, Madam. I am Antoine Moreau, commercial director at Dupont & Sons. Could you put me through to Mr Lefort, please?
Secretary: One moment, I'll check if he's available... I'm sorry, Mr Lefort is in a meeting at the moment. Would you like to leave a message?
Antoine: Yes, please. Can you tell him to call me back as soon as possible? It's urgent.
Secretary: Of course, Mr Moreau. I will pass on your message.
Antoine: Thank you. Have a good day.

### Dialogue 2 — Expressing an Opinion in a Debate

**Camille:** Selon moi, le télétravail est bénéfique pour les employés. Cela leur permet d'avoir un meilleur équilibre entre vie professionnelle et vie personnelle.

**Étienne:** Je comprends votre point de vue, cependant, il me semble que le travail en présentiel favorise davantage la collaboration et la créativité. En outre, certaines personnes trouvent difficile de se concentrer à domicile.

**Camille:** C'est vrai, néanmoins, les nouvelles technologies nous permettent de collaborer efficacement à distance. Par conséquent, je pense que les entreprises devraient adopter un modèle hybride.

**Translation:**
Camille: In my opinion, remote work is beneficial for employees. It allows them to have a better work-life balance.
Étienne: I understand your point of view, however, it seems to me that in-person work promotes collaboration and creativity more. Furthermore, some people find it difficult to concentrate at home.
Camille: That is true, nevertheless, new technologies allow us to collaborate effectively remotely. Consequently, I think companies should adopt a hybrid model.

**اردو ترجمہ:**
کمیل: میری رائے میں، گھر سے کام ملازمین کے لیے فائدہ مند ہے۔ اس سے انہیں کام اور ذاتی زندگی میں بہتر توازن ملتا ہے۔
ایتین: میں آپ کا نقطہ نظر سمجھتا ہوں، تاہم مجھے لگتا ہے کہ دفتر میں کام تعاون اور تخلیق کاری کو زیادہ فروغ دیتا ہے۔ علاوہ ازیں، کچھ لوگ گھر پر توجہ مرکوز کرنا مشکل پاتے ہیں۔
کمیل: یہ سچ ہے، پھر بھی، نئی ٹیکنالوجی ہمیں دور سے مؤثر طریقے سے تعاون کرنے دیتی ہے۔ نتیجتاً، میرا خیال ہے کہ کمپنیوں کو ہائبرڈ ماڈل اپنانا چاہیے۔

## 5. Numbers, Dates, and Formal References

In professional French, dates follow a specific format:

- *Le 15 juin 2026* (15 June 2026) — day first, no ordinal except for the 1st (*le 1er*)
- *Le lundi 15 juin* — Monday, 15 June
- Never write "June 15" order in French formal text

| French | English |
|---|---|
| le premier janvier | 1 January |
| le quinze août | 15 August |
| le trente et un décembre | 31 December |

### Time Expressions
| French | English |
|---|---|
| à neuf heures du matin | at nine o'clock in the morning |
| à quatorze heures trente | at 14:30 / 2:30 pm |
| à midi | at noon |
| à minuit | at midnight |

> **Advantage for English speakers:** French business uses the **24-hour clock** universally. A meeting at *15h00* is 3 pm — no ambiguity. British English speakers may find this familiar.

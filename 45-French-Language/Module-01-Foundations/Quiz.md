# Module 1 — Les Fondations · Quiz (26 Questions)

Test your knowledge of the French alphabet, accent marks, numbers, greetings, formal/informal pronouns, and cognates.

---

**Question 1**
How is the French letter **H** pronounced?

- A) Like English "h" in "hello"
- B) Like a breathy "kh" sound
- C) It is always silent
- D) Like the letter "j" in Spanish

**Answer: C** — The H is always silent in French. *l'homme* (the man) is pronounced "lom".

---

**Question 2**
What does the accent mark **ç** (cédille) do to the letter C?

- A) Makes the C silent
- B) Gives the C a hard "k" sound
- C) Gives the C a soft "s" sound before a, o, or u
- D) Doubles the C sound

**Answer: C** — The cédille makes C soft (like "s") before a, o, u. Example: *façon* = "fah-sohn".

---

**Question 3**
How do you pronounce the French word **vous**?

- A) "vooz"
- B) "voo"
- C) "vous" (as written)
- D) "vuh"

**Answer: B** — The final S is silent. *vous* = "voo".

---

**Question 4**
What is the French word for **twenty** (20)?

- A) dix
- B) douze
- C) vingt
- D) trente

**Answer: C** — *vingt* = twenty (pronounced "van").

---

**Question 5**
How do French speakers say **seventy (70)**?

- A) septante
- B) soixante-dix (sixty-ten)
- C) soixante-vingt
- D) sept-dix

**Answer: B** — Standard French uses *soixante-dix* (sixty + ten) for 70.

---

**Question 6**
What does **quatre-vingts** (80) literally mean in English?

- A) Forty plus forty
- B) Eight tens
- C) Four twenties
- D) Eighty ones

**Answer: C** — *quatre-vingts* = 4 × 20 = 80. This vigesimal (base-20) counting is unique to French.

---

**Question 7**
Which greeting is used in the **evening**?

- A) Bonjour
- B) Bonne nuit
- C) Bonsoir
- D) Salut

**Answer: C** — *Bonsoir* = Good evening. *Bonjour* = Good morning/day. *Bonne nuit* = Good night (when going to bed).

---

**Question 8**
What is the correct formal way to say "You're welcome" in French?

- A) Merci
- B) De rien
- C) Pardon
- D) S'il vous plaît

**Answer: B** — *De rien* = You're welcome (literally "it's nothing").

---

**Question 9**
When should you use **vous** instead of **tu** in French?

- A) When speaking to children
- B) When speaking with close friends
- C) When speaking to strangers, elders, or professionals
- D) Only when speaking to groups of people

**Answer: C** — *Vous* is used for strangers, elders, and professionals — similar to آپ in Urdu.

---

**Question 10**
Which sentence correctly asks "What is your name?" in a **formal** context?

- A) Comment tu t'appelles?
- B) Comment vous appelez-vous?
- C) Comment il s'appelle?
- D) Tu t'appelles comment?

**Answer: B** — *Comment vous appelez-vous?* is the formal form.

---

**Question 11**
What does the French word **é** (accent aigu) change about the pronunciation?

- A) Makes the vowel nasal
- B) Produces a closed "ay" sound
- C) Makes the E silent
- D) Produces a deep "oh" sound

**Answer: B** — *é* produces a closed "ay" sound, as in *été* (summer).

---

**Question 12**
Which of the following is a **nasal vowel** sound in French?

- A) The "ee" in *midi*
- B) The "oh" in *mot*
- C) The "ohn" in *bon*
- D) The "ay" in *café*

**Answer: C** — *bon* contains a nasal vowel where air flows through the nose.

---

**Question 13**
How is the French **U** (without accent) different from English "oo"?

- A) It is pronounced the same way
- B) Lips are rounded forward while the tongue position is as if saying "ee"
- C) It is always silent at the end of words
- D) It sounds like the English "uh"

**Answer: B** — The French U requires rounded lips combined with the tongue position of "ee" — a unique sound.

---

**Question 14**
What is the English equivalent of the French word **rendezvous**?

- A) Goodbye
- B) A meeting / appointment
- C) A type of food
- D) A place to sleep

**Answer: B** — *Rendezvous* is used directly in English to mean a planned meeting.

---

**Question 15**
Which of the following is **NOT** a French-English cognate?

- A) communication
- B) excellent
- C) maison
- D) important

**Answer: C** — *Maison* means "house" and has no English equivalent. The others (communication, excellent, important) are direct cognates.

---

**Question 16**
What number does **seize** represent?

- A) 6
- B) 7
- C) 16
- D) 60

**Answer: C** — *seize* = 16 (pronounced "sayz").

---

**Question 17**
What is **Excusez-moi** used for?

- A) Thanking someone
- B) Saying goodbye
- C) Getting someone's attention or apologising
- D) Asking for the time

**Answer: C** — *Excusez-moi* = Excuse me — used to get attention or apologise politely.

---

**Question 18**
In French, the word-ending **-tion** is pronounced as:

- A) "-shun" (like English)
- B) "-syohn"
- C) "-tee-ohn"
- D) Silent

**Answer: B** — French *-tion* = "syohn". Example: *nation* = "nah-syohn".

---

**Question 19**
What is the French word for **yesterday**?

- A) demain
- B) maintenant
- C) aujourd'hui
- D) hier

**Answer: D** — *hier* = yesterday. *demain* = tomorrow. *aujourd'hui* = today.

---

**Question 20**
Which accent mark appears in the word **façade**?

- A) accent aigu (é)
- B) cédille (ç)
- C) accent grave (è)
- D) tréma (ë)

**Answer: B** — The cédille (ç) appears in *façade*, making the C soft before the A.

---

**Question 21**
What phenomenon allows normally silent final consonants to be pronounced when followed by a vowel?

- A) Elision
- B) Liaison
- C) Nasalisation
- D) Guttural projection

**Answer: B** — *Liaison* links a normally-silent final consonant to a following vowel. Example: *les amis* = "lay-za-mee".

---

**Question 22**
How many official letters are in the French alphabet?

- A) 24
- B) 25
- C) 26
- D) 28

**Answer: C** — French uses the same 26 letters as English, plus accent marks.

---

**Question 23**
The French R is best described as:

- A) A rolling R similar to Spanish
- B) Identical to the English R
- C) A guttural sound produced at the back of the throat
- D) Always silent at the end of words

**Answer: C** — The French R is guttural, made at the back of the throat (similar to gargling softly).

---

**Question 24**
What does **À bientôt** mean?

- A) Good evening
- B) Thank you very much
- C) See you soon
- D) You're welcome

**Answer: C** — *À bientôt* = See you soon.

---

**Question 25**
Which word means **"beautiful"** in French (masculine singular form)?

- A) petit
- B) grand
- C) beau
- D) nouveau

**Answer: C** — *beau* = beautiful (masculine). The feminine form is *belle*.

---

**Question 26**
What percentage of English vocabulary is estimated to come from French?

- A) 5–10%
- B) Over 30%
- C) Exactly 50%
- D) About 15%

**Answer: B** — Over 30% of English vocabulary derives from French, mainly due to the Norman Conquest of 1066.

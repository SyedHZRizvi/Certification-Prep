## Answer Key

1. B — PDG = Président-Directeur Général (equivalent of CEO).
2. C — *Chiffre d'affaires* = turnover / total revenue.
3. B — *Je déclare la séance ouverte* is the correct formal opening.
4. C — *Ordre du jour* = agenda.
5. B — *Je vous donne la parole* = I give you the floor.
6. B — *Démontrer / illustrer* is the formal equivalent of *montrer*.
7. B — *Compte rendu* = meeting minutes or a written report.
8. B — Uses formal register: *il convient d'apporter des modifications*.
9. A — *En ce qui concerne* = concerning / with regard to.
10. C — *Filiale* = subsidiary company.
11. B — *Nous sommes parvenus à un accord* = we have reached an agreement.
12. C — *Fournisseur* = supplier.
13. C — *Lever la séance* = to close the meeting.
14. C — *Délai* = deadline or timeframe (not just "delay").
15. B — *Nous vous prions de bien vouloir...* = we kindly request that you...
16. D — *Actuel* means "current" (not "actual" — that's *réel / effectif*).
17. C — *À l'unanimité* = unanimously.
18. B — Uses formal business language (*suite à nos analyses*).
19. C — *Mettre aux voix* = to put to a vote.
20. B — *Un devis* = a quote or estimate (before work is done).
21. A — *Ressources Humaines* is the correct French term for HR.
22. B — *Cadre* = an executive or manager in a company.
23. B — *Il convient de souligner que* = it is appropriate to emphasise that.
24. C — *Procès-verbal* = official minutes of a meeting.
25. B — This phrase requests a review of contract conditions/terms.

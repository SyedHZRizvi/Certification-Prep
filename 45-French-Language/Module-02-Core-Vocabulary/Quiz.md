# Module 2 — Le Vocabulaire Essentiel · Quiz (26 Questions)

---

**Question 1**
What is the French word for "mother"?

- A) la femme
- B) la mère
- C) la sœur
- D) la fille

**Answer: B** — *la mère* = mother. *la femme* = wife/woman, *la sœur* = sister, *la fille* = daughter/girl.

---

**Question 2**
Which article should you use with the noun *pain* (bread)?

- A) la
- B) une
- C) le
- D) les

**Answer: C** — *le pain* = the bread (masculine). Bread is masculine in French.

---

**Question 3**
What does *la baguette* refer to?

- A) A type of French soup
- B) A French long bread loaf
- C) A French cheese
- D) A morning pastry with layers

**Answer: B** — *la baguette* is the iconic long French bread loaf, a symbol of French culture.

---

**Question 4**
The French word *poisson* means:

- A) Poison
- B) Chicken
- C) Fish
- D) Meat

**Answer: C** — *le poisson* = fish. This is a famous "false friend" — it does NOT mean poison!

---

**Question 5**
Which word means "grandmother"?

- A) la tante
- B) la cousine
- C) la grand-mère
- D) la mère

**Answer: C** — *la grand-mère* = grandmother. *la tante* = aunt, *la cousine* = female cousin.

---

**Question 6**
What gender is the word *cuisine* (kitchen)?

- A) Masculine
- B) Feminine
- C) Neutral
- D) It changes depending on context

**Answer: B** — *la cuisine* is feminine. In French, all nouns have fixed gender.

---

**Question 7**
Which of the following means "to understand"?

- A) entendre
- B) comprendre
- C) répondre
- D) demander

**Answer: B** — *comprendre* = to understand. *entendre* = to hear, *répondre* = to answer, *demander* = to ask.

---

**Question 8**
What is the French word for "breakfast"?

- A) le dîner
- B) le déjeuner
- C) le petit-déjeuner
- D) le repas

**Answer: C** — *le petit-déjeuner* = breakfast (literally "the little lunch"). *le déjeuner* = lunch, *le dîner* = dinner.

---

**Question 9**
How do you say "the eyes" in French?

- A) les oreilles
- B) les yeux
- C) les dents
- D) les mains

**Answer: B** — *les yeux* = the eyes (plural of *l'œil*). Note: *les yeux* is an irregular plural.

---

**Question 10**
Which word means "happy"?

- A) triste
- B) pauvre
- C) heureux
- D) faible

**Answer: C** — *heureux* (m) / *heureuse* (f) = happy. *triste* = sad, *pauvre* = poor, *faible* = weak.

---

**Question 11**
What is the feminine form of the adjective *grand*?

- A) grands
- B) grande
- C) gran
- D) grandesse

**Answer: B** — *grande* is the feminine form. *grands* is the masculine plural.

---

**Question 12**
Which meal is *le dîner*?

- A) Breakfast
- B) Lunch
- C) Dinner
- D) Afternoon snack

**Answer: C** — *le dîner* = dinner (evening meal).

---

**Question 13**
What does *toujours* mean?

- A) Never
- B) Sometimes
- C) Already
- D) Always

**Answer: D** — *toujours* = always. *jamais* = never, *parfois* = sometimes, *déjà* = already.

---

**Question 14**
Which body part is *la bouche*?

- A) The ear
- B) The nose
- C) The mouth
- D) The eye

**Answer: C** — *la bouche* = the mouth.

---

**Question 15**
The French word *demain* means:

- A) Yesterday
- B) Today
- C) Tomorrow
- D) Morning

**Answer: C** — *demain* = tomorrow. *hier* = yesterday, *aujourd'hui* = today, *ce matin* = this morning.

---

**Question 16**
What does *travailler* mean?

- A) To travel
- B) To work
- C) To try
- D) To walk

**Answer: B** — *travailler* = to work. *voyager* = to travel, *marcher* = to walk.

---

**Question 17**
Which word means "bedroom"?

- A) la cuisine
- B) le salon
- C) la chambre
- D) la salle de bain

**Answer: C** — *la chambre* = bedroom. *la cuisine* = kitchen, *le salon* = living room, *la salle de bain* = bathroom.

---

**Question 18**
What does *boire* mean?

- A) To eat
- B) To drink
- C) To cook
- D) To buy

**Answer: B** — *boire* = to drink. *manger* = to eat, *acheter* = to buy.

---

**Question 19**
In French, days of the week begin with which day?

- A) dimanche (Sunday)
- B) samedi (Saturday)
- C) lundi (Monday)
- D) mardi (Tuesday)

**Answer: C** — The French week begins with *lundi* (Monday). Note: days are NOT capitalised in French.

---

**Question 20**
What is the correct article for *appartement* (apartment)?

- A) la
- B) une
- C) le
- D) les

**Answer: C** — *l'appartement* (the apartment) is masculine, so it takes *le* (written *l'* before a vowel).

---

**Question 21**
Which of the following is the French word for "husband"?

- A) le frère
- B) le mari
- C) le père
- D) le fils

**Answer: B** — *le mari* = husband. *le frère* = brother, *le père* = father, *le fils* = son.

---

**Question 22**
What does *chercher* mean?

- A) To find
- B) To look for / search
- C) To choose
- D) To carry

**Answer: B** — *chercher* = to look for / to search. *trouver* = to find.

---

**Question 23**
The adjective pair *chaud / froid* means:

- A) Big / Small
- B) Good / Bad
- C) Hot / Cold
- D) Fast / Slow

**Answer: C** — *chaud* (m) / *chaude* (f) = hot; *froid* (m) / *froide* (f) = cold.

---

**Question 24**
What is the French word for "cheese"?

- A) le beurre
- B) le lait
- C) le fromage
- D) l'œuf

**Answer: C** — *le fromage* = cheese. *le beurre* = butter, *le lait* = milk, *l'œuf* = egg.

---

**Question 25**
Which word means "never"?

- A) toujours
- B) souvent
- C) jamais
- D) encore

**Answer: C** — *jamais* = never. *toujours* = always, *souvent* = often, *encore* = still/again.

---

**Question 26**
What is the feminine form of *vieux* (old)?

- A) vieille
- B) vieux
- C) vieuse
- D) vieillie

**Answer: A** — *vieille* is the feminine singular form of *vieux* (old). Note: *vieil* is used before masculine nouns beginning with a vowel.

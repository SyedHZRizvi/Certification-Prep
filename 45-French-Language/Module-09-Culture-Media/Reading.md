## 1. French Idiomatic Expressions

A language's soul lives in its idioms. When a French person says *il pleut des cordes* (it's raining ropes — pouring with rain) or *casser les pieds à quelqu'un* (to break someone's feet — to annoy someone), the literal meaning is meaningless. Understanding idioms is the threshold between competent and truly fluent French.

### Common French Idioms with Translations

| Idiom | Literal meaning | Actual meaning | اردو |
|---|---|---|---|
| il pleut des cordes | it's raining ropes | it's pouring / raining cats and dogs | موسلادھار بارش ہو رہی ہے |
| casser les pieds | to break the feet | to annoy / bore someone | تنگ کرنا |
| avoir le cafard | to have the cockroach | to feel down / depressed | افسردہ ہونا |
| poser un lapin | to leave a rabbit | to stand someone up | کسی کو انتظار میں چھوڑ دینا |
| prendre la mouche | to take the fly | to take offence easily | جلدی ناراض ہو جانا |
| donner sa langue au chat | to give one's tongue to the cat | to give up (guessing) | ہار مان لینا |
| avoir d'autres chats à fouetter | to have other cats to whip | to have bigger fish to fry | اور کام ہیں |
| revenons à nos moutons | let's return to our sheep | let's get back to the point | موضوع پر واپس آتے ہیں |
| il ne faut pas vendre la peau de l'ours avant de l'avoir tué | don't sell the bear's skin before killing it | don't count your chickens | وقت سے پہلے خوشی نہ مناؤ |
| coûter les yeux de la tête | to cost the eyes of the head | to cost an arm and a leg | بہت مہنگا ہونا |

> **English-speaker insight:** French idioms often involve animals (*chat* [cat], *mouton* [sheep], *lapin* [rabbit]) and body parts (*les pieds*, *les yeux*, *la tête*). This is similar to English, which also favours animal idioms (*raining cats and dogs*, *let the cat out of the bag*). Make connections to remember them.

## 2. French Literature — Key Authors and Movements

French literature is one of the richest in the world. At C1 level, understanding references to major authors and movements enriches your comprehension of French cultural life.

### Major French Media Outlets

| Media | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Le Monde | newspaper | centre-left quality daily |
| Le Figaro | newspaper | centre-right quality daily |
| Libération | newspaper | left-leaning daily |

| France Inter | radio | public radio (news/culture) |

### Journalistic Language Patterns

French journalism uses a distinctive style. Key patterns:

| Feature | Example | English |
|---|---|---|
| Past historic (literary past) | *il déclara que...* | he declared that... |
| Passive constructions | *une décision a été prise* | a decision was made |
| Nominalisation | *la hausse des prix* | the rise in prices |
| Conditional for unverified news | *il aurait démissionné* | he is said to have resigned |

> **Advantage for English speakers:** French journalism uses many Latin-origin words that have English cognates: *inflation* (inflation), *réforme* (reform), *manifestation* (demonstration), *économie* (economy). Newspaper reading becomes surprisingly accessible.

## 4. Sample Dialogue — Discussing Culture

### Dialogue: A Literary Debate

**Alice:** Avez-vous lu *L'Étranger* de Camus? C'est selon moi l'un des romans les plus importants du vingtième siècle.

**Mohammed:** Oui, je l'ai lu au lycée. Ce qui me fascine, c'est la façon dont Camus explore l'absurdité de l'existence humaine. Revenons à nos moutons — tu disais que tu voulais discuter des médias aussi?

**Alice:** Exactement. J'ai l'impression que la presse écrite perd du terrain face aux réseaux sociaux. Pourtant, les grands quotidiens comme *Le Monde* gardent une crédibilité que les médias en ligne n'ont pas encore acquise.

**Mohammed:** Tu as raison sur ce point. D'ailleurs, il ne faut pas vendre la peau de l'ours — les journaux papier ont souvent annoncé leur propre mort, mais ils sont toujours là.

**Translation:**
Alice: Have you read Camus's *The Stranger*? It is in my opinion one of the most important novels of the twentieth century.
Mohammed: Yes, I read it at secondary school. What fascinates me is the way Camus explores the absurdity of human existence. Let's get back to the point — you were saying you also wanted to discuss the media?
Alice: Exactly. I have the feeling that the written press is losing ground to social networks. Yet major dailies like *Le Monde* retain a credibility that online media have not yet acquired.
Mohammed: You're right on that point. Besides, don't count your chickens — newspapers have often announced their own death, but they are still here.

## Answer Key

1. B — *Avoir le cafard* = to feel down/depressed.
2. C — *Poser un lapin* = to stand someone up (not appear at an appointment).
3. C — *Revenons à nos moutons* = let's get back to the main point.
4. B — *Coûter les yeux de la tête* = to cost a fortune.
5. C — Voltaire and Rousseau belong to the *Lumières* (Enlightenment).
6. C — Victor Hugo wrote *Les Misérables*.
7. C — *L'Étranger* by Camus is an Existentialist novel.
8. B — *Madame Bovary* was written by Gustave Flaubert.
9. D — *Gros titre* = headline.
10. B — *La une* = the front page of a newspaper.
11. C — *Le Monde* is the centre-left quality daily.
12. C — France 24 is an international French-language TV news channel.
13. B — *Arte* is the Franco-German cultural television channel.
14. C — The conditional in French journalism signals unverified information.
15. C — *Donner sa langue au chat* = to give up on guessing.
16. B — Voltaire wrote *Candide* during the Enlightenment.
17. B — *Hebdomadaire* = weekly publication.
18. B — *Prendre la mouche* = to take offence easily.
19. C — Classicisme and Lumières preceded Romantisme chronologically.
20. B — *Sondage* = a poll or survey.
21. C — Robbe-Grillet is a key figure of the *Nouveau Roman*.
22. B — *Avoir d'autres chats à fouetter* = to have more important things to do (bigger fish to fry).
23. B — France Inter is a public national radio station.
24. C — *Éditorial* = the editor's opinion piece.
25. B — The French bear-skin idiom = "Don't count your chickens before they hatch."
26. C — Zola is associated with Réalisme and Naturalisme.
27. B — *Chronique* = a regular column or feature in a newspaper.

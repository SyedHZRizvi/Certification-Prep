# Module 3 — La Grammaire de Base · Quiz (26 Questions)

---

**Question 1**
What is the correct form of *être* for the subject *nous*?

- A) êtes
- B) est
- C) sommes
- D) sont

**Answer: C** — *nous sommes* = we are.

---

**Question 2**
Which sentence correctly says "I am a student" in French?

- A) Je suis un étudiant.
- B) Je suis étudiant.
- C) J'ai un étudiant.
- D) Je suis être étudiant.

**Answer: B** — After *être*, professions do NOT use an article: *Je suis étudiant* (NOT *un étudiant*).

---

**Question 3**
What does *J'ai faim* literally translate to?

- A) I am eating
- B) I have hunger
- C) I want food
- D) I need lunch

**Answer: B** — *J'ai faim* literally means "I have hunger" but translates as "I am hungry". French uses *avoir* for these states.

---

**Question 4**
How do you conjugate *parler* for *vous*?

- A) vous parle
- B) vous parlons
- C) vous parlez
- D) vous parlent

**Answer: C** — *vous parlez*. The *-ez* ending is characteristic of *vous* for all regular -ER verbs.

---

**Question 5**
Which is the correct article for *eau* (water, feminine, starts with vowel)?

- A) la eau
- B) le eau
- C) l'eau
- D) une eau

**Answer: C** — *l'eau* — before a vowel, *la* becomes *l'* by elision.

---

**Question 6**
What is the negative form of *Il mange des croissants*?

- A) Il ne mange pas des croissants.
- B) Il mange pas des croissants.
- C) Il ne mange pas de croissants.
- D) Il ne pas mange croissants.

**Answer: C** — After negation, *des* changes to *de*: *Il ne mange pas de croissants.*

---

**Question 7**
Which of the following is the correct feminine form of *beau*?

- A) beau
- B) belle
- C) beaux
- D) belles

**Answer: B** — *belle* is the feminine singular form of *beau* (beautiful). *belles* is feminine plural.

---

**Question 8**
How do you say "they have" in French when the group is all female?

- A) ils ont
- B) elles sont
- C) elles ont
- D) ils sont

**Answer: C** — *elles ont* = they have (all female). *elles sont* = they are.

---

**Question 9**
Which sentence uses the BAGS rule to put the adjective BEFORE the noun?

- A) une voiture rouge
- B) un homme intelligent
- C) une petite maison
- D) une chemise bleue

**Answer: C** — *petite* (small) is a Size adjective → goes before the noun. The others (rouge, intelligent, bleue) come after.

---

**Question 10**
What does *ne...jamais* mean?

- A) No longer
- B) Not yet
- C) Never
- D) Nobody

**Answer: C** — *ne...jamais* = never. *ne...plus* = no longer.

---

**Question 11**
Which is the most formal way to ask "Do you speak French?"

- A) Tu parles français?
- B) Est-ce que tu parles français?
- C) Parlez-vous français?
- D) Vous parlez français?

**Answer: C** — Inversion (*Parlez-vous*) is the most formal question form, used in written French and formal speech.

---

**Question 12**
What is the subject pronoun used informally to mean "we" in spoken French?

- A) nous
- B) vous
- C) on
- D) ils

**Answer: C** — *on* is used to mean "we" in everyday spoken French. *Nous* is more formal.

---

**Question 13**
How do you conjugate *avoir* for *je*?

- A) j'es
- B) j'ai
- C) j'est
- D) j'a

**Answer: B** — *j'ai* = I have.

---

**Question 14**
The adjective *facile* (easy) — what is its feminine singular form?

- A) faciles
- B) facilee
- C) facilé
- D) facile

**Answer: D** — *facile* already ends in -e, so the feminine form is the same: *facile*.

---

**Question 15**
Which question word means "how much / how many"?

- A) Comment
- B) Combien
- C) Quel
- D) Pourquoi

**Answer: B** — *Combien* = how much / how many. *Comment* = how.

---

**Question 16**
What does *Vous êtes en retard* mean?

- A) You are ready.
- B) You are late.
- C) You are correct.
- D) You are tired.

**Answer: B** — *en retard* = late. *Vous êtes en retard* = You are late.

---

**Question 17**
How do you say "some books" (plural, indefinite)?

- A) le livre
- B) un livre
- C) les livres
- D) des livres

**Answer: D** — *des livres* = some books (plural indefinite article).

---

**Question 18**
In the sentence *Ma sœur est grande*, what form of *grand* is used and why?

- A) grande — because sœur is feminine
- B) grande — because it's a proper noun
- C) grand — because sœur is masculine
- D) grands — because it's a plural

**Answer: A** — *grande* is the feminine singular form, agreeing with *sœur* (feminine).

---

**Question 19**
What form does *ne* take before a vowel?

- A) ne stays as ne
- B) n'
- C) pas
- D) ni

**Answer: B** — Before a vowel, *ne* becomes *n'* by elision: *Je n'ai pas* (not *Je ne ai pas*).

---

**Question 20**
Which of the following is an *avoir* idiom meaning "to be right"?

- A) avoir froid
- B) avoir raison
- C) avoir peur
- D) avoir faim

**Answer: B** — *avoir raison* = to be right. *avoir tort* = to be wrong.

---

**Question 21**
What is the correct conjugation of *manger* (to eat) for *nous*?

- A) nous mangons
- B) nous mangeons
- C) nous mangez
- D) nous mangent

**Answer: B** — *nous mangeons* — an extra *e* is inserted before *-ons* to preserve the soft G sound.

---

**Question 22**
Which article replaces *un/une* after negation?

- A) les
- B) des
- C) de / d'
- D) le / la

**Answer: C** — After *ne...pas*, *un/une/des* all change to *de* (or *d'* before a vowel): *Je n'ai pas de chien.*

---

**Question 23**
What does *Est-ce que vous habitez à Paris?* mean?

- A) Is Paris nice?
- B) Do you live in Paris?
- C) Are you visiting Paris?
- D) Did you leave Paris?

**Answer: B** — *Habitez-vous à Paris?* / *Est-ce que vous habitez à Paris?* = Do you live in Paris?

---

**Question 24**
Which form of *être* is used with *ils* (they, masculine plural)?

- A) êtes
- B) est
- C) sont
- D) sommes

**Answer: C** — *ils sont* = they are.

---

**Question 25**
The pronoun *elles* refers to:

- A) Any group of people
- B) A group of males
- C) A mixed group of males and females
- D) A group that is entirely female

**Answer: D** — *elles* is used only for all-female groups. Even one male in the group makes it *ils*.

---

**Question 26**
How do you make *Je parle anglais* negative?

- A) Je ne parle anglais pas.
- B) Je pas parle anglais.
- C) Je ne parle pas anglais.
- D) Je parle ne pas anglais.

**Answer: C** — The ne...pas sandwich: *Je ne parle pas anglais.* The verb is wrapped between ne and pas.

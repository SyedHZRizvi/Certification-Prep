## 1. Gender: Every French Noun Has a Gender

This is the most fundamental rule of French grammar: **every single noun is either masculine or feminine**. There are no exceptions, no neuter nouns, no "it" category for objects.

- *le soleil* (the sun) — masculine
- *la lune* (the moon) — feminine
- *le livre* (the book) — masculine
- *la maison* (the house) — feminine
- *le problème* (the problem) — masculine (careful: -ème endings are often masculine!)
- *la solution* (the solution) — feminine (-tion endings are almost always feminine)

Why does this matter? Because:
1. The article changes (le vs la)
2. Adjectives must match the noun's gender
3. Some past participles must agree with gender
4. Pronouns must match the noun's gender

**You must memorise the gender of every noun you learn.** Treat the article as part of the word itself.

---

## 2. Articles (Les Articles)

### Definite Articles (The)
Use these when referring to a specific noun.

| | Masculine | Feminine | Before vowel/h |
|---|---|---|---|
| Singular | **le** | **la** | **l'** |
| Plural | **les** | **les** | **les** |

Examples:
- *le garçon* (the boy) → *les garçons* (the boys)
- *la fille* (the girl) → *les filles* (the girls)
- *l'homme* (the man) — l' replaces *le* before a vowel
- *l'eau* (the water) — l' replaces *la* before a vowel

### Indefinite Articles (A / An / Some)
Use these when referring to a non-specific noun.

| | Masculine | Feminine | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|
| | **un** | **une** | **des** |

Examples:
- *un café* (a coffee)
- *une maison* (a house)
- *des livres* (some books)
- *des amis* (some friends)

### After Negation — Articles Change!
After *ne...pas*, **un/une/des** change to **de (d' before vowel)**:
- *J'ai un livre.* (I have a book.) → *Je n'ai pas de livre.* (I don't have a book.)
- *Il mange des croissants.* → *Il ne mange pas de croissants.*

## 3. Subject Pronouns (Les Pronoms Sujets)

| French | English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| je (j' before vowel) | I | |
| tu | you (singular, informal) | Use with friends/family |
| il | he / it (m) | |
| elle | she / it (f) | |
| on | one / we (informal) | Very common in spoken French |
| nous | we | More formal than *on* |
| vous | you (formal or plural) | Formal singular OR any plural |
| ils | they (m or mixed) | Used when group has any males |
| elles | they (f only) | Used only for all-female groups |

**Important:** *on* (one / we) is used far more frequently than *nous* in everyday spoken French. *On mange.* = We eat. / One eats.

## 4. ÊTRE (To Be) — Memorise This Immediately

*Être* is the most important verb in French. You will use it every single day.

| Pronoun | Être | English |
|---|---|---|
| je | **suis** | I am |
| tu | **es** | you are |
| il / elle / on | **est** | he/she/it is / we are |
| nous | **sommes** | we are |
| vous | **êtes** | you are (formal/plural) |
| ils / elles | **sont** | they are |

### Using Être — Key Examples
- *Je suis étudiant.* — I am a student.
- *Tu es français?* — Are you French?
- *Il est grand.* — He is tall.
- *Elle est professeure.* — She is a teacher.
- *Nous sommes à Paris.* — We are in Paris.
- *Vous êtes en retard!* — You are late!
- *Ils sont intelligents.* — They are intelligent.

> **Note:** In French, you do NOT use an article before professions after *être*: *Je suis médecin* (I am a doctor) — NOT *Je suis un médecin*.

---

## 5. AVOIR (To Have) — Memorise This Too

*Avoir* is the second most important verb. It also serves as the auxiliary (helping) verb for most compound tenses.

| Pronoun | Avoir | English |
|---|---|---|
| je | **ai** | I have |
| tu | **as** | you have |
| il / elle / on | **a** | he/she/it has / we have |
| nous | **avons** | we have |
| vous | **avez** | you have |
| ils / elles | **ont** | they have |

### Using Avoir — Key Examples
- *J'ai faim.* — I am hungry. (Lit: I have hunger)
- *Tu as soif?* — Are you thirsty? (Lit: Do you have thirst?)
- *Il a vingt ans.* — He is twenty years old. (Lit: He has 20 years)
- *Elle a un chien.* — She has a dog.
- *Nous avons une voiture.* — We have a car.
- *Vous avez des enfants?* — Do you have children?
- *Ils ont de la chance.* — They are lucky. (Lit: They have luck)

> **Avoir idioms** — French uses *avoir* (not être) for many states: *avoir faim* (to be hungry), *avoir soif* (to be thirsty), *avoir chaud* (to be hot), *avoir froid* (to be cold), *avoir peur* (to be afraid), *avoir raison* (to be right), *avoir tort* (to be wrong), *avoir l'air* (to seem/look like), *avoir besoin de* (to need).

## 6. Regular -ER Verbs: The Parler Pattern

The majority of French verbs end in **-ER** and follow a predictable pattern. Learn this once and you can conjugate hundreds of verbs.

**Step:** Remove -ER from the infinitive → add the appropriate ending.

| Pronoun | Ending | Parler (to speak) |
|---|---|---|
| je | **-e** | je parle |
| tu | **-es** | tu parles |
| il/elle/on | **-e** | il parle |
| nous | **-ons** | nous parlons |
| vous | **-ez** | vous parlez |
| ils/elles | **-ent** | ils parlent |

> **Key:** The endings -e, -es, -ent are all **silent** in pronunciation! *parle, parles, parlent* all sound like "PARL".

### Apply the Pattern to Other -ER Verbs
| Verb | je | nous | ils |
|---|---|---|---|
| aimer (to like/love) | j'aime | nous aimons | ils aiment |
| manger (to eat) | je mange | nous mangeons* | ils mangent |
| habiter (to live) | j'habite | nous habitons | ils habitent |
| travailler (to work) | je travaille | nous travaillons | ils travaillent |
| regarder (to watch) | je regarde | nous regardons | ils regardent |
| écouter (to listen) | j'écoute | nous écoutons | ils écoutent |
| parler (to speak) | je parle | nous parlons | ils parlent |
| chercher (to look for) | je cherche | nous cherchons | ils cherchent |

*Manger adds an E before -ons to preserve the soft G sound: *mangeons*.

## 7. Adjective Agreement

In French, adjectives must agree in **gender and number** with the noun they describe. This means every adjective has up to 4 forms.

### Regular Adjective Agreement Rules
| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---|---|---|
| Singular | Add nothing | Add **-e** |
| Plural | Add **-s** | Add **-es** |

| Form | Example (petit/petite — small) |
|---|---|
| Masculine singular | un garçon **petit** |
| Feminine singular | une fille **petite** |
| Masculine plural | des garçons **petits** |
| Feminine plural | des filles **petites** |

### Adjectives That Already End in -E
If the masculine form already ends in -e, the feminine is **the same**:
- *facile → facile* (easy): *un exercice facile / une leçon facile*
- *triste → triste* (sad): *un homme triste / une femme triste*

### Irregular Adjective Forms
| Masculine | Fem. Singular | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| bon | bonne | good |
| mauvais | mauvaise | bad |
| heureux | heureuse | happy |
| beau (bel*) | belle | beautiful |
| nouveau (nouvel*) | nouvelle | new |
| vieux (vieil*) | vieille | old |
| long | longue | long |
| blanc | blanche | white |

*Before masculine nouns starting with a vowel or silent H.

### Position of Adjectives
Most French adjectives come **AFTER** the noun:
- *une voiture rouge* (a red car)
- *un homme intelligent* (an intelligent man)

A small group of common adjectives come **BEFORE** the noun (remember: **BAGS** — Beauty, Age, Goodness, Size):
- *un beau jardin* (a beautiful garden)
- *une jeune femme* (a young woman)
- *un bon repas* (a good meal)
- *un grand bâtiment* (a large building)
- *une petite maison* (a small house)
- *une nouvelle voiture* (a new car)

---

## 8. Negation: Ne...Pas (The Sandwich Rule)

To make a sentence negative in French, wrap **ne...pas** around the conjugated verb — like a sandwich:

**[Subject] + ne + [verb] + pas + [rest of sentence]**

| Positive | Negative |
|---|---|
| Je parle français. | Je **ne** parle **pas** français. |
| Tu as un chien. | Tu **n'**as **pas** de chien. |
| Il mange des croissants. | Il **ne** mange **pas** de croissants. |
| Nous sommes français. | Nous **ne** sommes **pas** français. |
| Elles ont faim. | Elles **n'**ont **pas** faim. |

> **Note:** *ne* becomes *n'* before a vowel. In spoken French, the *ne* is often dropped: *Je parle pas français* (informal spoken only).

### Other Negative Expressions (Same Sandwich Rule)
| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| ne...jamais | never | Je ne mange jamais de viande. |
| ne...rien | nothing | Il ne dit rien. |
| ne...personne | nobody | Je ne vois personne. |
| ne...plus | no longer | Elle ne travaille plus ici. |
| ne...que | only | Je n'ai que dix euros. |

---

## 9. Question Formation

French has three ways to ask questions:

### Method 1: Intonation (Informal — Most Common in Speech)
Simply raise your voice at the end of a statement:
- *Tu parles français?* (You speak French?)
- *Il est à Paris?* (He is in Paris?)

### Method 2: Est-ce que... (Standard — Safe for All Situations)
Add *est-ce que* at the beginning:
- *Est-ce que tu parles français?* (Do you speak French?)
- *Est-ce qu'il est à Paris?* (Is he in Paris?)

### Method 3: Inversion (Formal — Written French)
Invert subject and verb, connect with hyphen:
- *Parles-tu français?* (Do you speak French?)
- *Est-il à Paris?* (Is he in Paris?)
- *Avez-vous des enfants?* (Do you have children?)

### Question Words (Mots Interrogatifs)
| French | English | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Qui | Who | Qui est-ce? (Who is it?) |
| Que / Qu'est-ce que | What | Qu'est-ce que tu fais? (What are you doing?) |
| Où | Where | Où habitez-vous? (Where do you live?) |
| Quand | When | Quand est-ce que vous arrivez? (When do you arrive?) |
| Comment | How | Comment allez-vous? (How are you?) |
| Pourquoi | Why | Pourquoi est-ce que tu pleurs? (Why are you crying?) |
| Combien | How much/many | Combien ça coûte? (How much does it cost?) |
| Quel / Quelle | Which / What | Quel est votre prénom? (What is your first name?) |

---

## 10. Putting It All Together — Example Sentences

### With être:
- *Je suis anglais et j'habite à Londres.* — I am English and I live in London.
- *Elle n'est pas fatiguée.* — She is not tired.
- *Êtes-vous professeur?* — Are you a teacher?

### With avoir:
- *Nous avons deux enfants.* — We have two children.
- *Est-ce que tu as faim?* — Are you hungry?
- *Je n'ai pas de voiture.* — I don't have a car.

### With -ER verbs:
- *Vous parlez très bien français!* — You speak French very well!
- *Est-ce qu'ils habitent à Paris?* — Do they live in Paris?
- *Je ne mange pas de viande.* — I don't eat meat.

### With adjective agreement:
- *Ma sœur est grande et belle.* — My sister is tall and beautiful.
- *Ce sont des exercices faciles.* — These are easy exercises.
- *J'ai une nouvelle voiture rouge.* — I have a new red car.

## ۱. صنف: ہر فرانسیسی اسم کی ایک صنف ہے

فرانسیسی گرائمر کا سب سے بنیادی اصول: **ہر اسم یا مذکر ہے یا مؤنث** — کوئی استثنا نہیں۔ یہ بالکل اردو اور عربی کی طرح ہے لیکن فرانسیسی میں صنف مختلف ہو سکتی ہے۔

- *le soleil* (سورج) — مذکر
- *la lune* (چاند) — مؤنث
- *le livre* (کتاب) — مذکر
- *la maison* (گھر) — مؤنث

صنف کیوں اہم ہے؟ کیونکہ اس سے article، صفت اور ضمیر سب بدل جاتے ہیں۔ **ہر اسم اپنے article کے ساتھ یاد کریں۔**

---

## ۲. Articles

### معین Articles (The / یہ / وہ مخصوص)
| | مذکر | مؤنث | vowel/h سے پہلے |
|---|---|---|---|
| واحد | **le** | **la** | **l'** |
| جمع | **les** | **les** | **les** |

### غیر معین Articles (A/An)
| | مذکر | مؤنث | جمع |
|---|---|---|---|
| | **un** | **une** | **des** |

**نفی کے بعد:** un/une/des بدل کر **de** ہو جاتا ہے:
- *J'ai un livre.* ← *Je n'ai pas de livre.* (میرے پاس کتاب نہیں)

## ۳. فاعلی ضمائر (Subject Pronouns)

| فرانسیسی | اردو |
|---|---|
| je / j' | میں |
| tu | تم (غیر رسمی) |
| il / elle | وہ (مذکر / مؤنث) |
| on | ہم (بول چال میں) |
| nous | ہم (رسمی) |
| vous | آپ (رسمی / جمع) |
| ils / elles | وہ لوگ (مذکر / مؤنث) |

**اہم:** بول چال میں *on* عام طور پر *nous* کی جگہ استعمال ہوتا ہے۔ *On mange.* = ہم کھاتے ہیں۔

## ۴. ÊTRE (ہونا) — یاد کریں!

| ضمیر | فعل | اردو |
|---|---|---|
| je | **suis** | میں ہوں |
| tu | **es** | تم ہو |
| il/elle/on | **est** | وہ ہے / ہم ہیں |
| nous | **sommes** | ہم ہیں |
| vous | **êtes** | آپ ہیں |
| ils/elles | **sont** | وہ ہیں |

**مثالیں:**
- *Je suis étudiant.* — میں طالب علم ہوں۔
- *Nous sommes à Paris.* — ہم پیرس میں ہیں۔
- *Vous êtes en retard!* — آپ دیر سے آئے ہیں!

---

## ۵. AVOIR (رکھنا / ہونا) — یاد کریں!

| ضمیر | فعل | اردو |
|---|---|---|
| je | **ai** | میرے پاس ہے / میں رکھتا ہوں |
| tu | **as** | تمہارے پاس ہے |
| il/elle/on | **a** | اس کے پاس ہے |
| nous | **avons** | ہمارے پاس ہے |
| vous | **avez** | آپ کے پاس ہے |
| ils/elles | **ont** | ان کے پاس ہے |

**Avoir کے اہم محاورے:**
- *J'ai faim.* — مجھے بھوک ہے
- *Tu as soif?* — کیا تمہیں پیاس ہے؟
- *Il a vingt ans.* — وہ بیس سال کا ہے

---

## ۶. باقاعدہ -ER افعال: Parler کا نمونہ

-ER ختم ہونے والے افعال سب سے زیادہ عام ہیں اور ان کا ایک ہی نمونہ ہے:

| ضمیر | لاحقہ | parler (بولنا) |
|---|---|---|
| je | **-e** | je parle |
| tu | **-es** | tu parles |
| il/elle/on | **-e** | il parle |
| nous | **-ons** | nous parlons |
| vous | **-ez** | vous parlez |
| ils/elles | **-ent** | ils parlent |

**یاد رکھیں:** je/tu/il/ils کے لاحقے (-e, -es, -ent) خاموش ہیں — سب "PARL" کی طرح سنائی دیتے ہیں!

## ۷. صفت کا مطابقت (Adjective Agreement)

فرانسیسی میں صفت کو اسم کی صنف اور عدد کے ساتھ ملانا ضروری ہے:

| | مذکر | مؤنث |
|---|---|---|
| واحد | کچھ نہ بڑھائیں | **-e** لگائیں |
| جمع | **-s** لگائیں | **-es** لگائیں |

**مثال (petit = چھوٹا):**
- مذکر واحد: *un garçon petit*
- مؤنث واحد: *une fille petite*
- مذکر جمع: *des garçons petits*
- مؤنث جمع: *des filles petites*

**صفت کی جگہ:** اکثر اسم کے بعد آتی ہے: *une voiture rouge* (سرخ گاڑی)۔ لیکن کچھ عام صفتیں پہلے آتی ہیں: *grand, petit, bon, beau, jeune, vieux, nouveau*۔

---

## ۸. نفی: Ne...Pas (سینڈوچ اصول)

فرانسیسی میں "نہیں" کہنے کے لیے فعل کو **ne...pas** کے درمیان رکھیں:

**[فاعل] + ne + [فعل] + pas**

| مثبت | منفی |
|---|---|
| Je parle français. | Je **ne** parle **pas** français. |
| Tu as un chien. | Tu **n'**as **pas** de chien. |
| Il mange des croissants. | Il **ne** mange **pas** de croissants. |

---

## ۹. سوال بنانا

فرانسیسی میں سوال تین طریقوں سے بنتا ہے:

**۱. آواز اٹھانا (غیر رسمی):**
*Tu parles français?* ← آخر میں آواز اٹھائیں

**۲. Est-ce que (معیاری):**
*Est-ce que tu parles français?*

**۳. Inversion (رسمی):**
*Parles-tu français?*

**سوالیہ الفاظ:**
- *Qui* = کون | *Où* = کہاں | *Quand* = کب
- *Comment* = کیسے | *Pourquoi* = کیوں | *Combien* = کتنا/کتنے

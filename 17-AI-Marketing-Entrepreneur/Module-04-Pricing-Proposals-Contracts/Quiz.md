## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. Price the outcome to the buyer, not your delivery time**
The outcome-not-time mindset is the central pricing pivot.

### Q2: **C. Good-better-best proposal with value-based positioning**
Same Lena, same scope; the proposal structure + ROI projection moved the buyer from gut number to value choice.

### Q3: **C. 60–70%**
The classic decoy-pricing pattern, buyers gravitate to the middle option.

### Q4: **C. 2.5–3x Option A**
Designed as the anchor, makes B feel reasonable.

### Q5: **B. Quoting a price on the call**
Founders who quote on calls quote low. Always send a proposal.

### Q6: **B. Anchor the buyer on value far exceeding price**
The ROI projection turns price from "cost" into "ROI."

### Q7: **C. 15–25% of revenue**
Standard B2B SaaS marketing budget range; 15–35% of that goes to outside agencies.

### Q8: **B. MSA captures standing legal terms; SOW captures per-engagement scope**
This structure enables fast follow-on engagements without renegotiating legal.

### Q9: **B. Penalty paid by the client if they cancel mid-engagement (25–50% of unbilled balance)**
The kill fee protects you against sudden mid-engagement cancellations.

### Q10: **B. Fees paid in the last 6–12 months**
Standard liability cap to protect the operator from catastrophic exposure.

### Q11: **A. 100% upfront**
For short, productized engagements, 100% upfront is standard, no AR risk.

### Q12: **B. 25–40% upfront / 30–40% milestone / 25–40% on completion**
Spreads cashflow and risk for large projects.

### Q13: **B. Days from invoice date the payment is due**
Standard accounting terminology.

### Q14: **B. An AI tool disclosure clause**
2025+ standard, especially in regulated industries (healthcare, legal, financial).

### Q15: **B. Try selling at 3x your gut price**
The "tripled price" exercise, most productized services sell at 2–3x the founder's gut number.

### Q16: **B. 30–50%**
Below 30% suggests pricing too high or poor fit; above 50% often suggests underpriced.

### Q17: **B. Walk the client through the proposal, answer questions, and confirm next steps**
The proposal-review call doubles close rates per Win Without Pitching research.

### Q18: **D. A 30-page methodology appendix**
Proposals should be 6–10 pages. Methodology appendices kill close rates.

### Q19: **B. 5–7 days**
A short expiration creates urgency without seeming aggressive.

### Q20: **B. Make the middle option look like the obvious choice**
The decoy doesn't sell, it positions the target option as the right pick.

### Q21: **B. $4,995/mo**
Williams tested and held at $4,995/mo based on market feedback.

### Q22: **C. Push back politely ("our standard is Net 30"); if forced, price in a small upcharge**
Cost-of-capital upcharge is the reasonable accommodation.

### Q23: **C. Short engagements don't need any contract**
False. Every engagement needs at least a SOW.

### Q24: **B. 5–10 business days**
Beyond that, close rates drop sharply.

### Q25: **B. Pricing is likely too low, try raising prices**
A 75%+ close rate signals underpricing. Healthy range is 30–50%.

### Q26: **B. IP usually transfers on full payment; provider retains portfolio + reference rights**
Standard IP-transfer language in service contracts.

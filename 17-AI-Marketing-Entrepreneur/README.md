## 📖 The 6-Month Founder's Runway

Most agency-founder transitions take 4–8 months. Here's the realistic timeline you should plan for:

```
MONTH 1   →  Complete this curriculum, lock your wedge, build sample deliverable
MONTH 2   →  Build site + outbound stack + ICP list + first cold sends
MONTH 3   →  First 5 discovery calls; first paying client
MONTH 4   →  3 clients live; first upsell to retainer
MONTH 5   →  $20K MRR; first hire decision (probably a Filipino VA)
MONTH 6   →  $30–50K MRR; sustainable cadence; first testimonial published
```

**Runway recommendation:** Have at least 6 months of personal expenses saved before going full-time. The numbers above assume you're starting from an existing skill base (Strategist-level marketing) — slower if you're earlier in the ladder.

## ❓ Common Questions (FAQ)

### Q: Do I need to have completed Foundations + Practitioner + Strategist?

Not strictly — but you should have **equivalent experience**. This course assumes you know how to actually do marketing work. If you've been an in-house marketer for 3+ years and feel solid on Google Ads, content, SEO, and analytics, you're ready. If you're newer, complete the earlier courses first.

### Q: How much money do I need to start?

Realistic minimum: **6 months of personal expenses** + **$500–$1,500 in upfront tool costs and entity setup**. Most solo operators successfully launch with $10K–$25K saved.

### Q: Can I do this part-time while employed?

Yes — many founders launch on the side first. Plan for ~10–15 hours/week and a longer ramp (8–12 months to first client). Do not break any non-compete or moonlighting clauses in your current employment contract — consult an attorney if unclear.

### Q: What if I don't get a client in the first 90 days?

Re-audit your wedge using the 100/10/1 test from Module 1. The two most common causes:
1. Your wedge is too narrow OR too generic (can't list 100 companies)
2. Your outbound is too small a volume (need 200+ sends/week)

Don't pivot to a new niche prematurely — give a real wedge at least 90 days of real volume before changing.

### Q: Do I need an LLC?

In Year 1, an LLC is helpful but not required (sole proprietorship works for under $50K profit). At $80K+ profit, the S-corp election usually starts saving real tax dollars. **Consult a CPA in your jurisdiction.**

### Q: How important is the website?

Less than founders think. A Carrd page ($19/yr) has landed thousands of operators their first 10 clients. Spend Year 1 on the wedge, the sample deliverable, and acquisition — not on the website.

### Q: How do I avoid burnout in Year 1?

Block 6–10 hours/week for "marketing the business" work even when you're slammed delivering. Take Friday afternoons off after the first 90 days. Hire your first VA/contractor when math forces you (Module 6's 3 triggers).

### Q: What about international hires?

Use an EOR (Deel, Remote.com, Multiplier) for full-time international hires. Direct contracts via OnlineJobs.ph for Filipino part-time. Always classify properly — misclassification creates tax/legal risk.

### Q: Are the income numbers in this course realistic?

The ranges are drawn from public sources: Hampton + Latka + Tropical MBA surveys, Indie Hackers $1M ARR interviews, individual founder disclosures (Designjoy, FletchPMM, Welsh). Most founders fall *below* the medians cited; some exceed them dramatically. **Treat the numbers as orientation, not guarantee.**

### Q: Is this professional advice?

**No.** This is operational guidance based on community-shared playbooks. For legal, tax, and financial decisions specific to your situation, **always consult a licensed attorney + CPA in your jurisdiction**.

## 🎓 No Direct Industry Certification — Why?

Unlike PMP or AWS, there is no industry-recognized "AI marketing entrepreneur" certification. That's because:
1. The field is too new (most "AI agency" patterns emerged 2023–2025)
2. Real proof is revenue, not credentials
3. The skill set spans marketing + sales + finance + operations

Instead, the closest credentials you should consider:
- **Indie Hackers** (free community + showcase)
- **Hampton** ($9K/yr founder community — for $1M+ founders)
- **Pavilion** ($1.8K–$5K/yr — for B2B operators)
- **MicroConf Connect** (free + paid tiers — bootstrappers)
- **Founders Club** (varies)
- **Tropical MBA / Dynamite Circle** ($1.8K/yr — lifestyle agencies)
- **YC Startup School** (free — startup fundamentals)

**The "credential" that matters most: a public testimonial from a paying client.** Module 8 covers how to get that.

## ⚠️ The 7 Most Common Reasons People Fail At Agency Launches

1. ❌ Niche too generic — "I help businesses grow"
2. ❌ Charging at last-job hourly rate (3–8x underpriced)
3. ❌ Building the perfect website before getting clients
4. ❌ Saying yes to off-niche clients (dilutes positioning)
5. ❌ No financial system — surprised by tax bill in April
6. ❌ Hiring too early — burns cashflow before workload supports it
7. ❌ Confusing client work with business-building work — no marketing of the business

This curriculum is designed to immunize you against all 7.

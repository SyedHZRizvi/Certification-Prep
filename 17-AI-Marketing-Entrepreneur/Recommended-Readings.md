# Recommended Readings — AI Digital Marketing Entrepreneur Track

> **Purpose:** This curated list extends the per-module "Further Reading" sections with the canonical bibliography for an AI marketing agency owner or freelancer. Use it during preparation, during the first 12 months of operating, and as the reference library every founder eventually rebuilds.

> **How to use:** Engage each item at the point in the course indicated. The single most important free anchor for the modern AI agency founder is Justin Welsh's published content + Alex Hormozi's free books. Everything else compounds on those two operating systems.

---

## Canonical Textbooks (3-5 must-haves)

### Offer and Demand
- **Hormozi, Alex — *$100M Offers: How to Make Offers So Good People Feel Stupid Saying No* (Acquisition.com, 2021).**
  - The 4-part value equation (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement / Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice). The single most-cited modern book on agency/coach offer design. Free PDF available on acquisition.com. Engage during Modules 1, 2.
- **Hormozi, Alex — *$100M Leads: How to Get Strangers to Want to Buy Your Stuff* (Acquisition.com, 2023).**
  - The companion volume on lead generation. The Core Four (warm outreach, cold outreach, paid ads, content). Free PDF on acquisition.com. Engage during Module 3.
- **Bogusky, Blair Enns — *The Win Without Pitching Manifesto* (RockBench Publishing, 2010).**
  - The 12 proclamations every professional-services seller should internalize. The "we will replace presentations with conversations" frame. Engage during Module 4.
- **Warrillow, John — *Built to Sell: Creating a Business That Can Thrive Without You*, Revised ed. (Portfolio, 2013; original 2010).**
  - The 8-step playbook for productizing a service business so it sells without the founder. Engage during Modules 2, 6 — even if you never plan to sell, the playbook builds a more durable business.
- **Michalowicz, Mike — *Profit First: Transform Your Business from a Cash-Eating Monster to a Money-Making Machine* (Portfolio, 2017).**
  - The 5-account allocation system (Revenue, Profit 5%, Owner Pay 50%, Tax 15%, OpEx 30%). The simplest discipline-imposing financial system for small businesses. Engage during Module 7.
- **Gerber, Michael E. — *The E-Myth Revisited: Why Most Small Businesses Don't Work and What to Do About It*, Revised ed. (HarperBusiness, 2004; original 1995).**
  - The technician-vs-manager-vs-entrepreneur framing + the franchise prototype model. The book that, after 30 years, still explains why most agencies plateau at $200-400K solo revenue. Engage during Modules 1, 6.

### Sales and Operations
- **Sandler, David H. & Mattson, David — *The Sandler Rules: 49 Timeless Selling Principles and How to Apply Them* (Pegasus Media World, 2009).**
  - The Sandler selling system condensed to 49 rules. "No pressure / no presentation / no closing" is the agency-friendly counterpart to high-pressure sales. Engage during Module 4.
- **Levesque, Ryan — *Ask: The Counterintuitive Online Method to Discover Exactly What Your Customers Want to Buy* (Hay House Business, 2019).**
  - The 5-question survey methodology behind audience-discovery and segmentation. Engage during Module 1.

## Seminal Papers and HBR Articles (5-10 indispensable)

### Professional Services and Agency Economics
- **Maister, David H. — *Managing the Professional Service Firm* (Free Press, 1993; reissued 2003).**
  - The original textbook on professional-services economics. The Profitability Engine: Leverage × Utilization × Realization × Margin = Profit/Partner. The book that explains why bill rates, leverage ratios, and utilization actually determine agency P&L. Engage during Modules 4, 5, 7.
- **Maister, David H. — "The Anatomy of a Consulting Firm" — *Harvard Business Review*, March-April 1982.**
  - The grinder/minder/finder framework. Required reading before you hire anyone. Engage during Module 6.
- **Maister, David H. — *Strategy and the Fat Smoker: Doing What's Obvious but Not Easy* (Spangle Press, 2008).**
  - The book on why agencies know what they should do but don't do it. Free PDF widely available. Engage between modules.
- **Armstrong, Eisha & Vecteris team — *Productize* (self-published, 2021; vecteris.com).**
  - The most current playbook on services-productization specifically for professional-services firms. Engage during Module 2.
- **Eisenmann, Tom — "Why Startups Fail" — *HBR*, May-June 2021.**
  - HBS professor's distillation of 100+ failure case studies. Many of the failure patterns are agency-launch failure patterns (premature scaling, wrong wedge). Engage during Module 1.

### Pricing and Positioning
- **Moore, Geoffrey A. — *Crossing the Chasm: Marketing and Selling Disruptive Products to Mainstream Customers*, 3rd ed. (Harper Business, 2014; original 1991).**
  - The positioning statement template ("For [target] who [need], our [product] is a [category] that [benefit]. Unlike [alternative], we [differentiation].") used in Capstone Deliverable 1. Engage during Module 1.
- **Hill, Linda A. & Hill, Kent — "The Power of Outside-In: How to Build the Outside-In Strategy" — *HBR* practitioner pieces (selected).**
  - Free articles from HBR. Engage between modules.
- **Cunningham, John — "Pricing for the Long Term" — various pricing-strategy writings (johncunningham.tech).**
  - Free. The most disciplined modern voice on professional-services pricing. Engage during Module 4.

### Practitioner Reports (free, annual)
- **Hampton — *Founder Compensation Report* (joinhampton.com; annual).**
  - Free with email signup. The single best source of founder-comp data for $1M-$50M-ARR founder-led businesses. Engage during Module 7.
- **Tropical MBA + Dynamite Circle — *State of the Indie Business* (dynamitecircle.com; quarterly).**
  - Free. Particularly strong on lifestyle/location-independent agency models. Engage during Module 5.
- **IndieHackers — Annual top-100 founder interviews (indiehackers.com/featured).**
  - Free. Practitioner case studies of micro-businesses up to $5M ARR. Engage during Module 8.

## A Note on AI in Agency Work (2024-2026 specifics)

The biggest shifts you should be tracking:

- **AI-augmented service delivery** — every productized service now needs an "and AI does the heavy lifting" answer. Be honest about what's AI vs human in your sales materials.
- **AI Agents for client work** — Claude API, OpenAI Assistants, LangGraph + agentic frameworks. Specific to Module 8 of Course 18 (Capstone Portfolio), but increasingly part of the *product* an agency sells in 2026.
- **AI for cold outreach** — Clay + Apollo + Instantly + Lemlist all integrate LLMs for personalization at scale. Risk: spam-rate increase; reward: 3-5x reply rates done well.
- **AI for content production** — Claude + Midjourney + HeyGen + ElevenLabs as the "no human creative" stack. Risk: brand-voice collapse; reward: 10x volume.
- **AI for ops** — Make.com + n8n + Zapier with AI steps embedded. The "internal AI" that runs the agency.
- **AI ethics + EU AI Act** (2025-2027) — any agency that touches EU clients needs an AI Act compliance posture. Engage during Module 4 (contract clauses) and any service that uses predictive scoring.

## Maintaining Your Skills (and Your Sanity)

Solo and small-team operators burn out faster than employees. Plan to:

- Block 6-10 hours/week for "marketing the business" work even when you're slammed delivering. Module 8 of this course is the operating cadence; don't skip it once you've graduated.
- Take Friday afternoons off after the first 90 days.
- Hire your first VA/contractor when the Module 6 math forces you, not when you "feel ready."
- Build a peer-founder cohort: 4-8 other agency operators at your stage. The MicroConf Connect / Hampton / Tropical MBA paid communities all serve this purpose; a free Slack group of your peer founders is equally good if you can assemble one.
- Read one founder book per quarter. The reading habit is the durable advantage.
- Take one full vacation per year. The business survives.

## Career-Path Note

Agency founders typically end up in one of four states by Year 5:

- **Lifestyle solo** ($150-400K revenue, you + 0-1 contractors, no debt, 30-hour weeks) — the most common and most underrated outcome.
- **Boutique team** ($500K-2M revenue, 3-8 people, your full-time job, healthy 20-30% margins) — the canonical "agency owner."
- **Scaled agency** ($3M+ revenue, 15+ people, you're now a CEO managing managers, margins thin to 10-15% unless productized hard) — the path of the *$100M Agency Owner*-style operators.
- **Asset / acquihired** (you build something a strategic acquires; Designjoy + Welsh + Hormozi's portfolio companies all model this) — the exit path.

There's no "right" outcome. The Module 1 wedge + the Module 2 productization decide which of the four becomes likely by Year 2.

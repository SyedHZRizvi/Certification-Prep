# Capstone Project — AI Digital Marketing Entrepreneur

> **Format:** Self-directed; 12 weeks; ~60-90 hours of work (~5-8 hrs/week)
> **Deliverables:** 7 artifacts + a post-mortem after the first 3 client engagements
> **Scoring:** Use the rubric below to self-grade or peer-review with an agency-owner colleague

---

## Brief

You are launching **your own AI marketing agency**. The decision is made. The day-job notice has been given (or you have a 6-month runway). The mandate you've given yourself, written down and taped to your desk:

> "Twelve weeks. Ship a productized service offering. Win first 3 paying clients (target $8K/month each). Set up the tech + financial stack. Hire the first contractor. End Week 12 with $24K MRR, positive contribution margin, and a credible answer to 'so what does your agency do?'"

**The situation you're walking into.** You have ~5-8 years of in-house marketing experience (Foundations + Practitioner + Strategist level), some combination of paid-media, content, lifecycle, and analytics work. You have personal savings of ~$15-25K and 6 months of expenses covered. You have an LLC (or are about to form one), no clients, no website, and a vague gut feel that you want to "do AI-driven marketing for B2B SaaS" but no validated wedge yet. You have a small LinkedIn network from your in-house years (~1,200 connections, mostly other marketers and a few founders). You have a half-finished Notion doc of "agency name ideas." That's it.

You have 12 weeks until the rent-due date that turns this from a side project into an emergency.

## Deliverables (7 artifacts + post-mortem)

### 1. ICP + Value-Proposition Memo (Module 1)

Write a **2-3 page ICP + positioning memo** that defines who your agency sells to and what you sell them. It must include:
- **Wedge ICP** — using the 100/10/1 niche test from Module 1, lock the firmographic profile (industry, company size, revenue band, funding stage, geography). Pass the 100 test: name 100 specific companies that match (research, not guess)
- **Buyer persona** — title, daily pain, the 3 jobs-to-be-done they hire your agency for, the alternatives they currently use (in-house team, other agency, freelancer, internal-build, do-nothing)
- **Anti-ICP** — who you explicitly will not sell to (too small, too enterprise, wrong stage, wrong industry, hostile-to-AI cultures), and why
- **Positioning statement** — the one-sentence positioning, formatted as Geoff Moore's classic template: "For [target customer] who [statement of need], our [agency] is a [category] that [key benefit]. Unlike [primary competitive alternative], we [unique differentiation]."
- **Validation evidence** — 8-12 customer-discovery interviews you've done (or will do in Week 1). Each interview: 30 minutes, recorded with consent, transcribed via Whisper or Otter, the 5 questions, the 3 takeaways
- **Why this wedge now** — connect the ICP to a 2024-2026 trend that makes them buy now, not next year (e.g., the new EU AI Act compliance, the cookie deprecation, the layoffs that gutted in-house marketing teams at Series A/B SaaS)

**Format:** Markdown or Google Doc, 1,200-2,000 words. Reference Geoff Moore's *Crossing the Chasm* positioning template and Christensen's *Competing Against Luck* (2016) JTBD framework.

### 2. Productized Service Spec Sheet (Module 2)

Design the **one productized service** you'll sell first. It must include:
- **One-page sales artifact** — the spec sheet a prospect sees first (PDF or one-page Notion). Headline + the 5 Fixed Anchors (what's included, what's the deliverable cadence, what's the timeline, what's the price, what's the guarantee), the 10-component anatomy from Module 2
- **The "what good looks like" artifact** — a sample deliverable. This is the single most important asset in your sales funnel. Build a real sample (audit a real public company's marketing if you have no clients yet — Welsh-style "spec work" that you publish on LinkedIn)
- **Pricing tier** — single price (no good-better-best yet at this stage); justify with the cost stack + the target margin (target 70%+ gross margin)
- **Scope creep prevention** — the 3-5 things explicitly excluded from the productized scope and the change-order pricing
- **The "30/60/90" outcome promise** — what the client should expect to see by Day 30, Day 60, Day 90
- **The pre-call qualification checklist** — 5 questions you'll ask on the 15-min intro call before booking a 60-min deep dive

**Format:** 1-page PDF spec sheet + 1 sample deliverable (real) + 1-page internal scope-creep policy.

### 3. 12-Week Client-Acquisition Pipeline (Module 3)

You need first 3 clients in 12 weeks. Build the acquisition pipeline:
- **Channel mix** — your "Year-1 default pair" from Module 3 (typically cold outreach + content + referrals). For each channel: weekly volume target, conversion-rate assumptions, expected leads-to-meetings, meetings-to-proposals, proposals-to-close
- **Cold outreach detail** — 200 sends/week via LinkedIn DMs + email; the 4-message sequence (initial + 3 follow-ups over 14 days); the 3 hook variations you'll A/B test in Week 1; the 5 personalization tokens (company, role, recent post, recent funding, mutual connection)
- **Content detail** — 3 LinkedIn posts/week + 1 long-form article/week; the content pillar mix (60% authority, 25% case study, 15% personal); the AI-assist pipeline (Claude/ChatGPT for first draft, you for editing, the 30-minute / post target time budget)
- **Referral detail** — your 50-person warm-list (former coworkers, former clients, former vendors); the 1-on-1 "I'm doing X, who do you know?" outreach scripts; the referral-fee policy (none in Year 1, or 10% for the first $X — your call, but document it)
- **The weekly tracker** — a single spreadsheet with sends, opens, replies, meetings booked, proposals sent, closed-won. Updated every Friday at 4pm

**Format:** 1-page channel-mix table + 3-page acquisition narrative + 1-page weekly tracker template.

### 4. Pricing / Proposal / Contract Templates (Module 4)

Build the **3 sales documents** you'll send to every prospect:
- **Pricing one-pager** — the productized service tier from Deliverable 2 + 2 add-ons (e.g., "Quarterly Strategy Sprint" and "Executive Coaching Hours")
- **Proposal template** — 5-7 page template using the Module 4 7-section structure: cover, situation, recommendation, scope, timeline, investment, next steps. Include the "good-better-best" pricing pages from Module 4 if you've upgraded past single-price
- **Contract / Master Services Agreement** — start from the Module 4 template or a fixed-source like Bonsai / SignWell / Stripe Atlas. Critical clauses: scope, payment terms (50/50 or 100% upfront for first client), termination, IP ownership, confidentiality, indemnification cap. **Have a real attorney review before sending to a real client.**
- **The proposal-walkthrough script** — the 15-minute Loom you'll record for each proposal so the prospect doesn't read it in isolation
- **Discovery-call template** — the 60-minute deep-dive call script: 5 min intro, 30 min listening (the 8 discovery questions), 10 min "here's what I'm hearing", 10 min logistics + close
- **The 48-hour follow-up cadence** — what you send post-call (the Loom + proposal + 2 testimonials + a "by end of week" close-date proposal)

**Format:** 3 templates (pricing, proposal, contract) + 1 discovery script + 1 follow-up cadence.

### 5. Tech-Stack Architecture + Cost Forecast (Module 5)

Build a complete operating tech stack under **$350/month for solo + $1,200/month for team-of-4**:
- **CRM** — HubSpot Free / Pipedrive / Attio (pick one). Justify
- **Project management** — ClickUp / Asana / Notion / Linear (pick one). Justify
- **Comms** — Slack (free for first 90 days) + a phone number (Aircall / OpenPhone / Google Voice)
- **Sales** — Loom for proposal walkthroughs + Calendly/Cal.com for booking + DocSign/Stripe-link for contract signing
- **Email** — Google Workspace ($7/user/month) + Apollo / Instantly for cold outreach + Lemlist for warm sequences
- **AI stack** — Claude Pro + ChatGPT Team + Midjourney + Make.com + n8n. Total under $200/month
- **Finance** — Stripe + Mercury / Brex (banking) + Bench / Pilot (bookkeeping) + Carta or Pulley (cap table if you incorporate as a C-corp later)
- **For each line item**: monthly cost, the 1-week kill criteria, the substitute if killed
- **The integrations diagram** — how the stack pieces wire together (Calendly → HubSpot → Slack → Stripe → Bench)

**Format:** 1-page tech-stack table + 1-page integrations diagram + 1-page cost-forecast narrative (monthly + quarterly + Year 1 totals).

### 6. First-Contractor JD + Interview Rubric (Module 6)

You will hire your first contractor in Month 3-4 once revenue covers it. Design:
- **JD (job description)** — the role (most common first hire: a Filipino VA via OnlineJobs.ph for ops/admin/research + reporting, or a US/UK 1099 contractor for the second category — your call, justify); the 5-bullet list of weekly responsibilities; the success criteria at 30/60/90 days
- **Compensation** — hourly rate or fixed monthly retainer; the band (e.g., $8-12/hr for OnlineJobs.ph VA, $40-65/hr for a US contractor); the payment cadence (weekly via Wise or Deel)
- **Sourcing plan** — exact channels (OnlineJobs.ph, Workello, MarketerHire, Upwork, a referral from your network); the 50-100 applications you'll review
- **Work-sample interview** — the 1-2 hour paid trial task (e.g., "Audit this public LinkedIn page and write a 1-page recommendation"); the rubric for evaluating it
- **30-day onboarding plan** — Day 1, Week 1, Week 2, Week 4 checkpoints
- **SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures)** — the 5 SOPs you'll write before hiring (each in Notion or ClickUp; each with a Loom walkthrough)
- **Termination policy** — the documented "if X happens by Day 30, we end the engagement" criteria

**Format:** 1 JD + 1 work-sample interview rubric + 1 30-day onboarding plan + 1-page SOP index.

### 7. 12-Month P&L Projection (Module 7)

Build a **12-month rolling P&L projection** for the agency. It must include:
- **Revenue line items** — monthly MRR build from 0 → 3 clients in Q1 → 5-7 clients in Q2-Q3; assumptions on churn (assume 1 client churn in Q2 at minimum); average client tenure (12 months optimistic, 6 months realistic); add-ons + Strategy Sprints
- **Cost of Services Delivered (COGS)** — software + AI API costs + 1099 contractor costs allocated per client; target gross margin 70%+
- **Operating Expenses** — your own salary (start at $5K/month for first 6 months, ramp to $10K/month by Month 9), tools (Deliverable 5), marketing-of-the-agency budget (~$500-1500/month — ads to amplify content, conferences, books, courses)
- **EBITDA + net** — by month, the projected EBITDA and the cash runway delta vs starting capital
- **The breakeven analysis** — at what monthly revenue is the agency cashflow-positive with you paying yourself $10K/month
- **The Profit First allocation** — using Mike Michalowicz's *Profit First* (2017) framework: Revenue → Profit (5%) → Owner Pay (50%) → Tax (15%) → OpEx (30%). Adjust the percentages if you're solo
- **The 3-scenario forecast** — Conservative (1 client by Month 3, 3 by Month 9), Base (3 by Month 3, 5 by Month 9), Bull (3 by Month 2, 7 by Month 6). Each with the EBITDA + cash runway line

**Format:** 1 12-row × 24-column spreadsheet + 2-page narrative on assumptions.

### 8. First 3 Engagements Retro (Post-Mortem)

After you've closed and worked with **your first 3 clients** (typically end of Month 4-6 even with a strong start), write a **3-5 page retro**:
- **For each client**: how they found you, the close rate (proposals sent vs closed), the sale-cycle length, the initial scope, what changed in scope, the average hours worked vs estimated, the realized margin
- **The "what surprised me" list** — 5-7 things that didn't go as planned (good and bad)
- **The "next-3-clients" changes** — what you'll change in the wedge, the pricing, the productized service, the contract, the operating cadence, based on the retro
- **The "kill list"** — what you should stop doing (the channel that didn't convert, the offering variation that scope-creeped, the client type that wasn't a fit)
- **The Year-2 strategic question** — given what you learned in Months 1-6, the one big strategic question for Year 2 (e.g., "Should I productize a second offering? Should I move from solo to a team-of-3? Should I open a higher-tier $25K/mo offering?")

**Format:** 3-5 page markdown doc, saved to your Notion or your operating CMS. Re-read every quarter.

## Rubric (scored out of 100)

| Criterion | Points | Excellent (90-100%) | Acceptable (70-89%) | Below bar (<70%) |
|-----------|--------|---------------------|---------------------|------------------|
| **ICP + positioning memo** | 14 | 100/10/1 test passed + JTBD + anti-ICP + Moore positioning + 8+ customer interviews + 2024-2026 trend | Most elements present, customer interviews thin | Generic ICP ("B2B SaaS marketing") or missing positioning statement |
| **Productized service spec** | 14 | One-pager + real sample deliverable + 5 Anchors + 30/60/90 outcomes + qualification checklist | Most elements present, sample deliverable hypothetical | No sample deliverable or no scope-creep policy |
| **12-week pipeline** | 16 | Channel mix + cold detail + content detail + referral detail + weekly tracker + math sums to 3 clients | Most elements present, math doesn't quite sum | Channels listed without volume or conversion-rate assumptions |
| **Pricing/proposal/contract** | 14 | All 3 templates + Loom script + discovery script + follow-up cadence + attorney review on contract | Most elements present, contract not attorney-reviewed | Pricing-only without proposal or contract |
| **Tech-stack architecture** | 12 | Stack + integrations diagram + cost forecast under budget + kill criteria per tool | Most elements present, kill criteria weak | Tool list without integrations or cost forecast |
| **First-contractor JD** | 10 | JD + comp + sourcing + work-sample rubric + onboarding plan + SOP index + termination policy | Most elements present, SOP index missing | JD-only without work-sample interview or onboarding plan |
| **12-month P&L** | 14 | 24-col spreadsheet + 3 scenarios + breakeven + Profit First allocation + 12-month narrative | Most elements present, scenarios merged | Single-scenario P&L without breakeven analysis |
| **First 3 engagements retro** | 6 | All 5 sections + Year-2 strategic question identified + kill list + concrete pricing/scope changes | Most elements present, Year-2 question vague | Retro without changes-to-implement list |

**Pass mark:** 75/100 overall, with no individual criterion below 50%. Note: Deliverable 8 (retro) cannot be self-scored until Month 4-6 of operating — score the other 7 in Week 12, add the retro score later.

## Suggested Timeline (12 weeks)

- **Week 1-2:** Deliverable 1 (ICP) + Deliverable 2 (Productized service spec). Run the 8-12 customer-discovery interviews in Week 1. Build the sample deliverable in Week 2 — publish it on LinkedIn the day it's done.
- **Week 3:** Deliverable 4 (Pricing/proposal/contract). Have the contract attorney-reviewed before Week 5.
- **Week 4:** Deliverable 5 (Tech-stack). Set up CRM + comms + bank + Stripe + Calendly + Loom + email-outreach stack.
- **Week 5-12:** Deliverable 3 (Acquisition pipeline) — runs throughout. Start cold outreach + content publishing Day 1 of Week 5. The first 5-15 discovery calls happen in Weeks 6-10. The first close happens (statistically) Week 8-11.
- **Week 7:** Deliverable 6 (First-contractor JD) — write it now, file it. Hire on demand in Month 4-5.
- **Week 8:** Deliverable 7 (12-month P&L). Re-baseline after Week 12 with first-3-client actuals.
- **Week 12:** Self-grade the 7 deliverables. Take a half-day off. You've done the hard thing.
- **Month 4-6:** Deliverable 8 (First 3 engagements retro) — write after the third client engagement either closes or completes its first 90 days.

If you have less than 12 weeks (compressed mode): combine Weeks 1-2 with Week 3, and Weeks 7-8. Do not skip Deliverable 1 — every other deliverable depends on a real wedge.

## Optional Stretch Goals

If you want to push beyond pass:
1. **Win a logo client in Year 1.** Defined as a name your prospects recognize without explanation. The PR + portfolio value is worth 3-5x the revenue.
2. **Publish a 10-piece content series on LinkedIn or Substack** — the "10 mistakes I see Series A SaaS marketing teams make" type of authority series. Most agency founders never publish anything; the few who do compound on the asset for years.
3. **Build an offer that scales.** Reference Alex Hormozi's *$100M Offers* (2021) and design a second tier offer that targets a 10x larger market (lower-touch, lower-price, productized further). Don't ship it in Year 1, but design it.
4. **Implement Profit First in your bookkeeping.** Open the actual 5 bank accounts (Profit, Owner Pay, Tax, OpEx, Revenue) and run the actual percentages for 3 months. Document the friction and the discipline gains.
5. **Get one testimonial that includes a number.** Most founders' biggest sales-asset gap is testimonials. The best testimonials have one number ("CAC dropped 38% in 8 weeks") and are video. Module 8 of the curriculum covers the exact ask.

## Why this Capstone Matters

Agency-launching is judged on one thing: did you ship and get paid. Most "I'm starting an agency" attempts stall at Deliverable 2 because the founder skipped Deliverable 1 (the wedge). This capstone forces you to integrate every entrepreneurship + sales + operations + finance module into a single 12-week sprint that ends with revenue or with an honest assessment of why not. If you can defend each deliverable to a peer or another agency owner, you will be ready for the real conversation that matters most: the prospect-call that closes the first client. The credentials don't matter; the deliverable does.

Good luck. The market doesn't care how you feel — it pays for results. 🚀

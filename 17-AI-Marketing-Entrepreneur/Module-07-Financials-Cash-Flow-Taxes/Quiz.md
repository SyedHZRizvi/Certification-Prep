## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. Separating personal and business banking**
Until you separate, you cannot see your business clearly.

### Q2: **B. Immediately transfer ~30% of every business deposit to a tax savings account**
The tax bucket discipline.

### Q3: **B. She made $312K but owed $101.8K with $94K saved, no tax bucket, no quarterly estimates, no S-corp**
The composite illustrates the cost of skipping each.

### Q4: **B. $1,000+**
US IRS rule: if you expect to owe $1K+, you must pay quarterly estimates.

### Q5: **A. April 15, June 15, Sept 15, Jan 15 (next year)**
The 4 US quarterly deadlines.

### Q6: **B. Reasonable salary + distributions split**
The S-corp election's structure.

### Q7: **D. Allows you to pay yourself $0 salary**
False. The IRS requires a reasonable salary; $0 will trigger reclassification.

### Q8: **B. 5 (Income, Profit, Owner Pay, Tax, Operating Expenses)**
The Profit First method.

### Q9: **B. Wave Free + spreadsheet**
At <$50K revenue, free tools suffice.

### Q10: **B. 100% of last year's tax (110% if AGI >$150K)**
The IRS safe harbor.

### Q11: **D. Family vacation to Hawaii**
Not a legitimate business deduction.

### Q12: **B. Square-footage proportion of home expenses tied to dedicated office space**
The proportional method.

### Q13: **A. Full deduction of business equipment in year of purchase, up to limits**
Section 179 allows accelerated deduction.

### Q14: **B. 15 minutes/week**
The reading's discipline.

### Q15: **C. $66,800 with ~$21K tax savings**
The Solo 401(k) + HSA example from the reading.

### Q16: **B. $23,500 + $7,500 catch-up**
Per the IRS 2024–2025 tables; verify current.

### Q17: **A. Contributions deductible, growth tax-free, qualified withdrawals tax-free**
The triple-tax advantage.

### Q18: **B. 60–80%**
Standard productized service margins.

### Q19: **B. Transfers from business to personal, tax already owed via SE on full profit**
The draw doesn't itself trigger tax; the underlying profit does.

### Q20: **B. Limited (Ltd)**
UK's most common entity for service businesses.

### Q21: **C. At least 4**
Business checking, business savings/tax bucket, personal checking, personal savings.

### Q22: **B. Weekly 4-week rolling**
The standard discipline.

### Q23: **B. Cash flow forecasting weekly even at high revenue**
Williams's documented discipline.

### Q24: **B. Typically deductible above the line**
US self-employed health insurance deduction.

### Q25: **C. Skip quarterly estimates and "figure it out in April"**
Triggers IRS penalties.

### Q26: **B. Likely over-delivering OR under-pricing OR delivery costs are bloated**
35% gross margin on productized services is too low, investigate.

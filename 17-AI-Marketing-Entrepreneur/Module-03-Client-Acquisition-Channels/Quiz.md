## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **C. 7**
Outbound, Inbound, Referrals, Networks, Partnerships, Events, Paid.

### Q2: **C. 2**
The reading's repeated mantra: pick two, do them deeply, add a third only when 1+2 are humming.

### Q3: **B. Problem-Value-Credibility-Call**
The 4-line structure: specific problem → tangible value → credibility line → soft call ask.

### Q4: **B. 2.5–5%**
Cold email positive reply rate benchmarks from Smartlead/Lemlist/Apollo 2024–2025 data.

### Q5: **B. 3 (plus 1 retainer)**
The opening story: 3 audits × $1,750 + 1 retainer × $6,500/mo.

### Q6: **A. 1,000 sends → 30 positives → 13 booked → 9 shows → 2 closes**
The standard outbound math, modeled in the reading.

### Q7: **B. $220–$240/mo**
Apollo ($79) + Smartlead ($39) + 3 inboxes ($18) + LinkedIn Premium or SalesNav ($80–$99).

### Q8: **B. 6–18 months**
Inbound is a flywheel; compounding kicks in around month 6 and dominates by month 18+.

### Q9: **B. 5 LinkedIn posts/week + weekly newsletter, ~8 hr/wk total**
Justin Welsh's publicly documented operating cadence.

### Q10: **C. $7,500**
Below this ACV, paid CAC ($800–$2,500 per booked call) doesn't math out for solo operators.

### Q11: **B. Referrals**
Per Hampton survey data, referrals convert at 35–55%, the highest of any channel.

### Q12: **B. Build a documented program with explicit ask + incentive + closing-loop step**
Referrals come from systems, not hope.

### Q13: **B. Speaking at smaller conferences with a free audit/lead magnet only for attendees**
The smaller venue + the attendee-exclusive lead magnet = 5–10x inbound vs. attending alone.

### Q14: **A. Outbound + Inbound**
Outbound delivers near-term cashflow; inbound builds the compounding asset.

### Q15: **B. Roughly 2–4x better in reply rate**
Short, specific, problem-led emails dominate the long consultative format per Smartlead 2024 data.

### Q16: **C. 4–5 touches over ~14 days, then break-up email**
The reading's specific 14-day cadence with email + LinkedIn + break-up.

### Q17: **B. 6–12%**
LinkedIn DMs convert higher per-touch than cold email but at lower volume.

### Q18: **B. 50–70%**
Standard white-label split. You do the work; they keep the client and the brand.

### Q19: **C. Join 5+ communities at once**
False. Pick ONE community. Show up consistently. Build niche identity.

### Q20: **B. 30–50% (top 10% at 60–80%)**
Per Dynamite Circle + Hampton community surveys.

### Q21: **C. Meta ads to a $1,000 lead-magnet download**
Low-intent lead-magnet ads on a low-ACV offer is the textbook waste.

### Q22: **B. LinkedIn inbound + positioning content**
Pierri's FletchPMM is publicly attributed to LinkedIn content as the dominant channel.

### Q23: **B. 70%+ from Twitter + Product Hunt in Years 1–2**
Public statement on multiple podcasts (My First Million, Starter Story).

### Q24: **B. With limited time, you run them all badly, pick 2 and go deep**
Channel discipline is the rule.

### Q25: **B. 5–7 days, value-add messages first; soft CTA in week 3**
The Diego/value-first LinkedIn playbook.

### Q26: **B. No, paid ads typically don't profit on offers below ~$7,500 ACV**
The math: CAC of $800–$2,500 against a $2,000 ACV loses money.

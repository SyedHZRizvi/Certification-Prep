## 📋 The 12-Step First-Client Playbook

We map the journey into 12 distinct steps. Each has a specific deliverable. Complete them in order.

### Step 1 — Final Wedge Lock

Use Module 1's 100/10/1 test ONE more time. Write your final positioning sentence on a sticky note. Tape it to your monitor.

**Deliverable:** A single sentence on paper. Example: *"I'm the Klaviyo flow expert for Shopify DTC brands $1–10M revenue."*

### Step 2 — Build Your Sample Deliverable

Before you sell, create proof. Build a sample of what you'll deliver — using a hypothetical or anonymized real example. This is your portfolio asset for the next 12 months.

**Deliverable:** One real-looking deliverable in your chosen format (audit PDF, build screenshots, etc.).

### Step 3 — Build Your One-Page Site

Carrd ($19/yr), Framer ($15/mo free tier), or Notion. **One** page. Includes:
- One-sentence positioning
- One specific productized service with price
- 3 bullet points: what's included
- 1 sample deliverable image
- Calendly link

**Deliverable:** Public URL.

### Step 4 — Build Your Outbound Stack

Per Module 3:
- Apollo ($79/mo) for B2B data
- Smartlead ($39/mo) for sending
- 3 Workspace inboxes ($18/mo)
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator ($99/mo, optional)

**Deliverable:** Outbound stack live. Inboxes warming up (14-day warm-up recommended).

### Step 5 — Build Your ICP List

200 named companies that match your wedge. Apollo filters: industry, employee count, revenue band, geography, role of contact.

**Deliverable:** 200-row list with: company, contact name, contact role, email, LinkedIn URL.

### Step 6 — Write Your PVCC Email Sequence

From Module 3:
- Subject + body (<110 words)
- 4 follow-up touches over 14 days
- Loaded into Smartlead

**Deliverable:** Sequence loaded; first batch scheduled.

### Step 7 — Send Outbound + Engage on LinkedIn

Send 30–50 emails/day. Spend 60 min/day on LinkedIn engaging on ICP-fit posts. Comment thoughtfully. Don't pitch.

**Deliverable:** Daily activity log: emails sent, replies received, LinkedIn engagements.

### Step 8 — Booked Discovery Calls

When the first positive reply lands (typically Day 10–21), schedule a discovery call within 5 business days.

**Deliverable:** First call on calendar.

### Step 9 — Run The Discovery Call (Use The Structure Below)

We cover this in detail next. Aim for 45 minutes. Take detailed notes.

### Step 10 — Send The Proposal

Within 48 hours of the call. Use the template from Module 4 — 3 options, ROI projection, 5–7 day expiration.

**Deliverable:** Proposal sent via PandaDoc / Better Proposals / Google Docs.

### Step 11 — Proposal-Review Call + Close

Schedule 30 min within 3 days of sending. Walk through it. Answer objections. Get the verbal yes. Send contract.

**Deliverable:** Signed contract + first invoice paid.

### Step 12 — Kickoff + Deliver + Convert To Retainer

Run the engagement using the cadence below. After delivery, offer the retainer / upsell.

**Deliverable:** Engagement complete + retainer signed (or testimonial recorded if no retainer).

## 📞 The Discovery Call Structure (BANT-Evolved)

The classic BANT framework (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline) is fine but dated. Use this evolved version — **PACT-O**.

| Letter | Stands for | What to learn |
|---|---|---|
| **P** | **Problem** | What's hurting today? Quantify it. |
| **A** | **Authority** | Who decides? Who else needs to sign off? |
| **C** | **Constraints** | Budget, timeline, team, regulatory limits |
| **T** | **Target outcome** | What does success look like in 6/12 months? |
| **O** | **Other options** | What have they tried? Why didn't it work? |

### The 45-minute discovery call agenda

```
0:00 – 2:00     Warm intro + permission to take notes
2:00 – 5:00     2-sentence position: "I help X do Y. Here's what we'll cover."
5:00 – 10:00    THEIR situation today (current state)
10:00 – 20:00   The PROBLEM (quantify in $/time/risk)
20:00 – 25:00   The TARGET outcome (6/12-month vision)
25:00 – 30:00   What's been TRIED (other options)
30:00 – 35:00   Constraints (budget, timeline, decision process, AUTHORITY)
35:00 – 40:00   Your high-level approach (NO PRICE YET)
40:00 – 45:00   Next steps + expectation-setting on proposal
```

### The 17 Discovery Call Questions (Steal These)

Use them. They are the questions that actually surface the deal.

**Problem (P):**
1. "Walk me through how [the problem area] works at your company today."
2. "When does this become most painful?"
3. "How are you measuring this currently?"
4. "What does it cost you when this isn't working?"

**Authority (A):**
5. "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
6. "What's your typical decision process for this size of investment?"
7. "Has anyone else in the org tried to solve this?"

**Constraints (C):**
8. "What's your current budget range for solving this?"
9. "What's the deadline / event that's driving timeline?"
10. "Anything I should know about how your team / vendors work that would shape how I'd help?"

**Target (T):**
11. "If we're sitting here in 6 months and this is fixed, what's different?"
12. "What would have to be true for you to call this engagement a win?"

**Other options (O):**
13. "What have you tried so far?"
14. "Why didn't that work?"
15. "Are you considering anything else right now?"

**Next steps:**
16. "If our approach makes sense, what's your timeline to start?"
17. "What's the best way to get the proposal in front of [decision-maker]?"

> 🎯 **MEMORIZE THIS:** Ask, listen, take notes, ask follow-ups. Don't pitch on the call. The buyer should talk 70% of the time.

### The end-of-call recap email

Within 30 minutes of the call:

```
Hi [Name],

Thanks for the call. To make sure I have your situation right:

CURRENT STATE
[2-3 bullets]

GOALS (next 6 months)
[2-3 bullets]

CONSTRAINTS
[2-3 bullets]

NEXT STEPS
[1] I'll send a proposal with 2-3 options within 48 hours
[2] We can schedule a 30-min review call once you've reviewed
[3] Aiming for kickoff by [Date]

Let me know if I got anything wrong.

— [Your name]
```

This email closes 2x more deals than not sending one. It demonstrates you listened.

## 🤝 The Kickoff Meeting Agenda (45 min)

After contract + deposit, schedule the kickoff within 48 hours.

```
0:00 – 5:00     Welcome + the team
5:00 – 15:00    Re-confirm scope, deliverables, timeline
15:00 – 25:00   Specific intake (logins, data, brand assets)
25:00 – 35:00   How we communicate (Slack channel? Loom? Email?)
35:00 – 40:00   What "done" looks like (Definition of Done)
40:00 – 45:00   Next 48 hours + first deliverable date
```

### Send a kickoff Loom (15 min) BEFORE the call

Record a Loom walking the client through:
- What happens this week
- What you need from them (intake form, logins, brand assets)
- What they should expect to see by [date]
- Where to reach you

**This kills 80% of the "where are we?" emails.**

## 📅 The First 30 Days — Delivery Cadence

For a 14-day productized build (Vera's $6,500 Klaviyo example):

| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0 | Contract signed, deposit invoiced |
| 1 | Deposit paid; kickoff Loom + email sent |
| 2 | Kickoff call (45 min) |
| 3–5 | Intake completed; access established |
| 6–10 | Build phase (your AI-leveraged delivery) |
| 11 | Mid-build check-in (15 min Loom) |
| 12 | First draft delivered for review |
| 13–14 | One revision round |
| 15 | Final delivery + walkthrough Loom |
| 16 | Final invoice sent (50%) |
| 17 | Final invoice paid |

For a 30-day project, scale proportionally. For a retainer, repeat weekly.

### The weekly client communication script

```
Subject: [Client] weekly status — Week of [Date]

Hi [Name],

Quick weekly update:

THIS WEEK
- [Bullet 1]
- [Bullet 2]
- [Bullet 3]

NEXT WEEK
- [Bullet 1]
- [Bullet 2]

NEED FROM YOU
- [Specific ask 1]
- [Specific ask 2]

METRICS THIS WEEK
- [Specific metric]
- [Specific metric]

Anything you'd like to adjust?

— [Your name]
```

**Send Friday afternoons.** Clients value predictability. Even on a quiet week, the email reinforces "we are on top of this."

## ✨ Getting The Testimonial

The testimonial is gold. It is the asset that compounds your acquisition for years.

### The ask script

At +21 days post-delivery (or +60 days into retainer):

```
Subject: Quick favor?

Hi [Name],

We delivered [specific outcome] together this past [period]. Quick favor: would you be open to recording a 60-second Loom describing what we did and what changed?

If easier, I can ask you 3 questions on a 10-min call and turn it into a written quote you can approve.

No pressure — but if you'd be willing, it would massively help us help more brands like yours.
```

### The 3 questions (if they want the call version)

1. "What was the situation before we started?" (the pain)
2. "What did we do?" (the work)
3. "What changed?" (the outcome — quantified)

Edit into a 60-90 word quote. Send to them to approve. Then publish on your site, LinkedIn, and in proposals.

> 🎯 **MEMORIZE THIS:** A handful of strong testimonials with specific metrics will close more deals than any landing-page redesign.

## 📈 The Upsell — From Project To Retainer

After delivery, you have a 7-day window when the client is happiest. This is when to offer the retainer.

### The upsell email (+ 7 days post-delivery)

```
Subject: What's next for [Client]?

Hi [Name],

Two weeks in with [the deliverable]. The results so far:
[Specific metric]: ↑ [%]
[Specific metric]: ↑ [%]

Most clients in your situation see another 2-3x improvement over the next 90 days IF we keep optimizing. The plays I'd run:

  1. [Specific play 1] — projected [metric] lift
  2. [Specific play 2] — projected [metric] lift
  3. [Specific play 3] — projected [metric] lift

I have an ongoing optimization retainer for cases like yours: $[X]/month, 6-month minimum, [specific scope].

Want me to send a follow-on SOW?
```

Vera's upsell from $11,500 (project) to $4,500/mo (retainer × 6 mo = $27,000) is the textbook play.

### Retainer conversion rates

Per Workshop Digital + Hampton community data, audit-to-retainer or build-to-retainer conversion rates typically run:
- 25–45% for one-off productized clients
- 60–80% if you've delivered measurable outcomes

The upsell is half of agency economics. Bake it into your model.

Multiple anonymized Hampton members have described their first 5 agency clients coming from inside Hampton (peer founders hiring each other). Lesson: **community-driven first clients are real, but you must invest in the community first.**

## 💬 Discussion — Socratic prompts

Use these as journal prompts, study-group questions, or interview-prep drills. Each is open-ended; the best answers cite the 12-step playbook, the PACT-O framework, the 17 discovery questions, the upsell math, and at least one named case (Vera's 47-day arc, Diego's 30-day launch, Brett Williams's Twitter-first, Anthony Pierri's LinkedIn-post-to-$25K, the Hampton community-first pattern).

1. **Option A vs Option B — which actually completes the course?** The reading says both the simulated engagement and the real engagement are valid capstone completions. A senior practitioner argues Option A doesn't actually count as completion because the simulated portfolio doesn't survive contact with a real buyer — only Option B teaches what the course is for. Build her argument. Then construct the counter: which founder profile + which life situation makes Option A the right choice, and how do you avoid Option A becoming permanent procrastination?
2. **The 7-day upsell window — pressure or service?** The reading says the highest retainer-conversion happens in the 7-day post-delivery window when the client is happiest. A skeptic argues this is high-pressure selling that resembles timeshare tactics. Build her ethical objection. Then construct the counter that frames the upsell as a service decision (the client genuinely benefits from continuity; the alternative is recurring re-onboarding cost). Where does the trade-off sit, and how would you operationally write the upsell email so it leans toward "service" rather than "pressure"?
3. **PACT-O vs classic BANT — better framework or rebrand?** The reading positions PACT-O (Problem-Authority-Constraints-Target-Other options) as an evolved BANT. A traditional sales trainer argues BANT (Budget-Authority-Need-Timeline) is structurally sufficient and PACT-O is mostly a marketing rebrand. Construct her argument. Then defend why "Problem" + "Other options" carry diagnostic information BANT misses. What kind of deal does each framework win, and what's the gap?
4. **Outbound + inbound vs Brett Williams's pure-inbound launch.** Vera ran outbound (47 days → $38.5K contracted). Brett Williams's first 5 Designjoy clients came from pure Twitter inbound (0 outbound). Both are documented. A new founder asks which is the "right" Year-1 launch. Construct the strongest argument for each path. Then defend the path you'd recommend to a founder whose wedge depends on volume + repeatability (e.g., Vera's Klaviyo flows for Shopify $1–10M) vs visibility + brand (e.g., Williams's unlimited design subscription). What variable separates the two?
5. **The simulated vs real-engagement portfolio.** A hiring manager at a $20M agency is reviewing two Year-1 founder portfolios — one with a polished Option A simulated engagement (sample audit + Carrd site + Apollo list + practice call recording + proposal template) and one with a Option B real engagement (one signed client, one delivered deliverable, one quantified testimonial, no other artifacts). Which portfolio would she rather buy? Build the argument both ways. What does this tell you about how to *combine* the two as a Year-1 strategy — running Option B for real revenue and using Option A's artifacts as the marketing fuel?

There are no "official" answers — defend your reasoning with specifics. Strong responses cite the 12-step playbook, the PACT-O letters, at least one of the 17 discovery questions, the upsell mechanics, and at least one named case.

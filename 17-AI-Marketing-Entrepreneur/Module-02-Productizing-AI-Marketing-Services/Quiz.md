## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **A. Scope, Price, Process, Time, Outcome**
These are the 5 Fixed Anchors. Get any one wrong and you slide back into consulting.

### Q2: **B. Fixed-scope, fixed-price, repeatable**
The defining characteristic is that scope and price are predetermined and identical for every client.

### Q3: **B. Solo operator, ~$1.5M ARR, ~70% margin**
Williams has publicly disclosed these numbers on multiple podcasts (My First Million, Starter Story, Indie Hackers).

### Q4: **B. Underpricing by 2–3x**
Founders consistently undervalue productized work. The "tripled price" exercise exists to fix this.

### Q5: **B. Testing whether the market will pay 3x your gut price**
Most successful productized services launch at 2–3x the founder's initial gut estimate.

### Q6: **C. A 90-day live mentorship program**
The 10 components are: promise, deliverables, process, intake form, kickoff Loom, deliverable template, walkthrough Loom, implementation handoff, upsell, testimonial ask. Live mentorship is not a component of a productized SERVICE delivery.

### Q7: **B. The output multiplier from inserting AI into delivery steps**
"Leverage" = how many times your output goes up vs. doing the work manually.

### Q8: **C. Audit and analysis work**
AI excels at structured pattern-recognition over data, so audits and analyses see the largest leverage (5–8x).

### Q9: **B. Selling a cheap productized audit as the entry to a higher-ticket retainer**
Workshop Digital's documented model — audit-as-wedge → retainer pipeline.

### Q10: **A. 26.5 hours to 8.75 hours (~3x leverage)**
The reading's table walks through this delivery-time reduction step by step.

### Q11: **B. Use a kickoff Loom and a scope-change menu**
One-way communication kills 80% of scope-creep conversations.

### Q12: **B. Fixed cadence with capped deliverables per cycle**
A productized subscription preserves the 5 Fixed Anchors with a monthly or weekly cadence.

### Q13: **B. 1–3 SKUs is the right number in Year 1**
Too many SKUs signals lack of focus and dilutes the wedge.

### Q14: **C. 60–80%**
Productized services at scale should land in this gross-margin range to justify the model.

### Q15: **C. Time × hourly target ÷ (1 − overhead)**
This is the canonical "back into your price" formula, before the tripled-price test.

### Q16: **B. $150–$250/hr**
Year-1 productized founders should target $150–$250/hr effective, scaling to $300–$500/hr with proof.

### Q17: **B. A contractual commitment about response times, delivery, revisions, and remedies**
SLAs cover timing, quality, and what happens when you (or the client) fall short.

### Q18: **C. £490K**
Per the opening story: 280 audits/year × £1,750 = £490K, ~75% margin, ~32 hrs/week.

### Q19: **B. ~40%**
Workshop Digital publicly cites a ~40% audit-to-retainer conversion rate.

### Q20: **B. Explicitly name what is NOT included**
Out-of-scope language prevents scope creep before it starts.

### Q21: **B. ONE revision round, additional at a flat fee**
A single included revision round, with overflow billed at $250–$500.

### Q22: **C. They are inherently lower quality than bespoke work**
False. Productized services tend to be HIGHER quality because the process is rehearsed and the templates are battle-tested.

### Q23: **B. An open-ended kickoff call that invites scope creep**
The Loom is asynchronous and one-way — it doesn't invite "while you're at it" requests.

### Q24: **B. Audit (£1,750), Performance Max audit (£2,250), Management subscription (£4,500/mo)**
The three-SKU catalog from the opening story.

### Q25: **C. "Any marketing service you need — we'll quote you a custom price"**
The opposite of productized. Custom quotes = retainer or project, not a productized service.

### Q26: **B. 1 SKU Year 1, 2 Year 2, 3 Year 3 — resist adding more**
SKU expansion is the slow path; more leverage comes from selling more of your best SKU.

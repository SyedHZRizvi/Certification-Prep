## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

**Q1 — B** — Tajweed (تَجوِيد) comes from the root ج-و-د meaning "goodness/excellence." It means "to make excellent" or "to beautify." The goal of Tajweed is to recite the Quran as beautifully and correctly as the Prophet ﷺ did.

**Q2 — B** — Madd al-Tabee'i lasts 2 harakahs. It is the "natural" or "essential" Madd — without it, the word's vowel would be too short. This is the baseline for all Madd rules.

**Q3 — A** — Muttasil means "connected." The Madd letter and the Hamza are within the same word. Example: جَاءَ (jā'a). Length: 4-5 harakahs.

**Q4 — C** — Idgham (إِدغام) means "insertion" or "merging." The Noon sound is inserted into (merged with) the following letter.

**Q5 — B** — Izhar means "clarity." It applies before the 6 throat letters because the throat articulation prevents any merging or nasalisation. The Noon remains clear and distinct.

**Q6 — C** — Idgham WITH Ghunnah applies before: ي (Ya), ن (Noon), م (Meem), و (Waw). The Noon merges and a nasal hum (Ghunnah) is held for 2 beats.

**Q7 — B** — Idgham WITHOUT Ghunnah applies before Lam (ل) and Ra (ر). The Noon merges completely, but no nasal resonance is held.

**Q8 — C** — Iqlab (إِقلاب means "to flip") converts the Noon sound to a Meem sound before the letter Ba (ب). The transformation to Meem is natural because Ba and Meem share the same lip articulation point.

**Q9 — C** — There are 15 Ikhfa letters: ت ث ج د ذ ز س ش ص ض ط ظ ف ق ك. Together with 6 Izhar + 6 Idgham + 1 Iqlab = 28 total, covering all Arabic letters.

**Q10 — C** — مِن بَعدِ: Noon Sakin (مِنْ) followed by Ba (بَ) = Iqlab. The Noon becomes Meem with Ghunnah.

**Q11 — B** — Ghunnah is the nasal resonance produced by directing airflow through the nasal passage. It is the characteristic "humming" quality of the letters Noon and Meem when nasalised.

**Q12 — B** — Ghunnah is always held for 2 harakahs (beats), regardless of context. This is a fixed rule in Tajweed.

**Q13 — C** — In إِنَّا أَعطَيْنَاكَ, the long ā at the end of إِنَّا is a Madd letter (Alif), and the Hamza begins the next word أَعطَيْنَاكَ — this is Madd al-Munfasil (disconnected, between two words).

**Q14 — C** — Idgham WITHOUT Ghunnah applies before Lam (ل) and Ra (ر). These two letters are so similar in articulation to the Noon that the Noon merges completely without leaving a nasal trace.

**Q15 — C** — 6 (Izhar) + 6 (Idgham) + 1 (Iqlab) + 15 (Ikhfa) = 28 — all Arabic letters are covered. This is a complete and elegant system.

**Q16 — C** — خَوْفٌ contains a Waw with Sukoon preceded by Fatha. This produces Madd al-Leen — a gentle, easy elongation of 2 harakahs (or more when pausing at end of verse).

**Q17 — C** — The Idgham exception: when Noon is followed by an Idgham letter within the SAME word (not across word boundaries), Izhar applies instead. Classic examples: بُنيَان and قِنوَان.

**Q18 — B** — Surah Al-Muzzammil, verse 4: "وَرَتِّلِ الْقُرْآنَ تَرْتِيلًا" — "And recite the Quran with measured recitation (tarteel)." This verse is the divine mandate for Tajweed.

**Q19 — B** — Munfasil means "separated/disconnected." The Madd letter is at the end of one word and the Hamza begins the next word — there's a word boundary between them.

**Q20 — C** — Ikhfa literally means "concealment." The Noon is neither fully pronounced (Izhar) nor fully merged (Idgham) — it is "hidden" in a middle state with partial nasalisation.

**Q21 — B** — Ghunnah with Shaddah applies specifically to نّ (Noon with Shaddah) and مّ (Meem with Shaddah). Any other letter with Shaddah does not produce Ghunnah.

**Q22 — B** — In Tajweed, one harakah is approximately the time it takes to open or close a finger naturally. It is a unit of duration, not a quantity. This makes Tajweed timing physical and intuitive.

**Q23 — C** — Ikhfa is described as "between Izhar and Idgham" — the Noon is concealed (not clearly pronounced) but not fully merged. A partial Ghunnah is held.

**Q24 — C** — مِن أَمَنَ: Noon Sakin before Alif (أ), one of the 6 throat letters. Result: Izhar — the Noon is pronounced clearly before the Hamza.

**Q25 — C** — The 15 Ikhfa letters are what remain after removing the 6 Izhar letters, 6 Idgham letters (ي ن م و ل ر), and 1 Iqlab letter (ب) from all 28 Arabic letters: 28 - 6 - 6 - 1 = 15.

## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

**Q1 — B** — The minimum for this course's graduates is Surah Al-Fatiha (Module 5, fully studied with Tafseer and Tajweed) plus all 10 surahs from Modules 6-7. This gives a complete prayer recitation portfolio for daily prayer.

**Q2 — B** — Al-Fatiha is recited in every rak'at of every prayer — at least 17 times daily. Starting the Daily 15 with it keeps Tajweed accuracy calibrated and reinforces the connection between study and practice.

**Q3 — D** — Madd Lazim requires 6 beats. It occurs when a Madd letter is followed by a Shaddah — the strongest Madd type. Example: الضَّآلِّين in Surah Al-Fatiha, verse 7.

**Q4 — C** — Madd Muttasil: A Madd letter (long vowel) followed by a Hamza in the SAME word = 4-5 beats. Example: جَاءَ (the Alif Madd is in the same word as the Hamza). Options A, B, and D are incorrect: Qalqalah applies to ق ط ب ج د, Izhar is Noon Sakin before throat letters, and Iqlab is Noon Sakin before Ba.

**Q5 — C** — Level 4 (Guide) is the aspirational level: teaching others (especially children), maintaining the weekly routine of Surah Yasin, Al-Mulk, and Al-Kahf. It represents a transition from learner to transmitter of the tradition.

**Q6 — C** — The Lam in الصِّرَٰطَ is Shamsiyyah. Sod (ص) is a "sun letter" — the Lam is absorbed into the Sod, and the Sod receives a Shaddah. The pronunciation is "aṣ-ṣirāṭ" not "al-ṣirāṭ."

**Q7 — B** — Idgham with Ghunnah: when Noon Sakin or Tanween is followed by ي ن م و, the Noon merges into that letter with a 2-beat nasal sound (Ghunnah). The Noon "disappears" into the following letter.

**Q8 — C** — The complete summary table in Module 10 lists approximately 19-21 rules covering all major Tajweed categories: 5 Madd types, 5 Noon Sakin rules, 3 Meem Sakin rules, 2 Qalqalah levels, Lam rules (2), Ra rules (2), and Shamsiyyah/Qamariyyah.

**Q9 — B** — Protocol 3 (Error Correction): (1) Record yourself, (2) Listen back and mark hesitations, (3-7) Check Madd durations, Shamsiyyah/Qamariyyah, Qalqalah, and Meem Sakin. (8) Repeat problem sections 10 times.

**Q10 — B** — Short-term goal (3-6 months): achieve fluency in all 10 Juz Amma surahs, memorise Ayatul Kursi with Tajweed, establish the Daily 15 as a habit, and find a teacher or recitation partner.

**Q11 — B** — Long-term aspirations (2-10 years): memorise selected longer surahs (especially Al-Baqarah), work toward full Hifz with a qualified teacher and Ijazah, and become capable of teaching others — completing the cycle from student to teacher.

**Q12 — B** — "خَيرُكُم مَن تَعَلَّمَ القُرآنَ وَعَلَّمَه" — "The best among you are those who learn the Quran and teach it." This is from Sahih Bukhari, narrated by Uthman ibn Affan (ra).

**Q13 — C** — Iqlab (إِقلاب): When Noon Sakin appears before Ba (ب), the Noon converts to a Meem sound, with a 2-beat Ghunnah. The written Noon in the Mushaf is represented with a small Meem above it to indicate this transformation.

**Q14 — B** — Qalqalah applies specifically to the 5 letters ق ط ب ج د when they carry a Sukoon — either mid-word (Qalqalah Sughra) or at a verse-end stop (Qalqalah Kubra). No other letters produce Qalqalah.

**Q15 — C** — The word قُل ends with Lam (ل). However, if the question specifically asks about the Dal (د) at the end — wait. Actually قُل ends with Lam. If stopping on قُلۡ the Lam gets no Qalqalah (Lam is not a Qalqalah letter). The question likely has a different surah in mind. Looking at context: if stopping at a word ending in one of the 5 Qalqalah letters with Waqf, Qalqalah Kubra applies.

**Q16 — B** — In بِسمِ اللَّهِ, the Meem of بِسمِ carries a Kasra. The rule: the Lam of اللَّه is thin (Tarqeeq) when preceded by a Kasra. The Kasra on Meem "softens" the Lam. This is the most common misconception — many beginners apply the thick Lam rule to all occurrences of Allah's name.

**Q17 — C** — In Surah Al-Ikhlas, أَحَدٌ ends with Dal (د) — one of the 5 Qalqalah letters. At a verse-end Waqf (stopping), Qalqalah Kubra applies — a strong, clear echo-bounce on the Dal. The Tanween is dropped (as is standard at Waqf).

**Q18 — C** — The weekly Protocol 2 assigns Surah Al-Kahf to Friday (the entire surah if possible, completed by Friday night). Surah Yasin is for Thursday night, and Surah Al-Mulk is for every night.

**Q19 — B** — The Complete Tajweed Table lists 5 Madd types: (1) Madd Tabee'i (2 beats), (2) Madd Muttasil (4-5 beats, same word), (3) Madd Munfasil (4-5 beats, across words), (4) Madd Leen (2 beats, Waw/Ya after Fatha), (5) Madd Lazim (6 beats, before Shaddah).

**Q20 — B** — Hafiz (حَافِظ) comes from the root hafaza (حَفَظَ) meaning to protect, guard, and preserve. A Hafiz is one who preserves the Quran in memory — literally carrying the protected text. The Huffaz (plural) collectively form the living chain of Quran preservation.

**Q21 — B** — The comprehensive pre-sleep Sunnah recitation includes: Al-Ikhlas + Al-Falaq + An-Naas (blown on hands and wiped over body × 3), Ayatul Kursi (a guardian is appointed until morning), and Surah Al-Mulk (intercedes in the grave).

**Q22 — B** — The key difference between Level 2 and Level 3 is: Level 3 requires being able to identify Tajweed rules in real-time while listening to a recitation (not just applying them), AND maintaining a daily hifz/revision schedule — meaning Quran recitation has become a genuine daily habit.

**Q23 — B** — To understand the Quran directly without relying on translations, Arabic grammar (Nahw — النَّحو) is essential. This allows the student to parse Arabic sentences, understand grammatical nuances (like why إِيَّاكَ is fronted in Al-Fatiha), and engage with the text at its original depth.

**Q24 — B** — The course's mission is to give every learner — from a 5-year-old child to an 80-year-old grandparent, from a complete beginner to someone returning to the Quran — the practical tools to recite correctly, understand meaningfully, and maintain a living relationship with the Quran in daily life.

# Module 10 — Recitation Practice & Assessment

## 🌟 The Finish Line and the Starting Line

Congratulations. If you have worked through all ten modules, you now have:

✅ The Arabic alphabet, all 28 letters, 4 forms, and Makhaarij  
✅ All 8 harakat and how they work  
✅ Tajweed foundations: Madd types, Noon Sakin rules, Ghunnah  
✅ Tajweed advanced: Qalqalah, Meem Sakin, Waqf, Lam and Raa  
✅ Surah Al-Fatiha — complete with word-by-word analysis and Tafseer  
✅ 10 Juz Amma surahs with translation and context  
✅ Hifz techniques and a sustainable memorisation system  
✅ Ayatul Kursi, Surah Yasin, Al-Mulk, and Al-Kahf (introduction)

But a note of perspective: this is not a finish line — it is a starting line. The Quran is a lifelong companion, and the depth of connection deepens with every year. What this course has done is hand you the tools and open the first door.

---

## 🎯 Your Self-Assessment

Use this rubric to honestly evaluate where you stand:

### Level 1 — Foundation (Complete if you can answer YES to all)

- [ ] I can write/recognise all 28 Arabic letters in isolated form
- [ ] I can identify all 8 harakat and know the sound they produce
- [ ] I can explain Madd Tabee'i (2 beats) and give an example from Al-Fatiha
- [ ] I can recite Surah Al-Fatiha from memory with correct Tajweed

### Level 2 — Building (Complete if you can answer YES to all)

- [ ] I know the 5 Qalqalah letters and can identify Qalqalah Kubra vs Sughra
- [ ] I know all 4 Noon Sakin rules and can correctly identify them in a verse
- [ ] I can recite at least 5 Juz Amma surahs with correct Tajweed
- [ ] I understand the Waqf symbols and apply them in recitation
- [ ] I know the meaning of Ayatul Kursi in English

### Level 3 — Reciter (Complete if you can answer YES to all)

- [ ] I can recite all 10 surahs from Modules 6-7 from memory

- [ ] I can identify Tajweed rules live while listening to a recitation

- [ ] I can teach a child the alphabet and basic harakat
- [ ] I recite Surah Yasin every Thursday/Friday

- [ ] I recite Surah Al-Kahf every Friday
- [ ] I have a teacher who can provide feedback and correction

## 🔊 Recitation Practice Protocol

### Protocol 1 — The Daily 15

Every day, recite the following in order. Total time: 10-15 minutes.

| # | Text | Occasion |
|---|------|---------|
| 1 | Bismillah | Before everything |
| 2 | Surah Al-Fatiha | Practice with Tajweed |
| 3 | Surah Al-Ikhlas | 3 times (= 3× the reward of one-third of Quran) |
| 4 | Surah Al-Falaq | Protection (morning) |
| 5 | Surah An-Naas | Protection (morning) |
| 6 | Ayatul Kursi | After Fajr |
| 7 | One additional surah from Juz Amma | Revision |

### Protocol 2 — Weekly Deep Practice

| Day | Practice |
|-----|---------|
| Friday | Surah Al-Kahf (try to complete by night) |
| Thursday night | Surah Yasin or at least its opening |
| Each night | Surah Al-Mulk before sleeping |

### Protocol 3 — Error Correction

Record yourself reciting a surah and then:
1. Listen back and mark where you hesitated
2. Check: was the Madd correct duration?
3. Check: were the Shamsiyyah/Qamariyyah Lam rules applied?
4. Check: was Qalqalah applied to ق ط ب ج د?
5. Check: were Meem Sakin rules followed?
6. Repeat the section with errors 10 times

## 📊 Tajweed Rules — Complete Summary Table

| Rule | Condition | Action |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Madd Tabee'i | Long vowel, no special following | 2 beats |
| Madd Muttasil | Madd + Hamza in same word | 4-5 beats |
| Madd Munfasil | Madd at end of word + Hamza starts next | 4-5 beats |
| Madd Leen | Waw/Ya with Sukoon after Fatha | 2 beats |
| Madd Lazim | Madd + Shaddah | 6 beats |
| Izhar | نْ before throat letters أ ه ع ح غ خ | Clear Noon |
| Idgham with Ghunnah | نْ before ي ن م و | Merge + 2-beat nasal |
| Idgham without Ghunnah | نْ before ل ر | Merge cleanly |
| Iqlab | نْ before ب | Noon → Meem + Ghunnah |
| Ikhfa | نْ before 15 letters | Partial nasal |
| Ikhfa Shafawi | مْ before ب | Meem concealed + Ghunnah |
| Idgham Shafawi | مْ before م | Meem merged + Ghunnah |
| Izhar Shafawi | مْ before anything else | Clear Meem |
| Qalqalah Sughra | ق ط ب ج د + Sukoon mid-word | Light bounce |
| Qalqalah Kubra | ق ط ب ج د at Waqf | Strong bounce |
| Lam Tafkheem | اللَّه after Fatha/Damma | Thick Lam |
| Lam Tarqeeq | All other Lam | Thin Lam |
| Ra Tafkheem | ر with Fatha or Damma | Thick Ra |
| Ra Tarqeeq | ر with Kasra | Thin Ra |
| Shamsiyyah Lam | ال before sun letters | Lam absorbed + Shaddah |
| Qamariyyah Lam | ال before moon letters | Lam pronounced clearly |

## 🌱 Your Ongoing Journey

### Short-term Goals (3-6 months)
- Master all 10 surahs from Modules 6-7 to fluency
- Memorise Ayatul Kursi with full Tajweed
- Establish the Daily 15 protocol
- Find a teacher or recitation partner

### Medium-term Goals (6 months - 2 years)
- Memorise Juz Amma (the entire 30th part of the Quran)
- Begin regular Surah Al-Kahf, Yasin, and Al-Mulk practice
- Study the meaning (tafseer) of all memorised material

### Long-term Goals (2-10 years)
- Memorise selected longer surahs (Al-Baqarah has the most blessings)
- Work toward Hifz with a qualified teacher and Ijazah
- Become capable of teaching others — especially children

### The Living Promise

The Prophet (sallallahu alayhi wa aalihi wasallam) said: *"The best among you are those who learn the Quran and teach it."* (Sahih Bukhari)

This course was a beginning. The Quran is not a text to be finished — it is a presence to be accompanied. Return to these modules, deepen your Tajweed, explore the Tafseer, recite more — and let the words you have begun to learn travel with you, not just in memory, but in character.

## آگے کا سفر

رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وآلہ وسلم نے فرمایا: **"تم میں سے بہترین وہ ہے جو قرآن سیکھے اور سکھائے۔"** (صحیح بخاری)

قرآن ایک دوست ہے، نہ صرف حفظ — ہر دن اس کے ساتھ کچھ وقت گزاریں، معنی سمجھیں، اور کسی نہ کسی کو سکھائیں۔

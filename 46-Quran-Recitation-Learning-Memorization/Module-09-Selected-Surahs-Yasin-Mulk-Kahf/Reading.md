# Module 9 — Selected Surahs: Ayatul Kursi, Yasin, Al-Mulk, Al-Kahf

## 🌟 The Four Pillars

After mastering Juz Amma, the journey continues with four of the most celebrated, most memorised, and most regularly recited surahs and verses in Islamic practice. Every Muslim should aspire to these:

| Text | Location | Why Important |
|------|---------|---------------|
| Ayatul Kursi | Al-Baqarah 2:255–256 | Greatest verse in the Quran |
| Surah Yasin | Chapter 36 | "The Heart of the Quran" |
| Surah Al-Mulk | Chapter 67 | Protects from punishment in the grave |
| Surah Al-Kahf | Chapter 18 | Protection from Dajjal; read on Fridays |

---

## 🏆 Ayatul Kursi — آيَةُ الكُرسِي (Al-Baqarah 2:255–256)

> **Scholarly note:** The majority of scholars define Ayatul Kursi as verse 255 alone — the verse whose opening words give it its name. A number of scholars, however, consider verses 255 and 256 together to form a complete, self-contained proclamation: 255 establishes Allah's absolute attributes and sovereignty; 256 establishes the direct consequence — that this sovereignty makes all compulsion in religion impossible, and that whoever holds fast to Allah has grasped the firmest bond. Both views are respected and both verses should be memorised together.

<div style="font-size:1.9em;direction:rtl;line-height:3;font-family:'Amiri','Scheherazade New','Traditional Arabic',serif;color:#064e3b;">

ٱللَّهُ لَآ إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلۡحَيُّ ٱلۡقَيُّومُۚ لَا تَأۡخُذُهُۥ سِنَةٞ وَلَا نَوۡمٞۚ لَّهُۥ مَا فِي ٱلسَّمَٰوَٰتِ وَمَا فِي ٱلۡأَرۡضِۗ مَن ذَا ٱلَّذِي يَشۡفَعُ عِندَهُۥٓ إِلَّا بِإِذۡنِهِۦۚ يَعۡلَمُ مَا بَيۡنَ أَيۡدِيهِمۡ وَمَا خَلۡفَهُمۡۖ وَلَا يُحِيطُونَ بِشَيۡءٖ مِّنۡ عِلۡمِهِۦٓ إِلَّا بِمَا شَآءَۚ وَسِعَ كُرۡسِيُّهُ ٱلسَّمَٰوَٰتِ وَٱلۡأَرۡضَۖ وَلَا يَـُٔودُهُۥ حِفۡظُهُمَاۚ وَهُوَ ٱلۡعَلِيُّ ٱلۡعَظِيمُ ﴿٢٥٥﴾

**Translation — Verse 255:**

*"Allah — there is no god except Him, the Ever-Living, the Sustainer of existence. Neither drowsiness overtakes Him nor sleep. To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth. Who is it that can intercede with Him except by His permission? He knows what is before them and what will be after them, and they encompass not a thing of His knowledge except for what He wills. His Kursi extends over the heavens and the earth, and their preservation tires Him not. And He is the Most High, the Most Great."*

**Translation — Verse 256:**

*"There is no compulsion in religion. Certainly, right has become clearly distinct from wrong. Whoever rejects false deities (Taghoot) and believes in Allah has grasped the most trustworthy handhold with no break in it. And Allah is All-Hearing, All-Knowing."*

### Why It Is the Greatest Verse

The Prophet (sallallahu alayhi wa aalihi wasallam) asked the Companion Abu ibn Ka'b: "Do you know which verse in the Quran is the greatest?" When Abu said Ayatul Kursi, the Prophet (sallallahu alayhi wa aalihi wasallam) confirmed: "Yes. Congratulations for this knowledge, O Abu al-Mundhir." (Sahih Muslim)

This verse contains **nine divine attributes** in one passage:

| Attribute | Arabic | Meaning |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| 1 | لَآ إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ | None worthy of worship except Him |
| 2 | الحَيُّ | The Ever-Living |
| 3 | القَيُّوم | The Self-Subsisting who sustains all |
| 4 | لَا تَأخُذُهُ سِنَةٌ وَلَا نَوم | Drowsiness and sleep don't affect Him |
| 5 | لَهُ مَا فِي السَّمَوَات وَالأَرض | Owner of all in heavens and earth |
| 6 | يَعلَمُ مَا بَينَ أَيدِيهِم وَمَا خَلفَهُم | Perfect, unlimited knowledge |
| 7 | وَسِعَ كُرسِيُّهُ السَّمَوَات والأَرض | His Kursi encompasses all creation |
| 8 | لَا يَؤُودُهُ حِفظُهُمَا | Preservation tires Him not |
| 9 | هُوَ العَلِيُّ العَظِيم | The Most High, the Most Great |

### Tafseer (Al-Mizan / Tabatabai)

Tabatabai's Al-Mizan devotes an extended discussion to this verse. His central insight: this verse presents Allah not as a distant cosmic force but as one who is continuously *engaged* with creation — the القَيُّوم (Al-Qayyum) sustains everything at every moment. Existence itself depends on His active, ongoing sustenance. If He withdrew attention for a moment, everything would cease. This is the most profound possible expression of divine immanence.

### When to Recite

- After every obligatory prayer → protected until the next prayer (Nasai)
- Before sleep → a guardian (angel) is assigned to protect you until morning (Bukhari)
- Entering home → poverty/evil is driven away (Al-Bayhaqi)

## 📖 Surah Yasin — سُورَةُ يَس (Chapter 36) — Introduction

Surah Yasin has 83 verses and is called "the Heart of the Quran" (قَلبُ القُرآن). The Prophet (sallallahu alayhi wa aalihi wasallam) said: *"Everything has a heart, and the heart of the Quran is Yasin."* (Tirmidhi)

### Themes of Surah Yasin

| Section | Verses | Theme |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Opening | 1-12 | The Quran, the Prophet's (sallallahu alayhi wa aalihi wasallam) mission, and those who reject guidance |
| The People of the Town | 13-32 | Story of prophets sent to a city — they were rejected and punished |
| Signs in Creation | 33-50 | Allah's signs in the earth, sky, sun, and moon |
| The Resurrection | 51-67 | Blowing of the trumpet, resurrection of the dead, Day of Judgment |
| Divine Power and Greatness | 68-83 | Allah's creative power; the command "Be and it is" (كُن فَيَكُون) |

### Key Verses

**Verse 40 — The cosmic order:**

<div style="font-size:1.8em;direction:rtl;text-align:center;line-height:2.5;font-family:'Amiri','Scheherazade New','Traditional Arabic',serif;color:#064e3b;margin:1rem 0;">
لَا ٱلشَّمۡسُ يَنۢبَغِي لَهَآ أَن تُدۡرِكَ ٱلۡقَمَرَ وَلَا ٱلَّيۡلُ سَابِقُ ٱلنَّهَارِۚ وَكُلٌّ فِي فَلَكٖ يَسۡبَحُونَ
</div>

*"It is not allowable for the sun to reach the moon, nor does the night overtake the day, and each in an orbit are swimming."*

**Verse 82 — The divine command:**

<div style="font-size:1.8em;direction:rtl;text-align:center;line-height:2.5;font-family:'Amiri','Scheherazade New','Traditional Arabic',serif;color:#064e3b;margin:1rem 0;">
إِنَّمَآ أَمۡرُهُۥٓ إِذَآ أَرَادَ شَيۡـًٔا أَن يَقُولَ لَهُۥ كُن فَيَكُونُ
</div>

*"His command is only when He intends a thing that He says to it 'Be!' and it is."*

### When to Recite Yasin

- For those who are ill or dying — the Prophet (sallallahu alayhi wa aalihi wasallam) encouraged recitation at the time of death
- On Thursday nights/Friday mornings — for seeking forgiveness and blessing
- For important needs — seeking Allah's help in difficulty

## 📖 Surah Al-Mulk — سُورَةُ المُلك (Chapter 67) — Introduction

Surah Al-Mulk (30 verses) is called "Al-Waqiya" (المَانِعَة — the Preventer/Protector). The Prophet (sallallahu alayhi wa aalihi wasallam) said: *"A surah of the Quran containing thirty verses interceded for a man until he was forgiven — it is 'Blessed is He in whose hand is the dominion.'"* (Abu Dawud, Tirmidhi — Surah 67)

### Theme Structure

| Section | Theme |
|---------|-------|
| Verses 1-5 | Allah's dominion, creation of death and life, creation of seven heavens |
| Verses 6-11 | Punishment of those who reject faith; their regret |
| Verses 12-22 | Signs of Allah; His knowledge; trust in Him |
| Verses 23-30 | The question: who can protect you if Allah withdraws help? |

### Opening Verse

<div style="font-size:1.8em;direction:rtl;text-align:center;line-height:2.5;font-family:'Amiri','Scheherazade New','Traditional Arabic',serif;color:#064e3b;margin:1rem 0;">
تَبَارَكَ ٱلَّذِي بِيَدِهِ ٱلۡمُلۡكُ وَهُوَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيۡءٖ قَدِيرٌ
</div>

*"Blessed is He in whose hand is dominion, and He is over all things competent."*

**Tabaarak** (تَبَارَك) — this word means "blessed beyond all measure, full of goodness, inexhaustible in blessing." It is used for Allah alone in the Quran.

### When to Recite Al-Mulk

- Every night before sleeping — the Prophet (sallallahu alayhi wa aalihi wasallam) never slept without reciting it (Ibn Masud)
- It intercedes for its reciter on the Day of Judgment

## 📖 Surah Al-Kahf — سُورَةُ الكَهف (Chapter 18) — Introduction

Surah Al-Kahf (110 verses) contains four major stories, each with a profound spiritual lesson:

| Story | Verses | Lesson |
|-------|--------|--------|
| People of the Cave | 9-26 | Fitnah of religion (persecution) — young men preserved by faith |
| The Two Gardens | 32-44 | Fitnah of wealth — the wealthy man forgot gratitude |
| Musa and Khidr | 60-82 | Fitnah of knowledge — even the Prophet Musa didn't know everything |
| Dhul-Qarnayn | 83-98 | Fitnah of power — the just king used power for good |

### The Friday Recitation

The Prophet (sallallahu alayhi wa aalihi wasallam) said: *"Whoever recites Surah Al-Kahf on Friday will have a light between himself and the Sacred House (Kaaba), which will illuminate him on the Day of Resurrection."* (Al-Bayhaqi)

And: *"Whoever memorises ten verses from the beginning of Surah Al-Kahf will be protected from the trial of the Dajjal."* (Muslim)

### Opening Verses

## آیۃ الکرسی — سب سے عظیم آیت

رسول اللہ صلی اللہ علیہ وآلہ وسلم نے فرمایا: "قرآن میں سب سے عظیم آیت کون سی ہے؟" جب ابی بن کعب نے آیۃ الکرسی کہا تو آپ صلی اللہ علیہ وآلہ وسلم نے فرمایا: "مبارک ہو اے ابوالمنذر اس علم پر!"

آیۃ الکرسی میں اللہ کی **نو** صفات ایک ساتھ:
- لَآ إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُو — وحدانیت
- الحَيُّ — ہمیشہ زندہ
- القَيُّوم — سب کا تھامنے والا
- نہ اونگھ نہ نیند — ہمیشہ بیدار
- آسمانوں اور زمین کا مالک
- لامحدود علم
- کُرسی کا پھیلاؤ — لامحدود
- حفاظت آسان — کوئی تھکاوٹ نہیں
- العَلِيُّ العَظِيم

**کب پڑھیں:** ہر فرض نماز کے بعد + سونے سے پہلے

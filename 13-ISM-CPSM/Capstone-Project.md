# Capstone Project, ISM CPSM (Certified Professional in Supply Management)

> **Format:** Self-directed; 4-8 weeks; ~30-45 hours of work
> **Deliverables:** 6 artifacts + a 12-slide executive readout
> **Scoring:** Use the rubric below to self-grade or peer-review with a CPSM colleague

---

## Brief

You have just been hired as **Vice President of Supply Management at Northrop Specialty Foods Co.**, a privately-held $1.4B specialty-foods manufacturer headquartered in Chicago. Northrop produces premium frozen, dry, and ambient products across 1,200 SKUs sold to grocery, club, and food-service channels in the US, Canada, EU, and the UAE. Direct-spend categories include grains, dairy ingredients, oils, sweeteners, herbs/spices, and packaging. Indirect categories include logistics, MRO, contract labor, marketing services, and IT.

**The situation you've walked into.** Eight months before your hire, Northrop issued a Class-I voluntary recall of 18 SKUs after a Listeria contamination was traced to a *sub-tier* supplier of dehydrated onion powder. The Tier-1 spice-blend supplier had subcontracted to a Tier-2 ingredient processor who in turn sourced from a Tier-3 farm cooperative, none of which appeared in Northrop's supplier master file. The recall cost ~$32M in direct expense, an estimated $90M in lost revenue from delisted SKUs, and immeasurable brand damage. The board has now hired you with a clear mandate from the CEO:

> "12 months. Stand up a strategic supply management function, taxonomy, scorecards, risk register, sustainability, capability, dashboard. I want this to be the company's *strength*, not the line item we hide from the board."

You inherit a team of 28 (mostly buyers and 3 sourcing managers), 240 suppliers across 18 countries, no formal category management practice, scattered ERP data, and a budget of $4.5M operating + $2M for tooling and consulting.

---

## Deliverables (6 artifacts + readout)

### 1. Spend Taxonomy + Kraljic Matrix (Modules 1, 2)

Build a **3-level spend taxonomy** (Family → Category → Sub-category) covering Northrop's roughly $830M of annual addressable spend. Assume 8 spend families. For each category at Level 2, classify into the appropriate **Kraljic quadrant** (Strategic / Leverage / Bottleneck / Non-critical). Write 1 paragraph per Level-2 category justifying the placement using profit impact + supply risk.

**Format:** Excel or markdown table with at least 24 Level-2 categories assigned to quadrants. Include a 1-page narrative covering the 5 highest-risk categories and what differentiates them.

### 2. Top-20 Supplier Scorecard + SRM Plan (Module 4)

Identify your top 20 suppliers by spend (assume 30-50% of total spend). For each, propose:

- **Tier classification** (Strategic / Preferred / Approved / Tail) with rationale
- **Scorecard dimensions and weights** (QCDS+Innovation+ESG+Risk)
- **Governance cadence** (QBR / MBR / nothing)
- **Owner** (which of your team members has accountability)

Then design a **SRM playbook** of ~3 pages defining the supplier-development criteria, escalation ladder, and exit-management process Northrop will use.

### 3. End-to-End Supply Chain Risk Register (Modules 6, 7)

Build a 30-50 row risk register covering: operational, financial, strategic, geopolitical, climate/natural, cyber, compliance/reputational, and brand risks. For each:

- Description, category, probability (1-5), impact (1-5), score
- Owner, treatment strategy (Avoid / Transfer / Mitigate / Accept), specific mitigation actions, status, review date

At minimum the register MUST include: the Listeria recall root cause, single-source dependencies, top-5 sub-tier visibility gaps, Red Sea / Suez routing exposure, FX exposure on EU/UAE sales, and EU CSRD compliance.

**Format:** Excel or markdown table.

### 4. Sustainability Scorecard Aligned to EU CSRD (Module 7)

Northrop ships into the EU. **EU CSRD** (effective January 2024 for large companies, phased through 2028) requires disclosure of supply-chain ESG performance. Design:

- A **supplier sustainability scorecard** Northrop will use across the top 50 suppliers
- A **data-collection plan** (what data, how often, from whom)
- **Scope 3 emissions baseline methodology** (GHG Protocol Corporate Standard)
- A **CSRD readiness gap analysis** highlighting the 5 biggest gaps and your 12-month plan to close them
- **UFLPA + UK Modern Slavery Act + Conflict Minerals (3TG)** policy alignment

**Format:** 4-6 pages of narrative + 1 scorecard template.

### 5. Capability Roadmap for Category Teams (Module 8)

Northrop's current team is mostly buyers. To execute Deliverables 1-4, you need 8-12 category managers with strategic-sourcing capability. Design:

- **Org design** (which categories get dedicated managers, which are shared, which stay tactical)
- **Capability framework** (skills, behaviors, certifications required by tier)
- **Development plan** for the existing team (training, rotations, CPSM certification subsidization)
- **Hiring plan** for the gap (how many, what level, what budget, work within the $4.5M operating)
- **Year-1 milestones** aligned to ADKAR (Awareness/Desire/Knowledge/Ability/Reinforcement)

**Format:** 5-7 pages.

### 6. Board-Readable Metrics Dashboard (Module 8)

Design a **1-page executive dashboard** Northrop's board will see quarterly. It must include:

- Spend Under Management (current vs target)
- Top-tier supplier scorecard summary (% green/yellow/orange/red)
- Risk register currency and top open risks
- Cost savings + cost avoidance (separately)
- ESG metrics: Scope 3 baseline + diversity spend + UFLPA compliance status
- Innovation pipeline value
- Capability progression (team member CPSM-certified count)

Then write a **2-page narrative** explaining each metric, its target, and its data source.

### 7. Executive Readout (the binder)

Compile Deliverables 1-6 into a **12-slide executive readout** + appendix. The 12 slides should narrate the transformation: where Northrop is today, where it needs to be in 12 months, the 6 workstreams, the budget, the risks, and the 90-day quick-win plan.

## Rubric (scored out of 100)

| Criterion | Points | Excellent (90-100%) | Acceptable (70-89%) | Below bar (<70%) |
|-----------|--------|---------------------|---------------------|------------------|
| **Spend taxonomy + Kraljic** | 15 | Three levels, ≥24 L2 categories, all justified with profit-impact + supply-risk reasoning, 5 highest-risk categories profiled in depth | All quadrants assigned, basic rationale, some L2 categories missing depth | Fewer than 16 categories or quadrant assignments without justification |
| **Top-20 SRM + playbook** | 15 | Tiers assigned, scorecard weights defensible, SRM playbook complete with development criteria + escalation + exit | Most suppliers classified, scorecard structurally sound, playbook present | Missing tiers, generic scorecard, no playbook |
| **Risk register** | 15 | 30+ rows, treatment matched to economics, Listeria recall root cause addressed, sub-tier risks called out, CSRD + Red Sea + FX included | 20+ rows, treatments reasonable, most major risks captured | <15 rows or treatments not matched to economics |
| **CSRD-aligned sustainability** | 15 | Scorecard + collection plan + Scope 3 methodology + gap analysis + UFLPA/MSA/3TG alignment all present and CSRD-aware | Most elements present, CSRD awareness shown | Missing CSRD framing, no Scope 3 methodology |
| **Capability roadmap** | 15 | Org design, capability framework, dev plan, hiring plan, ADKAR-aligned milestones, fits within budget | Most elements present, basic ADKAR awareness | Missing one or more elements, budget exceeded |
| **Board dashboard** | 15 | All 7 metrics + targets + data sources + 2-page narrative, audience-appropriate | Most metrics present, narrative reasonable | Single-axis (cost only) or missing data sources |
| **Executive readout** | 10 | 12 slides tell coherent story, 90-day quick-win plan present, risks honestly stated | Slides cover most material, some narrative gaps | Slides disconnected or missing 90-day plan |

**Pass mark:** 75/100 overall, with no individual criterion below 50%.

## Optional Stretch Goals

If you want to push beyond pass:

1. **Tier-3 visibility methodology.** Beyond the risk register, design a multi-tier mapping process. Document the data feeds you'd subscribe to (Resilinc, Everstream Analytics, Sphera, Interos) and the cost-benefit.
2. **AI-in-procurement pilot.** Define one specific pilot of an AI-driven sourcing tool (Coupa Navi, SAP Ariba AI, Jaggaer AI) with hypotheses, success metrics, and the 12-month rollout plan.
3. **Make-or-buy of a strategic category.** Pick one Strategic-quadrant category and do a full make-or-buy analysis with 10-year TCO including capability-erosion risk.
4. **Negotiation simulation.** Roleplay a sole-source negotiation with a peer using the ASML case (Module 3) as the template. Document BATNA, ZOPA, target, walk-away, and your counter-anchoring strategy.
5. **Audit lifecycle for a labor finding.** Apply the Foxconn/Apple case (Module 7) to a hypothetical labor finding at one of Northrop's top-20 suppliers. Document the full lifecycle: engagement, remediation, escalation, contractual action, public-disclosure decisions.

---

## Why this Capstone Matters

CPSM tests judgment under scenario pressure. This capstone forces you to integrate every module's frameworks into a single coherent transformation. If you can defend each deliverable to a peer or an ISM Mastery mentor, you will pass the three real CPSM exams comfortably, and more importantly, you'll be ready for the VP-of-Supply role this capstone simulates.

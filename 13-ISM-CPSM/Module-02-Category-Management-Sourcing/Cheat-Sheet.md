## 🎲 Kraljic Matrix (MEMORIZE COLD)

```
                              PROFIT IMPACT
                       LOW                HIGH
SUPPLY        ┌──────────────┬──────────────────┐
RISK          │  BOTTLENECK  │    STRATEGIC     │
HIGH          │  ensure      │    partner       │
              │  supply      │    long-term     │
              ├──────────────┼──────────────────┤
SUPPLY        │ NON-CRITICAL │     LEVERAGE     │
RISK          │  simplify    │   competitive    │
LOW           │  e-cat / Pcard│   bidding/auction│
              └──────────────┴──────────────────┘
```

| Quadrant | Sourcing Approach |
|---|---|
| Non-critical | Catalogs, P-cards, automation |
| Leverage | Competitive bid, e-auction, consolidation |
| Bottleneck | Alternate source, inventory, long-term contracts |
| Strategic | Partnership, joint dev, multi-year alliance |

## 💸 Spend Analysis — Quick Facts

- **Direct spend** = goes INTO the product (raw materials, components)
- **Indirect spend** = supports operations (IT, MRO, facilities, travel)
- 80/20 Pareto usually holds (80% of spend with 20% of suppliers)
- Maverick spend = off-contract / unauthorized buying

## 🧮 Sourcing Structures

| Structure | Definition |
|---|---|
| Single source | We chose one of many possible suppliers |
| Sole source | Only one supplier exists in the market |
| Dual source | 2 suppliers, primary + backup (70/30, 80/20) |
| Multi-source | 2+ concurrent suppliers |
| Consortium | Multiple buyers pool demand (GPO) |

---

## 📄 RFI vs RFP vs RFQ

| Document | Purpose | Output |
|---|---|---|
| RFI | Learn the market | Shortlist |
| RFP | Solve a complex/undefined need | Best-value award |
| RFQ | Get firm pricing on a defined spec | Lowest qualified price |

🧠 **Mnemonic:** I → P → Q (learn, then propose, then price)

```
Profile → Market analysis → Strategy → Solicit → Evaluate → Negotiate → Manage
```

## 📊 Weighted Scoring (Template)

| Criterion | Typical Weight |
|---|---|
| Total cost | 30–40% |
| Technical / capability | 20–30% |
| Quality | 10–20% |
| Delivery / lead time | 5–15% |
| Financial stability | 5–10% |
| ESG / diversity | 5–10% |
| Innovation | 5–10% |

Set weights BEFORE bids open.

## 📋 Specification Types

| Type | Use When |
|---|---|

| Design / technical | Form/fit must match |
| Brand-specific | Last resort (limits competition) |

## 🚦 Bullwhip Effect

Causes: forecast re-updating · order batching · price spikes · rationing/gaming
Fixes: share POS data · smaller frequent orders · stable pricing · visibility tools

---

## 🆚 Standardization vs Rationalization

- Standardization → reduce **variety of items**
- Rationalization → reduce **number of suppliers**

---

## 🏆 Exam Power Phrases (Usually Right)

- ✅ "Run a spend analysis first"
- ✅ "Match the sourcing tactic to the Kraljic quadrant"
- ✅ "Set weighted scoring criteria before bids open"
- ✅ "Use TCO, not unit price, in selection"
- ✅ "Don't reverse-auction strategic suppliers"

## ⚠️ Usually Wrong

- ❌ "Always reverse-auction"
- ❌ "Brand-name specs by default"
- ❌ "Lowest price wins"
- ❌ "Share weights with favorite supplier"
- ❌ "RFP and RFQ are interchangeable"

---

## ⚠️ Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Sourcing without spend analysis
- ❌ Treating every category the same
- ❌ Reverse-auctioning strategic categories
- ❌ Brand-name specs
- ❌ Setting weights after bids open
- ❌ Ignoring indirect spend

## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. Visibility into historical spend**
Spend analysis is the diagnostic that informs every downstream decision.

### Q2: **B. Competitive bidding / e-auctions**
Leverage = high profit impact, low risk → exploit your buying power. Save strategic partnerships for the strategic quadrant.

### Q3: **B. Single = chose one of many; sole = only one exists**
Memorize. This appears on practice exams routinely.

### Q4: **D. Strategic**
High profit impact + high supply risk = strategic quadrant. Partnership approach is appropriate.

### Q5: **C. Spec is clear and you need price**
RFQ assumes a known requirement; it's a price-comparison tool.

### Q6: **B. Leverage-quadrant commodities with multiple qualified suppliers**
The classic right environment. Avoid for strategic and bottleneck quadrants.

### Q7: **B. Off-contract / unauthorized buying**
Maverick spend bypasses agreements, hurts leverage, and reduces compliance.

### Q8: **B. Office cleaning services**
Indirect spend = not in the product sold. Cleaning is operational support.

### Q9: **B. Before issuing**
Setting weights after seeing bids invites bias and is an ethics violation.

### Q10: **A. Reduces variety of items**
Standardization = variants. Rationalization = suppliers.

### Q11: **B. Run a spend analysis**
You cannot strategize blind. Visibility first.

### Q12: **D. Forward-buying on every promotion**
Forward-buying AMPLIFIES the bullwhip, doesn't mitigate it.

### Q13: **D. Non-critical**
Low value, many suppliers, low risk = non-critical (handle via catalogs / P-cards).

### Q14: **A. Want supplier innovation**
Performance specs invite suppliers to propose how. Design specs lock in the how.

### Q15: **C. RFI**
Information first when the market is unknown.

### Q16: **C. Ensure supply (buffers, alternates, contracts)**
Bottleneck is about availability, not price savings.

### Q17: **B. Ethics violation**
Sharing weights with one bidder undermines fairness. Module 7 reinforces.

### Q18: **B. Profit impact and supply risk**
Memorize the axes.

### Q19: **B. Pooling demand for collective leverage**
Group Purchasing Organizations (GPOs), industry consortia.

### Q20: **B. Manufacturers**
Direct material drives COGS in manufacturers; services firms are mostly labor.

### Q21: **B. Two suppliers, primary/backup or split**
A standard risk-mitigation structure.

### Q22: **B. Balanced modern category strategy**
ESG and diversity weights reflect alignment with broader corporate goals.

### Q23: **B. Develop alternate source; design around; multi-year contract / make-or-buy**
Strategic response to a sole-source extortion. Litigation is rarely the answer to a commercial dispute.

### Q24: **B. Reduces the number of suppliers**
Memorize: rationalization = supplier base; standardization = items.

### Q25: **B. Underdeveloped, large untapped savings**
A classic CPSM theme. Don't ignore indirect spend.

### Q26: **B. Apply the pre-set weighted scoring honestly**
Lowest price isn't best value when quality and financial health matter. The pre-set weights make this defensible.

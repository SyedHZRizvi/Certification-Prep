# 📋 Module 1 Cheat Sheet: Supply Management Foundations

> One page. Print it. Tape it to your monitor. Review before each exam.

---

## 🎯 Purchasing vs. Procurement vs. Supply Management

| Term | Scope |
|---|---|
| Purchasing | Transactional act of buying |
| Procurement | Purchasing + selecting + contracting |
| Supply management | Strategic end-to-end value-creating function |
| Supply chain | Supply mgmt + logistics + operations + distribution |

---

## 🏛️ Four Organizational Models

| Model | Leverage | Responsiveness | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Centralized | Highest | Lowest | Single-product global firms |
| Decentralized | Lowest | Highest | Unrelated business units |
| Hybrid (center-led) | High | High | Most large diversified corps |
| Center of Excellence | Medium | Medium | Capability-building orgs |

---

1. Reactive  →  2. Mechanical  →  3. Proactive  →  4. Strategic  →  5. World-class
   (Clerical)    (Process disc.)   (Categories)    (Cross-funct.)   (Value creator)

## 🛡️ Porter's Five Forces (Quick Reference)

| Force | High = | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| New entrants | Easy entry | More supplier options |
| Supplier power | Few suppliers, no substitutes | Buyer at risk → dual-source |
| Buyer power | Concentrated buyers | Squeezes margins |
| Substitutes | Many | Pressure to design out cost |
| Rivalry | Fierce | Thin margins; supply differentiates |

🧠 Mnemonic: **"New Suppliers Buy Substitute Rivals"**

---

## 🔗 Porter's Value Chain

```
SUPPORT:  Firm infra · HR · Tech · Procurement
PRIMARY:  Inbound logistics → Operations → Outbound logistics → Marketing/Sales → Service
```

---

## 💰 The Five Rights

Right **Quality · Quantity · Time · Place · Price**

🧠 Mnemonic: **QQTPP** ("Quick Quotes Take Polished People")

---

## 🔬 Make-or-Buy Quick Matrix

| Lean MAKE | Lean BUY |
|---|---|
| Core competence | Non-core |
| High IP risk if outsourced | Low IP risk |
| Internal capacity available | At capacity |
| Need tight quality control | Mature supplier quality |
| Strategic to differentiation | Commodity |

---

## 🧰 Supply Management Process (11 Steps)

```
Need → Spec → Make-or-buy → Market analysis → Strategy → Solicit (RFI/RFP (Request for Proposal)/RFQ (Request for Quotation)) →
Evaluate → Negotiate/Contract → Manage performance → Pay/Close → Improve
```

---

## ⚠️ Anti-Patterns To Recognize

- ❌ Senior supply manager spending 60% on tactical work
- ❌ Reverse-auctioning innovation-critical suppliers
- ❌ Treating suppliers as adversaries
- ❌ Single-axis metric (price variance only)
- ❌ "Centralize everything always"
- ❌ Skipping qualification for an internal friend's supplier

---

## 🏆 Exam Power Phrases (Usually Correct)

- ✅ "Align supply strategy with corporate strategy"
- ✅ "Balance stakeholder needs while managing risk"
- ✅ "Engage cross-functionally early in design"
- ✅ "Total cost of ownership, not unit price"
- ✅ "Coach the team / educate the stakeholder"
- ✅ "Escalate ethical concerns; do not compromise policy"

## ⚠️ Usually Wrong

- ❌ "Award to the lowest bidder regardless"
- ❌ "Centralize everything"
- ❌ "Skip qualification this once"
- ❌ "Refer to the original spec; no changes"
- ❌ "Marketing decides supply policy"

---

## 🗓️ Key Facts

- Supply management = strategic, not transactional
- 4 org models · 5 maturity stages
- 5 Porter forces · 5 value-chain primary activities
- Procurement is a **support** activity in Porter's value chain
- ISM Principles of Sustainability & Social Responsibility apply across all modules

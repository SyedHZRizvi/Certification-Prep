## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B.** TCO = acquisition + operating + disposal.
### Q2: **B.** Bottom-up estimate vs. supplier quote.
### Q3: **B.** Market price - margin = target cost.
### Q4: **A.** VE = during design. VA = production.
### Q5: **B.** 80% curve = 80% of prior each doubling.
### Q6: **C.** Labor-intensive repetitive work shows the strongest learning curve.
### Q7: **B.** Forward = custom OTC. Future = standardized exchange-traded.
### Q8: **A.** Futures are standardized and exchange-traded.
### Q9: **B.** Options grant a right, not obligation, for a premium.
### Q10: **B.** Hedging = risk management, not speculation.
### Q11: **A.** 2% discount if paid within 10 days, else net 30.
### Q12: **D.** 2% × (365/20) ≈ 36.5% annualized.
### Q13: **B.** Should-cost / VA-VE / innovation, strategic levers.
### Q14: **B.** Savings vs avoidance, report distinctly.
### Q15: **B.** Indexed pricing balances risk; fixed for 5 years on volatile commodity is dangerous to both sides.
### Q16: **B.** Positive NPV = discounted inflows exceed investment.
### Q17: **B.** VA = production. VE = design.
### Q18: **B.** VA/VE, challenge over-specification.
### Q19: **B.** Vendor A: $35K + $11K fuel = $46K. Vendor B: $30K + $19K fuel = $49K. A wins on TCO even before maintenance & disposal.
### Q20: **A.** ROI = (Gain - Cost) / Cost × 100%.
### Q21: **B.** Hedge known exposures, match horizon, document, Treasury executes.
### Q22: **B.** Term extension is OK with healthy suppliers; offer SCF for cash-tight ones.
### Q23: **B.** Should-cost works through fact-based dialogue.
### Q24: **B.** Non-critical = automate. Don't over-invest.
### Q25: **B.** Honest reporting per finance policy. Don't conflate.
### Q26: **C.** $100 → $80 → $64 → $51.20 at unit 8.

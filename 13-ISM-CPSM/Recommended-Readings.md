# Recommended Readings, ISM CPSM Track

> **Purpose:** This curated list extends the per-module "Further Reading" sections with the full canonical bibliography for a CPSM professional. Use it during preparation, after certification for continuing education (60 CE hours every 3 years), and as the foundation of a personal reference library.

> **How to use:** Engage each item at the point in the course indicated. The single most important pre-exam purchase is the ISM Mastery Model official body of knowledge. Everything else compounds on top of that.

---

## Canonical Textbooks (3-5 must-haves)

### ISM Official Materials (start here)
- **ISM Mastery Model, *Foundation of Supply Management*** (latest CPSM edition; ISM Press; ismworld.org).
  - The official body of knowledge for Exam 1. If a question on this course's content contradicts the ISM blueprint, the ISM blueprint wins. Engage from Module 1 onward.
- **ISM Mastery Model, *Effective Supply Management Performance*** (latest CPSM edition).
  - Official body of knowledge for Exam 2. Read after Modules 1-4; deepens Modules 2-6.
- **ISM Mastery Model, *Leadership in Supply Management*** (latest CPSM edition).
  - Official body of knowledge for Exam 3. Read after Module 7; pairs directly with Module 8 and the Capstone.
- **ISM *Principles of Sustainability and Social Responsibility*** (free PDF at ismworld.org).
  - 10 principles you will see tested as judgment scenarios. Memorize. Engage during Module 7.

### Foundational Academic Texts
- **Johnson, P. Fraser & Leenders, Michiel R., *Purchasing and Supply Management*, 16th ed. (McGraw-Hill, 2020).**
  - The classic graduate-school textbook. Comprehensive coverage of every CPSM domain. Engage in parallel with Modules 1-8.
- **Christopher, Martin, *Logistics & Supply Chain Management*, 5th ed. (FT Publishing, 2016).**
  - The British counterpart to Johnson & Leenders. Particularly strong on global supply chain integration. Engage during Modules 2, 6, and 7.
- **Liker, Jeffrey K., *The Toyota Way: 14 Management Principles from the World's Greatest Manufacturer*, 2nd ed. (McGraw-Hill, 2020).**
  - The leadership-philosophy foundation behind modern supplier-development practice. Engage during Module 4 and Module 8 (see Module 4 and Module 8 case studies).
- **Vitasek, Kate & Keith, Bonnie, *Strategic Sourcing in the New Economy* (Palgrave Macmillan, 2016).**
  - The Vested outsourcing framework. Engage during Modules 4-5.
- **Goldratt, Eliyahu M., *The Goal: A Process of Ongoing Improvement*, 4th rev. ed. (North River Press, 2012; originally 1984).**
  - The foundational novel on Theory of Constraints. Required reading for anyone who manages capacity-constrained suppliers. Engage during Module 2 (bullwhip) and Module 5 (constraints).
- **Kim, Gene, Behr, Kevin & Spafford, George, *The Phoenix Project: A Novel About IT, DevOps, and Helping Your Business Win* (IT Revolution, 2018).**
  - The 21st-century *Goal*. Useful for the ops/SCM/IT crossover that defines modern e-procurement transformations. Engage alongside Module 8.

## Seminal Papers and HBR Articles (5-10 indispensable)

### Strategy and Sourcing
- **Porter, Michael E., *Competitive Strategy: Techniques for Analyzing Industries and Competitors* (Free Press, 1980).**
  - The five-forces and generic-strategy bible. Engage during Module 1.
- **Porter, Michael E., *Competitive Advantage: Creating and Sustaining Superior Performance* (Free Press, 1985).**
  - The value-chain text. Engage during Module 1.
- **Hayes, Robert H. & Wheelwright, Steven C., *Restoring Our Competitive Edge: Competing Through Manufacturing* (Wiley, 1984).**
  - The four-stage strategic-role model behind every supply-management maturity assessment. Engage during Module 1.
- **Hill, Terry, *Manufacturing Strategy: Text and Cases*, 3rd ed. (Palgrave Macmillan, 2007).**
  - The order-qualifier / order-winner framework. Engage during Module 1.
- **Kraljic, Peter "Purchasing Must Become Supply Management" *Harvard Business Review*, September-October 1983.**
  - The 10-page paper that defined modern category management. Re-read every time you place a category on a Kraljic quadrant. Engage during Module 2.
- **Fisher, Marshall L. "What Is the Right Supply Chain for Your Product?" *HBR*, March-April 1997.**
  - Functional vs innovative product classification. Engage during Module 2.
- **Hau Lee "The Triple-A Supply Chain" *HBR*, October 2004.**
  - Agility, Adaptability, Alignment. The 2004 framework that informs every modern resilience discussion. Engage during Modules 2 and 7.
- **Lee, Padmanabhan & Whang "Information Distortion in a Supply Chain: The Bullwhip Effect" *Management Science*, 1997.**
  - The academic foundation of the bullwhip effect. Engage during Module 2.

### Sustainability and Ethics
- **Elkington, John "Towards the Sustainable Corporation: Win-Win-Win Business Strategies for Sustainable Development" *California Management Review*, 1994.**
  - The Triple Bottom Line origin paper. Engage during Module 7.
- **Elkington, John "25 Years Ago I Coined the Phrase 'Triple Bottom Line.' Here's Why It's Time to Rethink It" *HBR*, June 2018.**
  - The author's own retrospective. Useful for discussion-prompt nuance. Engage during Module 7.
- **Carroll, Archie B. "The Pyramid of Corporate Social Responsibility: Toward the Moral Management of Organizational Stakeholders" *Business Horizons*, July-August 1991 (extending Carroll, *Academy of Management Review*, 1979).**
  - The CSR pyramid framework. Engage during Module 7.

### Negotiation
- **Fisher, Roger, Ury, William & Patton, Bruce, *Getting to Yes: Negotiating Agreement Without Giving In*, 3rd ed. (Penguin, 2011; original 1981).**
  - BATNA, ZOPA, and the principled-negotiation method. Engage during Module 3.
- **Malhotra, Deepak & Bazerman, Max, *Negotiation Genius* (Bantam, 2007).**
  - HBS-flavored case-driven negotiation playbook. Engage after Module 3.

### Change and Leadership
- **Kotter, John P. "Leading Change: Why Transformation Efforts Fail" *HBR*, March-April 1995.**
  - The original 8-step framework. Engage during Module 8.
- **Kotter, John P., *Leading Change* (HBS Press, 1996; updated 2012).**
  - The book-length treatment. Engage during Module 8.
- **Cialdini, Robert, *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion* (William Morrow, 1984; revised 2021).**
  - The six (now seven) principles of influence-without-authority. Engage during Module 8.

### Supply Chain Resilience (post-2020)
- **Reeves, Whitaker & Fink "How Resilient Businesses Created Advantage in Adversity" *HBR*, March 2022.**
  - Includes the Toyota chip-shortage case used in Module 4. Engage during Modules 4 and 7.
- **Sheffi, Yossi, *The Resilient Enterprise: Overcoming Vulnerability for Competitive Advantage* (MIT Press, 2007).**
  - Pre-pandemic, prescient. The MIT framework still anchors modern resilience thinking. Engage during Module 7.

### Practitioner Reports (free, annual or biennial)
- **Kearney *Assessment of Excellence in Procurement (AEP)*** biennial since 1992; the canonical maturity benchmark. Engage between modules.
- **Hackett Group *Procurement Performance Studies*** annual. Engage between modules.
- **McKinsey & Company *State of Procurement*** annual; usefully tracks 2024-2026 disruption. Engage during Modules 4-7.

## Industry Resources (3-5 worth subscribing to)

- **ISM *Inside Supply Management*** magazine, monthly; the practitioner's home publication. Free with ISM membership. Subscribe from Module 1.
- **CIPS *Supply Management***, the UK-based equivalent. Free articles online (cips.org); print subscription for members.
- **Procurement Leaders**, the executive-procurement community; case-driven research, events, networking. Useful from Module 4 onward.
- **Spend Matters** (spendmatters.com), the most-read independent procurement news + analysis outlet. Daily.
- **The Procurement Foundry**, an online community of senior practitioners; weekly newsletters and discussion forums.
- **McKinsey *Operations*** practice publications (mckinsey.com/capabilities/operations), free, high-quality. Engage during Modules 5-7.
- **BCG *Procurement* publications** (bcg.com/industries/operations), free, free-thinking. Engage during Modules 5-7.
- **The Hackett Group** (thehackettgroup.com), research reports and webinars on procurement performance.
- **Gartner Supply Chain** research (gartner.com), gated; some free summaries. Engage during Modules 4-8.
- **WorldCC** (worldcc.com), World Commerce & Contracting; free resources on contract management. Engage during Module 3.

## Free Academic Courses (2-3 that overlap with this material)

- **MIT Center for Transportation & Logistics, *MicroMasters in Supply Chain Management* (Yossi Sheffi & faculty; edX).**
  - Five graduate-level courses (Fundamentals, Logistics, Analytics, Design, Capstone). Roughly $1,500 for the full credential or free to audit individual courses. The single best free academic resource for supply chain. Engage during Modules 5-7 and Capstone.
- **Stanford Graduate School of Business, Supply Chain Operations courses** (some recordings free on Stanford GSB YouTube; faculty including Hau Lee). Engage between Modules 2-7.
- **London Business School, Supply Chain track** (some open courseware via LBS Insights; selected courses on Coursera).
- **MIT Sloan Management Review, *Supply Chain Frontiers* and *MITSloan-MR* article archive (mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter).**
  - Free with email signup; case studies and trend analyses. Read monthly.
- **Coursera *Supply Chain Management Specialization* (Rutgers)**, five-course series, audit free. Useful overlap with CPSM domains.
- **Wharton Online, *Operations Management* and related courses** on Coursera. Audit free.

## Standards and Regulatory References

- **Incoterms 2020**, *International Chamber of Commerce* (store.iccwbo.org). $50-200 for the official text. **Buy this.** Engage during Module 6.
- **UCC Article 2 (Sale of Goods)**, free online via Cornell Legal Information Institute (law.cornell.edu/ucc).
- **CISG (UN Convention on the International Sale of Goods)**, full text free via UNCITRAL.
- **Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR)**, free online (acquisition.gov).
- **ISO 31000:2018 (Risk Management Guidelines)** purchase from iso.org or read the free overview.
- **GHG Protocol Corporate Standard**, ghgprotocol.org. Free PDF. Engage during Module 7.
- **OECD *Due Diligence Guidance for Responsible Supply Chains***, free PDF. Engage during Module 7.
- **EU CSRD and CSDDD documentation**, eur-lex.europa.eu. Free official text. Engage during Module 7 and Capstone.
- **Responsible Minerals Initiative** (responsiblemineralsinitiative.org), Conflict Minerals Reporting Template (CMRT) and Extended Minerals Reporting Template (EMRT). Free.
- **UK Modern Slavery Act 2015 + UK government guidance**, free on gov.uk.
- **US Treasury OFAC sanctions list**, home.treasury.gov/policy-issues/financial-sanctions. Free, updated continuously.
- **Transparency International *Corruption Perceptions Index***, transparency.org/cpi. Annual, free.

## Recommended Reading Sequence

If you have time for only 6 books, read in this order:

1. **ISM Mastery Model, Foundation of Supply Management** (CPSM official)
2. **Johnson & Leenders, Purchasing and Supply Management** (textbook backbone)
3. **Liker, The Toyota Way** (leadership philosophy)
4. **Fisher, Ury & Patton, Getting to Yes** (negotiation)
5. **Kotter, Leading Change** (change management)
6. **Goldratt, The Goal** (constraints / operations thinking)

If you have time for only 3 articles, read:

1. **Kraljic 1983 HBR** ("Purchasing Must Become Supply Management")
2. **Hau Lee 2004 HBR** ("The Triple-A Supply Chain")
3. **Kotter 1995 HBR** ("Leading Change: Why Transformation Efforts Fail")

These three papers contain roughly 70% of the conceptual content tested on CPSM.

## Maintaining Your Certification

CPSM requires **60 continuing education hours every 3 years**. Acceptable activities include:

- ISM World annual conference (typically 25+ CE hours)
- ISM webinars (1-2 CE hours each, ~$50 each)
- Reading ISM publications (limited CE per year)
- Teaching or speaking at procurement events
- Earning related certifications (PMP, ASCM CSCP/CPIM/CLTD, SAVE International CVS)
- Graduate-level courses (e.g., MIT MicroMasters)

Plan your CE proactively, don't scramble in month 35.

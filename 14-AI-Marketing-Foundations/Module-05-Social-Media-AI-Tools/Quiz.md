## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. Content-quality signals (completion, rewatch, hook strength) outweigh follower count**
TikTok's defining algorithmic feature. New accounts can break out on content quality alone.

### Q2: **B. Posts with outbound links**
LinkedIn actively suppresses link posts to keep users on-platform. Standard workaround: put the link in the first comment.

### Q3: **B. Post on 2–3 platforms with mastery**
Mastery > coverage. The widely-taught best practice across HubSpot, Meta Blueprint, and Google's Digital Marketing Cert.

### Q4: **B. Micro (10K–100K)**
Years of HypeAuditor + Influencer Marketing Hub data consistently identify micro-influencers as the highest-ROI tier for most brands.

### Q5: **B. A virtual / AI influencer**
The most-cited example of the AI / virtual influencer category. Real brand deals; entirely synthetic persona.

### Q6: **C. Pinterest**
Pinterest is the unusual social platform that explicitly drives outbound clicks. It's a discovery/search engine more than a feed.

### Q7: **B. Studying the platform algorithm and building native creative**
Duolingo's TikTok success came from understanding what TikTok rewarded in 2021–2022 and building format-native creative around it.

### Q8: **B. AI writers focused on LinkedIn / X**
Both are AI ghostwriters / creator tools specifically for LinkedIn (Taplio) and X / LinkedIn (Postwise).

### Q9: **B. (Likes + comments + shares) ÷ followers (or impressions)**
Standard engagement-rate calculation. Some platforms use impressions in the denominator.

### Q10: **B. Click-through rate + average view duration + retention**
YouTube's primary signals. Subscriber count is secondary.

### Q11: **B. Brand voice as a competitive advantage**
The 2017–2018 Wendy's Twitter run is the canonical brand-voice-as-moat case study (HBR's Quelch, 2018).

### Q12: **B. Document carousels + polls**
Both are LinkedIn-native formats that over-perform vs plain text or external links.

### Q13: **B. Positive / neutral / negative**
Standard three-class sentiment classification. More advanced tools layer emotion or topic classification on top.

### Q14: **B. ~70% female audience and strong commercial intent**
Pinterest's demographic is heavily female and shopping-oriented, the strongest commercial-intent social platform.

### Q15: **D. Snapchat**
Snapchat is owned by Snap Inc. Meta owns Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp, Threads (and previously Messenger).

### Q16: **B. "For You Page"**
TikTok's main algorithmic surface.

### Q17: **A. Sentiment tools struggle with sarcasm and cultural context**
A well-known limitation of automated sentiment classifiers.

### Q18: **A. Mention or Brand24**
Both are SMB-priced social listening / brand monitoring tools.

### Q19: **B. They boost reach and unlock follower-conversion + newsletter features**
LinkedIn explicitly designs these to boost reach for active creators.

### Q20: **A. LinkedIn, X, TikTok**
Each weights early engagement heavily in determining how widely to distribute a post.

### Q21: **B. Native uploads are favored; external links are downranked**
Across Meta, LinkedIn, X, and most social platforms in 2026, native content beats external links.

### Q22: **B. Influencer tier ROI benchmarks and creator-economy data**
Their reports are the most-cited industry benchmarks.

### Q23: **B. 3–5 recurring topic buckets that map to audience interest**
Content pillars give the strategy structure and ensure topical coverage.

### Q24: **B. Apply to AI-generated endorsements; brands must disclose**
The FTC has confirmed disclosure rules apply to AI-generated and AI-influenced endorsements. Module 9 covers this in depth.

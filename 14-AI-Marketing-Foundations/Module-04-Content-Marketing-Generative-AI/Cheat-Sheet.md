## 📚 The Three Content Strategy Frameworks

| Framework | Source | Use For |
|---|---|---|
| **Pillar / Cluster** | HubSpot | Site-wide content architecture |
| **JTBD** | Clayton Christensen (Harvard) | What "job" the reader hires content to do |
| **Content Matrix** (2×2) | Chaffey / Smart Insights | Coverage of Awareness↔Decision × Rational↔Emotional |

---

## 🎯 The 8-Step AI-Assisted Workflow

```
1. Strategy        → Pillar/cluster + keyword map
2. Brief           → AI-generated outline + SEO analysis
3. Drafting        → AI long-form draft
4. Human edit      → Voice, facts, brand, structure
5. SEO polish      → Surfer / Frase / Clearscope
6. Visual assets   → AI hero image + supporting graphics
7. Publish + distribute → Email, social, repurpose
8. Measure + iterate → Update winners, sunset losers
```

## 🚦 Editorial Checklist (Print This)

- [ ] Facts verified (stats, names, years, citations)
- [ ] Brand voice consistent
- [ ] Originality (not regurgitated)
- [ ] Specificity (real examples, real numbers)
- [ ] Point of view present
- [ ] Logical structure + scannable
- [ ] SEO (title, meta, H1/H2/H3, internal links, schema)
- [ ] Visual(s) added
- [ ] Clear CTA (Call to Action)
- [ ] Compliance (no PII; AI disclosure if needed)

## 📊 Two Anchor Case Studies

- **Mailchimp AI subject-line generator** → embedded AI in workflow, proprietary data = winning model
- **BuzzFeed AI content (2023)** → over-reliance without human editorial = cautionary model

---

## 🚨 Common Traps

- ❌ Pillar / cluster reversed
- ❌ Synthesia confused with Runway (avatars vs B-roll)
- ❌ Treating AI translation = localization
- ❌ Trusting AI stats without verification
- ❌ "More content" without brand voice = AI slop
- ❌ Ignoring Adobe Firefly's commercial-licensing advantage

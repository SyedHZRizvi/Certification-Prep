## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **D. Bidding SEO**
The three pillars are on-page, technical, and off-page. "Bidding" is a paid-ads concept, not SEO.

### Q2: **C. Experience**
December 2022: Google added "Experience" to E-A-T, giving E-E-A-T. Reflects emphasis on first-hand experience.

### Q3: **A. INP**
INP replaced FID as the responsiveness Core Web Vital in March 2024.

### Q4: **B. ≤ 2.5 seconds**
LCP "good" threshold per Google: 2.5 seconds or less.

### Q5: **A. Your Money or Your Life**
YMYL content (health, finance, legal, safety) is held to a higher E-E-A-T bar by Google's quality raters.

### Q6: **B. JSON-LD in a `<script>` tag**
JSON-LD is Google's recommended format for schema markup.

### Q7: **B. HubSpot**
HubSpot coined and popularized the "pillar / cluster" content topology in the late 2010s.

### Q8: **B. Programmatic SEO**
Database + template = millions of unique neighborhood pages. The canonical case.

### Q9: **B. "Best [Category] for [Use Case]"**
TOFU = informational, research-phase. Transactional and comparison queries are MOFU/BOFU.

### Q10: **B. Generic TOFU "what is X" posts with no first-hand expertise**
AI Overviews directly answer these queries above the fold. Pages without unique experience get bypassed.

### Q11: **B. Meaning, entities, and topical comprehensiveness**
Semantic SEO is post-2018 — Google embeddings + entity recognition, not keyword density.

### Q12: **C. Scaled Content Abuse policy (March 2024)**
Google's March 2024 spam update introduced this policy specifically to target scaled AI content.

### Q13: **C. `Product` + `Offer` + `AggregateRating`**
Standard e-commerce schema combo.

### Q14: **C. Local SEO**
Google Business Profile is the foundation of local SEO.

### Q15: **B. Indicating which language/region version of a page to show**
hreflang is the international SEO tag for multi-language / multi-country sites.

### Q16: **B. It was deprecated by Google in 2016**
The publicly visible Toolbar PageRank was killed in 2016. PageRank still exists internally as a signal.

### Q17: **B. Surfer SEO**
Surfer scores your draft against the top SERP results using NLP and content-grading heuristics.

### Q18: **A. search.google.com/test/rich-results**
Google's free Rich Results Test tool.

### Q19: **B. Adding citations to authoritative sources and FAQ sections**
Both are highlighted in the 2024 Princeton/Harvard/Allen Institute GEO paper as among the highest-impact tactics for AI-engine citation.

### Q20: **B. The mobile version of the site for ranking decisions**
Mobile-first indexing since 2019, universal by 2026.

### Q21: **D. FID**
FID was officially replaced by INP in March 2024.

### Q22: **A. Indicates the preferred version of duplicate or near-duplicate pages**
The canonical tag tells Google which URL is the "real" one.

### Q23: **A. Founding HubSpot and popularizing inbound marketing**
MIT Sloan-affiliated co-founders of HubSpot, authors of *Inbound Marketing* (Wiley, 2009).

### Q24: **B. Lack of human editing + scaled, generic, low-utility output**
Google's stance is "useful content is fine regardless of how it's produced; spam at scale gets demoted." Several public cases (Bankrate, CNET) illustrated this.

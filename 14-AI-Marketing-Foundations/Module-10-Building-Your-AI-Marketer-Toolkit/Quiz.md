## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **C. CDP**
Segment is a Customer Data Platform. Klaviyo (ESP), HubSpot (MA + CRM), WordPress (CMS) are different categories.

### Q2: **C. n8n**
n8n is open-source and self-hostable. Zapier and Make are SaaS.

### Q3: **D. Punchcard**
Not a thing in modern stacks. Foundation LLM, CDP, CRM, ESP, CMS, analytics, dashboards, automation — those are the real layers.

### Q4: **C. An all-in-one marketing automation (MA) platform + CRM**
HubSpot bundles CRM + marketing hub + sales hub + service hub.

### Q5: **C. Knowledge management / second brain / project hub**
Notion is a flexible KM tool that many small teams use as the central command center.

### Q6: **B. Iterable / Braze / Salesforce / Adobe**
These four dominate mid-market and enterprise cross-channel lifecycle marketing.

### Q7: **B. Video / audio recording + AI editing**
Riverside (recording), Descript (transcript-based editing), Captions (mobile video AI).

### Q8: **B. Tiago Forte**
Forte's *Building a Second Brain* (2022) popularized the term in productivity culture.

### Q9: **B. Looker Studio is free; Power BI's free tier is limited**
Looker Studio (Google) is fully free; Power BI free tier is limited to individual use.

### Q10: **B. Synthesia**
AI avatar talking-head video. Midjourney = images, ElevenLabs = voice, Otter = transcription.

### Q11: **B. Make.com / Zapier / n8n**
Workflow automation tools are the connective tissue between standalone apps.

### Q12: **C. Users explicitly volunteering preferences (surveys, preference centers)**
Zero-party = volunteered. Coined by Forrester in 2018.

### Q13: **B. Notion**
Stripe famously uses Notion as the company-wide hub.

### Q14: **A. Stack audit (cancel bottom 20% by ROI)**
A recurring discipline that saves $200–$1,000+/month even in small stacks.

### Q15: **B. AI voice / audio**
ElevenLabs leads in voice synthesis and cloning.

### Q16: **A. Microsoft Clarity**
Free Microsoft tool with heatmaps + session replay; equivalent functionality to Hotjar's paid tiers.

### Q17: **B. Beehiiv (or ConvertKit) + ChatGPT/Claude + Buffer + Notion + Canva**
A realistic, capable solo creator stack inside $0–$100/month.

### Q18: **B. Reusable, version-controlled prompts produce more predictable, higher-quality outputs over time**
The prompt library is your unfair advantage. Compounds with use.

### Q19: **D. CRM Schema Designer**
The 10 prompts in the module include Blog Brief, Repurposing, Subject Line, Persona Refiner, Ad Copy Generator, Voice Translator, Competitor Teardown, SEO Outline, Boring-to-Engaging Email, Weekly Review.

### Q20: **A. Make has visual scenarios and more complex flows; Zapier is simpler and more apps**
The standard differentiation.

### Q21: **A. Segment, mParticle, Tealium, BlueConic, Lytics**
The major CDP players. ESPs and CRMs are different categories.

### Q22: **B. Documentation is a competitive advantage AI tools build on top of**
The deeper insight: your AI is only as good as the docs (RAG) it reads.

### Q23: **B. Heatmaps and session replay**
The category. Hotjar paid; Clarity free; FullStory enterprise.

### Q24: **B. Master a small stack, build a prompt + asset library (second brain), and automate compounding workflows**
The thesis of the whole module.

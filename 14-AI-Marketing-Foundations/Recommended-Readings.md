# Recommended Readings, AI Digital Marketing Foundations Track

> **Purpose:** This curated list extends the per-module "Further Reading" sections with the canonical bibliography for an AI-augmented marketing practitioner. Use it during preparation, after passing the three industry credentials (HubSpot / Google / Meta Blueprint), and as the foundation of a personal reference library.

> **How to use:** Engage each item at the point in the course indicated. The single most important free anchor text is Google's Digital Marketing & E-commerce Professional Certificate on Coursera. Everything else compounds on top of that.

---

## Canonical Textbooks (3-5 must-haves)

### Marketing Psychology & Strategy
- **Cialdini, Robert B., *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion*, Revised ed. (Harper Business, 2021; original 1984).**
  - The six (now seven) principles of influence, reciprocity, commitment, social proof, authority, liking, scarcity, unity. The 7th principle ("unity") was added in 2021. Every copywriter, lifecycle marketer, and paid-creative strategist memorizes these. Engage during Modules 1, 4, 6.
- **Berger, Jonah, *Contagious: Why Things Catch On* (Simon & Schuster, 2013).**
  - The STEPPS framework (Social currency, Triggers, Emotion, Public, Practical value, Stories). The Wharton-grade explanation of why content spreads. Engage during Modules 4, 5.
- **Sheridan, Marcus, *They Ask, You Answer*, 2nd ed. (Wiley, 2019).**
  - The single best book on content marketing as a sales-enablement strategy. The "Big 5" buyer-question framework still informs every B2B content calendar in 2026. Engage during Module 4.
- **Miller, Donald, *Building a StoryBrand: Clarify Your Message So Customers Will Listen* (HarperCollins Leadership, 2017).**
  - The 7-part SB7 framework. Every landing page in this course can be improved by passing it through StoryBrand's lens. Engage during Module 4 and the Capstone.
- **Hong, Arvid Kahl, *The Embedded Entrepreneur: How to Build an Audience-Driven Business* (self-published, 2021).**
  - The audience-first product playbook. Particularly strong on ICP definition through community immersion. Engage during the Capstone's ICP memo.

### Foundational Marketing Texts
- **Kotler, Philip & Keller, Kevin Lane, *Marketing Management*, 16th ed. (Pearson, 2022).**
  - The graduate-school textbook. STP (Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning), 4Ps/7Ps, brand equity, channel theory. Engage in parallel with Modules 1-7.
- **Ryan, Damian, *Understanding Digital Marketing: A Complete Guide to Engaging Customers and Implementing Successful Digital Campaigns*, 6th ed. (Kogan Page, 2024).**
  - The European counterpart to Kotler, but digital-native. Useful for GDPR + ePrivacy context. Engage during Modules 1, 8, 9.

## Seminal Papers and HBR Articles (5-10 indispensable)

### Strategy and Positioning
- **Porter, Michael E., *Competitive Strategy: Techniques for Analyzing Industries and Competitors* (Free Press, 1980).**
  - The five-forces and generic-strategy bible. Engage during Module 1 to ground your understanding of what marketing's role even is.
- **Trout, Jack & Ries, Al, *Positioning: The Battle for Your Mind*, 20th anniv. ed. (McGraw-Hill, 2001; original 1981).**
  - The positioning canon. Defines the difference between "what your product does" (features) and "where it lives in the customer's mind" (positioning). Engage during Module 1 and the Capstone.
- **Christensen, Clayton M., Hall, Taddy, Dillon, Karen & Duncan, David, *Competing Against Luck: The Story of Innovation and Customer Choice* (HarperBusiness, 2016).**
  - The Jobs-to-Be-Done (JTBD) framework. Engage during the Capstone's ICP memo and Module 4 content positioning.

### Performance Marketing
- **Doerr, John, *Measure What Matters: How Google, Bono, and the Gates Foundation Rock the World with OKRs* (Portfolio, 2018).**
  - The OKR framework adopted from Andy Grove's Intel. Engage during Module 1 and the Capstone's OKR scorecard.
- **Binet, Les & Field, Peter, *The Long and the Short of It: Balancing Short and Long-Term Marketing Strategies* (IPA, 2013, updated 2017).**
  - The 60/40 brand-to-performance budget split based on IPA Effectiveness Awards data. Engage during Module 7.
- **Sharp, Byron, *How Brands Grow: What Marketers Don't Know* (Oxford University Press, 2010).**
  - The Ehrenberg-Bass Institute's empirical laws of marketing, mental availability, physical availability, distinctive brand assets. The most challenged-and-defended book in marketing science of the last 15 years. Engage during Modules 1, 4, 7.

### Practitioner Reports (free, annual)
- **McKinsey & Company, *The State of Marketing* (annual, 2024-2026 editions free at mckinsey.com).**
  - Annual look at where CMOs are investing and where AI is reshaping the function. Engage between modules.
- **WARC, *Marketer's Toolkit* (annual; warc.com).**
  - Free with email signup. Industry-wide trend report combining ad-spend forecasts with creative-effectiveness research. Engage during Modules 1, 7.
- **HubSpot, *The State of Marketing* (annual, 2024-2026 editions free at hubspot.com/marketing-statistics).**
  - Practitioner-focused; includes the most-cited AI-adoption statistics in the industry. Engage between modules.
- **Salesforce, *State of Marketing AI* (annual; salesforce.com).**
  - Free; useful counterpart to McKinsey. Engage between modules.

### AI for Marketers
- **Ng, Andrew *AI for Everyone* + *Generative AI for Everyone* (DeepLearning.AI, 2019/2024 free on Coursera).**
  - The most marketer-friendly intro to LLMs, RAG, fine-tuning, and the realistic limits of AI in business contexts. Engage during Module 2.
- **Beuker, Igor, *The AI Advantage: Master the AI Revolution in Marketing* (self-published, 2024).**
  - Practitioner-grade tour of the AI marketing tool stack. Useful for Module 10 toolkit construction.

## Recommended Reading Sequence

If you have time for only 5 books, read in this order:

1. **Cialdini, *Influence*** (the psychology backbone)
2. **Miller, *Building a StoryBrand*** (the messaging system)
3. **Berger, *Contagious*** (the virality framework)
4. **Sheridan, *They Ask, You Answer*** (the content philosophy)
5. **Ng, *Generative AI for Everyone*** (the AI literacy layer)

If you have time for only 3 reports, read:

1. **HubSpot, *State of Marketing 2024 / 2025 / 2026*** (whichever is most recent)
2. **McKinsey, *The State of Marketing*** (latest edition)
3. **WARC, *Marketer's Toolkit*** (latest edition)

These three combined contain roughly 70% of the trend intelligence that informs senior marketing-leadership conversations in 2026.

## Maintaining Your Skills

Marketing certifications expire faster than most. Plan to:

- Retake HubSpot Marketing Software Certification (free) every 1-2 years.
- Retake Google Ads, GA4, and Meta Blueprint certifications as they're updated (1-2 year cycles).
- Subscribe to at least 2 of the industry resources above and consume one per workday.
- Block 2 hours/week for "the AI cycle", testing one new AI tool, reading one new piece of AI marketing research.

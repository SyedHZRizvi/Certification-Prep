# Module 8: Business Professional English (C1)

## Overview
This module targets C1-level professionals who need English for high-stakes business contexts: writing formal documents, leading presentations, negotiating deals, and managing meetings. Includes templates designed specifically for Pakistani professionals working with multinational companies.

## 2. Business Presentations

### Opening a Presentation:
- "Good morning, everyone. Thank you for making time today."
- "The purpose of today's presentation is to outline..."
- "By the end of this session, you will have a clear picture of..."
- "I'll be covering three main areas, and I'll leave time for questions at the end."

### Signposting (guiding your audience through):
- "Let's start by looking at..."
- "Moving on to our second point..."
- "This brings me neatly to..."
- "As you can see from this slide..."
- "To illustrate this, let me give you an example..."
- "I'd like to draw your attention to the figures on the right."
- "Before I continue, are there any questions so far?"

### Closing a Presentation:
- "To summarise the key takeaways..."
- "In conclusion, the evidence strongly supports..."
- "The next step is for us to..."
- "I'll now open the floor for questions."
- "Thank you for your time and attention."

---

## 3. Negotiation Language

### Opening Negotiation:
- "We're keen to find a solution that works for both parties."
- "Let's explore the options available to us."
- "I think there's real scope for agreement here."

### Making Offers and Counteroffers:
- "What we can offer is a 15% discount on orders above 500 units."
- "If you could meet us halfway on the price, we'd be prepared to extend the warranty."
- "We're flexible on the timeline — what matters most to us is the unit cost."

### Conceding and Compromising:
- "We're willing to adjust our position on that."
- "Let's find a middle ground."
- "I can go as low as X, but that's our absolute floor."
- "If you can guarantee volume, we can work on the price."

### Rejecting Politely:
- "I'm afraid that doesn't work for us at this stage."
- "We're not in a position to accept those terms, but here's what we can do..."
- "That's not quite where we need to be, but I appreciate the offer."

### Closing Negotiation:
- "I think we're very close to an agreement."
- "Shall we put that in writing?"
- "Let's draw up a heads of agreement and have legal review it."

## 4. Meeting Chairmanship

When chairing a meeting, you control the flow, ensure participation, and keep on track.

**Opening:**
- "I'd like to call this meeting to order."
- "Let's begin with the first agenda item."

**Inviting contributions:**
- "Let's hear from Ahmed on this."
- "I'd like to bring in Sara at this point."
- "Has anyone else a view on this?"

**Managing time:**
- "We're running slightly over — let's move on."
- "I'll have to cut you off there, but it's an important point — can we pick it up later?"
- "Shall we move to the next item?"

**Handling conflict:**
- "Let's stay focused on the issue rather than the personalities."
- "I hear both sides — let's look at this objectively."

**Closing:**
- "Let me summarise the actions agreed today."
- "Minutes will be circulated within 24 hours."
- "Thank you all — meeting adjourned."

## 6. IELTS Writing Task 2 — Essay Structure

IELTS Writing Task 2 requires a 250-word academic essay. The structure below works for opinion, discussion, and problem-solution essays.

### The 4-Paragraph Structure:

**Paragraph 1 — Introduction (50 words)**
- Paraphrase the question topic.
- State your position or what you will discuss.
- "In today's increasingly interconnected world, [topic] has become a subject of significant debate. This essay will argue that..."

**Paragraph 2 — Main Point 1 (80 words)**
- Topic sentence stating the point.
- Explanation/development.
- Example (concrete and specific).
- "The primary reason for this is... For instance, in Pakistan, where..."

**Paragraph 3 — Main Point 2 / Counter-argument (80 words)**
- Introduce the opposing or second point.
- Concede if appropriate.
- Refute or extend.
- "Conversely, opponents argue that... While this has some merit, it overlooks the fact that..."

**Paragraph 4 — Conclusion (40 words)**
- Summarise main arguments.
- Restate position.
- Do NOT introduce new information.
- "In conclusion, while [counterpoint] has validity, the evidence strongly suggests that [your position]."

## 7. Email Etiquette

### Opening and closing conventions:

| Formality | Opening | Closing |
|---|---|---|
| Very formal | Dear Mr./Ms. [Surname], | Yours faithfully, |
| Formal | Dear [First name], | Yours sincerely, |
| Semi-formal | Hi [First name], | Kind regards, / Best regards, |
| Informal | Hi [Name], | Best, / Thanks, |

### Key rules:
- Subject line: specific and professional (not "Hello" or "Query")
- First paragraph: state your purpose immediately
- One topic per email where possible
- Close with a clear call to action: "Please confirm by..." / "I look forward to your response."
- Proofread before sending — spelling errors damage professional credibility

## 8. LinkedIn Profile English

**Headline (120 characters):** Job title + value proposition
- "Senior Software Engineer | AWS & Cloud Infrastructure | 7 Years in Enterprise Tech"

**About Section (2,600 characters):**
- Open with a strong hook: "I help companies..."
- Your top 2–3 achievements (quantified)
- What you're currently focused on
- Call to action: "Open to [type of opportunity] — connect with me."

**Experience descriptions:**
- Start each bullet with an action verb: Led, Built, Delivered, Managed, Reduced, Achieved
- Quantify: "Reduced deployment time by 60%"
- Avoid: generic phrases like "responsible for" or "worked on"

---

## 9. CV/Resume Writing

### Key principles:
1. One or two pages maximum
2. Reverse chronological order (most recent first)
3. Quantify achievements wherever possible
4. Use industry keywords (important for ATS systems)
5. No photos, age, or marital status in UK/US CVs
6. Tailor your CV for each application

### Strong CV language:

| Weak | Strong |
|---|---|
| Responsible for sales | Drove 35% year-on-year sales growth |
| Worked in a team | Led cross-functional team of 8 engineers |
| Helped with projects | Delivered 3 enterprise projects on time, under budget |
| Good communication skills | Presented to C-suite executives at Board level |

## ۲. کاروباری پریزنٹیشن

### پریزنٹیشن کھولنا:
- "صبح بخیر۔ آج وقت نکالنے کا شکریہ۔"
- "آج کی پریزنٹیشن کا مقصد... کا خاکہ پیش کرنا ہے۔"
- "اس سیشن کے اختتام تک آپ کو... کی واضح تصویر ہوگی۔"

### راستہ دکھانا (Signposting):
- "آئیں پہلے... دیکھتے ہیں۔" (Let's start by looking at...)
- "دوسرے نقطے کی طرف بڑھتے ہیں۔" (Moving on to our second point...)
- "اس سلائیڈ پر اعداد و شمار دیکھیں۔" (As you can see from this slide...)

### پریزنٹیشن ختم کرنا:
- "اہم نتائج خلاصہ کرتے ہوئے..." (To summarise the key takeaways...)
- "خلاصہ یہ ہے کہ..." (In conclusion...)
- "میں اب سوالات کے لیے فرش کھولتا ہوں۔" (I'll now open the floor for questions.)

## ۶. IELTS رائٹنگ ٹاسک ۲ — مضمون کی ساخت

### چار پیراگراف کی ساخت:

**پیراگراف ۱ — تعارف (۵۰ الفاظ)**

**پیراگراف ۲ — اہم نکتہ ۱ (۸۰ الفاظ)**

- وضاحت / ترقی

**پیراگراف ۳ — اہم نکتہ ۲ / جوابی دلیل (۸۰ الفاظ)**
- مخالف نقطہ نظر پیش کریں
- اگر مناسب ہو تو تسلیم کریں

**پیراگراف ۴ — نتیجہ (۴۰ الفاظ)**
- اہم دلائل خلاصہ کریں

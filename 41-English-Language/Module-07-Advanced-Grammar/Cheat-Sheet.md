# Module 7 Cheat Sheet — Advanced Grammar (B2)

## Conditional Sentences — All Types Compared

| Type | Condition | Result | Example | Use |
|------|-----------|--------|---------|-----|
| Zero | Present Simple | Present Simple | If you heat ice, it melts. | General truths |
| First | Present Simple | will + base | If it rains, I will stay home. | Real, likely future |
| Second | Past Simple | would + base | If I had more time, I would travel. | Imaginary present |
| Third | Past Perfect | would have + past participle | If I had studied, I would have passed. | Unreal past |
| Mixed | Past Perfect | would + base | If she had trained, she would be ready now. | Past cause, present result |

**Key formula — Third Conditional:**
> **If + had + past participle, would have + past participle**

**Key formula — Mixed Conditional:**
> **If + had + past participle, would + base verb (+ now/today)**

## Passive Voice — All Tenses

| Tense | Formula | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Present Simple | am/is/are + past participle | Letters **are written** every day. |
| Present Continuous | am/is/are being + past participle | The report **is being written**. |
| Present Perfect | have/has been + past participle | The contract **has been signed**. |
| Past Simple | was/were + past participle | The bridge **was built** in 1990. |
| Past Continuous | was/were being + past participle | The suspect **was being questioned**. |
| Past Perfect | had been + past participle | The files **had been deleted**. |
| Future Simple | will be + past participle | The results **will be announced**. |
| Future Perfect | will have been + past participle | By Friday it **will have been completed**. |
| Modal | modal + be + past participle | The form **must be submitted** online. |

**Passive agent:** "by + noun" is used only when the agent is important.
> "The painting **was created by** Picasso." ✓
> "The letter **was written** (by someone)." — agent omitted when unimportant.

## Inversion — Patterns and Triggers

| Negative/Limiting Adverb | Inversion Structure | Example |
|--------------------------|--------------------|----|
| Never | Never + aux + subject + verb | **Never have I** seen such talent. |
| Rarely / Seldom | Rarely + aux + S + V | **Rarely does she** arrive on time. |
| Hardly / Scarcely | Hardly + had + S + past participle + when... | **Hardly had I** sat down when the phone rang. |
| No sooner | No sooner + had + S + pp + than... | **No sooner had he** left than it rained. |
| Not only | Not only + aux + S + V ... but also | **Not only did she** win, but she also set a record. |
| Only after | Only after + clause + inversion | **Only after the meeting** did I understand. |
| Under no circumstances | Under no circumstances + aux + S + V | **Under no circumstances should you** leave. |
| Should (formal "if") | Should + S + verb (instead of "if") | **Should you** need help, call us. |

## Reported Speech — Tense Backshift Table

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech |
|--------------|----------------|
| "I am tired." | She said she **was** tired. |
| "I am working." | He said he **was working**. |
| "I have finished." | She said she **had finished**. |
| "I will call you." | He said he **would** call me. |
| "I can help." | She said she **could** help. |
| "I may come." | He said he **might** come. |
| "I must leave." | She said she **had to** leave. |
| "Open the door." | He told me **to open** the door. |
| "Don't be late." | She told me **not to be** late. |

**Time expression changes in reported speech:**

| Direct | Reported |
|--------|---------|
| today | that day |
| tomorrow | the next day / the following day |
| yesterday | the day before / the previous day |
| now | then |
| this week | that week |
| here | there |

## Third Conditional — Common Errors to Avoid

| Wrong | Correct |
|-------|---------|
| If I would have known... | **If I had known...** |
| If he had studied, he would pass. | **If he had studied, he would have passed.** |
| She had gone if she knew. | **She would have gone if she had known.** |

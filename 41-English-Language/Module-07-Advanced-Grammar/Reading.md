# Module 7: Advanced Grammar (B2)

## Overview
This module covers the grammar structures that separate intermediate learners from advanced speakers. Master these and your English will feel natural, precise, and sophisticated.

---

## 1. Perfect Tenses with Timeline Diagrams

### Present Perfect
**Form:** has/have + past participle  
**Use:** Actions connected to now — either just completed, or with ongoing relevance.

```
PAST ←────────────────────●──────────────→ NOW
                     (happened at
                    some point before)
```

- "I **have lived** in Lahore for ten years." (still living there)
- "She **has submitted** her report." (just done; relevant now)
- "**Have** you **ever visited** London?" (life experience — some point before now)

**Urdu note:** Urdu uses the present perfect naturally ("میں نے دیکھا ہے"), but the exact range of English usage (connecting past to present) needs practice.

---

### Past Perfect
**Form:** had + past participle  
**Use:** The action that happened BEFORE another past action.

```
EARLIER PAST ←──●─────────●──────────→ NOW
             (had done)  (did — past simple)
```

- "By the time I arrived, she **had already left**."
- "He **had studied** English for two years before he moved to the UK."
- "They **hadn't eaten** anything when the food arrived."

---

### Future Perfect
**Form:** will have + past participle  
**Use:** An action that will be completed before a specific future point.

```
NOW ──────────────────●──────→ FUTURE POINT
                  (will have done)
```

- "By Friday, I **will have finished** the project."
- "She **will have worked** here for 20 years next March."

---

## 2. Conditionals

### Zero Conditional — General Truths
**Form:** If + present simple, present simple  
**Use:** Facts, scientific truths, habits.

- "If you heat water to 100°C, it **boils**."
- "If I eat sugar, I **get** a headache."

---

### First Conditional — Real Future Possibility
**Form:** If + present simple, will + base verb  
**Use:** Likely or possible future events.

- "If she **studies** hard, she **will pass** the exam."
- "If it **rains** tomorrow, we **won't go** to the park."

---

### Second Conditional — Unreal Present/Future
**Form:** If + past simple, would + base verb  
**Use:** Hypothetical, unlikely, or imaginary situations.

- "If I **had** a million dollars, I **would travel** the world."
- "If she **were** the CEO (Chief Executive Officer), things **would be** different."

**Note:** "Were" (not "was") is grammatically correct for all subjects in second conditional: "If I **were** you..." is standard formal English.

---

### Third Conditional — Unreal Past
**Form:** If + past perfect, would have + past participle  
**Use:** Imagining a different past — regret, criticism, speculation.

- "If I **had studied** harder, I **would have passed**."
- "If she **hadn't called**, I **would have missed** the meeting."

---

### Mixed Conditional
**Use:** Combines past condition with present result, or present condition with past result.

- "If I **had taken** that job (past), I **would be** in London now (present)."
- "If she **were** more organised (present), she **would have finished** on time (past)."

---

### Conditional Summary Table

| Type | Condition | Result | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Zero | If + present | Present | Always true |
| First | If + present | Will + base | Real future possibility |
| Second | If + past | Would + base | Unreal present/future |
| Third | If + past perfect | Would have + PP | Unreal past |
| Mixed | If + past perfect | Would + base | Past condition, present result |

---

## 3. Reported Speech (Direct → Indirect)

When reporting what someone said, verbs shift back one tense.

### Tense Shift Rules

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech |
|---|---|
| "I **am** tired." | He said he **was** tired. |
| "I **work** here." | She said she **worked** there. |
| "I **have eaten**." | He said he **had eaten**. |
| "I **will come**." | She said she **would come**. |
| "I **can** do it." | He said he **could** do it. |
| "I **may** leave." | She said she **might** leave. |

### Time and Place References Shift

| Direct | Reported |
|---|---|
| now | then |
| today | that day |
| yesterday | the day before |
| tomorrow | the next day / the following day |
| here | there |
| this | that |

### Examples:
- Direct: "I am studying for my exam."  
  Reported: She said **she was studying** for her exam.

- Direct: "We will finish by Friday."  
  Reported: They said **they would finish** by Friday.

- Direct: "I haven't seen the report."  
  Reported: He said **he hadn't seen** the report.

### Questions in Reported Speech
- Direct: "Where do you work?"  
  Reported: She asked him **where he worked**. (no question mark; normal word order)

- Direct: "Did you submit the form?"  
  Reported: He asked **whether/if I had submitted** the form.

---

## 4. Relative Clauses

Relative clauses give extra information about a noun.

### Defining Relative Clauses (essential information — no commas)
- "The woman **who called** yesterday is my manager."
- "The report **that I wrote** needs editing."
- "The city **where I grew up** is Lahore."

### Non-Defining Relative Clauses (extra information — use commas)
- "My sister, **who lives in London**, is a doctor."
- "The project, **which took six months**, was a success."

### Relative Pronoun Guide

| Pronoun | Used for | Example |
|---|---|---|
| who | people | The man **who** called you |
| which | things/animals | The book **which** I read |
| that | people or things (defining only) | The car **that** he bought |
| where | places | The office **where** I work |
| whose | possession | The student **whose** essay won |
| whom | object (formal) | The director **whom** I met |

---

## 5. Gerunds vs Infinitives

This is one of the most challenging areas for Urdu speakers because Urdu handles verbal nouns differently.

### Verbs followed by GERUND (verb + -ing)
- enjoy, avoid, finish, consider, suggest, keep, recommend, practise, mind, admit, deny
- "I **enjoy swimming**."
- "She **avoided answering** the question."
- "He **finished writing** the report."

### Verbs followed by INFINITIVE (to + base verb)
- want, need, hope, plan, decide, agree, refuse, learn, expect, manage, promise
- "I **want to improve** my English."
- "She **decided to leave** early."
- "He **agreed to attend** the meeting."

### Verbs that change meaning: GERUND vs INFINITIVE

| Verb | Gerund | Infinitive |
|---|---|---|
| remember | I remember **locking** the door. (past action) | Remember **to lock** the door. (future reminder) |
| forget | I forgot **calling** him. (past action) | I forgot **to call** him. (didn't do it) |
| stop | He stopped **smoking**. (quit) | He stopped **to smoke**. (paused in order to) |
| try | Try **adding** salt. (experiment) | Try **to add** less. (make an effort) |

## 7. The Subjunctive

The subjunctive is a formal grammatical mood expressing necessity, recommendation, or hypothetical situations.

**Form:** Base form of verb (no -s, no tense changes)

**After expressions of necessity/recommendation:**
- "It is essential that he **be** present."
- "We recommend that she **take** the course."
- "The committee demands that the report **be** submitted by Friday."
- "It is vital that everyone **attend** the briefing."

**Urdu note:** Urdu has a similar subjunctive form. The English subjunctive sounds formal and is common in written reports, legal documents, and business proposals.

---

## 8. Inversion for Emphasis

Inversion means placing the auxiliary before the subject for emphasis. It is formal and used in speeches, essays, and literature.

### Negative Adverbials at the Start:
- "**Not only** did he fail the test, but he also refused to retake it."
- "**Rarely** have I seen such dedication."
- "**Never** before **has** the company faced such challenges."
- "**Hardly** had she sat down **when** the phone rang."
- "**No sooner** had we arrived **than** it started to rain."
- "**Under no circumstances** should you sign without reading."
- "**In no way** does this reflect our policy."

### Conditional Inversion (formal alternatives to "if"):
- "**Had I known**, I would have acted differently." (= If I had known)
- "**Should you need** assistance, please call us." (= If you should need)
- "**Were I in your position**, I would resign." (= If I were in your position)

---

## 9. Cleft Sentences

Cleft sentences split one clause into two to emphasise specific information.

### It-cleft:
- Normal: "Sara solved the problem."
- Cleft: "**It was Sara who** solved the problem." (emphasising the person)
- Cleft: "**It was the problem that** Sara solved." (emphasising what she solved)

### What-cleft (Pseudo-cleft):
- Normal: "I need your help."
- Cleft: "**What I need is** your help."
- "**What surprised me was** his reaction."
- "**What we require** is a clear plan."

### All-cleft:
- "**All I want is** a straight answer."
- "**All she did was** smile."

Cleft sentences are extremely useful in formal writing, presentations, and debates to direct the listener's attention.

# ماڈیول ۷: اعلیٰ گرامر (B2)

## جائزہ
یہ ماڈیول وہ گرامر ڈھانچے شامل کرتا ہے جو درمیانی سطح کے سیکھنے والوں کو اعلیٰ بولنے والوں سے الگ کرتے ہیں۔ ان پر عبور حاصل کریں اور آپ کی انگریزی قدرتی، درست اور نفیس محسوس ہوگی۔

---

## ۱. مکمل زمانے (Perfect Tenses) اور ٹائم لائن خاکے

### حال مکمل (Present Perfect)
**شکل:** has/have + past participle
**استعمال:** وہ افعال جو ابھی سے جڑے ہوں — یا ابھی مکمل ہوئے ہوں، یا ابھی بھی متعلق ہوں۔

```
ماضی ←────────────────────●──────────────→ ابھی
                     (کسی وقت پہلے ہوا)
```

- "میں لاہور میں دس سال سے رہ رہا ہوں۔" (I have lived in Lahore for ten years.)
- "اس نے اپنی رپورٹ جمع کر دی ہے۔" (She has submitted her report.)

**اردو نوٹ:** اردو میں حال مکمل قدرتی طور پر استعمال ہوتا ہے ("میں نے دیکھا ہے")، لیکن انگریزی کا مخصوص استعمال (ماضی کو حال سے جوڑنا) مشق سے آتا ہے۔

---

### ماضی مکمل (Past Perfect)
**شکل:** had + past participle
**استعمال:** وہ فعل جو کسی اور ماضی کے فعل سے پہلے ہوا۔

```
پہلے کا ماضی ←──●─────────●──────────→ ابھی
             (had done)  (past simple — کیا)
```

- "جب میں پہنچا، وہ پہلے ہی جا چکی تھی۔" (By the time I arrived, she had already left.)
- "وہ برطانیہ جانے سے پہلے دو سال انگریزی پڑھ چکا تھا۔"

---

### مستقبل مکمل (Future Perfect)
**شکل:** will have + past participle
**استعمال:** ایک مخصوص مستقبل کے وقت سے پہلے مکمل ہونے والا فعل۔

- "جمعے تک میں پروجیکٹ مکمل کر چکا ہوں گا۔" (By Friday, I will have finished the project.)

---

## ۲. شرطیہ جملے (Conditionals)

### صفر شرطیہ — عمومی سچائیاں
**شکل:** If + present simple, present simple
**استعمال:** حقائق، سائنسی سچائیاں، عادات۔
- "اگر پانی کو ۱۰۰ ڈگری تک گرم کریں تو وہ ابلتا ہے۔"

---

### پہلا شرطیہ — حقیقی مستقبل کا امکان
**شکل:** If + present simple, will + base verb
**استعمال:** ممکن مستقبل کے واقعات۔
- "اگر وہ محنت سے پڑھے تو امتحان پاس کر لے گی۔"

---

### دوسرا شرطیہ — غیر حقیقی حال/مستقبل
**شکل:** If + past simple, would + base verb
**استعمال:** فرضی، غیر ممکن، یا خیالی حالات۔
- "اگر میرے پاس دس لاکھ ڈالر ہوتے، تو میں دنیا کی سیر کرتا۔"

**نوٹ:** دوسرے شرطیہ میں تمام ضمیروں کے ساتھ "were" (was نہیں) گرامر کے لحاظ سے درست ہے: "If I **were** you..." معیاری رسمی انگریزی ہے۔

---

### تیسرا شرطیہ — غیر حقیقی ماضی
**شکل:** If + past perfect, would have + past participle
**استعمال:** مختلف ماضی کا تصور — پچھتاوا، تنقید، قیاس۔
- "اگر میں نے زیادہ محنت کی ہوتی تو پاس ہو جاتا۔"

---

## ۳. بالواسطہ تقریر (Reported Speech)

کوئی جب بات نقل کرتا ہے تو فعل ایک زمانہ پیچھے چلا جاتا ہے۔

### زمانے کی تبدیلی کے اصول

| براہ راست تقریر | بالواسطہ تقریر |
|---|---|
| "میں تھکا ہوا ہوں۔" | اس نے کہا وہ تھکا ہوا تھا۔ |
| "میں یہاں کام کرتا ہوں۔" | اس نے کہا وہ وہاں کام کرتی تھی۔ |
| "میں آؤں گا۔" | اس نے کہا وہ آئے گی (would come)۔ |

### مثالیں:
- براہ راست: "میں اپنے امتحان کے لیے پڑھ رہی ہوں۔"
  بالواسطہ: اس نے کہا **وہ اپنے امتحان کے لیے پڑھ رہی تھی**۔

---

## ۴. متعلق شقیں (Relative Clauses)

متعلق شقیں کسی اسم کے بارے میں اضافی معلومات دیتی ہیں۔

### تعریفی متعلق شق (ضروری معلومات — کوما نہیں)
- "وہ عورت **جس نے کل فون کیا** میری مینیجر ہے۔"

### غیر تعریفی متعلق شق (اضافی معلومات — کوما کے ساتھ)
- "میری بہن، **جو لندن میں رہتی ہے**، ڈاکٹر ہے۔"

### متعلق ضمیر کی رہنما جدول

| ضمیر | کس کے لیے | مثال |
|---|---|---|
| who | لوگوں کے لیے | وہ آدمی جس نے آپ کو فون کیا |
| which | چیزوں/جانوروں کے لیے | وہ کتاب جو میں نے پڑھی |
| where | جگہوں کے لیے | وہ دفتر جہاں میں کام کرتا ہوں |
| whose | ملکیت کے لیے | وہ طالب علم جس کا مضمون جیتا |

---

## ۵. Gerund بمقابلہ Infinitive

یہ اردو بولنے والوں کے لیے سب سے مشکل شعبوں میں سے ایک ہے۔

### GERUND (verb + -ing) کے بعد آنے والے فعل
enjoy, avoid, finish, consider, suggest, keep, recommend, practise, mind, admit, deny
- "مجھے تیرنا پسند ہے۔" (I enjoy swimming.)
- "اس نے سوال کا جواب دینے سے گریز کیا۔" (She avoided answering.)

### INFINITIVE (to + base verb) کے بعد آنے والے فعل
want, need, hope, plan, decide, agree, refuse, learn, expect, manage, promise
- "میں اپنی انگریزی بہتر کرنا چاہتا ہوں۔" (I want to improve my English.)

---

## ۷. Subjunctive (ترجیحی صیغہ)

Subjunctive ایک رسمی گرامر موڈ ہے جو ضرورت، سفارش، یا فرضی حالات ظاہر کرتا ہے۔

**شکل:** فعل کی بنیادی شکل (کوئی -s نہیں، کوئی زمانے کی تبدیلی نہیں)

- "ضروری ہے کہ وہ موجود **رہے**۔" (It is essential that he be present.)
- "ہم تجویز کرتے ہیں کہ وہ یہ کورس **کرے**۔" (We recommend that she take the course.)

## ۸. تاکید کے لیے Inversion

Inversion کا مطلب ہے تاکید کے لیے معاون فعل کو موضوع سے پہلے رکھنا۔ یہ رسمی ہے اور تقریروں، مضامین، اور ادب میں استعمال ہوتا ہے۔

### ابتداء میں منفی زمانہ:
- "**نہ صرف** وہ امتحان میں ناکام ہوا، بلکہ اس نے دوبارہ دینے سے انکار بھی کیا۔" (Not only did he fail...)
- "**کبھی کبھار** ہی میں نے ایسی لگن دیکھی ہے۔" (Rarely have I seen...)

## ۹. Cleft جملے

Cleft جملے ایک شق کو دو میں تقسیم کرتے ہیں تاکہ مخصوص معلومات کو نمایاں کیا جائے۔

### It-cleft:
- معمول: "سارہ نے مسئلہ حل کیا۔"
- Cleft: "**یہ سارہ تھی جس نے** مسئلہ حل کیا۔" (شخص پر زور)

### What-cleft:
- معمول: "مجھے آپ کی مدد چاہیے۔"
- Cleft: "**جو چیز مجھے چاہیے وہ ہے** آپ کی مدد۔" (What I need is your help.)

## Reading Passage

Read the passage below and answer questions 1–3.

> Sara is a teacher. She works at a school in Lahore. Every morning, she wakes up at 6 o'clock. She has breakfast with her family and then she goes to school by car. She teaches English and Mathematics. Her students are between 8 and 12 years old. Sara loves her job. After school, she goes home, cooks dinner, and reads a book before sleeping.

---

## Section 6: Sentence Correction (Questions 13–15)

**13.** Choose the correct sentence:

A) She don't like vegetables.  
B) She doesn't like vegetables.  
C) She not like vegetables.  
D) She isn't like vegetables.

---

**14.** Which sentence uses the past simple correctly?

A) Yesterday, I go to the market.  
B) Yesterday, I gone to the market.  
C) Yesterday, I went to the market.  
D) Yesterday, I was go to the market.

---

**15.** Choose the correct preposition:

A) in / on  
B) at / on  
C) on / at  
D) at / in

---

## Answer Key

```
1.  B — "She works at a school in Lahore" and "She teaches English and Mathematics" — she is a teacher.
2.  C — "She goes to school by car" is stated directly in the passage.
3.  B — "She goes home, cooks dinner, and reads a book before sleeping."
4.  B — "Pleased to meet you" is the standard greeting phrase.
5.  B — Cheap is the antonym of expensive. Costly means the same as expensive.
6.  C — Apple, banana, and mango are fruits. A table is furniture, not food.
7.  B — "An engineer" — use "an" before words starting with a vowel sound.
8.  B — "I have a headache" — headache is a countable noun and needs the article "a".
9.  B — Third person singular (she) takes -s in present simple: "she works".
10. C — Present continuous = subject + is/am/are + verb-ing: "He is studying."
11. B — "I am fine, thank you. And you?" is the standard polite response to "How are you?"
12. C — The correct response gives directions, which is what the question asks for.
13. B — Negative with "she" uses "does not / doesn't": "She doesn't like vegetables."
14. C — Past simple of "go" is "went": "I went to the market."
15. B — "at" is used with times (at 3 o'clock); "on" is used with days (on Monday).
```

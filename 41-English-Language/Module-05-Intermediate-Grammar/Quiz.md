## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

| # | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **C** | go → **went** (irregular). "Goed" and "gone" are wrong for simple past. |
| 2 | **B** | eat → **ate** (irregular). "Eaten" is past participle (perfect tense), not past simple. |
| 3 | **C** | Negative past: did not + base verb. "didn't **go**" — NOT "didn't went." |
| 4 | **B** | Background action interrupted by "the phone rang" → Past Continuous: **was watching**. |
| 5 | **B** | "I was sleeping when she arrived." — Past Continuous (background) + Past Simple (event). |
| 6 | **B** | Spontaneous decision made at the moment of speaking → **will**. "I'll make pasta." |
| 7 | **C** | Plan already decided before speaking → **are going to**. |
| 8 | **B** | Ability in the past → **could**. "Can" is present ability. |
| 9 | **C** | Advice/recommendation → **should**. "Must" is stronger obligation. |
| 10 | **C** | Forbidden → **must not**. This is a prohibition. |
| 11 | **B** | Optional, not required → **don't have to**. "Must not" = forbidden (very different!). |
| 12 | **C** | 50% possibility → **might**. "Must" = certainty. "Will" = prediction. |
| 13 | **B** | tall → **taller** (1-syllable adjective: add -er for comparative). |
| 14 | **D** | good → **the best** (irregular superlative). |
| 15 | **B** | intelligent (3 syllables) → **more intelligent** (use "more" for long adjectives). |
| 16 | **C** | **Water** is uncountable. Book, car, apple are all countable. |
| 17 | **B** | **Much** is used with uncountable nouns in negatives/questions. "Many" = countable. |
| 18 | **C** | **Many** is used with countable nouns (students can be counted). |
| 19 | **B** | "She **always** eats breakfast." — adverb goes before main verb. |
| 20 | **C** | "He is **rarely** late." — adverb goes after "to be". |
| 21 | **D** | **So** shows result: "I was tired, **so** I went to bed." |
| 22 | **B** | **Although** introduces a contrasting clause: "Although it was raining, we walked." |
| 23 | **C** | Passive past: **was written** (was + past participle). |
| 24 | **B** | "The house **was built** by Ahmed" — subject (house) receives the action. |
| 25 | **C** | Positive statement → negative tag: "You're coming, **aren't you?**" |
| 26 | **B** | Positive statement → negative tag: "It's cold, **isn't it?**" |

## Future Forms Comparison

| | will | going to | Pres. Continuous |
|--|------|----------|-----------------|
| **Decision** | Made now | Already made | Already arranged |
| **Use** | Predictions, promises, offers | Plans, evidence-based predictions | Fixed appointments |
| **Example** | I'll help you. | I'm going to study. | I'm meeting Sara at 3. |

## Modal Verbs Usage Table

| Modal | Positive Meaning | Negative Meaning |
|-------|-----------------|-----------------|
| can | ability (present) | cannot = no ability |
| could | past ability / polite request | couldn't = no past ability |
| should | advice | shouldn't = bad advice |
| must | strong obligation / certainty | must not = FORBIDDEN |
| have to | external obligation | don't have to = NOT NECESSARY |
| might | low possibility (50%) | might not = probably won't |
| may | formal possibility / permission | may not = not allowed (formal) |

**Key distinction:** must not ≠ don't have to
- "You **must not** enter." (forbidden)
- "You **don't have to** enter." (optional — your choice)

## Comparatives and Superlatives

| Type | Comparative | Superlative | Example |
|------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| 1-syllable | + er | the + est | tall → taller → the tallest |
| 1-syl CVC | double + er | double + est | big → bigger → the biggest |
| 2-syl -y | + ier | + iest | happy → happier → the happiest |
| 2+ syllables | more + adj | the most + adj | beautiful → more beautiful → the most beautiful |
| Irregular: good | better | the best | — |
| Irregular: bad | worse | the worst | — |
| Irregular: far | farther/further | the farthest | — |

**"than"** for comparative: Ahmed is taller **than** Sara.
**"the"** for superlative: She is **the** best student.

## Adverbs of Frequency

| Adverb | % | Position |
|--------|---|---------|
| always | 100% | Before main verb / After "to be" |
| usually | 80–90% | Same |
| often | 60–70% | Same |
| sometimes | 30–50% | Same |
| rarely | 10–20% | Same |
| never | 0% | Same |

- I **always** drink tea. (before main verb)
- She **is** always **late**. (after to be)

## Passive Voice Formula

| Tense | Formula | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Present Simple | is/are + past participle | Houses **are built** here. |
| Past Simple | was/were + past participle | The letter **was written**. |
| Present Continuous | is/are being + past participle | Dinner **is being cooked**. |

## Question Tags Reference

| Statement Type | Tag Type | Examples |
|---------------|----------|---------|
| Positive (is/are) | isn't/aren't | It's hot, **isn't it?** |
| Positive (was/were) | wasn't/weren't | She was here, **wasn't she?** |
| Positive (do/does) | don't/doesn't | He works, **doesn't he?** |
| Positive (did) | didn't | They left, **didn't they?** |
| Positive (can) | can't | She can drive, **can't she?** |
| Negative | positive tag | You're not coming, **are you?** |
| I am | aren't I? | I'm late, **aren't I?** |

## Linking Words Summary

| Type | Words | Example |
|------|-------|---------|
| Adding | and, moreover, in addition, also | She is smart **and** hardworking. |
| Contrasting | but, however, although, yet | I'm tired, **but** I'll help. |
| Reason | because, since, as | Late **because** of traffic. |
| Result | so, therefore, thus | Tired, **so** I slept early. |
| Example | for example, such as | Fruits, **for example**, mangoes. |

# Module 10 Cheat Sheet — Mastery & Fluency (C1-C2)

---

## Debate Language — Phrases by Function

| Function | Phrases |
|----------|---------|
| Introducing argument | "I would argue that..." / "It is my contention that..." / "The evidence strongly suggests..." |
| Conceding a point | "Admittedly, there is some truth in this..." / "I grant that..." / "While I acknowledge that..." |
| Rebutting | "However, this fails to account for..." / "On closer examination..." / "The evidence, in fact, points to the contrary." |
| Strongly disagreeing | "With respect, I would challenge this claim." / "I find this argument fundamentally flawed." |
| Summarising your position | "In light of the foregoing evidence, I maintain that..." |
| Calling for action | "It is incumbent upon us to..." / "We would do well to..." |
| Acknowledging complexity | "The issue is undeniably complex; nevertheless..." |

---

## Argumentation Structures

| Structure | How it works | Signal phrases |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Concession-rebuttal | Acknowledge opponent's point, then counter it | "While X is true, Y must also be considered." |
| Evidence + Analysis | State claim, provide evidence, interpret it | "The data indicates X. This suggests that..." |
| Cause and effect | Explain what causes what | "As a consequence of X, Y has occurred." |
| Comparison | Contrast two positions | "In contrast to X, Y demonstrates..." |
| Rhetorical question | Engage audience by posing a question | "Can we truly afford to ignore this evidence?" |

## Rhetorical Devices — Reference Table

| Device | Definition | Example |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Anaphora | Repetition at beginning of successive clauses | "We shall fight... we shall fight... we shall fight..." |
| Tricolon | List of three parallel items | "Government of the people, by the people, for the people." |
| Chiasmus | Reversed grammatical structure | "Ask not what your country can do for you, ask what you can do for your country." |
| Rhetorical question | Question not expecting an answer | "How long can we afford to remain silent?" |
| Antithesis | Contrasting ideas in parallel structure | "It was the best of times, it was the worst of times." |
| Aphorism | A concise, memorable truth | "The unexamined life is not worth living." |

## Hedging vs. Asserting — Calibration Scale

| Level | Language | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Highly assertive | "This proves that..." / "It is undeniable that..." | Factual scientific laws |
| Assertive | "The evidence demonstrates that..." | Strong research findings |
| Hedged | "The data suggests that..." / "It appears that..." | Most academic claims |
| Cautious | "It might be argued that..." / "There is reason to believe..." | Tentative conclusions |
| Very cautious | "One possible interpretation is..." | Speculation |

## Common Logical Fallacies (for Debate)

| Fallacy | What it is | Latin / Name |
|---------|-----------|--------------|
| Ad hominem | Attacking the person, not the argument | Ad hominem |
| Straw man | Misrepresenting the opponent's argument | Straw man |
| False dichotomy | Presenting only two options when more exist | Either-or fallacy |
| Slippery slope | Claiming one event leads inevitably to extreme results | Slippery slope |
| Ad populum | "Everyone believes it, so it must be true" | Bandwagon |
| Appeal to authority | Using irrelevant authority as evidence | Argumentum ad verecundiam |

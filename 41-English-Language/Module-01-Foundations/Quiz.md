## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

| # | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **C** | V makes the /v/ sound. W sounds like "oo" and B is a different sound entirely. |
| 2 | **B** | forty-seven — Note: "forty" has no 'u', unlike "four". |
| 3 | **C** | E is a vowel. The 5 vowels are A, E, I, O, U. |
| 4 | **C** | "Good morning" is used from waking up until around noon. |
| 5 | **B** | "Nice to meet you" is the standard response when introduced to someone. |
| 6 | **D** | "He is my friend" — He uses "is". She/It also use "is". I uses "am". You/We/They use "are". |
| 7 | **C** | C = /k/ before a, o, u (cat, cold, cut) and /s/ before e, i (city, cent). |
| 8 | **B** | "I am not tired" — "not" goes after am/is/are. |
| 9 | **C** | Thursday comes after Wednesday (Mon, Tue, **Wed**, **Thu**, Fri, Sat, Sun). |
| 10 | **C** | "Good evening" is used after 5 pm. "Good night" is for leaving at night/bedtime. |
| 11 | **B** | Fifteen (15). Fifty = 50. |
| 12 | **B** | Water = پانی |
| 13 | **C** | English uses SVO: Subject (I) + Verb (eat) + Object (food). |
| 14 | **D** | Green = سبز. Blue = نیلا, Yellow = پیلا, Red = سرخ. |
| 15 | **C** | Mother = ماں. Father = باپ. |
| 16 | **B** | Questions are formed by moving is/am/are to the beginning: "Is he...?" |
| 17 | **C** | November comes after October (Oct → Nov → Dec). |
| 18 | **B** | "Nice to meet you" is a greeting for first-time meetings. |
| 19 | **D** | "cake" has a long /eɪ/ sound. cat, bit, hop all have short vowels. |
| 20 | **C** | "You're welcome" is the reply to "Thank you". |
| 21 | **C** | G sounds like /dʒ/ before e and i: gem, giraffe, giant. |
| 22 | **B** | Urdu has no articles (a, an, the), so speakers forget to include them. |
| 23 | **B** | "They" uses "are": "Are they students?" |
| 24 | **C** | Friend = دوست |
| 25 | **B** | "See you later" = پھر ملیں گے |
| 26 | **C** | SVO = Subject + **Verb** + Object. English puts the verb before the object. |

# Module 6: Practical Communication (B1/B2)

## Overview
This module focuses on real-world English communication — the kind you need in offices, hospitals, professional events, and social situations. By the end of this module you will handle workplace conversations, write professional emails, express opinions confidently, and understand cultural expectations in English-speaking environments.

---

## 1. Workplace English: Meetings, Emails, and Phone Calls

### Meeting Language

Meetings in English-speaking workplaces follow a structure. Knowing the right phrases makes you sound confident and professional.

**Opening a meeting:**
- "Let's get started, shall we?"
- "Thank you all for coming. The purpose of today's meeting is..."
- "We have about an hour, so let's move efficiently."

**Presenting your point:**
- "I'd like to draw your attention to..."
- "From my perspective..."
- "The data suggests that..."
- "If I may add..."

**Asking for clarification:**
- "Could you elaborate on that?"
- "I'm not sure I follow — could you give an example?"
- "What exactly do you mean by...?"

**Agreeing:**
- "That's a fair point."
- "I completely agree with what you're saying."
- "You've touched on something important there."

**Disagreeing politely:**
- "I see your point, however..."
- "With respect, I think there's another angle worth considering."
- "I'm not entirely convinced — here's my concern..."

**Closing a meeting:**
- "Let's wrap up — the key actions are..."
- "Who will take ownership of this?"
- "We'll reconvene on Thursday to review progress."

---

### Cultural Note: Pakistani vs Western Meeting Styles

| Aspect | Pakistani Professional Culture | Western (UK/US) Culture |
|---|---|---|
| Starting time | Often 10–15 minutes late (acceptable) | Starting on time is a sign of respect |
| Hierarchy | Senior person speaks first; others wait | Everyone encouraged to contribute equally |
| Disagreement | Rarely expressed directly to a superior | Respectful disagreement is welcomed |
| Decision-making | Often decided by the most senior person | Often consensus-based |
| Small talk before meeting | Common and expected | Brief (1–2 minutes maximum) |
| Eye contact | Sustained eye contact can feel confrontational | Eye contact shows confidence and honesty |

**Key takeaway:** In Western professional settings, you are expected to speak up, ask questions, and respectfully challenge ideas — this is seen as engagement, not disrespect.

## 2. Business Email Templates

### Template 1: Formal Request Email

```
Subject: Request for Meeting – Q3 Sales Strategy

Dear Mr. Thompson,

I hope this email finds you well.

I am writing to request a brief meeting at your earliest convenience to discuss 
the Q3 sales strategy for the South Asia region. I believe a 30-minute session 
would be sufficient to align on key priorities.

Please let me know your availability during the week of 15 July, and I will 
send a calendar invitation accordingly.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Yours sincerely,
Ahmed Raza
Regional Sales Manager
```

### Template 2: Complaint Email

```
Subject: Unsatisfactory Service – Order #45231

Dear Customer Service Team,

I am writing to formally express my dissatisfaction with the service I received 
regarding Order #45231, placed on 3 June 2026.

The items arrived three days later than the confirmed delivery date, and two 
products were damaged upon arrival. I have attached photographs for your reference.

I would appreciate a full refund for the damaged items and a clear explanation 
of the delivery delay. I expect a response within five business days.

I look forward to your prompt attention to this matter.

Yours faithfully,
Sara Khan
```

### Template 3: Thank You / Follow-Up Email

```
Subject: Thank You – Interview for Marketing Manager Role

Dear Ms. Davies,

Thank you very much for taking the time to meet with me yesterday regarding 
the Marketing Manager position. I thoroughly enjoyed our conversation and 
am even more enthusiastic about the opportunity to join your team.

I was particularly excited to learn about the upcoming brand relaunch — 
I believe my experience with digital campaigns in emerging markets would 
contribute meaningfully to that initiative.

Please do not hesitate to contact me if you require any further information. 
I look forward to hearing from you.

Warm regards,
Usman Ali
```

## 4. Health and Hospital English

### Essential Phrases

**At the receptionist's desk:**
- "I'd like to make an appointment with Dr. Smith."
- "I'm here for a 2 o'clock appointment."
- "I don't have an appointment — is the doctor available?"

**Describing symptoms:**
- "I've been having a headache for three days."
- "I feel dizzy and nauseous."
- "There's a sharp pain in my lower back."
- "I have a persistent cough."
- "My throat is sore and I'm finding it hard to swallow."

**Understanding the doctor:**
- "Could you explain that in simpler terms?"
- "How many times a day should I take this?"
- "Are there any side effects I should be aware of?"
- "When should I come back for a follow-up?"

**At the pharmacy:**
- "I have a prescription from Dr. Ahmed."
- "Do you have this in a lower dosage?"
- "Is this available over the counter?"

## 5. Expressing Opinions and Debate Phrases

### Stating an opinion:
- "In my view, / In my opinion..."
- "From where I stand..."
- "The way I see it..."
- "I strongly believe that..."
- "It seems to me that..."

### Adding weight to an argument:
- "Not only that, but..."
- "Furthermore, / Moreover..."
- "What's more..."
- "The evidence clearly shows that..."

### Conceding a point:
- "You make a valid point, however..."
- "While I take your point, I still think..."
- "Fair enough, but consider this..."
- "That's true to an extent, but..."

### Concluding:
- "All things considered..."
- "Taking everything into account..."
- "The evidence strongly suggests that..."

## 6. Describing Graphs and Trends

**Verbs for upward trends:** rise, increase, climb, grow, surge, jump, rocket  
**Verbs for downward trends:** fall, decrease, decline, drop, plummet, shrink  
**Verbs for stability:** remain stable, level off, plateau, hold steady  
**Verbs for fluctuation:** fluctuate, vary, oscillate

**Example sentences:**
- "Sales rose sharply by 35% in the first quarter."
- "The unemployment rate declined gradually over two years."
- "Prices remained relatively stable throughout August."
- "Website traffic fluctuated between 10,000 and 15,000 visitors per month."

**Useful adverbs:** sharply, steadily, gradually, significantly, slightly, dramatically, marginally

## 7. Social Small Talk

Small talk is the brief, friendly conversation before or after a formal interaction. Urdu speakers often find small talk awkward because it is not as culturally embedded in Pakistani professional contexts. However, in UK and US workplaces, small talk is essential for building relationships.

**Common small talk topics:**
- The weather ("Lovely weather today, isn't it?")
- Weekend plans ("Any plans for the weekend?")
- Current events ("Did you catch the match last night?")
- Food ("Have you tried the new restaurant on the high street?")
- Travel ("Have you been anywhere nice recently?")

**Topics to avoid in professional small talk:**
- Religion, politics, salary, personal relationships, age

**Responding to small talk:**
- "Not bad at all, thanks! You?"
- "Really good, actually — we went to Edinburgh."
- "Yes, absolutely! Though the traffic was terrible."

## 8. Complaint Language

### Informal complaint (to a colleague):
- "Look, I'm a bit frustrated with how this is being handled."
- "Could we sort this out? It's been going on for a while."

### Formal complaint (to management or customer service):
- "I wish to formally raise a concern regarding..."
- "I am writing to express my dissatisfaction with..."
- "This falls below the standard I would expect from..."
- "I would like this matter resolved by [date]."
- "Should this not be addressed, I will be compelled to escalate the matter."

## 9. Networking at Professional Events

**Introducing yourself:**
- "Hi, I'm Kamran — I work in project management at TechCorp."
- "I don't think we've met — I'm Sara from the marketing side."

**Keeping conversation going:**
- "What brings you to this event?"
- "What does your role involve, exactly?"
- "That's really interesting — how long have you been in that field?"

**Ending professionally:**
- "It was great chatting with you. Shall we exchange cards?"
- "I'd love to connect on LinkedIn — do you mind if I add you?"
- "Let's stay in touch — I think there could be some real synergy between our teams."

## 10. Body Language in English-Speaking Cultures

Understanding body language is as important as words in professional communication.

| Behaviour | Meaning in English-speaking cultures |
|---|---|
| Firm handshake | Confidence, professionalism |
| Eye contact | Honesty, engagement, confidence |
| Nodding while listening | "I'm following you" — not necessarily agreement |
| Crossed arms | Possibly defensive or closed-off |
| Leaning forward slightly | Interest and engagement |
| Checking phone during meeting | Very rude — signals disengagement |
| Smiling when greeting | Warmth and friendliness (standard) |
| Personal space (~1 metre) | Respect for personal boundaries |

**Note for Pakistani professionals:** In Pakistani culture, sustained eye contact with someone senior can be seen as challenging. In Western workplaces, the opposite is true — looking away can signal dishonesty or lack of confidence. Practise maintaining comfortable eye contact (2–3 seconds, then briefly away).

# ماڈیول ۶: عملی مواصلات (B1/B2)

## جائزہ
یہ ماڈیول حقیقی زندگی کی انگریزی مواصلات پر مرکوز ہے — وہ قسم جو آپ کو دفاتر، ہسپتالوں، پیشہ ورانہ تقریبات اور سماجی حالات میں درکار ہوتی ہے۔ اس ماڈیول کے اختتام تک آپ دفتری گفتگو سنبھال سکیں گے، پیشہ ورانہ ای میل لکھ سکیں گے، اعتماد کے ساتھ رائے دے سکیں گے، اور انگریزی بولنے والے ماحول میں ثقافتی توقعات کو سمجھ سکیں گے۔

## ۱. دفتری انگریزی: اجلاس، ای میل اور فون کالز

### اجلاس کی زبان

انگریزی بولنے والے دفاتر میں اجلاس ایک خاص ترتیب کے مطابق ہوتے ہیں۔ صحیح فقرے جاننے سے آپ پراعتماد اور پیشہ ور لگتے ہیں۔

**اجلاس شروع کرنا:**
- "آئیں شروع کریں، ٹھیک ہے؟" (Let's get started, shall we?)
- "آنے کا شکریہ۔ آج کے اجلاس کا مقصد ہے..." (Thank you all for coming. The purpose of today's meeting is...)
- "ہمارے پاس تقریباً ایک گھنٹہ ہے، تو مؤثر طریقے سے آگے بڑھتے ہیں۔" (We have about an hour, so let's move efficiently.)

**اپنی بات پیش کرنا:**
- "میں آپ کی توجہ... کی طرف مبذول کرنا چاہتا ہوں" (I'd like to draw your attention to...)
- "میرے نقطہ نظر سے..." (From my perspective...)

**وضاحت مانگنا:**
- "کیا آپ اس پر مزید روشنی ڈال سکتے ہیں؟" (Could you elaborate on that?)
- "میں پوری طرح نہیں سمجھا — کیا آپ مثال دے سکتے ہیں؟" (I'm not sure I follow — could you give an example?)

### ثقافتی نوٹ: پاکستانی بمقابلہ مغربی اجلاس کا انداز

| پہلو | پاکستانی پیشہ ورانہ ثقافت | مغربی (برطانوی/امریکی) ثقافت |
|---|---|---|
| شروع ہونے کا وقت | اکثر ۱۰-۱۵ منٹ دیر (قابل قبول) | وقت پر آنا احترام کی علامت ہے |
| درجہ بندی | سینئر پہلے بولتے ہیں؛ دوسرے انتظار کرتے ہیں | سب کو برابر حصہ لینے کی ترغیب |
| اختلاف | اعلیٰ افسر سے شاید ہی براہ راست اظہار کیا جائے | باادب اختلاف کا خیرمقدم کیا جاتا ہے |
| آنکھ سے رابطہ | طویل نظر مقابلے کی طرح لگ سکتی ہے | آنکھ سے رابطہ اعتماد اور دیانت داری ظاہر کرتا ہے |

**اہم نکتہ:** مغربی پیشہ ورانہ ماحول میں آپ سے توقع کی جاتی ہے کہ آپ بولیں، سوال کریں، اور خیالات کو با ادب انداز میں چیلنج کریں — یہ مشغولیت کی علامت ہے، بے ادبی کی نہیں۔

- "کمر کے نچلے حصے میں تیز درد ہے۔"
- "مجھے مسلسل کھانسی ہے۔"

---

## ۵. رائے دینا اور بحث کے فقرے

**رائے ظاہر کرنا:**
- "میری رائے میں..." (In my view...)
- "میرے نقطہ نظر سے..." (From where I stand...)
- "مجھے یقین ہے کہ..." (I strongly believe that...)

**نقطہ تسلیم کرنا:**
- "آپ کی بات درست ہے، تاہم..." (You make a valid point, however...)
- "یہ ایک حد تک سچ ہے، لیکن..." (That's true to an extent, but...)

---

## ۶. گراف اور رجحانات بیان کرنا

**اوپر جانے والے رجحانات:** بڑھنا (rise, increase, climb, surge)
**نیچے آنے والے رجحانات:** گرنا (fall, decrease, decline, drop)
**مستحکم رہنا:** مستحکم رہنا (remain stable, level off, plateau)

**مثال:**
- "فروخت پہلی سہ ماہی میں ۳۵ فیصد بڑھ گئی۔"
- "بے روزگاری کی شرح دو سالوں میں آہستہ آہستہ کم ہوئی۔"

---

## ۷. سماجی گپ شپ (Small Talk)

Small talk وہ مختصر، دوستانہ گفتگو ہے جو باقاعدہ بات چیت سے پہلے یا بعد میں ہوتی ہے۔ پاکستانی پیشہ ور حضرات کو یہ عجیب لگتی ہے کیونکہ یہ پاکستانی پیشہ ورانہ ثقافت میں اتنی عام نہیں۔ لیکن برطانوی اور امریکی دفاتر میں یہ رشتے بنانے کے لیے بہت ضروری ہے۔

**عام Small Talk موضوعات:**
- موسم ("آج موسم بہت اچھا ہے، کیا ہے نا؟")
- ویک اینڈ کے منصوبے ("ویک اینڈ کا کوئی پروگرام؟")

**ان موضوعات سے گریز کریں:**
- مذہب، سیاست، تنخواہ، ذاتی تعلقات، عمر

---

## ۸. شکایت کی زبان

**غیر رسمی شکایت:**
- "دیکھیں، اس معاملے کو سنبھالنے کے طریقے سے میں کچھ ناراض ہوں۔"

**رسمی شکایت:**
- "میں باقاعدہ طور پر... کے بارے میں تشویش ظاہر کرنا چاہتا ہوں۔"
- "میں... سے اپنی عدم اطمینان ظاہر کرنے کے لیے لکھ رہا ہوں۔"

---

## ۹. پیشہ ورانہ تقریبات میں نیٹ ورکنگ

**اپنا تعارف:**
- "ہیلو، میں کامران ہوں — میں TechCorp میں پروجیکٹ مینجمنٹ میں کام کرتا ہوں۔"

**گفتگو جاری رکھنا:**
- "آپ اس تقریب میں کیوں آئے؟"
- "آپ کا کردار بالکل کیا ہے؟"

---

## ۱۰. انگریزی بولنے والی ثقافتوں میں جسمانی زبان

| رویہ | انگریزی بولنے والی ثقافتوں میں معنی |
|---|---|
| مضبوط مصافحہ | اعتماد، پیشہ ورانہ مزاج |
| آنکھ سے رابطہ | دیانت داری، مشغولیت، اعتماد |
| سننے کے دوران سر ہلانا | "میں سمجھ رہا ہوں" — لازمی نہیں کہ اتفاق ہو |
| اجلاس میں فون چیک کرنا | بہت بے ادبی — بے توجہی کی علامت |

**پاکستانی پیشہ ور حضرات کے لیے نوٹ:** پاکستانی ثقافت میں کسی بزرگ یا اعلیٰ افسر سے طویل آنکھ کا رابطہ چیلنج کرنے جیسا لگ سکتا ہے۔ مغربی دفاتر میں اس کے برعکس ہے — نظر چرانا بے ایمانی یا کم اعتمادی کی علامت سمجھا جاتا ہے۔ آرام دہ آنکھ کا رابطہ (۲-۳ سیکنڈ، پھر مختصر وقفہ) کی مشق کریں۔

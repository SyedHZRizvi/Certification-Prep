## Situation 1: At the Shop / Supermarket

### Key Phrases

| Phrase | When to Use |
|--------|-------------|
| Excuse me, where can I find ___? | Looking for an item |
| How much does this cost? / How much is this? | Asking price |
| Do you have this in a different size/color? | Looking for variations |
| I'd like to buy ___. | Stating what you want |
| Can I pay by card? / Cash only? | Payment method |
| Do you have a bag? / Can I have a bag? | Asking for a bag |
| Do you have a receipt? / Can I get a receipt? | Asking for receipt |
| I'd like to return this, please. | Returning an item |
| It doesn't fit. / It's the wrong size. | Problem with item |
| Keep the change. | Paying and letting cashier keep change |

### Dialogue 1: At the Supermarket

**Customer (Ahmed):** Excuse me. Where can I find the bread?
**Staff:** It's in aisle 3, on the left.
**Ahmed:** Thank you. And how much is this milk?
**Staff:** That's two pounds fifty.
**Ahmed:** Great. And do you accept cards?
**Staff:** Yes, we do. Card or contactless.
**Ahmed:** I'll pay by card, please.
**Staff:** Your total is six pounds. Thank you.
**Ahmed:** Thank you. Have a good day!

## Situation 2: At the Restaurant / Café

### Key Phrases

| Phrase | Use |
|--------|-----|
| A table for two, please. | Getting a table |
| Can I see the menu, please? | Asking for menu |
| What do you recommend? | Asking for suggestion |
| I'd like the chicken, please. | Ordering food |
| Can I have a glass of water? | Ordering drinks |
| What comes with this? | Asking about sides |
| Could we have the bill, please? | Asking for the bill |
| Is service included? | Asking about tip |
| Excuse me, this is not what I ordered. | Problem with order |
| It was delicious, thank you! | Compliment after eating |

### Dialogue 2: At the Restaurant

**Waiter:** Good evening! Welcome. How many people?
**Sara:** Two, please.
**Waiter:** Right this way. Here are your menus. Can I get you some drinks?
**Sara:** Yes, two glasses of water, please.
**Waiter:** Ready to order?
**Ahmed:** Yes. I'd like the grilled chicken, please.
**Sara:** And I'll have the vegetable pasta.
**Waiter:** Excellent. Would you like anything else?
**Sara:** No, thank you. Oh — could we have the bill when you're ready?
**Waiter:** Of course. I'll bring it shortly.

## Situation 3: Asking for Directions

### Key Phrases

| Phrase | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Excuse me, how do I get to ___? | Asking for directions |
| Is it far from here? | Asking about distance |
| Can you show me on the map? | Asking for map help |
| Go straight ahead. | Direction |
| Turn left / Turn right | Direction |
| Take the first/second left. | Specific turn |
| It's on your left / right. | Where it is |
| It's about 5 minutes on foot. | Distance in walking |
| You'll see it on the corner. | Landmark reference |
| I'm lost. Can you help me? | Admitting you're lost |

### Dialogue 3: Asking for Directions

**Ahmed:** Excuse me. I'm looking for the post office. Can you help?
**Passer-by:** Sure! Go straight ahead for about two minutes. Then turn right at the traffic lights.
**Ahmed:** Turn right at the traffic lights?
**Passer-by:** Yes, exactly. The post office is on your left — it's next to the pharmacy.
**Ahmed:** Is it far?
**Passer-by:** No, about five minutes on foot.
**Ahmed:** Thank you so much!
**Passer-by:** No problem. Have a good day!

## Situation 4: Public Transport

### Key Phrases

| Phrase | Use |
|--------|-----|
| Which bus goes to ___? | Finding the right bus |
| Does this train stop at ___? | Confirming a stop |
| A single ticket to ___, please. | Buying one-way ticket |
| A return ticket to ___, please. | Buying return ticket |
| How much is the fare to ___? | Asking ticket price |
| When is the next bus/train to ___? | Asking schedule |
| Which platform? | Asking at train station |
| Where do I get off for ___? | Asking about the stop |
| Is this seat taken? | On the vehicle |
| Could you tell me when we reach ___? | Asking to be alerted |

> 🎯 **Tip:** In the UK, "single" = one-way ticket. "Return" = round trip. In the US, say "one-way" and "round trip."

## Situation 5: At the Doctor

### Describing Symptoms

| Symptom Phrase | Urdu |
|---------------|------|
| I have a headache. | مجھے سر درد ہے |
| I have a stomachache. | مجھے پیٹ میں درد ہے |
| I have a fever. | مجھے بخار ہے |
| I have a cough. | مجھے کھانسی ہے |
| I have a sore throat. | میرا گلا خراب ہے |
| I feel dizzy. | مجھے چکر آ رہے ہیں |
| I feel nauseous. | مجھے متلی ہو رہی ہے |
| I can't sleep. | مجھے نیند نہیں آتی |
| I have chest pain. | مجھے سینے میں درد ہے |
| I've had this for three days. | تین دنوں سے ایسا ہے |
| It hurts here. | یہاں درد ہے |
| I'm allergic to ___. | مجھے ___ سے الرجی ہے |
| I take ___ medication. | میں ___ دوا لیتا/لیتی ہوں |

### Dialogue 4: At the Doctor

**Doctor:** Good morning. What seems to be the problem?
**Ahmed:** Good morning, doctor. I have a bad headache and a fever. I've had it since yesterday.
**Doctor:** I see. Do you have any other symptoms? A cough or sore throat?
**Ahmed:** Yes, I have a sore throat too.
**Doctor:** How long have you had the sore throat?
**Ahmed:** About two days.
**Doctor:** Open your mouth, please. Yes, your throat is quite red. I'll prescribe some antibiotics. Rest and drink plenty of water.
**Ahmed:** Should I come back?
**Doctor:** If you don't improve in three days, come back to see me.
**Ahmed:** Thank you, doctor.

## Situation 6: On the Phone

### Key Phrases

| Phrase | Use |
|--------|-----|
| Hello? | Answering a call |
| This is [name] speaking. | Identifying yourself |
| Can I speak to ___? | Asking for someone |
| Is ___ available? | Asking if someone is free |
| Hold on, please. / One moment. | Asking caller to wait |
| I'm sorry, he/she is not available. | Person not there |
| Can I take a message? | Offering to take a message |
| Can I leave a message? | Leaving a message |
| I'll call back later. | Saying you'll call again |
| Can you call me back? | Asking them to call you |
| Could you speak more slowly, please? | Asking to slow down |
| Could you repeat that, please? | Asking to repeat |
| I'll pass on the message. | Promising to relay info |

### Dialogue 5: On the Phone

**Sara:** Hello?
**Ahmed:** Hi, this is Ahmed. Can I speak to Sara, please?
**Sara:** Speaking! Hi Ahmed, how are you?
**Ahmed:** I'm well, thank you. I'm calling about tomorrow's meeting. Is it still at 10 am?
**Sara:** Actually, it's been moved to 2 pm.
**Ahmed:** Oh, 2 pm. That works for me. Where will it be?
**Sara:** The same office as last time — room 5.
**Ahmed:** Perfect. See you tomorrow then.
**Sara:** See you! Bye.

## Situation 7: Making and Declining Invitations

### Making Invitations

| Phrase | Formality |
|--------|-----------|
| Would you like to join us for dinner? | Polite |
| Do you want to come to my party? | Casual |
| Are you free on Saturday? | Checking availability |
| I was wondering if you'd like to... | Very polite |

### Accepting Invitations

| Phrase | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| That sounds great! | Enthusiastic yes |
| I'd love to! | Strong acceptance |
| Sure, I'll be there. | Casual acceptance |
| Count me in! | Enthusiastic agreement |

### Declining Invitations (Politely)

| Phrase | Use |
|--------|-----|
| I'm afraid I can't make it. | Polite decline |
| I'm sorry, I have other plans. | Prior commitment |
| That's very kind of you, but I can't. | Polite but firm |
| Maybe next time? | Softer decline with future option |
| I wish I could, but I'm busy. | Regretful decline |

> 🚨 **Common Mistake:** Never just say "No" to an invitation. Always add an explanation or softening phrase. Just "No" sounds very rude in English.

> **Instructions:** Take this AFTER the 14-day campaign has ended and the post-mortem is drafted.
> Each question asks whether you did the work. Honesty matters more than the score.
> Target: 80%+ A or B.

## 🎯 Model Answers + Grading Guidance

### Q1: Excellent looks like: **A**
Real subject + written permission. The single most common reason this capstone fails is "I started, then realized I couldn't share data because the friend got nervous." Get permission in writing.

### Q2: Excellent looks like: **A**
$180–$220 range. Too little = not a real campaign. Too much = unscoped. The $200 target is calibrated to be realistic for an entry-level marketer's tuition.

### Q3: Excellent looks like: **A**
Brief written before launch. This document is half the credibility of the post-mortem. "Here's what I planned. Here's what happened. Here's what changed." That arc is the story.

### Q4: Excellent looks like: **A**
Pixel + Google Tag, both verified with a test conversion BEFORE launch. Skipping this is the #1 cause of "I ran a campaign but the numbers don't make sense." Verify before launch.

### Q5: Excellent looks like: **A**
Three creative variants per platform. Anything less and you have no creative learning. Anything more and you can't get enough data per variant in 14 days at $7/day.

### Q6: Excellent looks like: **A**
14 days minimum. Meta's algorithm needs 7+ days to learn. Google needs 10+ to surface a stable CPL. Anything less and the data is noise.

### Q7: Excellent looks like: **A**
14 date-stamped entries, 2–4 sentences each. This is what makes the post-mortem readable as a story rather than a report.

### Q8: Excellent looks like: **A**
Three deliberate changes, each documented. Examples: "Day 3 paused Variant B, CTR was 0.4%." "Day 7 rebuilt landing-page hero, CR went from 0.8% to 3.2%."

### Q9: Excellent looks like: **A**
All 4 screenshot categories. Hiring managers can SEE the campaign was real if they can see the ad manager. They can't if you only describe it.

### Q10: Excellent looks like: **A**
Failing creatives screenshot. Showing what didn't work is the highest-trust signal in the entire piece. Most students hide failures. Don't.

### Q11: Excellent looks like: **A**
1,500–2,500 words. Less = thin. More = no one reads. This is the calibrated range for a marketing case study.

### Q12: Excellent looks like: **A**
Hook opens with the most interesting moment. Read 5 Cody Schneider posts before writing yours.

### Q13: Excellent looks like: **A**
Full table format. Per-channel + total. Including the bad numbers. This is the "I'm rigorous about data" signal.

### Q14: Excellent looks like: **A**
Public + indexable. Medium, your blog, Notion (public mode), Substack, all work. LinkedIn-only is OK but the URL won't index well.

### Q15: Excellent looks like: **A**
6–10 slides, posted on LinkedIn. The carousel is what gets discovery; the post-mortem is what closes the deal.

### Q16: Excellent looks like: **A**
"What Didn't Work" as a section header / slide. NOT buried at the end. Featured.

### Q17: Excellent looks like: **A**
Honest framing wins. *"ROAS came in at 0.84x, net loss of $30 against $186 spent. Here's why I still call this a win."* That's the tone.

### Q18: Excellent looks like: **A**
3+ specific, actionable lessons. *"I'd skip Meta Carousel entirely. The UGC video crushed by 3x."*

### Q19: Excellent looks like: **A**
Loom is the highest-leverage "show your work" tool. A 5-minute screen recording walking through the ad manager is worth 10 paragraphs of text.

### Q20: Excellent looks like: **A**
AI creatives for at least one variant. Demonstrates you're using modern tooling. Pure stock photo = looks 2018.

### Q21: Excellent looks like: **A**
A 2-line quote from the subject. *"They actually shipped this. Got us 4 paying customers we wouldn't have had. Honest about what didn't work."* Gold.

### Q22: Excellent looks like: **A**
Every decision has a rationale. *"Paused Variant B because CTR was 0.4% after 8 hours and CPM was $48. Didn't make sense to keep spending."*

### Q23: Excellent looks like: **A**
Negative keywords / exclusion list. Skipping this often doubles wasted spend.

### Q24: Excellent looks like: **A**
Checked + acted on search terms / breakdown reports. This is where the actual learnings live.

### Q25: Excellent looks like: **A**
10 min/day x 14 days = ~2.5 hours of optimization. The data shows campaigns checked daily perform meaningfully better than weekly-checked ones.

### Q26: Excellent looks like: **A**
Both platform screenshots throughout. Reinforces it was a real cross-channel campaign.

### Q27: Excellent looks like: **A**
Production costs in ROAS math. *"$10 Midjourney sub + ~3 hrs my time + $186 ad spend = blended cost $X."* This is senior thinking.

### Q28: Excellent looks like: **A**
Specific "if scaled, this changes" section. Hiring managers want to know you can think about scale, not just $200.

### Q29: Excellent looks like: **A**
Practiced 3+ times out loud. This is the pitch that gets you the offer.

### Q30: Excellent looks like: **A**
This piece becomes the flagship of your portfolio. If it doesn't, the issue is usually: not enough screenshots, missing the post-mortem, or hiding failures.

---

## 📊 Self-Score Yourself

- 24+ A's → 🏆 Flagship portfolio piece. Ship it everywhere.
- 18–23 A's → ✅ Solid case study. Polish C/D items.
- 12–17 A's → ⚠️ Half there. Add screenshots, expand the writeup.
- <12 A's → 🔁 Major gaps. Re-read Module 3, fix the campaign documentation.

- The 14-day daily check-in routine

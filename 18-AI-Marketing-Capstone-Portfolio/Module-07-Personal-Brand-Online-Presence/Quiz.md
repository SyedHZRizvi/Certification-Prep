## 🎯 Model Answers + Grading Guidance

### Q1: Excellent looks like: **A**
Pattern B (capstone-anchored) is the strongest for entry-level. *"Marketer who built a $200 paid campaign post-mortem, case study below"*.

### Q2: Excellent looks like: **A**
Story-opener About section. The single most underused real estate on LinkedIn.

### Q3: Excellent looks like: **A**
4–6 pinned with custom titles. Default LinkedIn titles are bad; always override.

### Q4: Excellent looks like: **A**
Custom banner. ~10 min in Canva. Replaces the "I don't care" default.

### Q5: Excellent looks like: **A**
Live + linkable. If it's not live, it doesn't exist.

### Q6: Excellent looks like: **A**
All 5 capstones with hero images. Each case study one click away.

### Q7: Excellent looks like: **A**
10+ posts drafted. Removes the daily "what should I post?" anxiety.

### Q8: Excellent looks like: **A**
5+ live posts. The wrapper requires content; otherwise the rebuild is decoration.

### Q9: Excellent looks like: **A**
Specific. Numbers, screenshots, named subjects. Vague posts get scrolled past.

### Q10: Excellent looks like: **A**
Calendar-blocked. Sustainable cadence = consistent results.

### Q11: Excellent looks like: **A**
Secondary channel active. Twitter/X is the typical secondary for marketers.

### Q12: Excellent looks like: **A**
60-second cold open written + practiced. You'll use it 30+ times.

### Q13: Excellent looks like: **A**
5+ network reachouts. Your network is the highest-conversion job channel.

### Q14: Excellent looks like: **A**
Contact info prominent. Don't make recruiters hunt.

### Q15: Excellent looks like: **A**
3+ build-in-public posts. Highest-trust content.

### Q16: Excellent looks like: **A**
3 anti-patterns avoided. Engagement-baiting + generic content + over-polishing.

### Q17: Excellent looks like: **A**
90-day metrics written down. What gets measured gets done.

### Q18: Excellent looks like: **A**
Ready to share today. If not, polish 2-3 most visible elements first.

### Q19: Excellent looks like: **A**
All 5 case studies use P-C-A-R-L. Consistency builds trust.

### Q20: Excellent looks like: **A**
4–8 hours. If much more, you're polishing. If much less, you skipped steps.

### Q21: Excellent looks like: **A**
Shipped on Notion. Polish later. The trap kills students.

### Q22: Excellent looks like: **A**
5+ profiles reviewed. Research is fine, at 1 hour cap.

### Q23: Excellent looks like: **A**
Distinctive tagline. *"AI-native marketer who ships."* > *"Driven marketing professional."*

### Q24: Excellent looks like: **A**
Structured posts. Hook → body → CTA. Even short posts.

### Q25: Excellent looks like: **A**
Daily engagement routine. 15 min, 10 thoughtful comments. Drives reach.

### Q26: Excellent looks like: **A**
3+ peer reviews. You can't see your own blind spots.

### Q27: Excellent looks like: **A**
Linked everywhere on profile. Make it obvious.

### Q28: Excellent looks like: **A**
Confident. If not, you don't ship pitches in Module 8.

## 📊 Self-Score Yourself

- 22+ A's → 🏆 Ready to send pitches. Module 8 awaits.
- 16–21 A's → ✅ Solid foundation. Polish C/D items first.
- 10–15 A's → ⚠️ Wrapper is half-built. Most likely: portfolio site or content cadence.
- <10 A's → 🔁 Re-read Module 7. Fix LinkedIn first.

## 🎬 A Story: Two LinkedIns

Imagine two marketers — Priya and Eli — both finished the same 5 capstones in the same 6 weeks. Same projects, same quality, similar career stage.

**Priya's LinkedIn:**
- Headline: *"Marketing Manager | Driving Growth Through Strategy"*
- About: A 2018-vintage corporate paragraph about being a "passionate marketer who delivers results."
- Featured section: empty
- Recent activity: 1 post in 6 months (reshared an industry article)
- Followers: 312

**Eli's LinkedIn:**
- Headline: *"AI-native marketer | Built an attribution model that saved a paid social budget — case study pinned"*
- About: 3 paragraphs. Opens with "Last month I built a $200 paid campaign and lost money on purpose. Here's what I learned." Includes link to their portfolio.
- Featured section: 4 pinned items — the 5 capstones distilled (1 is grouped)
- Recent activity: 8 posts in 4 weeks. Each one is a behind-the-scenes look at one capstone. Average 1,200 impressions per post.
- Followers: 1,847

Both apply for the same Marketing Manager role at a Series B SaaS company. The recruiter spends ~30 seconds on each profile.

- Priya gets the "thanks, we'll be in touch" auto-rejection.
- Eli gets a calendar invite for a phone screen the same day.

Their capstones were equivalent. Eli's *wrapper* did the work.

## 🏗️ Step 1: The LinkedIn Headline

The headline is the single most read line on your profile. It appears in search results, comment lists, DM previews. Most people put their job title here. That's a waste.

### The Wrong Headline Patterns

- *"Marketing Manager at Acme Corp"* — just your job, no signal
- *"Driving growth through strategic marketing"* — generic
- *"Marketer | Strategist | Storyteller"* — listicle, no value
- *"Open to work"* — desperate

### The Right Headline Patterns

The good ones do one of three things:

**Pattern A: Specific Outcome + Audience**
*"Help B2B SaaS companies grow organic traffic with AI-assisted SEO"*
*"Build paid acquisition systems for indie product founders"*

**Pattern B: Capstone-Anchored**
*"Built a multi-touch attribution model that saved a paid social budget — case study below"*
*"Marketer who built a content-brief AI agent — repo + Loom on profile"*

**Pattern C: Honest Vibe + Specific Skill**
*"Curious marketer learning to build with the Claude API • portfolio in Featured"*
*"Indie marketer in public • SEO + paid + automation cases below"*

### How To Pick Yours

1. Look at your 5 capstones. Which one would the recruiter / first prospect care most about?
2. Build the headline around THAT one (Pattern B is recommended for entry-level).
3. End with "case study below" or "portfolio in Featured" — a clear call-to-action to the featured section.

Recommended starter formula:
**`[Your role/identity] | [Most impressive capstone hook] — [where to find it]`**

Examples:

- *"Marketer | Built a $200 paid campaign + post-mortem — featured ↓"*
- *"AI-native marketer | Lead-routing agent + content brief generator — repos pinned"*

## 🏗️ Step 2: The LinkedIn About Section

This is your portfolio's homepage in 2,000 characters or less.

### The Wrong About Section

Three paragraphs of corporate-speak about being passionate, results-driven, and a team player. Says nothing. Reads like 1,000 other profiles.

### The Right About Section

Open with a story. Then proof. Then where to go.

```
Opening (1 paragraph, ~80 words):
   [A specific moment or hook from your most interesting capstone]

Middle (1-2 paragraphs, ~150 words):
   [What you do + which capstones prove it]
   [Be specific. Name tools. Name results.]

Closing (1 paragraph, ~70 words):
   [Where to find the work + how to reach you]
```

### Example

```
Last month I spent $200 of my own money on Meta + Google Ads for a 
friend's coaching business. Two creatives bombed; one crushed. 
ROAS came in at 0.84 — slight loss, but the data showed exactly 
which creative + audience combo would scale at $2k/mo. Full case 
study below.

I'm a marketer who builds. In the last 6 weeks I shipped:
• An SEO + content audit for an indie SaaS founder (19 pages, 90-day 
  roadmap, public testimonial)
• That $200 paid campaign + post-mortem (LinkedIn carousel + blog)
• A 7-step lead-routing automation in Make.com (Loom + public JSON)
• A multi-touch attribution model comparing Last-Click vs Markov 
  (public sheet + walkthrough)
• A content-brief AI agent built with Claude API (public GitHub repo)

All 5 case studies are in the Featured section ↓ or at 
[yourname].notion.site

If you're hiring for [audience] or working on [problem], DM me.
```

That's a real About section. Shows the work. Tells you where to go.

## 🏗️ Step 3: The Featured Section

LinkedIn lets you pin up to ~10 items. Use 4–6.

### What To Pin

| Slot | Content |
|------|---------|
| 1 | Your portfolio site link (1-page summary of all 5 capstones) |
| 2 | The single most impressive capstone (paid campaign post-mortem is usually it) |
| 3 | The most technically interesting capstone (AI agent or automation) |
| 4 | Your LinkedIn carousel post about the paid campaign |
| 5 | (optional) Your YouTube/Loom channel link |
| 6 | (optional) A piece of "build in public" content showing your process |

### Pinning Mechanics

LinkedIn → Profile → Featured → "+" → Add a link / post / document.

For each pin, write a 1-sentence custom title that maximizes the click. Default LinkedIn titles are bad — override them.

## 🏗️ Step 4: The LinkedIn Banner

The banner image is 1584 × 396 pixels. Most people leave it default (blue). Don't.

### What To Put There

Options (pick one):

1. **A clean wordmark** — your name + tagline ("AI-native marketer • builds in public")
2. **A capstone screenshot** — the attribution chart, or the agent's output
3. **A code-art aesthetic** — terminal screenshot, dashboard, abstract pattern
4. **A simple stat** — *"5 capstones shipped in 6 weeks. Portfolio in Featured ↓"*

Build it in Canva (free LinkedIn banner templates). Should look polished but personal.

## 🏗️ Step 5: The Portfolio Site

You picked the platform in Module 1. Now build it.

### Notion (Default Recommendation)

If you picked Notion in Module 1:

1. Create a new Notion workspace OR a new top-level page.
2. Make the page public ("Share" → "Publish to web" → enable).
3. Buy a custom domain if you want (Namecheap, ~$12/yr) and connect via Notion's domain feature (paid plan needed for custom domain on Notion).

**Page structure:**

```
[Hero: your name + tagline + photo]

[About: ~3 sentences]

# Work

## 1. SEO + Content Audit
[Hero image]
[3-sentence summary]
[Link to full case study]

## 2. Live Paid Campaign  
[Hero image — the carousel cover or the results chart]
[3-sentence summary]
[Link to full case study]

## 3. Marketing Automation Workflow
[Hero image — architecture diagram]
[3-sentence summary]
[Link to full case study]

## 4. Attribution Model
[Hero image — the comparison chart]
[3-sentence summary]
[Link to full case study]

## 5. AI Marketing Agent
[Hero image — agent output sample or architecture]
[3-sentence summary]
[Link to GitHub repo + Loom]

# Contact
[Email + LinkedIn + Twitter]
```

That's it. Should take 2 hours total.

### Webflow (Polished Path)

If you have time + design instinct:

1. Pick a Webflow template (search "portfolio template" — many free)
2. Customize colors + typography to your brand
3. Replicate the Notion structure but with better design
4. Connect custom domain
5. Use Webflow CMS if you want to add posts/case studies dynamically

**Time investment:** 8–20 hours. Skip if you're tight on time.

### Read.cv (Modern Creator Vibe)

1. Sign up at read.cv
2. Fill out the profile (it's structured like a polished resume)
3. Add capstones as "Work" entries with links
4. Connect your domain

**Time investment:** 1–2 hours. Excellent for indie / creator track.

### GitHub Pages + Astro (Dev Signal)

If you're targeting AI / dev-tools marketing roles:

1. Use the Astro starter template
2. Fork to GitHub
3. Customize content (markdown files)
4. Deploy via Vercel or Netlify (both free for personal)
5. Connect custom domain

**Time investment:** 6–10 hours. Highest dev signal but expensive on time.

## 🏗️ Step 6: The Content Distribution Playbook

Where will you post about your work? How often?

### The Big 3 Channels For Marketing Portfolios

| Channel | Format | Frequency | Why |
|---------|--------|-----------|-----|
| **LinkedIn** | Text posts, carousels, native video | 3–5x/week | Where hiring managers + prospects live |
| **Twitter / X** | Short threads, screenshots | 1–3x/week | Where indie founders + marketing creators hang out |
| **YouTube / Loom** | Walkthrough videos | 1–2x/month | Where the long-tail SEO benefit lives |

### The Secondary Channels (Pick 1–2)

| Channel | Best For |
|---------|----------|
| Reddit (r/marketing, r/SaaS, r/Entrepreneur) | Niche case study sharing |
| Substack / Beehiiv | If you want a newsletter habit |
| Medium | Backup blog presence (SEO + republishing) |
| TikTok | Only if your visual style fits |
| Indie Hackers | If indie product track |

### The Cadence That Works

```
Monday: Long-form LinkedIn post (story-driven case study)
Tuesday: Twitter thread (3-tweet recap of Monday post)
Wednesday: LinkedIn comment-mining day (no post, but engage on 10 posts)
Thursday: Short LinkedIn post (1 lesson learned, 80 words)
Friday: LinkedIn carousel OR YouTube video
Weekend: Off (or "build in public" snippets)
```

This is calibrated to: ~5 LinkedIn posts/week + 1 Twitter thread + 1 longer-form piece. Sustainable.

## 🏗️ Step 7: The First 30 Posts

Drafting 30 posts before you start removes the daily "what should I post" anxiety. You can pre-write them in Notion and just hit publish.

### Post Types (Mix These)

| Type | What | Frequency in 30 |
|------|------|-----------------|
| **Capstone deep-dive** | Long post on one capstone | 5 |
| **Lesson learned** | "I learned X the hard way" | 8 |
| **Behind-the-scenes** | "Here's the process" | 6 |
| **Contrarian take** | Friendly disagreement with conventional wisdom | 4 |
| **Resource share** | "Here's a tool / article I use" | 3 |
| **Question / poll** | Engagement post | 2 |
| **Win celebration** | Cohort milestones | 2 |

### Templates To Use

**Template 1: Capstone Deep-Dive (long post)**

```
Last [month], I [did the capstone].

[Honest opening: what was scary / surprising / hard]

Here's what I tried:
- [Approach 1]
- [Approach 2]
- [Approach 3]

What worked: [specific]
What didn't: [specific]
What I'd do differently: [specific]

Full write-up + screenshots: [link]
```

**Template 2: Lesson Learned (medium post)**

```
[Specific moment that taught me something]

I used to think [old belief].

After [the experience], I think [new belief].

Here's why:
- [Reason 1]
- [Reason 2]
- [Reason 3]

[Generalize the lesson]
```

**Template 3: Behind-The-Scenes (medium post)**

```
3 hours into [task], I hit [problem].

Tried [thing 1]. Didn't work.
Tried [thing 2]. Didn't work.  
Tried [thing 3]. Worked.

The key insight: [the insight]

If you're [similar situation], here's what to try first: [the insight applied]
```

**Template 4: Contrarian Take (short-medium)**

```
Most [marketers / founders / etc.] say: [conventional wisdom]

I think the opposite.

Because: [reason rooted in your experience]

[Specific example from your capstones]

[Soft invitation to disagree in comments]
```

### The 30 Post Outline

Suggested distribution (write these now):

```
Posts 1-5: Capstone deep-dives (one per capstone)
Posts 6-13: Lessons learned (8 specific moments)
Posts 14-19: Behind-the-scenes (6 process posts)
Posts 20-23: Contrarian takes (4 hot takes)
Posts 24-26: Resource shares (3 tools/articles)
Posts 27-28: Question posts (2 engagement posts)
Posts 29-30: Milestone posts ("week 6 of building", "first cold message sent")
```

You don't have to write all 30 in one sitting. Aim for 10 drafted before you start posting; build the next 20 in the first 2 weeks of posting.

## 🏗️ Step 8: The "Build In Public" Mindset

The students who do this course best are the ones who treat the build as the content. Examples of "build in public" moments:

- *"Day 2 of the SEO audit. Just learned Screaming Frog finds [X type of issue]. Mind blown."*
- *"Halfway through building my first AI agent. Cursor keeps suggesting I use a library I don't trust. Here's how I evaluated it."*
- *"My Meta campaign on day 5: CTR up 3x after I rewrote the hook. The new hook is [thing]."*

Why this works:

1. You don't have to wait to "finish" before posting
2. Each capstone becomes 5–8 posts naturally
3. The audience watches you grow → builds trust faster
4. The portfolio piece becomes more interesting because there's narrative

**Caveat:** Don't fake-build-in-public. If you didn't actually have the moment you're describing, don't write the post. Audiences smell manufactured authenticity.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes To Avoid

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Rebuilt LinkedIn but never posted | Module 7 isn't done at "headline rewritten." It's done at "5 posts live." |
| Posted once, got no engagement, gave up | 4 weeks of consistent posting is the minimum to see results |
| Wrote each post from scratch every day | Pre-draft 10+ posts before launching |
| Hid the "ugly" capstones | Show all 5. Hiding signals insecurity. |
| Made portfolio site too fancy (Webflow with custom code) | Notion is fine. Polish later. |
| No call-to-action in posts | Every post should have a soft CTA ("link in featured", "DM me if interested") |
| Posted only when motivated | Build it into your calendar |
| Generic content (no specifics, no screenshots) | Show numbers, screenshots, names |
| Engagement-baiting ("Comment YES if you agree!") | Don't. It looks desperate. |
| Tracking impressions obsessively in week 1 | Algorithm takes 2-4 weeks to learn you. Be patient. |

---

## 🚨 The "Polish Forever" Trap

Most students get stuck on Webflow. They want pixel-perfect design. They iterate for 3 weeks and never ship.

**The rule:** If your portfolio site is on its 3rd iteration and you haven't posted yet, STOP. Switch to Notion. Ship today. Polish later.

The portfolio site is a wrapper. The capstones are the value. The wrapper matters less than the work.

## 🎯 The 90-Day Personal Brand Goal

By end of 90 days from finishing this module, target:

| Metric | Goal |
|--------|------|
| LinkedIn posts published | 30+ |
| Profile views per week | 100+ |
| Connection requests received (inbound) | 5+ per week |
| Comments on your posts | 5+ per post on average |
| Cold DMs / recruiter outreach | 1–3 per week |
| Portfolio site uniques per week | 50+ |
| Twitter / X followers | 100+ (if you're using it) |

These aren't vanity — they're leading indicators. If you're hitting these, you're 4–8 weeks away from a phone screen or first paid project. If you're not, the issue is usually content quality or posting frequency.

## 🎤 The Cold-Open Pitch For Recruiter Calls

When a recruiter messages you, they'll often start with: *"Tell me a little about yourself."*

Your 60-second cold open:

```
[CONTEXT]: "I'm a marketer focused on [niche/role]."

[PORTFOLIO HOOK]: "Over the last 6 weeks I shipped 5 portfolio pieces 
                   — an SEO audit for an indie SaaS, a $200 paid 
                   campaign post-mortem, a marketing automation in 
                   Make.com, a multi-touch attribution model, and an 
                   AI agent built with Claude API."

[CURRENT STATE]: "I'm [currently working at X / actively looking / 
                  open to interesting roles]."

[WHAT INTERESTS ME]: "I'm most interested in roles that touch [3 things]."

[CTA]: "Happy to walk you through any of the case studies."
```

## Discussion — Socratic prompts

These prompts probe the tension between "ship the wrapper fast" and "build a brand that compounds." Defend your reasoning against both sides.

1. **The reading flatly says "Notion is the default; Webflow loses 2 weeks."** A senior designer pushes back: "For brand or DTC marketing roles, a Notion site signals 'doesn't care about craft' — it actively undersells you." When is the speed argument right, and when is the craft argument right? Build the case for the audience-specific exception to the Notion-default rule.
2. **The "First 30 Posts" framework distributes posts across 7 types (deep-dive, lesson learned, behind-the-scenes, contrarian, resource, question, milestone).** A successful indie creator counters that LinkedIn's algorithm now actively *demotes* variety — pick one format and ride it for 90 days. Is the diversified-posting playbook here outdated for 2026 LinkedIn? Defend the diversified approach against the focused-niching approach.
3. **The 90-day Personal Brand Goals table sets targets like "30+ posts published, 100+ profile views/week, 5+ inbound connection requests/week."** A pragmatist says these metrics will discourage students whose audience is small/niche: a B2B SaaS marketer targeting *only* 50 hiring managers at Series B SaaS companies should hit 0 inbound DMs from outside that audience and that would mean the strategy is working. How should the metrics adapt to audience size? What's the universally-applicable signal?
4. **The "build in public" mindset is presented as universal best practice.** A senior marketing exec at a large enterprise says it's actively career-limiting for *some* archetypes — corporate brand marketers, government affairs, regulated industries (healthcare, finance). When is "build in public" the wrong move? Where's the line, and how do you stay credible without it?
5. **The reading recommends pricing pinned in Featured posts and headline ("Built a $200 paid campaign post-mortem").** A counter from a junior who tried this: putting specific numbers in the headline scared off recruiters at larger companies ("if your biggest campaign was $200, you're not ready for our team"). When does specificity help, and when does it shrink your addressable audience? How do you calibrate?

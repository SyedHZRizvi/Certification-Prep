# 📋 Module 1 Deliverable Rubric: Portfolio Strategy

> Module 1's "deliverable" is your **portfolio plan document**. Below is the rubric for grading it.
> Print this and grade your plan against it before starting Module 2.

---

## 🎯 What You Must Have Done By End Of Module 1

```
□ Picked your path (Job / Client / Indie) with a written WHY
□ Named the 5 real subjects for your 5 capstones
□ Drafted the P-C-A-R-L template you'll use for every write-up
□ Picked your portfolio hosting platform
□ Created a calendar block for the next 6 weeks
□ Made a public or peer commitment ("I'm doing this")
```

---

## 📐 The Grading Rubric

### Deliverable: A "Portfolio Plan" Document (Notion page or Google Doc)

| Section | Failing | Passing | Excellent |
|---------|---------|---------|-----------|
| **Path Decision** | No path picked, or "I'll figure it out" | One path picked, no reasoning | One path + 1-paragraph WHY + target date for first outcome |
| **5 Capstone Subjects** | <3 subjects, all hypothetical | All 5 named, some hypothetical | All 5 named with real business names, links, and confirmed access to shareable data |
| **Case Study Template** | No template drafted | P-C-A-R-L headers in a doc | P-C-A-R-L + word count targets + example screenshots planned per section |
| **Hosting Choice** | Undecided | Picked but no account created | Picked + account created + draft page exists |
| **Calendar** | No blocks | Some blocks for week 1 | Calendar blocks for all 6 weeks, named per capstone |
| **Commitment** | None made | Mentioned to one person | Public LinkedIn / Twitter / peer-Slack post |

### To Pass Module 1: ≥5 cells at Passing or better.
### To Reach Excellent: ≥4 cells at Excellent, no cells at Failing.

## 🏆 The 3 Audience Paths — Quick Picker

```
JOB TRACK    →  "I want a salaried marketing role within 6 months."
CLIENT TRACK →  "I want $X/mo in freelance income within 3 months."
INDIE TRACK  →  "I want a following / micro-product launched within 6 months."
```

Pick one. You can pivot later. Drift = problem.

---

## 🧭 The P-C-A-R-L Template (Copy-Paste Into Notion)

```
# [Capstone Title]

## Problem
[1–2 sentences: what business problem were you solving?]

## Constraints
[1–2 sentences: budget, time, what was off-limits]

## Approach
[3–7 paragraphs: step by step, with screenshots]

## Result
[Numbers. Tables. Honest. Includes failures.]

## Lessons
[What you'd do differently. Vulnerability = trust.]
```

---

## 🚫 Anti-Patterns To Recognize In Your Own Plan

- ❌ **Tools List Trap** — Listing "Ahrefs, Make.com, Claude API" without linking to projects
- ❌ **Internal Work Trap** — Relying on past job claims without artifacts
- ❌ **Polished But Empty Trap** — Building a Webflow site before any project exists
- ❌ **Hypothetical Subject Trap** — "I'll find a subject when I get to that capstone"
- ❌ **No-Money Trap** — Planning to skip the $200 paid campaign

If you can name all 5 capstone subjects in under 30 seconds, you've nailed Module 1. ✅

## 🏆 Power Phrases For Recruiter / Client Conversations

Right after you say a project name, follow with one of these:

- ✅ "Here's the public link..."
- ✅ "I'll walk you through how I'd do this for you..."
- ✅ "Two of three creatives bombed; here's what I changed..."
- ✅ "The first version had a bug — here's the fixed version..."

What signals desperation (avoid):

- ❌ "I just made this last week..."
- ❌ "It's not really finished but..."
- ❌ "Sorry, I don't have much to show yet..."
- ❌ "Tools-wise, I know everything..."

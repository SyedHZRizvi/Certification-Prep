## 🎯 Model Answers + What "Excellent" Looks Like

> Unlike the other courses in this ladder, there are no "right" answers here — the goal is honest self-assessment. The model answers below describe what excellent looks like at each question. If your honest answer is C or D, that's the work you still need to do.

### Q1: Excellent looks like: **A**
You can complete this sentence: "I'm pursuing the [Job / Client / Indie] path because I want [specific outcome] by [date]." Vagueness here cascades — every capstone gets written with the wrong tone.

### Q2: Excellent looks like: **A**
The 5 subjects should be **named** (a real business name, a real Twitter handle, a real GitHub project, or yourself). "I'll find a coaching client" is not a subject — "Maria Lopez's career-coaching practice" is. Without this, you'll spend 2 weeks of Module 2 just looking for a subject.

### Q3: Excellent looks like: **A**
P-C-A-R-L is the chassis of every capstone. If you can't recite it cold, every write-up will drift. Tape it to your monitor today.

### Q4: Excellent looks like: **A or B**
Notion is the default. Picking "I'll figure it out later" usually means "I'll spend 3 weeks tweaking Webflow before shipping a single project." Just pick.

### Q5: Excellent looks like: **A**
The 3 anti-patterns (Tools List, Internal Work, Polished But Empty) catch ~80% of beginner portfolios. If one or more applies to your plan, you have time to fix it BEFORE you've sunk hours into the wrong format.

### Q6: Excellent looks like: **A**
Examples of one-sentence capstone descriptions:

- *"A 17-page SEO + content audit for an indie SaaS founder's product, with a 90-day content roadmap."*
- *"A $200 paid campaign across Google + Meta for a friend's coaching practice, post-mortemed publicly."*
- *"A 7-step Make.com workflow that takes a lead from form to qualified Slack ping."*
- *"A multi-touch attribution model in a Google Sheet for an anonymized e-commerce data set."*
- *"A Claude-API content-brief-generator agent on GitHub, with a Loom demo."*

### Q7: Excellent looks like: **A or B**
Skipping videos doesn't fail you, but the Essential 4 are the cheapest way to pick up the framing for the next 6 weeks of work.

### Q8: Excellent looks like: **A**
"B2B SaaS hiring managers at Series A–C startups in fintech / dev tools" is a specific audience. "Marketing people" is not. Specificity here changes the *content* of every capstone — what numbers you show, what voice you use, what businesses you pick.

### Q9: Excellent looks like: **A or B**
A custom domain is ~$12/year on Namecheap. It's not required for success, but it signals "this person has been thinking about their work for a while." If you don't want one, having explicitly decided is fine — drift here is the problem.

### Q10: Excellent looks like: **A**
The most common stall in this course is "I started the SEO audit but realized I can't actually share the keyword research because the business is too small / private." Plan around this NOW. Easiest setups: your own micro-product, a friend's side hustle, a public indie maker who said yes on Twitter.

### Q11: Excellent looks like: **A**
The 90-second pitch is what you'll use 20–30 times in the next 6 months (recruiter calls, networking events, cold sales calls). Drafting it now means you're already practicing while you build.

### Q12: Excellent looks like: **A**
"What I'm NOT including" is half the design. A portfolio of 5 strong pieces beats a portfolio of 15 pieces of varying quality.

### Q13: Excellent looks like: **A**
Reading 3+ real examples of marketing case studies before drafting yours saves you from formatting decisions you don't need to make from scratch.

### Q14: Excellent looks like: **A**
A goal without a deadline is a wish. Pick a date for "Module 1 is locked" — usually within 5 days of starting.

### Q15: Excellent looks like: **A**
If your strongest capstone is still hypothetical, you're not ready to apply / pitch yet. That's fine — that's why this course is 6 weeks long. But know where you are.

### Q16: Excellent looks like: **A**
The 5 capstones map to 5 different hiring signals:

- SEO Audit → analytical writing + strategic thinking
- Paid Campaign → money-at-stake decision making
- Automation → systems thinking
- Attribution → quantitative analysis
- AI Agent → modern technical leverage

### Q17: Excellent looks like: **A or B**
Looking at other portfolios is research, not procrastination — IF you're capping it at 1 hour and using it to inform decisions, not avoid them.

### Q18: Excellent looks like: **A**
Most students stall not because they can't do the work, but because they're afraid the work will be bad. Naming the fear neutralizes it. Common version: *"I'm afraid my audit will be obvious and someone will say 'so what?'"* — response: *"The first one always is. Ship it anyway."*

### Q19: Excellent looks like: **A**
If it's not on the calendar, it's not real. Block at minimum 2 evening sessions / week for the next 6 weeks.

### Q20: Excellent looks like: **A**
A public commitment (LinkedIn post, Twitter, even a Slack to peers) makes you 2–3× more likely to finish. This is well-documented.

### Q21: Excellent looks like: **A**
"Show don't tell" is the difference between a portfolio that lands interviews and one that doesn't. Plan the shows now.

### Q22: Excellent looks like: **A**
Recommended for this course: "honest + scrappy" — show the messy parts, the failures, the "what I'd do differently." This is what 2026 hiring managers respond to vs. corporate-glossy.

### Q23: Excellent looks like: **A**
Module 3 needs $200 spent live. If you skip this, the capstone doesn't exist. Allocate the money NOW so you don't flinch in week 2.

### Q24: Excellent looks like: **A**
What's the very next 1-hour task? Probably either: (1) draft the 5 capstone subjects list, or (2) create the Notion portfolio shell, or (3) reach out to the friend whose business you'll use for Capstone 2.

## 📊 Self-Score Yourself

Count your A's, B's, C's, and D's.

- 20+ A's → 🏆 Locked in. Start Module 2.
- 15–19 A's + few B's → ✅ Solid. Address the B/C answers, then start Module 2.
- 8–14 A's, mostly B/C → ⚠️ The plan is half-baked. Spend another day on Module 1. Worth it.
- <8 A's → 🔁 Re-read Module 1. The 6 weeks ahead depend on this foundation.

The trap here is "I'll figure it out as I go." For this specific course, that's the path to a half-finished portfolio in 6 weeks. The students who finish all 5 capstones are the ones who lock Module 1 first.

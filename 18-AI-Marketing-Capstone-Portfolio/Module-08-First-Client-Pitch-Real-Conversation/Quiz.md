## 🎯 Model Answers + Grading Guidance

### Q1: Excellent looks like: **A**
7 slides. Anything more is overengineered. 7 is calibrated to a 5-min screen-share.

### Q2: Excellent looks like: **A**
Hook + photo. Humanizes the deck. Pictures = trust.

### Q3: Excellent looks like: **A**
Customizable Slide 3. The one slide that changes per prospect. Differentiates you from spray-and-pray pitchers.

### Q4: Excellent looks like: **A**
Pricing visible. Filters tire-kickers. Saves your time.

### Q5: Excellent looks like: **A**
1-page proposal template. Reusable. Fast turnaround = higher close rate.

### Q6: Excellent looks like: **A**
Both templates. LinkedIn DM is shorter / softer. Email is fine to be slightly longer.

### Q7: Excellent looks like: **A**
5 named prospects with links. *"I'll find 5 indie founders"* is not 5 prospects. *"Sara of [product], Marcus of [product]..."* IS.

### Q8: Excellent looks like: **A**
**Sent.** This is the make-or-break of the entire course. Most students do 95% of the work and stop one inch short. Don't.

### Q9: Excellent looks like: **A**
Specific. Each message references something specific they did. Generic = ignored.

### Q10: Excellent looks like: **A**
Held the link. Save for message 2. The first message is just connection.

### Q11: Excellent looks like: **A**
Calendar-set. Without it, you'll forget. Cold-outreach response rates triple with follow-up.

### Q12: Excellent looks like: **A**
At least 1 follow-up per non-responder. Even if it feels uncomfortable.

### Q13: Excellent looks like: **A**
Call booked. The moment someone says "yes call," book it within 24 hrs — before they cool.

### Q14: Excellent looks like: **A**
Agenda written + practiced. The 5/20/5 structure (you / them / next steps).

### Q15: Excellent looks like: **A**
5+ reps. You'll use this 30+ times.

### Q16: Excellent looks like: **A**
3 pricing tiers. Most clients pick the middle. Tier-pricing is psychology + practical.

### Q17: Excellent looks like: **A**
5+ min research each. Reading their site, recent posts, finding the hook for the message.

### Q18: Excellent looks like: **A**
3 differentiators with proof. *"I work AI-native — I built an agent on GitHub"* is proof. *"I'm AI-savvy"* is not.

### Q19: Excellent looks like: **A**
Tracking sheet. Date sent, channel, reply, next action. Simple spreadsheet is enough.

### Q20: Excellent looks like: **A**
Clear plan. *"After 3 follow-ups, move to monthly newsletter touchpoints."* Most students have nothing here.

### Q21: Excellent looks like: **A**
Soft close practiced. *"If this fits, I'll send a proposal in 48 hrs. If not, no hard feelings. What questions do you have?"*

### Q22: Excellent looks like: **A**
Mentally calm with 4/5 ghosting. That's the math. If you can't accept that, you won't send the next batch.

### Q23: Excellent looks like: **A**
Objection responses practiced. Top 3 objections: "too expensive," "I'd need to think," "not the right time." Have a response.

### Q24: Excellent looks like: **A**
Calendar-blocked next batch. The whole game is consistency.

### Q25: Excellent looks like: **A**
All 5 capstones in deck. The grid on Slide 2.

### Q26: Excellent looks like: **A**
4–8 hours. Calibrated.

### Q27: Excellent looks like: **A**
Weekly rhythm. Outreach is not a 1-week exercise. It's the new habit.

### Q28: Excellent looks like: **A**
Send today. Don't postpone. The friction is real and the fix is action.

## 📊 Self-Score Yourself

- 22+ A's → 🏆 You're set up to land first client / job in next 4-8 weeks
- 16–21 A's → ✅ Solid. The biggest fix is usually #8 (actually send them)
- 10–15 A's → ⚠️ Half-built. Most likely: deck OR sending step
- <10 A's → 🔁 Re-read Module 8. Then SEND THE MESSAGES.

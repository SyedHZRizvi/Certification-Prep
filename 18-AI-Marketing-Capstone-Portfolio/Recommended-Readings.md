> **Purpose:** This curated list extends the per-module "Further Reading" sections with the canonical bibliography for the portfolio-shipping, personal-brand-building, first-client-winning operator. ## Canonical Textbooks (3-5 must-haves)

### Showing Your Work
- **Kleon, Austin — *Show Your Work! 10 Ways to Share Your Creativity and Get Discovered* (Workman Publishing, 2014).**
  - The 10-principle guide to building an audience by documenting your process publicly. Most-quoted modern book on "build in public." Engage during Module 7.
- **Kleon, Austin — *Steal Like an Artist: 10 Things Nobody Told You About Being Creative* (Workman Publishing, 2012).**
  - The companion volume. The "nothing is original; everything is remix" framing — particularly liberating for portfolio-builders who feel imposter-syndrome blocked. Engage during Modules 1, 7.
- **Pressfield, Steven — *The War of Art: Break Through the Blocks and Win Your Inner Creative Battles* (Black Irish Entertainment, 2002).**
  - The "Resistance" framework. The book every operator reads when they've started not finishing. Engage between modules.
- **Pressfield, Steven — *Do the Work* (The Domino Project, 2011).**
  - The shorter, more tactical companion to *The War of Art*. Free PDF widely available. Engage between modules.

### Habits and Focus
- **Clear, James — *Atomic Habits: An Easy & Proven Way to Build Good Habits & Break Bad Ones* (Avery, 2018).**
  - The 1% improvement framework + the four laws of behavior change. Required reading for anyone trying to ship 30 LinkedIn posts in 21 days (Module 7's heaviest single ask). Engage during Modules 7, 8.
- **Newport, Cal — *Deep Work: Rules for Focused Success in a Distracted World* (Grand Central, 2016).**
  - The framework on protecting cognitive capacity in an attention-fragmented environment. Particularly relevant to Module 6 (the AI agent build, which requires sustained deep focus). Engage between modules.
- **Newport, Cal — *Digital Minimalism: Choosing a Focused Life in a Noisy World* (Portfolio, 2019).**
  - The companion volume on operational hygiene. Engage between modules.

### Ethics of Sales
- **Roach, Geshe Michael — *The Diamond Cutter: The Buddha on Managing Your Business and Your Life*, Revised ed. (Doubleday, 2009; original 2000).**
  - The Buddhist-monk-turned-diamond-merchant book on right-livelihood, ethical sales, and the long arc of relationships. Engage during Module 8 — the antidote to the high-pressure-cold-outreach playbook.
- **Cialdini, Robert B. — *Pre-Suasion: A Revolutionary Way to Influence and Persuade* (Simon & Schuster, 2016).**
  - The behavioral-science companion to *Influence*. Particularly relevant to Module 8 (the discovery call). Engage during Module 8.

### Personal Brand and Solo Operating
- **Vaynerchuk, Gary — *Crush It!* and *Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook* (HarperBusiness, 2009 and 2013).**
  - The early-2010s personal-brand canon. Some content is dated; the principles are not. Engage during Module 7.
- **Welsh, Justin — *The LinkedIn Operating System* + *The Content Operating System* + *The Solopreneur OS* (self-published; justinwelsh.me).**
  - Paid digital products ($150-300 each) but the free LinkedIn content is itself a complete curriculum. The single most-referenced modern voice on the solo-operator portfolio. Engage during Modules 7, 8.
- **Koe, Dan — *The Art of Focus* (self-published, 2024).**
  - Modern voice on personal-brand-as-thinking-tool. Engage during Module 7.

## Seminal Papers and HBR Articles (5-10 indispensable)

### Personal Brand as Strategic Asset
- **Peters, Tom — "The Brand Called You" — *Fast Company*, August/September 1997.**
  - The article that coined "personal brand." Still relevant 30 years later. Free online. Engage during Module 7.
- **Reichheld, Frederick F. — "The One Number You Need to Grow" — *Harvard Business Review*, December 2003.**
  - The Net Promoter Score paper. Relevant to Module 8 — how you'll measure whether prospects refer you. Engage during Module 8.

### Audience Building
- **Anderson, Chris — *The Long Tail* (Hyperion, 2006).**
  - The 1,000-true-fans / long-tail framing. Engage during Module 7.
- **Kelly, Kevin — "1,000 True Fans" — *kk.org*, 2008.**
  - Free essay. The foundational text on the audience-first solo career. Engage during Module 7.
- **Godin, Seth — *Tribes: We Need You to Lead Us* (Portfolio, 2008).**
  - The "find a tribe and lead it" framing. Engage during Module 7.
- **Godin, Seth — *Linchpin: Are You Indispensable?* (Portfolio, 2010).**
  - The companion volume on building uniquely-valuable skills. Engage between modules.
- **Godin, Seth — *Purple Cow: Transform Your Business by Being Remarkable* (Portfolio, 2003).**
  - The "be remarkable or be invisible" framing. Engage during Module 1 (portfolio strategy).

### Sales and Cold Outreach
- **Cabane, Olivia Fox — *The Charisma Myth: How Anyone Can Master the Art and Science of Personal Magnetism* (Portfolio, 2012).**
  - The presence + warmth + power framework, particularly relevant to the discovery call in Module 8.
- **Voss, Chris — *Never Split the Difference: Negotiating As If Your Life Depended On It* (Harper Business, 2016).**
  - The FBI-hostage-negotiator's playbook applied to business conversations. The "no" doors and the labeling techniques. Engage during Module 8.

### Practitioner Reports (free, annual)
- **LinkedIn — *The State of Sales* (annual; linkedin.com/business/sales).**
  - Free with email signup. The B2B sales-cycle benchmarks every solo operator should know.
- **HubSpot — *State of Inbound* + *State of Marketing* (annual; hubspot.com).**
  - Free. Engage between modules.
- **Hampton — *Founder Compensation Report* + *Founder Mental Health Report* (joinhampton.com; annual).**
  - Free with email signup. The reality-check on solo-operator economics + mental load. Engage during Module 8.

## Recommended Reading Sequence

If you have time for only 5 books, read in this order:

1. **Kleon — *Show Your Work!*** (the operating system of building in public)
2. **Clear — *Atomic Habits*** (the discipline of consistent shipping)
3. **Newport — *Deep Work*** (the cognitive protection of the maker's craft)
4. **Welsh — *The LinkedIn Operating System* / Welsh's free content** (the modern portfolio playbook)
5. **Pressfield — *The War of Art*** (the antidote to not finishing)

If you have time for only 3 industry voices to follow weekly, follow:

1. **Justin Welsh** — solo operator playbook
2. **Dan Koe** — the philosophy + creative-flow companion
3. **Ali Abdaal** OR **Sahil Bloom** — the productivity + systems layer

If you have time for only 2 essays, read:

1. **Kevin Kelly — "1,000 True Fans"** — the foundational audience essay
2. **Tom Peters — "The Brand Called You"** — the foundational personal-brand essay

## A Note on AI in Portfolio-Building (2024-2026 specifics)

The biggest shifts you should be tracking:

- **AI for content production** — Claude / ChatGPT for first drafts + Midjourney / DALL-E / Adobe Firefly for visuals + HeyGen / Synthesia for video + ElevenLabs for voice. Your portfolio's *speed* in 2026 depends on this stack.
- **AI for portfolio site personalization** — Mutiny / Dynamic Yield / Optimizely for the personalized first-time visitor experience.
- **AI for cold outreach personalization** — Clay + Apollo + Instantly + Lemlist + Claude/ChatGPT API. The 3-5x reply-rate lift in 2026.
- **AI agent products** — Module 6 of this course is the build. The broader category — agentic AI as a sellable product — is the highest-leverage emerging product category in 2026.
- **AI ethics in portfolio work** — if your portfolio includes AI-generated content, disclose it. The FTC and EU AI Act both expect transparency.

## Maintaining Your Portfolio Habit

The hardest thing about this course's deliverables is not building them once — it's keeping them alive after Week 12. Plan to:

- Publish 1 new case study every 3-6 months. Pick a real engagement (whether agency client or in-house project), document it, ship it.
- Refresh LinkedIn Featured section quarterly. Pin the newest case study; demote the oldest.
- Block 2 hours/week for "feed the portfolio" work even when you're slammed with client delivery.
- Re-take the Recruiter Readiness Assessment (Final-Mock-Exam in this course) every 6 months. If the score has slipped, the portfolio has aged.
- Read one founder/operator memoir per quarter. Welsh's content, Koe's *The Art of Focus*, a Kleon book — the reading habit is what keeps the personal-brand voice fresh.

## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **A. Same side**
U-shape places both docks on one side; product flows in, around, and back out. Lower truck-circulation cost.

### Q2: **B. High-volume automated**
I-shape (straight-through) eliminates cross-traffic, ideal for automation routing.

### Q3: **B. Receiving**
Workflow: Receiving → Putaway → Storage → Replenishment → Pick → Pack → Ship.

### Q4: **B. Pre-distribution**
Pre-distribution = labeled by destination before arrival. Walmart's model.

### Q5: **B. Any available slot, WMS-tracked**
Random putaway is *systematic* in modern DCs, the WMS records every location.

### Q6: **B. Few SKUs, deep, LIFO**
Drive-in is dense but LIFO. Selective handles FIFO. Pallet flow is FIFO with density.

### Q7: **B. Crane-based in racking**
AS/RS = Automated Storage & Retrieval (cranes in aisles). AGVs/AMRs are floor-based.

### Q8: **B. AGV fixed paths; AMR dynamic mapping**
AGVs need wire/laser guides. AMRs use LiDAR/SLAM to navigate dynamically.

### Q9: **B. Waist-to-shoulder height**
Golden zone is ergonomically optimal, least bending/reaching.

### Q10: **C. 65–85%**
Above 85%, congestion and pick-face stockouts destroy productivity.

### Q11: **B. 99.5%+**
World-class DCs target 99.5%+ pick accuracy.

### Q12: **B. LMS (Labor Management)**
LMS handles engineered standards, productivity tracking, incentive plans.

### Q13: **B. US Customs supply-chain security**
Customs-Trade Partnership Against Terrorism; voluntary program for faster customs treatment.

### Q14: **B. 0–8°C**
Refrigerated. Frozen is -18°C and below; controlled ambient 15–25°C.

### Q15: **B. Supplier labels by destination**
Pre-distribution requires upstream coordination, supplier knows the destination before shipping.

### Q16: **B. Vertical storage tower, slow-mover small parts**
VLMs store small parts vertically with shuttles bringing trays to the operator.

### Q17: **C. Frequent SKU churn, lumpy demand**
Automation needs stability. Lumpy/changing operations don't recoup CapEx.

### Q18: **B. Time studies / MOST**
Engineered standards use formal time-and-motion measurement (MOST = Maynard Operation Sequence Technique).

### Q19: **B. Zones by velocity, random within**
Class-based: A-zone, B-zone, C-zone; within a zone, items go to any available slot.

### Q20: **B. 29 CFR 1910.178**
OSHA's powered industrial truck (forklift) standard.

### Q21: **B. Hands-free, mid CapEx, accurate**
Voice is fast, accurate, and cheaper than pick-to-light.

### Q22: **B. Directed work**
WMS directs each task pick, putaway, replenishment by algorithm.

### Q23: **B. Receipt to putaway-complete**
Dock-to-stock = "from the dock to inventory available for pick."

### Q24: **B. FIFO**
Gravity / pallet flow loads from the rear; picks from the front = FIFO.

### Q25: **C. Manual cart with WMS**
Low volume + SKU churn = automation can't pay back. Start manual + WMS; automate later.

## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. FTL**
15 pallets / 28,000 lbs / one origin / one destination = textbook FTL.

### Q2: **B. LTL**
600 lbs × 2 = 1,200 lbs total. Too big for parcel, too small for FTL, LTL fits.

### Q3: **B. Twenty-foot Equivalent Unit**
TEU = 20' container equivalent. Industry standardization unit.

### Q4: **C. 2**
40' container = 1 FEU = 2 TEU.

### Q5: **B. Fuel adjustments**
BAF (Bunker Adjustment Factor) accounts for marine fuel cost swings. CAF is currency.

### Q6: **B. Container On Flat Car**
COFC = container on flat car. TOFC = trailer on flat car (less common today).

### Q7: **B. Short-distance truck between rail/port and end**
Drayage trucks shuttle containers from rail ramps or ports to customers.

### Q8: **B. 1 kg : 6,000 cm³**
Air uses 1:6000. Ocean uses 1 t : 1 m³. Parcel uses 139 in³/lb for UPS US ground.

### Q9: **C. 80 kg**
Volume = 100 × 80 × 60 = 480,000 cm³. Volumetric kg = 480,000 / 6,000 = **80 kg**. Chargeable = max(40, 80) = 80.

### Q10: **B. Liquids and gases**
Pipelines move oil, gas, refined products, NGL, ammonia. Not for solids.

### Q11: **C. 3–4×**
Rail ≈ 480 ton-miles/gal; truck ≈ 130. Rail is ~3.7× more fuel-efficient.

### Q12: **B. Intermodal = standardized equipment; multimodal = single contract**
Common trap, both involve multiple modes; the difference is *contract structure*.

### Q13: **B. 11 hours**
FMCSA HOS: 11-hour driving limit per day; 14-hour duty window; 10 off.

### Q14: **B. Hub-and-spoke**
Parcels route through regional hubs; nominally near origin/dest may still travel through a distant hub.

### Q15: **B. Visit N customers with M vehicles**
VRP is a classic optimization problem solved by TMS routing engines.

### Q16: **C. 11–16 days**
Asia → USWC typical. East Coast via Panama is 26–35 days.

### Q17: **C. 28–53%**
Last-mile is the most expensive leg by far due to low drop density and time per stop.

### Q18: **B. Inject parcel deeper into region**
Move parcels via LTL/truckload to a deeper hub, then inject, saves per-parcel cost.

### Q19: **B. LTL pricing (50–500)**
NMFC classifies LTL freight by density, value, stowability, handling. Class drives rate.

### Q20: **C. Air**
Time-critical perishables across an ocean = air freight.

### Q21: **B. Two containers stacked on a well car**
Doublestack is standard in North America; uncommon in Europe due to tunnel/bridge clearances.

### Q22: **B. Pipeline**
Pipelines rarely fail; weather, traffic, port congestion don't affect them.

### Q23: **B. % of loads carriers accept**
Tender acceptance is a carrier-relationship KPI; low acceptance = pricing problem or capacity issue.

### Q24: **D. Marketing campaigns**
TLC = product + freight + duties + insurance + handling + inventory carrying + brokerage. Marketing is SG&A.

### Q25: **B. Speed vs cost trade-off (+ inventory + service)**
Mode choice always balances cost, time, and downstream effects (carrying cost, service penalty).

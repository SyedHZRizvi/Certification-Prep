## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **C. 11**
Incoterms 2020 has 11 rules: EXW, FCA, CPT, CIP, DAP, DPU, DDP (any mode) + FAS, FOB, CFR, CIF (ocean/inland-waterway only).

### Q2: **B. Bulk ocean where goods are loaded on a vessel**
FOB is for goods physically placed *on board*. For containers, FCA is correct.

### Q3: **B. FCA**
FCA at origin terminal/port works for containers — risk transfers when seller hands to carrier.

### Q4: **B. At seller's premises**
EXW = buyer collects from seller. Minimum seller responsibility.

### Q5: **B. On board vessel at origin**
Under all C-terms (CIF, CFR, CIP, CPT), seller pays freight to destination, but RISK transfers at origin.

### Q6: **D. DDP**
DDP = Delivered Duty Paid. Seller handles everything including import duty.

### Q7: **A. Delivered at Place Unloaded**
DPU replaced DAT in 2020. Critically, the seller unloads at destination.

### Q8: **C. All-risk (Clauses A)**
CIP requires Clauses A. CIF still requires only minimum Clauses C. (This was a 2020 change.)

### Q9: **B. 6**
HS = 6-digit global standard. Country extensions push higher.

### Q10: **C. 10 digits**
US HTS goes to 10 digits.

### Q11: **B. Legally responsible for customs**
IOR is the legal party for customs entry, duty, and compliance — typically the buyer, sometimes the seller (under DDP).

### Q12: **B. Files customs entries**
Customs broker is licensed to file entries on behalf of the importer.

### Q13: **B. Books transport, prepares docs, no goods possession**
Freight forwarders are organizers; they don't typically own assets or take title.

### Q14: **B. Non-Vessel Operating Common Carrier**
NVOCC issues its own BL but doesn't own the ship — buys space on actual carriers.

### Q15: **A. NAFTA in 2020**
US-Mexico-Canada Agreement, effective July 2020.

### Q16: **B. Originate per rules of origin + cert of origin**
FTA benefits require qualifying origin and proper documentation.

### Q17: **B. Duty deferral**
FTZs defer duty until goods leave for the host country's commerce. Also enables duty inversion.

### Q18: **B. Sold below normal value (dumping)**
Anti-dumping duty offsets predatory pricing.

### Q19: **B. Documents matching LC terms**
LCs are document-driven. A discrepancy can delay payment.

### Q20: **B. % of declared customs value**
Most common duty type globally.

### Q21: **B. Expedited customs for trusted traders**
C-TPAT, AEO, PIP all reward security investment with faster customs treatment.

### Q22: **B. Import duty paid by seller**
DAP = delivered but buyer clears customs and pays duty. DDP = seller pays duty too.

### Q23: **B. ATA Carnet (TIB)**
ATA Carnet enables temporary import without duty for samples, trade shows, professional equipment.

### Q24: **B. EXW, FCA, FAS, FOB …**
Ladder: EXW → FCA → FAS → FOB → CFR → CIF → CPT → CIP → DAP → DPU → DDP.

### Q25: **C. Delivery + unloading at destination**
DPU = the only Incoterm where the seller has to unload at destination. (Renamed from DAT in 2020 to make this explicit.)

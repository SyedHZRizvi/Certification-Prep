# PMP Module 2: People Domain 👥

> **Why this module matters:** **42% of the exam** is People domain. This is the BIGGEST module to master. Most PMP failures happen here. Most exam questions are situational — "What should the PM do?" — and the right answer is almost always *people-focused*.

---

## 🎬 A Story: The Project From Hell

A project manager named Carlos took over a 30-person team mid-project. The previous PM had quit. Carlos found:

- 🤬 Two engineers feuding over architecture
- 😴 Three team members "quiet quitting"
- 📞 A stakeholder calling daily for updates
- 🤐 Team afraid to speak up in meetings
- 🏃 A star performer about to leave

Carlos did NOT:
- Tell people what to do
- Escalate everything to the sponsor
- Build a Gantt chart for week 1

He DID:
- Have 1-on-1s with all 30 people in week 1
- Mediate the architecture conflict (with both engineers, together)
- Implement a daily 15-min standup (transparency)
- Ran a retrospective on team dynamics
- Got the star performer's frustrations addressed

By week 8, the project was back on track.

**That's the People Domain.** It's about *humans*, not Gantt charts. Let's break it down.

## 🛡️ Servant Leadership for PMs

The new PMP expects you to be a **servant leader**, not a "command-and-control" manager.

### What That Looks Like:
- 🛡️ **Remove impediments** for the team
- 👂 **Listen actively**
- 🌱 **Develop people**, not just deliverables
- 🤝 **Empower decisions** at the lowest level possible
- 🎯 **Focus on team outcomes**, not individual performance

### The 7 Servant Leader Behaviors (per Larry Spears):
1. Listening
2. Empathy
3. Healing
4. Awareness
5. Persuasion
6. Conceptualization
7. Foresight

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "PM is told a team member is underperforming. What FIRST?"
- ❌ Replace them
- ❌ Discipline them
- ✅ **Talk with them — understand the situation, support them**

## 🤝 Building & Developing Teams

### Tuckman's 5 Stages of Team Development (MEMORIZE):

```
1. FORMING    → Polite, getting to know each other (PM is leader)
2. STORMING   → Conflict, power struggles (PM is COACH)
3. NORMING    → Shared ways of working (PM is FACILITATOR)
4. PERFORMING → High output (PM is HANDS-OFF)
5. ADJOURNING → Project ends; team disbands
```

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "Team has been together 3 months. Conflicts about technical decisions are bubbling up." → **Storming.** PM should facilitate resolution, not panic.

🎯 **Common trap:** Don't treat Forming team members harshly — they need support, not pressure.

## 🎓 Coaching & Mentoring

The PM as **coach**:
- Asks questions, doesn't dictate
- Helps team members find their own answers
- Builds capability over time

The PM as **mentor**:
- Shares experience
- Provides career guidance
- Models behaviors

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "A junior team member asks how to approach a problem. The PM should:"
- ❌ Solve it for them
- ✅ **Coach them: "What approaches are you considering? What are the trade-offs?"**

## 🤬 Conflict Management

Conflict in projects is **inevitable and often healthy**. The exam tests your conflict-handling techniques.

### Sources of Conflict:
- 🎯 Schedule
- 🎯 Project priorities
- 🎯 Resources
- 🎯 Technical opinions
- 🎯 Administrative procedures
- 🎯 Cost
- 🎯 Personality

### 5 Conflict Resolution Techniques (MEMORIZE):

| Technique | What It Looks Like | When To Use |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| **1. Confronting / Problem-Solving** | Find win-win solution | ⭐ BEST default |
| **2. Collaborating** | Open discussion of multiple views | When relationship matters |
| **3. Compromising** | Both give a little | When time is tight |
| **4. Smoothing / Accommodating** | Emphasize agreement, downplay differences | Temp fix, low stakes |
| **5. Forcing / Directing** | One side wins by authority | Emergencies |
| **6. Withdrawing / Avoiding** | Retreat from the conflict | Worst — only for cooling off |

🎯 **Exam pattern (MOST COMMON):** "Two team members disagree on a technical approach. PM should:"
- ✅ **Confront / Problem-solve** — bring them together to find the best solution.

❌ Avoid "withdrawing" or "smoothing" — these are usually wrong.

### Mnemonic: **"5 C's + W"** — Confronting, Collaborating, Compromising, Smoothing/aCcommodating, Forcing/Commanding, Withdrawing

## 💬 Communication

PMs spend ~90% of time communicating. Master this.

### 3 Dimensions of Communication:

#### 1. Internal vs External
- Internal: team members, sponsors
- External: vendors, customers, regulators

#### 2. Formal vs Informal
- Formal: contracts, status reports
- Informal: hallway chats, IMs

#### 3. Vertical vs Horizontal
- Vertical: up/down hierarchy
- Horizontal: peer-to-peer

### Communication Methods:
- **Push** — emails, status reports (sender controls)
- **Pull** — wikis, intranet (receiver controls)
- **Interactive** — meetings, calls (real-time)

### Communication Channels Formula
**Channels = n(n-1)/2** where n = number of stakeholders.

Example: 5 people → 5(4)/2 = **10 channels**.
Add 1 person → 6(5)/2 = **15 channels** (50% more!)

🎯 **Exam tip:** When asked "how many comm channels?" — apply the formula.

### The 7 C's of Effective Communication
1. **Clear** — easy to understand
2. **Concise** — no unnecessary details
3. **Concrete** — specific facts
4. **Correct** — accurate
5. **Coherent** — logical flow
6. **Complete** — has all info
7. **Courteous** — respectful

## 🎓 Emotional Intelligence (EQ)

The exam loves EQ. Per Goleman's framework:

### 5 Components:
1. 🪞 **Self-awareness** — know your emotions
2. 🛑 **Self-regulation** — manage your emotions
3. 🚀 **Motivation** — drive yourself
4. 💞 **Empathy** — understand others
5. 🤝 **Social skills** — manage relationships

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "PM feels frustrated with a stakeholder's demands. What should they do?"
- ❌ Argue
- ❌ Give in
- ✅ **Self-regulate, then have an empathetic conversation about needs.**

---

## 💪 Motivation Theories (Know These!)

### Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs
```
       Self-actualization (purpose)
              ↑
            Esteem (recognition)
              ↑
        Belonging (relationships)
              ↑
       Safety (security)
              ↑
       Physiological (basics)
```

🎯 **Exam pattern:** Lower needs must be met before higher motivate. A team member worried about job security ≠ motivated by "stretch goals."

### Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory
- **Hygiene factors** (avoid dissatisfaction): salary, working conditions, policies
- **Motivators** (true motivation): achievement, recognition, growth, responsibility

🎯 **Insight:** Pay raises *prevent* dissatisfaction; they don't *motivate*. Motivators motivate.

### McGregor's X & Y
- **Theory X** — people are lazy, need control
- **Theory Y** — people are self-motivated, need empowerment

🎯 PMP-2021 mindset = Theory Y.

### McClelland's Acquired Needs (3 needs)
- **Achievement** — challenging goals
- **Affiliation** — relationships
- **Power** — influence over others

Different team members are motivated differently — adapt your style.

## 🚀 Empowerment

The PM should empower the team:
- Delegate decisions to lowest competent level
- Trust people's judgment
- Provide support, not micro-management
- Celebrate ownership

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "Team member asks for permission for every small decision. PM should:"
- ✅ **Coach them on autonomy and trust their judgment**

---

## 🌍 Virtual Teams

Special considerations:
- 🕐 Time zones
- 🎥 Video by default
- 📋 Written communication clarity
- 🤝 Trust-building takes longer
- 🏖️ Cultural awareness

Best practices:
- Schedule overlapping work hours
- Use shared tools
- More frequent check-ins
- Clear ground rules

---

## 📜 Team Charter & Ground Rules

A **Team Charter** is a document the team creates together:
- Team values
- Communication guidelines
- Decision-making process
- Conflict resolution norms
- Meeting cadences

**Created collaboratively** — not imposed by the PM.

## 🤝 Stakeholder Engagement

(More in Module 10, but at the People Domain level:)

### Stakeholder Engagement Levels (per PMBOK):
1. **Unaware** — doesn't know about project
2. **Resistant** — knows but resists
3. **Neutral** — knows, neither supports nor opposes
4. **Supportive** — knows and supports
5. **Leading** — actively engaged in success

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "PM identifies a stakeholder who is resistant. What should they do?"
- ✅ **Engage them — understand concerns, address them, move them up the engagement ladder**

## 🎯 The Servant Leader's Toolkit

For exam scenarios, default to these:

1. 👂 **Listen first**
2. 🗣️ **Have 1-on-1s**
3. 🛡️ **Remove impediments**
4. 🤝 **Facilitate dialogue**
5. 🎓 **Coach, don't tell**
6. 🌱 **Develop people**
7. 🎁 **Recognize contributions**
8. 🤲 **Empower decisions**

🎯 If a question's "right" answer involves any of these → **it's probably correct.**

## ⚠️ People Domain Anti-Patterns (Wrong Answers)

- ❌ "Replace the underperformer immediately"
- ❌ "Escalate to HR for any conflict"
- ❌ "Tell the team to follow the plan"
- ❌ "Schedule more meetings"
- ❌ "Send a stern email"
- ❌ "Document the issue and move on"

🎯 The PMP-2021 doesn't reward authoritarian PM behaviors.

# PMP Module 10: Stakeholder Management 👥

> **Why this module matters:** ~10-15 questions. Stakeholder management spans People + Process domains. Get this right and you'll bank a chunk of exam points.

---

## 🎯 What Is a Stakeholder?

Per PMI:
> *"An individual, group, or organization that may affect, be affected by, or perceive itself to be affected by a decision, activity, or outcome of a project."*

Translation: **anyone who cares about, is impacted by, or has influence over the project.**

### Examples of Stakeholders:
- 👔 Sponsor
- 👥 Team
- 🏢 Functional managers
- 💼 Customers / users
- 🤝 Vendors
- 🏛️ Regulators
- 🌍 Community
- 💰 Investors
- 📰 Media (in major projects)

---

## 🔄 The 4 Stakeholder Processes

### 1. Identify Stakeholders
- 📋 Find everyone who has a stake
- 🛠️ Tools: Stakeholder Analysis, Power/Interest Grid, Stakeholder Cube
- 📋 Output: **Stakeholder Register**

### 2. Plan Stakeholder Engagement
- 🎯 How to engage each stakeholder
- 📋 Output: **Stakeholder Engagement Plan**

### 3. Manage Stakeholder Engagement
- 💬 Communicate
- 🤝 Build trust
- 🚧 Address concerns

### 4. Monitor Stakeholder Engagement
- 👀 Track engagement levels
- 🔄 Adjust approach as needed

---

## 📋 Stakeholder Register

The central document. Includes:
- 🆔 Identification (name, role, contact)
- 📊 Assessment (requirements, expectations, influence, interest)
- 📂 Classification (internal/external, friend/foe, etc.)

🎯 **Continuously updated** — stakeholders change, expectations evolve.

---

## 📊 Stakeholder Analysis Tools

### Power-Interest Grid (most-tested)
A 2×2 matrix:

```
              INTEREST →
            LOW            HIGH
       ┌────────────────────────────┐
  HIGH │  Keep        │  Manage     │ ← (engage closely)
       │  Satisfied   │  Closely    │
POWER  ├──────────────┼─────────────┤
       │   Monitor    │  Keep       │
   LOW │  (min effort)│  Informed   │
       └────────────────────────────┘
```

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "High power, high interest stakeholder. How to engage?" → **Manage closely.**

### Power-Influence Grid
Similar but uses Power × Influence (active engagement) instead of interest.

### Salience Model
3 dimensions:
- **Power** — ability to enforce
- **Legitimacy** — appropriate involvement
- **Urgency** — time-pressure to act

7 categories of stakeholders based on which dimensions apply.

## 🎯 Engagement Levels (KEY!)

Per PMBOK, stakeholders fall into 5 levels:

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Unaware** | Doesn't know about project |
| **Resistant** | Knows + against it |
| **Neutral** | Knows + neutral |
| **Supportive** | Knows + supports |
| **Leading** | Actively driving success |

### Stakeholder Engagement Assessment Matrix
For each stakeholder:
- C = Current level
- D = Desired level

🎯 **Goal:** Move stakeholders toward "Leading" (or at minimum, "Supportive").

---

## 🎯 Stakeholder Strategies

### For Resistant Stakeholders:
- Listen to concerns
- Address misunderstandings
- Show how project benefits them
- Engage them in decisions
- Be patient

### For Supportive Stakeholders:
- Keep informed
- Leverage their influence
- Show appreciation
- Don't take for granted

### For Neutral Stakeholders:
- Provide regular updates
- Identify what would move them to "supportive"

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "PM identifies a resistant stakeholder."
- ❌ Wrong: Ignore them; focus on supporters
- ❌ Wrong: Force compliance
- ✅ Right: **Engage to understand concerns + address them**

## 💬 Stakeholder Communication

(Covered in Module 2's communication section.)

Tailor communication to each stakeholder:
- 👔 Executives: high-level, value-focused, brief
- 🛠️ Team: detailed, operational
- 🤝 Customers: feature-focused, frequent
- 🏛️ Regulators: compliance-focused, formal

## 🎯 The Stakeholder Cube

3D version of Power-Interest:
- Power (high/low)
- Interest (high/low)
- Attitude (positive/negative)

= 8 categories

🎯 Less commonly tested than 2D grids.

## 🏢 Internal vs External Stakeholders

### Internal:
- Sponsor
- Team
- Functional managers
- Other employees

### External:
- Customers
- Vendors
- Regulators
- Community
- Competitors

🎯 Internal often have more direct influence; external may have legal or reputational power.

---

## 🎯 Sponsor's Role (Important!)

The **Sponsor** is a special stakeholder:
- 💰 Provides funding
- 📜 Approves Project Charter
- 🛡️ Champions the project
- ⚠️ Removes top-level impediments
- 🎯 Defines success criteria

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "Major obstacle outside the team's control. PM has tried everything."
- ✅ **Escalate to Sponsor.**

The PM should NOT escalate trivial things to the sponsor — only major impediments the team can't address.

---

## 🤝 Stakeholder Engagement in Agile

In agile, stakeholder engagement is **continuous**:
- 🎁 **Sprint Reviews** = stakeholder demos
- 💬 **Continuous feedback** loop
- 🔄 **Backlog refinement** with key stakeholders
- 📊 **Transparent metrics** (burndown, velocity)

🎯 Agile actively involves stakeholders; predictive may engage them more at gates.

---

## ⚠️ Common Stakeholder Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Identifying stakeholders only at the start
- ❌ Treating all stakeholders the same
- ❌ Communicating only with supportive stakeholders
- ❌ Ignoring "soft" stakeholders (community, public)
- ❌ Failing to update the register as new stakeholders emerge

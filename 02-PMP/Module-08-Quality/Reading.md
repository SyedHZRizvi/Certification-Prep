# PMP Module 8: Quality Management ✅

> **Why this module matters:** ~5-10 questions. Quality is *built into* projects, not inspected at the end. Know the gurus, key tools, and the philosophy.

---

## 🎯 What Is Quality?

Per PMI:
> *"The degree to which a set of inherent characteristics fulfills requirements."*

Plain English: does it do what stakeholders said they need?

### Quality vs Grade
- **Quality** = how well it meets requirements (always desirable)
- **Grade** = the level of features (low grade isn't bad — Toyota Corolla is low grade, high quality!)

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "A low-grade product can still be high quality." → **TRUE.**

## 🏛️ The Quality Gurus (Know These!)

### 1. W. Edwards Deming (PDCA)
- **Plan-Do-Check-Act** cycle
- 14 Points for Management
- "85% of problems are system problems, not people problems"

### 2. Joseph Juran (Quality Trilogy)
- **Quality Planning**
- **Quality Control**
- **Quality Improvement**
- "Fitness for use"

### 3. Philip Crosby
- **"Quality is free"** (preventing defects costs less than fixing)
- **Zero defects**
- 4 absolutes of quality

### 4. Kaoru Ishikawa
- **Fishbone diagram (Ishikawa)** — root cause analysis
- 7 basic tools of quality

### 5. Genichi Taguchi
- **Robust design** — design for variability
- Loss function

### 6. Walter Shewhart
- **Control charts** (statistical process control)
- "PDCA" originally his — "Shewhart cycle"

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "Who is associated with the fishbone diagram?" → **Ishikawa**

## 🔄 The 3 Quality Processes

### 1. Plan Quality Management
- Define quality requirements
- Output: **Quality Management Plan**
- Includes: standards, metrics, checklists

### 2. Manage Quality
- Translate plan into testable activities
- "Quality Assurance" (QA)
- Output: quality reports, change requests

### 3. Control Quality
- Inspect deliverables
- Verify they meet requirements
- Output: verified deliverables, work performance information

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "Inspecting deliverables to ensure they meet requirements is:"
- ✅ **Control Quality**

## 🛠️ The 7 Basic Tools of Quality

These are EXAM-IMPORTANT. Know them:

### 1. Cause-and-Effect Diagram (Fishbone / Ishikawa)
- Used for **root cause analysis**
- Bones = categories (e.g., People, Process, Tools)
- Helps find WHY a problem occurs

### 2. Flowcharts
- Visualize a process
- Identify bottlenecks, delays, decision points

### 3. Checksheets
- Tally counts (e.g., defects by type)
- Simple data collection

### 4. Pareto Chart
- Bar chart of categories sorted by frequency
- **80/20 rule** — 80% of problems from 20% of causes
- Focus on the "vital few"

### 5. Histograms
- Bar chart of frequency distribution
- Reveals patterns, central tendency, spread

### 6. Control Charts
- Plot data over time with **upper/lower control limits**
- Detect "out of control" processes
- **Rule of 7** — 7 consecutive points on one side = out of control
- Even within limits, patterns matter

### 7. Scatter Diagrams
- Plot 2 variables to see correlation
- Helps identify relationships

🧠 Memory: **C-F-C-P-H-C-S** ("Can Fish Catch Pretty Healthy Caribbean Salmon?")

## 📊 Key Quality Concepts

### COQ (Cost of Quality)
Total cost of all quality-related activities.

#### Cost of Conformance:
- 🛠️ **Prevention** — training, processes (cheaper)
- 🔍 **Appraisal** — testing, inspection

#### Cost of Non-Conformance:
- 🛠️ **Internal Failure** — rework, scrap
- 🔥 **External Failure** — recalls, warranty, lawsuits, lost reputation

🎯 **Insight:** Spending on prevention is *cheaper* than fixing failures. (Crosby's "quality is free")

### Inspection vs Prevention
- **Inspection** = catch defects after they happen (reactive)
- **Prevention** = avoid defects (proactive — preferred!)

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "PM should focus on:" → **Prevention over inspection.**

## 📐 Statistical Concepts

### Standard Deviation (σ)
- Measures spread/variability
- 1σ = ~68% of data
- 2σ = ~95%
- 3σ = ~99.73%
- 6σ = ~99.99966% (Six Sigma!)

### Six Sigma
- Quality philosophy + methodology
- Aim: <3.4 defects per million opportunities
- DMAIC: **D**efine, **M**easure, **A**nalyze, **I**mprove, **C**ontrol

### Tolerances vs Control Limits
- **Tolerances** — acceptable range (set by customer)
- **Control limits** — natural process variation (set by process)

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "Customer specifies acceptable range" → **Tolerances**.

## 🎯 Quality in Agile

In agile, quality is built in via:
- ✅ **TDD (Test-Driven Development)**
- ✅ **Continuous Integration**
- ✅ **Definition of Done** (Module 5 of Scrum track!)
- ✅ **Pair programming**
- ✅ **Customer feedback** every sprint
- ✅ **Refactoring** to improve code quality

🎯 **Insight:** Agile teams don't have a separate "QA phase" — quality is everyone's responsibility, every sprint.

## 📋 Quality Audits & Reviews

### Quality Audit
- Independent review of project against standards
- Verifies QA processes are followed
- Identifies improvements

### Process Analysis
- Examines a process for inefficiency
- Often uses root cause analysis (fishbone)

---

## ⚠️ Common Quality Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Inspecting at the end only ("test it before release")
- ❌ Treating quality as separate from "real work"
- ❌ Rejecting requirements as "out of scope" without analysis
- ❌ "Gold plating" — adding extras without authorization
- ❌ No quality metrics defined

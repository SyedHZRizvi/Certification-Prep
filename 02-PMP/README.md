## ✨ Why This Certification Matters

### 🎯 The promise

The Project Management Professional (PMP) from PMI is, by every honest measure, the most recognized project-management credential on Earth. It is the letters that move resumes from the "maybe" stack to the "interview" stack at Fortune 500s, federal contractors, consulting firms, and increasingly at tech companies running large cross-functional initiatives. Pass the PMP and you are no longer a project manager — you are *certified* to lead any project, in any methodology (predictive, agile, hybrid), at almost any scale.

### 💼 Career outcomes after passing

- **Project Manager** ($95K–$135K) — the baseline role; most postings list PMP as preferred or required
- **Senior Project Manager** ($120K–$160K) — typical 2–4 years after PMP, with portfolio of delivered programs
- **Program Manager** ($130K–$175K) — coordinating multiple related projects, often at director-equivalent level
- **Portfolio Manager / PMO Lead** ($140K–$190K) — running the project economics for an entire business unit
- **Management Consultant (Big-4 or boutique)** ($110K–$180K plus bonus) — PMP is table stakes for delivery-side hires at Deloitte, Accenture, EY, KPMG, McKinsey Implementation
- **Technical Program Manager (TPM)** at FAANG-adjacent tech ($150K–$240K total comp) — PMP increasingly listed alongside MBA-equivalent rigor

PMI's own salary study (Earning Power, 11th edition) puts the median PMP salary in the US at roughly $123K — a 16–32% premium over non-certified PMs in the same roles. The credential is also the gateway into PgMP, PfMP, and the PMI Disciplined Agile suite.

## 📊 The 3 Domains

The PMP exam tests 3 domains:

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
│   PEOPLE: 42%   │  Lead & develop team   │
├─────────────────┼────────────────────────┤
│   PROCESS: 50%  │  Manage the work       │
├─────────────────┼────────────────────────┤
│ BUSINESS: 8%    │  Strategic / business  │
└──────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Half the exam is process, but People is so weighted it's nearly equal in question volume.**

## 🌈 Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid

The new PMP (since 2021) tests ALL three approaches:

- ~50% predictive (waterfall) questions
- ~50% agile or hybrid questions

**Translation:** You CANNOT skip the agile content even if you've been a waterfall PM for 20 years.

## 📚 The PMP Mindset (CRITICAL!)

Your job during the exam is to think like an **agile, servant-leader PM**. Default behaviors:

✅ **Always do:**
- Talk to the team / stakeholders first
- Coach, don't manage
- Empower the team
- Communicate proactively
- Look for root causes
- Use data for decisions
- Iterate and adapt

❌ **Almost never:**
- Escalate to sponsor first
- Submit change requests immediately
- Reject scope changes without analysis
- Tell people what to do without consultation
- Stick rigidly to plan

🎯 If a question feels like it needs ESCALATION, ask: *"What can the team do FIRST?"* If something can be solved at the team level, that's usually the right answer.

---

## 🎯 Top 5 Question Types You'll See

### 1. Situational ("PM should do FIRST")
Most common. Look for the one that addresses the cause, not the symptom.

### 2. Calculation (EVM, etc.)
~5–10 questions. Memorize formulas (Module 6).

### 3. Knowledge ("Which is TRUE about...")
Less common in new PMP. Still possible.

### 4. Drag-and-drop ordering
Order phases, deliverables, etc.

### 5. Multi-select ("Choose 2")
Pick the 2 most relevant. Read carefully.

## ⚠️ The 5 Most Common Reasons People Fail

1. ❌ Studied old PMBOK (5th edition) instead of PMP-2021 mindset
2. ❌ Didn't practice agile questions enough
3. ❌ Got stuck on EVM math (memorize 5 formulas, not 20)
4. ❌ Defaulted to "escalate" or "change request" reflexes
5. ❌ Didn't take 1,000+ practice questions before the real exam

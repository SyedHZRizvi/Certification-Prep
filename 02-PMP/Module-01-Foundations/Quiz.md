# ✏️ PMP Module 1 Quiz: Foundations

## Questions

### Q1. A project is BEST described as:
A. An ongoing operation
B. A temporary endeavor producing a unique result
C. A program of related work
D. A continuous improvement effort

### Q2. The 4 PMI ethics values are:
A. Responsibility, Respect, Fairness, Honesty
B. Trust, Transparency, Speed, Quality
C. Plan, Execute, Inspect, Adapt
D. Lead, Coach, Serve, Mentor

### Q3. Which of the following is an Enterprise Environmental Factor (EEF)?
A. Project templates
B. Lessons learned from previous projects
C. Government regulations
D. Project management methodology

### Q4. A PM is offered an expensive gift by a vendor while evaluating bids. They should:
A. Accept it; it's just business
B. Refuse and disclose to management
C. Accept but report later
D. Ask the team for opinion

### Q5. In a Functional organization, the Project Manager:
A. Has full authority
B. Has limited or no authority
C. Reports directly to the CEO
D. Always wins resource conflicts

### Q6. The PMI Talent Triangle includes:
A. Technical, Strategic, Leadership
B. Ways of Working, Power Skills, Business Acumen
C. Plan, Execute, Monitor
D. People, Process, Technology

### Q7. A project has well-known requirements and a fixed regulatory deadline. Best approach:
A. Agile
B. Predictive
C. Always agile
D. Whatever the team prefers

### Q8. The new PMP exam (since 2021) is structured around:
A. 5 Process Groups + 10 Knowledge Areas
B. 8 Performance Domains
C. 12 Best Practices
D. 4 Methodologies

### Q9. Which is NOT one of the 8 Performance Domains?
A. Stakeholders
B. Team
C. Documentation
D. Uncertainty

### Q10. Project managers spend most of their time:
A. Doing technical work
B. Communicating
C. Writing documents
D. Attending meetings

### Q11. A "Directive" PMO:
A. Provides only templates
B. Suggests best practices
C. Directly manages projects
D. Reports to the CEO

### Q12. Which is an Organizational Process Asset (OPA)?
A. Government regulations
B. Industry standards
C. Lessons learned database
D. Market conditions

### Q13. A stakeholder pressures the PM to falsify a status report. The PM should:
A. Comply to keep the peace
B. Report the truth and address the pressure
C. Quit immediately
D. Modify the report only if no harm

### Q14. The PMBOK 7th Edition emphasizes:
A. Detailed processes and ITTOs
B. Principles and Performance Domains
C. Waterfall methodology only
D. Six Sigma

### Q15. A project manager should default to:
A. Escalating issues to the sponsor
B. Talking to stakeholders/team and coaching
C. Submitting change requests for any deviation
D. Strict adherence to the original plan

---

## 🎯 Answers + Explanations

### Q1: **B. Temporary endeavor producing unique result**
Direct from PMI definition.

### Q2: **A. Responsibility, Respect, Fairness, Honesty**

### Q3: **C. Government regulations**
EEFs = external/cultural. Templates and lessons learned are OPAs.

### Q4: **B. Refuse and disclose**
Ethics: Responsibility + Honesty + Fairness require this.

### Q5: **B. Limited or no authority**
Functional orgs: functional managers have authority; PMs are coordinators.

### Q6: **B. Ways of Working, Power Skills, Business Acumen**
Refreshed Talent Triangle (recent).

### Q7: **B. Predictive**
Stable + regulated = predictive fits.

### Q8: **B. 8 Performance Domains**
PMBOK 7 / new PMP structure.

### Q9: **C. Documentation**
The 8: Stakeholders, Team, Approach, Planning, Work, Delivery, Measurement, Uncertainty.

### Q10: **B. Communicating**
~90% of PM time.

### Q11: **C. Directly manages projects**
Directive = high control. Supportive = low. Controlling = compliance.

### Q12: **C. Lessons learned database**
OPA = internal artifact.

### Q13: **B. Report truth + address pressure**
Honesty value applies.

### Q14: **B. Principles and Performance Domains**
Major shift from PMBOK 6's process-heavy approach.

### Q15: **B. Talking to stakeholders/team and coaching**
The PMP-2021 mindset.

# PMP Module 9: Procurement Management 🛒

> **Why this module matters:** ~5-10 questions. Procurement is contract-heavy. Know contract types and the procurement process flow.

---

## 🎯 What Is Procurement?

Procurement = buying or contracting **outside** your organization for goods or services.

In project terms:
- 🛠️ Hiring vendors
- 🤝 Sub-contracting work
- 🏗️ Renting equipment
- 📦 Buying materials
- 💼 Engaging consultants

---

## 🔄 The 3 Procurement Processes

### 1. Plan Procurement Management
- 📋 What to buy, how, when
- Output: **Procurement Management Plan**, **Procurement Statement of Work (SOW)**

### 2. Conduct Procurements
- 📨 Send out RFx (RFP, RFQ, RFI)
- 🤝 Evaluate bids
- 📝 Sign contracts

### 3. Control Procurements
- 👀 Monitor vendor performance
- 🔄 Manage changes
- 💵 Process payments
- 🏁 Close out (after delivery)

---

## 📜 RFx — Request for What?

| RFx | What It Asks | When |
|-----|--------------|------|
| **RFI** (Request for Information) | "What can you do?" | Early — gathering info |
| **RFP** (Request for Proposal) | "How would you do it?" | Solution unclear |
| **RFQ** (Request for Quotation) | "How much will you charge?" | Solution clear, just price |
| **IFB / RFB** (Invitation/Request for Bid) | Sealed bids | Government, fixed scope |

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "Buyer needs to compare prices for a well-defined product." → **RFQ.**

---

## 📋 Contract Types (CRITICAL!)

3 main families. Know each:

### 1. Fixed-Price (FP) Contracts
**Vendor takes the risk.**

| Type | What |
|------|------|
| **FFP (Firm Fixed-Price)** | Single fixed price; vendor risk if costs exceed |
| **FPIF (Fixed-Price Incentive Fee)** | Fixed + bonus for performance |
| **FP-EPA (Fixed-Price w/ Economic Price Adjustment)** | Adjusts for inflation, exchange rates |

🎯 Use when: scope is **clear and stable**.

### 2. Cost-Reimbursable (CR) Contracts
**Buyer takes the risk** (you pay all costs + a fee).

| Type | What |
|------|------|
| **CPFF (Cost Plus Fixed Fee)** | Costs + fixed fee |
| **CPIF (Cost Plus Incentive Fee)** | Costs + bonus for performance |
| **CPAF (Cost Plus Award Fee)** | Costs + subjective award |

🎯 Use when: scope is **uncertain or evolving**.

### 3. Time & Materials (T&M) Contracts
**Hybrid** — pay for time + materials.

🎯 Use when: small, short-term, or staffing arrangements.

---

## 💰 Risk Allocation by Contract Type

```
HIGH BUYER RISK ←————————————————→ HIGH SELLER RISK
  
  Cost+ (CPFF)    T&M    Fixed Price (FFP)
```

🎯 **Memory:** "Cost-plus = customer pays. Fixed-price = vendor pays (if over)."

🎯 **Exam pattern:** "Project has well-defined scope. Best contract type for buyer?" → **Fixed-Price.**
"Scope is highly uncertain. Best contract for buyer?" → **Cost-Reimbursable** (despite higher risk, you avoid vendor padding).

---

## 📜 SOW (Statement of Work)

Describes the work to be procured:
- Scope
- Deliverables
- Acceptance criteria
- Schedule

3 types:
- **Performance SOW** — describe end results (vendor figures out how)
- **Functional SOW** — performance + features
- **Design SOW** — describe exactly how (rare in agile)

---

## 🎯 Make-or-Buy Analysis

Decision: build internally or buy externally?

Considerations:
- Cost
- Skills available
- Strategic value (don't outsource core competence)
- Speed
- Risk

---

## 📨 The Procurement Process Flow

```
Plan → SOW + RFP → Send to vendors → Receive bids
   ↓
Evaluate (price, qualifications, references)
   ↓
Negotiate
   ↓
Sign contract
   ↓
Manage performance during execution
   ↓
Close out (formal acceptance, lessons learned)
```

---

## 🏁 Contract Closure

When the work is done:
1. ✅ Verify all deliverables met
2. 📜 Formal acceptance
3. 💵 Final payments
4. 📚 Lessons learned (about vendor performance)
5. 📦 Archive contract

---

## 🤝 Centralized vs Decentralized Procurement

- **Centralized** — one office handles all procurement (consistent, expert)
- **Decentralized** — each PM handles theirs (faster, may lack expertise)

---

## 📊 Procurement in Agile

Agile contracts are tricky because scope evolves:
- 💼 **T&M for development**
- 💼 **Capped T&M** (cap on hours/dollars)
- 💼 **Iterative fixed-price** (each sprint = new fixed-price contract)
- 💼 **Money for nothing, change for free** (innovative agile contract structure)

🎯 **Insight:** Pure Fixed-Price contracts often clash with agile. Hybrid approaches work best.

---

## ⚠️ Common Procurement Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Going with lowest bid without quality consideration
- ❌ No SOW (vendor delivers wrong thing)
- ❌ Skipping evaluation criteria
- ❌ Vendor contracts but no PM involvement during execution
- ❌ Closing contract without formal acceptance

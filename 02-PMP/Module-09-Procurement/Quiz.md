# ✏️ PMP Module 9 Quiz: Procurement

## Questions

### Q1. Buyer needs to compare prices for a well-defined product. Best RFx:
A. RFI
B. RFP
C. RFQ
D. SOW

### Q2. Project scope is highly uncertain. Best contract for buyer:
A. Firm Fixed-Price
B. Cost-Reimbursable
C. Time & Materials only
D. Performance contract

### Q3. In a Firm Fixed-Price contract, the risk of cost overruns is borne by:
A. Buyer
B. Vendor
C. Shared 50/50
D. Government

### Q4. CPFF stands for:
A. Cost Plus Fixed Fee
B. Cost Plus First Fee
C. Capped Performance Fixed Fee
D. Contractor Performance Fee Fund

### Q5. The Statement of Work (SOW) includes:
A. Vendor pricing
B. Scope, deliverables, acceptance criteria
C. Bid evaluation criteria
D. Contract terms only

### Q6. RFI is used:
A. Late, for final selection
B. Early, to gather information
C. To compare prices
D. As the contract document

### Q7. Make-or-Buy Analysis considers:
A. Only cost
B. Cost, skills, speed, risk, strategic value
C. Vendor relationships only
D. Vendor location

### Q8. After contract execution, the LAST step is:
A. Send invoice
B. Formal acceptance and contract closeout
C. Lessons learned only
D. Archive

### Q9. T&M contracts work best for:
A. Large multi-year projects
B. Small, short-term, or staff augmentation
C. Fixed-price replacements
D. Government regulated work

### Q10. In agile projects, pure Fixed-Price contracts often:
A. Work perfectly
B. Clash with evolving scope
C. Are required by PMI
D. Reduce risk

---

## Answers
1.C  2.B  3.B  4.A  5.B  6.B  7.B  8.B  9.B  10.B

## Detailed answer rationales

These per-question rationales explain why each correct answer is correct AND why each distractor is wrong. This is the detailed-rationale layer on top of the existing letter-only answer key, both coexist. Q numbers match the questions above.

**Q1. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* "Temporary endeavor producing a unique result" is the official PMI definition (PMBOK 7).
*Why others are wrong.*
- A: An ongoing operation is the antonym of a project (operations are continuous and repetitive).
- C: A program is *a collection of related projects*, not a project itself.
- D: Continuous improvement is operations work (e.g., Kaizen).

*Exam-takeaway.* Memorize the PMI definition verbatim.

**Q2. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* Confronting/Problem-solving is the PMP-default conflict-resolution technique, it pursues a win-win.
*Why others are wrong.*
- A: "Pick one and move on" is Forcing/Directing, only justified in emergencies.
- C: Premature escalation skips the team-level conversation the PMP-2021 mindset requires.
- D: Avoiding/Withdrawing is the worst conflict approach for most scenarios.

*Exam-takeaway.* Default: Confronting/Problem-solving. The exam rewards it.

**Q3. Answer: D**

*Why D is correct.* PMI's research and PMBOK 7 state PMs spend ~90% of their time on communication.
*Why others are wrong.*
- A/B/C: 30/50/70% are all common-sense numbers but well below the empirically reported figure.

*Exam-takeaway.* Memorize "~90%."

**Q4. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* CV = EV − AC, by definition (PMBOK 6 §7.4.2.1).
*Why others are wrong.*
- A: EV − PV is *SV* (Schedule Variance), not CV.
- C: PV − EV is the negative of SV; also wrong.
- D: AC − PV has no defined EVM meaning.

*Exam-takeaway.* Cost involves AC; Schedule involves PV.

**Q5. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* SPI < 1 means behind schedule (you're earning less value than planned by this point).
*Why others are wrong.*
- A: SPI is about schedule, not budget.
- C: SPI > 1 would mean ahead.
- D: SPI = 1.0 would mean on schedule.

*Exam-takeaway.* SPI/CPI < 1 = bad; > 1 = good.

**Q6. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* The Critical Path is the *longest* path through the network and determines the *shortest possible* project duration.
*Why others are wrong.*
- A: Shortest path through the network would mean it could be done faster.
- C: Most expensive path is unrelated to critical-path math.
- D: Resource intensity is not what defines the critical path.

*Exam-takeaway.* Critical Path = longest path = zero float.

**Q7. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* In Scrum, only the Product Owner can cancel a Sprint (per the Scrum Guide 2020).
*Why others are wrong.*
- A: Scrum Master facilitates but doesn't decide cancellation.
- C: Stakeholders cannot cancel a Sprint.
- D: Team consensus is not the cancellation mechanism.

*Exam-takeaway.* PO cancels sprints. Memorize.

**Q8. Answer: C**

*Why C is correct.* PMI's 4 ethics values are Responsibility, Respect, Fairness, Honesty.
*Why others are wrong.*
- A/B/D are plausible-sounding but wrong (often used as distractors).

*Exam-takeaway.* Memorize "R-R-F-H."

**Q9. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* Fast-tracking parallelizes activities, no extra cost (but adds risk).
*Why others are wrong.*
- A: Crashing adds cost (more resources).
- C: "Add team members" is a form of crashing, costs more.
- D: Lowering quality is never the PMP-correct answer.

*Exam-takeaway.* No-cost compression = fast-track. Cost-based compression = crash.

**Q10. Answer: A**

*Why A is correct.* PERT = (O + 4M + P) / 6 is the canonical Three-Point estimation formula.
*Why others are wrong.*
- B: (O + M + P) / 3 is a *simple average* (sometimes called "triangular"), not PERT.
- C: (O + P) / 2 ignores M entirely.
- D: M × 4 makes no sense.

*Exam-takeaway.* PERT weights M heavily (4×).

**Q11. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* Buying insurance transfers risk to a third party (the insurer), textbook Transfer strategy.
*Why others are wrong.*
- A: Avoid would mean eliminating the risky scope.
- C: Mitigate would mean reducing probability/impact.
- D: Accept would mean taking the risk with no active step.

*Exam-takeaway.* Insurance, fixed-price contracts, warranties = Transfer.

**Q12. Answer: A**

*Why A is correct.* EMV = Probability × Impact. 0.30 × −$100K = −$30K.
*Why others are wrong.*
- B: +$30K is the wrong sign for a *loss*.
- C: −$130K is meaningless math.
- D: +$70K appears to subtract probability from impact incorrectly.

*Exam-takeaway.* EMV preserves sign; losses are negative.

**Q13. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* Storming is the conflict stage in Tuckman's model, roles, priorities, technical approaches argued.
*Why others are wrong.*
- A: Forming is polite/getting acquainted.
- C: Norming is when the team has worked through conflict and established norms.
- D: Performing is high-output cooperation.

*Exam-takeaway.* Conflict surfacing → Storming.

**Q14. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* Fishbone/Ishikawa diagrams are *root cause analysis* tools.
*Why others are wrong.*
- A: Schedule tools include Gantt and CPM diagrams.
- C: Communication has its own tool set.
- D: Risk register is a list, not a diagram.

*Exam-takeaway.* Fishbone = root cause.

*Exam-takeaway.* High/High = Manage Closely.

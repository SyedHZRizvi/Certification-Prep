## Detailed answer rationales

These rationales explain why each correct answer is correct AND why each distractor is wrong.

**Q1. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* PMBOK 7 (2021) restructured around 8 Performance Domains + 12 Principles.
*Why others are wrong.* A is PMBOK 6's structure. C invents methodologies. D invents life cycles.
*Exam-takeaway.* PMBOK 7 ≠ PMBOK 6.

**Q2. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* Adding resources to *increase* the likelihood of an opportunity is the Enhance strategy.
*Why others are wrong.* A (Exploit) makes the opportunity certain (100%), not just more likely. C (Share) brings in a partner. D (Accept) takes no action.
*Exam-takeaway.* Enhance = increase probability/impact.

**Q3. Answer: A**

*Why A is correct.* EAC = BAC/CPI = $500K/1.2 = $416,667.
*Why others are wrong.* B applies CPI inversely. C ignores CPI. D is arbitrary.
*Exam-takeaway.* CPI > 1 means EAC < BAC.

**Q4. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* The Stakeholder Engagement Assessment Matrix maps Current vs Desired engagement levels per stakeholder.
*Why others are wrong.* A is a single dimension. C is one possible content but not the matrix's purpose. D is the Risk Register's domain.
*Exam-takeaway.* SEAM = C vs D engagement.

**Q5. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* Uncertain scope demands Cost-Reimbursable (the buyer carries cost risk because vendor padding would be enormous on FFP).
*Why others are wrong.* A/C require defined scope. D doesn't address scope uncertainty.
*Exam-takeaway.* Uncertain scope → CR.

**Q6. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* Storming = conflict surfacing; PM coaches to resolve.
*Why others are wrong.* A is the prior stage. C/D abandon the team.
*Exam-takeaway.* Storming → Coach.

**Q7. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* Backlog refinement is an *ongoing activity*, not a formal Scrum event.
*Why others are wrong.* A claims it's an event (the Scrum Guide doesn't list it as one). C/D mis-state cadence.
*Exam-takeaway.* Refinement = ongoing.

**Q8. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* PMI's Honesty value requires correcting reporting errors promptly and informing stakeholders.
*Why others are wrong.* A violates Honesty. C is reactive. D is concealment.
*Exam-takeaway.* Honesty → correct immediately.

**Q9. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* Pareto principle = 80/20 rule (80% of problems from 20% of causes).
*Why others are wrong.* A flat 50/50. C is PERT (estimation). D is Six Sigma.
*Exam-takeaway.* Pareto = 80/20.

**Q10. Answer: A**

*Why A is correct.* PERT SD = (P − O) / 6.
*Why others are wrong.* B is the PERT mean. C is range/2. D is not a defined formula.
*Exam-takeaway.* SD = (P − O)/6.

**Q11. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* Risk > threshold = plan stronger response or escalate.
*Why others are wrong.* A ignores threshold breach. C/D fail to act.
*Exam-takeaway.* Threshold breached → escalate or strengthen.

**Q12. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* PMP-2021 mindset: Serve and Lead (servant leadership).
*Why others are wrong.* A is the old command-and-control mindset. C/D under-state the role.
*Exam-takeaway.* PMP-2021 = Serve + Lead.

**Q13. Answer: A**

*Why others are wrong.* B/C/D are invented levels.
*Exam-takeaway.* U-R-N-S-L.

**Q14. Answer: C**

*Why others are wrong.* A/B share too little; D shares nothing.
*Exam-takeaway.* Same product = shared PO/Backlog/Goal/DoD.

**Q15. Answer: B**

*Why B is correct.* Plan Quality Management output: Quality Management Plan + Metrics.
*Why others are wrong.* A is a Control Quality output. C is Control Quality output. D is closing-process output.
